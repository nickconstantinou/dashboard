# The Season So Far: An FPL International Break Discussion with Tom Campbell

## Introduction

The Premier League has reached its traditional international break, offering Fantasy Premier League managers a rare three-week respite from the relentless gameweek deadlines. But for dedicated FPL enthusiasts, the urge to analyze, debate, and strategize never truly subsides. In this episode of "Who Got The Assist," hosts Sam and Nick are joined by long-time FPL community figure Tom Campbell for a zoomed-out conversation that steps back from the granular gameweek analysis to examine the broader landscape of the season.

Tom Campbell, known on social media as @TomCampbell on X, brings considerable credibility to the discussion. He's a regular guest on the Planet FPL podcast and The Always Cheating FPL podcast, making this appearance something of a hat-trick completion for the popular FPL podcasting circuit. The timing is particularly relevant as the community grapples with several significant storylines: Mohamed Salah's announced departure, the race toward the elusive 200-point milestone, and the peculiar points distribution that has defined this campaign.

The conversation occurs on Tuesday, March 24th, with managers advised to hold their wildcard decisions until after the FA Cup final resolves. This episode serves as both a retrospective on the season's trajectory and a preview of the strategic considerations that will shape the run-in.

## Key Concepts and Arguments

### Tom Campbell's Unconventional Season Strategy

The conversation opens with Tom providing an honest assessment of his FPL campaign, characterizing it as "relatively catastrophic" while acknowledging a degree of self-inflicted wound. His approach this season has been deliberately unconventional, driven by a desire to inject fun into what he perceived as increasingly pervasive ambivalence toward the game. Rather than playing it safe with the established template, Tom committed to "huge swings" from gameweek one, most notably by excluding Erling Haaland entirely—a decision that proved painful during Haaland's explosive start but has been mitigated by the striker's subsequent decline.

Tom revealed that he nearly didn't play this season at all, experiencing significant FPL fatigue after last campaign. The insight that many listeners will find relatable is his diagnosis of the difference between reasons for staying and excuses for not leaving—a psychological framing that he applied to his own situation. Rather than simply tolerating FPL as he had in previous seasons, he chose to actively reshape his engagement with the game by taking calculated risks and pursuing the "scenic route" rather than the optimal path.

His gameweek 31 strategy exemplified this philosophy: using a Free Hit to load up on Fulham defenders, including captaincy on a Fulham defender—a move he himself acknowledged no "person in their right mind" would recommend. The gambit didn't pan out, but Tom stressed that the underlying intent was to pursue something spectacular rather than merely accumulate points safely. He's now burned through all his chips, having used them in researched but unconventional ways that prioritize entertainment over rank preservation.

### The 200 Club Race

The discussion turns to one of FPL's most celebrated milestones: the 200-point barrier. No player has achieved this threshold yet this season, but several candidates are approaching the mark. The "200 club" represents rarefied status in FPL circles, typically achieved only by elite performers across a full Premier League campaign. The hosts discuss which players have the most realistic path to reaching this milestone by season's end, examining both the remaining fixture difficulty and the individual trajectories of top-performing assets.

### Mo Salah's Impending Departure

Perhaps the most significant narrative discussed is Mohamed Salah's announcement that he will leave Liverpool at the season's end. This news, breaking just before recording commenced, draws considerable reflection on Salah's extraordinary FPL legacy. Year after year, Salah has been a staple of successful FPL teams, providing consistent returns that made him an essential asset regardless of price fluctuations. The hosts consider what his departure means for both Liverpool's appeal next season and the broader FPL landscape, noting that losing such a reliable premium option will fundamentally alter budget allocation strategies for millions of managers.

### Points Distribution Analysis

Tom has compiled detailed data on FPL points distribution across the season, and the hosts express enthusiasm for exploring this information later in the episode. The preview suggests some "shocking realizations" that emerged from this analysis, indicating that the season's scoring patterns have defied conventional expectations in notable ways. This kind of aggregate data helps managers understand where their own performance falls relative to the broader population and what scoring environments have predominated.

### Strategic Considerations for the Run-In

With approximately a fortnight until FPL resumes following the FA Cup final, the conversation touches tentatively on wildcard strategy. The hosts acknowledge that now is not the time to finalize wildcard teams, but they begin seeding ideas about which players and templates might emerge as the season reaches its climax. The interplay between fixtures, form, and availability will become increasingly critical as every gameweek carries greater weight.

## Detailed Takeaways

**The Value of Playing FPL on Your Own Terms**: Tom's season demonstrates that FPL is ultimately a game designed for enjoyment. His deliberate choice to pursue unconventional strategies rather than chase ranks reflects a conscious decision to rekindle his engagement with FPL after experiencing fatigue. For managers feeling similarly burnt out, his approach offers a template: rather than abandoning the game entirely, reframing how you engage with it can restore the fun factor. The "scenic route" may not optimize your rank, but it may preserve your sanity and continued participation.

**Haaland's Decline Has Significant FPL Implications**: Tom's decision to exclude Haaland from the start proved disastrous when the striker began with "like an absolute train," but his patience has been rewarded as Haaland has dropped off considerably. This narrative arc illustrates both the risks of anti-establishment picks and how form cycles in FPL can pendulum dramatically. Haaland owners who stuck with him through the early season have experienced diminishing returns, while those who found alternatives have not suffered as much as feared.

**Chip Strategy Requires Long-Term Thinking**: Tom's confession that he's "burnt" all his chips by gameweek 31 represents a common FPL trap: using valuable tools too early or too aggressively. While his reasoning was connected to his desire for entertainment, the practical implication is having fewer strategic options available for the crucial run-in period when fixtures become condensed and form becomes more volatile. Managers should consider preserving at least one major chip (wildcard or free hit) for the business end of the season.

**Mo Salah's Departure Reshapes Premium Strategy**: With Salah leaving, FPL managers must confront a fundamental shift in how they allocate their budgets. For multiple seasons, Salah has been the premium midfielder who justified his price through consistent returns. His absence forces a complete reconsideration of premium options, potentially opening opportunities for value plays in the Liverpool squad or forcing managers toward other premium options like Erling Haaland or other elite midfielders. This should inform wildcard planning as managers consider their 2025-26 squads.

**The International Break Offers Strategic Opportunity**: The three-week gap, while frustrating for those wanting to make immediate changes, actually provides valuable time for strategic planning. The hosts explicitly advise against hasty wildcard decisions before the FA Cup resolves, recognizing that team news and fixture confirmations can dramatically alter the landscape. Managers should use this period to research, analyze form, and prepare their approach rather than acting reactively when the season resumes.
