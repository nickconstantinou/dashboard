FPL Gameweek 35 team news: Friday’s live injury updates

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

Arsenal

Arsenal will be without Kai Havertz and Jurrien Timber for this weekend's fixture. Both players have been ruled out due to injuries sustained ahead of Gameweek 35.

Martin Odegaard's availability for the upcoming match is uncertain. The midfielder came off during Arsenal's Champions League encounter against Atletico Madrid, and manager Mikel Arteta introduced an element of doubt regarding his participation.

On a more positive note, Arsenal emerged from Wednesday's match in Madrid without any new injury concerns. The club expects to provide a final update on the squad's fitness status tomorrow morning.

In tactical developments, Declan Rice was repositioned into a defensive midfield 'six' role during recent fixtures, with Martin Zubimendi operating in a more advanced position. Arteta indicated that the team can adapt its approach depending on the opposition they face.

---

Liverpool

Mohamed Salah's muscle injury is considered relatively minor, and he remains expected to feature again this season. However, the Egyptian international will not be available for Sunday's fixture. Salah is priced at £14.0m in FPL.

Goalkeeper Alisson has yet to rejoin full training but is described as very close to returning to the pitch. He has not been ruled out of Sunday's trip to Manchester United. The club expects to receive a medical update later today or tomorrow regarding his readiness.

Milos Kerkez, priced at £5.6m, left the training pitch on Wednesday due to some niggles. The defender is expected back on the grass ahead of Gameweek 35 and should be available for selection.

---

Manchester City

Rodri continues his recovery from a groin injury. Pep Guardiola confirmed the midfielder is making good progress, but it remains too early to determine whether he will feature this weekend.

---

Burnley

Scott Parker had been scheduled to face the media on Thursday but parted ways with Burnley on the same day. Interim manager Mike Jackson will take charge of the team for the remainder of the season. Jackson will not hold a pre-match press conference ahead of Gameweek 35.
