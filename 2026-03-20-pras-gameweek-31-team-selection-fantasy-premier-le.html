Pras' Gameweek 31 Team Selection | Fantasy Premier League Tips 2025/26

Introduction: Navigating the First Blank Gameweek

Welcome back to another Fantasy Premier League team selection video from Pras, the voice behind the FPL Wire. In this edition, we're hitting a significant milestone in the 2025/26 FPL season: Gameweek 31 marks the first blank gameweek, and with it comes the complex fixture chaos that defines the business end of any FPL campaign. The video was recorded after the three English clubs still competing in Europe—Crystal Palace, Nottingham Forest, and Aston Villa—all progressed in their respective competitions, adding another layer of intricacy to an already complicated fixture puzzle.

Pras opens by acknowledging that this is the time when FPL managers begin making decisions that may contradict their long-term strategies, driven by the immediate pressures of blank gameweeks. He's recording with just under 24 hours until the Friday deadline, providing real-time analysis of how European results impact the fixture landscape. The good news, as Pras sees it, is that more English teams progressing means more fixture congestion and therefore more carnage—a situation that generally benefits managers who plan carefully. He's feeling positive about how the evening unfolded, particularly given the Crystal Palace result.

The Crystal Palace Situation: Why Gameweek 32 Is No Longer a Double

The centerpiece of this video centers on Crystal Palace's progression in European competition, which has direct implications for their fixture schedule. Pras walks viewers through an updated chart showing exactly where Palace now fit into the remaining gameweeks. The key takeaway is that Palace's fixture against Manchester City cannot be accommodated in Gameweek 31, making it a guaranteed blank for all managers who own Palace assets. This is particularly relevant for anyone still holding onto the Palace striker, who might have been hoping for a last-minute fixture rearrangement.

More significantly for the broader FPL landscape, Pras explains that the Manchester City match would have slotted into Gameweek 32 had Palace been eliminated from Europe. However, because Palace progressed to the next round of their European competition, they now have that second leg to play, meaning Gameweek 32 is also confirmed as not being a double gameweek for them. The expected double now lands in Gameweek 33, with another likely double in Gameweek 36. This confirms what Pras and other analysts had predicted as the base case scenario, and the fact that more information still points to this outcome gives managers greater confidence in their planning.

Pras also mentions that for the carnage scenario to unfold—in which Manchester City would need to play in both Gameweek 31 and 32—they would need to beat Liverpool in their upcoming match, Chelsea would need to win their FA Cup tie, Arsenal would need to win theirs, and then the television schedulers would need to make the predicted fixture arrangements. Many variables remain up in the air, but the base case of a Gameweek 33 double with Arsenal playing twice remains the most likely outcome.

The Three Viable Chip Strategies

Rather than prescribing a single approach, Pras outlines the three clear chip strategies that FPL managers are employing this season, each with distinct timing and rationale. Understanding these strategies is essential because your chip deployment significantly influences which players you should target in the coming weeks.

The Most Popular Strategy involves playing your wildcard in Gameweek 32, deploying the bench boost in Gameweek 33, and using the free hit in Gameweek 34. Pras notes that this is the most common approach, though he personally disagrees with playing the wildcard this early. He argues that managers should wait until after the FA Cup quarterfinals have concluded before pressing the wildcard button, as waiting won't cost you much in terms of player prices and will give you more information about which fixtures are actually moving.

The Alternative Strategy that Pras is personally leaning toward is to free hit in Gameweek 33, bench boost in Gameweek 36, and wildcard in Gameweek 35. He admits he prefers this approach because his current team already looks strong for Gameweeks 32 and 34, meaning he doesn't feel the urgency to wildcard immediately. This strategy becomes more attractive if the fixtures ultimately align as expected, and it's semi-popular among experienced managers who have similar team structures.

The Niche Third Strategy involves wildcards in Gameweek 33, using a hit in Gameweek 34, and potentially forgoing the bench boost entirely if they've already used it earlier in the season. Some managers in this camp will look at their Gameweek 32 team and decide they'd rather wildcard in 33 instead. This approach works for those who already have strong 32 and 34 teams but need to reconstruct for the double weeks. The bench boost can then be deployed in either Gameweek 35 or 36 depending on fixture scheduling.

Pras emphasizes that all three strategies are viable, but the fact that only one of them places the wildcard in Gameweek 32 is why waiting makes sense. During the upcoming international break, managers will know with 100% certainty which fixtures are moving to Gameweek 33 and which are staying in 34, eliminating much of the guesswork.

Fixture Analysis: Targeting the Right Teams

Pras then dives deep into specific team targeting based on which chip strategy you employ, introducing a fixture analysis chart from Solio that shows projected goals for each team across the remaining gameweeks. This analysis is critical because it helps managers understand which players will provide the most value depending on their chip plans.

For managers planning to use their free hit in Gameweek 33—which is relevant for those on the wildcard who want to plan ahead—the data clearly shows that Liverpool is the team to target. Pras points out that Liverpool has both Fulham at home and Crystal Palace at home in Gameweeks 32 and 34 respectively, making them excellent rotation options. This is why Mohamed Salah remains a crucial asset, though his injury status will become clearer in Liverpool's upcoming press conference. The second-highest projected team for this strategy is Manchester United, who also feature favorably in both Gameweeks 32 and 34, making them the second-best option for a free hit 33 strategy.

Fulham also emerges as an excellent option in this analysis, which explains why Bobby Wilson has become such a popular pick this week. Fulham's attack ranks highly across both weeks, while Aston Villa and West Ham also feature in the upper echelons. Pras notes that between teams ranked fourth and below, there's very little separating them, so the final choice often depends on goal-scoring share within each team and penalty-taking duties. He specifically mentions Sebastian Sessegnon as an interesting option, particularly if Salah were to miss any fixtures, as Sessegnon would likely take penalty duties.

On the defensive side for Gameweek 33, Liverpool, Manchester United, West Ham, Tottenham, and Nottingham Forest are the teams to focus on, explaining why Nottingham Forest defenders have been gaining popularity in recent weeks.

For those planning a free hit in Gameweek 34 instead, the analysis shifts dramatically. The right-hand column of the fixture chart shows that Chelsea's attack becomes the priority, making players like Nicolas Jackson, Pedro Neto, and Cole Palmer attractive options. Newcastle also features prominently, as do Brighton. This creates an interesting decision point for managers: should they target Fulham this week (because they're good for Gameweek 34) or should they target Newcastle (because they're good for Gameweek 34 and can be brought in now as a bench option)?

Pras provides a clear framework for this decision: if you're uncertain about whether you'll free hit in 34 and need a player you can bench in the meantime, go for Newcastle. If you know you'll definitely free hit in 34 and want to play your attacker that week, go for Fulham. On defense for Gameweek 34, Arsenal remains the highest option despite their blank, followed by Newcastle, Brighton, and Aston Villa, though Pras notes that Brighton and Villa are probably not worth bringing in specifically for this purpose.

Detailed Takeaways

The fixture picture has crystallized but still requires patience. With Crystal Palace progressing and confirming no double in Gameweek 32, managers now have greater clarity on the double gameweek structure. Gameweek 33 is increasingly confirmed as the prime double, with 34 also potentially holding value. Waiting until after the international break to deploy your wildcard gives you maximum information with minimal cost, as price changes are minimal over this short period.

Your chip strategy should dictate your transfer targets. There's no universal "best" team to target this week—instead, you should build your squad around when you plan to use each chip. If free hitting in 33, prioritize Liverpool and Manchester United assets. If planning a 34 free hit, focus on Chelsea and Newcastle options. This targeted approach prevents the common mistake of spreading investments too thin across multiple teams.

Rotation flexibility is the deciding factor between similar options. When choosing between Newcastle and Fulham for your bench this week, the decision shouldn't be about which team has better fixtures in isolation. Instead, consider whether you need a player who can play immediately (Fulham for 34) or one who can sit on your bench until you need them (Newcastle if you might not free hit at all).

The top of the leaderboard remains catchable. Pras's observation that he's only 50 points from top 100k despite being in the 500k region demonstrates that significant rank movement is still possible. The blank gameweek period, while stressful, also creates opportunities for managers who make sharp decisions to leapfrog those who panic.

European progression directly shapes your asset values. The fact that Crystal Palace's progression eliminated a potential Manchester City double in 32 affects far more than just Palace owners. It confirms that Arsenal will likely be the primary double in 33, meaning their defenders and attackers carry additional value for those planning bench boost strategies.
