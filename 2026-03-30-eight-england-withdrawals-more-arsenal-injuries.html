Eight England Withdrawals + More Arsenal Injuries: The Complete Breakdown

The Full Story

The March 2026 international break has descended into an injury nightmare for clubs, with Arsenal bearing the brunt of the damage. Eight players have now withdrawn from Thomas Tuchel's England squad alone, while a total of eleven Gunners players have returned early from international duty across various nations.

For England specifically, the situation developed in stages. Three players—Aaron Ramsdale, Dominic Calvert-Lewin, and AC Milan's Fikayo Tomori—departed as planned for non-injury reasons. However, five others left with fitness concerns. The most alarming case is Noni Madueke, who was forced off after just 38 minutes of England's draw with Uruguay while wearing a knee brace. Adam Wharton also picked up an issue during that match, and John Stones—a supposed starter—pulled out beforehand due to a calf problem.

The Arsenal contingent saw an especially chaotic 48 hours. Declan Rice and Bukayo Saka were among 11 players permitted to join up late on Friday as Tuchel managed workloads, but they "did an Abe Simpson" and walked straight back out, returning to Arsenal for medical assessment. The Gunners' injury toll extended beyond England as well: Piero Hincapie left Ecuador's squad with what appeared to be a muscle injury from their draw with Morocco, while Spain announced Martin Zubimendi's withdrawal due to discomfort in his right knee. Meanwhile, Everton's Iliman Ndiaye has returned to his club from Senegal duty with left foot discomfort sustained at club level.

Why This Matters

This represents a significant crisis for Arsenal at a pivotal moment in their season. With 11 players now returning early from international duty, Mikel Arteta faces a mounting injury list that could derail their Premier League title challenge. The timing is particularly concerning given the compressed fixture schedule that typically follows international breaks.

For fantasy football managers and sports bettors, these withdrawals signal urgent opportunities to reassess player availability and potential game-time allocations. The uncertainty around key assets like Rice, Saka, and Madueke creates immediate selection dilemmas ahead of the next round of Premier League fixtures.

Beyond individual clubs, this situation intensifies the ongoing tension between club and country obligations. Tuchel's workload management strategy—allowing players to report late and potentially rest them entirely—suggests national team managers are increasingly sympathetic to club concerns, but the wave of injuries anyway demonstrates the limits of such accommodations.
