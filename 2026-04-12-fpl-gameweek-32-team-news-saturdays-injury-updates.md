# Manchester United Defensive Picture Remains Murky as Carrick Pumps Brakes on Martinez Return

Manchester United's trip to Dublin this week brought some bodies to the Irish capital—but the questions around Old Trafford's backline remain largely unanswered.

Benjamin Sesko and Bryan Mbeumo were present with the squad despite pulling out of their respective national team camps on precautionary grounds, a development that will fuel optimism among Fantasy Premier League managers looking to bring them in for Gameweek 32. But Carrick offered no confirmation on their availability, leaving the pair's status for Monday's clash with Leeds United firmly in limbo.

More pressing for United fans and FPL managers alike is Lisandro Martinez. The Argentine defender has returned to training after his calf problem, and Carrick stopped short of ruling him out—but equally stopped short of guaranteeing anything.

"It's just that call we make," Carrick said. "We wouldn't be pushed into anything. It's important that the boys are ready, as important as the next game always is, the most important is always the bigger picture. So, that's a decision that we'll take. He's back training, which is great, and back on the grass, but we've certainly got to make the right decision and make sure he's ready."

Translation: Martinez is close, but nobody at Carrington is panicking to force him back for one match. Managers considering the Argentine as a differential may need to wait another week—or at least accept meaningful selection risk.

Patrick Dorgu traveled with the squad but was limited to individual training work as he continues recovering from his hamstring issue. He's further along than Matthijs de Ligt, who remained in Manchester and isn't yet back on the grass at all.

"Treatment and rehab," Carrick explained when pressed on de Ligt. "The reason he didn't come [to Ireland] is he's not quite on the grass. Patrick came back because he's further on [in his recovery], but Matta isn't quite at this stage yet. So, it was important for him to continue his rehab, really, and try and get him back. There's nothing else around it."

That remains a long-road situation. De Ligt owners have been waiting since his back issue first flared, and there's still no timeline for reintegration.

Diogo Dalot didn't join teammates in Dublin but the United website suggests he should be fine to feature against Leeds. Harry Maguire, meanwhile, will miss Gameweek 32 entirely through suspension—one match the centre-back won't be available to plug any of the defensive gaps around him.

For FPL purposes, the takeaway is a familiar one: proceed with caution. Sesko and Mbeumo represent the most promising late-week developments if they passed through the Dublin camp without issue, but Carrick's press conference offered few certainties beyond the obvious. Martinez is a name to watch, not a name to bank on.
