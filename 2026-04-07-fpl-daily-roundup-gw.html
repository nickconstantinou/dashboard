The Fantasy Premier League season has arrived at one of its most consequential decision points. The six teams confirmed for Double Gameweek 33 are Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds United, and Manchester City. This represents a substantial tactical opportunity because managers can field eleven players each scoring from two matches, with captain selections effectively doubled—goals, assists, and appearance points all multiplying through the armband. However, and this is the crucial caveat that both major FPL analysts have emphasised, the individual Gameweek 32 fixtures for these doubling teams range from difficult to brutal. Bournemouth travel to Arsenal, Leeds go to Manchester United, Manchester City host Chelsea, and Brighton travel to Burnley. Of those, only Brighton's trip to Turf Moor looks genuinely favourable. The result is a structural problem: wildcards played this week require fielding strong sides in a gameweek where the best assets don't have appealing fixtures, while simultaneously trying to build a squad for the bench boost in Gameweek 33. This tension has left many managers uncomfortable with their options.

For those still holding their wildcard, the decision boils down to two primary strategies. The first involves playing the wildcard in Gameweek 32, bench boosting in Double Gameweek 33, using a free hit in the blank Gameweek 34, and then targeting a triple captain in Gameweek 36 when another double is expected. This approach maximises the potential of having both bench boost and triple captain in the same double gameweek—a significant advantage when climbing the overall ranks. The alternative strategy saves the bench boost for Gameweek 36 instead, using a free hit in Gameweek 34 without needing to build around the immediate double. The choice depends heavily on which chips remain in any individual manager's inventory.
