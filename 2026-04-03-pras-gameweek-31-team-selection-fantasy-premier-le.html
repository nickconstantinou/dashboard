Pras' Gameweek 31 Team Selection | Fantasy Premier League Tips 2025/26

Introduction

The FPL Wire returns with what might be the most consequential team selection video of the season so far. PRA, the voice behind one of Fantasy Premier League's most trusted channels,录制完这段视频时，2025/26赛季的第一个空白比赛周（Blank Gameweek）已经迫在眉睫。对于数百万FPL玩家来说，这是一个充满压力的时刻——那些拥有水晶宫球员的人正面临着不可避免的空白，而关于芯片策略（chip strategy）的争论也达到了白热化的程度。

For those unfamiliar with the mechanics, a blank gameweek occurs when a team's fixtures are postponed, typically due to their involvement in cup competitions. With Crystal Palace, Nottingham Forest, and Aston Villa all progressing in European competition, the fixture congestion across the league has created a cascading effect that ripples through multiple gameweeks. PRA emphasizes that this isn't just about Gameweek 31—it's about understanding the chess match playing out across Gameweeks 32, 33, 34, and beyond.

The timing of this video is particularly crucial because the Friday deadline means managers have under 24 hours to make their moves. But PRA argues that despite this pressure, patience may be the smarter play. As he puts it, "You have to plan which is all fun" — but the key word is *plan*, not *panic*.

The Crystal Palace Situation: Confirmed Blank, But That's Not the Whole Story

PRA spent time updating his famous fixture chart after Crystal Palace secured their progression in European competition. The results have crystallized the situation considerably. Crystal Palace's continued European journey means their fixture against Manchester City cannot fit into Gameweek 31, creating a guaranteed blank for anyone holding Palace assets.

But the implications extend further than many managers realize. PRA explains that even if Palace had been eliminated from European competition, their Champions League second-leg fixture would have prevented the Manchester City game from fitting into Gameweek 32. "Gameweek 32 confirmed will not be a double gameweek now," he states with certainty. This is critical intelligence for managers who were hoping for a Palace double in Gameweek 32.

The confirmed picture emerging is that Gameweek 33 will likely serve as the primary double gameweek involving Crystal Palace, with another potential double in Gameweek 36 becoming increasingly probable. PRA describes this as "the lay of the land at the moment" and notes that every new piece of information continues to support this base case scenario. In fact, he argues that the current scenario is even *more* likely now than it was just two hours earlier when he first began analyzing the results, because the probability of a double in Gameweek 32 has now dropped to zero.

Recapping Gameweek 30: The Bruno Captaincy Pays Dividends

Shifting to recent history, PRA reflects on a successful Gameweek 30 that saw him achieve a "nice gameweek rank" after backing Bruno Fernandes as captain. While he acknowledges he could have gone with Mohamed Salah instead and had debated it, ultimately "Bruno captain delivered." Supporting cast members Thiago and Dango performed well, while the double Arsenal defense—particularly fortunate for Ryan Gabriel owners after Timber went off injured—contributed an "exclusive clean sheet pretty much only for Ryan Gabriel defense owners."

James Hill continues his impressive run of form, and PRA notes that despite the good week, he's still sitting around 500,000 overall. His trajectory has been volatile—he dropped from 500k to 700k after one bad week, and now he's back to 500k. However, he finds this volatility encouraging: "These jumps make me more positive and optimistic that it's possible to catch." Specifically, he's approximately 50 points from top 100k, which he describes as "very much doable" — though he acknowledges they'll need some luck along the way.

The Three Chip Strategies: A Framework for the Run-In

This section represents the core analytical content of the video. PRA breaks down what he sees as the three viable chip strategies for the blank gameweek run-in, acknowledging that "there's nothing more in terms of chip strategy because we've done it a lot and there's nothing else to say."

Strategy One: The Most Popular Approach

The dominant strategy most managers are adopting involves playing the Wildcard in Gameweek 32, Bench Boost in Gameweek 33, and Free Hit in Gameweek 34. PRA notes this timing is essentially Friday-based — the question is whether to press the button immediately or wait. His view is clear: "You should not hold off. Press it when the FA Cup quarterfinals have happened. You're not going to miss out on much in terms of prices." This advice hinges on the fact that waiting through the international break provides complete clarity on which fixtures move where, with 100% certainty about which fixtures go into Gameweek 33 and which stay in Gameweek 34.

Strategy Two: The Alternative He's Considering

PRA reveals he's personally leaning toward a different approach: Free Hit in Gameweek 33, Bench Boost in Gameweek 36, and Wildcard in Gameweek 35. "I just like it because my team looks nice in Gameweek 32 and 34," he explains. This strategy assumes that certain assets can be carried through the blank without too much damage, preserving flexibility for later gameweeks when the fixture swings more favorably. He acknowledges this will be "semi-popular" but likely not as widely adopted as the first strategy.

Strategy Three: The Niche Play

A third, more specialized option involves Wildcarding in Gameweek 33, taking a hit in Gameweek 34, and potentially bench boosting later in Gameweek 35 or 36. This appeals to managers who either already used their Bench Boost earlier or who look at their Gameweek 32 team and decide it's simply not worth the investment. PRA notes that FPL personality LR discussed this option in his main podcast, and it's a legitimate alternative for specific team structures.

PRA summarizes his thinking on the strategic landscape: "The fact that there are three strategies and only one of them has a Wildcard in Gameweek 32 makes me think that why don't you just wait?" His recommendation is to use the international break productively, gather complete information, and then execute your preferred plan with full certainty.

The Solio Fixture Analysis: Targeting Based on Your Chip Plan

PRA then pivots to a detailed analysis using data visualization from Solio (acknowledging he should have added their logo). This analysis shows projected goals for attack across different chip strategies, providing concrete guidance for each approach.

For Free Hit in Gameweek 33

If you're planning to Free Hit in Gameweek 33, the analysis is unequivocal: Liverpool is the team to target. PRA emphasizes that while Mohamed Salah's injury status remains uncertain (the answer will come from Slot's press conference tomorrow), managers should be "more bullish on Liverpool because they have good fixtures — they have Fulham at home and Crystal Palace at home in Gameweek 32 and 34." This dual fixture strength in surrounding weeks makes Liverpool assets particularly valuable for those not Wildcarding in Gameweek 32.

Manchester United emerges as the second-highest projected attack for both Gameweek 32 and 34, making Bruno Fernandes, Kobbie Mainoo, or Alejandro Garnacho solid secondary targets. PRA notes that "Man United attack also works in that strategy."

Fulham's attack also features prominently — "This is why Wilson is popular this week" — while Aston Villa's Marcos Paulo (likely meant as a category of player) rounds out the considerations. PRA notes that between the fourth and fifth highest projected teams, "there's really nothing much in it," and the choice ultimately depends on goal share and individual player form.

On defense for this strategy, Liverpool, Manchester United, West Ham, Tottenham, and Nottingham Forest are the teams to focus on, which explains why "people are looking at Fulham a lot more."

For Free Hit in Gameweek 34

The right-hand column of the analysis tells a completely different story. Here, Chelsea's attack is the priority. Newcastle also features prominently, making them a viable alternative.

This creates an immediate fork in decision-making for managers: "Some people are saying should I go for Fulham versus should I go for Newcastle. Both have good fixtures. My answer is right here." His framework is elegant in its simplicity: "Depends on what your strategy is." For managers who might switch their chip strategy and don't plan to Free Hit in Gameweek 34, Newcastle is the play. For those committed to Free Hitting in Gameweek 34, Fulham offers better value.

For Gameweek 34 Free Hitters targeting Arsenal, PRA notes the irony of their blank in the current gameweek, yet "they still are the highest because obviously they double as well." Newcastle, Brighton, and Aston Villa round out the defensive targets depending on your specific strategy.

Detailed Takeaways

1. Patience May Beat Urgency in the Wildcard Decision

The temptation to press the Wildcard button immediately is understandable given the Friday deadline, but PRA argues compellingly that waiting through the FA Cup quarterfinals provides invaluable clarity at minimal cost. The international break creates a natural pause point where all fixtures become certain. If prices don't shift meaningfully in that window — and PRA believes they won't — then waiting is objectively superior to acting prematurely.

2. Crystal Palace's Blank is Confirmed, But Its Ripple Effects Matter More

While many managers are focused on the immediate Gameweek 31 blank, the more strategic insight is that Gameweek 32 is also unlikely to feature a Palace double. The real double appears to be Gameweek 33, with another potential in Gameweek 36. This reshapes the entire chip strategy calculus, particularly for managers trying to decide between carrying Palace assets or cutting them loose.

3. Your Chip Strategy Dictates Your Transfer Targets, Not the Other Way Around

Rather than asking "who should I buy this week?" managers should first determine their chip plan and then let that plan determine their targets. The Solio analysis demonstrates this principle clearly: Liverpool and Manchester United assets make sense for Free Hit 33 managers, while Chelsea and Newcastle assets are the picks for Free Hit 34 managers. The same player can be a great buy or a terrible buy depending entirely on your strategic horizon.

4. Newcastle vs. Fulham is a False Dilemma — Context Resolves It

Both teams have attractive fixtures, but the right choice depends entirely on whether you plan to Free Hit in Gameweek 34. If you're using your Free Hit elsewhere or not at all, Newcastle is the better choice because you can bring them in now and bench them if your plans change. If you're committed to Free Hitting in Gameweek 34, Fulham's fixture advantage that week makes them the superior pick.

5. Three Strategies Are All Viable — But Execution Clarity Matters More Than Perfect Strategy

PRA explicitly states that all three chip strategies he outlines are legitimate options. The real danger isn't choosing Strategy B over Strategy A — it's acting inconsistently or panicking mid-execution. "This is like classic overthink FPL but it's not," he says, acknowledging the complexity while insisting it's manageable with clear thinking. Pick your strategy, wait for complete information if possible, and commit fully.
