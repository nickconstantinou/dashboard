# Leeds United Injury Crisis Deepens Ahead of Crucial Double Gameweek

## Multiple key players ruled out as Leeds face potential fixture pile-up

Leeds United manager Daniel Farke has confirmed that midfield anchor Anton Stach and defender Joe Rodon will both miss Gameweek 32 and are realistically expected to return in May, dealing a significant blow to the club's FPL asset values just as a lucrative Double Gameweek approaches.

Stach sustained an ankle injury during Leeds' cup match, while Rodon suffered the same injury concern. Speaking to the media on Thursday, Farke revealed the full extent of the injury crisis, stating: "Normally, you would say it's not realistic for both of them that they will be able to help us in April and we will just have them back in May, but of course, our medical department is trying everything. If they would be able to feature at some point in April, it would be a bonus."

Daniel James has also been ruled out with an adductor strain sustained in the training session before the cup game.

The situation is compounded by three additional injury doubts: Noah Okafor (back problems), Gabriel Gudmundsson (groin issues following international duty), and Jaka Bijol (knock). All three missed team training on Thursday, leaving their availability for Monday's fixture at Old Trafford against Manchester United uncertain.

However, Farke retained some optimism: "Thank God the game is just on Monday, so we have a bit more time. I'm hopeful that we have all three of them ready for the game."

The timing of these injuries could not be worse for FPL managers, as Leeds are scheduled for a Double Gameweek in Gameweek 33, making their players highly sought-after assets. The loss of Stach and Rodon removes two potential budget options from the squad, while the uncertainty around Okafor, Gudmundsson, and Bijol creates difficult selection decisions ahead of the deadline.

Managers holding Leeds assets should monitor Farke's press conference on Sunday for the latest updates, as the club attempts to salvage their playoff hopes without several key players.
