# Igor Tudor Departs Tottenham After 44 Days as Club Seeks Third Manager of the Season

**The Full Story**

Tottenham Hotspur and Igor Tudor have mutually agreed to part ways after just 44 days, marking the Croatian's shock departure from the north London club. The club issued a formal statement confirming the mutual agreement, with Tudor's tenure officially ending on March 29, 2026. Two of Tudor's coaching staff—goalkeeping coach Tomislav Rogic and physical coach Riccardo Ragnacci—also departed alongside him. Tottenham acknowledged Tudor's recent bereavement in their statement, sending support to him and his family during what the club described as a "difficult time." Bruno Saltor has taken charge of training for the remaining players at Hotspur Way while the club searches for Tudor's permanent successor.

Tudor's brief reign produced dismal results, with the Croatian failing to win any of his five Premier League matches in charge. Across all competitions, his record read one win, one draw, and five defeats from seven matches. His first game in charge was the catastrophic 4-1 north London derby defeat to Arsenal, and he never appeared to settle on a consistent tactical approach, experimenting with multiple formations during his brief tenure. This marks the third managerial change at Tottenham this season, following the dismissal of Thomas Frank in February.

**Why This Matters**

The managerial chaos at Tottenham represents one of the most turbulent seasons in the club's recent history. Having cycled through three different head coaches before the season's conclusion, the club faces an urgent need to stabilise ahead of a crucial run-in. For Fantasy Premier League managers, this upheaval presents both uncertainty and opportunity, as the incoming manager's tactical preferences could significantly impact player selections heading into the final stretch of the season. The club's woeful home form—ranking dead last in the Premier League for home results—contrasts sharply with their relatively respectable away record of ninth, suggesting the psychological impact of managerial instability may be affecting performances at the Tottenham Hotspur Stadium.

**Context & Background**

Tottenham's season has been characterised by instability at the top, with the club hierarchy evidently struggling to find a manager who can deliver results. Thomas Frank's dismissal in February appeared to open the door for Tudor, but the Croatian's inability to arrest the slide prompted another rethink after just six weeks of work. The timing of yet another managerial change—during an international break—suggests the club acted decisively once it became clear Tudor was not the solution.
