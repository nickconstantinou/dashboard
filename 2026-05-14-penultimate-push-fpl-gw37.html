Penultimate Push: FPL GW37 Preview

Looking Ahead to Gameweek 37

As the season approaches its climax, Fantasy Premier League managers face crucial decisions for the penultimate gameweek. The conversation has turned to potential assets that could make the difference in the final stretch of the season. Specifically, the hosts are discussing Arsenal picks as options for GW37, presenting potential opportunities for managers seeking to maximize their returns in the closing fixtures.

Reflection on Gameweek 36

This week's been a bit of a nightmare in FPL. Both hosts experienced a disastrous GW36 in their FPL teams, describing the performance as rather horrendous. The difficulties came from multiple angles, compounding the frustration for both managers. One significant factor contributing to the poor showing was the situation surrounding premium assets.

All three of the hosts' Man City players were benched or not in the squad for GW36, which dealt a substantial blow to their potential returns. This situation highlights the risks of concentrating too heavily on assets from a single team, particularly those competing in European competitions where rotation is common at this stage of the season.

The Identical Team Dilemma

An interesting element of the discussion involves the hosts' team structures. We have identical teams—absolutely identical—with the same player selections and transfers made throughout the season. However, the results tell a different story: the ranks aren't identical. This paradox demonstrates how close the margins can be in FPL, where identical decisions can lead to vastly different outcomes based on captain choices, timing of transfers, and other factors.

The gap in rankings is substantial. Tom is approximately 100,000 points behind in rank, which creates a challenging situation as he attempts to recover ground. The hosts acknowledge the difficulty of the task ahead, recognizing it will require exceptional performance in the remaining gameweeks to bridge such a significant differential.

Captaincy Considerations

One of the most debated topics heading into GW37 revolves around the armband. It's a pretty split crowd on captaincy this week, with managers divided over their selections. This lack of consensus presents an opportunity for those willing to take calculated risks. Going differential with the captaincy could provide the edge needed to climb ranks if the chosen player delivers, while conventional selections may simply maintain position rather than gain ground on rivals.

The penultimate gameweek offers one of the final chances for managers to make significant rank moves. Whether considering Arsenal assets, navigating the fallout from last week's City disappointment, or debating captaincy strategy, every decision carries increased weight as the season reaches its conclusion.
