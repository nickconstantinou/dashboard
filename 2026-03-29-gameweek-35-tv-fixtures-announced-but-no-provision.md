# Gameweek 35 TV Fixtures Confirmed: Fantasy Managers Left Waiting on Double Gameweek Clarity

## The Full Story

Premier League officials confirmed the television fixtures for Gameweek 35 on Friday, but disappointment swept through the Fantasy Premier League community as no provisional Double Gameweek announcement accompanied the schedule release. The fixtures are now locked in across a four-day period from Friday, May 1 through Monday, May 4, with eight matches spread across Sky Sports and TNT Sports coverage.

The confirmed Gameweek 35 fixture schedule reads as follows: Leeds United host Burnley on Friday, May 1 at 20:00 (Sky Sports), followed by Aston Villa versus Tottenham Hotspur on Saturday, May 2 at 12:30 (TNT Sports). The Saturday afternoon slate includes Bournemouth versus Crystal Palace, Brentford versus West Ham United, Newcastle United versus Brighton Hove Albion, and Wolves versus Sunderland. Arsenal face Fulham in the evening kickoff at 17:30 (Sky Sports), with Manchester United's clash against Liverpool closing Sunday's action at 15:30 (Sky Sports). The final two matches—Chelsea versus Nottingham Forest and Everton versus Manchester City—take place on Monday, May 4.

A notable complication surrounds the Aston Villa-Tottenham fixture. The match is currently scheduled for Saturday, May 2 at 12:30, but will shift to Sunday, May 3 at 19:00 BST if Aston Villa advance to the Europa League semi-finals. Meanwhile, the Leeds United-Burnley match being moved to Friday of Gameweek 35 has sparked particular concern regarding potential fixture congestion for Leeds, who may have their Gameweek 34 match against Bournemouth postponed if they reach the FA Cup semi-finals on Saturday, April 25.

## Why This Matters

For Fantasy Premier League managers, the absence of a provisional Double Gameweek announcement represents a significant missed opportunity for strategic planning. Double Gameweeks—where teams play twice in a single gameweek—offer enormous value, as selecting players from those teams essentially provides double the points potential for the same player price. Managers who know about upcoming double fixtures weeks in advance can plan transfers, save chips, and position themselves for massive point swings.

The Leeds United situation carries particular weight for both real-world and fantasy considerations. With Premier League survival still very much on the line for Daniel Farke's side, successive 72-hour turnarounds between the FA Cup semi-final, a potential postponed Gameweek 34 fixture against Bournemouth, and the newly scheduled Friday Gameweek 35 match against Burnley would place enormous physical and tactical strain on the squad. This congestion could affect player availability, fitness levels, and rotation decisions—all factors that directly impact FPL asset values and selection decisions.

The broader context involves the increasingly complex fixture scheduling created by European competition involvement, domestic cup commitments, and the traditional Premier League calendar. For FPL managers, navigating these disruptions requires as much advance notice as possible, making today's announcement's omission of Double Gameweek details a frustration felt across the game's millions of participants.
