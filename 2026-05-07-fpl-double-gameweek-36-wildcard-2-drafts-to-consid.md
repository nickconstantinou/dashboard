# FPL Double Gameweek 36 Wildcard: Key Draft Considerations

## Arsenal's Favorable Finish

Arsenal face West Ham (away), Burnley (home), and Crystal Palace (away) to end the season, with all matches remaining in London. This fixture run presents a compelling case for investing in Arsenal assets.

For defensive options, David Raya (£6.1m) and Gabriel Magalhaes (£7.2m) have the easiest trio of fixtures remaining to end the season. Both represent strong value picks given the favourable run of matches.

Arsenal's Bukayo Saka (£9.8m) delivered a double-digit FPL points haul in just a single half of football last time out. A goal in midweek only strengthens his appeal as an attacking option for the run-in.

## Man City Attackers: Premium Options

Rayan Cherki (£6.6m) has emerged as a differential pick with impressive underlying numbers. The Frenchman trails only Bruno for chances created over his last four matches (14). Additionally, Cherki ranks top for shots (17) in that time alongside his City teammate Erling Haaland (£14.6m).

Erling Haaland is the runaway Gameweek 36 captain poll leader. The Norwegian remains the premium captaincy choice for managers seeking guaranteed returns.

## Mid-Price Forward Options

Danny Welbeck (£6.3m) has 13 Premier League goals to his name for Brighton this season. At his price point, he represents solid value among budget forwards.

Harry Wilson (£5.9m) boasts 10 goals and eight assists overall this term for Fulham. His combined goal involvement makes him an attractive budget option.

## Crystal Palace Fixture Concern

Crystal Palace may have the distraction of a Europa Conference League final days later. This could impact their league form and should be factored into decisions regarding Palace assets.

## Bruno Fernandes Record Chase

Bruno Fernandes has 21 assists in FPL, but 19 in official stats. This puts him one shy of equalling the all-time Premier League assists record. Managers should note this discrepancy when evaluating his FPL returns.
