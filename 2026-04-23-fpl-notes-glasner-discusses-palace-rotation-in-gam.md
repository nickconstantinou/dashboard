# Palace Rotation Concerns in Crucial Gameweeks 34 and 35

## Adam Wharton's Return

Adam Wharton was officially injured and not in the squad for Crystal Palace in Gameweek 33. However, the young midfielder will be back for the clash with Liverpool in Gameweek 34. Wharton is priced at £5.0m and represents an option for Fantasy Premier League managers tracking Palace's midfield situation.

## Rotation Plans for Busy Schedule

Manager Oliver Glasner has confirmed that he will rotate players to keep them fresh for both the Premier League and Conference League. When discussing his approach, Glasner stated that he will "keep always playing the freshest legs and the freshest minds" in order to achieve "the best output and outcome in the Premier League and, of course, in the Conference League."

However, Glasner has been clear that he will not sacrifice the league for European competition, stating firmly: "No, [we won't sacrifice the league for Europe]. No, we won't do this right now."

## Defenders More Stable Than Attackers

Palace's defenders have been less prone to rotation than their teammates further forward, making them potentially more reliable assets during this busy period. This suggests that attacking players may face more rotation risk in the coming fixtures.

## Gameweek 35 Fixture Congestion

Gameweek 35 presents particular challenges for Palace managers. The gameweek is sandwiched by two clashes with Shakhtar Donetsk in the Conference League semi-final. This European commitment could influence team selection and create uncertainty around Premier League minutes.

## Key Offensive Players

During a recent match, Brennan Johnson had Palace's best opportunities. The player curled narrowly wide after nodding a header spectacularly off target from eight yards out. Johnson, priced at £6.5m, has been operating as a stand-in for Sarr in the lineup.

Ismaila Sarr emerged as a 60th-minute substitute and did have the ball in the net, only for it to be chalked off for a very obvious handball call against Jean-Philippe Mateta.

Meanwhile, Konstantinos Mavropanos posed a significant set-piece threat after scoring three recent goals. He forced a superb save from Dean Henderson with a first-half header, highlighting his aerial danger from dead-ball situations.

## Looking Ahead

Palace will rekindle some interest for the run-in, with an as-yet-unannounced Double Gameweek still to come. This future double gameweek could make Palace assets more attractive, particularly given the current rotation uncertainty surrounding their attacking players.
