UCL Fantasy: Matchday 16 best captains

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

Bukayo Saka – The Standout Choice

Bukayo Saka emerges as a compelling captaincy option for Matchday 16 of the UEFA Champions League Fantasy game. At a price of £9.8m, the Arsenal winger has demonstrated his offensive capabilities in recent Premier League action, having scored a goal and provided an assist in his most recent outing. His goal and assist at the weekend highlight just how effective he can be when he's on the pitch.

A further factor working in Saka's favour concerns his involvement in Arsenal's Champions League campaign. The team were the highest-scoring side during the Champions League 'league phase', providing Saka with ample opportunity for returns. Additionally, Saka shares penalty duties for Arsenal, giving him extra avenues to points beyond open-play contributions.

Importantly for fantasy managers considering the captain's armband, Saka was withdrawn at half-time during Arsenal's recent 3-0 Premier League victory. A half-time withdrawal, with the game already won at 3-0, hinted at him being preserved for Tuesday's UCL match, suggesting he should be fresh and fully available for the Champions League fixture.

Harry Kane – Confirmed Penalty Taker

Harry Kane represents another viable captaincy candidate for Matchday 16. The Bayern Munich striker is priced at €10.9m and holds confirmed penalty taker status for his club. Unlike some other teams where spot-kick duties can be less certain, there's no ambiguity here, making Kane a reliable source of guaranteed shots on goal.

Kane's overall output this season has been remarkable, having produced 60+ attacking returns across all competitions this season. This sustained productivity suggests he offers consistent point-scoring potential regardless of the opponent.

Gabriel – Budget Defensive Option

For managers seeking budget-friendly captaincy alternatives, Gabriel presents an interesting option at €5.8m. The Brazilian defender has delivered nine goal contributions across the Premier League and Champions League this season, combining defensive clean sheet potential with unexpected attacking returns.

Matchup Considerations

When assessing potential captaincy opponents, managers should note that Atletico Madrid have managed just one clean sheet in their last 15 Champions League matches. This defensive vulnerability could inform decisions regarding which attacking players to back for Matchday 16.
