# How Top FPL Managers Navigated Gameweek 35

## Chip Strategies in Gameweek 35

Gameweek 35 saw significant chip activity among elite FPL managers. Six managers from the group known as "The Great and The Good" activated their Wildcard chips during this period. These late Wildcard activations represented dramatic moves as the season reached its crucial final stretch.

Additionally, two managers—Pras and Zophar—coordinated their Bench Boost chip activations in perfect unison, suggesting they may be revision partners sharing the same strategy.

### Bench Boost Returns

The Bench Boost chip proved valuable for several managers in Gameweek 35. Pras extracted a solid return of 20 points from his bench assets. Meanwhile, Zophar achieved a standout 30-point return from his Bench Boost, with unlikely contributions from Bournemouth defenders driving much of that total.

## Huss E's Standout Performance

Huss E delivered an impressive showing in Gameweek 35, leading all managers who played only their starting XI with 71 points—accomplishing this without deploying the Bench Boost chip. This result elevated him to 2,201st place overall.

Huss E's consistency reflects an established track record. In the previous season, he finished 41st overall, and across six seasons, he has accumulated five top-20,000 finishes. This demonstrates sustained elite-level performance within the FPL community.

## Transfer Market Activity

Several notable transfers caught attention in Gameweek 35. Mark Sutherns made the most eye-catching move by switching to Saka, which stood out as the standout transfer of the week.

Gabriel emerged as the most popular transfer target, with nearly every manager selecting him as a safe and sensible option. His widespread ownership indicated a consensus pick among top-tier managers.

Meanwhile, FPL Harry executed a move from Semenyo to Cherki, generating discussion within the FPL community about the reasoning behind that particular swap.
