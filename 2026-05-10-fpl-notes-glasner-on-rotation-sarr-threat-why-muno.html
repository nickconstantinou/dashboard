Crystal Palace FPL Notes: Rotation Decisions, Sarr's Continued Threat, and Munoz's Bonus Point

Palace's Season Context

Crystal Palace have now played their 56th game of the season, reflecting a demanding campaign across multiple competitions. Despite the fixture congestion, the team finds itself in a comfortable position. According to manager Oliver Glasner, Palace are now completely safe from the threat of relegation, allowing the squad to focus on their remaining fixtures without that particular pressure.

Rotation Approach

Glasner's rotation strategy proved more conservative than many anticipated heading into this fixture. Rather than implementing significant changes, the Palace boss made merely two alterations from the previous European match, and notably both substitutions came in attacking positions.

In terms of personnel, Brennan Johnson at £6.5m and Jorgen Strand Larsen at £5.9m were the beneficiaries of the limited rotation, earning starts over Yeremy Pino at £5.8m and Jean-Philippe Mateta at £7.6m respectively. However, Pino's omission came through illness rather than tactical selection, as Glasner confirmed the winger was sick and therefore could not participate.

Ismaila Sarr's Threat

Ismaila Sarr at £6.4m continues to demonstrate his threat in the final third, scoring his 20th goal of the season across all competitions. The forward found the net from close range at the second attempt, underlining his persistent goal-scoring danger. Beyond his goals, Sarr's output was particularly impressive with six shots total during the match—the most of any player on the pitch. This volume of attempts highlights his consistent involvement in Palace's attacking moves and makes him a persistent differential option for FPL managers considering their forward line.

Why Munoz Earned Bonus Points

Daniel Munoz at £5.9m managed to salvage some reward from what proved to be a blank for the team, banking a bonus point despite registering no direct attacking returns. The explanation for his bonus lies in his exceptional creativity from wing-back. Munoz created four chances during the match, with three of those classified as 'big' opportunities. This level of chance creation—even without a goal or assist to show for it—demonstrated enough statistical merit to earn him bonus points in FPL terms. His underlying performance metrics suggest he remains a viable budget option for managers seeking defensive coverage with attacking upside.
