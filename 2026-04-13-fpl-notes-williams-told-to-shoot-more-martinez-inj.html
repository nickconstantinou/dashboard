FPL notes: Williams told to “shoot more” + Martinez injury latest

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

Neco Williams has been told to shoot more. More significantly, he's doing exactly that.

The Nottingham Forest defender (£4.7m) equalised against Aston Villa on Sunday with a thunderous strike from outside the penalty area—one of four attempts he made on the day, the most of any player on the pitch. The effort was his 39th shot of the 2025/26 season, at least seven more than any other defender in the league. He is now taking corners too.

"The manager has been telling me to shoot outside the box more and that's what I did today," Williams said after the 1-1 draw at the City Ground. "I took a few and one went in, so I'm pleased with that."

The instruction from Vitor Pereira is notable. Williams isn't being deployed purely as a defensive outlet or a crossing threat—he's being actively encouraged to innovate from distance, and against Burnley in Gameweek 33, he offers a genuine budget route into Forest's attack. At £4.7m, with set-piece involvement and a manager explicitly telling him to shoot more, he's a differential worth serious consideration even without a double gameweek on the horizon.

Wood Back, But Building

Chris Wood's return adds another dimension to Forest's forward line. The New Zealand striker (£7.1m) made his comeback after six months out, entering the match in the 65th minute and prompting Igor Jesus (£5.8m) to shift into the No. 10 position. Pereira was unambiguous about Wood's value: "His experience, quality, scoring goals, he smells the goals in the box. His physical and mental condition. He has the ability to help us."

But the caveats matter. Wood is building sharpness after a significant layoff. His introduction against Villa was carefully managed, and while he's a proven Premier League scorer, his minutes will be monitored. For FPL purposes, he's a high-upside bench option or a punt for the run-in—not a reliable starter yet.

Rogers, However

The more complicated situation belongs to Morgan Rogers (£7.4m). The England international delivered his second assist in consecutive Gameweeks, his low cross deflecting off Forest's Murillo and looping into the net. He also clipped the bar and, from roughly eight yards out, missed a gilt-edged chance that he blazed over. The xG calculator would not have been kind.

But what should concern Fantasy managers isn't the waste—it's the minutes. Rogers was substituted on 77, a decision that left him one DefCon point short of the threshold. Unai Emery was candid afterward: "He is able to play three matches in a row in a week. He is able to respond in the level because he is doing it. Sometimes he is being clinical, or not. Sometimes as well maybe he is more tired than other matches."

Translation: Rogers is rotation fodder when Villa face a midweek fixture, even when he's performing. For managers weighing whether to trust him as a premium asset, that's a meaningful risk that doesn't show up in form guides or price tags. Emery is managing his workload deliberately, and that pattern isn't going away with three matches per week.

Callum Hudson-Odoi (£5.7m) assisted Williams' equaliser—his third assist in four Gameweeks. Elliot Anderson (£5.6m) returned from his Europa League suspension to bank defensive contribution points. Morgan Gibbs-White (£7.4m) was subdued but nearly scored when his second-half effort was saved.

Forest at home to Burnley next. The shots, the corners, and the manager's blessing are all pointing in Williams' direction. Rogers, for all his talent, remains a player whose manager will pull him when the fixture density demands it.
