The Top-Scoring FPL XI of 2026 So Far: A Complete Breakdown

The Full Story

The 2026 Fantasy Premier League season has delivered a dramatic shakeup at the top of the points table, with last year's elite performers—Erling Haaland, Mohamed Salah, and Antoine Semenyo—entirely absent from the current top-scoring XI. As of March 31, 2026, the FPL landscape has been transformed by budget-friendly picks and defensive powerhouses who are delivering exceptional value.

Caoimhin Kelleher (£4.8m) has emerged as the standout goalkeeper option, accumulating 54 points through a combination of five clean sheets, five save points, and six bonus points across 12 matches. The Irish shot-stopper, who plays for Brentford, has been particularly impressive since the turn of the year, including a memorable Panenka penalty save against Enzo Le Fee in January. Kelleher even missed Gameweek 28 due to the birth of his child before returning to keep another clean sheet in a goalless draw with Bournemouth and adding another shutout at Leeds United. His three price rises in 2026 haven't deterred managers from investing, and he continues to offer tremendous value at his price point.

Among defenders, Arsenal's Gabriel Magalhaes (£7.2m) has been virtually untouchable, leading all FPL defenders with 76 points—seven more than any other backline option. The Brazilian has been a rock for Arsenal, contributing six clean sheets, 12 DefCon points, and nine bonus points in just 12 matches. His attacking output has been remarkably consistent, scoring with his left foot at Vitality Stadium and Molineux while also providing an assist against Chelsea. Liverpool's Virgil van Dijk (£6.3m) sits second among defenders with 69 points despite Liverpool's struggles, recording four clean sheets, six DefCon points, five bonus points, and three goals from set plays in 13 matches. The Dutch defender even provided the assist for Alexis Mac Allister's late winner at the City Ground in February.

The bargain picks come from Bournemouth, where James Hill (£4.2m) has been the definition of consistency, blanking in only three of his 12 starts while accumulating 60 points. Hill leads all defenders with 146 DefCons in 2026 and has provided three assists, primarily through his excellent distribution. His team-mate Adrien Truffert (£4.7m) rounds out the top XI with 57 points, contributing two assists, four clean sheets, 10 DefCon points, and five bonus points across 12 appearances.

Why This Matters

For the millions of FPL managers worldwide, this analysis represents crucial intelligence for the final stretch of the 2025/26 season. The data demonstrates that premium forwards—long considered essential to any competitive squad—are no longer mandatory, with defensive assets from mid-table clubs offering superior returns per million spent. This trend has profound implications for squad construction strategies, as managers who invested heavily in Haaland (£14.4m) or Salah (£14.0m) have watched budget defenders accumulate points at nearly the same rate while freeing up funds for value picks elsewhere.

The rise of Brentford's Kelleher and Bournemouth's defensive duo of Hill and Truffert also reflects a broader tactical shift in the Premier League itself, where defensive organization and set-piece proficiency are generating more consistent returns than ever before. For FPL managers, this means the traditional wisdom of "never captain a defender" is increasingly outdated, as players like Gabriel and van Dijk are delivering attacking contributions alongside their defensive solidity.

Furthermore, the fact that Liverpool has won only four of their last 13 Premier League matches yet still features prominently in the top-scoring XI through van Dijk highlights the importance of individual player performance over team results—a critical lesson for FPL strategists who may have overlooked Liverpool assets based on the Reds' inconsistent league form.
