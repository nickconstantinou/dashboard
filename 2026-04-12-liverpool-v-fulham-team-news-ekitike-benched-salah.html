Slot's Champions League Calculus Cost Gravenberch His Place as Salah Returns for Liverpool

Arne Slot rotated five players for Saturday's Premier League showdown with Fulham, the most notable casualty being Ryan Gravenberch—whose demotion to the bench ended a streak stretching back to May 2024.

The Dutch midfielder's removal from Liverpool's starting XI is the headline teamsheet surprise, but it arrives by design rather than discipline. Slot's side face Paris Saint-Germain in the second leg of their Champions League quarter-final on Tuesday, and the Reds boss made clear on Friday that workload management had become his primary concern. "Gomez was one of the players he was worried about, regarding the quick turnaround between games," the source material notes—and that anxiety extended across his squad.

Gravenberch had started every Premier League match since his benching last May. Saturday marks the end of that run.

Mohammed Salah, by contrast, benefits directly from the rotation. The Egyptian remained unused in Wednesday's first-leg defeat in Paris, and Slot has restored him to the line-up for the 17:30 BST kick-off at Anfield. His presence alongside Curtis Jones, Cody Gakpo, and the debutant Rio Ngumoha gives Liverpool firepower even amid the reshuffle.

Also returning is Andrew Robertson—one of the few certainties Slot can offer this fanbase before the left-back departs Anfield at season's end. His inclusion, alongside the starts for Jones and Gakpo, suggests Slot has calibrated his selection not for preservation but for competitiveness against a Fulham side that has won three of its last five league matches.

Hugo Ekitike's benching registers less as a shock and more as a confirmation of where the young forward sits in this squad hierarchy. Slot's words Friday offered the warning; Saturday's teamsheet delivered the verdict.

Fulham Stay the Course

If Liverpool's selection story is complicated, Fulham's is almost serene. The Cottagers are unchanged from the XI that beat Burnley three weeks ago. Rodrigo Muniz keeps his place ahead of Raul Jimenez. No injuries, no rotation, no drama—just consistency from a side sitting comfortably enough in mid-table to resist temptation.

That contrast in squad management—racking your lineup against European congestion versus fielding the same eleven for stability—may say more about where both clubs find themselves than any scoreline will.

Liverpool XI: Mamardashvili, Frimpong, Konate, van Dijk, Robertson, Szoboszlai, Jones, Salah, Wirtz, Ngumoha, Gakpo. *Subs:* Woodman, Gomez, Kerkez, Isak, Mac Allister, Chiesa, Ekitike, Gravenberch, Nyoni.

Fulham XI: Leno, Castagne, Andersen, Bassey, Robinson, Iwobi, Berge, Wilson, King, Bobb, Rodrigo Muniz. *Subs:* Lecomte, Jimenez, Cairney, Cuenca, Chukwueze, Lukic, Sessegnon, Diop, Smith Rowe.
