What Glasner said about Palace’s Gameweek 36 clash vs Everton

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

Conference League Progress Sets Up Busy Fixture Run

Crystal Palace secured their place in the UEFA Conference League final on Thursday following a 5-2 aggregate win over Shakhtar Donetsk. This victory sets up a crucial period for Oliver Glasner's side, with Palace one of two clubs playing twice in Gameweek 36, alongside Manchester City.

The Eagles' progression to the final marks a significant milestone for the club, but it also presents immediate challenges as they navigate a packed fixture schedule during the run-in.

Lessons From the Shakhtar First Leg

Glasner reflected on past experience when discussing the approach to their upcoming fixtures. Following the first leg win against Shakhtar Donetsk, Palace's players trained the next day, and the manager felt the squad was not in good shape for their subsequent Premier League match at Bournemouth. This experience shaped his thinking moving forward.

"You could feel the players were tired," Glasner noted, drawing from that previous instance as a reference point for managing workload during this busy period.

Players Given Recovery Time

In contrast to the first leg scenario, Palace played their 55th game of the season against Shakhtar on Thursday, and nobody looked fatigued according to Glasner. The players also had a night out in London after the match, which the manager acknowledged as part of squad management.

To help maintain freshness, Glasner gave Palace players a day off immediately following the Shakhtar victory. "Yes, I've just given them [the day] off tomorrow. One day," the manager confirmed.

Approach to the Everton Fixture

Despite the intensity of recent fixtures and with key players having been rested in three of the last four Premier League matches, Glasner was clear about his intentions for the Everton game. The Palace boss stated firmly that his side will not wave the white flag against Everton.

This signals that while rotation has been part of the strategy in recent league matches, the manager expects a competitive performance against the Toffees.

Fixture Outlook for Gameweek 36

Palace's double gameweek status makes them an attractive option for Fantasy Premier League managers, though the busy schedule raises questions about player availability and freshness. The decision to rest key players in recent league matches suggests Glasner is managing resources carefully across competitions.

More quotes from Glasner are expected on Friday, which may provide further clarity on team selection and his thinking heading into the double gameweek.
