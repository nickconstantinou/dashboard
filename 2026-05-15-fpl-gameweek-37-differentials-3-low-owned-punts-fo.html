FPL Gameweek 37 Differentials: Three Low-Owned Punts for the Run-In

With the season approaching its climax, Fantasy Premier League managers are seeking value in less-owned players who can deliver returns during the run-in. Here are three differential options ahead of Gameweek 37.

---

Keane Lewis-Potter – Brentford (£4.8m)

Keane Lewis-Potter has emerged as an intriguing budget option for the final stretch of the season. Since returning to Brentford's starting XI in Gameweek 30, he has supplied two assists in seven matches. His underlying numbers are particularly encouraging, as he ranks second among defenders for expected goal involvement (xGI) since that point.

The Bees have also been defensively solid, with four clean sheets since the start of March – the second-most of any team behind Arsenal. This defensive solidity provides a reassuring floor for attacking assets like Lewis-Potter.

Priced at just £4.8m, Lewis-Potter offers a budget-friendly route into Brentford's attack during a period where many managers are looking to maximize value across their squad.

---

Harvey Barnes – Newcastle United (£6.1m)

Harvey Barnes has been in excellent form, netting in consecutive Gameweeks. His season total stands at 16 goals across all competitions in 2025/26. The Newcastle winger is averaging a shot every 35.9 minutes in 2025/26 – the fastest rate of any Newcastle player.

Manager Eddie Howe has publicly backed Barnes, stating he is a good player with goals in him who took his goal well and has a great chance to start the next game. This endorsement suggests Barnes is likely to feature prominently in Newcastle's remaining fixtures.

At £6.1m, Barnes represents a mid-price option who could deliver significant returns during the run-in given his current form and shot volume.

---

Kiernan Dewsbury-Hall – Everton (£5.3m)

Everton's Kiernan Dewsbury-Hall has attracted significant attention from the Fantasy community, being the fifth-most transferred-in player this Gameweek. At £5.3m, he represents a popular budget option as managers look to load their squads with value.

Everton's run of fixtures has made them an attractive side to target, and Dewsbury-Hall's involvement in the team makes him a viable differential for managers looking to differentiate their squads during the crucial closing stages.

---

Additional Consideration: Beto – Everton

While not included in the primary three picks, Everton's Beto has been in prolific form with six goals in his last eight matches – the most of any forward from Gameweek 28 onwards. He has received eight Opta 'big chances' during this period and averaged a shot every 31.9 minutes. Managers seeking a budget forward option may want to consider Beto alongside the above selections.
