FPL Notes: Doku's Continued Form, Cherki Hooked, and the Missing Semenyo Assist

Manchester City's 3-0 Victory Over Brentford

Manchester City kicked off their Gameweek 36 double-header with a 3-0 win over Brentford. The match saw notable performances from several FPL assets, along with tactical decisions that affected player returns.

Jeremy Doku's Consistent Returns

Jeremy Doku (£6.5m) has maintained strong form since returning from injury. The winger now has five attacking returns in as many starts since returning from injury. This consistent output makes him a viable budget option for FPL managers looking for attacking value.

Rayan Cherki's Early Substitution

Rayan Cherki (£6.6m), who was the most-bought player of the Gameweek, did not even make the hour-mark. Both Cherki and another player came off after 59 minutes. Pep Guardiola wanted more natural goalscorers in the box, explaining the substitution. Brentford had largely been successful at frustrating City, with the score 0-0 at the time of Cherki's departure. This early hook may concern managers who expected the midfielder to play a full 90 minutes.

Why Haaland Didn't Record an Assist

Erling Haaland (£14.7m) was involved in City's goals but did not receive an assist for one particular chance. Opta recorded Haaland as having two bites at the cherry during one sequence, with the first one saved. Haaland's initial shot was saved by Caoimhin Kelleher before he converted the rebound. Since his second attempt was a goal from his own saved shot, no assist was awarded to any player.

Semenyo's 90-Minute Outing

Antoine Semenyo (£8.1m) lasted 90 minutes in this fixture, after earlier substitutions in the previous two league games. This full appearance is encouraging for managers who were worried about his fitness or rotation risk, though this claim appears to reference a different context or team than the main Manchester City fixture.

Brentford's Off-Day

Brentford had a difficult attacking performance, registering only four shots across the game. This explains why several Brentford attackers failed to deliver attacking returns despite potentially being on the pitch for reasonable minutes.
