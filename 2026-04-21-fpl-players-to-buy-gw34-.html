FPL Players to Buy for Gameweek 34: Your Ultimate Guide to the Blank Gameweek

Fantasy Premier League managers face a pivotal moment in the season as Gameweek 34 arrives with its unique challenges. With several top teams enjoying blank gameweeks—meaning Manchester City, Bournemouth, Brighton, Leeds United, Chelsea, and Burnley all sit this round out—managers must navigate a reduced fixture list of just seven matches while maximizing their points potential. Whether you're deploying a free hit chip, wildcard, or simply looking to optimize your squad with free transfers, this Gameweek presents both risks and opportunities that could make or break your season.

The matches available this week include Sunderland versus Nottingham Forest, Fulham against Aston Villa, Liverpool hosting Crystal Palace, West Ham versus Everton, Wolves against Tottenham, Arsenal versus Newcastle, and Manchester United battling Brentford. Understanding which players offer the best projected points across these fixtures is essential for making smart transfer decisions, and the analysis that follows breaks down the optimal choices at every position—from goalkeeper to striker.

Goalkeeper Strategy: Arsenal and Liverpool Lead the Way

The analysis begins with goalkeeper selection, where Arsenal's matchup against Newcastle United stands out as the most favorable fixture for clean sheet potential. The presenter identifies Rya (presumably referring to Arsenal's goalkeeper) as leading the keeper table with 3.8 projected points, making him the likely most popular choice among free hit players. For managers who have the flexibility to add him with a free transfer and aren't using a chip, Rya represents a fantastic option, particularly given Arsenal's strong defensive record and the team's high number of projected clean sheets, which rank alongside Liverpool as the best in the league for this fixture.

A notable concern emerged regarding Liverpool's goalkeeper situation. Alisson was stretchered off during Liverpool's win over Everton, which saw third-choice goalkeeper Woodman brought on as a substitute. The presenter strongly advises against attempting to be clever by picking Woodman on a free hit, despite his cheap price tag. Instead, managers seeking Liverpool exposure should target Van Dijk in defense, as Woodman's participation remains uncertain and the situation could resolve quickly if Alisson's injury proves to be a minor concern. It appears that Alisson suffered what was described as a "bad cut," suggesting he might recover in time for the next match.

An intriguing alternative keeper emerges in Sunderland's Roffsy, who has been a "force in home games this season" and ranks as a strong alternative keeper on a free hit with 3.5 projected points—just 0.3 behind Rya. The presenter also mentions Kinsky (Tottenham's goalkeeper) as a worthwhile backup or even starterable option this week if Vicario remains injured, noting that for 3.9 million, he offers reasonable value with a projected 3.7 points if guaranteed a start. This makes him particularly useful for managers in a position where they lack a keeper in their squad and aren't using a chip.

Defender Analysis: Porro's Free Hit Appeal and Premium Options

Moving to defenders, the presenter highlights Pedro Porro as the standout Tottenham defender worth considering, particularly for free hit strategies. Porro recorded a goal and bonus points in the last match, resulting in a 10-pointer—his joint biggest score of the season. The analysis passes the "eye test" as well, with Porro demonstrating late runs into the box, taking corners on the left side, and even some free kicks. Over the last six gameweeks, Porro has registered eight shots, ranking third most among all defenders. The presenter characterizes him as "the perfect free hit pick" given the favorable fixture against Wolves, though realistic expectations suggest he probably ends up with another two-pointer if he doesn't connect on a big chance.

The reasoning here centers on the concept that a free hit punts should target players with ceiling potential in isolated fixtures, and Porro fits that profile perfectly. The presenter has personally locked Porro into his free hit draft and explicitly notes that managers shouldn't want to hold him beyond this gameweek, making this a pure one-week play rather than a long-term investment. Those interested in seeing the presenter's complete transfer plans can reference the transfer plans video from Sunday evening, which includes his current free hit team selection.

Van Dijk and Gabriel occupy the top spots jointly this week among defenders, with Taroski from Everton emerging as a compelling option who ranks fourth best among defenders for the final five gameweeks with 19.8 projected points. Taroski has averaged 10.31 defcons per 90 this season, ranking third best among defenders after Sessie and Lacra. The presenter highlights an advanced strategy for Taroski owners: simply bench him against Manchester City, because whoever you start instead that week will obviously have a higher ceiling than Taroski facing City. This kind of strategic benching can squeeze even more points out of owning Taroski while avoiding his toughest fixture.

For managers needing a cheap defender both this week and for the remainder of the season, Anderson from Fulham offers an alternative to Van Dijk at 3.9 projected points this week. He narrowly misses the main table but projects 18.4 points for the final five gameweeks with 10.22 defcons per 90, making him a solid budget option.

Midfield Priorities: Bruno Fernandes Steals the Show

The presenter positions Bruno Fernandes as the clear favorite for captaincy this week, topping all players with 6.6 projected points on review. For managers who don't already own Bruno, he represents a priority buy for the final five gameweeks. The reasoning is straightforward: his offensive contributions, set piece duties, and consistent involvement in Manchester United's attacks make him the highest-ceiling midfielder available this specific gameweek.

The second-ranked midfielder—and one the presenter acknowledges will receive "mixed reception in the comments"—is Mohamed Salah with 6.0 projected points. The presenter is clear that rushing to buy Salah with a free transfer wouldn't be advisable, but on a free hit, he presents a "fun option." The emotional angle is worth noting: this could represent the final time managers ever own Salah in their teams, and while "sentiment doesn't get you any extra points in FPL," a potential fairy tale ending if he scores in the last-ever gameweek of owning him adds an element of enjoyment to the game. FPL is, after all, meant to be fun, and sentimental picks have their place in the experience.

Zasai emerges as another strong midfielder option with 9.9 defcons per 90 and a "one in three games" hit rate for defcons. He also contributes corners and free kicks, adding set piece value to his underlying statistics. Despite playing right back in the last match, that positional change "didn't stop him coming away with a return," assisting Van Dijk's late winner from a corner. The presenter expresses a preference for Zasai over Vers, though both rank as strong options and appear dead even on review for this gameweek.

The analysis then shifts to Newcastle, who have conceded 12.98 expected goals over the last six gameweeks—the worst record in the league. This defensive vulnerability makes Declan Rice an excellent pick this week with either a free transfer or on a free hit or wildcard. Wilson from Newcastle also enters the conversation, particularly given that he's likely on penalties while Gimenez is off the pitch. The presenter explains that with Gimenez unavailable, there's "a very strong chance Wilson is on penalties," and on top of that, he's got set piece duty, making him "a fantastic option whether on the chip or not this week."

Kuna has emerged as the favorite second Manchester United midfielder, boasting two goals, two assists, and two blanks in his last six appearances. The presenter notes there's "no harm in doubling up" if managers want both Bruno and Kuna. An eye-opening comparison reveals that Casemiro—despite going "under the radar all season"—has accumulated eight goals and four assists (12 goal involvements), which is "exactly the same as Kuna's output." Casemiro is averaging 11.97 defcons per 90 with a 40% hit rate, and he's right on the cusp of finishing in the team of the season. However, the presenter suggests Kuna will likely be the "highest ceiling pick" going forward, making him the preferred second United midfielder despite Casemiro's impressive statistical output.

Honorable midfielder mentions include Gibbs White, who scored 4.6 points off the back of a 20-point haul including a hat-trick against Wolves, and now faces Sunderland next. Gakpo (4.3 points), Anderson from Forest (4.2), Shavy Simmons (4.2), and Rogers (4.1) round out the additional options.

Forward Analysis: Limited Options but Some Gems

The presenter admits the forwards list "isn't the most exciting" this week, with options limited to EAC, Isac Bowen, Salanki, Thiago Watkins, and Brobby. EAC—despite being the top-ranked forward this week, just barely ahead of Bowen—faces fitness concerns, as he's "not fit enough to play 90 still, and he's not long back after months out." This is a significant consideration, as managers need to weigh whether a player who might be substituted early or rested mid-match can deliver adequate value. However, the limited nature of forward options this week means managers using free transfers or wildcards may have no choice but to select from this group.

Watkins and Salanki both represent viable options, with Salanki particularly interesting given Wolves' defensive record. Wolves have registered just 3.79 expected goals over the last six gameweeks, ranking "comfortably worst in the league" over that period, which makes Salanki a potential free hit pick facing such a favorable fixture. The presenter notes that Salanki has played four consecutive 90-minute matches, suggesting he's fully match-fit and primed for this encounter. Thiago Watkins, meanwhile, has scored 21 goals this season and faces Manchester United away—a match where opposition defenders Martinez and Delick remain injured and Yuir will be back after serving a one-match suspension. This creates a slightly more favorable attacking environment for Watkins despite the tough opponent.

The presenter expresses particular enthusiasm for targeting Wolves this week, as their recent defensive record makes them a team to exploit across all positions. Even Tottenham defenders could be worth considering against Wolves, given how poor their recent form has been defensively.

The presenter concludes by revealing the optimal three-team composition based on projected points from the team of the week page in their app. The recommended lineup features Rya in goal, with defenders Dano, Gabrielle, Porro, and Van Dijk forming the backline, midfielders Bruno, Salah, Rice, and Zasai, and forwards Soslay, Wilson, and Bowen in a 4-5-1 formation. The reasoning is that managers should prefer having a striker rather than an extra defender when benching players, since strikers offer higher scoring potential when they do blank. Alternatively, managers could swap out a budget defender like Dano for Thiago, Salanki, or Watkins if budget permits, as these premium forwards offer greater ceiling potential.

The team construction is always limited to 100 million in budget, though managers might be able to squeeze additional projected points depending on their specific team value and existing holdings. The presenter acknowledges that many players might already be owned from the team of the week recommendations, so some substitutions may be necessary based on individual squad composition.

Key Strategic Takeaways

The first critical insight is that blank gameweeks require strategic thinking about chip usage and transfer timing. Managers not using chips should prioritize free transfers that make sense beyond just this single gameweek, while free hit and wildcard players can afford to take more speculative one-week punts on high-ceiling players in favorable fixtures. This distinction fundamentally shapes player selection strategy.

The second takeaway concerns the importance of fixture-vs-form analysis. Wolves represent the premier target this week given their 3.79 xG over six gameweeks—the worst in the league—making Tottenham attackers, including Porro, excellent free hit selections. Conversely, Arsenal and Liverpool defense should be prioritized for clean sheet potential, while Newcastle's defensive vulnerabilities make Rice and Wilson strong midfield options.

The third insight relates to set piece takers and penalty specialists. The presenter emphasizes that "it's never a bad idea to chuck a penalty taker on a free hit" because even an otherwise average performance can become a 10-pointer if someone wins a penalty and he scores it. Wilson exemplifies this logic given his penalty likelihood while Gimenez is absent.

The fourth takeaway involves strategic benching of premium assets against tough opponents. Taroski owners should bench him against Manchester City, replacing him with any other startable defender for that fixture, thereby maximizing points while avoiding a likely blank against one of the league's best attacks.

The fifth and final insight concerns the emotional dimension of FPL. While purely sentimental picks rarely justify themselves mathematically, the presenter acknowledges that owning Salah for his potential final FPL appearance could provide intangible enjoyment that enhances the overall experience. This balance between optimal points-maximization and enjoying the game represents a personal choice each manager must make.
