European Competition Results and Their FPL Implications

The European competitions have wrapped up, and the results paint a clear picture for FPL managers considering which players to target. Liverpool and Arsenal both advanced in the UEFA Champions League, maintaining their status as priority targets for managers. Meanwhile, Manchester City, Chelsea, Newcastle, and Tottenham have all been eliminated from European competition, which fundamentally changes their fixture schedules and makes them more attractive FPL options going forward.

Tottenham deserves special mention as the only English team to actually win their second leg apart from Liverpool and Arsenal. The atmosphere at their stadium was electric, with the crowd getting behind the team in a more attacking system that Zophar believes will continue. Unfortunately for FPL managers, Solanke was left out of the squad due to injury while Richarlison served a suspension, but both Muani and Mateta had decent performances. This rotation strategy is particularly relevant for FPL: main players like Neco Williams, Murillo, Gibbs-White, Aina, Anderson, Sangare, and Milenkovic all came on as substitutes around the 60th and 90th minute marks, meaning they accumulated minimal minutes. Crystal Palace's narrow advancement clears up the short-term landscape for FPL managers, as their fixture cannot be rearranged into Gameweek 32 as some had feared.

Fixture Schedule Analysis

Understanding the fixture computer becomes essential for long-term FPL strategy. The most likely scenario, based on analysis from the FPL Spaceman and other sources, projects doubles for Brighton, Chelsea, Arsenal, Newcastle, Burnley, and Manchester City in Gameweek 33, which would create blanks for those teams in Gameweek 34. The Palace-City double would then drop into Gameweek 36 instead.

This analysis comes with caveats. Manchester City, Chelsea, and Newcastle face particular uncertainty because their fixtures could potentially shift to midweek slots rather than the traditional weekend dates. If they receive midweek TV fixtures, they might not face a blank this week at all, and they might not even receive a double in the expected gameweek. The Premier League will announce the full TV schedule for the run-in after the FA Cup round around April 7th, which should clarify many of these uncertainties.

Liverpool presents an interesting case given their Champions League draw. They'll face a demanding six-day turnaround, playing against Galatasaray on Wednesday, then Fulham on Saturday, and another Champions League match against Galatasaray on Tuesday. Their schedule reads Manchester City, PSG, PSG, Fulham, Fulham, PSG in approximately 10 days—a grueling run that will inevitably lead to rotation in the Fulham fixture. For this reason, despite Liverpool's strong run of fixtures, Zophar is hesitant to target their players.

West Ham represents a interesting case marked with a star in Zophar's analysis. Their fixture is expected to remain in Gameweek 34 because they have a Monday night game in Gameweek 33, and TV companies typically don't like moving Monday night fixtures. However, there remains a possibility that West Ham could have the double in Gameweek 33 and a blank in 34, making them a "maybe blank" according to Zophar's terminology.

For teams like Sunderland, Newcastle, Tottenham, and Forest, both home games in Gameweeks 32 and 34 make them worthwhile keeping if you're following this particular strategy. Manchester United, with fixtures in 32 and 34 that are quite favorable plus a winnable home fixture against Bournemouth, represents a team of significant interest.

Transfer Strategy and Current Squad Status

Zophar has already executed one key transfer: selling Rice for Wilson. This gives him two free transfers to work with and approximately 2.3 million in the bank. However, he's not planning to use both transfers this Gameweek. Instead, he wants to preserve at least one free transfer for several important reasons.

The upcoming international break will feature playoffs for various countries, meaning strong players from strong teams will be fielded extensively. With numerous Champions League matches still to be played during this period, the risk of injuries is substantial. Turning up after the international break with multiple injuries and zero free transfers would be a disastrous position for any FPL manager.

His strategy, as it stands, involves using a Free Hit in Gameweek 33 to maximize the double gameweek while maintaining a solid structure in Gameweeks 32 and 34 with his regular squad. He's identified three primary teams to target for transfers: Tottenham, Fulham, and Manchester United. Liverpool could have been on the table, but the expected rotation in their Fulham fixture makes them less attractive.

West Ham also represents a potential target, but Zophar prefers to wait until Gameweek 32 for clarity on where that Gameweek 34 fixture will land. Additionally, West Ham isn't the greatest entry point this week, so delaying that move slightly makes sense.
