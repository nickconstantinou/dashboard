The Full Story

With the Premier League entering its decisive phase, Fantasy Premier League managers across the globe are scrambling to maximize their points output in the final seven Gameweeks of the 2025-26 season. Five-time top 1k finisher Tom Freeman has outlined five key strategies to help managers make significant rank improvements during this critical period, with a particular focus on exploiting the fixtures and form of Brighton and Hov

The centerpiece of Freeman's analysis centers on a recommended "Gameweek 32 Wildcard/Double Gameweek 33 Bench Boost/Blank Gameweek 34 Free Hit" strategy—a common approach among serious FPL managers that involves using multiple chips strategically across the season's climax. Brighton's fixtures make them particularly attractive: they visit Burnley in Gameweek 32, then potentially "double" against Tottenham Hotspur and Chelsea, before facing Wolverhampton Wanderers at home in Gameweek 36 and traveling to goal-shy Leeds United in Gameweek 37.

Rather than pursuing the obvious route of targeting Brighton's most-bought player Danny Welbeck (£6.2m), Freeman advises managers to invest heavily in Brighton's backline. Manager Fabian Hurzeler's side has been exceptional defensively, recording two clean sheets and conceding just four goals in their last six Premier League matches. More tellingly, they currently lead the league for expected goals conceded (xGC) during that same six-match span, suggesting their defensive solidity is backed by underlying data rather than mere luck.

Why This Matters

For the millions of FPL managers worldwide, the difference between a respectable finish and a truly exceptional one often comes down to the final seven Gameweeks. With most managers having exhausted their chips earlier in the season, those who strategically plan their deployments now can gain enormous ground on rivals. The recommendations from Freeman, whose five top-1k finishes validate his expertise, carry particular weight in the community.

The focus on Brighton's defense rather than attack highlights a crucial FPL principle: value often lies in overlooked assets. While Welbeck has attracted significant ownership due to his goal-scoring returns, his £6.2m price tag may not offer the same differential advantage as budget defensive options who can deliver clean sheet points, bonus points, and attacking contributions at a fraction of the cost. This approach allows managers to allocate funds elsewhere—potentially to premium forwards or midfielders who deliver explosive hauls.

    Context & Background

    Brighton's remarkable turnaround under Fabian Hurzeler has made them a fascinating FPL asset class this season. The 31-year-old German manager, one of the youngest permanent managers in Premier League history, has implemented a high-pressing, possession-based system that has translated into both clean sheets and creative opportunities for his players.

    Burnley's vulnerabilities add further context. They rank 19th for crosses conceded from their right flank in the last six matches—a factor that could prove particularly relevant for Kadioglu, who has made the left-back position his own at the Amex Stadium and contributes significantly to Brighton's attacking output.

    The truncated second strategy apparently discusses Brighton No. 10 Jack Hinshelwood (£5.1m) as another low-owned option, suggesting the article continues to advocate for Brighton assets across positions rather than concentrating investment in one area.

    What's Next

    Managers should monitor Brighton's Gameweek 32 performance against Burnley as a litmus test for the defensive recommendations. If clean sheet potential and Kadioglu's attacking contributions materialize, the differential value of these budget assets could prove transformative for managers willing to take the calculated risk.

    The Double Gameweek 33—where Brighton face both Tottenham and Chelsea within a tight timeframe—presents the most significant opportunity and risk. Danny Welbeck's potential rotation concerns (he may not feature in both matches due to the tight turnaround) underscore why defensive assets may provide more reliable returns during congested fixture periods.

    Finally, managers should watch for any further team news updates, particularly regarding Dunk's return from suspension and any injuries to key Brighton players, as these factors could dramatically shift the value proposition of Freeman's recommended strategy.
