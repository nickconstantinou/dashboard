Why FPL Gameweek 32 Wildcard Plans Should Wait: A Comprehensive Analysis

As the Fantasy Premier League season enters its decisive final stretch, managers face one of the most consequential decision points of the entire campaign. With Gameweek 31 freshly concluded and a three-week gap before the next deadline, now is precisely the wrong moment to commit to major chip strategies—despite the temptation many managers feel to lock in their wildcard plans immediately. This is the central argument from FP Harry, one of FPL's most respected content creators, whose latest video challenges conventional wisdom about when and how to deploy your remaining chips as the season hurtles toward its conclusion.

The timing of this video is crucial. We're currently in an international break, followed by an FA Cup weekend, with Gameweek 32 still three weeks away. This extended gap, while seemingly boring for eager managers, actually represents the most important two-week period where doing nothing with your FPL team could prove far more valuable than hasty action. The FA Cup results, which will determine which teams receive blank or double gameweeks in the closing fixtures, have the potential to completely transform your chip strategy—and acting before those results are known could leave you significantly disadvantaged.

FP Harry brings considerable credibility to this discussion. Having climbed from 167,000 to 36,000 in overall rank over the past four gameweeks—achieving four consecutive green arrows—his recent trajectory demonstrates that his strategic approach is working. He scored 65 points in Gameweek 31, a blank gameweek where eleven of his players were inactive, yet still managed to move up the rankings. With five free transfers banked, his wildcard and both the free hit and bench boost chips still available, Harry finds himself in a position of relative strength that many managers would envy—and he's keen to share why patience, not impulse, should guide decisions in this critical window.

The FA Cup Variable: Why You Must Wait

The entire strategic landscape of the FPL season's final seven gameweeks hinges on what happens during the upcoming FA Cup weekend. This isn't hyperbole—the results of these matches will determine which teams face blank gameweeks and which receive double fixtures, directly impacting the value of every player on your radar. Yet despite this enormous significance, many managers are already committed to wildcarding in Gameweek 32 without knowing what those FA Cup results will be.

The sequence matters enormously here. First comes the international break, which means no Premier League action but plenty of opportunity for player injuries or fatigue to emerge. Then arrives the FA Cup weekend, featuring several high-stakes ties that could dramatically reshape the fixture calendar. Only after those results are confirmed will we receive announcements from the Premier League regarding where fixtures have moved and which teams will benefit from doubles in Gameweeks 33 and 34. Harry emphasizes that the ideal approach is to "delete the app, log yourself out, come back once the FA Cup is out of the way" rather than making rash decisions during this uncertain period.

The complexity of predicting FA Cup outcomes extends beyond simply guessing winners. Even when the likely victors are clear—such as Chelsea hosting Port Vale or Arsenal traveling to Southampton—knowing exactly where their postponed fixtures will be rescheduled remains genuinely uncertain. Teams that win their FA Cup matches could have their Gameweek 34 fixtures moved to the midweek before (creating a double in Gameweek 33) or pushed to the midweek after (leaving them with a single fixture in Gameweek 34). This distinction matters enormously for chip strategy, yet it often can't be confirmed until well after the FA Cup results are known.

The Matchups That Could Reshape Everything

While some FA Cup fixtures appear straightforward on paper, FP Harry identifies several ties that introduce genuine uncertainty into the planning process. The Manchester City and Liverpool encounter represents the most obvious example—both Premier League powerhouses capable of eliminating each other, though a City victory feels slightly more probable given their home advantage. More intriguingly, the West Ham versus Leeds fixture carries similar unpredictability, with both clubs possessing the quality to progress while also being capable of suffering cup exits that would reverberate through FPL strategy.

Chelsea's situation deserves particular attention given their recent struggles. Despite their poor form—Harry notes that "it does not feel a good time to be a Chelsea fan"—the fixture against Port Vale appears winnable on paper. However, the emotional unpredictability of FA Cup football means that even struggling Premier League sides can stumble against lower-league opponents. If Chelsea were somehow eliminated, it would "completely ruin the bench boost" strategy for managers who had built their wildcard around acquiring multiple Chelsea assets for a Gameweek 33 double.

The same logic applies to Arsenal. Despite being overwhelming favorites against Southampton, an upset would fundamentally alter chip strategies across the FPL community. Harry explains that if both Chelsea and Arsenal were knocked out, managers "could save the wild card or just use transfers" in ways that would look "completely different for the remainder of the season." The domino effects extend beyond these two clubs—Newcastle's potential involvement in FA Cup complications adds another layer of complexity to planning.

Even the teams guaranteed to win their ties don't necessarily simplify matters. Harry notes that Chelsea beating Port Vale "isn't an exact science that they will double in gameweek 33, their fixture could move from gameweek 34 just into the midweek after and they would still have a single gameweek in gameweek 34." This subtle distinction highlights why waiting for official announcements rather than presuming based on past patterns remains essential.

Managers building around these teams' potential doubles would be better served waiting to see results before committing. Personal Rank Position Should Influence Decision Timing: Harry's comfortable position—five points above safety, climbing rank—allows him the luxury of patience. Managers in different circumstances might need to prioritize differently. Understanding your specific buffer and trajectory helps determine whether waiting for maximum information or acting quickly offers better expected value.
