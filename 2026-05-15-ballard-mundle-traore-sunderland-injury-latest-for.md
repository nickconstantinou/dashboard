# Sunderland Injury Latest for FPL Gameweek 37

## Romaine Mundle

Romaine Mundle's Fantasy Premier League season has come to an end. The Sunderland midfielder, priced at £4.9m, suffered a hamstring injury that has ruled him out for the remainder of the campaign. Mundle had been a popular budget option for FPL managers throughout the season, but will play no further part in Gameweek 37 or beyond.

## Dan Ballard

Dan Ballard remains unavailable for selection. The Sunderland defender continues to serve a three-match ban following his red card in Gameweek 35. Managers holding Ballard in their FPL squads should plan accordingly, as the defender will not be available until after this suspension is complete.

## Bertrand Traore

Bertrand Traore presents a more encouraging picture ahead of Gameweek 37. The winger returned to training last week after recovering from a knee injury. However, he was overlooked for the matchday squad in Gameweek 36.

Sunderland manager Regis Le Bris provided insight into Traore's situation, noting that the injury was a re-injury rather than a recurring problem. "For him, with his knee, it was totally new," Le Bris explained, suggesting the nature of the injury differed from previous issues. "It was a re-injury for Bertrand, so probably I needed more time to be comfortable with his selection."

Despite the setback, Traore's availability appears to be improving. Le Bris indicated that Traore's situation this week was much better than previous weeks, describing the player's condition as "more consistent" and "much, much better" than earlier in his recovery.

Le Bris also noted that Traore's recovery trajectory differed from that of teammates Jocelin and Nilson, who had returned to action in the preceding two gameweeks. Looking ahead to the match at the Hill Dickinson Stadium, Le Bris suggested Traore could be back in the matchday squad for Gameweek 37.

## Squad Status

Beyond these individual cases, Sunderland enter Gameweek 37 in a positive position regarding availability. Regis Le Bris confirmed on Friday that the club has a fully-fit squad aside from Romaine Mundle. This means the Black Cats have no additional injury concerns heading into their upcoming fixture.
