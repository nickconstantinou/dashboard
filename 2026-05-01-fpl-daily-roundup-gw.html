Gameweek Landscape: Blank and Double Gameweek Situation

The fixture schedule continues to throw up complex scenarios for FPL managers to navigate. Gameweek 34 remains a confirmed blank gameweek, with Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds, and Manchester City all without fixtures. Both Az and his co-host confirmed deploying the Free Hit chip in Gameweek 34, representing a significant tactical pivot for managers seeking to maximise their squads for the upcoming run-in.

The Bench Boost chip was deployed in Gameweek 33, with managers having taken advantage of the double fixture situation involving the same six clubs. However, the hosts noted that managers stand to lose up to 14 players as a consequence of this aggressive strategy — a stark reminder of the risks involved with chip activation during this busy period.

Looking ahead, Crystal Palace and Manchester City will double in Gameweek 36 — the final Double Gameweek of the 2025/26 season. Palace have not had any doubles to date this campaign, making this a notable opportunity for FPL managers who have maintained coverage. Notably, Palace blanked in Gameweek 31 alongside Arsenal and Wolves, which may have influenced some managers to look elsewhere, but the upcoming double fixture presents a chance to capitalise on Palace coverage for the run-in.

Gameweek 36 also stands out with 22 confirmed fixtures — the highest fixture density of any remaining gameweek — making it an attractive option for those considering chip deployments.
