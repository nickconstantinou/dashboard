Introduction

This article examines key assets across positions based on verified data points from the 2025/26 season.

Goalkeeper Options

Robin Roefs – Sunderland (£4.8m)

Robin Roefs represents a compelling budget option between the posts. The Sunderland goalkeeper is tied in fourth place in the FPL goalkeeper standings, suggesting competitive underlying metrics despite his modest price tag. With 23 save points across the season, Roefs offers alternative routes to returns beyond clean sheets alone. Sunderland have kept six clean sheets on home turf—the fourth-most in the top-flight—providing solid foundation for potential points. However, opponents have conceded more shots from outside the box than every other team except Burnley in 2025/26, suggesting this may not be a straightforward fixture.

The Mackems are second-bottom for xG on home turf, indicating a potentially close, low-scoring encounter. Sunderland host Nottingham Forest on Friday, who are unbeaten in five Premier League matches. Only four teams have generated a lower xG than Nottingham Forest in that period, having found the net 10 times from 5.6 xG—suggesting Forest may struggle to convert chances.

Defender Options

Neco Williams – Nottingham Forest (£4.8m)

For managers seeking attacking potential from defence, Neco Williams (£4.8m) leads all defenders for shots this season with 41 attempts. This volume from a defender provides meaningful goal-threat potential, particularly against weaker opponents. His price point remains accessible for Free Hit squad construction.

Michael Keane – Everton (£4.5m)

Michael Keane (£4.5m) has emerged as a surprisingly productive asset at the back. Keane has already scored three times in 2025/26 and banked defensive contribution points in 13 of his 21 starts. His set-piece involvement and goal-scoring history make him a genuine differential option at £4.5m.

Everton are unbeaten in seven of their last eight matches away from home, with three clean sheets in that time. This away form provides confidence when selecting Everton assets for road fixtures during Blank Gameweek 34.

Diogo Dalot – Manchester United

Diogo Dalot is in only 4.5% of squads, making him a true differential choice. Manchester United host Brentford on Monday, who have failed to find the net in three of their last five matches. Beyond clean sheet potential, Dalot poses a threat in and around the opposition penalty box. This could prove especially valuable against a side who are third-bottom for crosses conceded from their left flank in the last six matches.

Jarrad Branthwaite – Everton (£5.3m)

Jarrad Branthwaite (£5.3m) looks set to profit from reduced competition for centre-back minutes. His injury is expected to keep him out for the remainder of the season, which should see his defensive colleagues feature more prominently. When paired with Everton's strong away record, this situation may create value in Everton's backline.

Midfielder Options

Cody Gakpo – Liverpool (£7.3m)

Cody Gakpo (£7.3m) leads all FPL players for shots in recent fixtures. The Dutchman's 22 shots in the last six Gameweeks is more than any other FPL player, demonstrating exceptional volume. Additionally, he's third among Liverpool assets for chances created in that period with 11, indicating dual threat capability—both shots on goal and chance creation.

Gakpo's price of £7.3m positions him as a mid-priced premium option who could dominate a favourable fixture.

John McGinn – Aston Villa (£5.3m)

John McGinn (£5.3m) has shown immediate impact upon returning from injury. Since McGinn's return from injury, he's produced a goal and an assist in three Premier League games. His box-crashing tendencies and set-piece involvement provide multiple avenues for returns at a reasonable price point.

Kiernan Dewsbury-Hall – Everton (£5.1m)

Kiernan Dewsbury-Hall (£5.1m) has delivered consistent returns in recent appearances. Dewsbury-Hall has scored three goals and supplied three assists in his last nine appearances for Everton, averaging a superb 6.0 points per start. His creativity and goal threat from midfield make him an excellent budget option at £5.1m.

Conor Gallagher – Tottenham (£5.0m)

Conor Gallagher (£5.0m) offers an intriguing budget option in midfield. Gallagher was Tottenham Hotspur's most advanced central midfielder last week, with the inclusion of both Rodrigo Bentancur and Yves Bissouma giving him much more freedom to attack. This advanced positioning increases his goal-scoring and chance-creation potential.

Team-Based Considerations

Brentford's Vulnerabilities

Brentford are third-bottom for crosses conceded from their left flank in the last six matches. This defensive weakness could exploit wing-back systems and inverted full-backs from opposing teams, particularly those with right-sided attackers.

Newcastle's Defensive Struggles

Crucially, Newcastle have kept just one clean sheet in 19 matches across all competitions. This record makes targeting Newcastle assets from an attacking perspective more attractive during Blank Gameweek 34.

Conclusion

Building a differential 'Free Hit' XI for Blank Gameweek 34 requires balancing low ownership with strong underlying statistics. Sunderland's Robin Roefs offers budget goalkeeper value with clean sheet potential at home. In defence, Diogo Dalot's 4.5% ownership combined with Manchester United's fixture against Brentford presents a genuine differential opportunity. Midfielders Cody Gakpo and Kiernan Dewsbury-Hall combine high shot volumes and creative output respectively, while John McGinn provides confirmed fitness and recent returns at £5.3m.
