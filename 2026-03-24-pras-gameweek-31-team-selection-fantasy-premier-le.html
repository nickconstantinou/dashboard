Pras' Gameweek 31 Team Selection | Fantasy Premier League Tips 2025/26

Introduction

Welcome back to the FPL Wire, where Fantasy Premier League enthusiasts turn for strategic insight during the most critical junctures of the season. In this episode, Pras breaks down his Gameweek 31 team selection as the community faces the first blank gameweek of the campaign—a moment that forces managers to confront difficult decisions they've been postponing for months.

The timing of this video is particularly significant because it was recorded immediately after the conclusion of midweek European fixtures, with three English teams—Crystal Palace, Nottingham Forest, and Aston Villa—all progressing to the next round. This development has substantial implications for the fixture landscape, and Pras explains precisely how these results reshape the strategic picture for the remainder of the season. Whether you're battling to climb the ranks or defending a lead, understanding these dynamics is essential for making informed decisions in the coming gameweeks.

Pras opens by noting his satisfaction with how the evening unfolded, particularly because more teams progressing in Europe means more blank gameweeks—a situation he describes as "all good news" from an FPL perspective. His enthusiasm is infectious as he prepares to walk viewers through the fixture matrix and the three viable chip strategies that managers are employing at this stage of the season.

Understanding the Blank Gameweek Landscape

The core focus of this video centers on how Crystal Palace's progression has fundamentally altered the blank gameweek picture. Pras demonstrates this using a visual chart that he's updated with the latest information, showing which teams will be blank in Gameweek 31 and which will have fixtures scheduled. The Crystal Palace row now clearly shows they've been "boxed out" of midweeks, meaning their Premier League fixture against Manchester City cannot fit into Gameweek 31 under any circumstances.

This creates a guaranteed blank for all Fantasy managers who own Palace assets—including the highly popular Eberechi Eze and, more importantly for many, the discounted Ismaila Sarr. However, Pras offers reassurance that those who have already sold Sarr needn't worry about him suddenly becoming essential again in Gameweek 31, as the blank is now confirmed.

The implications extend far beyond a single gameweek. Because Palace have progressed to the next round of their European competition, their second-leg fixture prevents a double gameweek from materialising in Gameweek 32—which many managers were counting on. Pras confirms that Gameweek 32 will not be a double, though Gameweek 33 remains highly likely to serve as the double that managers have been planning around. He also notes that Palace should have another double in Gameweek 36, giving long-term planners additional options.

The scenario that many were hoping for—Manchester City playing in both Gameweek 31 and 32 to create a triple or massive double—has become significantly less likely. For this to happen, Manchester City must beat Liverpool in their Champions League tie, Chelsea must win their FA Cup match, Arsenal must win their FA Cup fixture, and then the television companies must schedule matches in the manner that creates maximum disruption. Pras describes this as "still carnage" but acknowledges that the base case we discussed in previous episodes remains the most likely outcome.

The Three Chip Strategies Explained

One of the most valuable aspects of this video is Pras' clear explanation of the three distinct chip strategies that managers are employing at this critical juncture. Each has merits depending on your current team structure, rank aspirations, and risk tolerance.

Strategy One: The Popular Approach

The most common strategy involves using the Wildcard in Gameweek 32, the Bench Boost in Gameweek 33, and the Free Hit in Gameweek 34. Pras notes that this sequence makes sense because Gameweek 32 has a Friday deadline—essentially tomorrow—so managers need to act quickly. He maintains his previous position that managers should not hold off on wildcarding simply to gain more information. The FA Cup quarterfinals will provide additional clarity, but the price movements between now and then are unlikely to be significant enough to justify waiting. Press the button when you have conviction, and don't fear missing out on minor price changes.

Strategy Two: The Alternative Approach

Pras reveals that this is the strategy he's personally inclined toward if the fixtures eventually look as expected. It involves using the Free Hit in Gameweek 33, the Bench Boost in Gameweek 36, and the Wildcard in Gameweek 35. The reasoning here is straightforward: his current team structure looks good for Gameweeks 32 and 34, so wildcasting in 32 would disrupt a functional squad. By waiting until 35, he preserves his team through the blank weeks and then rebuilds for the final run-in.

He acknowledges this strategy is "semi-popular" but likely less common than the first approach. The trade-off is clear—you accept some uncertainty in Gameweek 33 in exchange for wildcarding with more information about which fixtures will be in that double gameweek.

Strategy Three: The Niche Approach

A third, more niche strategy involves wildcarding in Gameweek 33, hitting Gameweek 34, and either bench boosting in 35 or 36 depending on your remaining chips. This approach appeals to managers who already wildcarded earlier in the season and are now looking to rebuild specifically for the double gameweek 33 rather than waiting. Pras mentions this was a strategy discussed by LR in the main podcast, and while not for everyone, it's a viable path for certain team structures.

The key insight Pras offers is that all three strategies are defensible, but the existence of three options should tell you something: no single approach is overwhelmingly dominant. The information we'll gain during the upcoming international break—specifically which fixtures are moving into Gameweek 33 versus staying in 34—will be worth waiting for, which is why Pras is personally inclined to wait before pressing any chips.

Fixture Analysis for Free Hit Decisions

Pras presents detailed fixture analysis data from Solio, breaking down projected goals for teams depending on which gameweek you're targeting for a Free Hit. This analysis is crucial because your chip strategy directly influences which players you should be targeting this week.

For managers planning to use a Free Hit in Gameweek 33, the data shows Liverpool as the team to prioritize. Pras notes that while there's uncertainty around Mohamed Salah's fitness—he's expected to play but managers should check Jürgen Klopp's press conference for confirmation—Liverpool's fixtures are exceptional. They have Fulham at home in Gameweek 32 and Crystal Palace at home in Gameweek 34, making them ideal for the Free Hit 33 strategy. The projected goals column clearly places Liverpool at the top for this approach.

Manchester United are projected as the second-highest scoring team for both Gameweeks 32 and 34, making them an excellent secondary option. Pras highlights that United assets work particularly well within this strategy, with Bruno Fernandes continuing to deliver as a captaincy option.

Fulham emerge as the third key target, which explains why many managers are prioritizing Alexander Isak and other Fulham assets this week. Pras explains that the gap between the fourth-highest projected team and those below is minimal—there's little difference between Fulham, West Ham, Aston Villa, and others, meaning goal share and individual player form becomes more important than team-level projections.

For defense, the Free Hit 33 picture shows Liverpool, Manchester United, West Ham, Tottenham, and Nottingham Forest as the teams to target. This is why Fulham defenders have become so popular recently.

For managers planning a Free Hit in Gameweek 34 instead, the focus shifts entirely to Chelsea's attack. The data shows Chelsea as the highest-projected team for that week, making Palmer, Pedro Neto, and other Chelsea assets the clear priority. Newcastle also appears prominently, giving managers flexibility between Newcastle and Fulham assets depending on their specific strategy.

Pras offers practical advice for those still deciding between Newcastle and Fulham this week: if you want to play Newcastle assets immediately and could bench them if you later choose Free Hit 34, go with Newcastle. If you specifically want to use those players in Gameweek 34 when Newcastle have their double, then target Fulham assets this week instead. The decision depends entirely on your chip strategy.

Arsenal appear in the Gameweek 34 defensive projections despite having a blank in that week, simply because their overall strength is so high—but the blank obviously removes them from consideration for Free Hit 34 strategies.
