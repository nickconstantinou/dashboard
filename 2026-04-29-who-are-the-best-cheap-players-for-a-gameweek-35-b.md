# Who Are the Best Cheap Players for a Gameweek 35 Bench Boost?

When activating the Bench Boost chip in Gameweek 35, managers need reliable cheap options across all positions. The following analysis draws from verified player data and fixture context to identify value picks who can deliver returns without breaking the budget.

## Goalkeepers

**Karl Darlow – £3.9m (Leeds United)**

Darlow has been an ever-present starter for Leeds United since Gameweek 22. In this period, he has kept four clean sheets in 13 appearances, in addition to seven save points. At £3.9m, he represents a solid budget option between the posts, particularly if Leeds enjoy favourable fixture conditions during this gameweek.

## Defenders

**James Hill – £4.2m (Bournemouth)**

Since he became a first-team regular in Gameweek 19, no defender has bettered his tally of 193 DefCon points. He's also supplied three assists in that period, making Hill a standout budget defender for managers seeking defensive returns alongside assists.

**Pascal Struijk – £4.3m (Leeds United)**

Boasting a decent rate of defensive contribution (DefCon) points, Struijk is the division's joint-best defender for shots in the box (28). His attacking threat from defence at £4.3m offers something different from typical cheap defenders who rely solely on clean sheets.

**James Justin – £3.9m (Leeds United)**

Should Gabriel Gudmundsson (£3.8m) be ruled out of Gameweek 35, then James Justin will presumably switch over to left wing-back. This positional shift could increase his involvement in attacking moves, making him an intriguing £3.9m enabler if circumstances align.

## Forwards

**Noah Okafor – £5.6m (Leeds United)**

Okafor has been in excellent form recently, having netted three goals in his previous three matches, while he's also returned an assist in that time. It's no surprise that he's currently the fifth-most transferred-in player for Gameweek 35, reflecting his rising popularity among FPL managers.

**Dominic Calvert-Lewin – £5.7m (Leeds United)**

Calvert-Lewin is an obvious forward candidate at home to Burnley, having scored 11 goals in 31 matches this season. The penalty-taker is often wasteful, having underperformed his xG by 2.99 in 2025/26. Despite the underperformance relative to expected goals, his consistent goal tally and penalty duties keep him relevant at £5.7m.

## Fixture Context

**Burnley Away Record**

The Clarets, who have already been relegated, are bottom for expected goals (xG) in their last six away matches, having generated only 3.82 xG. This depleted attacking threat benefits defenders and goalkeepers facing them.

**Sunderland Away Record**

Opponents Sunderland have failed to find the net in eight of their 17 away matches. Managers considering Sunderland assets should weigh this scoring frailty when building Bench Boost lineups.

**Crystal Palace Rotation Risk**

Crystal Palace have UEFA Conference League semi-finals before and after Gameweek 35, with Glasner already hinting at rotation. Managers with Crystal Palace assets should consider this uncertainty when activating Bench Boost, as regular starters may be rested.

## Summary

The most cost-effective approach for Gameweek 35 Bench Boost appears to concentrate budget in the middle and attacking positions while using the cheapest viable options in defence and goalkeeping. Leeds assets feature prominently across multiple positions, offering clustered value. Managers should monitor the Crystal Palace situation closely and check Burnley's fixture appeal before finalising their lineups.
