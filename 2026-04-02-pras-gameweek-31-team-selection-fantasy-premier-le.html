Breaking Down the Blank Gameweek Chaos: Chip Strategies, Fixture Analysis, and the Crystal Palace Dilemma

The FPL landscape shifted dramatically this week as Fantasy Premier League managers confront their first blank gameweek of the season. In his latest team selection video on the FPL Wire, Pras—known widely by his initials—dives deep into the implications of European competition results, fixture chaos, and the strategic decisions that will define the coming weeks. With a Friday deadline looming and managers facing difficult decisions about chips and player selections, this video offers a masterclass in FPL planning during one of the most complex periods of the fantasy calendar.

Pras recorded this video after witnessing three English teams progress in their respective European competitions, with Crystal Palace's advancement proving particularly significant for the fixture landscape. The progression of more teams through European competition means more midweek commitments, which directly translates to more blank gameweeks—something Pras describes as "carnage" but views optimistically as inevitable reality managers must navigate.

The Crystal Palace Problem: Why GW32 Is Now Confirmed NOT a Double

The most immediate consequence of this week's European results centers on Crystal Palace's fixture against Manchester City. Pras walks viewers through his updated fixture chart, highlighting the Crystal Palace row where midweek commitments now create a guaranteed blank in Gameweek 31. This is crucial information for managers who have held onto Eberechi Eze or other Palace assets, as the question of whether that blank can be avoided has now been definitively answered.

More significantly, Pras explains that because Palace now have their second leg of the next European round scheduled, the Man City fixture cannot simply shift to Gameweek 32. This confirmation means that Gameweek 32 is officially no longer a double gameweek for Manchester City, eliminating one of the anticipated double gameweek opportunities managers had been counting on. The base case scenario that Pras and other analysts have discussed—positioning the major double in Gameweek 33 with another likely Palace double in Gameweek 36—remains the most probable outcome, and this new information actually strengthens that likelihood.

For managers worried that selling Eze or other Palace players before the blank might cost them a favorable fixture, Pras offers reassurance: the fixture simply cannot fit anywhere convenient, and managers should proceed with their plans without fear of missing out. This certainty allows for clearer planning regarding which assets to prioritize and which chips to deploy in the coming weeks.

Three Paths Through the Chaos: The Chip Strategy Spectrum

Pras identifies three distinct chip strategies that FPL managers are likely employing during this blank gameweek period, each with its own logic, risk profile, and player targeting implications.

Strategy One: The "Wait and Deploy" Approach

The most popular strategy involves wild carding in Gameweek 32, bench boosting in Gameweek 33, and free hitting in Gameweek 34. Pras acknowledges this is likely the most common approach but questions whether managers are being too hasty with their wild cards. He advises against pressing the wild card button immediately, suggesting instead that managers wait until after the FA Cup quarterfinals have been completed. The reasoning is straightforward: waiting provides more information about which fixtures will actually appear in which gameweeks, and the price movements during this international break period are unlikely to be significant enough to justify premature chip deployment. By waiting, managers gain clarity on whether the anticipated doubles actually materialize, potentially saving themselves from building a team around fixtures that don't materialize as expected.

Strategy Two: The "Free Hit First" Alternative

The second strategy, which Pras reveals is the one he is personally leaning toward, involves free hitting in Gameweek 33, bench boosting in Gameweek 36, and wild carding in Gameweek 35. His reasoning centers on his current team looking acceptable for Gameweeks 32 and 34, making the earlier wild card unnecessary. This approach requires patience but potentially allows for more informed decision-making once the fixture landscape becomes clearer. Pras admits this strategy will likely be "semi-popular" but not as widespread as the first option, which means those who execute it well could gain ground on the majority of their competition.

Strategy Three: The "Wild Card Later" Niche

The third and most niche strategy involves wild carding in Gameweek 33, taking a hit in Gameweek 34, and potentially bench boosting later in either Gameweek 35 or 36 depending on team composition. This approach is designed for managers who either already used their bench boost earlier in the season or who looked at their Gameweek 32 team and decided it was acceptable enough to delay the wild card. Pras notes this was a strategy discussed by LR on his main podcast, highlighting that it remains a viable option for those with specific team structures that make earlier chip deployment less necessary.

Pras emphasizes that all three strategies are "viable" but stresses that the dominance of Strategy One means waiting for more information is generally advisable. "The fact that there are three strategies and only one of them has a wild card in Gameweek 32 makes me think why don't you just wait?" he explains. The international break will provide 100% clarity on which fixtures appear in which gameweeks, making delayed decisions far more informed than premature ones.

The Solio Fixture Ticker: Precision Planning for Each Strategy

To help viewers visualize the implications of their chip decisions, Pras introduces a fixture analysis tool from Solio (a prominent FPL statistics and planning resource) that projects attacking potential for each team across different gameweeks depending on which chip strategy a manager employs.

For Free Hit Gameweek 33 Strategists

The analysis reveals that managers planning to free hit in Gameweek 33 should prioritize Liverpool's attack above all other options. This makes Mohamed Salah's potential injury status—which will be clarified in Arne Slot's press conference the following day—critically important. Beyond Liverpool, Manchester United projects as the second-highest attacking option for both Gameweeks 32 and 34, making Bruno Fernandes and company attractive additions for this strategy. Pras specifically mentions Alejandro Garnacho as a consideration, noting that if Salah misses any games, Diogo Jota becomes valuable for minutes and potential penalty duties.

Fulham's attack also shows well in this analysis, which Pras connects to the popularity of Harry Wilson this week. Cole Palmer's Chelsea teammates also warrant consideration, with discussions around whether to prioritize Newcastle players (better for Gameweek 34 if not free hitting) or Fulham players (better if committing to Gameweek 34 free hit). Pras suggests that if managers are "agnostic" between the two, rotation risk should guide the decision—Newcastle players can be brought in this week and benched if switching strategies, while Fulham assets are better if Gameweek 34 free hitting is the plan.

For budget options in this strategy, Pras highlights Tyrell Malacia as "good for minutes" and a sensible pick, while also mentioning that bringing in a Manchester United midfielder like Diogo Dalot or Lisandro Martínez could work well. He hints this might be something he explores more next week.

For Free Hit Gameweek 34 Strategists

The right-hand column of the Solio analysis targets managers planning to free hit in Gameweek 34 instead. Here, Chelsea's attack becomes the priority, with Noni Madueke and Cole Palmer both flagged as strong options for that horizon. Newcastle also shows well, making them attractive for managers not committing to the Gameweek 34 free hit but wanting assets that perform in that week regardless.

On the defensive side for Gameweek 34 strategists, Arsenal remains the highest-ranked option despite their blank in Gameweek 31, primarily because they are expected to double in the target gameweek. Newcastle, Brighton, and Aston Villa also appear as defensive targets worth considering.

With Palace's Man City fixture confirmed as a Gameweek 31 blank and unable to shift to Gameweek 32, managers can stop worrying about timing their Palace sales around potential fixture rescheduling. This clarity should inform immediate decisions about whether to hold or sell assets.

Waiting on the wild card is generally advisable. The majority of managers planning a Gameweek 32 wild card can benefit from patience. The FA Cup quarterfinals and international break will provide complete clarity on the fixture landscape, and the price movements during this period are unlikely to justify premature chip deployment. "Press it when the FA Cup quarterfinals have happened. You're not going to miss out on much in terms of prices," Pras advises.

Free hitting in Gameweek 33 means prioritizing Liverpool and Manchester United assets immediately, while planning for Gameweek 34 free hit means looking at Chelsea and Newcastle. Managers should not make player decisions in isolation but rather in the context of their overall chip strategy.

Rotation risk determines Newcastle versus Fulham decisions. For managers unsure which gameweek they will free hit in, the Newcastle versus Fulham choice becomes a question of flexibility. Newcastle players can be brought in this week and benched if strategy changes, while Fulham assets make more sense if Gameweek 34 commitment is planned. This subtle distinction can prevent managers from being stuck with players they cannot use.

The base case remains the most likely scenario. Despite the uncertainty, the anticipated double in Gameweek 33 with Palace likely doubling again in Gameweek 36 represents the highest probability outcome. Managers building their strategies around this assumption are betting on the most likely scenario, though they should remain aware that European results could still alter the landscape.
