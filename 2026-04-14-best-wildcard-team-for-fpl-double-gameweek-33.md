# The FPL Double Gameweek 33 Wildcard: What You Need to Know

Double Gameweek 33 will be one of the most consequential weeks of the season for Fantasy Premier League managers — and not just because of the fixtures. The chips you play, and when you play them, could define your entire run-in.

If you're hitting the Wildcard this week, you're already making a significant commitment. But your strategy shouldn't end there. Whether you have a Free Hit remaining, whether you've already used your Bench Boost, and how many free transfers you're sitting on will all shape what your Wildcard squad should look like.

---

## The Missing Fixture Hanging Over Everything

There's still a fixture that hasn't been scheduled: Manchester City versus Crystal Palace, originally due in Gameweek 31, remains without a date. When it eventually gets rearranged, it will create another Double Gameweek for both clubs — and given Palace's likely progress in European competition, that double looks set to drop into Gameweek 36. That matters for your long-term planning. If you're building a Wildcard squad now, you're not just solving Gameweek 33. You're trying to thread a needle across Gameweeks 33, 35, 36, 37, and 38 simultaneously.

---

## Chip Strategy Is Everything

The decisions you've already made — or haven't made — are now working for or against you.

**If you still have a Free Hit and plan to use it in Blank Gameweek 34**, you can build your Wildcard with a clearer long-term view. Without needing to field a full squad of 14 or 15 doublers in Gameweek 33 (because you can't stack chips in the same week), you have more freedom to plan for the run-in. Arsenal defenders, for instance, have excellent fixtures from Gameweek 35 onwards — and with the title race potentially still finely balanced after Gameweek 32's results, that Arsenal backline could be a significant asset down the stretch. You can park them on your bench for Gameweek 33, ready to activate when the fixtures turn.

**If you don't have a Free Hit remaining**, you're in a trickier position. You'll need to strike a balance between loading up on Gameweek 33 doublers and assembling something that can field a near-full XI in the Blank Gameweek 34. That's a tighter needle to thread, and it may push you toward certain player selections you wouldn't otherwise make.

**If you still have a Bench Boost**, the timing of when you deploy it — likely Gameweek 35 or 36 — will also influence your Wildcard choices. Interestingly, given the strong fixtures enjoyed by budget picks from Brighton and Leeds, a Gameweek 33 Wildcard squad with a Bench Boost in mind probably won't look drastically different from one without. The underlying fixture logic is similar.

---

## Transfer Allowance and the Short-Term Trap

How many free transfers you have going into this week matters more than it might seem. If you're sitting on three to five free transfers, you have more flexibility to play an aggressive Wildcard — loading up on doublers for Gameweek 33 and then shipping them out for better options further down the line if required. This is particularly relevant if you're heading into Blank Gameweek 34 without a Free Hit. You can afford to take a short-term punt knowing you have the budget to pivot.

If you're short on free transfers and need to straddle multiple gameweeks, your Wildcard strategy will need to be more conservative.

---

## One Name to Watch: Nico O'Reilly

Manchester City's Nico O'Reilly was shaping up as one of the first names on many Gameweek 33 Wildcard teams — a budget-friendly defensive asset with strong underlying numbers. But he came off during the Chelsea match with a possible muscle injury, and Fantasy managers should await further clarification before committing to him. His fitness will be updated on Friday. If he misses out, it changes the economics of City coverage and opens up alternative routes in the squad.

---

## The Core Trade-Off

The fundamental tension at the heart of every Gameweek 33 Wildcard is this: how heavily do you lean into Gameweek 33 itself versus the fixtures that follow? The answer depends entirely on your chip inventory and transfer position. There is no universally correct squad — only the correct squad for your specific situation.

If you have the Free Hit in your pocket, go long. If you don't, go balanced. And if you're still weighing up the Bench Boost, know that your squad probably isn't going to look that different either way. The Brighton and Leeds budget picks are doing a lot of the heavy lifting either way.

This is the week to be honest with yourself about where you stand. Then build accordingly.
