# FPL Q&A: Evaluating Palace and Comparing Leeds Assets

## Palace Player Viability

When assessing Crystal Palace players for FPL purposes, the evidence presents a mixed picture.

Jean-Philippe Mateta has been subbed off around the 60th/70th-minute mark in each of Crystal Palace's games against Shakhtar and was benched in the subsequent league game. This pattern of early substitution and benching in the following league fixture suggests limited minutes availability, which undermines his potential value in the short term.

However, not all Palace assets show concerning trends. Ismaila Sarr played the full 90 minutes against Shakhtar last night, indicating he may be managing better minutes than other attacking options at the club. Meanwhile, Daniel Munoz (£5.9m) played only 45 minutes in the last game, suggesting a potential rotation risk or fitness concern.

For managers considering Palace assets, the data favors Sarr based on the recent evidence of minutes played, while Mateta's recent usage pattern warrants caution.

## Leeds Attack: Calvert-Lewin Assessment

Dominic Calvert-Lewin (£5.8m) has emerged as a statistically notable option. He is top amongst all players for big chances with eight over the last six Gameweeks. This conversion rate places him ahead of all other players in the official data during this period.

However, his overall season returns tell a more cautious story. Calvert-Lewin has scored double digits only twice this season, at home to Palace in Gameweek 17 and away to Wolves in Gameweek 5. This limited ceiling despite the recent chance creation raises questions about sustainability.

The broader context for Leeds suggests their season may effectively be resolved. If West Ham lose to Arsenal on Sunday, Leeds will be safe by the time they play Spurs on Monday. With survival effectively secured, Leeds players may have reduced motivation for the remaining fixtures, potentially affecting both performance and team selection.

## Brighton Fixture Analysis

Brighton's remaining three fixtures are against Wolves, Leeds and Man Utd. All three teams they play over the next three Gameweeks are likely to have nothing to play for: Wolves, Leeds and Man Utd.

Brighton are realistic contenders for a European spot, which means they maintain strong motivation for their remaining matches. This combination of favorable fixtures against unmotivated opponents and genuine European qualification ambition makes Brighton attacking assets attractive for the run-in.

## Chelsea European Picture

The Blues do have something to play for, with European spots still open for contention. This context matters for any Chelsea assets managers may be considering.
