# FPL Gameweek 37 Early Scout Picks: Arsenal Attacking Double-Up

## Arsenal's Favourable Fixture

There's only one place to start this week. Title favourites Arsenal are at home to relegated Burnley in Gameweek 37. This fixture presents a significant opportunity for FPL managers looking to capitalise on a favourable matchup.

## Arsenal Attackers

The Gunners' offensive assets warrant serious consideration for Scout Picks this week. Gabriel Magalhaes (£7.3m) and Bukayo Saka (£10.0m) are widely viewed as essential selections given Arsenal's commanding position and the calibre of opponent they face.

Supporting this attacking interest, Viktor Gyokeres has emerged as Arsenal's leading shot taker from Gameweek 32 onwards. Only Erling Haaland and Igor Thiago have generated more non-penalty expected goals (NPxG) across the same period, highlighting his threat in the final third.

## Burnley's Defensive Woes

Burnley enter this fixture in poor form away from home. The relegated side have conceded 12 goals across their last four away games, leaving them vulnerable against an Arsenal side fighting for the title.

## Budget Options

For managers seeking budget alternatives, Everton defenders offer value. Michael Keane (£4.5m) is currently averaging 10.55 DefCons per 90 minutes in 2025/26. Meanwhile, Kiernan Dewsbury-Hall (£5.2m) averages 5.2 points per start this season—a figure only six midfielders have beaten.

## Crystal Palace Challenge

Crystal Palace are without an away clean sheet since February, suggesting attacking assets against them hold merit for the Gameweek 37 schedule.

## Timing Considerations

Manchester City won't contest their Gameweek 37 fixture until Tuesday due to their involvement in Saturday's FA Cup final. This means FPL managers cannot react to City players' minutes, injuries or form before the traditional deadline, complicating any City-related investments.

## Nottingham Forest Situation

Home to injury-hit Nottingham Forest, who are now safe from relegation, presents a different scenario. Having secured survival, their motivation levels may differ, though their injury situation weakens their defensive options.

## Chelsea's Struggles

Chelsea are currently winless in seven matches, making their assets less appealing for immediate Gameweek 37 planning unless the schedule presents exceptional value.
