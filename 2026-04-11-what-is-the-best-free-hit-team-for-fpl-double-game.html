What is the best Free Hit team for FPL Double Gameweek 33?

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

Six clubs play twice in Gameweek 33, and that abundance of doubles makes the Free Hit chip increasingly attractive. With Manchester City, Arsenal, Chelsea, Brighton, Bournemouth, and Leeds United all doubling, there's a real opportunity to load up on players from thesefixture-rich sides and maximise returns. That said, Gameweek 32—and a slate of European matches—still has the potential to reshape the landscape before the deadline arrives. Still, an early look at a draft side and its alternatives provides a useful starting point.

---

The Draft Side

The starting XI below features at least one representative from each of the six doubling clubs—except Burnley. The Clarets' poor form this season makes their players considerably less appealing, despite their double.

Goalkeeper: Karl Darlow (Leeds United, £3.9m) Leeds host bottom-placed Wolverhampton Wanderers and travel to a Bournemouth side that has struggled to score from open play in recent weeks. Darlow secured successive shutouts before the international break and conceded just once against Manchester City in Gameweek 28. At £3.9m, he offers solid budget value between the posts.

Defence: Nico O'Reilly (£5.0m) The Manchester City defender is in exceptional form as he pushes for a spot in England's World Cup starting XI. He scored a brace in the EFL Cup final against Arsenal and then registered two assists in the FA Cup quarter-final versus Liverpool. His attacking output from a defensive position gives him a notably higher ceiling than most defenders. The double begins with a trip to Arsenal—a tricky opener—but that's followed by a meeting with Burnley, making the fixture swing favourable.

Defence: Jan Paul van Hecke (£4.5m, Brighton) Van Hecke has started every match he's been available for and likely has the highest ceiling among Brighton's defenders. Brighton as a unit have conceded zero or one goals in all but one of their last 12 league fixtures. He offers a modest attacking threat and a solid DefCon record. The double begins with a trip to Tottenham, who possess the Premier League's worst home record, before hosting Chelsea. Brighton won the reverse fixture in Gameweek 6.

Defence: Marcos Senesi (£5.1m, Bournemouth) Senesi needs little introduction to FPL managers this season. The Bournemouth centre-back leads all defenders in DefCon actions and boasts the highest success rate in the metric across the entire league—73.3%—on top of four assists. The Cherries have tightened up at the back recently, keeping a handful of clean sheets with more likely on the horizon.

---

The Single-Gameweek Anchor

Bruno Fernandes (£10.3m, Manchester United) is the sole single-Gameweek player in this draft, and he keeps his place through sheer force of form. The Portuguese has delivered three double-digit point hauls in his last four appearances and five in his last eight. He leads the league with 17 assists and has eight goals to his name. With Manchester United hosting Chelsea—a side desperate to revive their top-five push but worryingly porous at the back—Fernandes is well-placed for both attacking returns and DefCon points. At £10.3m, he's expensive, but his recent output is impossible to ignore.

---

Strategy Notes

The draft above prioritises players from clubs with favourable double fixtures while avoiding Burnley entirely. The combination of Darlow, O'Reilly, van Hecke, and Senesi gives a defensive foundation with both clean sheet potential and attacking upside. Fernandes provides the premium midfielder anchor despite his single fixture.

Managers should monitor the outcome of Gameweek 32 before finalising their side—injuries, suspensions, or unexpected form shifts could alter the calculus. But for those ready to commit early, this draft offers a balanced mix of budget options, premium picks, and doubles from clubs in good defensive shape.
