Fantasy Football Scout: Double Gameweek 33 and Blank Gameweek 34 Confirmed as Leeds and Bournemouth Secure FA Cup Spots

The Full Story

The fixture picture for Double Gameweek 33 and Blank Gameweek 34 in Fantasy Premier League (FPL) has been finalized following Leeds United's dramatic victory over West Ham United in Sunday's FA Cup quarter-final tie. The result means Leeds United and Bournemouth—two clubs still active in the FA Cup—will now play twice in Gameweek 33 before blanking entirely in Gameweek 34.

These two clubs join Manchester City, Burnley, Chelsea, and Brighton and Hove Albion as the six teams confirmed for Double Gameweek 33. The same six teams will then blank in Gameweek 34, completing what FPL managers have been anticipating since the FA Cup draw determined which clubs would advance to the semi-finals.

The Double Gameweek represents both opportunity and risk for fantasy managers, particularly those holding the Bench Boost chip, which rewards managers for having a deep bench by activating all substitutes simultaneously. However, early injury concerns have emerged from the Leeds camp following their Sunday match. Gabriel Gudmondsson (£3.8m) missed the quarter-final with a minor groin issue, while Joe Rodon (£3.9m) and Anton Stach (£4.8m) both hobbled off during the game, raising questions about their availability for the upcoming double fixtures.

Why This Matters

This fixture confirmation is critically important for FPL managers for several reasons. First, with only a handful of Gameweeks remaining in the 2025-26 FPL season, every fixture advantage carries significant weight in overall rankings. The six teams in Double Gameweek 33 represent the only doubling options available, meaning managers must carefully balance their squads to maximize returns from these clubs while accounting for their blank in Gameweek 34.

The financial implications are substantial. Budget players from Leeds and Bournemouth—particularly those priced under £5.0m—become extremely valuable for managers using the Bench Boost chip, as these cheap options can be fielded in both fixtures while freeing up funds for premium assets elsewhere. However, the injury concerns surrounding Leeds' budget options complicate this strategy considerably.

Managers who wildcarded in Gameweek 32 face particular difficulty, as they must now navigate two gameweeks where their wild-slot players from Leeds or Bournemouth will either double (Gameweek 33) or not play at all (Gameweek 34). Leeds are away at Manchester United and Bournemouth are away at Arsenal in Gameweek 32, offering no immediate returns before the double kicks in.
