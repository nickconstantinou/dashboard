Perhaps the most urgent advice Harry offers in this video is a direct plea to managers considering early wildcard transfers: do not do it. He repeats this message multiple times with increasing emphasis, addressing those who are thinking of wildcarding in Gameweek 32 specifically.

In that midweek window between the FA Cup results and the Gameweek 32 deadline, the official announcements regarding doubles and blanks will finally begin rolling out. Making transfers now, before that information arrives, is a gamble that Harry explicitly warns against. Yes, you might gain 0.2, 0.3, or even 0.5 in player value by transferring early. If something unexpected happens in the FA Cup—whether it's an upset result or a situation that changes the predicted doubles and blanks into different Gameweeks—it could force you to completely rethink your chip strategy while being locked into transfers that no longer make sense.

The practical advice is to stay as flexible as possible this week. Don't use your transfers unless you absolutely have to. Focus on buying players who are valuable in the long term regardless of what happens with your chip strategy. And above all, wait for the FA Cup results to be out of the way before making any major decisions.
