FPL Notes: Gibbs-White and Sanchez Injury Latest, Pereira on Rotation

Head Collision Rules FPL Assets Out

Morgan Gibbs-White (£7.7m) and Robert Sanchez (£4.8m) collided head-on during the match and both were taken off after physio attention. Gibbs-White, representing Nottingham Forest, has a deep cut rather than concussion, per Vitor Pereira. The manager stated: "Gibbs-White has a deep cut. We will see, but he is a fighter... The only thing is to look for the face and the pain, because he has pains now."

If any of the four players with head injuries suffered a concussion, they would be ruled out of Gameweek 36.

Chelsea Head Injury Concerns

Teenagers Jesse Derry (£4.5m) and Zach Abbott (£3.8m) had a sickening clash of heads during the match, with Derry coming off worse. Derry, a winger, was taken to hospital after on-field treatment, but mercifully the prognosis sounds positive.

In a timely recovery from injury, back-up goalkeeper Filip Jörgensen (£4.3m) made his return to the Chelsea squad after two months out. He came on for Sanchez.

Additional Chelsea Injury Woes

Alejandro Garnacho (£6.4m) and Pedro Neto (£7.0m) became the latest additions to Chelsea's injury list after taking knocks in training on Sunday.

Pereira's Rotation Strategy

Vitor Pereira has saved most of his tinkering for Europe this season, but he went rotation-crazy for the first time in the Premier League, making eight changes from Thursday's UEFA Europa League win over Aston Villa.

Gibbs-White, Elliot Anderson (£5.6m), Nikola Milenkovic (£5.1m) and Neco Williams (£4.8m) were introduced after 45 minutes as substitutes, with Chris Wood (£7.1m) coming on a bit later.

Ola Aina (£4.7m), Ibrahima Sangare (£4.9m), Murillo (£5.2m) and Dan Ndoye (£5.5m) meanwhile failed to recover from injury to feature for Nottingham Forest.

Match Result

Another superb win for Nottingham Forest at Stamford Bridge edged them closer to safety—and condemned Chelsea to their longest Premier League losing run in over 30 years.
