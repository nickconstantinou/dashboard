# Man City v Crystal Palace: FPL Captaincy Analysis

## Context: The Final Double Game Week

This episode of Captain of the Coast represents the last double game week preview of the season, featuring Jonny Pringle and Ruaidhri McLaughlin-Dowd analyzing the Manchester City versus Crystal Palace fixture for Planet FPL 2025/26.

## Manchester City's Title Push

Manchester City kept their Premier League title hopes alive with a 1-1 draw at Everton on Monday. However, the panel acknowledges these chances remain slim despite the crucial point secured.

The team has demonstrated precedent for dramatic comebacks in title races. In 2012, City recovered from 3-1 down against Sunderland to draw 3-3. Similarly, in 2022, they overturned a 2-0 half-time deficit against West Ham to earn a 2-2 draw. These examples show City capable of producing remarkable turnarounds when required.

## Recent Performance Concerns

City's recent displays have fallen below their usual standards. Against Burnley, the team created numerous chances but failed to convert them into goals, with missed opportunities becoming increasingly frustrating as the match progressed. The performances against both Burnley and Everton have not matched the levels City showed earlier in the season.

Rodri's absence continues to be felt significantly. The midfielder's ability to control proceedings during difficult periods proves invaluable, and his injury contributed to City's struggles against Burnley, allowing the game to slip away in ways that may not have happened with him on the pitch.

## Crystal Palace Considerations

Royal Republic has confirmed Palace are likely to rotate players on the final day of the season, making them an attractive fixture for opposing teams seeking favorable matchups.

The panel noted an interesting pattern regarding Palace's season-ending fixtures. Whoever receives Crystal Palace as their final game of the season appears to win the league. Palace ended last season against Liverpool, presenting them with a guard of honor situation. This pattern suggests teams facing Palace in the final match may benefit from rotated opposition.

## FPL Captaincy Assessment

For managers selecting their captain from City assets, the Palace fixture presents potential value given Palace's rotation plans. However, City must also consider whether their focus might shift toward the FA Cup final if Arsenal win their match, potentially affecting intensity in remaining league games.

## Chip Strategy Notes

If West Ham draw with Arsenal, Manchester City would need to score heavily in their remaining matches against Brentford and Palace to overturn the goal difference deficit. This scenario would require an all-out attacking approach, potentially benefiting FPL managers with City forwards in their squads. Managers should anticipate that Arsenal will likely put multiple goals past Burnley and Palace, increasing the margin City must overcome.
