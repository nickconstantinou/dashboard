Who are FPL’s best all-round defenders?

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

The Full Story

Fantasy Premier League managers looking to gain an edge in the closing weeks of the 2025/26 season should pay close attention to defensive assets, according to analysis from Fantasy Football Scout. The introduction of Defensive Contribution (DefCon) points has fundamentally shifted how managers evaluate defenders, moving beyond the traditional focus on clean sheets and attacking returns to a more holistic assessment of player value.

Clean sheet rates this season are running higher than recent campaigns, with an average of 5.21 clean sheets per 10-gameweeks across the league—up significantly from 4.68 in the previous season and 4.13 the year before. This trend benefits owners of defenders from top-performing defensive units, particularly those representing Arsenal, Brighton and Hove Albion, Manchester City, and Manchester United, who have demonstrated strong abilities to prevent goals, limit attempts, block shots on target, and reduce big chance creation.

Arsenal emerges as the standout defensive side over the course of the campaign, while Brighton and Leeds United join the Gunners and City as the most resolute backlines in recent weeks. Over the last six matches, Brighton have been particularly impressive, conceding just four goals and five big chances while posting the best expected goals conceded figure (xGC) of 5.50. Leeds have also been stingy, conceding fewer shots on target than all but Brighton. Manchester United, despite their general defensive solidity across the full season, have managed only five clean sheets—fewer than every team except the current bottom three of the table.

Why This Matters

The evolution of FPL strategy driven by DefCon points represents a significant shift in how the game is played. Managers can no longer simply target defenders from attacking teams; they must now weigh defensive contribution metrics alongside traditional attacking returns. This changes the entire calculus for squad building, particularly in the critical final stretch where every point matters.

For FPL managers, this analysis offers a roadmap for identifying defenders who can deliver value through multiple channels—clean sheets, defensive contributions, and occasional attacking returns. Teams like Brighton, who have been defensively excellent while also generating attacking threats, represent particularly valuable assets. Meanwhile, Bournemouth's situation illustrates the importance of underlying metrics: while the Cherries have also conceded just four goals in their recent six matches, their xGC of 11.21 suggests they've been fortunate rather than genuinely solid, making them riskier picks.

West Ham United present an interesting counterpoint, being labeled the "unluckiest" side in the league with an xGC delta of +6.87, though their recent form shows improvement with three clean sheets in their last seven matches.

Key Details

  • Current clean sheet rate: 5.21 per 10-gameweeks (up from 4.68 last season and 4.13 the year before)
  • Best defensive teams (full season): Arsenal, Brighton, Manchester City, Manchester United
  • Best defensive teams (last 6 matches): Arsenal, Brighton, Leeds United, Manchester City
  • Brighton's recent numbers: 4 goals conceded, 5 big chances allowed, xGC of 5.50
  • Bournemouth's recent numbers: 4 goals conceded but xGC of 11.21 (suggesting overperformance)
  • Manchester United: Only 5 clean sheets all season, worse than bottom three teams
  • West Ham United: xGC delta of +6.87 (unluckiest side in league), 3 clean sheets in last 7 matches

    Context & Background

    This analysis follows an earlier piece examining the best all-round midfielders in FPL, using the same methodology to evaluate defenders. The DefCon points system, introduced in recent seasons, awards additional points for actions like tackles, interceptions, blocks, and clearances, making defenders from teams that dominate possession and engage in high defensive actions more valuable than ever.

    The timing of this analysis—published March 31, 2026—places it at a crucial juncture in the season when managers are making final transfers and wildcard plays. Understanding which defenders combine defensive solidity with attacking potential becomes especially valuable during this period, as fixtures become more selective and premium assets compete for budget allocation.

    The broader trend of rising clean sheet rates suggests that goalkeepers and defenders are becoming more valuable relative to previous seasons, where high-scoring games favored forward-heavy strategies. Managers who adapt to this reality by investing in the right defensive assets stand to gain clean sheet points while potentially capturing attacking returns from full-backs and wing-backs in strong systems.

    What's Next

    The final stretch of the 2025/26 season will test whether defensive teams like Brighton can maintain their standards while others regress toward the mean. Managers should monitor xGC trends closely to identify sides whose defensive performance may be unsustainable—such as Bournemouth—and those whose underlying numbers suggest improvement ahead, like West Ham.

    Fantasy Football Scout's analysis indicates that the combination of defensive solidity, clean sheet potential, and attacking returns makes players from teams like Arsenal and Brighton particularly attractive for the run-in. The publication notes that detailed breakdown statistics for the last six matches are available to members, suggesting deeper analysis exists for managers willing to invest in premium FPL tools.

    For casual FPL managers working with the publicly available data, the message is clear: don't overlook defenders this season, especially those from the tightest backlines. In a season where clean sheets are more plentiful and DefCon points add extra value, the right defensive picks could make the difference between a successful season and a missed wildcard.
