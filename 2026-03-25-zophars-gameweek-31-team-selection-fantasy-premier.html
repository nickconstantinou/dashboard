Zophar's Gameweek 31 Team Selection | Fantasy Premier League Tips 2025/26

Introduction

For Fantasy Premier League managers navigating the final stretch of the 2025/26 season, Gameweek 31 represents a critical juncture. This video, recorded by popular FPL content creator Zofa (known as Zophar), provides his detailed team selection strategy for this pivotal gameweek—the last one before the international break and the three-week gap that will reshape the FPL landscape. With the European competitions now concluded for most English clubs, the fixture schedule has become clearer, and managers must decide whether to invest in players with double gameweeks approaching or hold steady for the run-in.

Zofa opens by celebrating his best performance of the season in Gameweek 30, scoring 68 points and achieving a gameweek rank of 209,000—a significant improvement that pushed his overall ranking up to 343,000 from approximately 500,000. His success came primarily from Anderson, who was subbed on for Muno and provided the crucial green arrow that lifted him nearly 60,000 places. With two free transfers available and approximately £2.3 million in the bank, Zofa has already executed one move—selling Rice for Wilson—and now evaluates his options for the remaining gameweeks before his planned Free Hit in 33 and Wildcard in 35.

European Results and Their FPL Implications

The European competitions concluded this week with significant ramifications for FPL planning. Liverpool and Arsenal successfully advanced in the Champions League, while Manchester City, Chelsea, Newcastle, and Tottenham were all eliminated from various competitions. Tottenham, notably, were the only English team to win their second leg apart from Liverpool and Arsenal, with the crowd rallying behind Tuda in what Zofa describes as an encouraging performance featuring a more attacking system that may continue going forward.

Notably, Sanki was left out of the Tottenham squad due to injury, while Richison served a suspension. Moani and Matate Stell performed decently, though Zofa doubts either will start regularly once Richison returns to the lineup. In the Europa League, Aston Villa progressed comfortably by beating Leil 2-0 at home, while Nottingham Forest fielded a heavily rotated side despite being 1-0 down from the first leg and ultimately progressed on penalties.

This rotation proved strategically important for FPL purposes. Players like Neco Williams, Murio, Gibbs White, AA Anderson, and Sari were all brought on in the second half—Sari around the 90th minute—meaning they accumulated minimal minutes. Even Milenovo played only 60 minutes. Zofa believes this careful management means Forest players should face no fatigue concerns heading into their fixture against Spurs on Sunday, and he doesn't anticipate many of these rotated players starting in that match. Only Belinkovic seems likely to retain his place.

Fixture Landscape and Gameweek 33 Planning

The Crystal Palace result carries particular weight for FPL managers because their progression narrows the blanks and doubles landscape. Had Palace been eliminated, their fixture could have been moved to April 7th, becoming a Gameweek 32 fixture, but now that scenario cannot occur. The games expected to drop into the double gameweek 33 primarily occupy the midweek slot that was previously blank, though complications remain for Manchester City, Chelsea, and Newcastle.

These three clubs' fixtures could move to the midweek slot instead of dropping into 33, meaning they might avoid both the blank this week and the double in 33. This uncertainty requires monitoring, particularly as the Premier League will announce the full schedule for the run-in after the FA Cup round around April 7th.

According to the Solio analysis cited by Zofa, the most likely scenario remains doubles for Brighton, Chelsea, Arsenal, Newcastle, Burnley, and Manchester City in Gameweek 33, with blanks for those teams in 34. The Palace versus City fixture would then drop into 36. This accounts for approximately 52.6% of permutations, making it the solid foundation for planning while remaining adaptable to changes.

Zofa's Strategic Approach and Team Targets

Zofa has clearly defined his overall strategy: using a Free Hit in Gameweek 33 for the double, followed by a Wildcard in Gameweek 35. He explains that his team is already decent in 32 and 34, making the Free Hit the optimal play rather than using it now or in 34. This approach frees him to focus on teams with strong fixtures in the immediate gameweeks—specifically Tottenham, Forest, Sunderland, Wolves, Fulham, and Liverpool.

However, Zofa immediately dismisses Wolves, Fulham, Liverpool, and Villa from serious consideration. Liverpool's situation has deteriorated following the Champions League draw, which pits them against Galatasaray in a challenging sequence. They play Wednesday, then Saturday against Fulham, then Tuesday again against Galatasaray, with the second leg at home. This means they face Manchester City, PSG, Fulham, and PSG within roughly 10 days—a schedule that makes rotation against Fulham inevitable, making them an unattractive option despite their strong form.

For West Ham, Zofa applies a qualifier, marking them with a star because he expects their fixture to remain in 34 rather than moving to 33. This expectation stems from West Ham having the Monday night game in 33, which Sky likely won't want to move given TV scheduling preferences. There remains a small possibility West Ham could have the double in 33 and blank in 34, but Zofa considers this unlikely and notes that the entry point this week isn't favorable anyway.

Arsenal becomes straightforward: since they don't have the free midweek slot in 34, their fixture must stay in 33, making them reliable for the double. Villa's poor form makes their players unappealing regardless of fixtures, while Manchester United presents significant interest given their strong gameweeks 32 and 34—Sunderland, Newcastle, Tottenham, Forest, and two home games make their assets worth considering.

Specific Transfer Targets and Execution

Zofa's completed transfer—Rice to Wilson—frees up resources while eliminating an Arsenal asset he no longer wants in his squad. He now has two free transfers available but intends to use only one, keeping one in reserve. This caution stems from the upcoming international break, during which many strong players and teams will feature in various national team commitments. With Champions League matches and other competitions continuing, the risk of injuries during this period makes preserving transfers prudent.

His primary transfer targets remain Tottenham, Fulham, and Manchester United players, with West Ham as a potential wait-and-see option. The Tottenham assets appeal because of their fixtures, as do Fulham's and Manchester United's. However, Zofa emphasizes that he wants clarity on West Ham's fixture before committing, preferring to wait until gameweek 32 when the situation becomes clearer.

Key Takeaways

The European rotation situation matters significantly for FPL. Nottingham Forest's heavily rotated lineup against Frankfurt, with key players receiving minimal minutes, means they're likely to start fresh against Spurs. This contrasts sharply with Liverpool's fixture congestion, where rotation is inevitable against Fulham despite their strong form. Managers should factor in not just who plays but how much they play in the days leading up to the deadline.

Gameweek 33 remains the focal point for strategic planning. The most likely scenario—doubles for Brighton, Chelsea, Arsenal, Newcastle, Burnley, and Manchester City with blanks in 34—provides a foundation, though managers must remain flexible. Zofa's Free Hit in 33 strategy acknowledges this while preserving options for gameweeks 32 and 34 through his Wildcard in 35.

Not all strong-looking fixtures are worth pursuing. Liverpool's attractive-looking home fixture against Fulham is undermined by the broader fixture pile and likely rotation. Similarly, West Ham's fixtures appear good on paper, but the uncertainty around whether they blank in 34 or 33 makes immediate entry premature.

Preserving transfers during international breaks makes sense. With playoffs for various countries occurring during the break and Champions League matches continuing, the injury risk increases. Zofa's decision to use only one of his two available transfers reflects prudent risk management rather than aggressive optimization.

Performance in gameweek 30 demonstrates the value of patience. Zofa's 68 points and 209K rank—his best of the season—came from making no transfers and simply captaining Bruno. This reinforces that sometimes the best move is no move, particularly when your team already has favorable fixtures.
