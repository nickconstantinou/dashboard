Best Cheap Players for Gameweek 36 Bench Boost

Introduction

Gameweek 36 presents an opportunity for Fantasy Premier League managers to deploy a Bench Boost chip. Selecting the right budget players can maximise points from substitutes while keeping costs low for star performers elsewhere. This analysis examines verified defensive options and fixture-based insights to inform those decisions.

Verified Cheap Defenders

Michael Keane (Everton – £4.5m)

Michael Keane represents a cost-effective defensive option at £4.5m. The Everton centre-back is averaging 10.65 defensive contributions per 90 minutes, a figure that outdoes his defensive partner James Tarkowski who is priced at £5.7m. This output suggests solid per-90 metrics that may translate to consistent points in favourable fixture scenarios.

Kevin Danso (Tottenham Hotspur – £4.2m)

Kevin Danso offers the cheapest route into Tottenham's defence at £4.2m. The Austrian defender has delivered contributions in five of his last six starts, indicating consistent involvement in attacking moves despite his defensive positioning. This recent form makes him a viable budget option for managers looking to field four cheap substitutes.

Tottenham Tactical Context

Since Ange Postecoglou's departure and the appointment of Des Zeroi, Tottenham have shown defensive improvement. Spurs have the league's lowest expected goals conceded (xGC) tally at 3.17 since the managerial change. This statistical context supports Danso's potential viability as a Bench Boost enabler, particularly if this defensive solidity continues.

Fixture-Based Considerations

Brighton's Defensive Record

Brighton and Hove Albion have shipped only 42 goals this season, with only the top two teams conceding fewer. The Seagulls also boast a joint-league-best four clean sheets in the last nine Gameweeks. This clean sheet record makes Brighton defenders attractive for managers seeking defensive points, though no specific Brighton players appear in the verified claims.

Wolves' Attacking Limitations

Wolverhampton Wanderers are the division's lowest scorers with 25 goals, by quite some distance. This attacking impotence presents clean sheet potential for defenders facing Wolves in Gameweek 36, though specific cheap players against Wolves are not detailed in the available claims.

Crystal Palace's Scoring Drought

Crystal Palace have failed to find the net in three of their last five Premier League matches. This attacking struggle suggests defensive options facing Palace could offer clean sheet potential, though again specific players are not identified in the claims.

Player-Specific Limitations

Ferdi Kadioglu (£4.5m – Brighton)

While Kadioglu is on an unbroken 31-match starting run in the league, his offensive output is limited. He has managed just three attacking returns all season and has met the DefCon threshold just twice. Managers should note that his selection for Bench Boost purposes carries significant risk regarding attacking returns, despite his consistent starting place.

Goalkeeper Contingency

Antonin Kinsky (£3.9m – Tottenham)

Kinsky's inclusion at £3.9m is contingent on first-choice goalkeeper Vicario (£4.7m) being ruled out. Any Bench Boost strategy involving Kinsky must account for this injury uncertainty, as his selection becomes viable only upon confirmed absence of the primary goalkeeper.
