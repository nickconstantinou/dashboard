Introduction

The Fantasy Premier League landscape has just become significantly clearer, and for managers, the implications are substantial. With the confirmation that Arsenal, Wolves, Manchester City, and Crystal Palace will all blank in Gameweek 31, the puzzle pieces are finally falling into place for the critical run-in period. FP Harry, one of the community's most respected FPL analysts, has released his comprehensive guide breaking down exactly what this means for your squad strategy, chip deployment, and transfer planning over the coming weeks.

The timing could not be more crucial. With the deadline less than 24 hours away and the international break immediately following Gameweek 31, managers are facing some of the most important decisions of the season. The key insight from this analysis is that patience and flexibility will be far more valuable than rushing to make transfers based on incomplete information. The next two weeks will reveal much more about the fixture landscape than we currently know, and acting too early could pigeon-hole you into a strategy that becomes suboptimal once the FA Cup results clarify the double and blank picture.

The Blank Confirmation and What It Means

The European results from Thursday evening provided the final piece of the puzzle that FPL managers had been waiting for. Crystal Palace's advancement in European competition confirmed what many had suspected: Manchester City and Crystal Palace will not play their postponed fixture in either Gameweek 31 or 32. This means that both clubs officially blank in Gameweek 31, and that fixture will not be played until at least the midweek of Gameweek 33 at the earliest.

This is significant because it removes the possibility that many managers had hoped for—that this game could be played earlier if both teams were knocked out of Europe. Since Manchester City lost their European tie but Crystal Palace progressed, the game cannot be rearranged into an earlier midweek slot. Gameweek 32 will now be a normal gameweek with all teams in action, while Gameweek 33 remains on course to be the biggest double gameweek of the season.

The predicted double fixtures, according to data from FPL.team and Planet FPL, show teams like Arsenal and Chelsea doubling in Gameweek 33, with Manchester City and Crystal Palace also predicted to double in that same gameweek. However, FP Harry emphasizes that this remains educated guessing until the FA Cup results are confirmed and the official announcements come before the Gameweek 32 deadline.

Chip Strategy: The Two Paths Forward

For managers still holding their wildcard and free hit, the strategic landscape splits into two primary pathways depending on one critical variable: the outcome of the Manchester City versus Liverpool FA Cup quarter-final.

Option One involves wildcarding in Gameweek 32, deploying the bench boost in Gameweek 33, and saving the free hit for Gameweek 34. This path becomes more likely if Liverpool defeat Manchester City in the FA Cup, as it would create a more favorable double and blank distribution that aligns with this sequence of chip usage.

Option Two flips the approach: using the free hit in Gameweek 33, wildcards in Gameweek 35, and bench boost in Gameweek 36. This scenario becomes more probable if Manchester City win the FA Cup tie, as it would create a different fixture rhythm that makes this chip ordering more efficient.

FP Harry acknowledges that this is not an exact science. Even with one outcome or the other, managers may still choose to deviate based on their specific squad composition and preferences. The FA Cup result is simply the major deciding factor that tips the balance toward one strategy or the other, but it is not the only consideration.

The Critical Warning: Don't Transfer Early

Perhaps the most important message in this entire analysis is the urgent recommendation to avoid making early transfers going into Gameweek 32. FP Harry explicitly states: "Please please please please do not make any early wildcard 32 transfers until after the FA Cup."

The reasoning is straightforward: with the international break immediately after Gameweek 31, followed by the FA Cup fixtures, and then a week later Gameweek 32, managers will have a two-week window where the double and blank picture becomes significantly clearer. The midweek between the FA Cup and Gameweek 32 is when official announcements about doubles and blanks will start coming through.

The cost of acting early is minimal compared to the potential cost of being locked into the wrong strategy. FP Harry notes that you might gain 0.2, 0.3, or even 0.5 in value by transferring early, but it is simply not worth it at this point in the season. What matters far more is being able to pivot once the fixture landscape becomes clearer.

Flexibility: The Key Strategic Principle

Rather than forcing transfers this week to chase short-term gains, FP Harry recommends prioritizing long-term flexibility. This means avoiding the temptation to immediately buy Newcastle players—even though they have excellent fixtures—even if they seem obvious picks.

The reasoning here is nuanced. Newcastle have a good fixture this week (Sunderland at home), followed by Crystal Palace away in Gameweek 32, and then a double in Gameweek 33 which would be covered by a free hit. However, if you end up on Option Two of the chip strategy (Free Hit 33, Wildcard 35, Bench Boost 36), Newcastle players become considerably less valuable because they blank in Gameweek 34. They do not have a fixture that week, making them poor investments unless you are definitely on Strategy One.

By contrast, FP Harry explains that his own approach involves not forcing himself to buy Newcastle players this week because they do not fit that well unless you are committed to Strategy One. Instead, he recommends buying players who are good for the long term regardless of which chip strategy you eventually adopt.

Captaincy Analysis: Bruno Fernandez Leads the Way

For Gameweek 31 specifically, FP Harry identifies Bruno Fernandez as his number one captaincy choice. The Manchester United playmaker is projected to be the most captained player this week, with approximately 75% of managers considering him for the armband.

Several factors support this recommendation. Bruno is the highest chance of a goal or assist according to the predictive models, he is the most in-form player, and he is the joint highest projected point scorer in the Fantasy Scout members area. The safety and reliability he provides makes him the default choice for most managers.

However, FP Harry acknowledges that there is a valid case for going against Bruno, primarily due to his away form being notably worse than his home form. His assist threat is definitely better at Old Trafford, and Bournemouth have been one of the best home defenses this season. For managers looking for a differential, Harry Wilson emerges as the best alternative. Burnley have conceded significant chances to right wingers throughout the season, making Wilson an attractive differential option.

Anthony Gordon is another consideration, particularly since he is playing up front in a big derby, is on penalties, and has a home fixture against Sunderland. Cole Palmer remains an option away at Everton despite Chelsea's recent poor performances, while Darwin Nunez should start for Liverpool as he is their main goal threat and the team cannot play well without him.

Team Analysis and Projected Scoring

One notable observation from this gameweek is that every team in Gameweek 31 is predicted to score less than two goals. Fulham sit at the top of the projections at home to Burnley with 1.83 expected goals, Manchester United are just below them at 1.72, and Newcastle are in and around 1.76. Even the elite teams like Chelsea and Liverpool are in the same range.

This relatively flat scoring expectation means there is not much to separate the captaincy options this week. The clean sheet projections are similarly modest, with only Fulham and perhaps Newcastle offering reasonable clean sheet possibilities.

Injury Updates and Bench Decisions

The video was recorded on a Friday morning, meaning press conferences had not yet taken place. The only confirmed injury news at that point was that Chelsea's Nicolas Jackson is likely to be out for six weeks.

FP Harry mentioned that he would be discussing injury updates for players like Takehiro Tomiyasu (who is in his team) during the deadline stream, which happens on Friday evening. He also noted that most managers are struggling to put out a full starting XI this week due to the blank, making benching decisions less critical than usual. However, he committed to covering specific benching decisions like Alderete versus Tomiyasu in the defense if needed during the stream.

Key Takeaways for FPL Managers

First, patience is paramount. Do not make transfers based on current predictions. Wait until after the FA Cup results confirm the double and blank picture before committing to any chip strategy. The potential gain of 0.2-0.5 in value is far outweighed by the risk of being locked into the wrong approach.

Second, prioritize flexibility over immediate gains. Avoid players who only fit one specific strategy. Newcastle players are excellent but only truly valuable if you commit to Strategy One. Players who work regardless of which path you take will serve you better in the long run.

Third, the FA Cup is the pivotal moment. The Manchester City versus Liverpool result will largely determine which chip strategy makes more sense. Once that is known, the path forward becomes much clearer.

Fourth, Bruno Fernandez is the default captain. While his away form is weaker than his home form, the safety and reliability he provides makes him the top choice for most managers. Harry Wilson offers the best alternative as a differential.

Fifth, Gameweek 33 is the destination. Regardless of which chip strategy you adopt, Gameweek 33 is likely to be the biggest double gameweek of the season. Your planning should work backward from that point.
