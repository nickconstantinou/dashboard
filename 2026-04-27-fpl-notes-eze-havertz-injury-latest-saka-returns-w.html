Saka Makes Comeback

Bukayo Saka (£9.8m) made his return to action, coming off the bench as a substitute for Arsenal. The England international had been absent from recent matchday squads, and his reappearance provides a boost for FPL managers who have held onto him through his injury layoff.

Calafiori Remains Unavailable

Despite manager Mikel Arteta suggesting on Friday that Riccardo Calafiori (£5.6m) would probably be back, the defender failed to appear in the matchday squad. Arsenal managers should continue to monitor the situation, as the Italian defender remains unavailable for selection.

Arsenal's Set-Piece Threat

The match-winning goal came from a set-piece situation, marking Arsenal's 22nd set-piece goal of the 2025/26 season. The Gunners demonstrated tactical variety early in the game, going short with their first three corners. However, Arsenal created limited attacking chances, managing only three first-half shots in what proved to be another sub-1.0 xG performance.

Newcastle Team News

Nick Pope (£5.0m) returned to action between the posts for Newcastle. Lewis Hall (£5.4m) was omitted from the starting lineup, with Eddie Howe explaining the selection decision: "I felt today for a number of reasons that Dan was the right pick in terms of height and physicality and dealing with Arsenal's threats." Martin Zubimendi (£5.0m) was forced off later in the game due to sickness. Howe made five changes to the Newcastle side for this fixture.

Injury Concerns for Wednesday

Eberechi Eze and Kai Havertz both picked up muscular niggles during the match. Speaking about the injuries, a source indicated: "There are muscular niggles. We don't think it's too much. We have to wait and see if they're going to be available for Wednesday." Both players' fitness will be monitored ahead of the midweek fixtures.
