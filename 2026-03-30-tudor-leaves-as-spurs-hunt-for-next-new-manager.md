# Igor Tudor Departs Tottenham After Just 44 Days as Club Searches for Third Manager of the Season

## A Tumultuous Six Weeks Comes to an Abrupt End

Tottenham Hotspur and Igor Tudor have mutually agreed to part ways, the club announced on March 29, 2026, bringing an end to one of the shortest managerial tenures in recent Premier League history. The Croatian coach, who was appointed to replace Thomas Frank in February, lasted only 44 days in north London, overseeing a mere seven matches across all competitions. Joining Tudor out the door are goalkeeping coach Tomislav Rogic and physical coach Riccardo Ragnacci, who also departed their respective roles with immediate effect. The club's official statement thanked all three for their "tireless" efforts during the past six weeks while acknowledging Tudor's recent bereavement, sending "support to him and his family at this difficult time."

The departure marks an extraordinary chapter in Tottenham's already chaotic 2025-26 season. Tudor becomes the club's third permanent manager in a single campaign, following Frank's dismissal in February. The Dane had himself replaced Ange Postecoglou earlier in the season, creating a revolving door at the managerial position that has left players, supporters, and staff in a state of constant upheaval. Under Tudor's brief stewardship, results were catastrophic: just one win in seven matches, with that solitary victory coming against lower-league opposition in the FA Cup before their eventual elimination. In the Premier League, the 49-year-old managed only a single victory in five matches, conceding a alarming 13 goals in the process.

## Why This Managerial Crisis Matters Far Beyond White Hart Lane

The dysfunction at Tottenham Hotspur represents something larger than a single club's troubles—it signals the increasingly precarious nature of top-flight football management in the modern era. The expectation that managers can implement tactical systems, build squad cohesion, and deliver results within mere weeks is creating an environment where even experienced coaches struggle to establish themselves. For Tudor, the Croatian's struggles included failing to settle on a consistent formation, repeatedly switching between different tactical setups during his brief tenure. His first match—a 4-1 humiliation in the north London derby against Arsenal—set the tone for what would become a deeply troubled spell.

Beyond the managerial instability, the chaos has real consequences for Fantasy Premier League managers and those with Tottenham assets. The constant change in tactical approach, training regimes, and team selection creates a deeply unpredictable Fantasy proposition. Bruno Saltor, a familiar face at the club, has assumed temporary control of first-team training at Hotspur Way, but his role is expected to be purely caretaker in nature. The club has stated that an announcement regarding a new Head Coach will be made "in due course," though media reports suggest an appointment is imminent, with the new manager expected to be in place before Gameweek 32 to allow maximum preparation time with returning international players.

## Context: A Season Defined by Instability

Tottenham's managerial carousel began when Ange Postecoglou was dismissed early in the campaign, with Thomas Frank brought in from Brentford as his replacement. The Danish coach's tenure lasted only a few months before his own dismissal in February, paving the way for Tudor's appointment. The rapid succession of changes has destabilized an already underperforming squad, with players reporting to training under different voices, philosophies, and expectations multiple times within a single season. The timing is particularly problematic, coming at a crucial stage of the campaign with European qualification hopes fading and fan patience wearing thin.

The fixture run-in presents both challenges and potential opportunities for whoever takes the helm. Tottenham face Sunderland next, and while the Black Cats have generally been excellent at home this season, they enter the match having lost their last three consecutive matches at the Stadium of Light. The international break may have disrupted Brighton's recent surge—the Seagulls have won four of their last five league matches—but the break has also allowed Spurs' international contingent to return to the capital in midweek, giving a new manager precious time to work with his squad. The fixture list offers a mixed bag: only three of Tottenham's remaining games are at home, which may actually suit them given their catastrophic home record this season (ranked 20th in the league for home results). They sit 9th for away form, suggesting a potential tactical approach centered on defensive solidity on the road. There are no Blank or Double Gameweeks expected in the remaining schedule, meaning continuity will be paramount.

The coming days will be critical for Tottenham Hotspur as they look to end their managerial search and provide stability for the final stretch of the season.
