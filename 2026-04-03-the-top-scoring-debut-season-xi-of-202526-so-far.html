The Top-Scoring Debut Season XI of 2025/26 So Far: Fantasy Premier League's Brightest Newcomers

The Full Story

The 2025/26 Fantasy Premier League season has witnessed an remarkable influx of talent from the Championship and abroad, with 14 players already surpassing the 100-point milestone in their debut campaigns as of early April 2026. With seven Gameweeks remaining in the season, Fantasy Football Scout has identified the standout performers among FPL's newest participants, highlighting those who have exceeded expectations while noting some high-profile disappointments including Xavi Simons (£6.5m), Randal Kolo Muani (£6.9m), and Maxim De Cuyper (£4.3m).

Sunderland has emerged as the dominant club in this debutant XI, providing four players across the lineup. Goalkeeper Robin Roefs (£4.8m), acquired from NEC Nijmegen for an initial £9 million, has been transformative for the Black Cats, recording 110 points despite currently nursing a hamstring injury. His impact was immediate—he kept a clean sheet on opening day and produced a crucial penalty save against Kevin Schade (£7.0m) in Gameweek 3, amassing 39 points by the end of Gameweek 6. Defenders Daniel Ballard (£4.7m) and Nordi Mukiele (£4.5m) have been equally influential, with Mukiele particularly remarkable given his £4.0m starting price tag. The former Paris Saint-Germain defender, who didn't debut until Gameweek 3, contributed one goal and five assists, including a 17-point haul against Wolverhampton Wanderers and a nine-match stretch where only one game yielded fewer than four points.

The defensive line is completed by Bournemouth's Adrien Truffert (£4.7m) with 122 points—his position as the top-scoring defender in this debut XI—and Newcastle United's Malick Thiaw (£5.1m) with 114 points. Truffert, brought in as a Milos Kerkez (£5.6m) replacement, has been instrumental in Bournemouth's home defensive record, which stands as the best in the league for big chances conceded at home (17). Thiaw, meanwhile, leads all defenders with nine big chances created and delivered two goals at Everton in Gameweek 13 before surpassing that performance with a 17-pointer against Crystal Palace. The midfield quartet features Anton Stach (£4.8m) from Hoffenheim with 122 points, Martin Zubimendi (117 points), Enzo Le Fee (108 points), and Florian Wirtz (105 points), though details on the latter three midfielders were not fully available in the source material.

Why This Matters

This analysis carries significant weight for the millions of FPL managers who invest considerable time and resources into identifying undervalued assets before the market catches on. The debutants highlighted in this piece represent potential treasure troves of value, as players transitioning from the Championship or foreign leagues often start at lower price points before their performances drive price increases. Understanding which first-year players have genuine FPL viability versus those who will struggle—like the aforementioned Simons, Kolo Muani, and De Cuyper—can mean the difference between a mediocre season and a championship-winning campaign.

For Sunderland specifically, this data underscores a remarkable defensive phenomenon: despite conceding the third-most shots in the league (455), they have shipped the fifth-fewest goals (36), with only Everton exceeding their expected goals conceded (xGC) differential at -9.21. This suggests that Roefs and the Sunderland defense are performing significantly above what statistical models would predict, making them potentially unsustainable assets—or alternatively, indicating genuine quality that the underlying numbers haven't fully captured. For FPL managers, deciding whether to trust the sustainable excellence of Ballard (who ranks near the top among defenders for shots in the box with 24 and headed attempts with 19) or cash in on inflated prices represents a crucial strategic fork in the road with approximately seven weeks remaining in the season.

    • Context and Background

    The 2025/26 season marks the second half of Fantasy Premier League's campaign, with the April 2, 2026 publication date indicating we're deep into the business end of the season. FPL operates on a system where players accumulate points through goals, assists, clean sheets, saves, and various other contributions, with prices fluctuating based on ownership percentages and transfer activity. Debutants represent a particularly volatile category because they lack price history from previous Premier League seasons, meaning their initial valuations are based on limited data.

    Sunderland's promotion to the Premier League via last season's play-off final appears to have been a watershed moment for the club, with their continued overperformance in the top flight suggesting either exceptional tactical discipline under their manager or the emergence of genuinely top-tier defensive talent in Roefs and Ballard. The £9 million valuation for Roefs, while significant for a goalkeeper, already appears to represent value given his 110-point haul. Similarly, Mukiele's transition from PSG—where he was clearly behind more established options—to a starting role at Sunderland has proven mutually beneficial, with the French defender delivering FPL returns that justify his early-season bargain price.

    Bournemouth's acquisition of Truffert as a Kerkez replacement also reflects the dynamic nature of Premier League squad-building, where departures create opportunities for new arrivals to establish themselves as FPL assets. Truffert's value lies not in explosive attacking returns but in consistent point-scoring through clean sheets and defensive contributions, making him a reliable budget option at £4.7m.
