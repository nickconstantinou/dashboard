FPL Gameweek 32 Roundup: Navigating Blanks, Injuries, and the Final Sprint

The Blank Gameweek Picture

The FPL landscape entering Gameweek 32 presents a clear and immediate challenge for managers: Arsenal, Crystal Palace, Manchester City, and Wolverhampton Wanderers all blank this week, ruling out a significant portion of the most popular assets in the game. With no confirmed double gameweeks stretching through the remainder of the season, managers must pivot away from heavy investments in those four clubs and focus their attention on the 16 teams that are in action.

This blank situation creates both opportunity and risk. Managers with large Arsenal stakes—particularly those captaining Gabriel dos Santos Magalhães, who leads the form charts with a remarkable 9.7 rating—face a frustrating week where their premium picks sit idle. Gabriel has accumulated 173 points this season at a price of £7.2m, but his 75% chance of playing status following a knee injury adds another layer of complexity. Managers must decide whether to accept the blank or cut their losses and pivot to in-form alternatives. The injury situation at Arsenal extends beyond Gabriel, with William Saliba (£6.1m) and Jurriën Timber (£6.3m) both carrying ankle injuries at 75% availability, leaving the Gunners' defensive assets in a precarious position heading into the blank.

Captain Strategy: Bruno Fernandes Dominates the Conversation

When it comes to captaincy for Gameweek 32, the data presents an overwhelming favourite. Bruno Borges Fernandes of Manchester United sits at the summit of the form table with an extraordinary 11.0 rating over his last four appearances, accumulating 189 total points this season. At £10.3m, he is owned by 44.4% of all FPL managers—a substantial minority, but nowhere near the concentration seen in some other premium positions. His Manchester United side face a fixture that makes him the default armband choice for anyone without a blank, and his recent trajectory suggests the returns will continue.

Among the differentials, Anthony Gordon (£7.4m) of Newcastle United emerges as a compelling option. Gordon carries a 7.5 form rating and has been accumulating points steadily, with 98 total to his name this season. His 7.4% ownership means that captaining him offers genuine rank-boosting potential if Newcastle deliver against their opponents. Morgan Gibbs-White (£7.4m) of Nottingham Forest continues to churn out attacking returns, with his 7.0 form and 133 season points making him a reliable alternative for managers seeking exposure to a Forest side that appears to be hitting its stride under Vítor Pereira.

João Pedro Junqueira de Jesus of Chelsea presents the most popular route, owned by a staggering 50.4% of the game at £7.8m. His 6.2 form is solid without being spectacular, and the 164 points he has accumulated this season reflect consistent involvement in a Chelsea attack that has shown promise. However, managers should note that with over half of all FPL teams likely captaining him or his teammates, the ceiling for rank movement from Chelsea exposure this week is somewhat limited.

Transfer Strategy: Value in Unexpected Places

With no double gameweeks confirmed across the remaining fixture schedule, the transfer market should focus on players who can deliver in single weeks while maintaining value for the run-in. João Victor Gomes da Silva of Wolves represents perhaps the most intriguing differential available, priced at just £5.3m with an 8.0 form rating that places him third in the entire league. His 0.4% ownership makes him a genuine lottery ticket for managers willing to take a chance on a Wolves player despite their blank in Gameweek 32. The long-term play here is clear: Wolves return to action in Gameweek 33, and Gomes da Silva's form suggests he can immediately contribute.

Danny Welbeck of Brighton offers another interesting proposition at £6.2m. His 6.0 form and 106 season points represent decent value, while Brighton's fixture run through the coming weeks appears favourable. Managers looking to replace expensive blanking assets might consider Welbeck as a temporary holding option who can deliver points without breaking the budget.

For those looking to upgrade their midfield, Hugo Ekitiké of Liverpool at £9.3m has attracted 35.5% ownership despite a relatively modest 4.8 form rating. The French forward has 125 points this season and plays for a Liverpool side that, under Arne Slot, continues to create chances consistently. His price makes him accessible compared to the premium forwards, and his underlying statistics suggest positive regression could be coming.

In defence, James Tarkowski (£5.7m) of Everton continues to fly under the radar despite 142 season points and a 6.0 form rating. Everton under David Moyes have been notably more solid at the back in recent weeks, and Tarkowski's involvement in set-piece situations gives him additional appeal beyond clean sheet points.

Players to Avoid

The injury situation at Arsenal demands caution across the board. Gabriel (£7.2m) is clearly talented and in exceptional form, but managers must respect the 75% availability and the fact that he blanks this week regardless. Carrying him as a passenger while hoping for his return in Gameweek 33 is a strategy that requires careful squad construction to support.

Similarly, Iliman Ndiaye (£6.2m) of Everton carries a foot injury at 75% chance of playing. While his 5.5 form is reasonable, the uncertainty makes him a risky pick this week, particularly when there are so many healthy alternatives available at similar price points.

For those still invested in Wolves players ahead of their blank, this is clearly not the week to panic-sell unless urgently needed. Wolves have fixtures beyond Gameweek 32, and players like André Trindade da Costa Neto (£5.2m) and Rodrigo Martins Gomes (£4.4m) have shown enough quality to warrant holding if the squad space exists.

Chip Strategy: Timing Everything

The confirmed fixture data shows 20 fixtures across each of Gameweeks 32 through 36, with no confirmed blanks or doubles in the system. The absence of double gameweeks significantly reduces the urgency of chip deployment, as there are no immediate opportunities to exploit elevated fixture schedules.

However, the FFS creator community has discussed strategies involving Gameweeks 33 and 34, with mentions of "wildcard" and "free hit" timing around those gameweeks. Managers should treat these as speculative predictions rather than confirmed information. Until the FPL API confirms double gameweeks, the best approach is to hold wildcard and free hit chips for genuine opportunities rather than forcing deployment based on predictions that may not materialise.

For those with bench boost still available, the uniform fixture density across the remaining gameweeks means there is no obvious target at present. Managers should continue monitoring for any fixture announcements that might create advantageous bench boost opportunities.

Where the Debate Lies

The primary tension in the community this week centres on Arsenal assets. Managers face a difficult choice between holding injured and blanking players for potential returns in Gameweek 33 and beyond, versus cutting ties and pivoting to players who can contribute immediately. Those who invested heavily in the Arsenal defence—particularly Gabriel and Saliba—are particularly squeezed, as their premium prices lock up significant funds that could be deployed elsewhere.

The secondary debate concerns captaincy beyond Bruno Fernandes. While his form is exceptional, some managers argue that backing a 44.4% owned player as captain provides no relative rank advantage. Gordon and Gibbs-White represent the main counter-arguments, with their lower ownership potentially yielding more significant rank swings if they deliver.

Three Key Actions for the Week

First, if you have Arsenal players carrying injuries—particularly Gabriel, Saliba, or Timber—you must make a decision before the Gameweek 32 deadline. Holding injured players through a blank is a double penalty that should only be tolerated if you have exceptional squad depth or are unconcerned with short-term rank movement. For most managers, this is the moment to assess whether the 75% availability chance justifies the risk.

Second, ensure your captain is Bruno Fernandes if you are without a blank. His 11.0 form and 189 points represent the strongest expected return available. If you are captaining anyone else without a compelling ownership-based argument, you are deliberately disadvantaging your rank.

Third, consider Gomes da Silva (£5.3m) as a transfer target for Gameweek 33. His 8.0 form is extraordinary for his price point, and his 0.4% ownership makes him a genuine differential that could define mini-league battles. Managers who identify him now and move early will have an advantage over those who react after seeing his price rise.
