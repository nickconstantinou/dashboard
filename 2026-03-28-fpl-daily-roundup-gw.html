FPL Gameweek Roundup: Navigating the Run-In with No Doubles and Mounting Injury Concerns

Today's Key FPL News & Injuries

The FPL landscape heading into the final stretch of the season has been thrown into sharp relief by a cluster of injury doubts that could reshape thousands of squads before the weekend. Most significantly, Arsenal's defensive spine is creaking at the worst possible time for the 43.1% of managers who own Gabriel dos Santos Magalhães at £7.2m. The Brazilian centre-back is carrying a knee injury but has been given a 75% chance of playing, according to the latest data — a borderline call that will have managers frantically refreshing their screens. He joins fellow Arsenal defenders Jurriën Timber (£6.3m) and William Saliba (£6.1m) on the same 75% availability threshold, both dealing with ankle issues. Arsenal blank in Gameweek 31 alongside Crystal Palace, Manchester City, and Wolves, making those three Gunners a moot point this week — but their availability for the fixtures beyond matters enormously given their standing in the form table.

Arsenal manager Mikel Arteta's side still dominates the top-flight conversation despite the injury cloud, with Gabriel sitting second in the entire form rankings on 9.7 and Timber fifth on 7.3. A triple Arsenal defence was a cornerstone of countless title-chasing squads not long ago, and the 75% figure for all three represents genuinely concerning uncertainty. Beyond North London, the xG tables from Fantasy Football Scout continue to provide context for which attackers are overperforming and which represent genuine underlying value — a critical lens when considering premium investments in the final seven Gameweeks.

A separate concern has emerged around a player identified in FFS reporting as Kroupi, with the forward withdrawn from France under-21 duty adding to the injury uncertainty. Managers are advised to monitor updates closely, as international call-ups that result in mid-season withdrawals have become an increasingly common curveball in the modern FPL calendar.

Gameweek Landscape

The confirmed fixture data paints a remarkably uniform picture across the coming Gameweeks. Gameweeks 32 through 36 each carry 20 confirmed fixtures — a full Premier League programme, meaning no Double Gameweeks are currently confirmed in the range spanning Gameweeks 29 through 38. Gameweek 35 was notably in the news this week after TV fixtures were officially announced, but Fantasy Football Scout was quick to clarify that the announcement brought no provisional Double Gameweek with it. For managers holding Free Hits in reserve, this is a significant development. The absence of confirmed doubles removes one of the primary catalysts that typically drive major squad reshuffles at this stage of the season.

The Gameweek 31 blank removes four sides entirely — Arsenal, Crystal Palace, Manchester City, and Wolves — which means managers with heavy investments in those teams will be looking at a reduced effective squad this weekend. Crystal Palace winger Ismaïla Sarr (£6.3m) and Wolves attackers João Victor Gomes da Silva (£5.3m) and André Trindade da Costa Neto (£5.2m) all sit inside the top 15 by form, making them particularly painful omissions for anyone who stacked their squad with blanks. Manchester City's summer signings Antoine Semenyo (£8.2m, Form 5.7) and Rayan Aït-Nouri (£5.7m, Form 5.3) are also benched considerations this week despite the Cityzens' otherwise promising run-in.

With no doubles on the horizon and a consistent fixture density of 20 games per Gameweek across the board, the strategic focus shifts firmly toward sustained form, fixture difficulty, and the occasional wildcard use rather than reactive chip deployment chasing phantom doubles.

The French forward sits just outside the top 40 by form but carries significant ownership and a proven ceiling under Arne Slot — a player whose price tag of £9.3m becomes more palatable when the underlying chance creation is scrutinised against the remaining fixture list.

Chip Strategy

With no Double Gameweeks confirmed between Gameweeks 29 and 38, the traditional chip strategy playbook has been largely invalidated this cycle. Managers who burned their Free Hit chasing a DGW that never materialised will be rueing the decision, while those who preserved chips face a different question: when, if ever, do the doubles arrive?

Fantasy Football Scout's analysis of boosting rank in the final seven Gameweeks will likely have emphasised that the absence of doubles creates a sustained performance environment rather than a volatile one — form becomes even more critical because there are no swing Gameweeks to reset the board. The Wildcard remains a potent tool for managers with glaring structural issues in their squad, but the urgency has diminished without a DGW on the immediate horizon.

The Bench Boost, meanwhile, has no obvious high-value Gameweek to target given the uniform fixture density. Managers may consider using it opportunistically when they spot a single Gameweek where their bench is particularly strong, rather than holding it for a double that may never come.

With Gabriel, Timber, and Saliba all sitting on that 75% fitness threshold, the community is split between those who hold firm on their Arsenal defenders believing the injury risk is priced in by the 75% availability figure, and those who are actively pivoting to alternatives such as Truffert at Bournemouth or Van Hecke at Brighton. João Pedro (£7.8m) occupies the middle ground, accepted as a premium starter but rarely the preferred captain choice over Bruno when the numbers are scrutinised.
