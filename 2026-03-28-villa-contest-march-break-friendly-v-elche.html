Aston Villa's March Break Friendly Against Elche Ends in Defeat as Jadon Sancho Sustains Shoulder Injury

The Full Story

Aston Villa found themselves in an unusual situation on Friday, March 27, 2026, as they contested a mid-season friendly against Spanish La Liga side Elche at the Pinatar Arena in Spain. The match came during a rare three-week gap between Premier League Gameweeks 31 and 32, prompting Villa manager Unai Emery to organize a friendly to keep his squad's fitness levels sharp. The Villans had been conducting a warm-weather training camp in Murcia over the preceding days, making the friendly against Elche a natural addition to their preparations.

Despite being significantly shorthanded—with 11 senior squad members away on international duty—Emery named a relatively strong starting XI for the encounter. However, only three players who had started Gameweek 31 were in the lineup from kickoff. The match resulted in a 2-1 defeat for Villa, with Pau scoring the hosts' only goal in the 43rd minute, finishing from a Douglas Luiz corner. Elche's stoppage-time headed winner completed the comeback after Villa had initially taken the lead.

The friendly quickly became overshadowed by injury concerns. Jadon Sancho (£5.8m) was forced off before halftime after landing awkwardly on his shoulder. The England international appeared to be in considerable pain and left the field with his arm in a makeshift sling following extended treatment. Additionally, Argentine midfielder Emi Buendía (£5.3m) went down holding his shoulder after the break, though he was able to continue playing.

Why This Matters

For Fantasy Football managers, Sancho's injury represents a significant concern heading into the crucial period of the season. The former Manchester United winger has been a key asset for many managers, and any extended absence could force difficult decisions in squad construction. The timing is particularly problematic given the compressed fixture schedule as clubs approach the business end of the campaign.

Beyond the fantasy implications, the match highlighted the broader logistical challenges clubs face during international breaks. With three weeks between league fixtures, managers must balance maintaining match fitness against the risks of injury in meaningless matches. Villa's experience—despite their ultimate defeat—serves as a cautionary tale for other clubs considering similar arrangements during future breaks.

The match also drew attention to Harvey Elliott's peculiar situation. Despite being fully fit and clearly talented, the young midfielder finds himself on the periphery of Emery's Premier League plans. His standout performance in this friendly—with two assists created and several chances of his own—only amplifies questions about why he isn't featuring more regularly in domestic competition.

Context & Background

The mid-season friendly represents an increasingly common practice among Premier League clubs when extended breaks occur between fixtures. With international breaks creating gaps in the domestic calendar, managers like Emery opt for competitive warm-up matches against foreign opposition to maintain fitness and match sharpness rather than relying solely on training sessions.

Villa's warm-weather camp in Murcia provided the perfect opportunity to schedule such a friendly, with Elche—a club familiar with Spanish football's lower echelons—serving as ideal opposition. The Pinatar Arena has become a popular destination for British clubs conducting winter training camps, offering quality facilities and suitable opponents.

Elliott's situation deserves particular attention. Described as being "trapped in Premier League purgatory," the talented youngster has found first-team opportunities limited despite clear ability. His performance in this friendly—creating two quality chances for Abraham and twice testing the goalkeeper himself—underscored his potential while raising questions about Emery's decision-making regarding his integration into the senior side.

What's Next

All attention now turns to the fitness assessments of both Sancho and Buendía. Fantasy Football Scout and other fantasy managers will be monitoring updates closely ahead of Gameweek 32 to determine their availability. Given that this was merely a friendly, Villa's medical staff will likely adopt a cautious approach with both players, especially with the Premier League run-in approaching.

For Elliott, this performance may serve as a reminder to Emery of his capabilities, potentially opening doors for more regular Premier League involvement. Alternatively, if the "exile" continues, it may intensify speculation about a summer transfer, with Elliott potentially seeking regular first-team football elsewhere.

The result itself—while disappointing—will be largely forgotten, but the timing of this match and its consequences could significantly impact Villa's season finish and countless fantasy teams' fortunes in the weeks ahead.
