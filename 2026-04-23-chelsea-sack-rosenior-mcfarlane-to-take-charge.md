# Chelsea Sack Rosenior, McFarlane Takes Temporary Charge

Chelsea have acted swiftly to relieve Liam Rosenior of his managerial duties following a devastating run of five successive Premier League defeats without scoring. The club announced the departure of the head coach and confirmed that Calum McFarlane will take temporary charge of the first team until the end of the current season.

This is not McFarlane's first involvement in leading the Chelsea first team this campaign. The club's coach previously oversaw matches during Gameweeks 20 and 21 earlier in the season, giving him prior experience in the interim managerial role.

## McFarlane's Two Matches in Charge

Under McFarlane's leadership, Chelsea have faced two challenging fixtures. The first saw the Blues travel to Craven Cottage where they suffered a 2-1 defeat against Fulham. The match was marked by Marc Cucurella's dismissal after just 22 minutes, leaving Chelsea to play the majority of the game with ten men. Despite the numerical disadvantage, Chelsea found the net through Liam Delap, who started the match in place of the benched Joao Pedro and grabbed the Blues' solitary goal in that fixture.

The second match under McFarlane's caretaker tenure was a creditable 1-1 draw at the Etihad Stadium against Manchester City. Enzo Fernandez proved instrumental in securing this point, being pushed into a more advanced number ten role after halftime by the interim boss. The Argentine midfielder then delivered the crucial late equaliser that secured a valuable point for his side.

## Key Absentees Against Manchester City

Chelsea's draw at Manchester City came without several key personnel. Robert Sanchez, Marc Cucurella, and Moises Caicedo all missed the fixture, unavailable through either injury or suspension. Their absences tested the squad depth but did not prevent Chelsea from earning a positive result against one of the league's top teams.

## Consistent Selection Under McFarlane

Five players have featured in both of McFarlane's matches as caretaker manager. Enzo Fernandez, Cole Palmer, Pedro Neto, Trevoh Chalobah, and Malo Gusto have started each game under the interim coach, suggesting a settled core that McFarlane has chosen to rely upon during his second stint in temporary charge.

## Upcoming Fixture

Chelsea's next Premier League assignment presents a notable scheduling quirk. Their opponents Nottingham Forest will be right in the middle of a two-legged UEFA Europa League semi-final against Aston Villa when the fixture takes place. Forest will be mid-way through their European campaign, potentially offering Chelsea an opportunity against a side with divided attention across competitions.
