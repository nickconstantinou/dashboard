# The FPL One-Season Wonders: When Premium Returns Came at Budget Prices

Fantasy Premier League managers have always searched for value—those hidden gems who deliver elite-level returns at rock-bottom prices. A new retrospective from Fantasy Football Scout explores the game's most memorable "one-season wonders": players who exploded onto the scene for one campaign, delivering returns that far exceeded their modest price tags, before fading back into irrelevance. The piece, which arrived during an international break lull, compiles a squad of 15 such players across positions—two goalkeepers, five defenders, five midfielders, and three forwards—who briefly illuminated FPL teams before becoming afterthoughts.

The article highlights two standout goalkeeper cases that exemplify the phenomenon. **Vito Mannone**, signed by Sunderland from Arsenal for £4.2m in summer 2013, didn't become first-choice until Gameweek 10 when starter Kieran Westwood suffered an injury. What followed was remarkable: 11 clean sheets across 28 starts, save points in 20 of those appearances, and a spectacular 14-save performance in a victory over Chelsea. Mannone finished with 134 points and a 4.6 points-per-game average—better than any first-choice keeper that season—earning him a spot on Fantasy Football Scout's Team of the Season. Yet the following campaign, he lost his place after Sunderland's infamous 8-0 defeat at Southampton and would only see sporadic action through 2016/17 before departing for Reading, eventually retiring after spells in the United States, Denmark, and France.

**Michel Vorm** represents perhaps the ultimate budget gem. Joining newly-promoted Swansea City for their first-ever Premier League season in 2011/12, the Dutch keeper was available at just £4.0m and racked up 14 clean sheets, 143 saves, and a massive 158 points—a total that matched what Jordan Pickford scored across the entire 2024/25 season. Only title-winning goalkeeper Joe Hart, priced at £7.0m, scored more that year. Vorm rose from his initial £4.0m price to a high of £5.3m and became one of the most-owned players in the game by season's end. His performance was particularly impressive given that goalkeepers historically struggled to earn bonus points under the EA Player Performance Index system that governed such awards at the time.

## Why This Matters

The one-season wonder phenomenon sits at the heart of FPL strategy. The game fundamentally rewards managers who can identify emerging value before the market catches on. Players like Mannone and Vorm demonstrate how a £4.0m-£4.2m investment could return 130-160 points—numbers comparable to premium options costing nearly double—freeing up budget for stars elsewhere. Understanding which players are likely to maintain output versus those riding unsustainable fortune is the difference between finishing in the top 10,000 and top 100,000.

This retrospective also reveals how external factors shape these anomalies. Mannone's opportunity came purely through injury to Westwood, while Vorm benefited from Swansea's unlikely promotion and a system that rewarded his save-heavy style. These cases remind managers that one-season wonders often aren't about hidden talent but rather perfect storm circumstances—a starting opportunity, a favorable fixture run, or a tactical system that maximizes certain statistics.
