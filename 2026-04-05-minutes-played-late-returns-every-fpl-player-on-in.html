Minutes played + late returns: Every FPL player on international duty

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

The Full Story

The late-March 2026 international break has concluded, with over 200 Premier League players representing their national teams across various competitions. Fantasy Football Scout has compiled a comprehensive breakdown of exactly how many minutes each FPL asset accumulated during international duty, providing managers with crucial data ahead of the critical final stretch of the 2025/26 season.

The game-time compilation, now largely automated through Datawrapper, covers all players who featured for their countries during the two-match international window. The data is presented club-by-club, showing minutes played in each international fixture (M1 and M2 columns), with "G+A" tracking goals and assists contributed. The article notes that gaps in the M2 column indicate a player wasn't in the matchday squad for one of their country's two matches—not necessarily the second game—while zeros indicate the player was in the matchday squad but remained unused.

The article, published on April 3, 2026, has attracted 147 comments from the Fantasy Football Scout community. The author TopMarx acknowledged that this is a relatively new process of logging game time and invited readers to report any discrepancies they spot in the data.

Why This Matters

For Fantasy Premier League managers, international break game time represents a critical data point that can influence team selection decisions. Players who returned from international duty with heavy minutes—or those who suffered late returns—may face increased fatigue risks heading into the season's final months. Conversely, players who didn't feature may be fresher and more likely to start in the next Premier League matchweek.

With the 2025/26 season approaching its climax and a busy fixture schedule ahead, understanding which players were rested, rotated, or heavily utilized by their national teams provides a tangible edge in squad management. The article specifically notes that managers needn't worry about late returnees affecting Gameweek 32, as there's still a week of FA Cup and European football before the deadline—meaning all players should be available for selection.

    • Context & Background

    International breaks during the Premier League season always create a delicate balancing act for FPL managers. The March international window is particularly significant because it falls during the season's business end, when every point matters in both the real league and Fantasy Premier League. National team managers often have different priorities than club managers—some use friendlies to experiment, while others treat every match as crucial for qualification or ranking purposes.

    This is the second season Fantasy Football Scout has attempted automated tracking of international game time, indicating a commitment to providing increasingly detailed data for their audience. The publication acknowledges the process isn't perfect and invites community feedback to improve accuracy—a reflection of how fantasy sports analysis has evolved to leverage technology while maintaining human verification.

    What's Next

    With the international break concluded, all attention now turns to Gameweek 32 and beyond. The article's note about the week-long buffer before Gameweek 32's deadline provides managers with certainty that no late-arriving players will miss their window of opportunity. The final stretch of the 2025/26 season features multiple competitions—the FA Cup semifinals and European club competitions running concurrently—which will create additional rotation considerations for Premier League managers.

    FPL managers should use this game-time data alongside other factors—fixtures, form, and injury status—to make informed decisions. The community discussion (147 comments and counting) suggests significant interest in how specific players performed, with particular attention likely paid to high-owned assets and those at clubs with heavy international representation. Future international windows will likely see continued refinement of this tracking process as the system matures.
