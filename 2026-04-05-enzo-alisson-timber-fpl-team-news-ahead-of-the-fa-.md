# FPL Team News: Enzo Fernandez Suspended, Alisson Out Long-Term, Salah Returns as FA Cup Approaches

The Premier League landscape shifted dramatically as six top-flight managers addressed the media Thursday and Friday, delivering crucial Fantasy Premier League updates ahead of this weekend's FA Cup quarter-final action. The most significant development emerged from Stamford Bridge, where Chelsea head coach Liam Rosenior announced that Argentine midfielder Enzo Fernandez has been suspended by the club for two matches following public comments about his future at the club. The 24-year-old will miss both Chelsea's FA Cup tie against Port Vale and next Sunday's league fixture against Manchester City. Rosenior stressed that "a line was crossed" in terms of the club's culture, though he emphasized the door remains open for Fernandez's return. The suspension is expected to make Fernandez a popular sale ahead of Gameweek 32.

At Anfield, Liverpool manager Arne Slot confirmed that goalkeeper Alisson Becker will remain sidelined with a hamstring injury beyond the upcoming Champions League quarter-final matches against Paris Saint-Germain. "He will be out for a bit longer," Slot stated. "We expect him back towards the end of the season." The Brazilian shot-stopper's absence leaves a significant gap in both Liverpool's real-life defense and FPL squads. However, there was brighter news regarding Mohamed Salah, who is fully fit and available after missing recent matches with a muscle complaint. "Is he fit? Yes, he is. Trained yesterday and today, he's available," Slot confirmed. The Egyptian's return comes at a critical time as he recently announced his impending departure from Liverpool. Meanwhile, striker Alexander Isak may see some minutes this weekend—the Swede completed his first training session on Thursday after 101 days out with an ankle injury, though Slot cautioned it would be "too soon to expect him in the starting lineup."

Arsenal's situation remains complex, with Mikel Arteta providing typically cryptic updates on his squad's fitness. Eze and Hincapie have been ruled out, while Malcom Madueke's injury is not considered serious. More positively, Martin Odegaard and Jurrien Timber could both return to action. At Manchester City, Pep Guardiola offered no updates on John Stones, Joao Cancelo, or Ruben Dias, leaving their availability uncertain. Leeds United striker Dominic Calvert-Lewin is a doubt with a hamstring problem, though midfielders including Stach should be available. West Ham winger Summerville requires assessment before the Hammers' cup engagement.

**Why This Matters**

For the estimated eight million FPL managers worldwide, this weekend's team news carries massive implications for chip strategies and wildcard planning. Fernandez's suspension removes a midfielder priced at £6.9 million from consideration at a critical point in the season, while Alisson's extended absence—potentially lasting six weeks or more—forces managers to either stick with an injured premium goalkeeper or pivot to alternatives. Salah's return, conversely, provides a massive boost for the 3.8 million managers who held onto him through his injury layoff. With the season entering its decisive phase, these availability changes could prove the difference between finishing in the top 10,000 or the top 100,000.

**Key Details and Quotes**

Chelsea's injury list extends beyond Fernandez. Reece James (hamstring) continues his recovery but "isn't ready yet," while Trevoh Chalobah (ankle) progresses well and Levi Colwill has returned to full training. Estevao and Jamie Gittens both completed their returns and will be available for Thursday's cup tie. At Liverpool, Wataru Endo (foot), Stefan Bajcetic (hamstring), Conor Bradley (knee), and Giovanni Looni (knee) remain unavailable. Jeremie Frimpong and Federico Chiesa, both of whom took knocks recently, appear fit.

**What's Next**

Managers should expect Fernandez to be transferred out en masse before the Gameweek 32 deadline. Those seeking to capitalize on Alisson's absence might consider Liverpool's defensive options more broadly, while Salah owners can breathe easily ahead of a crucial period. Isak's gradual return introduces another option for Newcastle assets as the season reaches its business end, though his limited minutes ceiling this weekend makes him a hold rather than an immediate buy. The next set of press conferences will likely clarify the situation at Manchester City ahead of their own cup involvement.
