# Fantasy Premier League Team News: Fernandez Suspended, Alisson Out, Salah Returns Ahead of FA Cup Quarter-Finals

The Premier League's build-up to this weekend's FA Cup quarter-finals has delivered significant Fantasy Premier League implications, with six top-flight managers providing crucial injury updates across Thursday and Friday. The standout development comes from Chelsea, where Argentine midfielder Enzo Fernandez has been suspended by the club for two matches following public comments about his future at Stamford Bridge. Head coach Liam Rosenior confirmed the sanction, stating that Fernandez will miss both the FA Cup tie against Port Vale and next Sunday's Premier League clash with Manchester City. The decision marks a significant moment for FPL managers, as Fernandez is expected to be a highly popular transfer target ahead of Gameweek 32. Meanwhile, the news is more positive for Chelsea elsewhere, with Brazilian wonderkid Estevao and English winger Jamie Gittens both returning to full fitness and available for selection. However, Reece James (hamstring), Trevoh Chalobah (ankle), Levi Colwill (knee), and Mykhailo Mudryk (suspended) remain unavailable.

Liverpool's news carries major implications for FPL managers invested in the club's assets. Goalkeeper Alisson Becker will remain sidelined for an extended period after suffering a hamstring injury, with manager Arne Slot confirming the Brazilian will miss both legs of the Champions League quarter-final against Paris Saint-Germain. "He is unavailable and also out of the PSG game. He will be out for a bit longer. We expect him back towards the end of the season," Slot said. This news significantly impacts Liverpool's defensive assets, as managers may need to consider alternatives between the posts. On a more positive note, Mohamed Salah is available for selection after recovering from a muscle injury—the Egyptian's first involvement since announcing his imminent departure from Anfield at the end of the season. Swedish striker Alexander Isak, who has been sidelined for 101 days following ankle surgery, returned to training on Wednesday and may feature in some capacity, though Slot indicated he would be carefully managed. Jeremie Frimpong and Federico Chiesa (who took a knock) are both fine, while Wataru Endo (foot), Stefan Bajcetic (hamstring), Conor Bradley (knee), and Giovanni Leoni (knee) remain unavailable.

Arsenal manager Mikel Arteta provided mixed updates, with Eze and Hincapie ruled out but more hopeful news regarding Mikel Odegaard and Jurrien Timber, who both could be available. Mikel Arteta declined to provide extensive details, staying true to his reputation for keeping injury information close to his chest. Manchester City boss Pep Guardiola offered no update on John Stones, Joao Cancelo, or Ruben Dias, leaving managers holding City defenders in a state of uncertainty. At Leeds United, striker Dominic Calvert-Lewin faces a race against time with a hamstring injury and is considered a doubt, though midfielder Pascal Struijk should be fine despite a hip problem. West Ham forward Summerville will be assessed ahead of their cup engagement.

The Fernandez situation represents one of the most significant club-imposed disciplinary actions involving an FPL asset this season, highlighting the tension between player expressions and club messaging, particularly as Chelsea push for Champions League qualification. For FPL purposes, his two-match absence creates both a selling opportunity and a potential bargain-hunting moment, as his price point may make him an attractive casualty for managers looking to restructure their squads. The Alisson injury is particularly consequential given Liverpool's strong run-in and their continued involvement in multiple competitions—the Reds face a congested schedule with the PSG Champions League ties adding further strain to their season. Managers using Liverpool defenders in their FPL teams will need to weigh whether the club's clean sheet potential remains sufficient despite the goalkeeper uncertainty, especially with young shot-stopper Vitezslav Jaros likely to continue in goal. Salah's return provides a major boost to attacking options, though his long-term future at Liverpool remains uncertain as he prepares to leave the club this summer.
