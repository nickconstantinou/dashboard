Man City v Brentford team news: Semenyo starts, Dias a sub

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

Match Context

Manchester City enter today's evening kick-off with the objective of reducing Arsenal's lead to two points. The champions face Brentford looking to close the gap at the top of the Premier League table.

Manchester City Starting XI

Nathan Ake comes into the Manchester City starting XI, replacing Abdokodir Khusanov in defence. Khusanov misses out due to a knock sustained ahead of the match. Pep Guardiola confirmed the centre-half has a "knock," ruling him unavailable for selection.

In midfield, Tijjani Reijnders replaces Nico Gonzalez in the lineup. The midfielder carries a price tag of £5.0m for Fantasy Football purposes and looks to be involved from the start at the Etihad.

Antoine Semenyo retains his place on the right flank despite a recent dip in form. The forward, priced at £8.1m, continues in the starting XI for this encounter.

Manchester City Injury List

Rodri again misses out with a groin injury. The influential midfielder remains unavailable as he continues his recovery.

Josko Gvardiol is not yet fit enough for competitive action despite returning to training this week. The defender continues to work individually and is not considered for selection.

On a positive note, Ruben Dias returns from a hamstring issue. However, the Portuguese defender is among the substitutes for this match rather than the starting lineup.

Brentford XI Changes

Keith Andrews has made two changes to the Brentford lineup. Kristoffer Ajer and Aaron Hickey receive recalls to the starting XI, strengthening the defensive options.

Conversely, Dango Ouattara and Sepp van den Berg drop to the Brentford bench. Both players must be ready to contribute if called upon during the match.

Key Fantasy Considerations

For Fantasy Football managers, Reijnders at £5.0m represents a budget option in the City midfield, while Semenyo at £8.1m offers attacking potential despite recent inconsistent returns. Dias on the bench suggests he may not be ready for a full 90 minutes, though his return to the matchday squad indicates progress in his recovery.
