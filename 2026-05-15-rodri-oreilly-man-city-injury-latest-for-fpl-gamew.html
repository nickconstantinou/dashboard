Rodri + O'Reilly: Man City Injury Latest for FPL Gameweek 37

Rodri's Fitness Uncertainty

Manchester City midfielder Rodri is dealing with a groin injury, and his availability for the upcoming FA Cup final remains uncertain. When pressed on whether Rodri could feature in the final, manager Pep Guardiola offered a non-committal response: "We'll see. We'll travel, and we'll see." The uncertainty surrounding the Spanish midfielder leaves Fantasy Premier League managers with a difficult decision heading into the final stages of the season.

No Other Concerns for Man City

On a more positive note, Guardiola confirmed that City have no other fitness concerns ahead of the FA Cup final. When asked if he had any other issues to contend with, the City manager stated: "No, everybody [else] is fit." This suggests the rest of the squad is available for selection, which should provide some comfort to FPL managers holding City assets beyond Rodri.

Nico O'Reilly Absent Against Crystal Palace

For managers considering Nico O'Reilly, there is a cause for slight concern. The young defender was absent from the Manchester City squad for the midweek victory over Crystal Palace. At his current price of £5.3m, O'Reilly represents an affordable City defensive option, but his omission from that match raises questions about his availability.

Gameweek 37 Considerations

Regarding the Gameweek 37 fixture, Manchester City are scheduled to travel to Bournemouth, with the match taking place next Tuesday. However, Guardiola made clear that he has not factored in this fixture when considering his team selection for the FA Cup final. When asked if the trip to Bournemouth featured in his thoughts, the City boss stated: "Zero. It's the final of the FA Cup." This suggests rotation or a weakened side could be fielded at Bournemouth, making Gameweek 37 appearances unpredictable.

FPL Implications

For FPL managers, Rodri's uncertainty is the primary concern, as his potential absence would significantly impact both City's defensive solidity and attacking options. Those holding Nico O'Reilly should monitor for any update on his fitness before the Bournemouth fixture, given his recent omission from the matchday squad.
