# Liverpool vs Fulham: Gravenberch Benched as Slot Prioritises PSG Return

Ryan Gravenberch has been dropped to the Liverpool bench for Saturday evening's Premier League meeting with Fulham — the Dutchman's first start omission in the league since May 2024.

The demotion is the most eye-catching selection decision from Arne Slot, who has made five changes to his side as Liverpool manage their squad ahead of the decisive second leg against Paris Saint-Germain on Tuesday. The Reds held PSG to a 0-0 draw in the first leg in Paris on Wednesday, leaving everything to play for at Anfield.

"Everyone who expected 11 players to start again after Wednesday was maybe a bit naive," Slot said on Friday. "We need to think about the quick turnaround. I'm happy with the condition of the squad."

## Salah Returns, Robertson Comes In

Mohammed Salah was an unused substitute in Paris but returns to the line-up against Fulham. So too does Andrew Robertson, who will leave Liverpool at the end of the season. Curtis Jones, Cody Gakpo, and the highly-rated Rio Ngumoha also come into the starting eleven.

The benching of Hugo Ekitike comes as less of a surprise. The French forward, who has been a regular starter in recent weeks, drops to substitute duty alongside Joe Gomez — another player Slot flagged concerns about regarding recovery from the midweek exertion.

Alexis Mac Allister and Milos Kerkez also make way.

## Fulham Unchanged

Fulham named an unchanged side from the team that beat Burnley three weeks ago. Rodrigo Muniz again starts up front, with Raul Jimenez remaining on the bench. The Cottagers arrive at Anfield in reasonable form, though this represents a considerably more straightforward selection puzzle for head coach Marco Silva.

Liverpool: Mamardashvili; Frimpong, Konate, Van Dijk, Robertson; Szoboszlai, Jones, Salah; Wirtz, Ngumoha, Gakpo.

Fulham: Leno; Castagne, Andersen, Bassey, Robinson; Iwobi, Berge, Wilson, King, Bobb; Rodrigo Muniz.
