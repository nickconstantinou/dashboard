## Strategy Two: Wildcard 32, Bench Boost 33, Free Hit 34

This represents one of the most popular strategies among the FPL community, though it also applies to managers who have already used their Wildcard and are Free Hitting in Gameweek 34 instead. In this scenario, managers are looking at all remaining fixtures across the season except for the Blank in Gameweek 34, meaning the focus becomes targeting players who have that Double in Gameweek 33.

The teams with doubles in Gameweek 33 include Manchester City, Leeds United, Brighton and Hove Albion, Chelsea, and Bournemouth. Harry also notes that Crystal Palace should receive consideration despite not doubling in Gameweek 33—they have West Ham at home that week and are expected to double in Gameweek 36, making them a long-term target. Arsenal and Newcastle also warrant slight consideration for long-term planning from Gameweek 35 onwards, as these are the teams managers will target once the Wildcard 35 window arrives.

For Manchester City, Harry is particularly enthusiastic. Erling Haaland, Jeremy Doku, and Phil Foden all represent excellent picks, and Harry emphasizes that not only do they double in Gameweek 33, but they're also likely to double again in Gameweek 36, effectively giving them an additional fixture on top of all the other clubs available. This makes them the standout priority for this strategy.

Leeds United slightly edges Bournemouth in Harry's preference due to their long-term fixtures, including a very favourable home match against Burnley in Gameweek 35. The main Leeds assets to consider include goalkeeper Meshak Dubravka, defender Pedro Porro, and forwards like Cody Gakpo and Mohamed Salah—but Harry notes we're waiting for injury updates on both Antoine Saint-Maximin and Joe Rothwell after they went off injured in the FA Cup at the weekend.

Brighton offers several interesting options, though Harry rates them as more differential than core picks. Joao Pedro is mentioned as a forward option, while in midfield, Pascal Gross is probably his favourite for guaranteed minutes, though both of the Hinchliffe-Gomez and Mitoma are worth considering as Differentials.

For Chelsea, Harry states it's really only Cole Palmer who stands out as a viable option. Defensively, options like Malo Gusto and Benoit Badiashile exist, but Harry doesn't anticipate Chelsea keeping enough clean sheets to make goalkeepers worthwhile.

Bournemouth defensively offers options like Illian Senes and defender Marcos Senesi, with Harry mentioning that the club's upcoming fixtures present reasonable opportunities. However, he doesn't imagine many managers will be rushing to add these players to their squads.

For Crystal Palace, despite not doubling in Gameweek 33, Harry recommends keeping an eye on them for Gameweek 36 since they're expected to double then. In Gameweek 33, they have West Ham at home, and the most nailed-on options include goalkeeper Sam Johnstone, defender Marc Guehi, and midfielder Eberechi Eze.
