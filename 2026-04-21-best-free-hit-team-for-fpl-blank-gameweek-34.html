Best Free Hit team for FPL Blank Gameweek 34

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

With the FA Cup semi-finals stealing the spotlight, six clubs will blank in Gameweek 34 — Manchester City, Chelsea, Bournemouth, Brighton and Hove Albion, Leeds United, and Burnley — leaving Fantasy Premier League managers scrambling. For those without a Free Hit chip already spent, the instinct is obvious: build a cherry-picked XI on a £100.0m budget and start fresh next week. Here's an early draft of what that side might look like, with the logic behind each call.

---

The Defence: Clean Sheets on Merseyside

Arsenal and Liverpool will sit near the top of any clean sheet odds table this week, and for good reason. Gabriel Magalhaes (£7.1m) and Virgil van Dijk (£6.2m) are the obvious — and expensive — routes into those backlines, but both justify the cost. Both are aerial threats at set-pieces, giving them DefCon point potential that stretches well beyond a clean sheet. Arsenal's title charge and Liverpool's renewed defensive solidity under Arne Slot make either a reliable anchor.

The third defensive slot calls for a differential. Pedro Porro (£5.1m) fits the bill. Tottenham's right-back has been pushing into midfield in recent away matches — what managers call an "inverted" role — which gives him license to roam into dangerous areas. The data supporting this pick is specific and curious: over the last two Gameweeks, no club has allowed more chances to be conceded from their left flank than Wolverhampton Wanderers. That's the side Jarrod Bowen (£7.7m) and Brenden Aaronson (£5.4m) have been exploiting for assists. Porro might fancy his chances from set-pieces against Wolves too — they've conceded three goals from dead-ball situations in their last two matches, the most of any team in the league over that span.

In goal, Mads Hermansen (£4.2m) is partly a budget necessity, but West Ham have tightened considerably under Nuno Espirito Santo — two clean sheets in five home matches — making him a viable option rather than a pure punt.

---

The Attack: Form Players, Favorable Fixtures

Bruno Fernandes (£10.3m) is the backbone of any Free Hit side this week. He's on penalties, nailed for 90 minutes every week, and — critically — the division's most prolific creator by a margin. Nothing about that changes because the calendar throws a blank our way.

Mohamed Salah (£14.0m) has scored in back-to-back Gameweeks and arrives at home to Crystal Palace. The Eagles may have one eye on their Conference League semi-final first leg against Shakhtar Donetsk five days later, which adds a layer of fixture appeal that doesn't always present itself so clearly.

The trickier calls come in the mid-price brackets. Morgan Gibbs-White (£7.5m) is one of the league's form players — 91 points since the turn of the year, more than any asset except Fernandes. Nottingham Forest travel to the Stadium of Face, and while that's not a straightforward assignment, Sunderland sit in the bottom eight for expected goals conceded (xGC) in their last six home matches. The underlying numbers support the pick even if the fixture name raises an eyebrow.

Morgan Rogers (£7.4m) has quietly returned to form after a quiet spell, with a goal and two assists in his last three matches. In that stretch, he's combined eight shots with six chances created — including four "big chance" involvements. Villa's fixture at Craven Cottage carries a similar logic to Forest's: Fulham are in the bottom eight for xGC at home over the same six-game window.

The final attack slot goes to Harry Wilson (£6.0m), completing a Free Hit XI that balances premium firepower with differentials who carry the data to back their inclusion.

---

The Wider Picture

This draft is necessarily early — the Gameweek 33 injury picture is still clearing up, and any fresh news from Monday's training sessions could shift the thinking. But the framework holds: lock in the form players with the best fixture data, sprinkle in a differential or two where the numbers are unusually compelling, and don't overthink the budget constraints when a £4.2m goalkeeper has a real path to a clean sheet.

The Free Hit exists precisely for weeks like this. The six blanks are a disruption, but they're also an opportunity to load up on the fixtures that genuinely deserve it — and this early read suggests the smart money is split between Merseyside's title contenders and a pair of away sides whose underlying numbers look better than their reputations.
