Key Details: The Standout Statistics

Goalkeepers: Pickford's Excellence and Brentford's Contradiction

Jordan Pickford (£5.6m) has emerged as the season's most valuable goalkeeper when measuring pure shot-stopping ability, leading the entire Premier League with +5.10 expected goals (xG) prevented. This metric measures how many goals a keeper has prevented compared to what the average goalkeeper would have conceded from the shots they've faced. His performance has translated directly to FPL success, with Pickford recording his 11th clean sheet of the campaign and climbing to second place in the goalkeeper points standings. The Everton and England No. 1 has been nothing short of exceptional, making his price tag look increasingly attractive compared to premium options.

The top five for xG prevented reads like a who's-who of the league's most reliable goalkeepers:

At the opposite end of the spectrum, some of the league's more expensive options have been costing their teams—and their FPL owners—significantly:

    Robert Sanchez (£4.8m) has been quietly excellent in an unexpected category: chance creation. The Brighton keeper has created six Opta-defined 'big chances' this season, which is double the next-best total among Premier League goalkeepers. Remarkably, he is one of only three goalkeepers in the entire league to register an FPL assist this term. For managers seeking differential options, Sanchez represents unusual value in an asset class not typically known for attacking returns.

    Caoimhin Kelleher (£4.8m) of Brentford has become the league's designated penalty specialist, having saved three penalties this season—making him the only Premier League goalkeeper to have stopped more than a single spot-kick all term. This alone makes him worth considering, especially given that Brentford have conceded a joint league-high seven penalties in 2025/26. Any opposition manager scheduling a penalty taker against the Bees will have Kelleher firmly in their thoughts.

    Defenders: Thiaw Dominates While O'Reilly Emerges

    Malick Thiaw (£5.1m) of Newcastle United has been a revelation this season, leading all Premier League defenders with nine big chances registered—the Opta metric that measures clear-cut scoring opportunities that a player has been involved in creating or receiving. Thiaw has backed this up with four goals, sharing the title of joint-top scorer among defenders in the league. His involvement in attack has been remarkable for a centre-back, and he nearly added to his tally in Gameweek 31 before having a strike controversially ruled out. Newcastle's defensive solidity combined with Thiaw's attacking threat makes him one of the premium defender options for the run-in.

    Looking at recent form over the last six Gameweeks, however, a different picture emerges. Nico O'Reilly (£5.0m) of Manchester City and Virgil van Dijk (£6.3m) of Liverpool are joint-top with four big chances each in this timeframe. O'Reilly's emergence as a genuine attacking threat from the City defense has been one of the stories of the season, and at £5.0m, he represents exceptional value compared to Van Dijk at £6.3m.

    Neco Williams (£4.7m) of Nottingham Forest has taken the most shots of any Premier League defender this season, with 35 efforts on goal. However, this volume has yielded just one goal, with only 12 of those shots coming from inside the penalty area. His underlying numbers suggest a significant overperformance would be needed to justify continued ownership, though his nailed-on status at Forest ensures continued opportunities.

    In stark contrast, Nico O'Reilly has been ruthlessly efficient with his opportunities. The young City defender has found the net four times in the league alone, with a remarkable 24 of his 31 total shots coming from inside the opposition's penalty area. His 2.02 expected goals figure suggests his scoring rate is well-supported by underlying quality, making him one of the season's best value picks at defender.

    Context and Background: How These Numbers Fit the Season Narrative

    The 2025/26 Premier League season has been characterized by surprise packages and emerging talent, and the statistics from Gameweek 32 reinforce these trends. Jordan Pickford's excellence comes as no surprise to those who have watched England and Everton's No. 1 perform at an elite level consistently, but the magnitude of his xG prevented figure highlights how much he's been asked to do behind an Everton side that has shipped chances at an alarming rate at times.

    The emergence of Nico O'Reilly as a genuine FPL asset represents one of the season's most significant plotlines. Manchester City's defensive rotation has been a feature of their campaign, but O'Reilly has forced his way into Pep Guardiola's plans through sheer performance. His ability to contribute at both ends of the pitch—defensively solid while also posing a genuine goal threat—makes him invaluable in the FPL context.

    Newcastle's Malick Thiaw has been equally impressive, representing the Magpies' transformation under their current management into one of the league's most defensively sound units while also maintaining attacking ambition from their defenders.

    What's Next: The Run-In Implications

    For FPL managers, these statistics provide crucial intelligence for the season's final stretch. Several key takeaways emerge:

    Goalkeeper Strategy: Pickford looks essential at his price point, while the struggling options like Leno, Johnstone, and Vicario should be avoided regardless of their team contexts. Kelleher's penalty-saving ability makes him an interesting differential, particularly given Brentford's fixture run.

    Defender Prioritization: Thiaw's numbers make him a strong premium option, but O'Reilly at £5.0m represents arguably the best value in the entire defender pool. His recent form and City's favorable fixtures make him essential for managers chasing leaders.

    Volume vs. Efficiency: Williams' shot volume is a trap—the Nottingham Forest defender's lack of conversion and few attempts inside the box suggest his ceiling is limited. Conversely, O'Reilly's efficiency demonstrates the importance of quality chances over raw volume.

    The international break provides managers with time to digest this information and plan their moves carefully. With the season entering its decisive phase, every transfer and captain choice could prove pivotal. The data from Gameweek 32 offers a roadmap, but execution remains in the hands of the managers themselves.
