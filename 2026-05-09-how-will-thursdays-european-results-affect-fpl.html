How will Thursday's European results affect FPL?

Thursday's European semi-finals delivered significant developments for two Premier League clubs with direct consequences for Fantasy Premier League managers. Aston Villa and Crystal Palace both progressed to finals, but their paths create distinct considerations for the remaining gameweeks.

Aston Villa: Europa League Final and FPL Implications

Aston Villa overturned a first-leg deficit to defeat Nottingham Forest and reach the Europa League final. This achievement has altered the mathematical landscape for Champions League qualification. If Villa win the Europa League and, domestically, finish exactly fifth, then the team below them will also qualify for the Champions League. That place is currently occupied by Bournemouth.

For FPL purposes, this creates uncertainty around key assets. Unai Emery may deem it necessary to rest the likes of Morgan Rogers (£7.5m) and Ollie Watkins (£8.8m) in the build-up to Istanbul. Villa's final European encounter, the Freiburg v Aston Villa showcase, is on Wednesday 20 May, between Gameweeks 37 and 38. This midweek fixture complicates planning for managers holding Villa assets, as rotation risk exists even if they secure Champions League qualification through the league.

Crystal Palace: Conference League Final and Double Gameweek Concerns

Crystal Palace beat Shakhtar Donetsk to make the Conference League equivalent, securing their place in the final. However, this success introduces complications for FPL managers targeting Palace players in the immediate term.

At Palace, Oliver Glasner is already rotating his attackers and hinted at more over the coming days. It greatly reduces the appeal of their players for this Double Gameweek 36. Managers considering Palace assets should account for this rotation policy, particularly for those in the final third.

The fixture schedule compounds this concern. Gameweek 38's hosting of Arsenal happens three days before meeting Rayo Vallecano in Leipzig, creating a compressed timeline that may force further squad rotation. This scheduling conflict makes Palace assets less attractive for the season's concluding gameweeks.

The Champions League Race Below

Bournemouth currently occupies the final Champions League qualification spot, but Brentford and Brighton and Hove Albion are close behind. The outcome of Villa's Europa League campaign directly affects whether a sixth-placed team enters Europe's elite competition next season, making the race for European positions exceptionally relevant for FPL strategy.

Practical FPL Considerations

FPL's Gameweek 37 deadline is now 18:30 on Friday 15 May. Managers with Villa or Palace assets should verify this timing when finalizing transfers. The combination of midweek European fixtures, rotation risk, and compressed domestic schedules suggests caution with heavy investments in either squad for the remaining gameweeks.
