Man United Injury Update for FPL Gameweek 35

Matthijs de Ligt Remains Unavailable

Manchester United defender Matthijs de Ligt continues to be sidelined due to a back injury. The centre-back, priced at £4.9m in FPL, remains out of contention for Gameweek 35. According to assistant manager Michael Carrick, de Ligt is "still a little bit further away" and is not involved in the current matchday plans. Alongside de Ligt, Lisandro Martinez also remains unavailable as he serves a suspension.

Matheus Cunha Returns to Training

Matheus Cunha has returned to training this week after missing Monday's victory over Brentford due to a hip injury. The Brazilian forward's return offers a potential boost to Manchester United's attacking options. However, there remains an element of doubt over his availability for Gameweek 35. Carrick stated: "Matheus has done a little bit of work, so we're hopeful. We're not sure but we're hopeful." This uncertainty means FPL managers should monitor the situation closely before finalising their selections.

Luke Shaw Fitness Unclear

Luke Shaw, priced at £4.5m, came off during the Brentford match but did not receive a specific mention when Carrick was questioned about the left-back position. Carrick did not address Shaw's fitness status when asked directly about the left-back's condition. This silence leaves uncertainty regarding Shaw's availability, and FPL managers holding the England international may need to wait for further confirmation.

Defensive Availability Concerns

With both de Ligt and Martinez unavailable, Manchester United's defensive resources face limitations. FPL managers considering United assets should factor in these absences when assessing their clean sheet potential. The combination of suspension and injury leaves questions over rotation and solidity at the back for the upcoming fixture.
