No Blank or Double Gameweek for Arsenal or Newcastle

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

The Full Story

Southampton's dramatic 2-1 victory over Arsenal at St Mary's Stadium on Saturday night has sent shockwaves through the Fantasy Premier League community, completely upending the strategic planning millions of managers had locked in for the decisive run-in. Mikel Arteta's side, widely considered one of the Premier League's two best teams alongside Liverpool, were dumped out of the FA Cup quarter-finals by a Championship side, eliminating any possibility of Double Gameweek 33 or Blank Gameweek 34 for both Arsenal and Newcastle United. This single result means the FPL landscape has fundamentally changed, with the "cat amongst the pigeons" as Fantasy Football Scout's editor Skonto Rigga Neale put it, dramatically reducing the value of certain chip plays and forcing a complete re-evaluation of end-of-season strategies.

The knockout punch from Southampton means Arsenal will not have another Double Gameweek this season, and Newcastle—whose fate was tied to Arsenal's cup run—will also miss out on any additional doubling opportunities. The FA Cup results from the weekend have now clarified the fixture situation for the critical final weeks, with Gameweek 33 now set to feature only six teams doubling rather than the eight that had been widely anticipated. Three teams will blank in Gameweek 34: Brighton versus Chelsea and Burnley versus Manchester City are confirmed blanks, with the final status dependent on the outcome of the West Ham versus Leeds quarter-final tie playing on Sunday.

Why This Matters

This development represents one of the most significant mid-season shifts in FPL strategy in recent memory. For weeks, managers had been building their squads specifically to capitalize on Arsenal and Newcastle's participation in both Double Gameweek 33 and Blank Gameweek 34—a combination that would have provided exceptional value for those holding Gunners assets. The sudden removal of this leverage forces a complete recalculation of chip deployment, potentially saving managers a valuable Free Hit that might otherwise have been essential to navigate the blank gameweek.

The strategic implications extend far beyond a single gameweek. A Double Gameweek 33 featuring Arsenal and Newcastle—arguably the two most in-form teams in the league—would have made a Bench Boost chip incredibly powerful, potentially delivering 15 doublers across the two fixtures. Now, that appeal is significantly diminished, and managers must weigh whether to use their Bench Boost later in the season when fewer teams will be doubling, or pivot to alternative strategies. The article notes that "many managers will be able to get through Gameweek 34 without a Free Hit, at least with their current squad," which represents a fundamental shift in how the community approaches the final weeks of the season.

Key Details

The specific fixture changes are as follows: in Gameweek 34, Brighton versus Chelsea and Burnley versus Manchester City are confirmed blanks, with West Ham versus Everton and Bournemouth versus Leeds remaining potential blanks depending on FA Cup results. Arsenal versus Newcastle, Liverpool versus Crystal Palace, Fulham versus Aston Villa, Manchester United versus Brentford, Sunderland versus Nottingham Forest, and Wolves versus Tottenham will all proceed as normal, bringing the total number of postponements down to three.

The mathematical impact on Double Gameweek 33 is substantial—managers will now only have access to six doubling teams rather than eight, substantially reducing the bench boost potential. Arsenal's single fixture in that gameweek will be against Manchester City, removing the "solid Bench Boost" opportunity that had been baked into many managers' plans. The article emphasizes that managers who had been planning to use their Wildcard in Gameweek 32 to load up on Arsenal assets now face a far less attractive proposition, as "playing them in Gameweek 33 when they only have one fixture" against the league leaders makes little strategic sense.

Context and Background

This story represents the intersection of real-world football scheduling and the complex strategic calculus that defines Fantasy Premier League. The FA Cup quarter-finals serve as the mechanism through which certain Premier League fixtures can be postponed, creating "blank" gameweeks when teams progress in the cup and "double" gameweeks when teams exit the cup early and have their league fixtures rearranged. The article notes that Fantasy Football Scout had been tracking two other affected pairs—Chelsea and Brighton, and Manchester City and Burnley—all of which would have Double Gameweek 33 and Blank Gameweek 34.

The timing of this development is particularly significant because it falls during the critical run-in period when FPL managers traditionally deploy their most powerful chips. The article references that "an Arsenal victory would have surely rubberstamped a Gameweek 32 Wildcard for many FPL managers," indicating that the community had been building toward this outcome as a near-certainty. The unexpected result means thousands of managers who had planned their entire seasons around these chip deployments must now improvise, creating both challenges and opportunities for those who can adapt quickly.

What's Next

Fantasy Football Scout has announced that a follow-up article weighing the pros and cons of various chip strategies will be published in the coming days, giving managers the analytical framework they need to make informed decisions. Additionally, a Gameweek 32 Wildcard draft article is scheduled to follow, providing specific player recommendations for those still planning to use their Wildcard in the current gameweek.

The final piece of the puzzle for Gameweek 34 will be confirmed on Sunday when the West Ham United versus Leeds United FA Cup quarter-final tie concludes, determining whether either of those clubs joins the blank gameweek. Looking further ahead, the article suggests that "Man City and Crystal Palace will be doubling" in Gameweek 36, potentially offering an alternative bench boost opportunity despite featuring fewer teams. Managers are advised to stay flexible and monitor team news closely as the season reaches its climax, with the strategic landscape capable of shifting with each passing cup result.
