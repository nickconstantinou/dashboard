The Blank Is Nigh | #FPL BGW31

Introduction

The Premier League season is hurtling toward its climax, and Fantasy Premier League managers find themselves at a critical crossroads. In this episode of the popular FPL podcast, hosts Tom and Sam dissect the aftermath of a particularly low-scoring gameweek 30 while sounding the alarm about the impending "blank" gameweek 31 that threatens to sidelines five Premier League teams. Recorded on Tuesday, March 17th, the conversation arrives at a pivotal moment when European competitions—the Champions League and Europa League—are also in full swing, adding another layer of complexity to FPL decision-making.

Tom opens with acknowledgment that gameweek 30 delivered remarkably few goals across the league, noting that Brentford versus Wolves was the only fixture preventing it from being a record-low scoring week. This context makes the discussion particularly relevant: with limited scoring opportunities, every managerial decision carries heightened weight. The episode serves as both a post-mortem on gameweek 30 performance and a tactical briefing for the challenging weeks ahead, making it essential listening for FPL managers seeking to salvage their seasons.

The hosts bring contrasting fortunes to this discussion. Tom enjoyed a breakout week that returned him to the top 100k for the first time since early February, while Sam endured another miserable outing that continues a concerning trend of declining rankings throughout the campaign. Their different trajectories provide complementary perspectives on the strategic choices facing FPL managers as the season reaches its business end.

Gameweek 30 Performance Analysis

Tom's Resurgence

Tom's gameweek 30 proved to be a exceptional success in what was otherwise a notoriously difficult week for scoring. He accumulated 71 points, a haul that transported him from approximately 140thousand to 80thousand in the overall rankings—a return to the top 100k for the first time since around gameweek 25 in early February. The achievement was particularly satisfying given the limited scoring opportunities available across the Premier League that weekend.

The catalyst for Tom's success came from identifying what he describes as "the one hall which transformed things"—apparently a clean sheet from an unexpected source—combined with making the correct captaincy choice. Tom reveals that on the podcast recorded the previous Tuesday, he had been "really bullish" about captaining Ekitique (Ivan Toney), but over the course of the week, he changed his mind and moved the captaincy to Bruno Fernandes. He explains his reasoning: while he initially felt anxious about the move, he became concerned about the fixture congestion facing his alternative option and the potential for rotation, whereas Bruno had demonstrated remarkable consistency with five consecutive double-digit hauls at that point. Tom describes Bruno as having "taken the mantle...as being the points defecator at home," suggesting the Manchester United midfielder had become the most reliable premium option for home fixtures.

Interestingly, Tom retained Erling Haaland despite considering selling him, citing concerns about the limited funds available for a suitable replacement. He ultimately moved a player out to bring in Andrew Robertson from Bournemouth, explaining that with the news about Liverpool injuries—including Connor Bradley's concussion and Virgil van Dijk's unavailability—he calculated he could only afford Robertson rather than the more expensive alternatives like Virgil van Dijk or Mohamed Salah. This proved inspired, as Robertson delivered a clean sheet while Tottenham's goalkeeper Vicario kept another against an attacking fullback with strong underlying data.

Sam's Continues Struggles

Sam's gameweek 30 told a very different story, yielding just 49 points and pushing him down to just inside the top 400,000. He reflects that he was ranked around 200thousand in gameweek 12, and his rank has been "slowly going backwards ever since." Perhaps most striking is his admission that through 30 gameweeks into the season, he has never achieved a single gameweek rank in the top one million—a statistic he finds "really quite embarrassing" and acknowledges undermines his credibility as an FPL content creator.

The problems for Sam were numerous. His goalkeeper Kellcher lost five points to Dravka on the bench despite Dravka conceding approximately 2.6 expected goals. Timber Williams, whom Sam backed to start, got injured early in the match. His highly-owned Gabriel (around 90% owned at his rank level) did manage a return, providing some small relief. Most painfully, Virgil van Dijk conceded a goal in the final minute, yielding only a two-pointer.

In midfield, Sam did at least get the captaincy decision correct by choosing Bruno Fernandes, who delivered as expected. However, both Rice and Bowen Anderson failed to secure clean sheets—the latter getting only eight points early before being booked and subsequently playing cautiously to avoid a second yellow. Sam's forward line proved disastrous: he sold Haaland in what he describes as a "brave" move to increase risk appetite, bringing in both João Pedro and Ekitique. Neither player started, rendering the entire transfer pointless. His bench contributed just 17 points, providing minimal rescue.

The Impending Blank Gameweek 31

The central focus of the episode turns to the upcoming gameweek 31, which Tom repeatedly warns is "nigh." This blank gameweek means five Premier League teams will not play, creating a fundamental challenge for FPL managers: they must either hold players from blanking teams (and accept zero returns) or use valuable transfers to replace them.

Tom emphasizes that the calendar presents a "small wrinkle" that most managers should consider before making transfers. Specifically, he advises that managers should "probably want to hold on to those free transfers until Friday this week" when the gameweek 31 deadline arrives. This recommendation acknowledges that teams playing in European competitions (the podcast references Arsenal, Chelsea, and Manchester City all having matches in progress) may have their Premier League fixtures affected by squad rotation or fixture changes. Waiting until Friday provides more clarity about which players will actually feature.

The discussion around wildcards is equally nuanced. Tom cautions against immediately playing a wildcard when the game reopens on Saturday, suggesting that managers might prefer to wait unless they have "better things to be doing with the international break." The implication is that patience could yield better value, as more information becomes available about team selections and fixture scheduling for the subsequent gameweeks.

Strategic Considerations for the Run-In

Beyond the immediate gameweek 31 concerns, the hosts touch on broader strategic themes relevant to the season's final phase. The conversation references the tension between "in form" players and "fixture" considerations—a classic FPL dilemma that becomes particularly acute in the business end of the season when every point matters enormously.

One specific player discussed is Wilson, with the hosts debating whether he can challenge Bruno Fernandes for the captaincy armband. This suggests Wilson's fixtures may offer particularly favorable opportunities in the coming weeks, making him a differential consideration for managers seeking an alternative to the heavily-owned Bruno.

The timing element receives substantial attention. With the international break approaching, managers face decisions about whether to use their wildcards before or after the break. The recommendation seems to favor waiting where possible, as the break provides an opportunity to reassess without the pressure of an immediate deadline.

Detailed Takeaways

Transfer Timing Matters Significantly: Tom's explicit advice to "hold on to those free transfers until Friday this week" represents a key strategic insight. With European competition fixtures potentially affecting Premier League scheduling, waiting until later in the week provides more complete information about which players will actually feature. This approach could prevent managers from making transfers based on incomplete information and potentially wasting transfers on players who don't play.

Captaincy Selection Requires Continuous Reassessment: Tom's decision to move from Ekitique to Bruno demonstrates the importance of not becoming wedded to initial plans. His concern about Ekitique's fixture congestion and potential rotation—contrasted with Bruno's remarkable consistency of double-digit hauls—shows how form and security can outweigh initial fixture analysis. Managers should remain flexible in their captaincy decisions, especially in high-variance periods like blank gameweeks.

Ownership Concentration Creates Vulnerability: Sam's experience with Gabriel (90%+ owned at his rank) illustrates the double-edged nature of popular picks. When everyone owns the same player and they blank, the collective rank suffers equally. This suggests that while popular picks provide safety, they offer limited rank gains when they succeed—and can feel particularly punishing when they fail.

The Risk-Reward Balance Shifts in Blank Weeks: Selling Haaland proved catastrophic for Sam because it coincided with a blank week where information was limited and player decisions were constrained. This underscores that aggressive moves require favorable conditions—when the information environment is uncertain, conservative approaches may preserve ranking better than high-risk transfers.

Patience With Wildcards Often Pays: Tom's recommendation to avoid immediately playing the wildcard when the game reopens suggests that waiting provides strategic advantage. The additional days allow for better information about team selections, potential fixture changes due to European competitions, and a more considered approach to the blanks and doubles coming in subsequent gameweeks.

Who This Is For

This episode is essential listening for FPL managers of all experience levels who are navigating the complexities of the season's final third. The discussion proves particularly valuable for those currently positioned in the mid-ranges of the overall rankings—those just outside the top 100k or hovering around the one million mark—as the strategic choices discussed can yield meaningful rank movements in either direction.

The episode delivers the most value to managers who are actively planning their gameweek 31 transfers and weighing whether to use their wildcard. Tom and Sam's contrasting fortunes provide a useful framework for understanding how different approaches to transfers, captaincy, and player selection can produce dramatically different outcomes even in the same gameweek. Managers who are frustrated with their season's trajectory will find Sam's candid discussion of his struggles both relatable and instructive, while successful managers can learn from Tom's decision-making process.

The timing makes this particularly relevant as the season reaches its most critical phase, where every decision carries amplified consequences for final rankings. Whether you're fighting for a top 10,000 finish, aiming for a top 100,000 rank, or simply trying to improve your position, the strategic insights about blank gameweeks, transfer timing, and captaincy selection provide actionable guidance for the weeks ahead.
