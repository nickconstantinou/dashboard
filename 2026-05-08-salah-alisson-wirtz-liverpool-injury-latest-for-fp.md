# Liverpool Injury News Ahead of FPL Gameweek 36

## Key Attacking Assets

Mohamed Salah (£14.0m) remains sidelined with a hamstring injury. Liverpool manager Arne Slot confirmed that Salah has not yet rejoined full training, though he indicated the Egyptian international is approaching a return. "Mo has also not trained with us yet but is also, like Ali, getting very, very, very close to training with us," Slot stated.

Florian Wirtz (£8.3m) was unwell in recent days but managed to train with the squad on Thursday. The German midfielder's availability for Gameweek 36 appears more promising following his participation in team training.

Giorgi Mamardashvili (£4.1m) is continuing his recovery from a leg injury. The goalkeeper is set to train today for the first time, with Liverpool's medical staff planning to assess his reaction to the session.

## Defensive and Squad Updates

Alisson continues to recover from a muscle injury and has not yet returned to training. However, Slot suggested the Brazilian goalkeeper is nearing a comeback, noting he is "very close" to rejoining the squad.

Ibrahima Konate missed some training sessions due to a personal reason but has since returned. The French defender was present at training on both Thursday and Friday.

Wataru Endo and Hugo Ekitike both appear on Liverpool's injury list. Endo is managing a foot injury while Ekitike is recovering from an Achilles problem. Both players remain unavailable for selection.

Several young players remain on the sidelines with longer-term issues. Conor Bradley (£4.9m) continues his recovery from a knee injury. Also on the injury list are Stefan Bajcetic (hamstring), Jayden Danns (hamstring), and Giovanni Leoni (knee), limiting Liverpool's squad depth in these areas.

## Fantasy Implications

For FPL managers weighing Liverpool assets this gameweek, the attacking picture remains uncertain. Salah's hamstring issue keeps him as a major doubt, while Wirtz's illness may have affected his training intensity. Mamardashvili could return to contention in goal following his first training session, though his match fitness will need assessment.

The defensive situation appears more stable with Konate back in training after his personal absence, though Endo and Ekitike join the longer injury list alongside the young players already sidelined.
