Pras' Gameweek 31 Team Selection: Navigating the First Blank Week and Planning Your Chip Strategy

The Blank Gameweek Has Arrived: What It Means for Your Season

The Fantasy Premier League landscape has shifted dramatically with the arrival of blank gameweek 31, the first double-week absence of the season. For FPL managers, this marks a pivotal moment where careful planning separates the elite from the also-rans. In his latest team selection video for The FPL Wire, content creator Pras walks viewers through the implications of this blank week, the latest European competition results that affect fixture planning, and the nuanced chip strategies that could define the season's final stretch.

Pras begins by acknowledging that blank gameweeks naturally provoke anxiety among managers, leading many to make moves that contradict their long-term visions. "It's the time when things start to take shape in terms of people start making moves that are against sort of what they want to do long term," he explains. This pressure-cooker environment makes it all the more important to maintain composure and stick to well-reasoned strategies rather than panicking based on short-term disruptions.

The video arrives at a particularly significant moment, recorded immediately after three English teams progressed in European competition. Crystal Palace's advancement through their tournament is highlighted as particularly important, while Nottingham Forest won on penalties and Aston Villa also secured progression. Pras expresses explicit satisfaction with this outcome: "More teams progressing in Europe means more carnage. So again, all good news." This counterintuitive enthusiasm stems from the reality that more English clubs in European competition creates more fixture congestion, which in turn generates more double gameweeks—opportunities for FPL managers to capitalize on accumulated matches.

Reviewing Gameweek 30: How Pras' Strategy Paid Off

Before diving into the complexities of blank gameweek planning, Pras takes a moment to assess his performance in the previous gameweek, which proved to be a substantial success. The video opens with positive news: it was a very good week both in terms of points scored and gameweek rank, with Bruno Fernandes delivering as captain after Pras ultimately decided against switching to Mohamed Salah despite considering the move.

The core of last week's success came from several key contributions. Bruno's captaincy proved decisive, while midfield options Thiago and Dango (Dango Ouattara) performed exceptionally well. The double Arsenal clean sheet—rarely owned at that point—added significant value, particularly since Ravensca and Gabriel defense owners benefited from an almost exclusive opportunity after Timber went off injured during the match. James Hill, the Bournemouth defender, continued his impressive run of form, contributing additional points to those who had retained him.

Despite this excellent showing, Pras reveals that his overall rank has remained volatile in the 500,000 range. He explains that after making a dramatic jump from 2 million to 500,000, he experienced a slight regression to 700,000 before climbing back to the 500,000 bracket. However, he frames this volatility optimistically: "These jumps make me more positive and optimistic that it's possible to catch." According to his analysis, he sits approximately 50 points away from top 100k—a gap he considers very achievable, though acknowledging that some luck will be required in the coming weeks.

Crystal Palace's European Progress: The Domino Effect on Fixtures

The video's most significant tactical update centers on Crystal Palace's advancement in European competition, which has concrete implications for the fixture calendar. Pras references a chart he presented in previous videos, now updated to reflect the new reality: Palace have progressed to the next round of their competition, which means their upcoming midweek matches are now blocked out.

This development has two critical consequences for FPL planning. First, Palace's fixture against Manchester City, which had been scheduled for gameweek 31, can no longer be accommodated, making it a guaranteed blank. Managers who had been concerned about whether Palace assets like Eze might suddenly gain a fixture need not worry—the blank is confirmed. Second, and perhaps more importantly, this means the Manchester City fixture also cannot fit into gameweek 32, eliminating the possibility of that week becoming a double gameweek as some had speculated.

Pras is careful to explain the calendar mechanics: "Crystal Palace have the second leg of their next round" in gameweek 32, which prevents any Palace double that week. This effectively narrows the window for doubles significantly. Based on current information, gameweek 33 has emerged as the confirmed double for Palace, with gameweek 36 increasingly likely to host another Palace double gameweek.

The good news, according to Pras, is that this updated information strengthens the base case scenario that he and other analysts have been projecting. While certain outcomes still need to occur for the full fixture picture to crystallize—Manchester City must beat Liverpool, Chelsea must win their FA Cup match, Arsenal must advance, and television schedulers must follow predicted patterns—the direction of travel remains consistent with earlier forecasts. "As long as we get more information, it still gives us the more likely case," Pras notes, suggesting that the scenario FPL managers have been planning around remains the most probable outcome.

The Three Chip Strategies: Finding Your Path to Glory

One of the video's most valuable sections addresses the various chip deployment strategies available to managers in these crucial weeks. Pras identifies three distinct approaches that represent the majority of viable options, each with its own logic, advantages, and ideal user profiles.

Strategy One: Wildcard in 32, Bench Boost in 33, Free Hit in 34

The most popular approach follows a straightforward progression: deploy the wildcard chip in gameweek 32 (with a Friday deadline immediately approaching), activate bench boost in gameweek 33 to maximize double gameweek returns, and then use the free hit in gameweek 34 to field an optimal team for that week's fixtures. This strategy assumes that managers have not yet used their wildcard and are willing to commit to a specific team structure for the compressed schedule ahead.

Pras acknowledges this is likely to be the dominant strategy but expresses some reservations about committing so early. He notes that the wildcard decision doesn't require immediate action despite the Friday deadline: "You're not going to miss out on much in terms of prices." His preferred approach is to wait until after the FA Cup quarterfinals have concluded, which will provide additional clarity about fixture arrangements without sacrificing meaningful opportunities.

Strategy Two: Free Hit in 33, Bench Boost in 36, Wildcard in 35

The second strategy inverts some of the timing, prioritizing a free hit during gameweek 33 (the confirmed Palace double) while deferring the wildcard to gameweek 35. This approach then uses bench boost in gameweek 36 when another Palace double is anticipated. "I just like it because my team looks nice in 32 and 34," Pras reveals, explaining that this timing aligns better with his current squad composition. He considers this approach "semi-popular" but expects it to be chosen by fewer managers than the first strategy.

Strategy Three: Wildcard in 33, Hit in 34 (Niche Option)

The third strategy is more unconventional, involving a wildcard in gameweek 33 followed by taking hits in gameweek 34 to navigate fixtures. Many managers pursuing this path may have already used their bench boost earlier in the season, which changes their calculus entirely. This approach assumes managers are content with their 32 and 34 teams and are willing to pay a hit price to optimize for the specific doubles rather than building a squad designed for those weeks from scratch.

Pras characterizes all three strategies as "viable," recognizing that different manager situations warrant different approaches. However, he makes a compelling case for patience, particularly regarding the wildcard timing: "The fact that there are three strategies and only one of them has a wildcard in gameweek 32 makes me think why don't you just wait?" His reasoning centers on the upcoming international break, which will provide complete clarity on which fixtures fall into gameweeks 33 and 34. "Within this international break we will know 100% which fixture is going into 33 and which is staying in 34," he argues, suggesting that this additional information is worth waiting for despite the compressed deadline.

Fixture Analysis: Using the Ticker to Target Your Chips

Pras introduces a sophisticated fixture ticker analysis, created using data from Solio, that helps managers visualize which teams to target depending on their chosen chip strategy. This tool organizes teams by projected attacking returns for specific gameweek windows, enabling targeted transfers and acquisitions before committing chips.

For Free Hit in 33 Strategies

When planning to free hit in gameweek 33, the data indicates that Liverpool represents the optimal target for attacking assets. Pras emphasizes that while questions remain about Salah's fitness—his status will be clarified in Slot's press conference the following day—managers should generally be bullish on Liverpool assets. The reasoning is straightforward: Liverpool faces both Fulham (home) and Crystal Palace (home) in gameweeks 32 and 34, surrounding their gameweek 33 double. This fixture profile makes Liverpool players excellent free hit selections in 33 while also being viable picks in the surrounding weeks.

Manchester United emerges as the second-highest projected attacking team for both gameweeks 32 and 34, making them equally suitable for this strategy. Players like Mainoo or Bruno Fernandes offer consistent exposure across the relevant weeks, while Ibumo represents another attractive option for managers seeking Manchester United exposure.

Fulham attack also projects well under this strategy, which Pras notes explains the current popularity of Wilson (Callum Wilson) and Rogers among the FPL community. Pras offers a nuanced observation about Seaboschi (Alexis Sanchez), suggesting that while the Fulham forward is often overlooked, he remains an excellent option particularly because he would likely take penalties if Salah misses a game through injury. "He should be on penalties if Salah misses out," Pras explains, highlighting the value of penalty-taking forwards in gameweek scenarios where goals are at a premium.

The analysis extends to defenses as well, with Liverpool, Manchester United, West Ham, Tottenham, and Nottingham Forest emerging as the defensive teams to prioritize when free hitting in gameweek 33. These teams offer the best projected clean sheet returns for the relevant fixtures.

For Free Hit in 34 Strategies

Managers planning a free hit in gameweek 34 should focus on entirely different teams according to the fixture ticker. Chelsea's attack emerges as the primary target, with players like Pedro Neto, Jackson, and particularly Palmer representing excellent entry points for this strategy. Newcastle also projects strongly for gameweek 34, offering managers an alternative pathway.

This creates an interesting dilemma for managers weighing whether to acquire Newcastle or Fulham assets this week. Pras provides clear guidance: "Depends on what your strategy is." For managers who might not end up using their free hit in 34 and need to field a competitive team immediately, Newcastle players make more sense because they can be benched in gameweek 32 if needed. Conversely, managers certain they will free hit in 34 should target Fulham players who maintain their value across the relevant gameweeks. "If you want to play them in 34 then go for Fulham," Pras summarizes.

Defensive Strategy Nuances

For defensive investments, the analysis reveals interesting patterns. Arsenal, despite blanking in gameweek 31, remain the highest-projected defensive team for gameweek 33 because they also double in that week. Newcastle, Brighton, and Aston Villa round out the recommended defensive targets depending on which strategy managers pursue.

Pras notes that Brighton players represent an interesting under-the-radar option that many managers might not be considering. While not the flashiest choice, Brighton defenders offer solid minutes and reasonable fixture difficulty, making them viable budget options for managers constructing free hit teams.

Key Takeaways for Gameweek 31 Planning

The Blank Week Doesn't Require Panic Selling

With Crystal Palace confirmed to blank in gameweek 31, managers holding Palace assets like Eze should not feel pressured to make emergency sales. The progression through European competition has actually clarified the fixture picture, making it easier to plan around a single-week absence rather than facing uncertainty about when that blank might occur. Pras explicitly reassures managers who had already sold Palmer or other key assets that they need not worry about unexpected fixture changes.

Patience with Wildcard Timing May Be Optimal

While the Friday deadline looms, Pras advocates strongly for waiting until after FA Cup quarterfinal results provide complete clarity. The information gained during this brief waiting period—specifically knowing exactly which fixtures fall into gameweeks 33 and 34—is more valuable than any minor price changes managers might fear missing. "You're not going to miss out on much in terms of prices," he emphasizes, suggesting that the marginal gains from acting immediately are far outweighed by the strategic benefits of waiting.

Your Chip Strategy Should Match Your Current Team

The three viable strategies each suit different manager situations. Managers whose current teams already look strong for gameweeks 32 and 34 might prefer Strategy Two (free hit 33, wildcard 35), while those who need a complete rebuild for the double weeks should lean toward Strategy One. Rather than forcing a predetermined approach, assess your current squad and choose the path that requires the least chip investment to reach an optimal team for the compressed schedule.

Fixture Tickers Reveal Hidden Opportunities

The analysis from Solio demonstrates that different chip strategies should target entirely different teams. Liverpool and Manchester United dominate the gameweek 33 free hit landscape, while Chelsea and Newcastle become critical for gameweek 34 free hits. Managers considering Newcastle versus Fulham assets this week should base their decision entirely on which strategy they plan to employ—if any—and whether they might need to bench those players in weeks where they don't blank.
