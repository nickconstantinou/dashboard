FPL Notes: Gibbs-White's Red-Hot Form and Forest's Tactical Setup

Morgan Gibbs-White Continues Explosion

Morgan Gibbs-White remains one of Fantasy Premier League's most essential assets. The Nottingham Forest midfielder struck powerfully into the bottom corner to score his 13th Premier League goal of the season, with 10 of those goals coming in 2026. His remarkable scoring run under Vitor Pereira sees him averaging a goal every 98.6 minutes—an excellent ratio for an attacking player priced at £7.6m. Gibbs-White is tied with Igor Thiago (£7.3m) for joint-most Premier League goals in 2026, highlighting his status as a genuine top-flight performer this calendar year.

Elliot Anderson's Defensive Contributions

Forest's budget option Elliot Anderson has proven reliable for FPL managers chasing clean sheet points. He's now hit the DefCon threshold in three of his last four matches, and 24 times across the season. At £5.6m, Anderson provides a cost-effective route into Forest's defence while offering consistent returns in the defensive metrics that matter for Fantasy purposes.

Forest's Tactical Approach

Pereira decided to use two strikers in an attacking 4-4-2 formation during Forest's recent away fixture. This setup maximises Gibbs-White's involvement while providing additional firepower alongside the striker pairing. The formation change appears to be paying dividends as the Tricky Trees extended their unbeaten run to eight games in all competitions.

Sunderland's Defensive Struggles

Sunderland have conceded nine times in their last two matches. Only Burnley (31) and Spurs (30) have conceded more than them (27) in the Premier League since the turn of the year. This dramatic drop-off makes Sunderland attackers attractive options against the Black Cats in the coming weeks.

Injury Concerns

Two Forest players picked up issues in the recent fixture. Jair Cunha landed awkwardly and had to come off with a potential shoulder issue. Meanwhile, Ibrahim Sangare picked up a minor knock. Managers should monitor these situations ahead of Forest's upcoming matches.

Forest's League Position and Upcoming Fixtures

The win has taken Forest eight points clear of 18th, which is currently occupied by Tottenham Hotspur, providing significant breathing room in the Premier League survival battle. Looking ahead, they prepare for Thursday's UEFA Europa League semi-final first leg against Aston Villa, followed by their Gameweek 35 trip to Chelsea. The busy schedule may influence rotation decisions for Pereira.
