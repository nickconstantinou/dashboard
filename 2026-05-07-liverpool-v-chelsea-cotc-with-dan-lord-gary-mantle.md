# Liverpool v Chelsea Preview: FPL Implications from Planet FPL

## Liverpool's Weekend Setback

Liverpool suffered a disappointment at the weekend, losing to Manchester United. This result adds extra weight to the fixture against Chelsea, as the Reds look to bounce back from a result that clearly frustrates supporters.

However, Liverpool remain in a strong position in the Champions League race. The team sits six points ahead of Manchester United with three games remaining in the season, backed by a superior goal difference.

## Manager Future clarified

The discussion on Planet FPL addressed Liverpool manager Arne Slot's position. It was suggested that if Liverpool secure Champions League qualification, Slot is likely to remain in charge next season. The conversation indicated that Slot staying at the club is becoming increasingly probable.

## Newcastle Transfer Moves

The show touched on Newcastle's recent recruitment strategy. The Magpies invested approximately 200 million on two forwards. Dan Lord and Gary Mantle debated whether this expenditure translates to guaranteed returns, noting the investment has not yet yielded consistent results.

## Alexander Isak Injury Update

For FPL managers considering Newcastle assets, Alexander Isak's availability remains severely limited. The striker is confirmed out for the rest of the season due to an ongoing problem. His issue has been classified as a growing strain, raising questions about whether he may play again this season.

The discussion suggested Isak does not appear capable of handling two games per week if Newcastle qualify for Champions League. Sources indicate he may not be available until January at the earliest. At his current price point, the injury situation makes him an unattractive option for the foreseeable future.

## Key Takeaways for FPL Managers

Liverpool's title challenge remains active despite the Manchester United defeat, with a six-point buffer providing comfort. The manager situation appears stable if Champions League football is secured. For those considering Newcastle forwards, Isak's extended absence makes him impossible to recommend at his price.
