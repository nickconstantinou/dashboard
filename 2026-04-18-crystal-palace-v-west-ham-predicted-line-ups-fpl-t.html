A fixture that rarely sets pulses racing has quietly become one of Gameweek 33's most consequential matchups — at least for Fantasy Premier League managers weighing their final moves.

Crystal Palace host West Ham United at Selhurst Park on Monday, April 20, with kickoff scheduled for 20:00 BST. The game has been confirmed as part of a Double Gameweek, meaning both sides offer doubled returns for anyone brave enough to load up on assets from either camp.

The Context That Makes This Interesting

Palace sit 13th with 42 points and a goal difference of -1. West Ham are 17th with 32 points and a goal difference of -17. Neither side is playing for much in the league table, which is precisely why this game deserves more attention than it typically receives — and why Double Gameweek activation here carries real strategic weight.

The more interesting question isn't whether either team has something to play for. It's which Palace and West Ham players are actually worth owning given their respective injury situations and form patterns.

Recent Form: What the Numbers Show

Over the last six Gameweeks, both sides have shown enough attacking intent to warrant a second look. Palace's recent run of WLWDW suggests a side that can compete without being prolific. West Ham's LWDLW sequence tells a grimmer story — consistently finding themselves on the wrong side of results.
