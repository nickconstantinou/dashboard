Pricing Context

Rayan Cherki (£6.6m), Antoine Semenyo (£8.1m) and Jeremy Doku (£6.4m) are the three names in question for FPL managers looking at Manchester City's midfield options. Price differences create immediate strategic implications for squad construction.

Goal Threat Analysis

Cherki comes out on top when it comes to goal threat, with four of his 17 shots from set plays—a joint-high with Haaland among all City players. His total of 19.8 minutes per shot is far superior to Semenyo (31.6) and Doku (37.8). The Frenchman is also ahead for minutes per shot in the box and expected goal (xG). Two of Cherki's attempts have been Opta 'big chances', compared to Doku's one.

Positional Role Comparison

In the current system, Semenyo has been shifted into a wider role, whereas Cherki is operating much closer to Haaland. This positional difference directly impacts their respective goal-scoring opportunities and involvement in penalty-box actions.

Recent Form Trajectory

As for Semenyo, his form has dropped off. Semenyo's shots from the last four matches consequently look like this, with zero big chances. This represents a significant concern for managers considering the £8.1m price point.

Fixture Context

Manchester City will double in Gameweek 36, making this comparison particularly timely for managers planning ahead.

Verdict

The evidence points toward Cherki as the strongest FPL option among the three. He combines the lowest price among the creative options with superior shot frequency, better positioning relative to Haaland, and stronger recent form metrics. However, managers should monitor team news and selection trends before committing.
