FPL Gameweek 35 Differentials: Three Low-Owned Picks to Consider

Dango Ouattara – Brentford – £5.8m

Dango Ouattara has emerged as a compelling differential option for FPL managers seeking value in midfield. The Brentford attacker ranks second for most big chances per 90 minutes among all Fantasy midfielders who have made more than five starts this season, averaging 0.71 such opportunities per match. His underlying creative output has been particularly impressive since Gameweek 25, having created eight big chances for his teammates during that period. At his current price point, Ouattara represents a budget-friendly route to Brentford's attacking threat, especially given his side's strong home form this calendar year.

Alexander Isak has endured a frustrating campaign, yet his recent form suggests he may be approaching a turning point. His opportunistic strike against Crystal Palace in Gameweek 34 could provide the catalyst for improvement as the season reaches its conclusion. Isak has found the net three times in his last eight league matches, indicating that goals may be returning at a more consistent rate. Bruno Guimaraes – Newcastle United

Bruno Guimaraes has been a revelation at St James' Park this season, delivering extraordinary returns in home matches. The Brazilian midfielder has scored eight goals and supplied five assists across 11 starts and two substitute appearances in Newcastle's home games, accumulating 96 points—the sixth-most of any player in the league. Perhaps most remarkably, Guimaraes has been directly involved in 52% of Newcastle's goals when on the pitch at St James' Park. For managers seeking a differential route into Newcastle's attacking assets, Guimaraes offers a compelling alternative to more heavily-owned teammates, combining elite goal involvement with the security of consistent home appearances.
