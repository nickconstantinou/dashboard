Double Gameweek 33: Why FPL Managers Shouldn't Sleep on Forest vs Burnley

The Double Gameweek 33 fixture between Nottingham Forest and Burnley doesn't look like much at first glance. Two relegation-threatened sides, both spiraling in poor form, meeting at the City Ground on Sunday 19 April (14:00 BST). But for Fantasy Premier League managers hunting differential value, this matchup deserves closer attention.

Both teams face Double Gameweek 33, meaning a single fixture transforms into two appearances for FPL purposes. That math alone elevates the tactical picture from mid-table irrelevance to genuine transfer-window opportunity.

The Form Book Says Avoid—But That's Precisely the Point

Nottingham Forest sit 16th with 33 points from 32 matches, a goal difference of -12, and a recent run of LDDWD. Burnley are worse off in every metric: 19th, 20 points, a catastrophic -30 goal difference, and form that reads LLDLL across their last five fixtures. Five consecutive losses. The Clarets are drifting toward the Championship.

So why would any rational FPL manager target either side?

Because ownership percentages on Forest and Burnley players are likely negligible at this stage of the season. A brace from an overlooked asset could be the swing needed in mini-leagues and overall ranks. The Double Gameweek multiplies that ceiling.

Nottingham Forest (16th, 33pts, -12 GD, LDDWD)

The question for FPL purposes is whether Forest's better expected goal involvement players over the last six Gameweeks show up in the starting XI or on the bench. Burnley (19th, 20pts, -30 GD, LLDLL)

Burnley's situation is more acute. Five straight defeats suggest a squad running low on confidence, but Double Gameweek exposure means any attacking output gets doubled. Managers targeting Burnley assets need to weigh minimal form against multiplied potential.

The Real Calculation

For FPL managers, the Forest vs Burnley Double Gameweek 33 isn't about supporting a title charge or chasing Champions League spots. It's a numbers game: low ownership, double fixtures, and two sides in enough distress that rotation could open doors for previously sidelined players.

Check press conference updates before finalizing transfers. But don't dismiss this fixture entirely—sometimes the best differentials hide in the ugliest form.
