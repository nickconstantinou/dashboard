Who is the best captain for FPL Gameweek 35?

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

Overview

Fantasy Premier League managers face a pivotal captaincy decision heading into Gameweek 35. With the poll data now available, the landscape of popular choices has come into clearer focus, revealing both the dominant favorites and potential differential options for managers looking to gain ground in their mini-leagues.

The Clear Favorite: Erling Haaland

Manchester City's Erling Haaland maintains his position as the standout choice for the armband this gameweek. The Norwegian striker has captured approximately 35% of captaincy poll votes, establishing a significant lead over his nearest challengers.

Haaland's appeal is reinforced by his status as the Premier League's top scorer this season. At £14.6m, he represents the premium option in the gameweek 35 captaincy market. His fixture—a trip to Everton on Monday night—provides a favorable backdrop for his attacking potential.

The Main Challenger: Bruno Fernandes

Trailing significantly behind Haaland but emerging as the primary alternative is Manchester United's Bruno Fernandes. The Portuguese midfielder currently sits second in the captaincy poll with just under 14% of the vote.

Fernandes brings compelling recent form to this discussion. He has returned his 20th Fantasy assist of the season and hasn't recorded a blank since Gameweek 27. Perhaps most notably, since Michael Carrick took charge of Manchester United, no player in the league has scored more points than Fernandes. This extraordinary consistency makes him a strong candidate for managers seeking an alternative to the popular Haaland choice.

The pricing differential is substantial—Fernandes costs £10.4m compared to Haaland's £14.6m—potentially freeing up budget for investments elsewhere in the squad.

Budget-Friendly Options

Dominic Calvert-Lewin

Leeds United's Dominic Calvert-Lewin has garnered approximately 13% of captaincy poll support for his side's home fixture against relegated Burnley. At £5.8m, he represents a budget-friendly route to captaincy points if managers believe in a Leeds attacking performance against a side with little to play for.

Noah Okafor and Igor Thiago

Beyond the main contenders, Noah Okafor and Igor Thiago have each attracted more than 5% backing in the poll. Okafor, priced at £5.6m, carries a flagged status that managers may wish to monitor before confirming him as captain. Igor Thiago at £7.4m represents another Leeds option in the differential conversation.

Differential Alternatives

With the captaincy landscape described as "wide-open" this gameweek, players from Aston Villa, Brentford, Leeds United, and Chelsea could provide genuine differential alternatives for managers looking to capitalize on less obvious selections. These fixtures may offer favorable matchup conditions that could yield unexpected returns for those willing to look beyond the obvious choices.

Key Statistical Context

Bruno Fernandes currently sits just eight points behind Erling Haaland in the overall FPL standings. This gap has narrowed considerably and reflects Fernandes' sustained excellence since Carrick's appointment. However, Haaland's superior ceiling—reflected in his 35% share of the captaincy poll—demonstrates that managers still view him as the highest-potential armband choice despite the premium cost.

Verdict Framework

The captaincy decision for Gameweek 35 ultimately hinges on two competing philosophies:

  • Haaland (35% of votes): Best for managers seeking alignment with the crowd and access to the league's highest goal-scoring threat in a favorable fixture
  • Fernandes (14% of votes): Best for managers seeking differentiation while still accessing elite-level form and consistency

    • The gap between these two options—both in terms of poll percentage and price—remains significant, making Haaland the default choice for most managers while leaving Fernandes as the strategic alternative for those pursuing a differential approach.
