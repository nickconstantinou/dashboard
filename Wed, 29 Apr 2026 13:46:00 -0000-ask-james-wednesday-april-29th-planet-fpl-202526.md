# Ask James: Wednesday April 29th | Planet FPL 2025/26

## Introduction

This week's Ask James column addresses key FPL considerations heading into the closing stages of the season. With limited options available and fixture congestion affecting certain teams, managers must carefully weigh their decisions.

## Cole Palmer Fitness Update

Cole Palmer was not 100% fit ahead of Gameweek 34. The Chelsea midfielder, priced at £10.5m, continues to carry some concern despite contributing to matches. James noted that while Palmer "didn't stop," suggesting he still featured, he was not operating at full capacity. The game is scheduled for Monday, and both Palmer and a second player (implied to be a teammate) are expected to start.

## Fixture Congestion Notes

Two important fixture updates emerged regarding team schedules. Bournemouth will not have a double gameweek in Gameweek 36, eliminating what could have been a valuable asset for managers seeking additional matches. Meanwhile, Brighton has one game a week for the remainder of the season, which should alleviate concerns about fixture congestion and rotation for any Brighton assets managers may hold.

## Antonee Robinson's Outstanding Form

Antonee Robinson, priced at £4.9m and playing for Fulham, is on an outstanding run of form at present. While Robinson plays for Fulham, James specifically noted his form "in the Forest" context, suggesting strong performances against Nottingham Forest or potentially referencing his defensive contributions. At his current price point, Robinson represents an affordable option with recent returns.

## James's Team Strategy

James revealed his ownership of several premium assets, including Cole Palmer, Joao Pedro, Bijou, and Noni Madueke. When discussing selection decisions, he mentioned needing to "play two out of Palmer, Joao Pedro, Bijou, and Van Hek," indicating difficult choices even at the top end of his squad. This highlights the concentration of value in certain price brackets and the challenges of fitting multiple premium forwards into one lineup.

## Casemiro Set Piece Threat

Casemiro emerges as a significant set piece threat according to James's analysis. The Brazilian midfielder is described as "phenomenal from set plays" with access to "the best creator in the league providing for him." James specifically noted that Casemiro's recent goal against Wolves was a planned set piece routine designed to connect Harry Maguire with Casemiro. The strategy involves Casemiro making a runner's movement away from Maguire as the free kick develops, creating space for the assist.

James suggests Casemiro might be the sixth highest scoring midfielder in the game currently, making him a differential option at his price point with underlying set piece value.

## Newcastle Defence Update

Ryan did not start the Leeds game, raising questions about his starting status. James noted Ryan is "obviously first choice" but the non-start against Leeds suggests rotation risk. On a more positive note, David Brooks is back available for Newcastle, providing defensive cover options for managers.

## Captaincy Conundrum

James characterized this week's captaincy options as "a flip" with "no wonderful alternatives." Managers face limited premium options this Gameweek, potentially making it a week for differential captain picks or spreading captaincy risk across multiple assets. The lack of clear standout options suggests patience and fixture-based decisions may prove most effective.
