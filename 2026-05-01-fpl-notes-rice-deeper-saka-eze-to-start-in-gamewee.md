# FPL Notes: Rice Deeper Role, Saka and Eze Eye Gameweek 35 Returns

## Match Report: Arsenal vs Atletico Madrid

Arsenal played out a 1-1 draw with Atletico Madrid in their midweek Champions League fixture. The match saw both teams share the points, with penalties playing a significant role in the outcome. Viktor Gyokeres scored from the penalty spot for Arsenal, converting his attempt confidently. For Atletico Madrid, Julian Alvarez equalised from the penalty spot after Ben White conceded a handball. The incident occurred when Diego Llorente's shot struck Ben White's leg and then his hand, awarding Atletico the penalty that Alvarez dispatched to draw the visitors level.

## Tactical Development: Declan Rice in Deeper Role

Manager Mikel Arteta deployed Declan Rice in a deeper-lying number six role during this match. The positional adjustment saw Rice operate as Arsenal's primary defensive shield in front of the back four. The tactical switch proved effective, with Rice completing 53 of his 55 pass attempts during the first half alone. Additionally, Rice accumulated 57 touches of the ball during the opening period, which represented the most of any player on the pitch. These numbers suggest Rice is comfortable operating in this deeper position while still maintaining a high volume of possession and progressive passing.

## Injury Updates: Havertz Ruled Out, Eze Returns

Kai Havertz was unavailable for this midweek fixture after picking up a muscle injury during the previous weekend's action. His absence removes a forward option from Arsenal's squad as they approach a crucial run of fixtures. In positive news for Crystal Palace assets, Eberechi Eze recovered from a muscular niggle that forced his substitution during Gameweek 34. Eze came on as a substitute in this match, suggesting he is available and may be considered for a starting place in the upcoming fixture.

## Player Watch: Saka Impresses as Substitute

Bukayo Saka entered the match as a substitute and featured on the right wing. Despite a lack of end product during his time on the pitch, Saka looked bright and threatening in his brief appearance. His performance after coming on indicates he is fully fit and ready to compete for a starting position. Saka's fitness level appears unaffected by any recent concerns, making him a viable option for managers considering him for their FPL squads.

## Gameweek 35 FPL Outlook

For FPL managers planning ahead, both Bukayo Saka and Eberechi Eze could potentially start in Gameweek 35. Given Eze's successful recovery and Saka's encouraging performance as a substitute, it would not be a surprise to see both players named in the starting eleven for their respective teams. Managers holding either player may be rewarded with a start in the upcoming fixture, providing additional attacking returns. However, Havertz owners should note his continued absence due to the muscle injury sustained last weekend.
