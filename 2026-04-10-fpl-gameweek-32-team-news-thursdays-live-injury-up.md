# FPL Gameweek 32 Team News: Thursday's Live Injury Updates

## The Full Story

Fantasy Premier League managers received a significant injury update on Thursday, April 9, 2026, as four Premier League managers—representing Burnley, Fulham, Leeds United, and Wolverhampton Wanderers—addressed the media ahead of Gameweek 32. The press conferences provided crucial information that could impact thousands of FPL teams making transfer decisions before the deadline.

Leeds United manager Daniel Farke delivered particularly concerning news, confirming that defensivemidfielder Anton Stach and center-back Joe Rodon will both miss Gameweek 32 with ankle injuries and are not expected to return until May. The German midfielder suffered his injury during the club's cup match, while Rodon's issue stemmed from a separate incident. Adding to Leeds' injury woes, Daniel James is also sidelined with an adductor strain sustained in training before the cup fixture. However, Farke expressed optimism regarding three other players: Noah Okafor (back), Gabriel Gudmundsson (groin), and Jaka Bijol (knock), all of whom are currently doubts but are expected to recover in time for Monday's trip to Old Trafford against Manchester United.

Fulham's press conference revealed that Tete, Kevin, and Reed all remain unavailable, though the club received positive news with their international players returning from national duty unscathed. Burnley confirmed Hannibal remains out, while Amdouni is still too soon to return, and Laurent is serving a ban. Wolverhampton Wanderers will be without both Johnstone and Doherty for the upcoming fixture.

Daniel Farke provided detailed insight into the injuries, stating: "Normally, you would say it's not realistic for both of them that they will be able to help us in April and we will just have them back in May, but of course, our medical department is trying everything. We will try, step by step, day by day, to improve the situation. If they would be able to feature at some point in April, it would be a bonus."

Regarding the three doubtful players, Farke added: "All three of them are doubts. If we were to play the game tomorrow, all three wouldn't be available. Thank God the game is just on Monday, so we have a bit more time. I'm hopeful that we have all three of them ready for the game."
