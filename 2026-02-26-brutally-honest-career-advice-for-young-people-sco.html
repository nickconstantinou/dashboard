Brutally Honest Career Advice for Young People | Scott Galloway

In this video, professor and entrepreneur Scott Galloway shares his brutally honest career advice for young people, centered around the idea that opportunity is a function of density. He references a fascinating study showing that we become the average of our five closest friends‚Äînot just in income, but in body mass index, political values, and even the neighborhoods we live in. This underscores his core message: where you live and who you surround yourself with fundamentally shapes your trajectory.

Galloway strongly advises young, ambitious people to get to the biggest city possible‚ÄîNew York or San Francisco in the US, London if in Europe. He argues that two-thirds of economic growth happens in just 20 cities, and being surrounded by the best players elevates your own game. While he acknowledges living in expensive cities like Brooklyn is difficult, he emphasizes that the competition forces you to become really good at what you do, and you'll be inspired by high-achievers who push you to level up.

Perhaps his most provocative point is the comparison between the US and Europe. Galloway contends that America is still the best place to make money because of its unique culture around risk and failure. He describes how British friends grew up fearing "tall poppy syndrome"‚Äîbeing punished for standing out‚Äîwhereas America celebrates those who "go for it," even if they fail. His strategy? Start nine businesses, because all you need is one win. He's had plenty of failures but argues that as long as you treated investors ethically, past failures don't hurt your ability to raise money again.
