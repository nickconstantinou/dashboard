Crystal Palace v West Ham United: Team News Analysis

Crystal Palace Squad Changes

Crystal Palace manager Oliver Glasner has made four changes to the XI that started Thursday's UEFA Conference League fixture against Fiorentina. This continues the club's rotation policy between European matches, with a similar group of players dropping to the bench for this Premier League encounter against West Ham United.

Midfield Absences

Adam Wharton remains unavailable for selection, ruled out with an adductor injury. The £5.0m-rated midfielder continues his recovery period.

In the engine room, Jefferson Lerma and Will Hughes have been handed starting berths, replacing the rotated players from the midweek European fixture.

Defensive Update

Maxence Lacroix has passed a fitness test and is available for selection despite manager hinting at potential ligament damage concerns following Thursday's match. The £5.1m defender features in the starting lineup.

Attacking Options on the Bench

Jean-Philippe Mateta has his legs spared once again and starts on the bench in the league. The £7.6m-rated forward is not included in the XI following the manager's rotation strategy.

Daichi Kamada has also dropped to the bench for this fixture.

Wide Forward Situation

Ismaila Sarr is named among the substitutes for the second Gameweek running. The wide forward misses out on a starting place despite Palace's attacking setup.

West Ham United Approach

For the visitors, manager Nuno Espirito Santo has stuck with the same XI that started West Ham's 4-0 win over Wolverhampton Wanderers, showing faith in the side that delivered that commanding performance.
