With international breaks, Champions League matches, and various domestic cup competitions still to be resolved, managers who maintain transfer flexibility will outperform those who spend recklessly. Zophar's desire to use only one transfer this gameweek reflects sound risk management that could prove decisive if key injuries emerge from international duty.

Finally, the analysis of Manchester United's fixture run reveals how a team's perceived quality matters less than their schedule positioning. United may not be a "top four" side this season, but their Gameweeks 32 and 34 fixtures align perfectly with Zophar's strategy, making Bruno Fernandes and company valuable assets precisely when they are most needed.
