FPL Wildcard Strategy: Gameweek 32 vs. Gameweek 35 — The Complete Analysis

The Full Story

For Fantasy Premier League managers still holding their Wildcard chip, the strategic decision of when to play it could make or break their season. The two most popular windows remaining are Gameweek 32 and Gameweek 35, and each presents distinct advantages and drawbacks that depend heavily on your squad composition and chip arsenal.

A Gameweek 32 Wildcard positions you ideally for the lucrative Double Gameweek 33, where six teams—Bournemouth, Brighton and Hove Albion, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds United, and Manchester City—face double fixtures. This approach allows you to load up on "doublers" while simultaneously setting yourself up for a Bench Boost in the same gameweek, potentially Fielding 14 players who all have two matches. The strategy also enables a Free Hit in Gameweek 34, when those same six teams blank out, letting you construct an entirely new squad optimized for that specific round.

The key teams to target for a Gameweek 32 Wildcard include Brighton, who travel to Burnley in what analysts describe as a "perfect entry point" for a triple-up, and Manchester City, who visit Chelsea in Gameweek 32 before their Double Gameweek 33. Chelsea have kept just one clean sheet in their last 15 Premier League matches, making City's attacking assets particularly appealing. Leeds United also emerges as a solid long-term investment, with favorable fixtures in Gameweeks 35 through 38 (against Burnley, Tottenham, Brighton, and West Ham).

Why This Matters

The Wildcard is one of FPL's most powerful chips, allowing managers to completely restructure their squad without penalty. Using it at the wrong time can cost you hundreds of thousands of overall rank—or the entire season. The Double Gameweek 33 represents one of the remaining high-scoring opportunities in the season, and a Gameweek 32 Wildcard maximizes your exposure to teams playing twice while also preserving your Bench Boost chip for the same gameweek.

This strategic decision becomes especially critical because the same six teams that double in Gameweek 33 blank in Gameweek 34. The Gameweek 32 Wildcard-to-Free Hit combination creates a two-week tactical swing that can generate enormous point differentials against rivals who haven't planned as carefully.
