# FPL Gameweek 37 Team News: Thursday's Live Injury Updates

Thursday's press conferences brought mixed news across the Premier League, with Arsenal providing the most substantial updates while other clubs offered limited information heading into Gameweek 37.

## Arsenal

Arsenal manager Mikel Arteta confirmed that his squad is in good shape ahead of the Double Gameweek, stating: "We have most players available now. Okay, we lost Ben [White] the other day, but the other ones are coming back as well."

However, the situation remains unclear for defender Riccardo Calafiori (£5.6m). The Italian international came off at half-time last weekend, and his availability remains uncertain. Arteta indicated: "We don't know whether he's going to be available. We still have days ahead. Hopefully, it's nothing serious."

The news on Ben White makes for concerning reading for FPL managers. Arteta confirmed the injury appears long-term, stating it is "not going to happen in many, many weeks."

Fellow defender Jurrien Timber is also likely still sidelined with an ankle issue. Arteta noted: "The two players that we have, they are not available at the moment."

When asked for further team news, Arteta admitted: "Not much [team news] at the moment, we still have a few days till Monday, so we'll have to wait and see."

## Liverpool

Liverpool's squad news from Thursday included positive updates on two key players.

Mohamed Salah (£13.3m) is available for selection as a substitute. Meanwhile, Florian Wirtz (£7.5m) is still affected by a stomach bug and could be a doubt for the Double Gameweek.

Goalkeeper Alisson (£5.5m) is reportedly close to returning from injury.

## Chelsea

Managers with Chelsea assets should note that the club's Gameweek 37 fixture is not until next Tuesday, meaning their Double Gameweek 37 assets will not activate until Gameweek 38.

## Bournemouth and Tottenham

Those targeting Bournemouth or Tottenham assets should be aware that team news for both clubs is unlikely to arrive before Friday's deadline. Managers will need to wait until closer to the deadline to finalise their plans for these sides.
