# Az Discusses His Latest Draft & Rank History | Pre Season | Fantasy Premier League Tips 2025/26

## A Season of Reflection: FPL Veteran Az Opens Up About Performance, Strategy, and the Road Ahead

The new Fantasy Premier League season is now just over a week away, and the FPL content community is in full swing with team reveals, draft discussions, and strategic planning. In this latest video from FDL Blackbox, Az—the longtime FPL content creator and Scout contributor—delivers something more substantive than a typical team reveal. Instead, he addresses the criticism head-on, walks through his historical performance with remarkable transparency, and offers a candid assessment of where his approach has gone wrong in recent seasons.

This video stands out from typical preseason content because Az chooses vulnerability over bravado. Rather than simply unveiling his squad and explaining his reasoning, he confronts the elephant in the room: his poorly performing content has drawn significant backlash on social media, with some questioning whether he deserves the "expert" label attached to his work through his partnerships with the Premier League's official FPL Show and Scout. The video therefore serves dual purposes—it previews his 2025/26 draft while functioning as an honest reckoning with three years of disappointing results.

The channel context matters here. FDL Blackbox operates as a collaborative effort between three creators—Az, Mark, and Andy—with Luke also joining the team. Andy has already posted his team reveal earlier in the week, and Az expresses hope that Luke will overcome his reluctance to go solo and contribute his own reveal. This creates a comprehensive suite of perspectives heading into the new season, though Az's contribution prioritizes raw honesty over flashy team announcements.

## The Honest Assessment: Addressing Criticism of FPL Content Creators

Az begins by acknowledging the considerable negativity currently circulating on Twitter and other platforms regarding FPL content creators. He admits he's received substantial criticism, much of it justified given his recent performances. Rather than deflecting or becoming defensive, he lays out his complete FPL record spanning nearly two decades, allowing viewers to form their own conclusions.

The "expert" label has become a particular point of contention. Az explains that the Premier League's official FPL Show uses this term as a marketing designation—he would never apply it to himself. When viewers have asked why he doesn't simply ask them to stop calling him an expert, he points out that he has no control over how the Premier League chooses to market their contributors. The alternative would be declining work he genuinely enjoys with organizations he respects. "If the choice is I'll be called an expert reluctantly but carry on doing it, or not do it, then obviously I'm going to take the former," he explains, framing this as a practical rather than egotistical decision.

This transparency reveals Az's fundamental philosophy: he creates content because he loves it, not because he claims superiority. He has a full-time job outside of FPL content creation and approaches his YouTube work as a passion project that has sustained him since the 2007/08 season. He's not willing to abandon something that brings him joy simply because critics on social media find his recent rankings embarrassing.

## A Decade and a Half of FPL History: The Full Record

Az's FPL journey spans from 2007 to the present, and he walks through his performance in distinct blocks, providing specific ranks and average finishes that tell the story of a player who experienced significant improvement, sustained success, and then a notable decline.

**The Learning Years (2007-2009):** Az characterizes his first three seasons as a learning period, with an average rank of approximately 186,000. He was essentially discovering the game during these years, building the foundational knowledge that would later inform his content creation.

**The Breakout Period (2010-2012):** This three-year stretch marked his emergence as a serious player. He achieved his first top 5,000 finish in 2010, finishing 3,800th overall. The following year brought a 175,000 rank, but 2012 proved decisive—he finished 10,600th, missing the top 10,000 by a single point. Az remembers this season vividly, describing how that agonizing near-miss drove his determination to return stronger.

**The Peak Years (2013-2016):** This represents Az's most consistent period of success. His average rank across these four seasons hovered around 20,000, including another top 5,000 finish and a remarkable 2,000th place in 2014. He describes this as "a good couple of years," noting that his three-year average during this period never dropped below 63,000—impressive by any standard.

**The Scout Cast Era (2016-2018):** Az joined the Scout cast toward the end of the 2016/17 season, finishing approximately 59,000th. However, the following year delivered his personal best: an 800th-place finish. He followed this with a 62,000 rank the next season, bringing his three-year average to around 40,000. During this period, he was climbing the official "hall of fame" leaderboards, though he never broke into the top 10 overall.

**The Blackbox Years (2019-2021):** Az began working with Blackbox in 2020, which coincided with a dip in his personal performance. He finished 135,000th in his first Blackbox season, though he recalls being significantly lower in the ranks before that point. The 2020/21 season showed improvement, and 2021/22 proved "really good," bringing his three-year average to approximately 52,000.

**The Decline (2022-2025):** Az doesn't sugarcoat this period. The 2022/23 season saw him finish 143,000th, though he was around one millionth in the world with six weeks remaining before staging a remarkable comeback that ultimately saw him pip Mark (his Blackbox co-host) by a single point on the final day. The 2023/24 season was, in his words, "embarrassing"—a 445,000 finish that resulted from aggressive gambling on his part. He explains that after sitting around 200,000-250,000, he faced a choice: accept a mediocre finish or push hard for top 100,000. He chose the latter, ignoring Haaland at home against Wolves, captaining Cole Palmer instead of other options, and selecting Romero over Porro. None of these "tilt" decisions paid off. The 2024/25 season saw similar struggles, with Az ignoring Mohamed Salah far too long and reacting too slowly to top picks throughout the campaign.

## Diagnosis: Why the Performance Dropped

Az offers a thoughtful analysis of why his results have deteriorated over the past three seasons. He identifies several interconnected factors, beginning with his fundamental approach to decision-making.

**Too Slow to React:** Az recognizes that he has consistently been too slow to identify and act on premium picks. Rather than adapting to emerging trends and data-driven insights, he has clung to players and strategies based on past performance rather than current opportunity.

**Template Resistance:** A key admission emerges: Az has consciously pushed against template teams—the consensus picks that most players select—perhaps too aggressively. "I've tried to go against the template too much," he acknowledges, suggesting that his desire to differentiate has led him away from proven selections.

**Ignoring Analytical Tools:** Az confesses that he has "completely ignored things like the review"—FPL's statistical tools and data visualizations. His approach has prioritized intuition and personal research over the sophisticated models that have become increasingly central to elite FPL performance.

**The Knowledge Gap:** Az draws an important distinction between his early success and recent struggles. In previous seasons, fewer analytical tools existed, but he compensated by watching considerably more football and using that direct observation to inform decisions. He has attempted to maintain this approach, but acknowledges that "the models and things like they need to be incorporated a little bit because they are so good at synthesizing that data."

**The Blackbox Effect:** Az also notes that Mark has experienced similar struggles during this period. He and Mark have "pushed too much against that over the last few years," and the results speak for themselves. Both have struggled when relying too heavily on intuition over data. Interestingly, Az mentions that he narrowly beat Mark on the final day of the 2022/23 season, adding a competitive dimension to their shared approach.

**Personal Pride:** Perhaps most revealing is Az's admission that his wife's FPL performance has become a particular motivation. Yalena has beaten him three years in a row, which Az describes as "just getting a bit embarrassing." This personal rivalry adds stakes beyond abstract rankings.

## The Path Forward: Incorporating Lessons Learned

Rather than simply dwelling on past failures, Az signals his intention to integrate more data-driven thinking into his 2025/26 approach. The implicit message is clear: his resistance to analytical tools has cost him, and the path to returning to his previous performance levels requires embracing rather than fighting the statistical revolution that has transformed elite FPL strategy.

He doesn't explicitly detail his current draft in this portion of the video, but his self-critique suggests he will be more willing to follow template picks, react faster to emerging premium options, and give appropriate weight to the models and reviews he previously dismissed.

## Key Insights for FPL Managers

**Understanding Your Approach Matters:** Az's willingness to analyze his own decision-making process provides a valuable model. Elite FPL performance requires self-awareness about whether your approach leans too heavily toward intuition or analysis, and why.

**Template Resistance Has Costs:** While differentiation can provide advantages, Az's experience demonstrates that fighting the consensus too aggressively leads to underperformance. The key is finding balance—respecting template picks while seeking value elsewhere.

**Data and Intuition Should Complement Each Other:** Az's career arc illustrates the danger of relying exclusively on either traditional scouting or purely statistical approaches. The most successful managers synthesize multiple information sources rather than privileging one over the other.

**Long-Term Consistency Beats Short-Term Gambling:** His 445,000 finish in 2023/24 resulted from abandoning disciplined strategy in pursuit of dramatic improvement. Sometimes accepting a mediocre finish protects overall rank better than high-risk gambles.

**Enjoyment Sustains Long-Term Engagement:** Despite acknowledging poor results and social media criticism, Az emphasizes that he continues because he genuinely enjoys the game and the community. This perspective—prioritizing sustainable engagement over obsessive optimization—represents a healthier approach for most players.
