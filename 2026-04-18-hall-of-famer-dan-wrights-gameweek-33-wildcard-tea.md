# The Fitness Call That Changed Everything: Dan Wright's High-Stakes Wildcard Play

For Dan Wright, FPL decision-making often comes down to a single variable. This Gameweek 33, that variable was Nico O'Reilly.

Wright—who has five top-10k finishes and ranks 17th in Fantasy Football Scout's Career Hall of Fame—entered the week facing a fork in the road. If O'Reilly (£5.0m) was ruled out for a couple of games, Wright planned to deploy his Free Hit instead of the Wildcard. The idea: grab O'Reilly on a Gameweek 35 Wildcard, just ahead of Manchester City's likely double in Gameweek 36.

But O'Reilly is fit. So the Wildcard stays in the holster.

That single piece of information reshuffled the entire chip strategy.

"The Gameweek 35 Wildcard would have pushed my Bench Boost to Gameweek 36," Wright explained. "That looks weaker than a Wildcard in Gameweek 33 and a Bench Boost in 35, in my opinion."

The calculus isn't clean, though. Wright acknowledges both paths carry trade-offs. A GW33 Wildcard with Bench Boost in GW35 commits you to a specific squad structure earlier than some managers might want—before we know exactly how the season's penultimate rounds will shape up. The alternative preserves flexibility but potentially leaves you with a thinner bench for the run-in.

For managers operating without elite-level finish credentials, the lesson isn't which option Wright chose. It's the process: let fitness news drive the decision, not sentiment or impatience. Wright skipped his Wildcard entirely in Gameweek 32 because the timing wasn't right. Waiting for clarity—then acting decisively once it arrived—is the mark of someone playing the long game.

The actual squad Wright assembled behind the Wildcard requires a Fantasy Football Scout account to view. But the real team reveal here is the strategy itself: a Hall of Famer treating each chip as a limited resource, spending it only when the boardstate demands it.
