# Fantasy EFL Play-Offs 2025/26: Everything You Need to Know

## What Are the Fantasy EFL Play-Offs?

The Fantasy EFL Play-Offs provides a focused three-Gameweek competition that runs alongside the real-world English Football League play-offs. Twelve teams now battle it out across three Gameweeks, all chasing promotion and the chance to step out under the famous Wembley arch. It offers Fantasy managers a condensed, high-stakes format after the main season concludes.

## The Three-Gameweek Structure

The format covers three Gameweeks: semi-final first legs (Gameweek 1), semi-final second legs (Gameweek 2), and the finals (Gameweek 3). Each Gameweek presents its own selection deadline based on when matches kick off, keeping the action flowing across a concentrated period.

## Selection Rules for Gameweeks 1 and 2

In each Gameweek, you pick seven players and two clubs. These selections apply to the semi-final legs, where managers must balance their picks across teams still in contention for promotion. The familiar five-use club limit from the main season no longer applies either, giving greater freedom when building squads.

## Selection Rules for Gameweek 3 (The Finals)

The format shifts slightly for the finals, where you select three clubs – one from the Championship, one from League One, and one from League Two. Gameweek 3 also increases the player limit per club to four, giving you more flexibility when building around the finalists. This structural change reflects the reduced number of clubs remaining in competition.

## Lockout System

Fantasy EFL uses a rolling lockout system, not a fixed deadline. Each player locks at the moment their match kicks off. This means managers can still make adjustments right up until individual matches begin, which requires close attention to the matchday schedule.

## Scoring and Gameplay

Scoring stays exactly the same as in the regular Fantasy EFL season. All the familiar rules apply, including points for goals, assists, clean sheets, and other contributions. There are no surprises for those already accustomed to the scoring system.

## Chip Strategy

The Play-Offs game strips things back compared to the regular season – there are no chips in play. Any unused chips also don't carry over. Managers should not expect to deploy Bench Boost, Free Hit, or other chips during this phase.

## Gameweek 1 Prize

For Gameweek 1 (Semi-Final first legs), first place earns two tickets to a 2025/26 Play-Off Final of your choice, along with a Fantasy EFL merchandise pack. This provides an immediate tangible reward for strong early selections.
