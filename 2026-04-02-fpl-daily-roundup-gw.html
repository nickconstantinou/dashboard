Players to Avoid and the Wisdom of Caution

Luke from FPL Blackbox offered specific guidance about which players to approach with caution, drawing on his philosophy of prioritising stability over speculation. His core advice concerned newly signed players, noting that "if we get to the start of season and you've got players in your team that even if they've just been signed by the other team and you expect them to be like a starter for that team, it may not happen straight away."

This wisdom applies equally to the current season context. Managers should be wary of over-committing to players who have recently changed clubs or been promoted to starting roles, as integration into new systems requires time that FPL managers cannot always afford to wait for. The temptation to immediately acquire newly signed stars who arrive with considerable hype should be tempered by recognition that even expensive acquisitions frequently need several weeks to establish themselves.

Luke also expressed scepticism about over-investing in the attacking defender meta, preferring instead to target "defcons"—players whose value derives primarily from defensive contributions and clean sheets. "I just don't seem to have any luck with clean sheets," he admitted, noting that his philosophy had evolved to focus on players who could reliably contribute defensively rather than chasing volatile attacking upside. This represents a contrarian approach in an FPL community that often prioritises attacking returns, but one grounded in hard experience.

Chip Strategy: The Wildcard Timing Debate

The chip strategy discussion has crystallised around the wildcard timing question, with creators offering sharply different perspectives that managers must evaluate based on their individual circumstances.

FP Harry has been "pretty clear over the past couple of weeks" that he is "definitely not locking in a wildcard in gameweek 32." His reasoning centres on the information disadvantage of acting before FA Cup results are known. "The FA Cup weekend in two weeks' time is the biggest non-FPL weekend left this season that will decide the doubles and blanks later on in the season," he explained. For Harry, wildcarding before this information arrives means potentially building a team around players who will not have the anticipated double gameweek opportunities, while simultaneously weakening the squad for Gameweek 32 itself.

The bench boost chip represents Harry's most powerful remaining asset, and he cautioned that wildcarding to set up this bench boost is potentially counterproductive. "Wildcarding that weakens your team for gameweek 32 while potentially enabling a gameweek 33 bench boost requires careful cost-benefit analysis," he noted, suggesting that the optimal bench boost activation depends on having maximum doublers in the squad, with the composition of those doublers only becoming clear after the FA Cup results are known.

Tom and Sam from the Blank Gameweek discussion offered different perspectives, suggesting that managers holding the wildcard should not use it immediately when the game reopened on Saturday but should hold until Friday to maximise information about team news and European fixture outcomes.
