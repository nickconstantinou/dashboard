Best Crystal Palace Players for FPL Double Gameweek 36: Updated!

Crystal Palace Double Gameweek 36 Overview

Crystal Palace will have a Double Gameweek in Gameweek 36, facing Everton away and Manchester City away. This represents a significant opportunity for FPL managers seeking to maximize points through the double gameweek mechanism.

Prior to this double, Crystal Palace had not had any doubles to date. The fixtures also come after Crystal Palace blanked in Gameweek 31, making this DGW a timely chance to make up lost ground.

Everton Away: Defensive Vulnerabilities

When visiting Goodison Park in Gameweek 36, Palace will face an Everton side with notable mixed form on home soil.

Everton rank 12th out of 15 teams for xG/xGC on the road, suggesting their underlying numbers have been concerning when playing away from home. More tellingly, Everton are without a clean sheet in their last six away matches, indicating a persistent defensive issue when traveling.

Despite these struggles, Everton have not shipped more than two goals in any away fixture in 2025/26. This suggests they remain competitive in defence but have struggled to keep clean sheets on their travels. Additionally, the team has kept a fourth-best 11 clean sheets this season overall, highlighting that their defensive record at home has been respectable.

Manchester City Home: Attacking Challenge

The second fixture presents a sterner test. Manchester City rank 2nd/3rd for xG/xGC in home fixtures in 2025/26, demonstrating their dominance when playing at the Etihad Stadium. City have not lost a home league match since August when Tottenham Hotspur won, underlining their formidable home record.

However, City have conceded twice as many home goals to bottom-half teams (eight) as to top-half clubs (four). This statistical split suggests Crystal Palace, depending on their league position, could find more opportunities than a typical top-six side might.

FPL Implications

For managers considering Crystal Palace assets, the fixture combination offers both opportunity and risk. The Everton away match appears the more favorable attacking opportunity given Everton's away defensive vulnerabilities. The Manchester City fixture is tougher but not without potential, particularly if Palace can exploit City's tendency to concede more goals to teams in the lower reaches of the table.
