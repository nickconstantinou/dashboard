# Tom Hadley's Gameweek 32 Wildcard Strategy: A Former World Number One Shares His Chip Plan

## The Full Story

Tom Hadley, a former world number one in Fantasy Premier League, has revealed his chip strategy for the critical run-in of the 2025-26 season. The data-driven FPL analyst has settled on a three-gameweek chip sequence: deploying the Wildcard in Gameweek 32, followed by the Bench Boost in Gameweek 33, and concluding with the Free Hit in Gameweek 34.

Writing for Fantasy Football Scout, Hadley explains that he entered the period with four free transfers available and a team that "would have been perfectly fine" for Gameweek 32 without using his Wildcard. However, the double gameweek in Gameweek 33 presented too compelling an opportunity to target with the Bench Boost, particularly after Arsenal's result against Southampton meant the chip is "not as template as it would have been."

The article includes Hadley's actual Wildcard team for Gameweek 32, though full access to these specific player selections requires a Chief Scout membership. Hadley notes he has £2.0m in the bank and outlines his likely transfer direction, while emphasizing that this chip strategy isn't objectively superior to alternatives. "The best part about the cup results last weekend is that no path is an obvious one," he writes. "More than ever, the chip strategy decisions are totally team- and situation-dependent."

## Why This Matters

For the millions of FPL managers worldwide, the chip strategy during the final weeks of the season can be the difference between finishing in the top 1% or missing out on valuable positions. The 2025-26 season has reached its decisive phase, where every decision carries amplified weight given the limited number of gameweeks remaining. Hadley's strategy represents one approach among several viable options, and his reasoning—particularly around targeting the Gameweek 33 double—offers insight into how experienced managers approach these high-stakes decisions.

The significance extends beyond individual team decisions to broader FPL strategy discourse. When a former world number one shares their approach, it influences how the wider community thinks about optimal chip deployment during the run-in. This is particularly relevant given that many managers are now facing their own critical decisions about when to use their remaining chips.

The next few gameweeks will reveal whether Hadley's chip strategy proves optimal. Gameweek 33 features a double for several teams, making the Bench Boost particularly valuable if managers have players from those clubs.
