Planet FPL Weekender Ep. 27: Navigating the Manchester City-Crystal Palace Fixture Chaos and European Reset

Introduction

The latest episode of the Planet FPL Weekender arrives at a pivotal moment in the 2025/26 Fantasy Premier League season, where fixture uncertainty has created a headache for managers up and down the country. Host James is joined by regular co-host David Hunter for their Friday session, and the pair immediately dive into the most pressing issue facing FPL players: the potential rearrangement of the Manchester City versus Crystal Palace fixture. With both clubs having suffered disappointing results in European competition midweek, the possibility that this postponed game could now be scheduled into either Gameweek 31 or 32 has thrown strategic planning into disarray. The hosts explore this complex scenario in depth, offering guidance on how to position your team ahead of Thursday's deadline when the situation should become clearer.

Beyond the fixture chaos, the episode covers the European results affecting English clubs, particularly Liverpool's performance against Tottenham, and wraps up with captaincy advice for the upcoming gameweek. The discussion embodies exactly what makes the Planet FPL content valuable to serious managers: it's not just about reacting to results, but understanding the ripple effects across the season and positioning yourself to capitalise on opportunities while others panic.

The Manchester City versus Crystal Palace Fixture Crisis

The core of this episode centres on what James describes as a situation where "some heads have exploded" following Crystal Palace's failure to beat AEK Lanica in their European fixture. The fundamental problem is straightforward: both Manchester City and Crystal Palace are at risk of exiting European competition, and if both clubs bow out, their postponed fixture can be rearranged into either Gameweek 31 or Gameweek 32. This seemingly minor detail has massive implications for FPL strategy because the scheduled deadline for Gameweek 31 falls before the rearranged fixture's position becomes confirmed.

James explains that the key date to watch is Thursday night, when both clubs' European fates will be sealed. Manchester City still have a chance to turn around their tie against Real Madrid, while Crystal Palace's elimination appears more likely. The critical uncertainty is whether the fixture will be placed in Gameweek 31, which would occur before the Gameweek 32 deadline, or Gameweek 32, which would come after. As James notes, "the answer essentially is yes" when asked whether the fixture could theoretically go into Gameweek 31, though he personally thinks it unlikely.

The reasoning behind potentially placing the match in Gameweek 32 rather than 31 becomes clear when examining the fixture congestion for both clubs. Manchester City are scheduled to play Chelsea on the Sunday of Gameweek 32, and placing the Palace match earlier would mean Pep Guardiola's side playing three matches in rapid succession: Chelsea on Sunday, Palace midweek, and then Arsenal on the following Sunday. Conversely, Gameweek 32 would sit more comfortably for City while also working for Palace, whose Gameweek 33 fixture is already scheduled for a Monday. James emphasises that "31 is it's not impossible" and that "Honestly, it's not," meaning managers cannot rule this scenario out entirely.

Strategic Implications for FPL Managers

This fixture uncertainty creates a genuine strategic dilemma for anyone holding Manchester City or Crystal Palace assets. James articulates the core problem: "I think these moves I can totally understand people want to go Haaland to Saka Pedro at the moment or particularly Saka if you don't own, right? I don't know. I'd maybe hold fire." The reasoning is compelling—if you sell Haaland or Saka now and the fixture gets placed in Gameweek 31, you could find yourself without your star player for a double gameweek when you most need them.

David adds an important clarification: "even if it's 32, they're the ones that you'd want to bench to have ready for the double in 32." This means that whether the match lands in 31 or 32, Manchester City assets remain valuable because they provide flexibility to bench boost or otherwise manipulate your squad around the double fixtures. The hosts repeatedly emphasise that the situation requires patience and suggests rolling transfers rather than making hasty decisions.

James reveals his own position: "I have, for example, owned Haaland and Saka. Ain't doing anything about it at the moment." He explains that selling Haaland and then seeing the fixture land in 31 or 32 "would be sick, wouldn't you?" The rhetorical question captures the frustration managers would feel after unnecessarily moving a premium asset. David agrees, stating "No. No. Makes sense. Yeah. like you've just said, if you were to take him out and then that that was to materialise, you'd be you'd be sick."

The discussion then turns to Crystal Palace assets, which are considerably less attractive. James notes that Palace would gain "an additional fixture away to Manchester City," making their players marginally more appealing, but nowhere near as valuable as City assets themselves. This means selling Palace players is less damaging than selling City assets, though the principle of holding remains.

European Results and English Club Performance

Moving away from the fixture chaos, the hosts review the European performances from the midweek action. Liverpool's fixture against Tottenham receives specific attention, though the transcript is somewhat fragmented at this point. The hosts are building toward a full review of English sides and their European fortunes, which will inform strategies for the remainder of the season.

Transfer Strategy and Chip Planning

Throughout the episode, James and David weave in broader transfer strategy advice that applies to the current situation. The overarching theme is one of patience and flexibility. James articulates a philosophy that seems to guide his own decision-making: "there's just so many variables that can happen here and there's absolutely zero chance of accurately predicting it really. So, you might as well just while these 50/50 calls are going on and one of the options is to do nothing and build up these transfers."

This approach recognises that with so much uncertainty around fixture rearrangements, wildcard activations, and chip strategies, making firm plans more than a week ahead is essentially guesswork. Instead, accumulating transfers by rolling weekly provides options to react when the situation becomes clearer. James explicitly recommends that "for many people where you can roll if you've got a decent team this week, roll a transfer guys generally."

The discussion also touches on chip strategy, particularly the bench boost. If Manchester City and Crystal Palace both get their rearranged fixture into Gameweek 32, it creates a double gameweek that could be combined with the bench boost chip in Gameweek 33. However, James notes the complexity: "if City lose to Liverpool, their fixture with Burnley stays in 34." This means City might not have a double in 33 at all, and managers would need to wildcard out of City assets to target doublers in 33 instead.

David summarises the chaos: "It's chaos. I love it. Yeah. Yeah. This is what we want for engage managers." The hosts seem genuinely excited by the strategic complexity, even as they acknowledge it creates headaches.

Captaincy Advice: Hugo Eate Emerges as Top Option

The episode concludes with captaincy advice for the immediate gameweek. James asks directly: "Um, is Hugo Eate the best captain this week, David?" David responds simply: "Yeah, I think he is. Yeah." The hosts then engage in some gentle banter, with James pretending to be offended that David immediately agreed without more discussion, though he ultimately accepts the recommendation.

While the transcript doesn't provide detailed reasoning for why Hugo Eate is the preferred captain choice this week, the implication is that his fixture and form make him the standout option among available captains. This represents practical, actionable advice amid all the broader strategic discussion—a reminder that even with complex long-term planning, each gameweek requires immediate decisions.

Key Takeaways for FPL Managers

Hold your Manchester City transfers until Thursday. With the City versus Palace fixture potentially moving to either Gameweek 31 or 32, selling premium City assets now could leave you without them for a double gameweek. The uncertainty won't be resolved until late Thursday night, so patience is essential.

Rolling transfers is the safest approach when facing 50/50 decisions. As James explains, with so many variables in play and essentially zero chance of accurately predicting the outcome, doing nothing and banking transfers provides optionality for when situations clarify. This is particularly valuable when you have a decent team already.

Consider wildcard timing around potential City doubles. If City get a double in Gameweek 32, you may want to wildcard in Gameweek 32 itself to bench boost in 33. However, if City lose to Liverpool, their fixture might stay in 34, making a different strategy necessary. Wait for more information before committing to wildcard timing.

Crystal Palace assets are far less valuable than City assets in this scenario. While Palace might gain a fixture, they're not worth prioritising over holding City players. If you must move players, Palace assets are more expendable than Haaland, Saka, or other premium City options.

Hugo Eate is the recommended captain for this gameweek. The specific reasoning isn't fully detailed in the transcript, but the hosts agree he's the standout captaincy option, making him the safest choice for managers seeking points.
