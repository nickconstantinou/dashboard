Arsenal's Favorable Run Continues

Arsenal enter Gameweek 35 with fixtures that present significant clean sheet potential. The team won all four return fixtures back-to-back without conceding a goal between Gameweeks 7 and 10, demonstrating defensive consistency. Gabriel Magalhaes stands out as a defensive asset, fresh from picking up his 15th clean sheet in Gameweek 34 at a price of £7.2m.

However, managers should note that Arsenal have a Champions League semi-final with the second leg coming between the Fulham and West Ham United matches. This fixture congestion could impact rotation decisions.

Brighton have strung together six wins and a draw from their last eight matches to soar into sixth place, putting them into Champions League contention. This strong form makes Seagulls assets attractive for the run-in.

Two teams, Crystal Palace and Manchester City, have more remaining matches than the other 18 clubs. These two sides double in Gameweek 36, presenting a valuable opportunity for managers with fixtures to plan accordingly.
