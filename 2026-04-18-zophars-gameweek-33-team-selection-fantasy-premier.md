# Zophar's Gameweek 33 Team Selection: Navigating the FPL Wildcard vs. Free Hit Dilemma

## Introduction: The Calm Before the Deadline Storm

For Fantasy Premier League managers, few periods are as agonizing as the international breaks that punctuate the season—and the gameweeks that follow them. The buildup to Gameweek 33 finds the FPL community in familiar territory: waiting on crucial injury news, weighing immediate gains against long-term strategy, and desperately trying to find the edge that separates a respectable finish from a truly memorable one.

Zophar, a prominent FPL content creator known for his analytical approach to team management, recorded his Gameweek 33 team selection video on a Friday morning with one question dominating the minds of managers everywhere: the status of Erling Haaland. Specifically, the anticipated update from Pep Guardiola regarding whether City's prolific striker would be available for the fixture against Burnley. "I hope he's vague," Zophar admits with a grin, explaining his unusual desire for ambiguity. "I hope he gives Arteta no news whatsoever. He tells him he's like maybe back in training. Could be in with a chance or even goes out with the all out. He's on fire in training. I would love to see it. Just for the sake of how Arteta has been messing with us FPL managers all season."

Beyond the Haaland uncertainty, Zophar opens with a candid assessment of his previous gameweek performance. He chose not to deploy his wildcard in Gameweek 32, a decision that ultimately yielded 59 points—roughly in line with the 55-60 point average that wildcard teams typically achieved, but well short of the optimistic 10-15 point gain he had hoped for. "I was hoping to gain maybe around 10, maybe an optimistic 15 points upon the wild card users last week," he explains, "but I got only 59 with the average for the wild cards around 55 to 60 depending on who you went with."

His current rank sits at approximately 295,000, a position that falls short of his season ambitions. "Ultimately, I want to finish at least in the 100k to call it a decent season," Zophar states, before offering an observation that speaks to the compressed nature of competitive FPL at this stage: "I think really the difference between 10k and 100k now is a bit of variance cuz just because how clued everyone is. It's only a 50/50 decisions that make the difference." This acknowledgment frames the strategic discussion that follows—not about finding dramatic differentials, but about making the correct marginal decisions that separate close finishes.

## The Strategic Crossroads: Route A vs. Route B

The heart of Zophar's video centers on what he describes as "two strategies"—essentially the choice between using a Free Hit in Gameweek 33 versus deploying the wildcard, with each path leading to different team compositions in subsequent gameweeks.

**Route A: Free Hit 33, Wildcard 35, Bench Boost 36**

This approach treats Gameweek 33 as an isolated opportunity to maximize points from the best available players, then uses the wildcard to rebuild for the double gameweek in 35, followed by the bench boost in 36.

The proposed Free Hit 33 squad reflects a focus on attacking options with favorable fixtures. At the back, Zophar considers doubling up on Leeds' defense with two goalkeepers, specifically targeting their home fixture against Liverpool. "I could go for strike, but I'm hoping to get lucky with maybe Bol spiking a goal since Leeds is the template one for the guys on wildcard 32." The reasoning extends beyond simple clean sheet potential: "These guys are just absolute defcon gods. They could get a clean sheet against Leeds at home and I don't fancy it as much against Newcastle away, but the defcon potential is higher in that fixture."

In midfield, the double City approach centers on the O'Reilly situation—specifically, hoping for vague injury news that might see the player rested against Burnley, making City mids more attractive than they would be if the full squad were available. Eze represents "a difference" from the template Wildcard 32 teams, while Tavia (Amdouni) offers an "easy pick for the Bournemouth mid on the penalties set pieces." Notably, this would be the first time Zophar has owned Palmer all season—a significant admission from someone who has watched the Chelsea star rack up exceptional returns.

The forward line prioritizes the obvious captaincy option in Haaland, with Calvert-Lewin selected despite his conversion struggles. "Calbert Lewin missed 10 big chances of the last 11, but he's still getting them, so might as well pick him, right? Minutes are good, has penalties." Pedro (Neto) at Chelsea receives consideration despite concerning recent form: "Chelsea now have scored just three goals over the last three matches, so they really need to come good."

Zophar acknowledges this team is "again quite template," but defends the approach: "If I feel these are the best players on the free hit, I don't want to go against them just for the sake of it, right? Cherki is a difference. Eze is a difference. Otherwise, most of these guys who wildcarded last week are going to have them. So, I really don't want to be different just because I need to be different. These guys need to score the points."

**Route B: Wildcard 33, Bench Boost 35**

The alternative strategy involves playing the wildcard immediately, building a team optimized for the Gameweek 35 double gameweeks while accepting a potentially weaker Gameweek 33 side.

The proposed Wildcard 33 team reads almost identically to the Free Hit side: Dalow, Bol, Sinessi, Hill, Palmer, Tavia, Cherki, Summervo, Haaland, Dalot, and João Pedro. "It's quite identical in fact, but the bench would be Eze, Bruno, Gabriel and Ronaldo," Zophar explains, acknowledging that the differences are marginal.

The critical question becomes how this team would set up for the Bench Boost in Gameweek 35. Zophar outlines the ideal bench boost squad: double Arsenal defense (Raya and Gabriel at home to Fulham), with Palace defenders facing a team playing European football on either side of the gameweek. "Palace would be playing in Europe either side of that week," Zophar notes, suggesting fatigue could benefit opponents. The bench boost would feature Dalow, Calvert-Lewin, Eze, "triple Leeds and Ronaldo" to maximize the number of double gameweek players.

However, Zophar identifies significant risk in this approach: "Could Leeds do well against Burnley? Yes. But they could also just be a two-pointers across the board. All you need to do is concede one goal and Calvert-Lewin to do Calvert-Lewin things. It could be six points there. Eze also to concede an eight points. It could be a very flattish bench boost as well. Not spectacular."

## The GW34 Bus Problem: Why Free Hit Creates Complications

Perhaps the most underappreciated challenge of the Free Hit strategy emerges when Zophar examines his hypothetical "bus team" for Gameweek 34—the team that would remain after using the Free Hit to temporarily field a different squad.

"I don't have a keeper. It's a blank spot there with Dravka and Bugen," Zophar explains, revealing that he would need to make at least one transfer just to fill the goalkeeper position. The defensive options are stronger: "I have Virgil, Gabriel and Muki who solo rates as the top defenses for that week. That's Palace, Arsenal and Forest. So that's covered." Anderson at Palace offers "defcon potential" despite an expected goal conceded, while Rogers remains attractive for Villa because "they have a free midweek in the sense they have no fixtures leading up to that. So again, Rogers should play against Fulham."

The forward situation becomes more complex. Ihattaren's fixture away to Manchester United presents an interesting proposition despite United's generally poor defensive record. "They've just kept five clean sheets all season. Only Burnley and Wolves have fewer. Still a good fixture. Could he outscore somebody like a Salah or Saka that people pick on a free hit? Very possible."

However, Zophar admits "the forward situation is a bit tricky," and he's considering whether to use a transfer to move from Haaland to someone like Bowen, which "would enable me to get maybe an Eze instead of Saka." The key insight is strategic preservation: "I want to use a maximum of two of my three free transfers this week not all of them that way I could at least take a one-week punt on gameweek 35 maybe a Thiago with at least some confidence. I don't want to go to gameweek 35 on my wildcard with just no transfers in the bank. Not an ideal situation."

This reveals the hidden cost of the Free Hit strategy—the immediate flexibility comes at the price of future transfer flexibility heading into the crucial wildcard gameweek.

## The Guardiola Factor: Parsing Injury News for Strategic Advantage

Throughout the video, Zophar returns repeatedly to the O'Reilly/Haaland situation, treating it as a pivotal variable in his decision-making. His unusual hope for vague news reflects a broader FPL strategy: when a player is flagged as "doubtful," managers who own them benefit from uncertainty that keeps others away, while managers who don't own them lose the opportunity to captain a potential starter.

The Burnley fixture represents a significant attacking opportunity for City, and any positive news about Haaland's availability would dramatically shift the captaincy calculations for managers across the game. Zophar's strategy of hoping for ambiguity suggests he's already invested in City coverage and wants the ambiguity to discourage others from joining him—not an uncommon approach in competitive FPL circles.

## The Core Trade-offs: What Actually Separates the Strategies

Zophar summarizes the fundamental difference between his two routes with characteristic clarity: "primarily being able to go card blank in gameweek 34 by picking free hit on any players I want doesn't seem too inspiring."

The math is relatively straightforward. Wildcarding in Gameweek 33 delivers essentially the same team as a Free Hit would, with marginal differences in bench composition. The extra chip advantage of the wildcard over the Free Hit—allowing permanent changes rather than temporary ones—doesn't provide meaningful value when the ideal squads are nearly identical. Meanwhile, Free Hitting in Gameweek 33 creates downstream problems: a weaker team in Gameweek 34, reduced transfer flexibility heading into the wildcard gameweek, and the need to navigate multiple transfer decisions with incomplete information.

"I don't really need to free hit this week," Zophar concludes, suggesting that Route B—wildcarding immediately—emerges as his preferred approach, despite the initial temptation of the Free Hit's flexibility.
