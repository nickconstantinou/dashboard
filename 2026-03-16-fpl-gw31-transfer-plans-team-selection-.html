Match Reviews and Player Analysis

Burnley vs Bournemouth: The Attacking Vacuum

The 0-0 draw between Burnley and Bournemouth represented a frustrating result for FPL managers who had invested in Bournemouth defenders or attackers from either side. The creator notes that while the result was a bore, those with Cherki will have appreciated the clean sheet—though the underlying performance metrics tell a concerning story.

Taabdir, in particular, continues to confound managers with his inability to convert chances. He managed just 0.58 expected goal involvements in this match, following up a 1.47 xGI performance in the previous game with another blank. The player hit the post for the second consecutive match, meaning he has now failed to score from just over two expected goal involvements across two games—an extraordinary level of underperformance.

Bournemouth as a team accumulated 2.5 expected goals but failed to find the net, representing significant missed opportunities. The creator expresses concern about Kwpay, who started this match but has started only three of his last five games. With competition for places and rotation risks, he represents a genuine FPL headache—a player who might need to be fielded just to get eleven players out, but one who offers no guarantees of returns.

The Curious Case of Burnley Defender Hill

Among the more remarkable statistical stories this season is Burnley's Luke Hill, who at just 4.2 million has become one of the most popular budget options in FPL. The defender has blanked only once in his last eight games, accumulating 53 points over that period—second only to Josko Gvardiol in the league.

However, the creator advises against considering Hill for Gameweek 31 specifically, noting that there are better defensive options available for the blank. This is an important distinction for managers to understand: while Hill has been excellent over the recent run, his fixture in the blank gameweek doesn't warrant inclusion if better alternatives exist.

Everton's Youngest Scorer in History

The most historic moment from the weekend came at Goodison Park, where 16-year-old and 73-day-old Dalman became the youngest scorer in Premier League history. This remarkable achievement sees him surpass what was previously thought to be an unbreakable record.

The creator points out that Dalman is actually the second sixteen-year-old to score this season, with the other being Portsmouth's Hwang Ui-jo—not Hungamoa as might be expected. The last time two sixteen-year-olds scored in a single Premier League season was 2002-03, which was FPL's inaugural season. Those two players were Wayne Rooney and James Milner, making this a particularly nostalgic moment for long-time FPL managers.

Credit must go to Everton for the way they approached this match. Missing both Taroski and Bran, they were forced into a centre-back pairing of Keehan and O'Brien—a defensive combination they had never previously played. Despite fielding a back four they had never used before, they nearly secured a famous victory.

Arsenal vs Everton: Defensive Assets Deliver

The Arsenal defensive assets continue to deliver exceptional value, with Gabriel and Saliba both making appearances in the designated player category. The creator notes that Gabriel secured maximum bonus points for an 11-point haul, while Saliba joined him in the benchmark after scoring exactly 10 points.

The only negative from an Arsenal perspective was Timber being forced off with a possible groin injury in the first half. While the creator hopes he'll be available for the League Cup final on Sunday, the injury makes him an easy sell anyway given the blank gameweek.

Chelsea vs Newcastle: Gordon's Value

The evening match between Chelsea and Newcastle was won by a rather bizarre Gordon goal—one of the strangest referee-related moments the creator has ever seen, with Paul Tierney inside the Chelsea team huddle on the halfway line before the game. Rosinha was critical of the referee after the game for a missed penalty that wasn't given, though the unusual pre-game scene apparently made no difference to the outcome.

The creator strongly considers Gordon for Gameweek 31, highlighting several compelling factors. He takes penalties for Newcastle, who face Sunderland at home—a fixture that looks highly favorable. The recommendation becomes especially tempting if expected starters like Muckele and Ru are ruled out through injury or rotation, potentially leaving Gordon as the primary penalty taker in an attack that should dominate the match.

The creator also notes that Sunderland have historically been a team that Newcastle have gotten the better of, making this fixture even more appealing from an FPL perspective. For those looking at Newcastle defenders, the recommendation is to consider either Livramento or Hall, with the creator personally favouring Livramento due to his goal threat and better defensive clean sheet potential compared to Hall.

Gameweek 31 Fixture Analysis

Projected Goals for the Blank

The creator walks through detailed projected goal data from FPL.page, beginning with the single gameweek analysis that matters most for managers trying to maximize points in the blank.

Newcastle top the projected goals list for Gameweek 31 with 1.9 expected goals at home to Sunderland—a highly favorable fixture that supports the case for investing in their attackers beyond just Gordon. Fulham come second in the projections, making their forwards viable options for managers looking for one-week punts.

Aston Villa rank third in the projected goals for Gameweek 31, making both Rogers and Watkins viable options as one-week investments. The creator suggests these represent reasonable choices given the fixture, though notes there are better options further down the list for those seeking maximum value.

The remaining projected goal scorers for the blank include Bournemouth, Liverpool, Chelsea, and Manchester United, though the creator doesn't specifically recommend any attackers from these sides for the blank gameweek.

Projected Clean Sheets for Gameweek 31

For managers looking to invest in defensive options for the blank, the clean sheet projections provide valuable guidance.

Fulham top the clean sheet probability at 44% given their home fixture against Burnley, making their defensive assets—particularly Anderson, Tete, and goalkeeper Leno—solid picks. Newcastle follow second with a 41% chance of a clean sheet, supporting the case for their defenders discussed earlier.

West Ham and Chelsea represent the next options, though the creator personally holds Dravka in goal and doesn't plan to upgrade to Leno despite the fixture. This decision reflects a broader strategic consideration: with only one transfer available in most cases, that transfer might be better saved for later gameweeks when it could prove more valuable.

The creator explains this thinking in detail, noting that extra transfers could be crucial when wildcards are used to set up for double gameweek 33 or the expected double in gameweek 36. Additionally, when managers use wildcard or free hit chips, the transfer doesn't carry over to the following week—meaning there's no bonus transfer earned. Therefore, preserving transfers for the right moment can be more valuable than spending them immediately.

However, the creator acknowledges this strategy depends on already having eleven players available for the blank. Those who are keeperless or cannot field a complete team should absolutely spend transfers to get eleven players out, as fielding fewer players would represent a significant points loss.

The Big Matches: Manchester City vs West Ham and Liverpool vs Tottenham

Manchester City's Struggle

The creator expresses surprise at Manchester City's performance against West Ham, describing how the champions had 24 shots and 71% possession but still struggled to score. The decision not to start Cherki was described as strange, though the player made an impact after coming on as a substitute.

Erling Haaland's blank was particularly significant—the second time in eight meetings that he has failed to score against West Ham. The striker had just 0.3 expected goals from four shots, all of which were low-quality chances. There was one particular cutback from Doku that Haaland scuffed wide, and the creator suggests the striker might be a little low on confidence right now.

For managers looking to sell Haaland ahead of the blank, the alternatives mentioned include Ekiti, Pedro Neto, and Watkins. The creator also notes that Bowen's assist came from Mvaneros's goal, which was literally West Ham's only shot and goal of the entire match—a remarkable achievement in terms of efficiency.

Liverpool's Rotation Masterclass

Perhaps the most significant FPL-related story came from Liverpool versus Tottenham, where approximately thirteen players were missing for Spurs. Liverpool manager Arne Slot saw this as an opportunity to rest several key players, with Salah, Nunez, and Ekiti all benched.

The standout moment was Zaslav's fifth free kick goal of the season, putting Liverpool ahead in a match they ultimately controlled. The creator also addressed questions about Ekiti's fitness, quoting Slot's response that the player is "basically okay, but sometimes if you play so many games and the games follow up so fast, if you fail a little bit, if you miss out on one or two training sessions, you feel it."

This rotation approach from Liverpool is particularly relevant for FPL managers, as it demonstrates that even when Liverpool face difficult fixtures, key players may be rested rather than guaranteed starters. This has implications for those holding Liverpool assets, as rotation risk exists even in favorable matches.

Detailed Takeaways

First, the blank gameweek demands pragmatism over perfection. Managers should prioritize getting eleven players out above all else, as the penalty for fielding fewer players far exceeds any marginal gains from optimal player selection. The creator's own approach reflects this—holding Dravka rather than upgrading to Leno because saving the transfer might prove more valuable than the clean sheet points in gameweek 31 itself.

Second, Newcastle attackers and defenders both look excellent for gameweek 31. The projected 1.9 goals against Sunderland represents the best offensive fixture, while the 41% clean sheet probability makes Livramento and Hall attractive defensive options. Gordon specifically stands out as a penalty-taking forward in a home fixture against a team Newcastle historically dominate.

Third, double gameweek planning should already be influencing current decisions. With six teams likely to receive doubles in gameweek 33, managers should be thinking about which assets to target once the FA Cup quarterfinals conclude. Preserving transfers now could prove crucial for wildcard planning later.

Fourth, be cautious with Liverpool assets despite their strong upcoming fixtures. The Tottenham match demonstrated that Arne Slot will rotate key players even in winnable matches, meaning those holding Salah, Nunez, or Ekiti face rotation risk regardless of fixture difficulty.

Fifth, the data confirms Bournemouth attacking assets remain severely underperforming. Taabdir's failure to score from over two expected goal involvements across two matches represents extraordinary bad luck, but managers need to weigh whether to persist with players who aren't converting chances regardless of underlying metrics.
