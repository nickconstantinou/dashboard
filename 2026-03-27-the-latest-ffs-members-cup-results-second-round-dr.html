FFS Members Cup: First Round Results Crown 128 Survivors as Tournament Enters Critical Phase

The Fantasy Football Scout (FFS) Members Cup has reached its first significant milestone, with the first round of competition now complete and the 128 remaining managers preparing for round two in Gameweek 32. The tournament, which offers £300 in total prizes funded by FFS, has whittled down an initial field through Gameweek 31's scores, with notable upsets and narrow victories defining the elimination round.

Of the six former champions who entered the competition, only two managed to advance past the opening stage. Mayanyi and Drizzle secured wins against their respective opponents, while Mohd Rodzi, Rossaldinho, rrcmc, and Chabs were all eliminated. The victorious former winners now face Mixology and RadGrlDad in the second round, setting up potential rematches against other decorated competitors.

Scoring in Gameweek 31 proved highly variable, with some managers posting dominant totals while others scraped through on modest point tallies. Perhaps most remarkably, pk all stars and 100K Short survived and advanced despite scoring just 50 points, highlighting how the randomness of fixture outcomes and player selections can determine cup fates. Meanwhile, Flying Nugget and norab were eliminated despite posting 69 points, demonstrating the unforgiving nature of head-to-head cup competition where your opponent's score matters as much as your own.

The tournament also produced its share of heartbreak, with Tieron Nobody losing 71-73 to bluemurder in one of the closest matchups of the round. Five managers—ruddjack, FPL Virgin, Milvus, Ckeulen, and Johnny 8—secured advancement with 52 points apiece.

Rank and pedigree proved inconsistent predictors of cup success. Matthias99th, ranked 1,482nd in the overall FPL standings, successfully progressed and now holds what appears to be the most favorable second-round matchup against oscardude1 (ranked 16,140th), with the combined ranks of both managers suggesting a lower-stakes clash than many other pairings. However, four other relatively high-ranked managers all fell despite strong overall positions: Pulisic 4 President (1,492nd), YNWA91 (1,511th), FPL Niall (3,251st), and Toalie (3,477th) were all eliminated. Conversely, ProfessorM, languishing at 4.39 million overall, advanced and will face The Great statuto, who sits at 1.99 million.

The FFS Members Cup operates under rules nearly identical to the official FPL cup competition. Managers are randomly paired each round, and the winner is simply whoever scores the highest points total minus any hits taken for transfers made during that gameweek. Ties are broken by the manager's position in the overall FPL rankings at that time. This format rewards not just point accumulation but also efficient squad management without unnecessary transfers.

The tournament will continue through the remainder of the Premier League season according to a fixed schedule: Round 3 in Gameweek 33, Round 4 in Gameweek 34, Round 5 in Gameweek 35, Quarter-Finals in Gameweek 36, Semi-Finals in Gameweek 37, and the Final plus third-place playoff in Gameweek 38. The fixture list for each upcoming round will be released before the respective gameweek deadline where possible, allowing managers to plan their final transfers.

For the broader FPL community, these cup competitions represent the purest test of weekly decision-making. Unlike the overall rankings, which reward consistent excellence across 38 gameweeks, cup competitions distill the game to its most volatile element: can you outscore your specific opponent this week? The results from round one—with high-ranked managers eliminated and low-ranked managers advancing—underscore that variance plays a significant role, and that the best FPL managers aren't necessarily the best cup players in any given gameweek.

The second-round draw has been completed, with all 128 surviving managers now knowing their path to the £150 first-place prize. As the Premier League season enters its critical final stretch with European competitions, domestic cup commitments, and fixture congestion affecting team selections, cup managers face increasingly complex decisions. The tournament will provide ongoing drama as each gameweek could end the dreams of dozens more competitors, with the final prize of a £150 Amazon voucher awaiting whoever survives all seven rounds to claim the title.
