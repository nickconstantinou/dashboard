FFS Members Cup Round One Concludes with Dramatic Upsets as 128 Managers Advance to Gameweek 32

The Fantasy Football Scout Members Cup has delivered its first major shake-up, with only two of six former champions surviving the first round and several high-ranked managers suffering unexpected eliminations. Round one, played during Gameweek 31, saw 128 FPL managers advance to round two, which will take place in the upcoming Gameweek 32. Among the notable casualties were former winners Mohd Rodzi, Rossaldinho, rrcmc, and Chabs, who fell to BilboBaggins, RamaJama, Joletouriste, and IvanGeorgiev respectively. The two survivors from the former champions group—Mayanyi and Drizzle—will now face Mixology and RadGrlDad in the second round.

The scores that defined round one ranged from the spectacular to the fortunate. FplSos and Ryan with Podnah led the pack with 76 points, while Wyrd bið ful aræd, Surgical Attack, and DrMammon each secured progression with 75 points. However, luck played an undeniable role in other matchups. Tieron Nobody was eliminated despite scoring 71 points, falling to bluemurder's 73. Flying Nugget and norab were knocked out on 69 points, while remarkably, pk all stars and 100K Short advanced with just 50 points—a testament to the unpredictable nature of single-gameweek matchups. Five managers—ruddjack, FPL Virgin, Milvus, Ckeulen, and Johnny 8—squeezed through on 52 points.

The competition between high-ranked and low-ranked teams produced mixed results. Matthias99th, sitting at 1,482nd in the overall rankings, advanced successfully, but the next four best-ranked teams all fell. This included Pulisic 4 President (1,492nd), YNWA91 (1,511th), FPL Niall (3,251st), and Toalie (3,477th), who were all eliminated. Meanwhile, ProfessorM, ranked down at 4.39 million, progressed to face The Great statuto (1.99m). The most anticipated second-round matchup appears to be Matthias99th against oscardude1 (16,140th), which represents the lowest combined ranks in the draw.

The FFS Members Cup operates on principles familiar to standard FPL play: the manager with the highest score minus transfer costs wins each tie. In the event of a tie, the manager with the better position in the overall FPL rankings advances. The competition follows a structured schedule from Gameweek 30 through the final in Gameweek 38, with prizes sponsored by Fantasy Football Scout. The winner receives a £150 Amazon voucher, the runner-up gets £100, and third place earns £50.

For the FPL community, this competition represents more than just bragging rights—it's a proving ground where differential picks, bold transfers, and strategic patience can overcome season-long standings. The fact that managers ranked in the millions can advance past those in the top few thousand underscores how single-gameweek formats reward different skills than cumulative ranking games. As the field narrows from 128 to 64 in Gameweek 32, managers who navigated the tricky post-international-break fixtures successfully will look to maintain momentum through the crucial spring run-in, when FPL championships are often decided.
