FFS Members Cup: Former Champions Fall Early as 128 Managers Advance to Second Round

The Fantasy Football Scout Members Cup has entered its second round with 128 Fantasy Premier League managers still in contention for the £300 prize pool. Following Gameweek 31's first-round action, the competition has already claimed several high-profile scalps, including four of the six former winners who entered the tournament.

Of the six previous champions in the field, only Mayanyi and Drizzle survived their opening matches. The eliminated former winners include Mohd Rodzi, Rossaldinho, rrcmc, and Chabs, who fell to BilboBaggins, RamaJama, Joletouriste, and IvanGeorgiev respectively. Mayanyi will now face Mixology in Round 2, while Drizzle draws RadGrlDad.

The Gameweek 31 results showcased the unpredictable nature of FPL cup competitions. High-scoring participants included FplSos and Ryan, both notching impressive 76-point tallies, while Podnah and Wyrd bið ful aræd progressed on 75 points, and Surgical Attack and DrMammon advanced with 74. However, fortune smiled unevenly: Tieron Nobody suffered a heartbreaking 71-73 defeat to bluemurder, and Flying Nugget and norab were eliminated despite scoring 69 points—a tally that would prove sufficient for many other matchups. Meanwhile, pk all stars and 100K Short advanced with just 50 points, and five managers made it through on 52 points: ruddjack, FPL Virgin, Milvus, Ckeulen, and Johnny 8.

The gap between overall rankings and cup success remained stark. Matthias99th, ranked 1,482nd overall, emerged victorious, but four other relatively high-ranked managers fell short: Pulisic 4 President (1,492nd), YNWA91 (1,511th), FPL Niall (3,251st), and Toalie (3,477th) were all eliminated. Notably, ProfessorM, languishing at 4.39 million in the overall rankings, advanced to face The Great statuto (1.99 million). The most lopsided second-round clash by combined rank features Matthias99th against oscardude1 (16,140th), both enjoying standout seasons.

Why This Matters

The FFS Members Cup represents one of the most prestigious community competitions in the Fantasy Premier League ecosystem, attracting thousands of participants who compete not just for prizes but for bragging rights within one of FPL's most engaged communities. For many managers, cup runs offer the season's most thrilling moments—a single bad week can end months of careful planning, while underdogs can suddenly find themselves in the spotlight against household names.

The competition's structure, mirroring the official FPL cup's rules, provides a level playing field where transfer costs affect outcomes and ranking determines tiebreakers. This creates strategic depth beyond simply maximizing points: managers must weigh whether aggressive transfers for a big gameweek are worth the potential cup penalty. The fact that ProfessorM at 4.39 million overall rank has survived to Round 2 demonstrates that the cup format rewards different skills than season-long optimization, offering hope to casual managers that a single week of inspiration could carry them deep into the competition.

The £300 prize pool, while modest, carries significant symbolic value, and the Amazon voucher structure (first place: £150, second: £100, third: £50) ensures meaningful rewards for the top performers.

    • Competition Format

    The FFS Members Cup operates identically to the official FPL cup: entrants are randomly paired, and the manager with the higher score after subtracting transfer costs wins the matchup. In the event of a tie, whichever manager sits higher in the overall FPL standings advances. Fixtures are released before each Gameweek deadline whenever possible.
