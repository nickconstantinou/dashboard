Fantasy Football Scout Members Cup: Round 1 Wraps Up With 128 Managers Advancing to High-Stakes Gameweek 32 Showdown

The Fantasy Football Scout Members Cup has reached an exciting juncture, as Round 1 concluded in Gameweek 31 with 128 managers still in contention for the £300 prize pool. The competition, which mirrors the structure of Fantasy Premier League's own cup format, has already delivered dramatic moments and unexpected results that have captured the attention of the FPL community.

Among the most notable outcomes, only two of the six former champions who entered the competition managed to advance. Mayanyi and Drizzle secured victories in their first-round matches, while former winners Mohd Rodzi, Rossaldinho, rrcmc, and Chabs were eliminated. Mayanyi will now face Mixology and RadGrlDad in Round 2, while Drizzle's next opponents will be determined by the draw.

Competition Format and Prizes

The FFS Members Cup operates identically to FPL's official cup, where the winner of each tie is determined by the manager with the highest score from that Gameweek, minus points spent on transfers. In the event of a tie, the manager ranked higher in the overall FPL standings after the Gameweek advances. The tournament follows a structured schedule running from Gameweek 31 through to Gameweek 38, when the final and third-place play-off will be held.

The prize structure offers £300 in Amazon vouchers distributed as follows: £150 for first place, £100 for second place, and £50 for third place. Fantasy Football Scout releases fixtures for each round before the Gameweek deadline wherever possible, allowing managers to strategize accordingly.

Looking Ahead to Gameweek 32

Round 2 is scheduled to take place in Gameweek 32, where the remaining 128 managers will face off in 64 matches. The draw has been published in full on the Fantasy Football Scout website, setting the stage for what promises to be another unpredictable round. Following Round 2, the competition will continue through Round 3 (Gameweek 33), Round 4 (Gameweek 34), Round 5 (Gameweek 35), Quarter-Finals (Gameweek 36), Semi-Finals (Gameweek 37), and finally the Final (Gameweek 38).

As the season enters its decisive phase, the Members Cup adds an extra layer of excitement for FPL managers who qualified through Gameweek 30's overall standings. Whether you're rooting for a former champion, an underdog climbing from the depths of the rankings, or simply enjoying the drama of knockout FPL, the competition offers something for every fantasy football enthusiast.
