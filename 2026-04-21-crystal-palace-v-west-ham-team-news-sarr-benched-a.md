# Sarr Benched Again as Glasner Rotates Around Conference League

Ismaila Sarr finds himself on the substitutes' bench for the second consecutive Premier League fixture, with Oliver Glasner continuing to shuffle his Crystal Palace side around UEFA Conference League commitments.

The Senegalese winger was named among the replacements for Monday night's clash with West Ham United at Selhurst Park, having also started Gameweek 33 from the bench against Fiorentina on Thursday. For Sarr's fantasy owners, this represents a frustrating pattern—regular starters in the Eagles' attack suddenly becoming unreliable assets in the league.

Glasner made four changes to the side that began against Fiorentina, with the same group of players cycling in and out of the XI. Jean-Philippe Mateta, Palace's leading league scorer, also had his legs spared in the Premier League fixture, as Brennan Johnson and Jørgen Strand Larsen led the line instead. The midfield pairing shifted once more, with Jefferson Lerma and Will Hughes entering the engine room—Adam Wharton missing out entirely due to an adductor injury, while Daichi Kamada dropped to the bench.

One player who did escape the tinkering was Maxence Lacroix. The French defender passed a fitness test to start despite Glasner hinting at possible ligament damage following Thursday's match. Whether Lacroix's availability holds beyond the 90 minutes remains uncertain, but for now, he's holding the backline together.

Across the capital, Nuno Espírito Santo showed no such inclination to rotate. West Ham named an unchanged XI from the side that demolished Wolverhampton Wanderers 4-0, maintaining the team that delivered that statement result.

The timing matters. Gameweek 33 represents the final regulation fixture before the much-anticipated double gameweeks arrive. For fantasy managers weighing whether to hold or sell Sarr, the bigger question is Glasner's long-term approach—not just this fixture, but every league match sandwiched between European ties.
