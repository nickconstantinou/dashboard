The FPL Daily Roundup: Gameweek 33 — The Biggest Double of the Season Arrives

The Fantasy Premier League landscape has reached its most consequential juncture of the 2025/26 campaign. With Double Gameweek 33 confirmed across six teams and the blank gameweek situation creating fixture chaos for those without the right coverage, managers face their most complex strategic decisions of the season. This comprehensive roundup synthesizes the insights from the FPL community's leading voices to deliver actionable guidance for managers navigating the final stretch.

Today's Key FPL News and Injury Updates

The FPL world received welcome news from Manchester City's Friday press conference ahead of what promises to be a title-deciding fixture against Arsenal. Manager Pep Guardiola confirmed that Nico O'Reilly, who had appeared to suffer a concerning injury against Chelsea in the previous gameweek, is "fine" and available for selection. This announcement represents a significant relief for the substantial portion of the FPL community who have invested in the young defender, whose price point of £5.1m combined with his consistent returns have made him a template pick throughout the season.

However, Guardiola's comments revealed the title race's precarious mathematics. "If we lose this game, it will basically be the title race is over for them," the City manager assessed of Arsenal's position. This framing carries substantial FPL implications: if City win or draw against Arsenal, rotation against Burnley in the subsequent fixture becomes unlikely. However, should City suffer defeat, Guardiola might prioritize the FA Cup fixture against Southampton, potentially resting key players and creating unexpected variance for managers who have loaded up on City assets for this double.

Elsewhere in Manchester, the news from Old Trafford proves far more concerning. Manchester United's defensive crisis has deepened considerably following their Monday night encounter with Leeds. Harry Maguire's additional one-match ban following red card and post-match comments means the centre-back will miss the Chelsea fixture entirely, while Martinez faces further assessment after a controversial second-half incident that saw him sent off for a hair pull on Calvert-Lewin. Kobbie Mainoo is likely unavailable, compounding the problems for Michael Carrick's side, who have kept just five clean sheets all season—fewer than only Burnley and Wolves.

For Crystal Palace managers, the situation demands careful monitoring. Manager Oliver Glasner confirmed that Ismaila Sarr has been benched in consecutive Europa Conference League fixtures, raising questions about his involvement in Premier League action. The pattern suggests Palace's minds are firmly on their European final, which comes after Gameweek 38, potentially creating rotation risk for key attacking assets in the closing weeks.

Chelsea's squad news emerged from Monday's training session, with manager Liam Rosenior providing updates on three Brazilian forwards. Enzo Fernandez escaped serious injury and is confirmed available, while Joao Pedro returned to the training pitch after missing Saturday's defeat to Manchester United with a minor thigh issue and will be assessed ahead of the Brighton trip. The more concerning development involves Estevao, the young signing who remains sidelined with a hamstring injury sustained recently. "With Este, he's definitely out for tomorrow, which is a real shame," Rosenior confirmed, adding that further tests and investigation are ongoing—a concerning phrase given the variable nature of hamstring recoveries in young players. Levi Colwill continues his recovery, playing 60 minutes for the under-21 side on Friday, with Rosenior expressing hope the defender could return before season's end.

The Gameweek 33 Landscape: Fixture Difficulty and Double Gameweek Dynamics

Double Gameweek 33 stands confirmed as the most substantial of the season, with six teams receiving doubled fixtures: Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds, and Manchester City. The fixture distribution creates clear hierarchies of attacking and defensive potential that the FPL community's analytical tools have been processing throughout the week.

Manchester City lead the projected goals table with 4.4 expected goals across their fixtures against Arsenal and Burnley. This figure has increased slightly as the week progressed, driven by improved expectations for their match against Arsenal—a remarkable prediction given Arsenal's reputation as one of the league's meanest defensive units. The Burnley fixture at Turf Moor represents the more straightforward opportunity, though the implications of the Arsenal result could affect squad rotation. Guardiola's side claim the highest clean sheet probability across the double at 62%, further reinforcing their status as the week's standout team for investment.

Chelsea sit second with 3.2 projected goals, holding steady from earlier estimates. The fixtures present an interesting split: home to Manchester United followed by a trip to Brighton. However, growing concern exists around Chelsea's away fixture, where the odds have "started to swing in favour of Brighton scoring more goals," potentially reducing Chelsea's clean sheet prospects significantly in that second match.

Bournemouth remain third with 3.1 projected goals, distributed unevenly across their fixtures. Their home game against Leeds represents the most favorable attacking opportunity, while their away trip to Newcastle presents considerably stiffer opposition. The key consideration for Bournemouth assets revolves around individual player potential rather than clean sheet floors.

Brighton have emerged as a fascinating option, having overtaken Leeds in projected terms as the week progressed. Their total of just over three goals across fixtures against Tottenham and Chelsea represents a notable increase from earlier predictions, with the model reflecting Brighton's recent form and their opponents' defensive vulnerabilities. "The chances are becoming more favourites with the way that their playing and their two opponents are playing," observed FPL Harry, suggesting Brighton's assets may be undervalued in many draft teams.

Leeds United complete the upper tier with projections heavily weighted toward their home fixture against Wolves. The 40% home clean sheet probability against Wolves contrasts sharply with just 22% away at Bournemouth, creating a scenario where concentration toward that first fixture represents the optimal approach. Burnley sit in sixth position with their double against Manchester City and Leeds representing the week's most challenging fixture set.

For the blank gameweek situation, confirmed data shows four teams blanking in GW31 (Arsenal, Crystal Palace, Manchester City, Wolves) and six teams blanking in GW34 (Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds, Manchester City). A speculative note from FFS articles suggests GW34's blank relates to FA Cup semi-final implications, though this remains unconfirmed through official sources. The fixture density for GW34 shows only 14 confirmed fixtures compared to 20 for subsequent gameweeks, reinforcing that GW34 presents the season's clearest blank gameweek opportunity for Free Hit deployment.

Captain Recommendations: The Haaland Dilemma and Differential Alternatives

Few decisions in FPL generate more anxiety than captain selection in pivotal double gameweeks, and Gameweek 33 presents an especially complex scenario. At the center of the debate stands Erling Haaland, Manchester City's prolific Norwegian striker who remains the default choice for the vast majority of managers.

The case for Haaland rests on multiple pillars. He leads all players in probability of a goal or assist and is predicted to be the highest scoring player across the double. Despite concerns about recent open-play form—managing just one open play goal in the Premier League over the last ten matches—Haaland recently scored a hat-trick in the FA Cup against Liverpool, demonstrating his finishing ability remains undiminished. The model projects Manchester City to score 4.4 goals across the double, and Haaland's expected share of those returns makes him the statistical captaincy leader.

Cole Palmer emerges as the primary alternative for managers seeking to differentiate. Chelsea face Manchester United in Gameweek 33, with United facing a defensive crisis that has been building since their encounter with Leeds. Martinez and Maguire are suspended, Mainoo is likely unavailable, and this depleted backline significantly improves Chelsea's attacking prospects. The 2-1 victory over Liverpool and other recent results suggest Palace can compete with anyone in their current state, positioning Palace as genuine contenders rather than underdogs.

For managers seeking to maximize differential potential, Brighton's assets present compelling options. Danny Welbeck offers several advantages: his form is strong, his minutes are secured, and the underlying data supports continued returns. With Brighton climbing the projected goals table and facing potentially vulnerable opponents in Tottenham and Chelsea, Welbeck could outperform his ownership percentage substantially if he starts both games. João Pedro represents another pathway, particularly given that his actual goal-scoring form recently is probably better than Palmer once penalties are excluded.

The critical consideration, as FPL Harry emphasized, is that "if you go against him [Haaland], you're effectively needing him to blank or your Gameweek could be almost over." In FPL Harry's YouTube poll, Haaland received 80% of the captaincy vote, with significant portions of managers planning to triple-captain him. This concentration creates a scenario where differential captaincy becomes mathematically attractive—if everyone captains Haaland and he delivers, everyone gains equally. Going against the consensus with a successful differential captain could provide the point differential needed to climb ranks meaningfully.

Transfer Strategy: Who's Recommended and Why

The analytical consensus points clearly toward prioritizing the confirmed double gameweek teams, with Manchester City and Chelsea presenting the strongest cases for investment.

For Manchester City coverage, the defensive options present the most compelling value. Marc Guéhi at £5.1m leads all defenders in season points at 151 and combines clean sheet potential with consistent defensive contributions. His 5.3 form represents solid recent returns, and his ownership at 33.9% suggests he's held by roughly one in three managers—a significant but not overwhelming presence. For managers seeking City coverage at different price points, the options become more speculative given the uncertainty around rotation following the Arsenal fixture.

Chelsea's Cole Palmer at £6.4m represents perhaps the season's most reliable premium midfield option. His 6.7 form and 120 season points demonstrate sustained excellence, while his 9.9% ownership provides meaningful differentiation compared to the heavier-owned City options. The fixture against Manchester United's depleted defense makes him a strong alternative captaincy option and a potential wildcard addition for managers without current exposure.

Brighton's Welbeck and João Pedro both merit attention for different reasons. Welbeck's form has attracted model attention throughout the week, while João Pedro offers the Brazilian flair that has delivered moments of genuine quality when selected. Both are available at mid-price points that allow premium investment elsewhere.

For Leeds exposure, James Justin at £3.9m represents outstanding budget value for those seeking defensive coverage. His 5.7 form and 78 season points don't jump off the page, but his nailedness and defensive contributions make him a reliable foundation option. Calvert-Lewin continues to attract attention despite limited returns, with his 16-pointer against Manchester United demonstrating the ceiling he carries when circumstances align.

Morgan Gibbs-White continues to justify his £7.5m price tag, with a perfect 10.0 form rating and 155 season points making him the standout differential among premium midfielders. His ownership at just 6.5% presents genuine differentiation opportunity for managers willing to take the risk on Forest's survival push translating to continued FPL returns.

The defensive priority for free hit strategies centres on Arsenal's assets, with Gabriel and William Saliba offering the most reliable clean sheet potential against Newcastle and Fulham. David Raya in goal represents the best value at his price point, while Declan Rice in midfield provides the set-piece involvement and goal-scoring threat that makes Arsenal's midfield so valuable.

Players to Avoid or Sell: Warnings From the Community

Several player situations have emerged where the analytical consensus advises caution or outright avoidance.

Saka has been confirmed out of Manchester City's game, and with Arsenal facing Atletico Madrid twice in the Champions League before their Fulham fixture, his availability for the Gameweek 35 wildcard window becomes highly uncertain. Managers planning around Saka for future chips should build contingency plans rather than assuming he'll feature when needed.

Newcastle's Anthony Gordon presents a complicated situation that demands careful monitoring. Eddie Howe has publicly stated that players must be "fully committed" to secure starting spots, and Gordon has been linked with Bayern Munich, creating legitimate questions about his focus. Gordon wasn't playing as the striker in recent Premier League games—that role went to Osula—and his hip injury at 75% availability adds further uncertainty. Managers should treat Newcastle assets with caution until the situation clarifies.

Crystal Palace assets require rotation-aware management. The pattern of Sarr being benched in European fixtures suggests Palace's priorities lie elsewhere, and the team faces significant European competition commitments during the week. While their projected clean sheets remain attractive, the uncertainty around key player involvement complicates investment decisions.

Brighton's Jaenbeul didn't make the recommended table despite potential, with the creator noting he was at fault for Brighton's late equalizer in a previous match. This reliability concern makes him one to avoid in favour of more secure alternatives.

Tottenham's Pedro Porro, despite delivering a 10-point haul in the previous match, represents a fixture-dependent punt rather than a reliable foundation pick. His corner-taking responsibility and late box runs create ceiling potential against Wolves, but the underlying form doesn't support long-term ownership. He makes perfect sense on a free hit that expires after this match, but not for wildcard builds targeting multiple gameweeks.

Chip Strategy: Wildcard Timing, Free Hit Advice, and Bench Boost Considerations

The chip strategy landscape has crystallized into two primary routes that the FPL community's analytical voices have been debating throughout the week.

Route A involves deploying the Free Hit in Gameweek 33, then Wildcarding in Gameweek 35, followed by a Bench Boost in Gameweek 36. This approach maximizes flexibility at each chip while accepting that managers will need to make transfers to navigate through blank gameweeks. The fundamental question this route poses is whether the ability to free hit in Gameweek 34—allowing managers to pick any players regardless of their current squad—justifies the strategic complexity.

Route B involves Wildcarding in Gameweek 33 itself, bypassing the Free Hit entirely. This strategy assumes managers can construct a team that will perform adequately through the upcoming fixtures without needing to reshuffle mid-gameweek. The fundamental trade-off involves immediate squad optimization against preserved chip flexibility for future gameweeks.

For those running the Bench Boost this gameweek, the results have been mixed. Among teams in the top 10K at the time of recording, those running bench boost were averaging approximately 69 points—a figure that validates the conventional wisdom about chip deployment. However, James from Planet FPL revealed he seriously considered abandoning his bench boost strategy on Saturday morning when examining his bench of expensive singles against tough fixtures. His internal calculation—that bench boost only works if the bench actually provides meaningful points—exemplifies the ruthless thinking required at elite levels.

The psychological dimension of chip deployment has emerged as a significant theme. Planet FPL's hosts observed that 2.7 million chips were played across the community this gameweek, creating a "massive experiment in competitive FPL strategy." With approximately 800,000 managers running bench boost and average top-10K scores reflecting the chip's impact, the gap between popular strategy and individual squad optimization has become increasingly important to understand.

For Gameweek 34, the confirmed blank gameweek affecting six teams (Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds, Manchester City) creates the optimal Free Hit opportunity of the season. The strategy involves completely reshaping your squad for this single gameweek, targeting players from the teams who are playing while accepting that your current investments in the blanking teams become irrelevant for one week.

The three-phase framework that has emerged from the FPL community involves treating GW33 as the strongest possible team with solid fixtures, GW34 as a potential free hit if needed (leveraging the fact that teams who blanked in 33 will double in 34), and GW35 as the bench boost opportunity, assuming fixtures align properly. This approach preserves chips for their highest-value applications rather than deploying them reactively.
