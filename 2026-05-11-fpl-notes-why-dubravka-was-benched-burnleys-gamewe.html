FPL notes: Why Dubravka was benched + Burnley’s Gameweek 38 appeal

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

Martin Dubravka's Unexpected Absence

Martin Dubravka was benched for the first time this season at Turf Moor on Sunday, a significant development for the 26.5% of managers who own him. Dubravka was replaced by Max Weiss, a Premier League debutant. Weiss was brought in at a price of £4.2m.

The decision appears to stem from Dubravka's contract situation. Dubravka is out of contract this summer and likely to depart Burnley. With this in mind, the benching seems aimed at preparing for life in the Championship, suggesting managers may want to consider alternative options for their FPL squads heading into next season.

Burnley's Gameweek 38 Appeal: Zian Flemming

Interim boss Mike Jackson made six alterations to Burnley's starting XI for the season finale. Among the changes, Zian Flemming (£5.3m) continued to demonstrate his relevance to the Clarets' attack.

Flemming had five shots on Sunday, the most from either side. He later made up for a couple of earlier missed opportunities to score, taking his tally to 10 goals for the season. For managers considering budget-friendly forward options, Flemming's volume of attempts and double-digit goal haul make him a player worth monitoring.

Aston Villa Assets: McGinn and Watkins

Aston Villa's final match of the season featured notable performances from two players who have been consistent FPL options this campaign.

John McGinn (£5.3m) provided the corner assist for Ross Barkley's headed equaliser. At Turf Moor, McGinn created four chances and attempted four shots, with his four created chances being a game-high total. In the last six matches, McGinn now ranks joint-third among all midfielders for chances created, with 17. Fresh from his European brace earlier in the week, McGinn demonstrated his ability to contribute across multiple categories.

Ollie Watkins (£8.7m) has maintained his consistency into the run-in. Watkins has now delivered five attacking returns in six league matches. His price point makes him a viable option for managers seeking attacking returns without the premium price tags of top-tier forwards.

Key Takeaways

For Gameweek 38 and beyond, Dubravka's benching raises questions about Burnley's direction heading into the Championship. Managers should weigh his contract expiry against potential replacement options. At Burnley, Flemming's 10-goal season and shot volume provide value at his price point. At Aston Villa, McGinn's chance creation record and Watkins's recent return frequency offer reliable options for managers seeking consistent performers.
