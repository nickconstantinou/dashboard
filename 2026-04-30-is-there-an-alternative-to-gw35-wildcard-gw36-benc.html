Is there an alternative to GW35 Wildcard + GW36 Bench Boost?

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

The Chip Landscape

Heading into Gameweek 35, over six million Fantasy Premier League managers still have a Wildcard chip in hand. This has led many to adopt a popular strategy: playing the Wildcard in GW35 to prepare for Double Gameweek 36, then activating the Bench Boost in the same gameweek to maximise points from 15 players.

Around 10 million managers have a Bench Boost in their back pocket, making it the most commonly held chip at this stage of the season.

The Double Gameweek 36 Situation

Double Gameweek 36 has officially been announced for Manchester City and Crystal Palace. This announcement is central to the Wildcard + Bench Boost strategy, as it offers a concentrated fixture opportunity for managers willing to commit both chips simultaneously.

However, the value of Crystal Palace assets requires careful evaluation before automatically assuming they are essential to this approach.

Crystal Palace Rotation Risk

Crystal Palace are the league's joint-third lowest goal scorers with 36 goals. More significantly, Palace are likely to rotate in Gameweek 35 as they prioritise winning the Conference League. Oliver Glasner has already signposted this in previous interviews, suggesting that key players may be rested or managed in the upcoming fixture.

This rotation risk complicates the appeal of Palace assets as Wildcard targets for GW36, as their double gameweek may not deliver the returns managers expect if the first match sees significant changes to the starting XI.

Alternative: Bournemouth's Defensive Strength

Bournemouth are unbeaten in 14 matches. This remarkable run has been built on defensive solidity, with the team meeting a defence that has conceded the fewest big chances at home (18) at their own ground.

A manager considering an alternative to the standard Wildcard strategy might target Bournemouth defenders heading into the final stages of the season, as their fixtures and defensive record offer a different route to points without relying on rotated Palace assets.

Alternative: Aston Villa's Favourable Run

Aston Villa are about to face Tottenham (home) and Burnley (away) – two sides in the relegation zone. This fixture run represents a favourable period for Villa assets, particularly those priced at key positions.

Ollie Watkins at £8.5m has four attacking returns from two home matches, demonstrating strong recent form in attacking areas. Morgan Rogers at £7.5m is on an upward curve. A 13-game run involved just one big chance from 37 shots, but he's had three of them in his latest four outings, suggesting he may be entering a more productive phase.

Evaluating the Alternative

Rather than committing to the GW35 Wildcard + GW36 Bench Boost combination, managers could consider alternative chip deployment:

  • Delaying the Wildcard to target Bournemouth assets and Aston Villa coverage
  • Holding the Bench Boost for a different Double Gameweek opportunity
  • Using the Wildcard in GW36 itself to react to team news and form

    • The Crystal Palace rotation risk – combined with their modest goal-scoring record – means their double gameweek may not justify the Wildcard investment if key players are rested in the first fixture.

    Managers with over six million Wildcards still available must weigh the guaranteed double fixtures against player availability uncertainties. An alternative approach focusing on form and fixture security may prove equally effective without requiring chip commitment.
