### Chip Strategy: The Three Viable Approaches

One of the most valuable portions of the video focuses on the three distinct chip strategies that Pras identifies as viable for the remainder of the season. He emphasizes that these strategies have been discussed extensively in the main FPL Wire podcasts, and he wants to reinforce the key points while adding some new context based on the latest fixture news.

**Strategy One: Wildcard in Gameweek 32, Bench Boost in 33, Free Hit in 34**

This remains the most popular approach among FPL managers. The logic is straightforward—use the wildcard in Gameweek 32 to load up on the best fixtures for that week, then activate the bench boost in Gameweek 33 to maximize points from the double gameweek, and finally use the free hit in Gameweek 34 to navigate the blank. Pras notes that this is a perfectly sound strategy, though he adds an important caveat: managers should not rush to press the wildcard button immediately in Gameweek 31. Instead, they should wait until after the FA Cup quarterfinals have been completed during the international break, as there's little to no price movement happening right now that would be missed by waiting a few more days.

**Strategy Two: Free Hit in 33, Bench Boost in 36, Wildcard in 35**

Pras reveals that this is the strategy he's personally leaning toward, assuming the fixtures eventually align with the projections. The appeal for him comes from his current team structure, which already looks favorable in Gameweeks 32 and 34. By free hitting in 33 rather than wildactoring, he preserves flexibility while still capitalizing on the double. This approach is less common than the first strategy but remains completely viable. The wildcard in 35 allows for adjustments based on what teams actually get blanks or doubles, while the bench boost in 36 targets what should be another double gameweek for certain teams.

**Strategy Three: Wildcard in 33, Free Hit in 34**

This is described as a more niche strategy favored by some managers, including the FPL Wire co-host LR. The idea here is to wildcard in Gameweek 33 itself if the fixtures look particularly favorable, then use the free hit in 34 to navigate the blank. Some managers employing this strategy might not even own a bench boost chip, having already used it earlier in the season. In that case, they would bench boost in either 35 or 36 instead.

Pras makes a compelling case for waiting to commit to any of these strategies. During the upcoming international break, managers will know with 100% certainty which fixtures are going into Gameweek 33 and which are staying in 34. Since only one of the three strategies involves wildandering in Gameweek 32, and that chip can be saved for later if needed, there's very little cost to waiting and gathering more information. This is classic overthinking in FPL, as Pras acknowledges, but it's also sound game theory when the stakes are this high.
