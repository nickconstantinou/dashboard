Zophar's Gameweek 33 Team Selection: Navigating the Free Hit vs. Wildcard Decision in Fantasy Premier League

Introduction

For Fantasy Premier League managers approaching the business end of the season, every transfer decision carries exponentially increased weight. Zophar, a popular FPL content creator known for his analytical approach to team selection, has released his Gameweek 33 team selection video at a critical juncture in the 2025/26 season. Recording this video on a Friday morning, Zophar finds himself at approximately 295,000 in the overall rankings—a position he acknowledges falls short of his season ambitions. His stated goal remains reaching the top 100,000, a target he believes is still achievable given that the difference between 10,000th and 100,000th place often comes down to minor variance rather than fundamental strategic missteps. "It's only the 50/50 decisions that make the difference," he notes, acknowledging how the proliferation of FPL content has leveled the playing field, making premium decisions increasingly difficult to differentiate.

The central dilemma Zophar explores in this video is the choice between two competing strategies: deploying a Free Hit in Gameweek 33 versus Wildcarding in Gameweek 33 with an eye toward a Bench Boost in Gameweek 35. This decision carries significant implications for how managers can maximize their points across what promises to be a crucial stretch of the season. Adding complexity to the equation is the lingering uncertainty surrounding Rodrigo (Rodri) of Manchester City—the player known colloquially in FPL circles as "O'Reilly"—whose availability status from manager Pep Guardiola remains a mystery at the time of recording. Zophar expresses a certain amount of Schadenfreude regarding this uncertainty, admitting he hopes Guardiola remains deliberately vague about Rodri's fitness, if only to reciprocate the frustration Arsenal manager Mikel Arteta has caused FPL managers throughout the season with his cryptic team selection announcements.

Looking Back: Gameweek 32 Performance

Before diving into the strategic options for Gameweek 33, Zophar provides a candid assessment of his Gameweek 32 results. He elected not to use his wildcard despite entering the gameweek with high hopes of gaining perhaps 10 to 15 points upon deploying the chip—optimistic targets by any measure. Instead, he managed to secure only 59 points, which fell within the average range for wildcard deployments (typically between 55 and 60 points depending on player selections). While this represents a respectable score, it fell well short of the windfall Zophar was anticipating.

On the positive side, his goalkeeper combination of Verugan (nickname for Raya, the Arsenal goalkeeper) and Dravka has proven exceptional since he brought Verugan into his team around Gameweeks 16-17. Verugan has been the top-scoring keeper in his portfolio, consistently delivering defensive returns. Virgil (van Dijk) contributed a clean sheet that Zophar admits was "a bit lucky," adding a valuable nine-pointer. Gabriel Munoz chipped in with an assist while also demonstrating his defensive contribution, though Zophar notes the Brazilian center-back was unlucky not to register more attacking returns, having several good attempts on goal.

The standout performer was Thiago (likely referring to Isak or another Newcastle forward), who Zophar kept on his bench and who delivered a 13-point haul—"the hero" as Zophar describes him. This performance becomes particularly relevant because Thiago now sits just one goal behind Harland in the Golden Boot race, making him an increasingly important asset for managers with ambitions of finishing strongly. Zophar confirms his intention to hold onto Thiago for the planned Gameweek 35 wildcard, recognizing his premium value in the season's closing stages.

Not all decisions paid off, however. Bruno (Fernandes) delivered an assist but ultimately proved "very, very disappointing" according to Zophar, who needed the Manchester United midfielder to come through in a crucial fixture. The situation was compounded by Bumo (Amad Diallo), another United player, who Zophar held specifically to capitalize on Leeds' defensive injuries in what he anticipated would be a favorable fixture. Instead, Bumo came off the bench rather than starting, leaving Zophar's transfer strategy unrewarded. Despite this setback, Zophar believes Bumo's spot will be secured for the next few games, potentially making him viable again down the stretch.

Rogers (from Aston Villa) contributed an assist but should "really have had a goal," according to Zophar—though interestingly, Rogers later took a penalty in the Europa League that same week and missed it. This raises questions about whether Rogers will retain penalty duties, though his goal involvement plus the missed penalty suggests he remains a player worth monitoring. Dango (Dango from Bournemouth, likely Antoine Dango or similar) showed decent underlying metrics with one big chance created and one big chance himself but couldn't convert, another reason Zophar's decision to hold his wildcard didn't fully work out.

The most painful moment came with Muki (Mukiele or similar), whom Zophar bench started in a late decision against Tottenham. Zophar admits he had "forgotten how good Muki was since he'd been injured for so long" and expected Tottenham to show some bounce under their new manager. The goal Muki scored was "a little bit jammy" and might even have been given as an own goal, but Zophar calculates Muki could have fetched 6 to 8 points on the day. His decision to bench Muki cost Zophar approximately 14 points on his bench—a painful miscalculation that significantly impacted his gameweek total.

The Core Strategic Dilemma: Route A vs. Route B

Zophar outlines two primary strategic paths available to managers, which he designates as "Route A" and presents as the second most popular strategy after wildcarding in Gameweek 32. Route A involves deploying the Free Hit in Gameweek 33, then Wildcarding in Gameweek 35, followed by a Bench Boost in Gameweek 36. This approach maximizes flexibility at each chip while accepting that managers will need to make transfers to navigate through blank gameweeks.

Route B (the alternative) involves Wildcarding in Gameweek 33 itself, bypassing the Free Hit entirely. This strategy assumes managers can construct a team that will perform adequately through the upcoming fixtures without needing to reshuffle mid-gameweek. Zophar notes that if he wildcards in Gameweek 33, his team would "be again very similar to the wild card 32 guys"—essentially the template team most managers using their wildcard last week constructed. He would potentially have the Cherokee spot (likely Chucky/Alonso or similar premium midfielder) and would carry Ryan but perhaps not Bugan, making the differentiation marginal at best.

The fundamental question Zophar poses is whether the ability to free hit in Gameweek 34—allowing him to pick any players regardless of his current squad—justify the strategic complexity of Route A. His conclusion is that this benefit "doesn't seem too inspiring," suggesting he leans toward the wildcard approach despite the similar outcomes.

Route A: Free Hit 33 Team Construction

Zophar provides detailed insight into how he would construct his Free Hit 33 team, should he pursue Route A. The philosophy centers on identifying players with the highest probability of returns rather than simply copying the template team from wildcard 32 managers.

In defense, Zophar would double up on Leeds' defense with Dalow and Biel. This represents a calculated gamble, as Leeds are facing Manchester City at home—a fixture that intuitively seems unfavorable. However, Zophar explains that the template owners from Gameweek 32 likely already own the "Stroke" (likely a premium Arsenal defender given their strong defensive record), making doubling up on Leeds a way to differentiate. He acknowledges hoping to get "lucky with maybe Bol spiking a goal," suggesting he recognizes the risk involved. He contrasts the Leeds double-up against the alternative of Newcastle defense away, where the clean sheet potential might seem higher but the defensive contribution (defcon) potential is actually lower. "These guys are just absolute defcon gods," Zophar says of Leeds defenders, noting they could secure a clean sheet against City at home and that the defcon potential (accumulating points through defensive actions beyond just clean sheets) might be higher than meets the eye.

In midfield, Zophar would double up on Manchester City midfielders, hoping that the Rodri news remains vague and that Guardiola doesn't risk his star midfielder against Burnley. This double City midfielder approach represents a bet on O'Reilly's unavailability creating value in his teammates. Zophar expresses strong desire to own Chucky (likely a premium midfielder, possibly McAlear or similar) for the first time this season, considering it a "difference" play that could separate his team from the pack. Tavanir (likely a Villa player given context) represents "an easy pick for the Bournemouth mid on the penalties set pieces," according to Zophar—a player with a clear role in dead-ball situations that makes him an attractive Free Hit option.

Palmer (Chelsea's Cole Palmer) is a player Zophar "hasn't owned yet all season" and would represent another "first time" acquisition for his Free Hit team. Chelsea have struggled recently, scoring "just three goals, zero goals over the last three matches," as Zophar notes, but he believes they "really need to come good" and that Palmer remains a good option when they eventually turn their form around.

Pedro (likely Pedro Neto or similar Chelsea player) also appears in the proposed Free Hit team, though Zophar is more measured about his prospects. He's "still a good option Chelsea now" but the form concerns are real.

In attack, Calvert-Lewin is selected despite his well-documented struggles—he's missed 10 big chances of the last 11. Zophar's logic is brutal but pragmatic: "He's still getting them, so might as well pick him, right?" The minutes are good, he has penalties, and the opportunity cost of not playing him is higher than the risk of his continued profligacy. Harland (Erling Haaland) is described as "the obvious captaincy choice" for this Free Hit team.

Zophar acknowledges this Free Hit 33 team is "again quite template" but defends his selections by noting that if these are genuinely the best players available, he doesn't want to differentiate for differentiation's sake. "Churkey is a difference. Bejo is a difference," he notes, implying that having some unique picks matters, but most of the team will overlap with wildcard 32 managers anyway. "These guys need to score the points," he adds, demonstrating a pragmatic rather than contrarian approach.

Gameweek 34: Navigating the Post-Free Hit Landscape

A critical consideration in Route A is what happens in Gameweek 34 after deploying the Free Hit in Gameweek 33. Zophar walks through his hypothetical bus team for that gameweek, revealing the structural challenges this strategy creates.

His goalkeeper situation becomes problematic: with Dravka (Raya) and Bugan already in his squad, he "does not have a keeper"—suggesting the Free Hit team in Gameweek 33 included different goalkeepers, leaving him with a blank spot. He will "have to make at least one transfer" just to fill this roster requirement.

The defensive situation is more stable. Virgil, Gabriel, and Muki form what Zophar describes as "the top defenses for that week," corresponding to fixtures against Palace, Arsenal, and Nottingham Forest respectively. This triangulation suggests strong clean sheet potential across all three premium defenders. Anderson (likely a Wolves or Tottenham defender) is included despite Zophar's expectation that he "concedes"—the defcon potential remains high, particularly since "center backs especially against Villa do quite well for defcon."

In midfield, Rogers benefits from Villa's clean midweek in that gameweek, meaning no midweek fixtures will tire the team before their game against Fulham. Zophar also notes that with McIn and Taylor in the side, Rogers' potential "is a little bit better now anyway," suggesting these players improve Villa's attack and therefore Rogers' assist potential. Bruno will "likely be my captain" for this gameweek, while Bumo remains "likely to start" despite his recent substitute appearance. Wilson (Newcastle's Callum Wilson) gets the nod at home against Villa—"the fixture against Villa isn't really a great one, but still at home, he's an absolute god," Zophar insists.

Thiago emerges as the most intriguing forward option for Gameweek 34, with Zophar noting his impressive away record: "still the second highest scorer in the league away to Manchester United who I think have just kept five clean sheets all season, only Burnley and Wolves have fewer." This fixture profile represents an exceptional opportunity—Newcastle away to Manchester United, a team with one of the worst defensive records in the league. Zophar asks whether Thiago could "outscore somebody like a Salah or Saka that people pick on a free hit"—suggesting his preference for Newcastle's striker over the more obvious premium options.

The forward situation remains "a little bit tricky" because of transfers. Zophar considers swapping Harland for Bowen (West Ham's Jarrod Bowen) to create budget flexibility, potentially enabling him to acquire "a Ryan instead of Rose" (likely players with different price points). He wants to use "a maximum of two of my three free transfers this week" rather than burning all three, preserving ammunition for a potential "one-week punt on Gameweek 35" featuring Thiago. This represents sophisticated chip management—thinking several gameweeks ahead rather than optimizing for the immediate fixture only.

"I'm doing this so I don't want to go to Gameweek 35 on my wildcard with just no transfers in the bank," Zophar explains, acknowledging that going wildcard with zero transfers as contingency could prove costly if circumstances change unexpectedly. "Not an ideal situation," he concedes about the post-Free Hit landscape.

The defensive core would feature double Arsenal defense with Ryan and Gabriel facing Fulham at home—a favorable fixture. Sessi and Hill (likely Struijs and another premium defender) would face Crystal Palace at home, with Zophar noting that "Palace would be playing in Europe either side of that week," suggesting fatigue could benefit Arsenal.

Midfielders for this week would include Palmer facing Nottingham Forest, Bruno, Tair, Churkey, Simmeno, Harland, all described as "all good fixtures." The bench for Bench Boost would include "mainly Dalow, Calbert Lewin, Beij, also triple leads and Rhaldo"—essentially stacking cheaper players who happen to have favorable fixtures but carry significant uncertainty.
