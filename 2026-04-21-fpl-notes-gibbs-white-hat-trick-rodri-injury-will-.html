FPL notes: Gibbs-White hat-trick, Rodri injury + will Pep rotate?

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

Morgan Gibbs-White went off. Nottingham Forest went absolutely wild.

His hat-trick against Burnley on Sunday brought the City Ground to its feet and gave Fantasy Premier League managers exactly what they wanted: a 29-point haul from a player priced at just £6.1m. At the other end of the Premier League title race, though, Manchester City emerged from a hard-fought 2-1 win over Arsenal with a different kind of problem on their hands.

Rodri's groin injury raises familiar fears

Rodri (£6.3m) was substituted in the second half and will be assessed in the coming days. Pep Guardiola was not yet able to put a timeline on his absence.

> "I don't know how long Rodri will be out. [It's the] Groin I think the doctor said but we will see. We will make a test tonight or tomorrow."

It is the same body area that cost Rodri large portions of last season. City won the title without him in the run-in, but the timing of this setback—midweek at Burnley, then Saturday's FA Cup semi-final against Southampton—could not be more inconvenient. If the assessment comes back poorly, Guardiola will need to lean on a midfield that has had precious little competition for places this season.

Pep finally admits rotation is coming

For three matches since the international break, Guardiola had made only one change: in goal. The same XI, almost on autopilot, grinding out results. But speaking after Sunday's win, the City boss acknowledged that the schedule now demands something different.

> "If you are a good team, we can do it together. These guys cannot play all the time. Now we go to Burnley and then London to play Southampton."

There is real urgency at the top of the table too. Beat Burnley on Wednesday and City go top, decided on goals scored or goal difference. Guardiola's message to his squad was pointed: do not lose focus. Everton, Brentford, Aston Villa, Crystal Palace, Bournemouth away—the run-in is brutal, and every result carries weight.

Cherki and O'Reilly give City real options

If rotation is coming, City's squad players have given Guardiola reason to trust them. Rayan Cherki (£6.4m) produced a moment of genuine quality to open the scoring against Arsenal, working space on the edge of the box and bending the ball into the far corner after previously striking the post. He has now returned 20 points across his last two appearances.

Erling Haaland (£14.5m）scored the winner—five shots, three big chances—and his season is not finished. But the more interesting discovery for City right now might be Nico O'Reilly (£5.1m), who started as a roaming left-back and produced the clever cutback that set up Haaland's goal.

Guardiola was lavish in his praise.

> "Incredible defensively, offensively and set pieces. What a player he is becoming. I don't know if he is the best player or the man of the season but the young one? Guys, it has to be."

The numbers support the hype. Matheus Nunes (£5.3m) also banked an assist, for Cherki's strike. City have goals coming from multiple positions, which matters enormously when a key midfielder is under a cloud.

What this means for FPL managers

The immediate concern is Rodri. If his injury rules him out for the Burnley game and beyond, City's midfield structure changes—and so does the value of the £6.3m enabler many managers have relied on. The opportunity, conversely, is in Cherki's price point and O'Reilly's growing involvement. Both are cheap enough to be rotation-proof, and both are delivering at a level that makes them impossible to ignore.

With City playing twice more this week—midweek at Burnley, then Saturday in the FA Cup—managers need to decide now whether to hold Rodri and hope, or pivot to one of the emerging alternatives.

The title race is too tight for caution.
