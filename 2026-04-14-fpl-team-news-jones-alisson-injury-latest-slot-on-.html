FPL team news: Jones + Alisson injury latest, Slot on Ngumoha

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

Arne Slot has admitted he would be surprised if Curtis Jones is available for Liverpool's Champions League quarter-final second leg against Paris Saint-Germain on Tuesday — but the midfielder was photographed in training on Monday, leaving the door just open enough.

Jones was forced off at half-time during Saturday's 2-0 win over Fulham after feeling discomfort in his groin. Speaking after that match, Slot confirmed the issue had been bothering Jones for several minutes before he was withdrawn, and his assessment carried little optimism.

> "He felt a little bit his groin. It wasn't the moment — he felt it already for a few minutes. So let's hope for the best, but I would be surprised if a player who comes off [like that] he's available for Tuesday."

The training picture — Jones among the group on the grass at the AXA Training Centre — offers a sliver of hope, though it's the kind of image that can mean very little in practical terms. Whether he takes part in the full session or merely completes some modified work is the detail that matters. The official word, if it comes, will arrive on Tuesday morning. Liverpool head to Paris needing to overturn a first-leg deficit after losing 1-0 at Anfield.

There is no ambiguity surrounding Alisson Becker. The Brazilian goalkeeper remains absent with a muscle problem and has not yet returned to full training with the squad.

> "Tuesday, I don't expect him. He's not training with the team yet. But he's working very hard on his recovery, so we hope to have him back in the near future."

That leaves Caoimhin Kelleher as the certain starter between the posts for a match Liverpool can ill afford to concede in. Alisson's continued absence is a running subplot of Liverpool's season, with the No. 1 having managed only a handful of appearances since returning from a previous hamstring issue.

The longer injury list is unchanged. Wataru Endo (foot), Stefan Bajcetic (hamstring), Conor Bradley (knee), and Giovanni Leoni (knee) all remain sidelined.

Rio Ngumoha's big-stage test

Away from the fitness bulletin, Slot was asked about Rio Ngumoha — and offered a fuller defence of the 17-year-old than he has given in some time. The context matters: Ngumoha has been thrust into meaningful minutes at an age when most players are still learning what senior football looks like, let alone performing in it. Tuesday's tie will demand more again.

Slot's backing centred on two arguments. First, the teenager's temperament.

> "I think everyone who knows his history a bit more, there's a lot of expectation around him for many years already, and he's always been able to focus on football, to become better, to become stronger, and to become ready at 16 years of age to score your first goals, 17 years of age and already [having] had multiple starts for the first team of Liverpool."

Second, the quality itself. Slot pointed to Ngumoha's ability to take on defenders in one-v-one situations at the highest level — both in the Champions League and the Premier League — as evidence the player belongs at this stage. The caveat Slot added is also revealing: he called it "a good thing to have so many experienced players around you, which he has in the dressing room." That phrasing acknowledges both Ngumoha's youth and the safety net Liverpool's squad still provides. Whether that safety net is enough when the pressure ratchets up in Paris is a question only the match can answer.

Liverpool's season hangs on a two-legged tie that is now five days from being settled. The team news offers no comfort on the injuries. The young player offering the most intrigue is also the one with the most to prove.
