# The Scout Squad: Our Top Picks for FPL Gameweek 35

Fantasy Football Scout's Scout Squad feature operates under specific budget requirements. At least one sub-£5.0m goalkeeper, at least one sub-£5.0m defender, at least one sub-£6.0m midfielder, at least one sub-£7.0m forward, and no more than three players from the same club must be included in the squad.

## Most Selected Players

The most picked players for Gameweek 35 include David Raya, Karl Darlow, Gabriel Magalhaes, Nico O'Reilly, Bruno Fernandes, Rayan Cherki, Erling Haaland, and Dominic Calvert-Lewin. Igor Thiago received four votes while James Hill earned three votes.

## Budget Goalkeepers

Karl Darlow of Leeds has kept three clean sheets in five matches and is priced at £3.9m, making him a viable budget option between the posts.

## Attacking Options

Erling Haaland remains the game's top scorer in terms of goals and points, playing for Manchester City at a price of £14.6m. His consistent scoring record continues to make him a premium asset despite his high cost.

Nico O'Reilly of Manchester City has contributed four goals and four clean sheets in his last seven league matches. He is available at £5.2m, offering budget-friendly attacking potential from the Manchester City squad.

Dominic Calvert-Lewin of Leeds has accumulated more expected goals (xG, 11.90) and big chances (23) than any other player from Gameweek 13 onwards. However, he has spurned 12 of his latest 14 key moments and has underperformed his xG by -2.51 goals. His price stands at £5.7m.

## Forward Considerations

Over 1.8 million FPL managers bought Ollie Watkins of Aston Villa last week. The forward is priced at £8.8m and has attracted significant managerial interest.

Morgan Rogers, also of Aston Villa, is the second most frequent shooter this season with 79 shots. He had only one big chance during a 13-game run, though three have occurred in his latest four games. Rogers is priced at £7.5m.

## Fixture Note

Burnley have just two goals in their last six matches and are already relegated, suggesting limited attacking threat in their remaining fixtures.
