FPL Gameweek 32 Round-Up: Blank Gameweek Pressure, Fixture Congestion, and Strategic Calls

The FPL landscape has shifted dramatically as we enter the business end of the season, with Gameweek 31 presenting managers with their first major blank gameweek test. Arsenal, Crystal Palace, Manchester City, and Wolves all blank this week, forcing difficult decisions across thousands of squads. With no doubles confirmed through the remainder of the campaign and fixture congestion peaking between Gameweeks 32 and 36, the strategic picture has become clearer—but not necessarily easier.

The timing of this blank gameweek could not be more awkward. With 20 teams in action in both Gameweek 32 and the subsequent gameweeks through Gameweek 36, there is no immediate double gameweek to target. The data confirms that GW39 will be the major blank GW with 20 teams blanking, making it the prime target for a Free Hit chip. GW32, meanwhile, offers the highest fixture density with all 20 teams in action—making it the logical Bench Boost target for those holding off until the run-in.

Injury concerns continue to muddy the waters for several premium assets. Gabriel dos Santos Magalhães, the Arsenal defender sitting at £7.2m with 9.7 form and 43.1% ownership, is a major doubt with a knee injury assessed at 75% chance of playing. His centre-back partner William Saliba (£6.1m, 7.0 form, 13.2% ownership) faces a similar ankle problem, also at 75% availability. Jurriën Timber (£6.3m, 7.3 form, 26.9% ownership) completes an Arsenal defensive triumvirate dealing with injury concerns. Managers holding Arsenal assets will need to monitor the news closely ahead of the deadline, as the Gunners' blank in GW31 makes their GW32 fixtures against Everton particularly valuable for those who survive the blank.

Bruno Borges Fernandes remains the undisputed form king, topping the charts with 11.0 form and 189 season points at £10.3m. The Manchester United talisman is owned by 44.3% of managers and faces a favourable run of fixtures that has seen his price climb substantially. His consistency—he averages over 9 points per game in his last five—makes him the default captain choice for those seeking reliability over ceiling.

However, the captain debate extends beyond the obvious choices. Anthony Gordon (£7.4m, 7.5 form, 7.3% ownership) has emerged as a differential gem for Newcastle, while João Victor Gomes da Silva (£5.3m, 8.0 form, 0.4% ownership) represents the ultimate budget enabler at Wolves with explosive recent returns. Morgan Gibbs-White (£7.4m, 7.0 form, 5.0% ownership) continues to deliver for Nottingham Forest and offers a middle-ground premium option. Among the more affordable options, Harry Wilson (£6.1m, 6.0 form, 25.0% ownership) has been a revelation for Fulham and deserves consideration for those looking to balance budget with returns.

Chelsea's João Pedro Junqueira de Jesus (£7.8m, 6.2 form, 50.4% ownership) remains the most owned player in the game despite middling recent form, highlighting the mass appeal of premium Chelsea assets. His ownership percentage suggests many managers are banking on a turnaround rather than current returns.

The transfer strategy for this week centres heavily on navigating the GW31 blank. Managers without coverage of the four blanking teams face difficult calls—either taking hits to bring in playing assets or accepting a reduced score. For those looking further ahead, the fixture congestion in GW32-36 suggests that carrying extra forwards and midfielders through the Bench Boost could pay dividends, particularly with all teams playing in GW32.

Given the fixture density data, the Bench Boost makes most sense in GW32 when every team is in action—managers can load their bench with premium options knowing they will all play. The Free Hit should be reserved for GW39 when 20 teams blank, allowing managers to completely reshape their squad for that week alone.

For the immediate GW31, the recommended captain picks lean towards players with home fixtures and strong recent returns. Fernandes remains the safest choice, though those seeking upside might consider Gordon or Gibbs-White given their form and fixtures. Managers with Manchester City assets face a particularly tricky situation—their blanks this week mean selling or benching premium City players is the only option, though the fixture run post-GW31 is favourable.

Players to avoid this week include those on blanking teams who have limited appeal even when playing. Without specific doubles on the horizon, the strategy of loading up on bench fodder from teams with good fixture runs makes sense. The absence of confirmed doubles through GW38 means the traditional "differential" approach of targeting double gameweeks may need to be abandoned in favour of consistent performers.

The FFS Members Cup has added another layer of drama to the season, with the second round set for GW32. The competition has already delivered its share of upsets, with several top-ranked managers falling in GW31 while lower-ranked players advanced. This underscores the randomness inherent in the cup format and serves as a reminder that even the best overall standings provide no guarantees when luck intervenes.

Three key actions emerge for managers this week:

First, finalise your GW31 transfers by ensuring you have coverage across the 16 teams in action. If you own Arsenal, Crystal Palace, Manchester City, or Wolves assets, identify replacements early rather than taking hits on deadline day.

Second, commit to your chip strategy. If holding the Bench Boost, GW32 is the target with all 20 teams playing. If planning a Free Hit, reserve it for GW39 when 20 teams blank. The fixture density data confirms GW32-36 all have maximum fixtures, making this the optimal window for bench boosting.

Third, captain Fernandes or a premium Liverpool asset. With the form data firmly in their favour and fixtures aligned, these players offer the highest probability of returns in what promises to be a challenging gameweek for many squads.

The run-in is upon us, and the decisions made in the next few gameweeks will define seasons. Navigate the blanks wisely, position your chips correctly, and may your captains deliver.
