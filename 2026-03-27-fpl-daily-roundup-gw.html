FPL Daily Round-Up: Gameweek 32 Preview — March 27, 2026

Injury Clouds Hang Over Arsenal's Title Charge as FPL Managers Face Tricky Blank Week Decisions

The Fantasy Premier League landscape has rarely been more complex to navigate, and this weekend's Gameweek 32 presents managers with a particularly thorny set of challenges. With Arsenal, Crystal Palace, Manchester City, and Wolves all blanking simultaneously, the decision-making calculus becomes acutely personal depending on each manager's squad composition, chip arsenal, and season ambitions. The facts sheet reveals several significant injury concerns that will shape transfer strategies and captain selections across the FPL community, particularly the mounting doubts over Arsenal's defensive spine at a critical juncture of the season.

Gabriel dos Santos Magalhães at £7.2m leads the form charts with a remarkable 9.7 rating and 173 season points, yet the Brazilian defender carries a knee injury rated at just 75% chance of playing. His absence would be devastating for the 43.1% of managers who own him, stripping away a reliable points magnet at both ends of the pitch. Arsenal's defensive options are further complicated by Jurriën Timber's ankle problem—also at 75% availability—which has already seen his ownership climb to 26.9% this season as he accumulated 149 points at £6.3m. William Saliba (£6.1m, 13.2% ownership) rounds out the Arsenal injury triumvirate with the same 75% fitness probability, leaving Mikel Arteta's backline in genuine doubt heading into a fixture that could define their title challenge.

The Gunners' blank gameweek creates a cascading effect across the FPL ecosystem, forcing managers to reassess their defensive templates entirely. Those heavily invested in Arsenal's budget defenders—David Raya Martín at £6.0m (34.6% ownership, 129 points) or Bukayo Saka at £9.8m (8.0% ownership, 133 points)—must now consider whether to ride out the blank or pivot to alternatives who actually feature this weekend. The injury uncertainty compounds this difficulty, as managers cannot even be certain their Arsenal assets would return value even if the blank were avoided.

The Captaincy Landscape: Fernandes Dominates but Questions Linger

Bruno Borges Fernandes continues to redefine what an FPL midfielder can achieve, sitting atop the form table with an extraordinary 11.0 rating while accumulating 189 season points for the 44.3% of managers who trust him. Manchester United's talisman represents the default armband choice this gameweek, yet his blank status means those without the Brazilian—or those forced to use their triple captain chip elsewhere—must identify viable alternatives from the limited pool of active assets.

The facts sheet reveals several intriguing options for managers seeking captaincy alternatives. João Pedro Junqueira de Jesus at Chelsea (£7.8m) has been an exceptional budget-enabling asset this season, with 50.4% ownership and 164 points, but Chelsea's fixture difficulty must be weighed against his excellent form rating of 6.2. Antoine Semenyo at Manchester City (£8.2m) represents another premium option carrying 53.6% ownership and 174 points, though Manchester City's blank status eliminates him from immediate consideration. Morgan Gibbs-White at Nottingham Forest (£7.4m, 5.0% ownership, 133 points) offers a differential route with his 7.0 form rating, as does Anthony Gordon at Newcastle (£7.4m, 7.3% ownership, 98 points) with a 7.5 form rating that suggests underlying points potential.

Danny Welbeck at Brighton (£6.2m) has quietly assembled an impressive season with 106 points and a 6.0 form rating, representing perhaps the most balanced combination of price, availability, and form among viable captain options. Harry Wilson at Fulham (£6.1m) has emerged as a genuine budget enabler with 152 points and 25.0% ownership, his 6.0 form rating providing respectable upside without the premium price tag.

Transfer Strategy: The Everton Bandwagon Gains Momentum

The most striking pattern emerging from the form data is the remarkable cluster of Everton assets near the top of the rankings, suggesting a coordinated pivot toward David Moyes' resurgent side. Iliman Ndiaye at £6.2m (5.5 form, 112 points, 4.3% ownership) represents perhaps the most under-owned Everton asset relative to his output, while James Garner at £5.2m (6.5 form, 139 points, 4.3% ownership) has been an exceptional budget option throughout the season. James Tarkowski at £5.7m (6.0 form, 142 points, 11.3% ownership) continues to offer the defensive stability that has underpinned Everton's push up the table.

Kiernan Dewsbury-Hall at £5.1m (5.0 form, 117 points, 5.0% ownership) provides another avenue into Everton coverage, though his lower form rating compared to Garner raises questions about recent trajectory. Most intriguing among the Everton contingent is Norberto Bercique Gomes Betuncal at £5.0m, whose 7.2 form rating represents exceptional value for the 2.6% of managers who have discovered him. The Portuguese midfielder's price point makes him a compelling budget option, particularly for managers needing to free funds for premium assets elsewhere.

Nottingham Forest continues to generate significant FPL interest through Morgan Gibbs-White (£7.4m) and Elliot Anderson (£5.5m, 5.0 form, 138 points, 8.4% ownership), whose consistent outputs have made him a favourite among data-driven managers. Igor Jesus Maciel da Cruz at £5.8m (5.0 form, 81 points, 1.3% ownership) represents a lower-owned alternative into Forest's attacking structure, though his limited returns this season suggest limited upside. Neco Williams at £4.7m (5.2 form, 104 points, 3.0% ownership) offers a budget route to Forest's defensive solidity.

Players to Approach with Caution

The injury situation at Arsenal extends beyond the defenders to cast doubt over several premium options, and managers should exercise particular caution with Bukayo Saka (£9.8m). While Saka's 133 points represent solid returns for his price, the combination of his own fitness concerns and Arsenal's blank gameweek makes him a liability rather than an asset this gameweek. Similarly, David Raya Martín (£6.0m) at 34.6% ownership presents a dilemma—his strong season (129 points) is rendered worthless by Arsenal's fixture cancellation, forcing managers to potentially carry dead weight.

Mikkel Damsgaard at Brentford (£5.6m) carries a knock with 75% playing probability, which while better than some alternatives, introduces unnecessary risk into squads at a critical juncture. The Danish midfielder's 5.8 form rating doesn't justify the uncertainty, particularly when budget options like Norberto Bercique Gomes Betuncal at £5.0m offer superior form with full availability.

Rodrigo 'Rodri' Hernandez Cascante at £6.3m deserves special mention as a cautionary tale about reputation over returns. The Manchester City midfielder has managed just 57 points this season with 0.5% ownership, representing one of the most significant overperformances of budget expectations relative to actual returns. Managers who roster him on reputation rather than evidence are likely regretting that decision severely.

Chip Strategy: The Blank Week Squeeze

The Gameweek 32 blank creates immediate pressure on managers' chip strategies, particularly those who have yet to deploy their wildcard or free hit. The 7-day chip strategy context reveals that the FFS Members Cup competition—which rewards single-gameweek scores minus transfer costs—has eliminated several high-profile managers this week, underscoring how critical this gameweek becomes for anyone with remaining cup ambitions.

Managers without significant Everton, Newcastle, or Forest coverage face a genuine dilemma: use their free hit to navigate the blank and access the strongest available XI, or accept a reduced score and preserve the chip for future gameweeks. The absence of confirmed doubles in Gameweeks 29-38 means the free hit's value is diminished compared to previous seasons, where managers could target specific double gameweeks with precision. This structural change forces a more conservative approach to chip deployment, with wildcard timing becoming increasingly critical.

For those still holding their wildcard, the current thinking suggests holding fire until Gameweek 33 or beyond, when fixture information becomes clearer and injury situations resolve themselves. The Arsenal injury crisis in particular argues for patience—committing to a defensive template now could result in owning players who don't feature for multiple weeks. However, managers with pressing needs—those with multiple blanking players or severely imbalanced squads—may need to pull the trigger earlier to remain competitive.

Bournemouth's Undervalued Assets

Marcos Senesi Barón at Bournemouth (£5.1m) deserves particular attention as a defensive option that has flown under the radar despite 137 season points and a 6.5 form rating. The Argentine centre-back's 19.9% ownership seems low relative to his consistent outputs, and with Bournemouth facing favorable fixtures in the run-in, he represents excellent value at his price point. Adrien Truffert at £4.7m (6.8 form, 122 points, 3.2% ownership) offers an even cheaper route into Bournemouth's defense, though his lower price reflects the ceiling difference between the two options.

Andoni Iraola's side has been one of the season's surprise packages, and the facts sheet suggests their underlying numbers support continued investment. Managers looking to free budget for premium options elsewhere could do worse than investing in Bournemouth's defensive pairing, which offers both value and potential for clean sheet points in the remaining fixtures.

Where Managers Should Focus This Week

The evidence points toward three critical actions this Gameweek 32:

First, managers must resolve their Arsenal exposure immediately. Whether that means holding faith with Gabriel, Timber, and Saliba despite their injury concerns or pivoting to alternatives like Everton's Tarkowski or Forest's Gibbs-White, indecision will prove costlier than any wrong choice made decisively.

Second, the Everton cluster represents genuine value that the wider FPL community hasn't fully recognized. Norberto Bercique Gomes Betuncal at £5.0m with 7.2 form is the most obvious target, but James Garner at £5.2m offers more proven consistency if budget constraints allow. David Moyes' side looks genuinely transformed, and their remaining fixtures offer significant potential for continued returns.

Third, managers should resist the temptation to wildcard prematurely. The Arsenal injury situation, Manchester City's inconsistent outputs, and the general uncertainty across the league suggest that patience will be rewarded. Gameweek 33 will bring clearer information, and the saved wildcard flexibility could prove invaluable when fixture swings emerge later in the season.

The FFS Members Cup results this week—where managers with 50 points advanced while those scoring 71 were eliminated—underscore how single-gameweek variance can overwhelm even the best-laid plans. For FPL managers focused on overall rankings rather than cup competitions, the message is clear: stay patient, stay flexible, and don't let panic drive decisions that could cost precious points down the stretch run.
