FPL Blank Gameweek 31 Complete Guide: Short-Term vs Long-Term Strategy for Fantasy Premier League Managers

The Critical Situation That Finally Got Resolved

The Fantasy Premier League landscape has shifted dramatically following this week's European results, and FP Harry is here to break down exactly what it means for your season. After weeks of uncertainty, Crystal Palace's advancement in European competition has officially confirmed what many managers suspected but hoped might not happen: gameweek 31 will be a blank gameweek with no fixtures for Arsenal, Wolves, Manchester City, and Crystal Palace. This fixture between Manchester City and Crystal Palace cannot be rescheduled into either the midweek of gameweek 31 or gameweek 32, meaning it will not be played until at least the midweek of gameweek 33 at the earliest.

With the deadline less than 24 hours away, this video serves as an essential guide for managers navigating one of the most complex periods of the FPL calendar. FP Harry walks viewers through everything they need to know, from chip strategy implications to player recommendations based on different scenarios. The core message is clear: this is not the time for hasty decisions. The next few weeks will see significant changes in the fixture landscape, and managers who maintain flexibility will be best positioned to capitalize when the picture becomes clearer.

Understanding the Two Chip Strategy Paths

The heart of this video centers on chip strategy, which FP Harry identifies as the most consequential decision point for FPL managers this season. There are two main paths that managers are likely to deploy, and the deciding factor is the outcome of the FA Cup match between Liverpool and Manchester City.

Option One involves playing the Wildcard in gameweek 32, followed by Bench Boost in gameweek 33, and then Free Hit in gameweek 34. This strategy is more likely if Liverpool defeat Manchester City in the FA Cup. Under this path, gameweek 33 is expected to be the biggest double gameweek of the season, making Bench Boost particularly valuable there.

Option Two involves Free Hit in gameweek 33, Wildcard in gameweek 35, and Bench Boost in gameweek 36. This path becomes more attractive if Manchester City defeat Liverpool in the FA Cup, as it would change the predicted doubles and blanks for those later gameweeks.

FP Harry emphasizes that this is not an exact science. Even if Manchester City win, some managers might still prefer Option One, and vice versa. However, the FA Cup result will fundamentally dictate what we see for predicted doubles and blanks later in the season, making it the single biggest factor in your chip planning.

The Newcastle Players Question

One of the most practical discussions in this video addresses the popular impulse to bring in Newcastle players this week. FP Harry identifies players like Malick Chowl and Anthony Gordon as attractive options given Newcastle's favorable fixtures, but he offers a crucial caveat: Newcastle players don't actually fit well unless you commit to Option One.

The reasoning is straightforward. Newcastle has a good fixture this week against Sunderland at home, followed by Crystal Palace away in gameweek 32. However, gameweek 33 is where Newcastle is predicted to double, and under Option Two, you would be using your Free Hit that week anyway. By gameweek 34, Newcastle blanks entirely with no fixture, meaning any investment in Newcastle players provides minimal value under the alternative chip strategy.

This analysis underscores a broader theme throughout the video: managers should not force themselves into buying Newcastle players this week because they don't really fit well unless you've already committed to a specific chip strategy. The recommendation is to try and keep as much flexibility as possible rather than pigeonholing yourself into one specific scenario.

The Golden Rule: Wait for FA Cup Results

Perhaps the most important advice in this entire video is the repeated plea for managers not to make early Wildcard transfers until after the FA Cup results are known. FP Harry states this plainly: "Please please please please do not make any early Wildcard 32 transfers until after the FA Cup."

The timing is crucial here. After gameweek 31, there is an international break followed by the FA Cup, and then a week later—roughly three weeks from now—gameweek 32 arrives. In that midweek between the FA Cup and gameweek 32, FPL managers will finally start receiving announcements about which teams have doubles and which teams are blanking.

The risk of jumping ahead is substantial. FP Harry notes that while you might gain 0.2, 0.3, or maybe even 0.5 in value by going early, it is simply not worth it at this point in the season. Even something as seemingly minor as Liverpool versus Manchester City and West Ham versus Leeds in the FA Cup can dramatically change the fixture landscape, potentially altering your chip strategy entirely.

The only exception would be if the absolute most obvious scenario plays out, but even then, the marginal gain doesn't justify the loss of flexibility. Teams going out of Europe and teams not even being in Europe in the first place further complicate the picture, making educated guesses about future fixtures essentially futile at this stage.

Gameweek 31 Captaincy Rankings

Moving to the immediate decision at hand, FP Harry provides his captaincy rankings for the blank gameweek 31. The top recommendation is Bruno Fernandes, who FP Harry identifies as the player most likely to get a goal or assist this week. Approximately 75% of FPL managers are considering Bruno as their captaincy pick for the Friday night fixture.

Bruno ranks highest in form and is the joint highest projected point scorer according to the FPL Scout members area. However, FP Harry acknowledges the significant caveat: Bruno's away form is definitely worse than his home form, and Manchester United are away to Bournemouth this week. Bournemouth has been one of the best home defenses this season, which does provide some justification for looking elsewhere.

Despite these concerns, Bruno remains number one for FP Harry because of the reliability and safety he provides. The difference between top captains this week is minimal because every team in gameweek 31 is predicted to score less than two goals. Fulham sit at the top playing at home against Burnley with a predicted 1.83 goals, Manchester United are just below them with 1.72, and Newcastle are around 1.76. Even Chelsea and Liverpool are in the same range. Clean sheets are also limited this week, with perhaps only Fulham and Newcastle having realistic chances of keeping them.

For managers looking to differential away from Bruno, FP Harry recommends Harry Wilson as the best alternative. The data shows that Burnley has conceded a lot of chances to right wingers this season, making Wilson an attractive option. Other alternatives include Anthony Gordon at home to Sunderland (though Newcastle just lost heavily in the Champions League and the derby last time out), Cole Palmer away at Everton despite Chelsea's poor performances recently, and Dominic Szoboszlai, who FP Harry believes is nailed on for Liverpool and their main goal threat—though there might be a Saturday morning update on Mohamed Salah that could impact this ranking.

Key Strategic Insights for FPL Managers

The first and most crucial takeaway is the importance of patience during this transitional period. The FA Cup results will fundamentally reshape the fixture landscape, and making hasty transfers before those results are known could lock you into a suboptimal strategy. The potential 0.2 to 0.5 value gain from early transfers is nowhere near worth the flexibility you sacrifice.

The second insight is that Newcastle players, despite their attractive fixtures, may not be the automatic choice many managers assume. Their utility depends heavily on which chip strategy you commit to, and committing too early to Newcastle assets before knowing your chip path could backfire spectacularly. FP Harry's own approach is to not force himself to buy Newcastle players this week because they don't fit well unless going with the first strategy option.

The third takeaway concerns maintaining maximum flexibility wherever possible. Rather than buying players who are good for only one specific scenario, managers should prioritize players who remain useful regardless of how their chip strategy evolves. This means avoiding early commitments and keeping your transfer options open through the international break and FA Cup period.

Finally, the captaincy situation in gameweek 31 requires careful consideration despite the limited options. Bruno Fernandes remains the safest choice despite his away form concerns, but Harry Wilson offers a compelling differential alternative backed by statistical evidence regarding Burnley's defensive vulnerabilities against right wingers.
