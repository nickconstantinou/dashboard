Who is suspended in FPL – and can anyone still get a yellow card ban?

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

For the first time this season, Fantasy Premier League managers can breathe easy about the accumulator's curse. With all 20 Premier League clubs having now played at least 32 matches, the threshold for yellow card bans has passed uneventfully — and Bernardo Silva was the final danger zone.

The Manchester City midfielder entered Gameweek 33 as the only player in the league still capable of picking up a two-match ban through bookings. His mission, should he choose to accept it: avoid a caution in Sunday's clash with Arsenal. He succeeded. Bernardo escaped the Etihad without a yellow, and with him, the last whisper of accumulation anxiety vanishes entirely.

What About the 15-Booking Threshold?

The Football Association's rules still technically allow for a three-match ban at 15 yellow cards. It's a high bar, but not theoretically impossible. Two players could still reach that figure — but only if they were booked in every remaining fixture. Even then, any resulting suspension wouldn't take effect until next season. Bernardo, notably, won't be a Manchester City player by then, having almost certainly moved on this summer.

So while the stat-sheet threat persists in the abstract, there's nothing actionable for FPL managers in either direction.

Who's Still Serving Bans in Gameweek 34

Three players remain on the naughty step this weekend.

Joelinton (£5.9m) and Yerson Mosquera (£4.3m) both serve the final game of two-match bans. Both are mid-price enablers in their respective sides, making their returns in Gameweek 35 marginally relevant for squad-building purposes.

Lisandro Martínez (£4.8m) has two games remaining on a three-match ban. The Argentine defender was红卡'd in Gameweek 31 before the FA added a charge for improper conduct — a combination that cost him an extended spell on the sidelines. United managers waiting on his return should mark Gameweek 36 in their diaries.

Returning This Weekend

The good news for Manchester United owners: Harry Maguire (£4.4m) is back.

The England international served a two-match absence after his Gameweek 31 dismissal and subsequent FA sanction. His return strengthens United's rearguard for the run-in — and at £4.4m, he remains one of the cheapest route into a side with ambitions on the European places.

For FPL managers, the math is straightforward. The accumulation threat has evaporated. The active bans are limited to three players, two of whom return next week. The yellow card cliff that once loomed over every GW33 fixture never materialised.

This is, in short, a clean week to manage.
