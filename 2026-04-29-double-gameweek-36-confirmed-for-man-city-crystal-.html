Double Gameweek 36 Confirmed for Manchester City and Crystal Palace

The final Double Gameweek of the 2025/26 season will feature Manchester City and Crystal Palace, with both clubs scheduled to play twice in Gameweek 36. According to Fantasy Football Scout, these two teams are the only clubs in the league who will have two fixtures during that gameweek, making it a significant opportunity for Fantasy Premier League managers.

The confirmation comes as the originally postponed fixture between Manchester City and Crystal Palace has been rescheduled. The two clubs' meeting at the Etihad, which was postponed in Gameweek 31, will now take place on Wednesday 13 May. This fixture addition creates the double header that gives both teams their second game of the gameweek.

Manchester City Player Notes

Erling Haaland, priced at £14.5m, demonstrated significant goal-scoring threat in the reverse fixtures against the teams Palace will face in Gameweek 36. The Norwegian striker scored three of Manchester City's four goals across those two matches. This performance highlights his potential as a captaincy option when managers seek to maximize returns from their double gameweek investments.

However, the underlying numbers suggest Manchester City may have benefited from some fortune in those fixtures. The combined expected goals tallies from the reverse games showed City with 2.04 xG against an opposition combined xG of 2.58, indicating the opposition created slightly better chances overall.

On the defensive side, Ruben Dias, valued at £5.5m, is currently injured and unavailable for selection. His absence may impact Manchester City's clean sheet potential during the double gameweek, which managers should factor into their planning.

Crystal Palace Player Notes

Crystal Palace lost both of their reverse fixtures during the first half of the season. They suffered a 1-2 defeat against Everton and a 0-3 loss against Manchester City in those matches. These results indicate the challenges Palace may face in their upcoming fixtures.

Despite the defeats, Palace created more promising chances than their results suggested. The Eagles' combined xG across those two games reached 3.41 compared to the opposition's 2.28, meaning they generated quality opportunities and were unfortunate not to secure better results.

Daniel Munoz, priced at £5.8m, offers Palace's most demonstrated goal threat heading into the double gameweek. He scored Palace's only goal across those two reverse fixtures, making him a notable attacking option among the Eagles' squad.

Strategic Considerations

This Double Gameweek represents the last opportunity of the 2025/26 season for managers to leverage double fixture advantages. Both Manchester City and Crystal Palace players become viable investments, though the fixture quality differs significantly between the two sides. Haaland's goal-scoring record against these opponents and Munoz's single-goal contribution provide concrete data points for manager decision-making, while the underlying xG figures suggest both attacking and defensive assets may warrant consideration depending on budget allocation strategies.
