Crystal Palace Rotation Watch: FPL Gameweek 36 Implications

Shakhtar Friendly: No Fresh Concerns

Crystal Palace emerged from their Shakhtar Donetsk friendly without any new injury concerns, according to head coach Oliver Glasner. Following the Thursday fixture, Glasner confirmed: "This was positive. So, no injury concerns." This news provides a degree of relief for Fantasy Premier League managers considering Palace assets heading into a congested fixture period.

Long-Term Absences Persist

Two players remain unavailable for selection. Eddie Nketiah (£5.4m) continues to recover from a hamstring issue, while Cheick Doucoure (£5.4m) remains sidelined with a knee/muscle problem and is also working on match fitness. Both players did not feature against Shakhtar and will not be available for the immediate run of fixtures.

Additionally, Evann Guessand (£6.2m) and Borna Sosa failed to recover in time for Thursday's game. Guessand is managing a knee injury, while Sosa's issue was described as unspecified. Neither player made the matchday squad, limiting Palace's available options in midfield and defence.

Fatigue Concerns After Shakhtar Fixture

Glasner flagged Adam Wharton (£5.0m) as a particular rotation concern, noting the young midfielder looked exhausted after the Shakhtar game. The Palace manager observed: "I could see Adam Wharton was really done at the end [on Thursday]." This suggests Wharton may require rest management across the busy schedule, making him a risky selection for managers seeking consistent minutes.

Similarly, Tyrick Mitchell (£5.0m) picked up a knock to his quad during the fixture and was visibly limping afterward. Mitchell's fitness status warrants monitoring ahead of Gameweek 36, as any recurrence could force him out of contention.

Guessand on the Brink of Return

There is a glimmer of hope regarding Evann Guessand. Despite missing the Shakhtar game, Guessand had participated in partial training sessions in the lead-up to Thursday's match. This progression suggests he may be closing in on a return to full training and match fitness. FPL managers monitoring Guessand's £6.2m price point should note this as a potential future asset, though his immediate availability remains uncertain.

Fixture Congestion and Title Race Implications

Palace faces a demanding schedule, playing three matches in six days between Thursday and Wednesday. Glasner acknowledged this burden, describing the schedule as "really tough." The compressed fixture list significantly increases rotation risk for Fantasy managers relying on Palace assets.

The significance of Palace's run-in extends beyond their own ambitions. The Eagles are scheduled to face both Manchester City and Arsenal in the closing stages of the season. Critically, Palace's match against Arsenal occurs just three days before the Gunners' UEFA Conference League final. This timing could influence Arsenal's approach to that fixture and potentially create favourable conditions for Palace.

Glasner's Prioritisation Stance

Addressing the implications for the title race, Glasner made clear he will not factor external outcomes into his team selection. "I'm not responsible for Arsenal, I'm not responsible for Manchester City; I am responsible for Crystal Palace," he stated. The manager confirmed his immediate priority is securing safety through victory against Everton: "First of all, we want to win against Everton because then we are safe... then we can think about the [Conference] League."

This stance suggests Glasner will select his strongest available XI for each match based on player welfare and Palace's own league position, rather than deliberately adjusting the title race outcome.

FPL Implications

For Gameweek 36 and beyond, managers should account for significant rotation risk among Palace assets. Wharton and Mitchell both showed signs of fatigue or minor injury following the Shakhtar game. The three-game-in-six-days schedule makes regular minutes difficult to guarantee. Guessand represents a potential future investment but remains unavailable for now, while Nketiah and Doucoure offer no immediate value.
