Trump says Iran war could end 'soon', but threatens Iran over oil disruption • FRANCE 24 English

Introduction

In his first extensive public remarks since the outbreak of hostilities between the United States and Iran, President Donald Trump has struck a dual tone—expressing confidence that the conflict can conclude swiftly while simultaneously issuing stark warnings about the consequences if Iran attempts to disrupt global oil supplies through the Strait of Hormuz. The remarks, delivered from the White House, represent the most detailed articulation of the administration's wartime strategy and political calculus since the strikes began.

The timing of these comments carries significant political weight. With midterm elections looming, the President finds himself navigating the complex intersection of military strategy, economic reality, and electoral pressure. Oil prices have already surged since the conflict began, creating a vulnerable flank for the Republican president as voters assess the administration's handling of both foreign policy and economic stewardship. Trump's statements reveal an administration attempting to project strength and resolve while managing the practical realities of global energy markets and the potential for escalation.

The Military Campaign: Trump's Victory Narrative

President Trump presented an unequivocal characterization of American military success in the opening moments of his remarks. "We've wiped every single force in Iran out very completely," the President declared, employing characteristic language of total victory. He elaborated that most of Iran's naval power has been sunk, positioning the strikes as having substantially degraded Tehran's military capabilities. The administration has continued to target Iran's drone and missile programs specifically, with Trump noting that Iranian drone capabilities are "way down" from their previous levels.

The scale of the military operation, as described by the President, has been extensive. Trump stated that American forces have struck over 5,000 targets to date, characterizing some of these as "very major targets." Perhaps most significantly, he indicated that the most important targets remain intact, suggesting a deliberate strategy of controlled escalation. "We've left some of the most important targets for later in case we need to do it," Trump explained, framing this as strategic reserve rather than incomplete planning. This approach suggests the administration has preserved significant military leverage while simultaneously signaling that the current campaign represents a measured response rather than full-scale warfare.

The President's confidence in a rapid conclusion to hostilities appears to rest on this assessment of military progress. "We continue to target Iran's drone and missile capabilities" remains a priority, he stated, suggesting that the elimination of these threats represents a key war aim. The President's characterization suggests a conflict that, while serious, is progressing favorably for American interests and can be concluded in the near term.

The Strait of Hormuz: The Oil Threshold

Beyond the battlefield progress, Trump's remarks revealed a second, equally significant dimension of the conflict—the economic warfare surrounding global oil supplies. The President articulated what appears to be a clear red line: any attempt by Iran to block or disrupt oil transit through the Strait of Hormuz would trigger a severe escalation. "I will not allow a terrorist regime to hold the world hostage and attempt to stop the globe's oil supply," Trump stated, his language elevating the stakes beyond regional conflict to global economic security.

The Strait of Hormuz represents one of the world's most critical chokepoints for oil transportation, with approximately one-fifth of global oil consumption passing through its waters. Trump explicitly linked this strategic concern to his warning, stating that if Iran "does anything to do that, they'll get hit at a much, much harder level." This threat constitutes the most direct military escalation warning issued by the President, suggesting that oil disruption would cross a threshold warranting dramatically increased military force.

The economic sensitivity of oil prices to the conflict was acknowledged directly by the President. "The price of crude oil has already soared since the war began," Trump conceded, demonstrating awareness of the domestic political implications. This price increase creates political vulnerability heading into midterm elections, as voters increasingly feel the pinch at gas pumps while assessing which party better manages both security and economic stability.

The Sanctions Reversal: Economic Pressure and Political Pragmatism

In a notable policy announcement, Trump revealed that his administration would be relaxing certain sanctions to address the oil price crisis. "We're also waving certain oil related sanctions to reduce prices," he stated, indicating a pragmatic departure from the maximum pressure campaign that characterized his first term. The President acknowledged that existing sanctions on several countries were contributing to price inflation and that removing these restrictions temporarily could help stabilize markets.

The specific mention of Russia in this context is significant. Despite the ongoing conflict in Ukraine and sustained Western pressure on Moscow, Trump indicated that sanctions relief would extend to Russia as well. "We have sanctions on some countries. We're going to take those sanctions off till this straightens out," the President explained, suggesting that the immediate priority is oil price stabilization over longer-term geopolitical objectives.

This approach reflects a delicate balancing act: maintaining sufficient pressure on Iran while ensuring global oil supplies remain robust enough to prevent economic destabilization. The President expressed optimism that the temporary relief might ultimately prove unnecessary in the long run. "Then who knows, maybe we won't have to put them on. There'll be so much peace," he offered, though this optimistic projection stood in tension with the continued hostilities and threats of further escalation.

Iran's Response: Defiance and Escalation

The President's remarks drew an immediate response from Tehran. Iran's Revolutionary Guard responded to Trump's comments with defiance, according to the report, though the transcript was truncated before detailing the full nature of their statement. This response suggests that the back-and-forth between the two nations continues to follow a pattern of escalating rhetoric and counter-rhetoric, with each side positioning itself as the defender against aggression.

The dynamics of this exchange illustrate the challenge facing both parties: finding an off-ramp from hostilities while maintaining domestic credibility and international prestige. Trump's warnings about the Strait of Hormuz appear designed to deter Iranian escalation while projecting American resolve, but such statements also risk closing diplomatic windows and hardening positions in Tehran.
