# Who Is Suspended in FPL – and Can Anyone Still Get a Yellow Card Ban?

## Current Suspensions in Fantasy Premier League

A handful of Premier League players are currently serving bans that affect their availability for Fantasy Premier League managers. Understanding who is suspended – and for how long – remains an important part of making informed transfer decisions.

## Dan Ballard – Season Over After Red Card

Dan Ballard's Fantasy Premier League season is over. The Northern Ireland international received a red card for violent conduct in Gameweek 35, resulting in a suspension that will keep him sidelined for the remainder of the campaign. Ballard became the latest player to be given his marching orders for tugging the hair of an opponent. At his price of £4.7m, managers will need to look elsewhere for defensive options through the final matches of the season.

## Mykhailo Mudryk – Doping Suspension Under Appeal

Mykhailo Mudryk hasn't featured since November 2024 and is in the process of appealing a four-year suspension for doping. The Chelsea player remains unavailable at a price of £4.9m. The ongoing appeal process means his situation could change, but for now, managers should treat him as unavailable for selection.

## Lisandro Martinez – Return From Suspension

Lisandro Martinez is set to return from a three-match suspension in Gameweek 36. The Manchester United defender, priced at £4.8m, will become available again for managers looking to strengthen their backline after serving his ban.

## Yellow Card Accumulation – No Longer a Concern

Regarding yellow card bans, no player can now reach the 15-mark by the end of the 2025/26 campaign. Anyone amassing 15 cautions by the end of the season would have picked up a three-game ban. However, with insufficient matches remaining in the season, yellow card accumulation is no longer a worry for any Premier League player. Managers no longer need to factor in suspension risk from yellow card accumulation when making late-season decisions.
