<p># FPL Strategy Guide: Should You Wildcard in Gameweek 32?

## Introduction

As the Fantasy Premier League season enters its crucial final stretch, managers face one of the most consequential decisions of the campaign: whether to play their Wildcard in Gameweek 32 to optimize their squad for the remaining seven weeks. FPL Harry, a popular FPL content creator, returns with a detailed video analyzing this exact question, providing two distinct draft strategies for managers who have decided to wildcard this week or are still on the fence about their chip strategy.

The timing of this video comes at a particularly challenging moment for many FPL managers. Liverpool attackers have failed to deliver in recent weeks, creating squad holes that need addressing. The upcoming fixture schedule presents complex trade-offs between immediate Gameweek 32 returns and maximizing the highly valuable Bench Boost chip in Gameweek 33, which features several teams with double fixtures. Harry acknowledges this has been "quite a difficult few weeks for a lot of people," making strategic wildcard planning more important than ever.

This video builds on a previous chip strategy guide published after FA Cup results confirmed the double Gameweek 33 fixtures. Harry explicitly notes that this content assumes viewers have already decided to wildcard in Gameweek 32 or have already activated it—for those still undecided on their overall chip approach, he directs them to that earlier video for foundational context.

## Understanding the Chip Strategy Landscape

The current FPL season presents a uniquely complex chip strategy environment because there is no universal "best approach" that works for all managers. Harry emphasizes that "it's very team dependent this season" and "there's not a real one strategy fits all approach." This is because different managers have used their chips at different rates, have different squad strengths and weaknesses, and face different fixture run-ins.

The most common chip configuration managers are working with includes Triple Captain, Wildcard, Bench Boost, and Free Hit. The key strategic question becomes how to deploy these chips to maximize their value across the final weeks, particularly given that the most powerful chips—Triple Captain and Bench Boost—are most valuable when deployed during double Gameweeks.

For managers keeping all their chips, the optimal strategy typically involves playing both Triple Captain and Bench Boost during double Gameweeks. This creates pressure to find two separate double Gameweeks throughout the remainder of the season, which influences whether Wildcard should be played now or saved for later. However, Harry notes that "majority of people actually don't still have their Triple Captain available," which changes the calculus significantly. Without Triple Captain, managers don't need to find a second double Gameweek, making alternative strategies more viable.

The rising popularity of the Free Hit 33 + Wildcard 35 + Bench Boost 36 pathway reflects this reality. This approach becomes particularly attractive, Harry explains, "especially as you might not have a Gameweek 32 wild card team that looks that strong." The decision ultimately depends heavily on your current squad's strength in Gameweeks 32 and 34—if your team already looks solid for those matches, saving the Wildcard for later makes more sense.

## The Wildcard 32 Strategy: Bench Boost 33 + Free Hit 34

For managers committed to playing their Wildcard in Gameweek 32, the primary motivation should be enabling a Bench Boost play in Gameweek 33. Harry is explicit: "The main reason to wild card this week in opposed to wild card 33 or even saving it further down the line is to allow yourself to bench boost next week." If you don't have the Bench Boost remaining, he doesn't "really think it's worthwhile" to wildcard this week.

The challenge with a Gameweek 32 Wildcard, however, is that the draft options simply aren't particularly strong. Harry breaks down the problematic fixture situation: Bournemouth travel to Arsenal (a difficult away fixture), Leeds travel to Manchester United, Manchester City face Chelsea, and Brighton go away to Burnley. While the Burnley fixture isn't terrible, overall "the only real reason to wild card this week is if you want to set up a bench boosted 33, or maybe your team really, really needs it."

This creates a tension: you're wildcarding to access the Gameweek 33 doubles, but the players you need to bring in for that double don't have favorable Gameweek 32 fixtures themselves. The strategy essentially involves taking a small hit in Gameweek 32 to set up better returns in Gameweek 33 when your bench is fully activated.

## Detailed Draft Analysis

Harry presents a specific squad construction optimized for the Bench Boost 33 + Free Hit 34 strategy, starting with goalkeepers. He selects **Verrugn** and **Darlow** as his goalkeepers, both of whom double in Gameweek 33 and form part of the bench boost activation. Verrugn faces the favorable matchup of away to Burnley in Gameweek 32. Harry notes there was some rotation uncertainty at Leeds during their cup match, but he's "fully expecting Darlow to be the number one goalkeeper going forward in the league as he has been for the past couple of months."

In defense, Harry opts for a triple Bournemouth stack on the bench, with **Tavanir** in midfield plus **Senessi** and **James Hill** as defensive options. He explains his reasoning: "I like their long-term fixtures. I like their attacking threat. I like their defcon, and they're very nailed. And they're good cheap options to help facilitate the rest of this bench boost squad." These budget options allow more premium spending in attacking positions while maintaining squad depth.

For the starting XI, Harry selects **Gabrielle** from Arsenal despite a slight doubt—he expects the defender to start in a Champions League match midweek but advises managers to "keep an eye on that" as he might need rotating out. **Van Heckway** from Leeds and **O'Reilly** from Manchester City complete the defensive lineup. The Manchester City/Chelsea game creates an unusual concentration: "this draft has got five players in that game. Luckily, only one of those is a defender. She'll basically just backing there to be a lot of goals in that match."

In midfield, **Pascal Gro** from Brighton serves as the attacking asset. Harry notes you could alternatively use Danny Wellbeck, but "I don't think you need a double up. But I think one from each of those teams is probably the way that I would go."

The expensive midfielder slot goes to **Palmer**, a choice Harry acknowledges will draw disagreement: "A lot of people will disagree with me including O Palmer in a Gameweek 32 wild card." And a lot of the midfielders are not particularly great who double in Gameweek 33 with Enzo suspended for Chelsea in Gameweek 32 after what he said about leaving the club over the international break." **Bruno** from Manchester United completes the midfield—ironically, Harry catches himself initially saying "Bruno" when he meant "Palmer" before correcting himself. Bruno doesn't double in Gameweek 33 but is included as a "player that you will want longterm."

The forward line features **Jao Pedro Harland** and **Calun** from Wolves. Harry notes "You could go for Wellbeck here" as an alternative, but "Outside of that, they're not great forwards to pick from in terms of the double. Those are probably the two others that I'd have a look at."

## Long-Term Squad Planning and Trade-offs

One significant consideration with this draft is that two players without Gameweek 33 doubles—**Bruno** at Chelsea and **Gabrielle** at Arsenal—would form part of your Bench Boost squad in Gameweek 33. Harry addresses this: "Now, you could look to go against Gabrielle in terms of this draft if you really want to maximize the double. You could go for another Leeds defender, someone like a Rodon if he's going to be available, if not someone like a V be or a Pascal Stout."

However, he ultimately recommends keeping Gabrielle because "long-term you will want some Arsenal in here." This exemplifies the broader trade-off managers face: optimizing for the immediate Gameweek 33 double versus maintaining strong long-term asset coverage.

Looking further ahead to Gameweek 35, Harry suggests the strategy involves "hopefully roll some transfers as best you can to build them up to buy some Crystal Palace players into your team once Gameweek 35 and 36 comes around where Palace are expected to double against Manchester City who you already have triple up from the original draft." This creates a pathway to pivot toward Palace assets when their favorable double arrives.

## An Alternative Strategy: Delaying the Bench Boost

Recognizing that the Gameweek 32 Wildcard draft isn't particularly attractive, Harry presents an alternative strategy that likely appeals to fewer managers but merits consideration: Wildcard 32 + Free Hit 34 + Bench Boost 36. This approach delays the Bench Boost to the final double Gameweek of the season rather than using it in Gameweek 33.

The benefit, Harry explains, is that it "allows you to pick" a stronger squad for the immediate Gameweeks rather than forcing weak options from the limited double Gameweek 33 teams. This strategy works better for managers whose current squad is reasonably strong for Gameweeks 32 and 34 but who still want to wildcard now rather than save it entirely for Gameweek 35.

## Key Takeaways

**The Wildcard 32 decision is entirely squad-dependent.** Your current team's strength in Gameweeks 32 and 34 should heavily influence whether you wildcard now or save it. If your team looks solid for the upcoming matches, consider the alternative strategies.

**Bench Boost is the primary motivation for Wildcard 32.** Harry emphasizes that without Bench Boost available, wildcarding this week offers insufficient value. The play is specifically about setting up a fully-bench-boosted squad for Gameweek 33's double fixtures.

**The draft quality is genuinely weak this week.** Multiple teams doubling in Gameweek 33 have poor Gameweek 32 fixtures, forcing managers to choose between taking a short-term hit or missing out on optimal double Gameweek coverage. This isn't a typical wildcard situation with easy choices.

**Palmer despite the difficult fixture remains viable.** While Palmer faces Manchester City this week, his double in Gameweek 33 and subsequent fixture against Forest make him worthwhile. The expensive midfield options with Gameweek 33 doubles are limited due to Enzo's suspension and other factors.

**Long-term planning should incorporate the Palace double.** Gameweeks 35-36 are expected to feature a Crystal Palace double against Manchester City (which you'd already have triple coverage of from this draft), making strategic transfer planning toward Palace assets a logical endpoint.
