The Template Team Philosophy

One of the most valuable insights in the video challenges the common FPL misconception that managers need differentials to gain rank. The creator argues that having a template team doesn't mean you can't climb significantly, referencing his own experience with a Wildcard that was described as "very template minus a couple of alterations." Despite the supposedly template nature of his team, the rank climb has been quite significant.

The creator reveals that his Wildcard had only about 1,300 identical teams in the whole world according to Live FPL's Clone feature, which tracks how many teams among the top nine million managers match your selection. This is on a Wildcard week where you'd expect more identical teams than any other gameweek, yet there were still only 1,300 matches. Over the entire season, there have been numerous gameweeks where his team didn't have a single clone—not one identical team in the entire world. The suggestion is that many managers think they're copying template teams when they're actually making subtle decisions that differentiate them significantly from the crowd.

The implication for Free Hit strategy is clear: aligning your Free Hit completely with the Team of the Season minus those with a blank (essentially the template premium picks) is a viable approach. Players like Areola, Gabriel, van Dijk, Bruno, Rice, Wilson, and Isak represent the most consistent performers, and simply loading up on these assets during favorable fixtures can be more effective than chasing obscure differentials that fail to deliver.
