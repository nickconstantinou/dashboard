FPL Strategy Without Chips: Best Players and Teams for the Season Run-In

April 8, 2026 — Fantasy Premier League managers who have already burned through their chips face a challenging path through the final weeks of the 2025/26 season. With Double Gameweek 33, Blank Gameweek 34, and a likely Double Gameweek 36 looming, those without Wildcards or Bench Boosts must carefully target teams with favourable fixture schedules and players capable of delivering consistent returns.

The core strategic approach for chip-less managers revolves around maximizing fixture counts. Manchester City and Crystal Palace each have one extra match compared to everyone else, likely scheduled for Gameweek 36. Manchester City will double in Gameweek 33, blank in Gameweek 34, and likely double again in Gameweek 36, creating a relatively straightforward run-in. The article suggests that taking a points hit to acquire City players like Nico O'Reilly (£5.0m), Antoine Semenyo (£8.2m), or Erling Haaland (£14.4m) could prove worthwhile, as the extra fixture should compensate for any transfer costs.

Crystal Palace presents a more complicated option. While they play every week without blanking, potential UEFA Conference League semi-finals surrounding Gameweek 35 introduce significant rotation risk. The current best Palace options appear to be goalkeeper Dean Henderson (£5.1m), full-back Daniel Munoz (£5.8m), defender Maxence Lacroix (£5.1m), and winger Ismaila Sarr (£6.3m), though their involvement in European competition adds uncertainty.

An alternative "slow and steady" approach targets teams with no blanks across the remaining schedule—specifically Arsenal, Newcastle United, and Nottingham Forest. These teams play every Gameweek without interruption, making them ideal for navigating Blank Gameweek 34 when six teams will fail to play. Arsenal leads the league with 15 clean sheets and just 22 goals conceded in 2025/26, making their defenders relatively secure even against difficult opponents. Newcastle has exited all cup competitions both domestically and in Europe, meaning they can fully focus on the league with no fixture congestion. Nottingham Forest offers particular value in Gameweek 33, where Neco Williams (£4.7m) and Morgan Gibbs-White (£7.4m) host Burnley at home—a fixture the article notes could easily outscore some of the more popular "doubler" options at Bournemouth, Brighton, and Leeds.

Why This Matters for FPL Managers

The distinction between having chips and being chip-less at this stage of the season can mean the difference between a top-10k finish and missing out on triple captain bonuses entirely. With so much on the line—title race implications for Arsenal and Forest, relegation battles affecting Leeds and Burnley—motivation among players across these teams remains high. Managers without chips must be more disciplined about fixture selection, focusing on teams that can deliver returns every single gameweek rather than those who might only shine in a single double.

Key Numbers and Facts:

  • Double Gameweek 33 features: Manchester City, Chelsea, Brighton, Bournemouth, Leeds, Burnley
  • Blank Gameweek 34: Six teams will blank
  • Arsenal: 15 clean sheets, 22 goals conceded (best defensive record in 2025/26)
  • Palace's risk factor: UEFA Conference League semi-finals potentially affecting Gameweek 35
