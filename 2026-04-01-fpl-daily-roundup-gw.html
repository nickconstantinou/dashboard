Today's Biggest FPL News: De Zerbi Takes the Tottenham Helm

In the most significant managerial development of the season, Tottenham Hotspur have appointed Roberto De Zerbi as their new head coach following the departure of the previous incumbent. The appointment carries immediate Fantasy implications—De Zerbi's reputation for attacking football and high defensive lines historically benefits premium forwards and creative midfielders in his sides. However, FPL managers should note that this appointment arrives mid-gameweek, meaning any tactical shift at Spurs will take time to materialise. The early evidence suggests patience may be required before Tottenham assets become essential picks.

Injury Update: Gunners' Defensive Doubts Create GW31/32 Dilemma

Arsenal face a blank in Gameweek 31, but their injury list is causing significant concern ahead of the Double Gameweek 32. Gabriel dos Santos Magalhães (£7.2m) remains the biggest worry—the Brazilian centre-back carries a knee injury that Fantasy Football Scout articles flagged as a 75% chance of participation. His 10.0 form rating and 173 season points make him one of the most owned defenders in the game at 43.1%, yet managers face an agonising wait. Jurriën Timber (£6.3m) has an ankle problem with the same 75% participation probability, while William Saliba (£6.1m) also carries an ankle knock. The triple threat to Arsenal's backline is particularly troubling given David Raya (£6.0m) also appears in the form table with 7.0 rating and 129 points at 34.6% ownership.

Bukayo Saka (£9.8m) is another Arsenal player carrying a knock, though his 75% participation chance suggests he should feature. The £9.8m winger has 133 season points despite a modest 6.0 form rating, and his availability will significantly impact captaincy discussions.

Across North London, Iliman Ndiaye (£6.2m) of Everton has a foot injury listed at 75% participation probability. The £6.2m midfielder has 112 season points and 4.3% ownership, making him a differential option if fit. Piero Hincapie (£5.1m) at Arsenal also carries a muscle injury at 75% chance of playing.

Crystal Palace's Adam Wharton (£5.0m) has a knock at 75% participation, though he's a lower-ownership option at 0.6%.

Chip Strategy: Patience the New Premium

Given the absence of confirmed doubles across the season's final stretch, chip strategy must adapt. The Free Hit chip holds diminished value without a blank gameweek to exploit—managers should consider saving it for potential future blanks that remain unconfirmed. Essentially, the traditional chip playbook needs abandoning—managers should focus on form, fixtures, and player availability rather than complex chip constructions.
