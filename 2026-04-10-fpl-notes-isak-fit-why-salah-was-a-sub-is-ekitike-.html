FPL notes: Isak fit, why Salah was a sub + is Ekitike at risk?

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

The Full Story

Liverpool's Champions League semifinal first leg against Paris Saint-Germain ended in a disappointing 2-0 defeat at Parc des Princes, with the Reds failing to register a single shot on target—a statistic that should alarm FPL managers invested in Liverpool's attacking assets. The match saw several significant developments relevant to Fantasy Premier League preparation as Gameweek 32 approaches.

Most notably, Alexander Isak (£10.3m) made his return from injury in the 78th minute, coming on for Hugo Ekitike (£9.3m). This substitution immediately raises questions about Ekitike's place in the starting XI for Liverpool's upcoming fixtures. Meanwhile, Mohamed Salah (£14.0m)—in what could be his final Champions League matches for Liverpool—did not feature at all, remaining unused on the bench throughout the 90 minutes.

Manager Arne Slot explained the decision to bench his talismanic forward, citing improved squad depth as injuries begin to ease. "It is always difficult to leave players out that work very hard and they deserve to start, Mo is definitely one of them but there are more players on the bench that work very hard to play," Slot said. "Add to that how much Mo has done for the club, how influential he's been for me, for the club, that makes it difficult but I think it's also quite common and not many times this season I had to make choices like that."

Why This Matters

For FPL managers, this match provides crucial intelligence heading into Gameweek 32's deadline. Isak's return directly threatens Ekitike's starting position—the French striker had been a popular budget option in Liverpool's attack but now faces competition from one of the league's most prolific finishers. Managers priced at £9.3m for Ekitike must decide whether to hold or cash out before the second leg at Anfield.

The Salah situation presents a different dilemma. With Liverpool trailing 2-0 and facing a crucial second leg, there's no guarantee the Egyptian will start in Gameweek 32. However, his complete omission from Wednesday's match—when Liverpool needed attacking options—suggests he may feature more prominently in the league fixture.

More broadly, Liverpool's attacking struggles this season are concerning. They've been outshot by opponents in most league games and managed just ten attempts on target across losses to Manchester City (FA Cup) and Brighton. With only a two-goal deficit to overturn against PSG, Liverpool must rediscover their offensive spark at Anfield.
