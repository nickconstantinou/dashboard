FPL team news: Gabriel, Saka, Rice + Timber injury latest

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

April 6, 2026 — Arsenal manager Mikel Arteta faces a mounting injury headache ahead of Tuesday's UEFA Champions League quarter-final second leg against Sporting CP, with Bukayo Saka and Jurrien Timber confirmed as unavailable while several other key players return to contention.

The Full Story

Arsenal held an open training session on Monday afternoon, and Arteta addressed the media shortly after, providing crucial updates on his squad's fitness situation ahead of the Sporting tie. The Gunners, who suffered a shock FA Cup defeat to Southampton on Saturday, received positive news on three fronts: Gabriel Magalhaes (£7.2m), Declan Rice (£7.3m), and Leandro Trossard (£6.6m) were all sighted in training and confirmed available for selection. Gabriel had hobbled off with a knee injury during the Southampton match, while both Rice and Trossard had withdrawn from international duty over the break and missed the cup fixture.

However, the outlook remains bleak for Saka and Timber. Neither player was observed at Monday's training session, and Arteta delivered a candid assessment when pressed on their availability: "No, they're having trouble, they're not ready yet. Let's see, hopefully they're going to be ready for the weekend, if everything goes well, and that's a massive boost because in recent days we've lost so many important players and that's something that we need to change immediately."

Ebelechi Eze (£7.2m) and Piero Hincapie (£5.1m) were also absent from the training ground. While Hincapie suffered a muscle issue representing Ecuador during the international break, Eze has been absent since the EFL Cup final in mid-March. Notably, some social media sources suggest Eze could recover in time for Gameweek 32, though this remains unconfirmed by the club.

Why This Matters

This injury situation carries significant weight for both Arsenal's immediate ambitions and Fantasy Premier League managers. The Gunners are in the midst of a crucial period, competing on multiple fronts in both the Premier League and Champions League, and the absence of Saka—who has been one of Arsenal's most consistent attacking threats this season—represents a substantial blow to their chances against Sporting and in the title race.

For FPL managers, the situation creates immediate tactical decisions. Gabriel, Rice, and Trossard returning to availability offers relief for those who have been without them, but the ongoing absences of Saka and Timber leave substantial gaps in Arsenal's squad. With the season reaching its critical phase, understanding who is available and when they might return is essential for making informed transfer and captaincy decisions.

Key Details

  • Players available: Gabriel Magalhaes (knee issue), Declan Rice (withdrawn from England squad), Leandro Trossard (withdrawn from national team duty)
  • Players unavailable: Bukayo Saka (£9.8m), Jurrien Timber (£6.3m), Eberechi Eze (£7.2m), Piero Hincapie (£5.1m)
  • Arteta's update: Saka and Timber "having trouble, they're not ready yet"—hope remains for weekend availability
  • Timeline: Eze reportedly could recover for Gameweek 32, though not confirmed by club
  • Hincapie injury: Muscle problem sustained while playing for Ecuador over international break
  • Previous absences: Timber and Eze have not played since the EFL Cup final in mid-March

    • Context & Background

    Arsenal's injury crisis has escalated dramatically over recent weeks. The club has seen multiple key players succumb to injuries during a physically demanding period of the season, with the international break proving particularly costly. Rice and Trossard both withdrew from their national team squads, while Hincapie picked up his injury representing Ecuador.

    Saka's absence is particularly notable given his importance to Arsenal's attacking play. The England international withdrew from the England squad over the international break and has not recovered in time for the Sporting fixture. Timber, who has been a valuable utility option for FPL managers at £6.3m, has been absent since the mid-March EFL Cup final, with Arteta previously suggesting he was in contention for Saturday's cup tie before ultimately failing to feature.

    What's Next

    Arsenal will face Sporting in the Champions League on Tuesday without Saka and Timber, relying on the returning trio of Gabriel, Rice, and Trossard. Arteta remains hopeful both Saka and Timber will be available for the weekend, which could prove pivotal in Arsenal's Premier League title challenge. FPL managers should monitor announcements closely, particularly regarding Eze's potential return for Gameweek 32, as the club continues to manage its extensive injury list during the season's business end.
