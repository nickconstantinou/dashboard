# Eagles Landing? | #FPL DGW36 Podcast Recap

## Overview

The Eagles Landing? podcast returned for a special episode covering Gameweek 35 and previewing the crucial Double Gameweek 36. Recorded on Tuesday the 5th of May, this episode marked the final double game week of the Fantasy Premier League season—an especially important milestone for managers looking to secure their league positions.

The episode structure included a review of Gameweek 35, featuring Tom's voice notes, followed by a comprehensive preview of the double game week assets available from Manchester City and Crystal Palace.

## Team Updates

Tom was absent from this episode due to his family expecting another baby. The host noted that this situation had been anticipated, expressing relief that all was well with the entire family. Despite his absence, Tom remained engaged with the podcast by sending voice notes that were spliced throughout the show, allowing his insights to remain part of the discussion.

Making his podcast debut was Mo, joining the show for the first time. This episode introduced Mo to listeners as he contributed to the FPL conversation alongside the host.

## FPL Preparation Insights

The episode revealed valuable information about free transfer availability heading into DGW36. Mo has two free transfers available, which the host noted as "perfect" for navigating the double game week. The host himself has three free transfers, and it was suggested that Tom also likely has three free transfers at his disposal. This transfer flexibility positions both managers well for optimizing their squads for the double fixtures.

## Double Gameweek 36 Preview

The core focus of this episode was the preview for DGW36, which represents the final double game week of the season. The hosts discussed assets from both Manchester City and Crystal Palace, providing analysis to help listeners make informed decisions about which players to target for their squads.

## Chip Strategy Discussion

Given that many FPL managers would be activating chips such as bench boost and triple captaincy during this high-value double game week, the hosts examined expected chip usage patterns. They noted that bench boost and triple captaincy would be popular choices this week, with one of the podcast participants potentially being chip active themselves.

## Production Notes

During the recording, Arsenal was playing in the background as they competed in Champions League action. The episode blended Tom's pre-recorded voice notes with live discussion between the host and first-time guest Mo, creating an engaging format despite Tom's absence from the live recording.
