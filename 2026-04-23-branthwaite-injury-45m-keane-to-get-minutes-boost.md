# Branthwaite Injury Boosts Keane's Fantasy Prospects

## Branthwaite Sidelined for Remainder of Season

Everton confirmed on Tuesday that Jarrad Branthwaite (£5.3m) suffered a hamstring injury in Sunday's Merseyside derby against Liverpool. The centre-back will now undergo rehabilitation at Finch Farm with Everton's medical team and is unlikely to feature again this season.

The injury is unrelated to the hamstring area which required surgery in October, with a scan confirming no procedure is needed this time around. This news closes Branthwaite's season and opens the door for rotation in Everton's backline.

## Keane's Path to Minutes

With Branthwaite sidelined, Michael Keane (£4.5m) becomes a more viable option for Fantasy managers seeking budget centre-back coverage. Keane has only started five of the last 12 Everton league matches, having begun Sunday's clash with Liverpool on the bench.

However, the 32-year-old has demonstrated strong output when called upon. Keane has scored three goals in 2025/26, with his two fellow centre-backs netting a solitary goal apiece. His attacking contribution extends to shot volume, as Keane averages a shot every 102.9 minutes—a quicker rate than both Branthwaite (113.3) and Tarkowski (180).

## Defensive Output and Points Potential

Keane also rivals his competitors on clean sheet probability. The former has banked DefCon in 54.2% of his starts, with Tarkowski up at 59.4%. In fact, he averages more points per start than both of his positional rivals, making him an efficient option when selected.

## Fixtures Offer Encouragement

Beyond the injury boost, Everton's fixture run provides additional appeal. Other than the Gameweek 35 clash with Manchester City, it's a pretty good run-in for the Toffees. Indeed, the schedule is enough for them to rank third on the Season Ticker based on their remaining fixtures.

For managers priced out of premium defensive options, Keane offers a combination of renewed opportunities and favourable fixture spacing heading into the season's conclusion.
