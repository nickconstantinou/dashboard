# The Scout Squad: Fantasy Premier League Gameweek 32 Top Picks Revealed

**The Full Story**

FantasyFootballScout.co.uk has released their highly anticipated Scout Squad selections for FPL Gameweek 32, featuring their panel's best player picks for the upcoming fixture. This edition marks the return of the popular feature after a three-week hiatus, with general manager Sam, deputy editor Tom F, editor Neale, and staff writer Marc each contributing their personalizedXI selections within strict budget constraints. The panel operates under a £83.0m total budget limitation, forcing writers to source cheaper alternatives when necessary. Each writer must adhere to specific requirements: at least one goalkeeper under £5.0m, one defender under £5.0m, one midfielder under £6.0m, one forward under £7.0m, and no more than three players from any single club.

**Why This Matters**

The Scout Squad feature represents one of the most influential FPL selection guides in the fantasy football community, with thousands of managers relying on these recommendations weekly. For the April 2026 period, FPL managers face critical decisions with Double Gameweek 33 looming, making Gameweek 32 selections particularly consequential for squad rotation strategies. The feature's focus on isolation picks—considering only the upcoming fixture without medium-term planning—provides managers with immediate tactical advice while acknowledging the approaching double gameweek creates complicating factors for lineup decisions.

**Key Details**

Gabriel Magalhaes (Arsenal), Bruno Fernandes (Manchester United), Erling Haaland (Manchester City), Danny Welbeck (Brighton), and Jarrod Bowen (West Ham) each received four votes from the six-player votes available across the four writers, making them the most recommended picks. Goalkeeper selections split between Senne Lammens, David Raya, and Bart Verbruggen with two votes each. The most notable recommendation comes from Marc's focus on Brighton midfielder Jack Hinshelwood, who has started six consecutive matches as an attacking midfielder, generating 10 close-range shots and two big chances while recording all three assists for Danny Welbeck during that period—suggesting a dangerous partnership ahead of Brighton's fixture against Burnley. Game time concerns exist for Welbeck heading into Double Gameweek 33.

**Context & Background**

This Gameweek 32 falls in the late-April portion of the 2025-26 Premier League season, a critical period where FPL managers must balance immediate point-scoring opportunities against future fixture planning. The feature's return after a three-week break coincided with broader content resumption at FantasyFootballScout.co.uk, indicating the site had been on reduced publication schedules during a fallow period in the football calendar. Marc specifically noted being surprised Hinshelwood hasn't featured more prominently in Brighton triple-up discussions, highlighting the panel's role in identifying under-the-radar picks that may not receive adequate attention in broader FPL discourse.

**What's Next**

The Scout Picks will be compiled from these Squad selections, with players receiving the most votes most likely to appear in the official FPL Scout Picks. Managers should watch for Double Gameweek 33 announcements, which will significantly impact transfer strategies heading into Gameweek 32 conclusions. The Welbeck-Hinshelwood connection at Brighton represents a particularly timely recommendation given their favorable fixture against Burnley and the potential for continued assist production that could deliver significant returns before rotation concerns emerge in the double gameweek.
