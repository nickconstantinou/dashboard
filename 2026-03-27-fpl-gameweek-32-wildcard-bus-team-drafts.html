FPL Gameweek 32 Wildcard Strategy: Navigating Double Gameweeks and Fixture Uncertainties

The Full Story

Fantasy Premier League managers face a complex strategic puzzle heading into Gameweek 32, as the long gap until the deadline presents both opportunity and uncertainty. With many managers planning to deploy their second Wildcard chip of the season, the complication lies in awaiting the outcomes of FA Cup quarter-finals and subsequent match rearrangements before committing to a definitive 15-man squad. The article from Fantasy Football Scout explores hypothetical team drafts that account for the anticipated fixture movements, recognizing that even finalizing chip strategy depends heavily on these results.

The central assumption driving current FPL planning is that Gameweek 34 matches clashing with FA Cup semi-finals will move earlier, creating a Double Gameweek 33 scenario—mirroring what occurred last season. Under the prediction that Arsenal and Chelsea will progress through their quarter-final ties, Newcastle United and Brighton and Hove Albion would be pushed into this advantageous Double Gameweek 33. The article presents two hypothetical Wildcard drafts, with Draft 1 predicated on Manchester City defeating Liverpool, which would please many managers given the existing assumption that City's unscheduled hosting of Crystal Palace will result in a Double Gameweek 36.

The Gameweek 32 deadline arrives on Friday, 10 April, and managers should have clarity on all fixture movements by that point, particularly regarding whether the West Ham United or Leeds United cup winner will have their match simply relocated to the midweek of Gameweek 34—a decision that hinges on the Manchester City versus Liverpool outcome.

Why This Matters

For the millions of FPL managers worldwide, this period represents one of the most consequential windows of the season for chip strategy. The second Wildcard is a limited resource, and deploying it incorrectly could cost managers hundreds of thousands of positions in the rankings or even qualification for valuable prizes. Understanding which teams will feature in Double Gameweeks—and which players represent the best value within those doubles—can be the difference between a successful season and a disappointing finish.

The article's focus on the Manchester City-Liverpool result demonstrates how interconnected FPL strategy has become with actual Premier League and cup competition outcomes. Managers must now track not just their own teams' fixtures but also results across multiple competitions to anticipate double gameweeks. This complexity adds a strategic layer beyond simply picking the highest-scoring players, rewarding those who can read the broader football landscape and plan accordingly.
