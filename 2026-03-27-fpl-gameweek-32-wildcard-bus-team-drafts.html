FPL Gameweek 32 Wildcard Strategy: Navigating Double Gameweeks and FA Cup Uncertainties

Fantasy Premier League managers face a critical decision point heading into Gameweek 32, with the long international break creating both opportunity and uncertainty. According to Fantasy Football Scout, many FPL managers plan to activate their second Wildcard chip of the season during this period, but the situation remains complicated by pending FA Cup quarter-final results and subsequent fixture rearrangements. The article presents two hypothetical squad drafts designed to maximize returns from the anticipated Double Gameweek 33, where teams knocked out of FA Cup competition will have matches rescheduled.

The strategy hinges on predicting which clubs will progress through the FA Cup quarter-finals. The analysis assumes Arsenal and Chelsea will advance to the semi-finals, which would push those clubs along with Newcastle United and Brighton and Hove Albion into Double Gameweek 33. Meanwhile, the outcome of the Manchester City versus Liverpool fixture carries significant weight in the Wildcard decision-making process. Fixture experts suggest that the winner of the West Ham United versus Leeds United tie may simply have their match shifted to the midweek slot of Gameweek 34. The entire situation should clarify by the Gameweek 32 deadline on Friday, 10 April.

The Strategic Landscape: Understanding Chip Timing

FPL managers receive two Wildcard chips per season, and the timing of their deployment can make or break a season. The current scenario presents a classic optimization problem: managers must decide whether to commit to a Wildcard now based on incomplete information or wait for greater clarity. The article emphasizes that finalizing a chip strategy will likely depend on the FA Cup results, as those outcomes determine which teams gain additional fixtures and which face blank gameweeks.

The anticipated Double Gameweek 33 represents a valuable opportunity because it would allow managers to field 16 or more players from teams with two matches that week. However, the accompanying Blank Gameweek 34—caused by FA Cup semi-final scheduling conflicts—requires its own mitigation strategy. Managers can either deploy a Free Hit chip to completely reshuffle their squad for that single gameweek or preserve free transfers from previous weeks to make targeted changes. Both draft options presented in the article focus on exploiting Double Gameweek 33 while providing pathways through the subsequent blank.

Key Player Recommendations and Differential Options

The first draft assumes Manchester City defeats Liverpool, which unlocks additional strategic options including a potential triple-up on City assets. Erling Haaland (£14.5m) remains the centerpiece despite a concerning scoring drought—he has only exceeded six points once since Gameweek 17. However, the expected Double Gameweek 36 featuring Manchester City's rescheduled match against Crystal Palace makes him difficult to omit despite his recent struggles.

Nico O'Reilly (£5.0m) emerges as an intriguing budget option who has frequently been used in midfield positions within the FPL system. The article highlights his unexpected attacking contributions, noting he scored two close-range headers during Sunday's EFL Cup final while playing at left-back. His versatility across positions makes him particularly valuable for managers seeking to maximize budget allocation.

Among differentials, Anthony Gordon (£7.4m) stands out for his recent form—he has scored in three consecutive matches while playing as a striker rather than his natural wing position. Ismaila Sarr (£6.3m) offers similar appeal as Palace's secondary penalty-taker who also has a double gameweek on the horizon. Daniel Munoz (£5.8m) represents another Palace differential with significant attacking potential, though he has been quieter since returning from a December-to-January knee injury. Arsenal defenders remain recommended despite the omission of Declan Rice (£7.3m), with the Gunners' backline considered the strongest in the league. Sarr competes directly with budget option El Hadji Malick Diouf (£4.1m) for Gameweek 33 selections.

Why This Matters for FPL Managers

The stakes in this decision are substantial. A well-timed Wildcard deployment can generate 50-100 additional points compared to poorly-timed chip usage, effectively representing the difference between finishing in the top 10,000 versus the top 100,000 globally. The article's focus on the "bus team" concept suggests these drafts prioritize reliability and guaranteed minutes over high-risk, high-reward differentials, making them suitable for managers in competitive league positions seeking to protect their leads.

The broader context involves approximately 7-8 million active FPL managers globally, many of whom face similar decision points simultaneously. When thousands of managers make the same chip decisions, it affects market prices and availability of popular players. Understanding these dynamics helps explain why waiting for confirmation before Wildcard activation might be prudent, even if it means potentially missing a few points in Gameweek 32.

Looking Ahead: The FA Cup Factor

The final pieces of the puzzle will fall into place following the FA Cup quarter-final matches. Once winners are confirmed, the Premier League will announce fixture rearrangements, allowing managers to finalize their Wildcard strategies with certainty. The article notes that similar patterns occurred last season, providing a template for what to expect. Managers should monitor announcements closely during the week of Gameweek 32, as the difference between a single-gameweek and double-gameweek asset could be worth 10-15 points or more.

Those choosing to Wildcard in Gameweek 32 must accept some level of risk regarding fixture congestion. However, the article suggests that being organized and prepared with multiple scenarios gives managers flexibility when making final decisions. The alternative—waiting until the FA Cup picture clarifies—means potentially entering Double Gameweek 33 with a suboptimal squad and fewer chips remaining to optimize.

Ultimately, this period represents a crucial inflection point in the FPL calendar. Managers who navigate the uncertainty successfully, whether through bold early Wildcard activation or patient waiting, position themselves strongly for the season's run-in. The article provides a framework for thinking through these decisions, though individual manager circumstances—including chip availability, overall rank, and risk tolerance—will determine the optimal approach for each unique situation.
