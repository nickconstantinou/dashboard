FPL Gameweek 32 Wildcard Strategy: What Managers Need to Know

The Full Story

With a significant gap before Fantasy Premier League Gameweek 32, FPL managers are facing one of their most complex strategic decisions of the season—whether to deploy their second Wildcard chip. However, the article from Fantasy Football Scout cautions that committing to a specific 15-man squad remains premature until FA Cup quarter-final results are confirmed and subsequent fixture rearrangements are announced.

The central challenge revolves around predicting which teams will advance through the FA Cup and how that will affect Double Gameweek allocations. The article's primary assumption is that Arsenal and Chelsea will progress through the quarter-finals, which would push those clubs along with Newcastle United and Brighton and Hove Albion into a Double Gameweek 33. This assumes the matches clashing with FA Cup semi-finals will be moved earlier, mirroring what happened last season.

Fantasy Football Scout presents two hypothetical Wildcard squad drafts, both centred on navigating Double Gameweek 33 while preparing for a Blank Gameweek 34 through either a Free Hit chip or saved free transfers. The drafts highlight Manchester City defender Nico O'Reilly (£5.0m) as a particularly attractive option—despite primarily playing left-back, he scored two close-range headers in Sunday's EFL Cup final and has frequently been deployed in midfield positions. Erling Haaland (£14.5m) features despite his struggles, with managers betting on a triple-up ahead of City's assumed Double Gameweek 36 against Crystal Palace. Meanwhile, players like Anthony Gordon (£7.4m), Ismaila Sarr (£6.3m), and Daniel Munoz (£5.8m) from Palace are flagged as differentials with double fixtures on the horizon.

Why This Matters

For the millions of FPL managers worldwide, this decision carries substantial financial implications. A well-timed Wildcard can be the difference between finishing in the top 100,000 or climbing into the game's top tiers. The article emphasizes that until the Manchester City versus Liverpool FA Cup result is known, full commitment to any squad is risky. The City-Liverpool outcome directly affects fixture planning, particularly for West Ham United or Leeds United depending on which club progresses through their cup tie.

The strategy discussed represents a broader tension in FPL: whether to plan aggressively based on predictions or maintain flexibility until information is confirmed. With Gameweek 32's deadline falling on Friday, 10 April, managers have a narrow window to execute their plans once clarity emerges from the FA Cup fixtures.
