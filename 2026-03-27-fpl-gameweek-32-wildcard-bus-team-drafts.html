FPL Gameweek 32 Wildcard Strategy: Navigating Double Gameweeks and FA Cup Chaos

The Full Story

Fantasy Premier League managers are facing a complex strategic puzzle heading into Gameweek 32, with the extended gap between fixtures providing both opportunity and uncertainty. According to FPL experts at Fantasy Football Scout, many managers intend to activate their second Wildcard chip during this period, but finalizing a 15-man squad remains difficult until FA Cup quarter-final results are confirmed and subsequent match rearrangements are announced.

The central assumption driving Wildcard strategy is that Gameweek 34 matches clashing with FA Cup semi-finals will move earlier, creating a Double Gameweek 33—a pattern that occurred last season. This prediction assumes Arsenal and Chelsea will progress through the FA Cup, which would push Newcastle United and Brighton into that coveted double gameweek.

Why This Matters

FPL success often hinges on chip timing, and this period represents a critical decision point for the approximately seven million people who play the game globally. Getting the Wildcard wrong—or deploying it before key information emerges—can cost managers hundreds of thousands of positions in the rankings. The FA Cup variables create a cascading effect on fixture schedules that ripples through every squad decision.

The article provides two hypothetical "bus team" drafts designed to help managers prepare while acknowledging that the Manchester City versus Liverpool result could fundamentally alter planning. Fixture experts suggest the West Ham or Leeds winner's match may simply shift to the Gameweek 34 midweek slot, making that specific result a potential linchpin for entire strategies.

Key Details

The Gameweek 32 deadline is Friday, 10 April 2026, by which point managers should have clarity on the FA Cup outcomes and resulting fixture movements.

The Man City-focused draft includes several specific recommendations:

  • Erling Haaland (£14.5m): Triple-captain consideration despite concerning form—only once exceeding six points since Gameweek 17
  • Nico O'Reilly (£5.0m): Budget option who has frequently played in midfield despite FPL classification as a defender, scored two close-range headers in the EFL Cup final on Sunday
  • Antoine Semenyo (£8.2m): Preferred over Declan Rice (£7.3m) for attacking returns
  • Anthony Gordon (£7.4m): Scored in three consecutive matches and has been deployed up front; fellow penalty-taker Ismaila Sarr (£6.3m) identified as a differential with a double gameweek on the horizon

  • Arsenal defense: Recommended as "the best backline around" with double coverage advisable

    • Budget defender options include El Hadji Malick Diouf (£4.1m) for the Double Gameweek 33.

    Context and Background

    This article sits within a broader FPL strategy landscape where chip management separates casual players from serious competitors. The first Wildcard typically sees deployment between Gameweeks 6-10, meaning managers holding their second chip into late March have either preserved it strategically or lacked compelling alternatives.

    The FA Cup's interference with league fixtures creates the annual "blank and double gameweek" phenomenon—a scheduling irregularity where some teams have zero matches while others have two. Smart managers use chips like Wildcard and Free Hit to exploit these inefficiencies, temporarily fielding 15 players all with matches while rivals field several who don't play at all.

The Manchester City versus Liverpool result carries particular weight for those targeting City's assumed unscheduled hosting of Crystal Palace, which could create a separate Double Gameweek 36 opportunity.
