The Full Story

Fantasy Premier League managers face a critical decision point heading into Gameweek 32, with the game's second Wildcard chip poised to become one of the season's most valuable assets. According to FantasyFootballScout's analysis published March 26, 2026, the extended gap until Gameweek 32 provides managers with time to strategize—but they're operating in uncertainty until FA Cup quarter-final results reshape the fixture calendar. The article presents hypothetical squad drafts organized around potential Double Gameweek scenarios, offering tactical guidance for managers navigating one of FPL's most complex chip-strategy puzzles.

The central assumption driving strategy is that matches clashing with FA Cup semi-finals will be rearranged into a Double Gameweek 33, mirroring what occurred last season. If Arsenal and Chelsea progress in the FA Cup—as the analysis predicts—their matches, along with those of Newcastle United and Brighton and Hove Albion, would shift to that Double Gameweek. This creates a Blank Gameweek 34 that managers must account for, either through a Free Hit chip or carefully banked transfers. The Manchester City versus Liverpool result adds another layer of complexity, potentially determining whether West Ham United or Leeds United see their fixtures rearranged.

The first draft scenario assumes Manchester City defeats Liverpool, which would please managers who've already committed to City assets given the expected Double Gameweek 36 against Crystal Palace. The article recommends a "triple-up" on Erling Haaland (£14.5m) despite his relative drought—having exceeded six points only once since Gameweek 17. Defensive recommendations center on Arsenal's backline, which the analysis deems "the best backline around," while budget options include Daniel Munoz (£5.8m) and El Hadji Malick Diouf (£4.1m) for their attacking potential. The Gameweek 32 deadline falls on Friday, April 10, giving managers approximately two weeks to finalize their Wildcard squads.

Why This Matters

For Fantasy Premier League's millions of active managers, chip strategy represents the difference between finishing in the top 100,000 and the top 10 percent. The Wildcard—one of just two available per season—offers unlimited transfers for a single Gameweek, making timing crucial. With Double Gameweeks representing the highest-scoring opportunities of the season, deploying the Wildcard correctly can generate 200-300 point swings against rivals who haven't planned as carefully.

The FA Cup complication adds a layer of chaos that rewards well-informed managers. Those who understand how fixture rearrangements work—and who can predict which teams advance—gain a structural advantage. The Manchester City-Crystal Palace Double Gameweek 36 is already anticipated, making Haaland and City assets valuable regardless of this week's outcome. But managers who pick wrong on teams that don't advance face a wasted chip and potentially devastating point deficits.
