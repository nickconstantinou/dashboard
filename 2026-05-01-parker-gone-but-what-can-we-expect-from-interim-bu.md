# Parker Gone – But What Can We Expect From Interim Burnley Boss Jackson?

## Introduction: A Mutual Departure

Burnley have confirmed this morning that Scott Parker has left his position as head coach. The parting of ways is reportedly "mutually agreed" and comes after the Clarets were relegated from the Premier League last week.

Mike Jackson takes interim charge for the Clarets' remaining four Premier League fixtures. The club will not make a new permanent appointment until the summer.

## Mike Jackson's Previous Caretaker Experience

You may remember the name from his last caretaker stint as Burnley boss, which came at the back-end of 2021/22. Jackson came in and went unbeaten in his first four matches, lifting the Clarets five points from the relegation zone.

During that period, the then-interim head coach dallied with a wing-back system in the following two fixtures.

## Tactical Approach and Statistics

Jackson followed on from Sean Dyche and stuck to the tried-and-tested gameplan, rolling out a 4-4-2 for his first five matches in charge.

Comparing the two managerial periods reveals interesting contrasts. Burnley had scored 25 goals in 30 matches under Dyche, keeping seven clean sheets. 44 goals flew in at the other end. Jackson oversaw two shut-outs, with the Clarets scoring and conceding the same number of goals (nine) in his eight matches at the helm.

## Burnley's Remaining Fixtures

The first of those is Friday's match at Leeds United. Burnley have actually beaten Leeds (2-0) and Wolverhampton Wanderers (3-2) already in 2024/25. What some wags have unkindly dubbed 'El Crapico' falls in Gameweek 38.
