<p># FPL Gameweek 31 Strategy: Why Patience is Your Greatest Asset Right Now

## The Crystal Palace Result That Changed Everything

The Fantasy Premier League landscape shifted dramatically this week when Crystal Palace secured their passage through to the next round of European competition on Thursday evening. For thousands of FPL managers, this result carried far more significance than a simple cup advancement—it confirmed what many had been dreading and preparing for simultaneously: that Arsenal, Wolves, Manchester City, and Crystal Palace will all blank in gameweek 31, leaving those managers scrambling to fill their benches with virtually nothing.

FP Harry, the content creator behind this analysis, wasted no time in addressing the elephant in the room for his audience. "The big thing we were waiting for this week was European results to have a bit of an indication on what it meant for future potential doubles and blanks, and particularly that Manchester City and Crystal Palace fixture," he explained. The Palace victory means that the fixture between these two sides cannot be rescheduled into either the midweek of gameweek 31 or gameweek 32. Instead, gameweek 32 will proceed as a normal gameweek, with gameweek 33 likely becoming the biggest double gameweek of the entire season. That City-Palace fixture will not be played until at least the midweek of gameweek 33 at the earliest, assuming no further complications arise from cup competitions or other scheduling conflicts.

This confirmation eliminates one of the theoretical paths that could have simplified FPL planning this season. Had both Manchester City and Crystal Palace been knocked out of European competition, there was a possibility the fixture could have been squeezed into an earlier midweek slot. However, Palace's advancement means that path is completely closed off, and managers must now plan their chip usage around this confirmed blank.

## The Two Chip Strategy Paths That Will Define Your Season

The heart of FP Harry's analysis centers on what he describes as two primary chip deployment strategies that FPL managers should be considering for the run-in. The choice between these paths hinges almost entirely on one match: the FA Cup encounter between Liverpool and Manchester City.

**Strategy One** involves playing your Wildcard in gameweek 32, then deploying Bench Boost in gameweek 33, and finishing with Free Hit in gameweek 34. This approach is more likely to be optimal if Liverpool defeat Manchester City in the FA Cup. The reasoning here is that a Liverpool victory would create a cleaner doubles and blanks situation, allowing managers to load up on players from teams with double fixtures in gameweek 33 before pivoting to a completely fresh squad for the blank in gameweek 34.

**Strategy Two** flips the order entirely: Free Hit in gameweek 33, Wildcard in gameweek 35, and Bench Boost in gameweek 36. This path becomes more attractive if Manchester City defeat Liverpool in the FA Cup, as the resulting fixture list would favor a different timing of doubles and blanks.

FP Harry was careful to emphasize that this is not an exact science. "Even if Manchester City win, you might still go with option one. And if Liverpool win, you might still go with option two," he noted. The FA Cup result provides a framework, but individual squad compositions and specific player holdings may push managers toward one strategy or the other. The key factor is that the FA Cup outcome will dictate what we can expect to see for predicted doubles and blanks in those later gameweeks, which is why making any major decisions before those results are known would be premature at best and potentially catastrophic at worst.

The timing element is particularly crucial because gameweek 32's fixtures will be announced before that deadline passes. However, the definitive doubles and blanks announcements will not come until the midweek between the FA Cup and gameweek 32—a three-week span from the current gameweek 31 deadline. This means managers face a considerable wait before they can make fully informed decisions about their chip usage.

## Why Newcastle Players Might Not Be the Obvious Choice

One of the more counterintuitive insights from this analysis challenges the prevailing wisdom that Newcastle players are automatic picks for the coming gameweeks. FP Harry identified Newcastle as a team many managers have been eyeing, with players like Anthony Gordon and Harvey Barnes generating significant interest. Yet he argued that Newcastle assets may not fit as cleanly into your squad as you might think, depending on your chip strategy.

"If we end up on a chip strategy of option two where we free hit 33, wildcard 35, Newcastle players are not actually very useful to you because of course they have a good fixture this week," FP Harry explained. The reasoning becomes clear when you trace through Newcastle's fixture schedule: they face Brighton at home in gameweek 31, then Crystal Palace away in gameweek 32, and if you are deploying a Free Hit in gameweek 33 to capitalize on their double fixtures, you would only benefit from them during that single chip. More significantly, Newcastle do not have a fixture in gameweek 34, which is currently blank for several teams. This means Newcastle players would sit dormant during that blank unless you specifically structured your squad to account for that absence.

The broader lesson here extends beyond just Newcastle. FP Harry advocated for maintaining maximum flexibility in your squad construction, particularly when you are still uncertain about which chip strategy path you will ultimately follow. "Trying to keep as much flexibility as possible" was his explicit advice, suggesting that managers should resist the urge to force in players who look attractive on paper but may not fit neatly into their eventual chip deployment plan.

This philosophy of flexibility also means being selective about when you use your precious weekly transfers. FP Harry urged managers to avoid using multiple transfers unless absolutely necessary this week and to focus on acquiring players who offer value across multiple gameweeks rather than those who are purely fixture-dependent.

## The Critical Importance of Waiting Until After the FA Cup

Perhaps the most emphatic advice in this entire analysis was FP Harry's repeated plea for managers to exercise patience with their Wildcard transfers. "Please please please please do not make any early Wildcard 32 transfers until after the FA Cup," he stated with unmistakable clarity.

The reasoning behind this directive is multifaceted. First, there is the obvious fixture uncertainty: with European competitions, cup results, and domestic scheduling all in flux, attempting to predict which teams will have double fixtures in which gameweeks before all the information is available is essentially gambling rather than strategic planning. Second, the value gains from moving early are minimal compared to the potential upside of waiting for complete information. FP Harry estimated that early movers might gain 0.2, 0.3, or perhaps 0.5 in player value—a relatively modest return when compared to the risk of making a transfer that becomes obsolete once the fixture list is confirmed.

The timeline reinforces why waiting is the prudent choice. After the gameweek 31 deadline, managers face an international break, followed by the FA Cup fixtures, and then gameweek 32 itself. The midweek between the FA Cup and gameweek 32 is when doubles and blanks will begin to be officially announced, providing managers with the concrete information they need to make informed decisions. Until that point, any Wildcard plans are based on speculation rather than confirmed facts.

"Don't try and pigeonhole yourself into one specific scenario," FP Harry added, reinforcing the importance of maintaining flexibility. Even with predictions available from resources like FPL.team and Planet FPL, which FP Harry praised as having "fantastic" combination data at this time of the season, the actual confirmed fixtures will differ from predictions until they are officially announced.

## Gameweek 31 Captaincy: The Case for Bruno and the Alternatives

For those still needing to make decisions within gameweek 31 itself, FP Harry provided his captaincy rankings, with Bruno Fernandes occupying the top spot despite some legitimate concerns about his away form. "About 75% of you are considering going for Bruno Fernandez on the Friday night as your captaincy pick," FP Harry noted, acknowledging that the Manchester United midfielder is by far the most popular choice this week. Bruno leads in several key metrics: he has the highest probability of a goal or assist, he is in the best form, and he ranks joint highest on projected point scorers according to the FPL Scout members area.

However, FP Harry acknowledged the significant caveat that Bruno's away form is notably worse than his home form. "His assist threat is definitely better and Bournemouth have been one of the best home defenses this season," he explained, suggesting that Bruno's ceiling may be lower than usual given the away fixture against a defensively sound opponent. Under Michael Carrick's management, Bruno has remained consistent, but the underlying numbers suggest some regression from his home performances.

For managers looking to pivot away from the crowd, Harry Wilson emerged as FP Harry's preferred differential option. The data supporting this choice is specific and compelling: Burnley have conceded a significant number of chances to right wingers throughout the season, and Wilson operates in that exact position. "Burnley have conceded a lot of chances to right wingers so far this season," FP Harry noted, "it's a bit of data which would really support going for Harry Wilson outside of that."

Other alternatives discussed included Anthony Gordon, who offers appeal as a penalty-taker playing up front in a derby fixture against Everton, Cole Palmer as an option despite Chelsea's recent poor performances, and Darwin Núñez, who FP Harry believes Liverpool "cannot play well without" as their main goal threat. An update on Mohamed Salah's status expected for Saturday morning could potentially impact the Núñez recommendation, but FP Harry maintained his view that Núñez remains "nailed" even with that uncertainty.

The general prediction for gameweek 31 suggests a relatively low-scoring round overall. "Actually every team in gameweek 31 is predicted to score less than two goals," FP Harry revealed, with Fulham leading the pack at home against Burnley with a predicted 1.83 goals, followed closely by Manchester United at 1.72 and Newcastle at 1.76. Chelsea and Liverpool are "around the same numbers," meaning there is little to separate the top attacking options this week. Clean sheets appear unlikely for most teams apart from potentially Fulham and Newcastle.
