FPL Challenge Best Picks + Team Reveal – Gameweek 35

Defensive Options Worth Considering

For FPL Challenge managers seeking clean sheet points in Gameweek 35, two teams stand out based on recent defensive displays.

Leeds United have kept three clean sheets in their previous six matches, which shows just how good they have been as of late defensively. Karl Darlow becomes a very good option for managers looking to invest in the Leeds defence this week. The key factor is that Leeds host a relegated Burnley side, who have shipped more goals than any other side in the league this campaign. This fixture combination makes Darlow an attractive budget-friendly option.

Arsenal have been the best defence in the league this season statistically, so relying on them for clean sheets makes sense. Their consistent defensive record throughout the campaign gives managers a reliable template option for their defensive line.

Attacking Options Against Weak Defences

Given that Burnley have shipped more goals than any other side in the league this campaign, bringing in a Leeds attacker this week could be a fantastic move. Leading the attack and taking penalties is Dominic Calvert-Lewin, who could reward Challengers after some great data as of late. The England striker is available at £5.8m and offers both goal and penalty-taking upside.

Teammate Antoine Semenyo has scored more goals than any other midfielder this season, so trusting him one last time against Everton could pay off significantly. The attacker is priced at £8.1m and has demonstrated excellent form throughout the campaign.

Manchester City Assets

Manchester City have been brilliant as of late – winning their previous six matches in all competitions. This form makes their assets particularly appealing for Gameweek 35.

The Norwegian has found the net more than any other player this season, so backing against him could be extremely risky. Erling Haaland remains priced at £14.6m and represents the premium option for managers seeking guaranteed attacking returns.

Defensive attacking Options

Neco O'Reilly, who has scored more goals than any other defender this campaign, offers a unique combination of defensive solidity and attacking threat. For managers seeking differential picks, the defender provides unexpected goal-scoring potential from the backline.

Midfield Priority

Bruno Fernandes is chasing an assist record, it doesn't rule him out from scoring. The Portuguese midfielder remains a dual threat for managers weighing up midfield options, offering both creative and goal-scoring contributions.

Suggested Team Structure

  • Goalkeeper: Karl Darlow (Leeds) – strong fixture against relegated Burnley
  • Defenders: Arsenal coverage + Neco O'Reilly for attacking upside
