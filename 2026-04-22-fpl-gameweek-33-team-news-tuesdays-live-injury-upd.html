FPL Gameweek 33 team news: Tuesday’s live injury updates

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

Pep Guardiola has confirmed Rodri will miss Manchester City's trip to Burnley on Wednesday, but the injury sounds manageable rather than serious — a mixed blessing for FPL managers holding the Spaniard through this crucial double gameweek.

"For tomorrow, he will not be ready," Guardiola said on Tuesday. "Then we'll see the next games, the semi-final against Southampton or the one in 12-13 days against Everton."

The City manager offered no timeline for Rodri's return beyond that assessment, and the situation warrants monitoring rather than panic. The midfielder was absent from Sunday's defeat to Nottingham Forest with a groin issue, and while Guardiola's language suggested mild rather than serious, the timing is awkward. City face Southampton in an FA Cup semi-final at the weekend, meaning rotation risk even once Rodri is back available.

Ruben Dias remains sidelined too. The centre-back, who has been out with a hamstring problem, did not travel to Forest and Guardiola offered a blunt verdict when asked about his availability: "Not yet." That leaves Nathan Aké and Manuel Akanji as City's primary central defensive pairing, with Manuel Neuer still filling in between the posts. There has been no update on long-term absentee Josko Gvardiol, who continues his recovery from a leg injury.

Guardiola did flag the compressed schedule as a significant factor for the Burnley match. City now play again just three days after Sunday's physically and emotionally demanding defeat.

"You have to see how people recover," he said. "Not just the demanding game that we had emotionally and physically."

For FPL purposes, City's defensive options — particularly Aké — remain relevant given the double gameweek, but the rotation risk in midfield is real.

---

Leeds United's Daniel Farke arrived at his press conference in a more relaxed position than some of his counterparts. He reported no fresh injury concerns ahead of the trip to Bournemouth, with the squad broadly unchanged from the one that beat Wolves.

The major absences remain: Anton Stach continues his recovery from an ankle problem and is not yet back in team training. Farke suggested a return in May is the realistic expectation rather than this week.

"Difficult to say [if he'll be back for the FA Cup semi-final]," Farke said. "Normally, with this injury, it would be just realistic to be back available in May. But right now, we're heading into the end of April, and each and every day that we get him back earlier is good. I think it's more or less right now a question of days and not weeks."

Daniel James is back in team training, which Farke described as positive, but the winger needs more session time before returning to match action.

"He needs, right now, of course, session after session. Anton is not back in team training so he won't travel with us to Bournemouth."

The more encouraging news for Leeds is Joe Rodon's recovery. The centre-back was originally expected to miss the Wolves match due to an ankle problem but returned ahead of schedule and made the bench.

"Joe Rodon was already involved just before the build-up, more or less, to the last game," Farke said. He is expected to travel to Bournemouth.

For FPL managers, Leeds look relatively clean at the back heading into their double gameweek, with Rodon's availability a boost to the side that kept a clean sheet against Wolves.

---

At Bournemouth, Andoni Iraola must still operate without two key attacking options. Tavernier Cook and Justin Kluivert remain unavailable, leaving the Cherries short in the final third. Iraola hinted at rotation given the fixture load, which FPL managers should factor into line-up expectations for Wednesday.

---

Burnley's squad picture is similarly constrained. Hannibal and Roberts remain sidelined with no other fresh concerns reported. Scott Parker's side face a difficult assignment against a City team who, even with rotation, represent a significant step up in opposition quality.

---

Key takeaways for FPL managers:

  • City have a defensive double but face rotation risk in midfield; Rodri's absence limits attacking returns from the engine room.
  • Leeds are in better shape than expected — Rodon's return is a boost and James is edging closer, though neither is certain for the Bournemouth game.
  • Bournemouth and Burnley remain depleted, making their double gameweek less attractive than it initially appeared.
  • Four teams are doubling this gameweek, but the injury and availability picture is uneven across the pack.
