# Tottenham's Season Spirals as Captain Cristian Romero Rules Out for Rest of Campaign

The news broke on Monday: Cristian Romero's season is over. Tottenham's £5.0m defender, their captain, their emotional core, will play no further part in a campaign that has careened from Champions League contention to relegation reckoning. The knee injury sustained in Sunday's 1-0 defeat at Sunderland—colliding with goalkeeper Antonin Kinsky—has ended what was already a miserable year for the Argentine.

"We don't know yet, we have to see in the next game, in the next days," manager Roberto De Zerbi said after the match, sounding genuinely uncertain. "I hope for us it's not too important problem, because he's a crucial player for us, he's a good guy, good player, top player, big personality and we need him to finish the season and to achieve our goal."

But that goal—survival—looks increasingly fragile.

De Zerbi's much-anticipated debut as Tottenham head coach brought little comfort. The Italian arrived with a reputation for progressive football, tactical imagination, a squad he supposedly wanted. His first match was a limp loss to a Sunderland side fighting its own bottom-half battles. Tottenham managed just 0.72 expected goals. Robin Roeves, Sunderland's goalkeeper, made seven saves—his most in a Premier League match all season. The visitors were second-best throughout.

Spurs did attempt something different. Randal Kolo Muani (£6.9m) and Richarlison (£6.3m) flanked Dominic Solanke (£7.2m) up front, with Lucas Bergvall (£5.2m) operating as the Number 10. Both full-backs—Pedro Porro (£5.1m) and Destiny Udogie (£4.3m)—inverted into midfield, a De Zerbi hallmark. Porro finished with three shots, three chances created, and took all of Tottenham's corners. Richarlison found some decent positions but couldn't convert.

But formation tweaks can't disguise a deeper malaise. Tottenham are winless in five. They've scored two goals in their last six matches. De Zerbi has neither time nor momentum on his side, and now he's lost his captain and most reliable defender.

"I don't want to say nothing, because I don't know," De Zerbi admitted when asked specifically about the knee. The uncertainty itself is telling.

### Mukiele Delivers for Budget Managers

For FPL managers hunting value, Nordi Mukiele (£4.5m) offered exactly what budget defenders are supposed to provide.

The Frenchman scored the only goal of the game—a deflected 20-yard effort that looped past Roeves—while playing advanced positions down the right flank. He finished with four shots total and defended well enough to help Sunderland keep their sixth home clean sheet of the season, placing them joint-third among Premier League clubs for home shutouts.

Omar Alderete (£4.1m) should also bank DefCon points following an overnight clearance awarded by Opta.

Mukiele's ceiling is modest, but at £4.5m, he's functional. Sunderland's home record makes him a viable punting option for managers trying to free funds for premium attacking assets.

---

### What This Means Going Forward

For Romero owners, the decision is made for you. Find cover elsewhere. The knee issue sounds structural, and with Tottenham staring down 12th place with five matches to go, there's simply nothing left to play for in terms of FPL relevance.

For Tottenham assets more broadly, caution is warranted. De Zerbi is clearly building something, but the transition will be painful. Porro's set-piece involvement at least guarantees some floor, but Solanke, Richarlison, and the rest are relying on a team creating less than one expected goal against a mid-table defense.

Sunderland, meanwhile, look sturdy at home. Their fixtures through the run-in offer clean sheet upside for those willing to speculate on budget defenders.

The title race, European qualification, relegation battles—all of it runs through the next few weeks. But for Tottenham and their captain, the season is already over. The only question left is whether De Zerbi can arrest the freefall before it becomes something worse.
