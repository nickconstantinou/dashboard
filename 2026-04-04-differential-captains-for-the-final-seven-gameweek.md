# Differential Captain Picks for the Final Seven Gameweeks: An FPL Strategist's Guide to Gaining Ground

## Three Under-the-Radar Options That Could Catapult Your Rank

As the 2025/26 Fantasy Premier League season enters its decisive phase, five-time top 1k finisher Tom Freeman has identified three differential captain options that could prove decisive for managers looking to make late-season moves. With the majority of the FPL community likely gravitating toward obvious choices like Salah or Son, picking up crucial points with less-owned assets could be the difference between a memorable finish and another year of "what if."

### Jarrod Bowen: The West Ham Home Favorite

For Gameweek 32, Freeman highlights West Ham's Jarrod Bowen (£7.6m) as a sound differential option when the Hammers host Wolverhampton Wanderers at home. Despite his strong underlying numbers, Bowen remains owned by just 9.1% of squads in the top 100k—a remarkably low figure given his recent output.

Bowen has produced four assists in the last six Gameweeks while creating 15 chances in that period, the most of any player except Bruno Fernandes (£10.3m). His appeal extends beyond direct goal involvement; Bowen offers multiple routes to points including penalty duties and defensive contributions tracked by FPL's DefCon metric. The matchup against Wolves is particularly attractive. While Wolves' performances have improved under Rob Edwards, their underlying numbers suggest some fortune: they've conceded just six goals from 12.3 expected goals conceded (xGC) over the past six matches—on par with Burnley in terms of chances allowed. More significantly, Wolves have conceded the most headed attempts in the league, music to the ears of set-piece taker Bowen. With this fixture representing a crucial relegation six-pointer at the table's bottom, Freeman sees Bowen as a captain option who combines form, fixtures, and opportunity.

### Matheus Cunha: Carrick's Creative Weapon for Blank Gameweek 34

For managers navigating Blank Gameweek 34, Freeman turns attention to Manchester United's Matheus Cunha (£8.0m). Since Michael Carrick's appointment, Cunha has been directly involved in six goals across 10 matches, accumulating 51 points from just eight starts and two substitute appearances—the second-most of any United player in that period.

Under Carrick's 4-2-3-1 system, Cunha operates as a left winger but possesses the tactical flexibility to move both inside and outside, causing mismatches for opposition defenders. His standout performance came in Gameweek 31, where his movement created constant problems for Alex Jimenez (£4.5m), who was eventually substituted on 72 minutes. The Blank Gameweek 34 fixture against Brentford presents a specific opportunity: Brentford have struggled defensively in the half-spaces over the last six Gameweeks, an area where Cunha thrives. Freeman acknowledges that captaining away from Bruno Fernandes (£10.3m) in his current form carries inherent risk, but argues that this particular matchup could play directly into Cunha's hands. For managers needing to differentiate their captaincy choice to gain ground on rivals, Cunha represents a calculated gamble with strong statistical backing.

### Anton Stach: The Budget Maverick for Gameweek 35

For managers willing to take a genuine leap of faith, Anton Stach (£4.8m) emerges as an option for Gameweek 35 when his side faces Burnley at home. The German midfielder has delivered a goal, assist, or DefCon points in 12 of his last 17 matches, averaging an impressive 5.1 points per start in that time.

Stach's set-piece involvement is particularly noteworthy: he ranks third among all players for key passes in 2025/26, with 29 of those key passes originating from corners and free-kicks—a league-high figure. He's also 17th for shots among players in his position. Freeman positions Stach as a consistent performer whose underlying numbers suggest continued output rather than a lucky hot streak. Burnley, coached by Scott Parker, provide a favorable opposition: they've conceded 24 goals since the turn of the year, the most of any club except Tottenham Hotspur, and sit bottom of the league for set-piece expected goals conceded across the full season. At just £4.8m with premium set-piece involvement and a fixture against the league's worst defensive side on set plays, Stach offers explosive captaincy potential at minimal ownership.

### The Strategic Context

Fantasy Football Scout's analysis comes at a critical juncture. The concept of "differential" picks—players owned by a small percentage of the game population—becomes crucial when trying to outpace rivals who may have similar core assets. A successful differential captain choice can yield double-digit percentage gains in rank, while a missed template pick compounds disadvantages.

The timing of the article on April 3, 2026, places it perfectly for managers reviewing their strategies during the quiet period before Gameweek 32's action resumes. Freeman's approach emphasizes not just form and fixtures, but the specific tactical systems and defensive vulnerabilities that can be exploited. His analysis consistently grounds recommendations in underlying data—expected goals, chances created, set-piece xGC—rather than simple goals-and-assists returns.

### What Lies Ahead

Managers should monitor several factors in the coming Gameweeks. Bowen's penalty involvement depends on West Ham's attacking shape and whether he's awarded spot kicks in high-stakes situations. Cunha's captaincy viability hinges on whether Carrick continues utilizing him as a floating winger or shifts to a more central role. Stach's appeal depends on Luton's continued inclusion in set-piece routines and whether their home fixtures remain as favorable as Freeman projects.

For FPL managers serious about climbing ranks in the season's final stretch, Freeman's three options provide a spectrum of risk and reward: Bowen offers the safest differential route with proven Premier League output, Cunha provides a mid-tier gamble against a specific defensive weakness, and Stach represents the "full-blown maverick" play that could deliver seismic rank movements for those willing to back their analysis over consensus ownership.
