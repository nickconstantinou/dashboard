Morgan Gibbs-White – Nottingham Forest | £7.6m

Morgan Gibbs-White has emerged as one of the most prolific midfielders in the Premier League since the turn of the year. The Nottingham Forest midfielder has scored 10 Premier League goals in 2026, placing him level with Igor Thiago and tied for the most goals in that period. His 63 points since the start of March represent the highest total of any FPL player over that timeframe, demonstrating his exceptional consistency and ability to deliver returns week after week.

Beyond his goal-scoring exploits, Gibbs-White has been one of the busiest players in front of goal in recent matches. Across seven matches since March, he recorded 18 shots, highlighting his volume of attempts on target and his willingness to take chances. Fantasy managers considering his price point of £7.6m will note that his current form positions him among the most rewarding assets in the game.

However, a significant fixture concern exists heading into Gameweek 35. Nottingham Forest have Europa League semi-finals scheduled around this period, with a home leg against Aston Villa on Thursday 30 April and an away leg on Thursday 7 May. These knockout commitments could result in rotation for league matches, potentially limiting Gibbs-White's minutes in the critical run-in.

Rayan Cherki – Manchester City | £6.4m

Rayan Cherki represents a budget-friendly alternative at £6.4m and has demonstrated promising form in recent weeks. The Manchester City midfielder has produced four attacking returns in four starts, suggesting he is settling into the team and building chemistry with his teammates. His underlying metrics at Burnley were particularly eye-catching, as he managed eight shots and eight chances created in that match alone—a tally that makes his lack of a return in that fixture somewhat puzzling.

Cherki's non-penalty expected goal involvement (NPxGI) across his last four matches stands at 2.56, the second-highest of any midfielder in the division. This underlying creativity indicates he is actively involved in his side's attacking moves and positioning himself in dangerous areas. When Cherki receives consistent minutes, his data suggests he delivers value, with an average of 6.2 points per start across the season.

The main consideration for managers is Cherki's price and consistency. At £6.4m, he offers significant savings compared to premium midfielders, making him an attractive enabler for constructing more expensive squads elsewhere.

Jarrod Bowen – West Ham United | £7.8m

Jarrod Bowen has been a reliable source of assists for West Ham United throughout a sustained spell of strong form. The Hammers winger has recorded nine assists in nine matches, a period during which he has averaged 6.0 points per start. This consistency in providing goal contributions makes him a valuable asset at £7.8m.

West Ham United's overall form supports the case for Bowen as a viable FPL option. The club have recorded three wins, two draws, and only one defeat in their last six league matches. This improvement in results provides a solid foundation for continued assist opportunities, as West Ham appear more likely to create and convert chances in the final stretch of the season.
