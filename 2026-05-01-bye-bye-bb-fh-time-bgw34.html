Chip Strategy Update

The Free Hit chip takes centre stage in gameweek 34, with both podcast hosts confirmed as deploying this valuable asset. This represents a significant tactical pivot for managers seeking to maximise their squads for the upcoming fixtures.

Meanwhile, the Bench Boost chip has already been activated, with managers having deployed this option in gameweek 33. This deployment came with considerable risk, as the host noted that managers stand to lose up to 14 players as a consequence of this aggressive strategy.

Player Availability Concern

One notable absentee from gameweek 33 was J Pedro, who registered as a complete no-show for his managers. This absence added to the difficulties faced by those managers who had committed their Bench Boost chip in that gameweek, compounding the losses incurred by the community of free hit and bench boost strategists alike.

Fixture Preview

The main fixture action for gameweek 34 is scheduled for Wednesday the 22nd of April. This date marks the completion of the second halves of the remaining double gameweek fixtures, presenting managers with their final opportunity to capitalise on concentrated fixture schedules.

In terms of entertainment value, the Bournemouth versus Leeds fixture failed to deliver excitement, with the match described as lacking intensity. There was considerable emphasis on clean sheets throughout this particular encounter, suggesting a conservative approach from both sides with defensive outcomes taking priority.

League Standing Observations

Southampton maintained their positive momentum by extending their unbeaten run in recent fixtures. This sustained form continues to position them as a club performing with confidence and resilience in their campaign.

On a personal note, the host celebrated achieving their first top 1 million rank of the season in gameweek 33, representing a meaningful milestone in their Fantasy Premier League pursuit this campaign.

However, automatic promotion hopes experienced a setback following a draw in the most recent match. This result has diminished the likelihood of achieving direct promotion, leaving the host and presumably other managers in similar positions with reduced margin for error in their remaining fixtures.
