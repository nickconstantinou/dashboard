Google's New AI Tools Are Actually Insane (We Tried Them All)

Google recently unveiled some remarkable new AI software that's pushing the boundaries of what's possible with artificial intelligence. The hosts of the Limitless podcast dive into these new tools, starting with LIIA 3 (likely referring to Lyria, Google's AI music generation model). They demonstrate its capabilities by prompting it to create a 2010s Kanye-style stadium anthem about their own podcast, generating lyrics, full production, and a complete 30-second song in real time while discussing the product.

The results are genuinely impressive. The generated song features catchy lyrics like "Josh and EJ on the mic, a brand new vision, 25 minutes of that next level AI talk" with solid production quality that sounds like a proper rap track. The hosts emphasize that this isn't just a gimmick‚Äîit could genuinely replace music licensing for short-form content creators on platforms like TikTok. The ability to quickly generate custom theme songs, jingles, or background music tailored to specific content represents a significant leap forward for creators.

Perhaps even more fascinating is LIIA 3's ability to generate songs from photos. The hosts test this by using a photo of snowy New York during a blizzard and prompting the AI to create a song reflecting that mood. In under 30 seconds, it produced a mellow, atmospheric track about "walking through a hazy dream" that perfectly captured the peaceful, lonely vibe of a snowy scene. This showcases Google's strength in multimodality‚Äîtheir AI doesn't just understand text, but can interpret visual content and generate appropriate audio responses.

The hosts highlight that this capability stems from Google's extensive experience with multimodal AI, including their live translation technology that handles 40+ languages. Unlike standard text-based language models, Google's world models have a deeper intuition about what they're "seeing." They also mention Synth ID, a digital watermarking service built into these AI-generated files to help identify AI-created content‚Äîa nod to the ethical considerations surrounding these powerful new tools.
