# Rice joins Arsenal's growing injury list as FPL managers face mounting fixture nightmare

Arsenal's injury crisis has deepened just as the club faces its most demanding week of the season. Manager Mikel Arteta confirmed on Tuesday that Declan Rice missed the morning's open training session, leaving his availability for Wednesday's Champions League quarter-final second leg against Sporting uncertain—and raising fresh alarm for Fantasy Premier League managers already navigating a depleted squad.

"We have to wait till tomorrow morning to see how a few of the boys are and make the right decision with them," Arteta said when pressed on Rice's absence.

When asked to clarify the nature of Rice's problem, the manager was reluctant to expand. "I don't want to do that. We have to wait and see how he is tomorrow. Obviously, he wasn't available to train."

Rice's potential involvement appears linked to his workload rather than a fresh structural injury. Arteta hinted at fatigue as a contributing factor, noting the midfielder has "played a lot of games," before adding that Rice "is going to try to do everything he possibly can to be with us tomorrow."

The timing could hardly be worse. Wednesday's meeting with Sporting at the Emirates comes just four days before Arsenal travel to the Etihad to face Manchester City in what many see as a pivotal Premier League title showdown. With both legs of the Sporting tie falling inside a congested run of league fixtures, Arteta's rotation options are already severely constrained.

## Saka's Achilles issue confirmed—but progress being made

While Rice's situation represents the newest concern, Arsenal's existing casualty list remains extensive. The club confirmed on Tuesday what many had suspected: Bukayo Saka is dealing with an Achilles problem that has kept him sidelined since Gameweek 32.

"It was something that he was carrying for a while," Arteta explained. "No [setback], there is progression. Hopefully, it is going to be a matter of days and not weeks, but he has to see, when he is loading more, how he responds to that kind of progression."

Arteta stopped short of guaranteeing either Saka or Jurrien Timber would be ready for Wednesday, offering only: "Maybe one of them, let's see."

Timber, sidelined with an ankle issue, remains in the same uncertain category. Also absent from Tuesday's session were Riccardo Calafiori and Martin Odegaard, both with unspecified problems, and long-term foot injury victim Mikel Merino.

On Calafiori and Odegaard, Arteta offered no meaningful update. FPL managers hunting for clean sheet assets or attacking returns from Arsenal's backline and midfield will find little to work with heading into the crucial double Gameweek.

The bottom line: Arsenal's walking a tightrope between fitness and fatigue across their most important stretch of the season. Wednesday's line-up remains a mystery, and the ripple effects for Gameweek 33's double fixture could be significant. For anyone managing Arsenal assets—or trying to navigate a blank—orbit around the club's next move—patience may be the only strategy available.
