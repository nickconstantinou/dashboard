# Double Gameweek 33 Set to Impact Up to Eight Premier League Teams as FA Cup Chaos Looms for FPL Managers

## Premier League confirms rescheduling plans that could reshape the Fantasy Premier League landscape for thousands of managers

The Premier League has confirmed that the upcoming FA Cup quarter-final fixtures will create a massive Double Gameweek 33 scenario, with up to eight teams potentially playing twice in a single gameweek while their opponents blank. The announcement, made on Thursday afternoon, provides FPL managers with critical information they need to plan their crucial season run-in—though the exact makeup of the double gameweeks won't be known until after this weekend's FA Cup quarter-final matches are completed.

Under the confirmed arrangements, any team that progresses to the FA Cup semi-finals will have their Premier League fixture moved from Gameweek 34 into Gameweek 33, creating a double fixture for both the advancing team and their original Gameweek 34 opponent. This scheduling quirk stems from the FA Cup semi-finals being scheduled for the same weekend as the blank Gameweek 34, forcing rescheduling to accommodate the cup competition.

The timing of Thursday's announcement has been described as a significant relief for FPL managers, as the Gameweek 32 deadline falls on Friday, April 10, 2026—meaning players will know exactly which teams are doubling and blanking before finalizing their lineup decisions. This advance notice allows for strategic chip deployment that would otherwise be impossible with typical short-notice scheduling changes.

For FPL purposes, this creates a predictable but complex planning challenge. What makes this year particularly significant is the scale of potential doubles—up to eight teams representing a substantial portion of the league's playing pool.
