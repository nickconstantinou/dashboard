The Semenyo to Cherki Debate

One of the most discussed switches in Gameweek 35 involves Antoine Semenyo and Rayan Cherki at Manchester City.

Current Tactical Situation

Semenyo, priced at £8.2m, has experienced a positional shift in recent matches. Prior to cup games, Semenyo was playing almost alongside Erling Haaland (£14.5m). However, since the cup fixtures, Semenyo has been shifted to a wide role. Meanwhile, Cherki (£6.5m) is operating closer to the Norwegian international as a no.10, positioning himself in a more central attacking position relative to Haaland.

Statistical Comparison

Over the last six matches, the statistical comparison between these two Manchester City assets reveals notable differences:

  • Expected Goals (xG): Cherki nearly matches Semenyo with 1.82 compared to Semenyo's 1.50
  • Expected Assists (xA): Cherki is significantly ahead with 2.22 compared to Semenyo's 0.38

    • This creativity gap is particularly relevant for FPL purposes, as Cherki's superior playmaking ability from his central no.10 position may translate to more assist opportunities, while Semenyo's wide role limits his chances of being the primary creative force.

    Emerging Options: Jack Hinshelwood

    Jack Hinshelwood, priced at £5.1m, has emerged as an intriguing budget option. At Brighton, Hinshelwood ranks second for Expected Goal Involvement (xGI) over the last six Gameweeks. Impressively, he ranks ahead of established assets like Bruno Fernandes and Rayan Cherki during this period. Given Brighton's strong recent form—making them realistic contenders for a European spot—this price point presents value for managers seeking affordable coverage.

    Crystal Palace Rotation Warning

    For managers holding Crystal Palace attackers, significant caution is warranted. If Palace reach the Europa League final, their attacking assets should be completely ignored. Rotation is likely to occur in Gameweeks 36, 37, and 38 as the club balances league and European commitments. Managers should plan accordingly and potentially look elsewhere for attacking coverage during the run-in.

    Conclusion

    The most significant tactical development for Gameweek 35 involves Cherki's central positioning at Manchester City, which may offer superior creativity compared to Semenyo's wide role. Combined with his lower price point (£6.5m vs £8.2m) and significantly higher xA output, Cherki presents a compelling case for managers seeking Manchester City coverage. Meanwhile, Hinshelwood at Brighton offers unexpected value given Brighton's European push and his strong xGI output over recent Gameweeks.
