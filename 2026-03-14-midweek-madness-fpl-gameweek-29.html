Midweek Madness: FPL Gameweek 28 Recap and Gameweek 29 Preview

*This comprehensive breakdown captures everything Fantasy Premier League managers need to know from this episode, including player performances, strategic decisions, and transfer planning ahead of a quick Tuesday turnaround.*

---

Introduction: A Chaotic Gameweek Sets the Stage for Midweek Action

The latest episode of this Fantasy Premier League podcast finds the hosts recording on Sunday, March 1st, reflecting on what they describe as the most entertaining gameweek of the season so far. Gameweek 28 delivered an extraordinary 36 goals across the matches, with 35 different goal scorers—statistical evidence of just how chaotic and unpredictable the weekend proved to be.

The hosts open by acknowledging their own contrasting fortunes. One host (Tom) managed a solid 78 points, climbing to just outside the top 100,000 ranks, while the other (Sam) experienced a particularly frustrating weekend that saw him drop outside the top 500,000 before a late rally saved some dignity. The discussion sets up a broader theme that runs throughout the episode: the frustration of FPL managers who feel they're constantly missing out on the big hauls while watching others profit from popular players who deliver exactly when needed.

With Gameweek 29's deadline arriving quickly on Tuesday evening at 6 PM, the hosts work through their own gameweek performances, analyze the injury news and absentees that dominated headlines, and begin formulating strategies for the midweek fixtures. The quick turnaround adds pressure to transfer decisions, making this episode particularly valuable for managers seeking guidance on how to react to the latest developments.

Gameweek 28 Recap: Captaincy Decisions and Missed Opportunities

The Haaland Absence and Captaincy Chaos

The biggest talking point from Gameweek 28 centered on Erling Haaland's unexpected absence from Manchester City's match. The Norwegian striker, owned by a massive percentage of FPL managers, failed to play, creating a captains' nightmare scenario that had ripple effects throughout the gameweek.

One host (Tom) reveals he spent considerable time deliberating between Bruno Fernandes and Haaland for the captain's armband, ultimately choosing Haaland based on his expectation that the striker would score more goals. When Haaland failed to play, Tom's vice-captain strategy—having Bruno Fernandes as the alternative—paid off spectacularly. Fernandes scored, providing a crucial lifeline that saved Tom's gameweek from disaster. This leads the hosts to discuss the importance of always considering vice-captaincy options, recalling previous seasons where similar situations (like Sergio Aguero's brace when a popular captain blanked) have decided championships.

The discussion highlights a key FPL philosophy: while you can't predict every absence, having a solid vice-captain plan provides essential insurance against exactly this type of scenario. Tom notes that after learning a similar lesson "many moons ago" when he forgot to set a vice-captain and watched a popular pick blank while his forgotten alternative hauled, he's now meticulous about considering backup options.

Individual Player Performances and Transfers

Tom details his gameweek 28 performance, explaining that despite a "horrific" Saturday when he sat on just 14 points by evening, the vice-captain windfall rescued his score to finish at 78 points. He specifically mentions several players who contributed:

Bukayo Saka delivered as expected, scoring an early goal to provide a "floor-raising" performance that week. The Arsenal winger continues his impressive consistency, though Tom jokes that his transfer in of James Hill "broke" the defender's previous run of six consecutive returns—a humorous admission that bringing in a player sometimes disrupts their momentum.

Eberechi Eze proved valuable with a 15-point haul, which Tom admits was particularly painful for Sam since he didn't own the Crystal Palace player. The discussion reveals how ownership concentration in popular players can either make or break a gameweek, as Tom notes Eze would likely be "owned by everybody" by midweek given his performance.

Trent Alexander-Arnold contributed with an assist and two goals, though the Liverpool defender was unfortunate not to earn bonus points due to the crowded goal-scoring returns in Liverpool's match. Gabriel Magalhaes added a nine-pointer, though Tom dismissively notes this was "basically wallpaper"—indicating how expectations change based on ownership and price points.

The hosts also discuss how unexpected absences create opportunities. With both Haaland and Joe Anderson missing, one host had Cole Palmer on the bench but received only a single point from the substitute appearance—a frustration that encapsulates the randomness of FPL in any given week.

Sam's Struggles: A Season of Missed Opportunities

The Frustrating Reality of Mid-Table Misery

Sam's gameweek 28 experience provides a cautionary tale that many FPL managers will find relatable. His weekend looked "really quite shambolic" at 2:45 PM when he had dropped outside the top 500,000, with Harry Wilson having already bagged two returns that Sam had missed by not owning the Fulham player.

What makes Sam's situation particularly frustrating is the broader pattern he's experiencing this season. He describes feeling like he's "missed every big call from every single player this season somehow," noting that whenever there's a popular player he doesn't own, that player inevitably delivers a 15-point haul. This psychological pattern—the grass always being greener on the other side of ownership—is one that plagues many FPL managers, particularly those hovering in the 250,000 to 350,000 rank range where Sam finds himself stuck.

His transfer decisions exemplified this bad luck. He brought in Virgil van Dijk for the injured Munoz, though he admits he was "50/50" on whether to sell Timber for the Liverpool defender instead. He also transferred in Bournemouth for Enzo Fernandez—a move that yielded nothing when Bournemouth failed to deliver against Crystal Palace. The non-transfer of Harry Wilson proved particularly costly given Wilson's two-goal return, though Sam notes with resignation that Wilson also picked up an injury, meaning fate may have intervened regardless.

The Psychological Toll of FPL

Perhaps the most relatable portion of the episode comes when Sam openly discusses his frustration with the season overall. After 28 gameweeks, he still hasn't had a "big week"—defined as reaching the top 1 million ranks—and describes the experience as "a grind" where every week that passes feels like another missed opportunity.

This honesty provides important perspective for listeners. Even experienced FPL managers who podcast about the game can experience the same frustrations as casual players. Sam notes he ended up with a green arrow (meaning he moved up in rankings) despite the frustrating weekend, but admits he's feeling "quite iffy about the rest of the season" and isn't confident things will improve. This emotional honesty adds depth to what could otherwise be a purely tactical discussion.

The hosts laugh about Tom's "world's smallest violin" comment regarding Sam's 100k-to-150k struggles, with Tom admitting that bouncing around those ranks would be preferable to Sam's situation. The camaraderie between the hosts makes this discussion more than just numbers—it captures the emotional rollercoaster that makes FPL both compelling and maddening.

Key Absentees from Gameweek 28

Joe Anderson from Liverpool also missed his match, leaving one host relying on the bench where he had Alder rather than his first-choice goalkeeper. This resulted in just four points from the goalkeeper position instead of the potential 15+ that Anderson might have delivered.

James Ward-Prowse apparently picked up an issue that the hosts will monitor ahead of Gameweek 29, adding to the uncertainty around team selections.

Harry Wilson's injury compounds Sam's frustration, as the Fulham player who delivered two returns now faces potential time out, complicating transfer strategies for those considering bringing him in.

The Timber Situation

One host mentions he's still holding onto Oleksandr Timber despite the defender's injury earlier in the season, suggesting he plans to hold the player until closer to Gameweek 31 rather than immediately transferring him out. This highlights a common FPL dilemma: when a player gets injured, do you immediately cut losses or hold hoping for a recovery and subsequent returns? The host's strategy suggests patience, particularly for premium assets where transfer value has already been significantly reduced.

The Tuesday Deadline Pressure

The hosts acknowledge they're aiming for a shorter podcast than usual to accommodate this compressed timeline.

Captaincy Considerations

While the hosts don't fully settle on captaincy picks for Gameweek 29, they provide framework for thinking through the decision. The discussion emphasizes looking at fixtures rather than just form, identifying teams with favorable matchups that could deliver clean sheets or scoring opportunities. The episode sets up the importance of checking team news closer to the deadline, particularly given the quick turnaround leaves less time for injury updates to emerge.

Key Takeaways for FPL Managers

1. Vice-Captain Strategy Is Non-Negotiable

The Haaland absence scenario demonstrates why always having a viable vice-captain option matters. Tom's weekend was saved not by his primary captain but by his backup. The hosts recommend thinking through your vice-captain choice as carefully as your main captain, particularly when popular players face potential rotation or injury risks.

2. Ownership Concentration Creates Opportunity and Risk

The discussion around popular players delivering when you don't own them (and blanking when you do) highlights FPL's psychological challenges. Sam's experience shows how ownership in "must-have" players can backfire when everyone else also owns them and they fail to deliver. Consider your ownership relative to the broader game—not every popular player needs to be in your team.

3. Midweek Fixtures Require Accelerated Decision-Making

The Tuesday deadline for Gameweek 29 emphasizes the importance of planning ahead rather than waiting until deadline day. The hosts recommend having transfer plans in place by Sunday evening, allowing Monday to be spent on injury updates and final adjustments rather than scrambling decisions.

4. Form Versus Fixtures: Finding the Balance

The episode doesn't provide specific recommendations but establishes the framework for evaluating Gameweek 29 options. Players in good form facing favorable fixtures represent the ideal combination, though premium options with tough fixtures may still warrant ownership based on their ceiling potential.

5. Emotional Management Matters

Both hosts demonstrate the emotional ups and downs of FPL, with Sam particularly open about his frustration. The takeaway isn't that emotions should be suppressed, but rather that managers should recognize when frustration might be clouding judgment. Taking a break from transfers when frustrated, as Sam suggests by saying he's "feeling quite iffy" about his season, can prevent rash decisions.

Who This Episode Is For

This FPL podcast episode delivers the most value to mid-tier FPL managers—those currently ranked between approximately 50,000 and 500,000—who are looking to break through to higher ranks but find themselves stuck in a cycle of average performances. The discussion between two experienced managers who openly share their struggles provides both tactical insight and emotional validation.

Casual FPL players will benefit from understanding the strategic frameworks the hosts use when approaching captaincy decisions and transfer planning. The vice-captain discussion alone provides immediate practical value for anyone who hasn't developed this habit.

Experienced managers looking for Gameweek 29 specific advice will find the episode useful for understanding how other managers are thinking about the quick turnaround, though the broader philosophical discussions about ownership and emotional management may feel less novel.

Most importantly, this episode captures something essential about FPL that pure statistical analysis cannot: the psychological dimension of managing a fantasy team across an entire season. Sam's candid admission that he hasn't had a "big week" in 28 attempts, combined with his determination to keep grinding, perfectly illustrates why FPL remains compelling despite—or perhaps because of—its frustrations.

*For managers heading into Gameweek 29 with a Tuesday deadline, the core message is clear: have your transfer plans ready early, always maintain a vice-captain strategy, and don't let the inevitable frustrations of a chaotic season diminish your long-term approach. The big week could be just around the corner.*
