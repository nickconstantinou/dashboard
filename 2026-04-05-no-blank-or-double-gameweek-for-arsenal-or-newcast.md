# Arsenal FA Cup Exit Reshapes FPL Landscape: What Double and Blank Gameweek Changes Mean for Managers

**The Full Story**

Arsenal's shock FA Cup elimination at the hands of Championship side Southampton on Saturday night has fundamentally altered the Fantasy Premier League landscape for the decisive run-in. Mikel Arteta's side suffered a 2-1 defeat at St Mary's Stadium, confirming that neither Arsenal nor Newcastle United will feature in Double Gameweek 33 or Blank Gameweek 34 this season. The result immediately reduced the number of teams doubling in Gameweek 33 from eight to six, while trimming the expected blanks in Gameweek 34 from four to three.

The final Gameweek 34 blank will be confirmed on Sunday following the conclusion of the West Ham versus Leeds FA Cup quarter-final tie. The other FA Cup quarter-final between Bournemouth and Leeds also remains pending, with the results determining whether Everton faces a blank as well. As it stands, Brighton vs Chelsea and Burnley vs Man City are confirmed blanks, while West Ham vs Everton remains a possible blank depending on Saturday's results.

**Why This Matters**

This development represents a significant shift in FPL strategy for the season's climax. Previously, many managers were planning to deploy their Wildcard in Gameweek 32 specifically to acquire Arsenal assets ahead of a lucrative double in Gameweek 33 and a guaranteed playing fixture in Blank Gameweek 34. Now, those plans require substantial reconsideration.

The reduction from eight to six doubling teams in Gameweek 33 diminishes the value of a Bench Boost chip in that week, as managers will have fewer players able to accumulate double returns. Conversely, the reduction from four to three blanks in Gameweek 34 means many managers may be able to navigate that difficult week without using their Free Hit chip—a valuable resource that can now potentially be deployed elsewhere.

**Key Details**

Arsenal's single fixture in Gameweek 33 comes against Manchester City, one of the Premier League's toughest away assignments. Newcastle similarly loses its double, leaving managers who invested heavily in assets from both clubs facing difficult decisions. The confirmed blanks for Gameweek 34 are Brighton v Chelsea and Burnley v Man City, with the third blank dependent on FA Cup outcomes. Liverpool v Crystal Palace, Fulham v Aston Villa, Manchester United v Brentford, Sunderland v Nottingham Forest, Wolves v Tottenham, and Arsenal v Newcastle are all confirmed as playing.

**Context and Background**

This FPL season has been defined by the complex interaction between FA Cup quarter-final fixtures and the league schedule. When Premier League teams progress in the FA Cup, their opponents face blank gameweeks, while the advancing teams can potentially receive doubles if they have league fixtures rescheduled. The original expectation of Arsenal and Newcastle featuring in both Double Gameweek 33 and Blank Gameweek 34 had heavily influenced chip strategy discussions throughout the community.

**What's Next**

Fantasy Football Scout has announced it will publish a follow-up article weighing the pros and cons of the revised chip strategy, with a particular focus on whether a Free Hit in Gameweek 33 becomes a viable alternative play. A Gameweek 32 Wildcard draft article is also scheduled to help managers adjust their squads in light of these changes. The publication notes that Gameweek 36 is likely to feature doubles for Manchester City and Crystal Palace, potentially making that a more attractive option for Bench Boost deployment than previously anticipated.
