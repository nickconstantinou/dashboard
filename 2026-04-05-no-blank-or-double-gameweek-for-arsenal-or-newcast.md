# Arsenal FA Cup Exit Reshapes FPL Fixture Calendar, Forcing Managers to Rethink Chip Strategy

## The Full Story

Arsenal's shock FA Cup elimination at the hands of Championship side Southampton has sent ripples through the Fantasy Premier League community, eliminating what would have been a crucial Double Gameweek opportunity for managers targeting the season's run-in. Mikel Arteta's side fell 2-1 at St Mary's Stadium on Saturday night, ending any possibility of the Gunners featuring in Double Gameweek 33 or Blank Gameweek 34. Newcastle United, already eliminated from the cup earlier in the competition, will also miss out on any double fixtures. The result means Gameweek 33 will now feature only six teams doubling—down from the eight that would have included Arsenal and Newcastle—while Gameweek 34 will proceed with just three confirmed blanks rather than the expanded fixture list that had been anticipated.

The three confirmed blanks for Gameweek 34 are Brighton versus Chelsea, Burnley versus Manchester City, and the West Ham versus Everton matchup, provided West Ham defeat Leeds in Sunday's FA Cup quarter-final. If Leeds upset West Ham, Bournemouth's fixture against Leeds would also be postponed, creating a fourth blank. All other matches—including Arsenal's home game against Newcastle, Liverpool's trip to Crystal Palace, Fulham's clash with Aston Villa, Manchester United hosting Brentford, Sunderland versus Nottingham Forest, and Wolves against Tottenham—are confirmed to proceed as scheduled. This represents a significant simplification of the fixture chaos that had been developing across the double and blank gameweeks.

## Why This Matters

For the millions of FPL managers who had built their season strategies around Arsenal's premium assets—particularly Bukayo Saka, Martin Ødegaard, and Gabriel Martinelli—the elimination fundamentally alters the calculus for the critical season run-in. The Double Gameweek 33, which was expected to feature both Arsenal and Newcastle doubling alongside Manchester City, Burnley, Brighton, and Chelsea, has lost two of its most potent attacking units. This means managers who had planned to load up on Arsenal players specifically for that double fixture now face a scenario where those investments yield only a single match against Manchester City in Gameweek 33.

The broader implications extend to chip deployment strategies that have been standard practice for serious FPL managers. The anticipated sequence of using a Wildcard in Gameweek 32, followed by Bench Boost in the eight-team Double Gameweek 33, then Free Hit through the Blank Gameweek 34, has been destabilized. With fewer teams doubling in Gameweek 33 and far fewer blanks in Gameweek 34, managers may now find their existing squads sufficiently equipped to navigate the fixture without employing any chips at all during those gameweeks. This could represent a paradigm shift in how managers approach the season's conclusion, potentially preserving powerful chips like Wildcard and Bench Boost for later gameweeks when they might yield greater returns.

## Context and Background

Fantasy Premier League managers have long understood that FA Cup quarter-final weekend creates a complex web of fixture complications, as Premier League matches involving clubs still in the competition are routinely postponed to accommodate the cup ties. This season, the intersection of FA Cup commitments with the Premier League's fixture pile-up had created an unusual scenario where multiple clubs would face blank and double gameweeks simultaneously. Arsenal's continued progress in both competitions had positioned them as central figures in these fixture manipulations, with a potential Double Gameweek 33 followed by a Blank Gameweek 34 that would have forced difficult squad management decisions on managers.

The elimination of Arsenal and Newcastle from the FA Cup removes two of the Premier League's most potent attacking sides from the Double Gameweek equation, fundamentally changing the expected value calculations for assets in those squads. Manchester City, whose fixture list remains subject to their own FA Cup result against Plymouth, and Brighton and Chelsea—whose Double Gameweek status remains confirmed—now represent the primary doubling options for Gameweek 33. The subsequent reduction in Blank Gameweek 34 blanks from what would have been a more extensive postponement list provides managers with greater certainty and fewer forced transfers heading into the season's final stages.
