Key Concepts & Arguments

The Crystal Palace Situation: Confirmed Blank Implications

The most significant news that Pras addresses at the top of the video concerns Crystal Palace's European progression. After the three English teams involved in European competitions—Crystal Palace, Nottingham Forest, and Aston Villa—all progressed in their respective competitions, Pras walks through the updated fixture matrix to explain what this means for blank gameweek planning.

As Pras demonstrates using his screen share, Crystal Palace's advancement has definitively confirmed that they will not play in Gameweek 31. The Eagles were scheduled to face Manchester City in what would have been a crucial fixture, but their progression in the Europa League means that game cannot fit into the league schedule. Specifically, Palace has the second leg of their next Europa League round scheduled, which pushes the Manchester City fixture to Gameweek 32. However, this also means Gameweek 32 will no longer be a double gameweek for Palace—as many managers had hoped.

This confirmation is crucial because it eliminates one of the potential double gameweeks that managers were counting on. According to Pras, the current expectation is that Gameweek 33 will now be the primary double that managers should target, with Palace likely to have another double in Gameweek 36. The base case scenario that FPL Wire and other analysis platforms have been projecting remains largely intact, though the likelihood of a double in Gameweek 32 has decreased significantly. For managers who had been holding Palace assets hoping for a double in 32, this news serves as a clear signal to adjust their strategies.

Chip Strategy: The Three Viable Approaches

One of the most valuable portions of the video focuses on the three distinct chip strategies that Pras identifies as viable for the remainder of the season. He emphasizes that these strategies have been discussed extensively in the main FPL Wire podcasts, and he wants to reinforce the key points while adding some new context based on the latest fixture news.

Strategy One: Wildcard in Gameweek 32, Bench Boost in 33, Free Hit in 34

This remains the most popular approach among FPL managers. The logic is straightforward—use the wildcard in Gameweek 32 to load up on the best fixtures for that week, then activate the bench boost in Gameweek 33 to maximize points from the double gameweek, and finally use the free hit in Gameweek 34 to navigate the blank. Pras notes that this is a perfectly sound strategy, though he adds an important caveat: managers should not rush to press the wildcard button immediately in Gameweek 31. Instead, they should wait until after the FA Cup quarterfinals have been completed during the international break, as there's little to no price movement happening right now that would be missed by waiting a few more days.

Strategy Two: Free Hit in 33, Bench Boost in 36, Wildcard in 35

Pras reveals that this is the strategy he's personally leaning toward, assuming the fixtures eventually align with the projections. The appeal for him comes from his current team structure, which already looks favorable in Gameweeks 32 and 34. By free hitting in 33 rather than wildactoring, he preserves flexibility while still capitalizing on the double. This approach is less common than the first strategy but remains completely viable. The wildcard in 35 allows for adjustments based on what teams actually get blanks or doubles, while the bench boost in 36 targets what should be another double gameweek for certain teams.

Strategy Three: Wildcard in 33, Free Hit in 34

This is described as a more niche strategy favored by some managers, including the FPL Wire co-host LR. The idea here is to wildcard in Gameweek 33 itself if the fixtures look particularly favorable, then use the free hit in 34 to navigate the blank. Some managers employing this strategy might not even own a bench boost chip, having already used it earlier in the season. In that case, they would bench boost in either 35 or 36 instead.

Pras makes a compelling case for waiting to commit to any of these strategies. During the upcoming international break, managers will know with 100% certainty which fixtures are going into Gameweek 33 and which are staying in 34. Since only one of the three strategies involves wildandering in Gameweek 32, and that chip can be saved for later if needed, there's very little cost to waiting and gathering more information. This is classic overthinking in FPL, as Pras acknowledges, but it's also sound game theory when the stakes are this high.

Free Hit Analysis: Team Selection Based on Strategy

Perhaps the most tactically valuable portion of the video involves Pras walking through specific team recommendations depending on which gameweek a manager plans to free hit. This analysis draws from data provided by Solio, and Pras presents it in a clear, Attack versus defense framework.

For managers planning to free hit in Gameweek 33, the data shows that Liverpool is the clear priority team to target. Pras explains that this is because the Reds have what he calls "the best horizon"—favorable fixtures in both Gameweek 32 (home against Fulham) and Gameweek 34 (home against Crystal Palace). This makes them ideal for a manager who wants to allocate resources toward Liverpool assets and then potentially switch teams for Gameweek 34. Manchester United ranks second in projected attack for both 32 and 34, making them an excellent secondary option. Fulham attack also projects well, which explains why players like Wilson and Rogers have become so popular this week.

In defense for a free hit 33, Liverpool, Manchester United, West Ham, Tottenham, and Nottingham Forest all show up as strong options. This is precisely why Fulham have become such a popular pick among the FPL community—offering both attacking potential and defensive security. Pras specifically mentions Sebastioni as a player worth considering, noting that if Salah misses any game time due to injury, Sebasti would likely be on penalties and offers excellent minutes. He also mentions Man United midfielders like Kuna or Ibumo as potentially good picks.

For those planning to free hit in Gameweek 34 instead, the analysis shifts dramatically. Chelsea's attack becomes the priority, making players like Pedro and Palmer excellent picks. Newcastle also projects strongly for their horizon, making them the key entry point for this strategy. Pras addresses the common debate: should managers go for Fulham or Newcastle this week? His answer is elegantly simple—it depends entirely on which week you plan to free hit. If you're wildandering or free hitting in 34, go Newcastle. If you're planning to play players throughout the entire run, go Fulham and be prepared to bench them during the blank.

In defense for a free hit 34, Arsenal remains the highest despite the blank—doubles matter, and they will have one. Newcastle, Brighton, and Aston Villa are the secondary targets.

Detailed Takeaways

Takeaway One: Wait to Wildcard

The most important strategic insight Pras offers is to resist the urge to press the wildcard button immediately in Gameweek 32. With FA Cup quarterfinals happening during the international break, and price movements minimal, there's virtually no disadvantage to waiting a few extra days. Managers who wait will have more complete information about which fixtures actually land in which gameweeks. If Manchester City loses to Liverpool and Chelsea win their FA Cup, the fixture landscape shifts significantly. Waiting costs nothing but information is everything.

Takeaway Two: Liverpool is the Hub Team for Free Hit 33

For managers planning to free hit in Gameweek 33, Liverpool should be the primary team to build around. Their fixtures in both surrounding gameweeks (32 and 34) are excellent, and their projected goal output ranks highest. This makes Salah, despite his potential injury concerns, still worth targeting. Managers should check the Thursday press conference for updates on Salah's availability, but even if he's out, the Liverpool attack remains strong.

Takeaway Three: Strategy is Determinant on Your Chip Plan

The entire video reinforces that specific player recommendations depend entirely on your broader chip strategy. There's no single correct answer—Newcastle is better for free hit 34, Fulham is better if you're playing through the blank. Arsenal defense is essential for free hit 34 because they're doubling despite the blank. This is why Pras encourages managers to decide on their chip plan first and only then build their transfer targets around that framework.

Takeaway Four: Gameweek 33 is the Key Double

Despite the changes to fixture projections, Gameweek 33 remains the expected double gameweek that managers should prioritize. The likelihood of Gameweek 32 being a meaningful double has decreased significantly with Palace's advancement, making 33 even more attractive. Regardless of which strategy you choose, bench boosting or wildandering in 33 should feature in everyone's calculations.

Takeaway Five: Blancks are Carcass—and That's Good

Pras expresses actual satisfaction that more English teams are progressing in European competitions. More European matches mean more fixture congestion, which means more blanks for FPL managers to navigate. This "carnage" creates opportunity. Managers who plan well can capitalize on those who panic, and the game becomes more about strategic thinking than simply picking the best players. In competitive FPL, blanks are features not bugs.
