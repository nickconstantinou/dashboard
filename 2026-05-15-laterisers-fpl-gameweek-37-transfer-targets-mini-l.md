# Lateriser's FPL Gameweek 37 Transfer Targets + Mini-League Tips

## Overview

Gameweek 37 presents FPL managers with an intriguing set of fixtures as the season approaches its climax. With limited gameweeks remaining, transfers must be made with both immediate returns and fixture equity in mind.

## Premium Options

### Erling Haaland

Erling Haaland remains the most expensive option in the game at £14.7m. The Manchester City striker has been a consistent performer throughout the season and represents the benchmark for premium forwards.

## Arsenal Attackers Worth Considering

The three Arsenal attackers worth considering for Gameweek 37 are Bukayo Saka (£10.0m), Viktor Gyokeres (£9.0m) and Leandro Trossard (£6.5m).

### Viktor Gyokeres (£9.0m)

Gyokeres has demonstrated impressive underlying metrics in recent matches. Only Haaland boasts more big chances than Gyokeres across the last six matches. Furthermore, he and Joao Pedro (£7.5m) are the top two for non-penalty expected goals (NPxG) in the same period, with only Haaland ahead for big chances.

### Leandro Trossard (£6.5m)

Trossard offers an interesting budget option within the Arsenal attack. He's had three successive 80+ minute appearances in all competitions while being Arsenal's leading shot-taker and big chance creator in the last two matches. He's recently posted strong expected goal involvement (xGI) tallies of 0.49 and 0.75, making him a potentially undervalued asset. Despite these credentials, Trossard is owned by just 1.1% of overall managers, suggesting significant differential potential.

## Under-the-Radar Options

### Iliman Ndiaye (£6.2m)

For managers seeking budget alternatives, Iliman Ndiaye presents an intriguing case. Despite registering xGI totals of 0.43 and 0.90, he somehow came away without any goals or assists, suggesting potential positive regression in future fixtures.

## Fixture Considerations

Manchester City will now play their FA Cup final between the deadline and their Gameweek 37 trip to Bournemouth. This fixture congestion could impact rotation risk for City assets, which is worth considering when evaluating their players.

## Mini-League Tips

Given the ownership figures, Trossard at 1.1% represents a significant differential opportunity within the Arsenal attack. His recent involvement metrics suggest he could be undervalued by the wider FPL community.

For managers chasing or protecting leads in mini-leagues, prioritising players with favourable fixture runs and low ownership percentages becomes crucial at this stage of the season. Trossard's combination of Arsenal involvement and low ownership makes him a candidate for gaining ground on rivals.
