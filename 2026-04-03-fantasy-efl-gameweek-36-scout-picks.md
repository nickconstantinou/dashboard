## Context & Background

Fantasy EFL mirrors the structure of the more widely known Fantasy Premier League but focuses on the English Football League's three divisions below the top flight. With 72 clubs competing across Championship, League One, and League Two, the game offers different dynamics than Premier League fantasy—smaller squads, more variable form lines, and greater opportunities for informed selections based on deeper tactical knowledge.

Double Gameweeks occur when teams have fixtures rearranged or when scheduling creates two matches within a single gameweek. For Fantasy EFL managers, these represent the game's most important opportunities, as a player featuring twice can potentially double their points output compared to single-gameweek alternatives. The key strategic consideration is identifying teams with favorable fixture combinations that allow maximum exposure to teams likely to collect points in both matches.

The expert panel at FantasyFootballScout.co.uk operates through a "Scout Squad" submission process, where each expert proposes their highest-confidence picks before the publication team finalizes recommendations based on consensus. When multiple experts align on a player—as occurred with Wing and Kilgour—the resulting picks carry additional weight as indicators of high-confidence selections rather than marginal preferences.
