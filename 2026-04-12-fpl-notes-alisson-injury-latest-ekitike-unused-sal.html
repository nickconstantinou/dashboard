FPL notes: Alisson injury latest, Ekitike unused, Salah + Rio bright

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

Alisson is sidelined again. Ekitike is a non-factor. But two Liverpool wingers—one nearing the end, one at the very beginning—are doing enough to complicate your decisions.

Liverpool and Fulham players will likely be surplus to the requirements of many FPL managers in Gameweek 33, as neither side doubles. They both have tricky fixtures, too, away at Everton and Brentford respectively. Nevertheless, they both could play a part in our thinking beyond that—especially with a sentimental farewell or two scheduled for Anfield in Gameweek 38. Here's what we saw and heard on Merseyside on Saturday.

---

The question worth asking after Liverpool's Saturday showing isn't about the result—it's about what Arne Slot might finally have unlocked.

Liverpool were actually pretty good in the first hour at Anfield, producing some nice, free-flowing football and playing with an energy and intensity we haven't seen much of recently. Perhaps the freshening up of the attack helped. Slot changed four of his front six, bringing in Rio Ngumoha (£4.3m), Mohamed Salah (£14.0m), Curtis Jones (£5.4m), and Cody Gakpo (£7.3m). And it was Rio and Salah who produced the match-winning moments, with superb curling goals.

The two wingers are at opposite ends of their Liverpool careers. Rio is a raw talent with bags of potential. Salah is a once-great fading force.

---

Salah: The Farewell Punt

Let's start with Salah, because he's the more familiar dilemma.

Saturday's goal was vintage. A strike he's scored time and time again over the years—he finished with one of a game-high five shots. He's a shadow of his former self, but now and again, there are still tantalising, fleeting glimpses of his greatness.

That tension is exactly why he's so difficult to handle in FPL. He may now be too unreliable—as both a minutes risk and a returns risk—as a medium-term premium pick. The ceiling hasn't disappeared entirely, but the floor has become unpredictable.

But here's what keeps him relevant for many managers: that Gameweek 38 farewell punt. If Liverpool are playing in front of an emotional Anfield crowd for what might be Salah's final home appearance, the incentive structures change. The minutes might be managed differently. The moments might come easier.

Whether that's enough to justify a slot in your squad depends on your league position, your transfers remaining, and your tolerance for uncertainty. But it's not a crazy punt.

---

Rio: The Teenager Slot Is Now Talking Up

Rio is a very different proposition—and increasingly, Slot is talking about him in very different terms.

For much of 2026, Slot has been cautious with the teenage winger. That's shifted. Speaking after Saturday's goal—the first Rio has scored at Anfield—Slot's assessment was notably bullish.

"I think I've said a few weeks, maybe months ago, that his playing time would increase because he got stronger and stronger, showed more and more when he came in, but also in training sessions, that it wasn't only a nice trick, but there was more and more end product to what he did."

There was more and more power to what he did, which is needed if you play against the best defenders in the world. And I don't think anyone is surprised that at this moment of time he scored his first goal at Anfield.

The specific quality Slot highlighted is worth sitting with:

> "He has such a special quality which you don't see a lot in football anymore: dominating the one-v-one situation. And that's exactly what he did when he scored his goal, making the ball free by twisting and turning and then hitting it in like a Mo Salah finish, I would say."

The Salah comparison is striking—and Rio's goal did look like a Salah finish. Not the Salah of the last 18 months, but the Salah of peak years. Clean, curling, precise.

Whether that makes Rio a trustworthy FPL selection right now is a different question. The underlying numbers, the minutes, and the rotation risk all still matter. At £4.3m, he's cheap enough to own as a bench enabler. But starting him is another matter.

What we can say is that Slot appears to be moving Rio from project to player. That's a shift worth tracking.

---

What This Means for Your FPL Strategy

Two conclusions, then:

For Salah, the case isn't about what he's been this season—it's about what Gameweek 38 might become. If you're looking for one swing at a premium differential, the farewell narrative is real. Just go in with eyes open.

For Rio, the case isn't about this season at all. It's about the trajectory. Slot's language has changed. The end product is emerging. At £4.3m, he's a long-term hold candidate for managers building toward 2026/27.

Neither is a clean investment. But clean investments are hard to find in GW32.
