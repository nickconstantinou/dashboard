We Used OpenClaw for a Week: The Honest Verdict

AI OpenClaw Review

Limitless just dropped their real-world test of OpenClaw - and it's not all glowing reviews. Here's what we learned from their week-long experiment.

The setup: Limitless configured OpenClaw with Telegram, scheduled tasks, and custom skills. Then they lived with it for a week.

What Worked Well

  • Automation was genuine: The scheduled tasks actually ran - daily digests, content monitoring, and reminders fired as expected.
  • Memory persistence: The agent remembered context across conversations, which is crucial for useful assistance.
  • Multi-channel worked: Switching between Telegram and other inputs was seamless.

The Challenges

  • Initial setup complexity: Getting everything configured took time - lots of API keys, permissions, and cron setup.
  • Occasional hallucinations: The agent occasionally made up information when uncertain.
  • Security concerns: Running an agent with access to your accounts requires trust - and trust takes time to build.

The Verdict

OpenClaw isn't for everyone. If you want a polished consumer product with zero setup, you're better off with ChatGPT or Claude. But if you want:

  • Full control over your AI assistant
  • Custom automation workflows
  • Self-hosted privacy

OpenClaw delivers. The "harsh truth" is mostly about expectations - this is developer-first tooling, not plug-and-play.
