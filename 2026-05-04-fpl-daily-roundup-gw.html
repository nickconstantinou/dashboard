FPL Daily Roundup

The final stretch of the Fantasy Premier League season is upon us, and with it comes a familiar concoction of injury setbacks, rotation headaches, and tactical roulette that separates the methodical managers from the panicked ones. As the calendar pushes toward its conclusion, those hovering in the upper echelons of their mini-leagues know that every transfer, every captaincy call, and every chip deployment could be the difference between glory and despair.

Today's Key FPL News & Injuries

The most significant blow to managers this week came courtesy of Newcastle's 2-1 defeat to Aston Villa on Monday, where Swedish striker Alexander Isak suffered a groin injury that has ruled him out for the remainder of the season. The £9.0m forward had been a staple in so many squads — a reliable source of goals who also offered rotation flexibility given Newcastle's fixture run-in. His absence leaves a gaping hole in attack, and those who have been relying on him will need to pivot rapidly toward the budget-friendly options or the premium alternatives remaining in the game.

Over at Villa Park, meanwhile, Unai Emery rang the changes for Sunday's Premier League fixture against Tottenham Hotspur, benching both Ezri Konsa and Ollie Watkins as part of a seven-person overhaul from Thursday's Europa League lineup. Skipper John McGinn and Amadou Onana were entirely absent from the matchday squad, with Emery admitting in his post-match comments that Villa were prioritising Thursday's Europa League second leg against Lyon. That leaves managers holding Villa assets in a precarious position heading into the run-in — particularly those with eyes on their remaining league fixtures. Victor Lindelof, Tyrone Mings, Ian Maatsen, Lamare Bogarde, Ross Barkley, Jadon Sancho, and Tammy Abraham all started the match, confirming that Villa's league form is very much secondary to their continental ambitions right now.

Brighton midfielder Mats Wieffer (£4.9m) is another concern, carrying an ankle injury that gives him just a 50% chance of playing this gameweek. The Dutchman has been in decent form, but managers will need to monitor the latest team news before committing. Liverpool's Mohamed Salah (£14.0m) remains absent with a muscle injury and is not expected back until May 24th — a timeline that likely rules him out of the season's remaining fixtures entirely, given the calendar.

Crystal Palace's 3-0 defeat to Bournemouth on Sunday painted a grim picture of squad exhaustion, with manager Oliver Glasner admitting the "tank was empty" after an intensive run of fixtures. The Eagles failed to register a single attempt on goal in the first half, with Will Hughes ill and Borna Sosa missing through a minor injury. Palace face Manchester City in Gameweek 36, and with European qualification still mathematically possible for both clubs, the question of how much rotation each side deploys becomes crucial for FPL purposes.

Gameweek Landscape

The confirmed fixture picture narrows significantly as we approach the business end of the season. Gameweek 36 emerges as the standout gameweek with two teams playing twice — and those two are confirmed to be Crystal Palace and Manchester City. That modest double fixture count means the Bench Boost becomes a much more niche proposition than the community chatter suggested earlier in the week.

Manchester City, as always, sit at the centre of the strategic conversation. Pep Guardiola's side faces a fixture pile-up that makes them the obvious focus for any managers deploying chips in the final stretch. City blank in Gameweek 34 alongside Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, and Leeds — but double in Gameweek 36 alongside Crystal Palace. The confirmed fixture density across the final three gameweeks shows Gameweek 36 with 22 confirmed fixtures, Gameweek 37 with 20, and Gameweek 38 with 20, meaning the season is not exactly sparse — but the double gameweek concentration is far more limited than we might have hoped.

Arsenal blank this gameweek (Gameweek 31), which means those holding Gabriel dos Santos Magalhães (£7.2m, 44.6% ownership) or David Raya Martín (£6.1m, 35.0% ownership) will need to park those assets temporarily. The Gunners travel to Liverpool knowing they need to keep pace after Sunday's dominant display — a fixture that will test their title credentials but provides no FPL comfort for those relying on clean sheet points.

Captain Recommendations

The captaincy debate this week centres on the fixture-rich and the form horses. Ollie Watkins (£8.8m) showed his quality before being benched on Sunday, maintaining a form rating of 5.2 and 130 season points at a 15.4% ownership. But with Villa clearly prioritising their Europa League commitments, any manager backing him this week is taking on significant rotation risk. The Swedish replacement at Newcastle — currently a hole left by Isak's injury — creates uncertainty rather than opportunity, with William Osula (£5.4m) the most direct budget option but unproven at this level.

Bruno Borges Fernandes (£10.4m, 47.2% ownership) remains the default captain for many managers, offering 209 season points and a 5.0 form rating that demonstrates consistency even in Manchester United's occasionally chaotic campaign. Casemiro (£5.8m) continues to fly under the radar at 4.7% ownership despite 164 season points and a 7.5 form rating — a genuinely differential option for those looking to free up funds elsewhere.

Among the form leaders, Morgan Gibbs-White (£7.7m) at Nottingham Forest continues to deliver, his 8.8 form rating and 168 season points making him one of the season's standout success stories. Forest face a fixture run that many managers believe will keep the England international ticking along, and at under £8m he represents genuine value at this stage of the season.

Viktor Gyökeres (£8.8m) carries a 5.8 form rating with 124 season points at Arsenal, though Arsenal's blank this gameweek means he is immediately parked. Jarrod Bowen (£7.8m) at West Ham has 171 season points and a 7.0 form rating that makes him equally compelling — West Ham's fixtures through the run-in look reasonable, and at £7.8m he slots into most budgets without strain.

Transfer Strategy

The consensus among FFS creators points toward a clear strategy: accumulate Manchester City assets, especially those playing in Gameweek 36, while moving away from injured or rotated forwards. Marc Guéhi (£5.1m) at City has been a revelation with 158 season points and a 5.8 form rating at just 32.7% ownership — the cheapest route into City's defence and one that many managers have inexplicably overlooked. Nico O'Reilly (£5.2m) at 18.2% ownership with 152 season points and a 6.8 form rating represents another under-owned route into the champions' attack.

Rayan Cherki (£6.5m, 15.9% ownership) offers creative upside at a reasonable price, while Abdukodir Khusanov (£5.4m) continues to see significant minutes despite his lower ownership percentage of just 0.7%. The £5.4m price point makes him an intriguing budget option for those needing to free up funds for premium attacking positions.

Among the form leaders, Noah Okafor (£5.6m) at Leeds leads the entire game with a 10.2 form rating, 109 season points, and ownership of just 2.8% — a genuinely differential pick that very few managers are exploiting. His Leeds teammate James Justin (£4.0m) offers a cheaper route into the same fixture run at £4.0m with a 5.5 form rating and only 2.1% ownership.

Igor Thiago Nascimento Rodrigues (£7.4m) at Brentford continues his stellar season with 175 season points, a 5.5 form rating, and 34.7% ownership — one of the most owned assets in the game for good reason. Brentford's fixtures look reasonable through the run-in, and at £7.4m he represents solid value without breaking the bank.

Players to Avoid or Sell

The injury to Alexander Isak fundamentally changes Newcastle's attacking calculus, and managers should be very wary of holding any Magpies forwards given the uncertainty around how Eddie Howe will set up without their primary goal source. The Swedish striker's £9.0m price tag could be held temporarily as a vehicle for a future transfer, but those same funds could be deployed more productively in City assets or the form horses discussed above.

Ollie Watkins remains a talented player, but Villa's team selection against Tottenham on Sunday demonstrated that their league commitments are secondary to their European ambitions. Managers should consider selling or at least benching Watkins unless the latest team news suggests he returns to the starting XI — the Europa League second leg against Lyon on Thursday makes rotation a near-certainty for the next league fixture.

Mohamed Salah (£14.0m) looks increasingly likely to miss the remainder of the season, and managers still holding him at his premium price point should move immediately. The Egyptian has been an incredible FPL asset, but at £14.0m those funds are desperately needed elsewhere, and his muscle injury timeline suggests no return before the campaign's conclusion.

Mats Wieffer (£4.9m) at 50% chance of playing creates a dilemma — managers wanting exposure to Brighton through the run-in would be better served by Pascal Groß (£5.5m, 2.6% ownership), Ferdi Kadıoğlu (£4.4m, 1.0% ownership), or Bart Verbruggen (£4.5m, 9.1% ownership) given the injury uncertainty.

Chip Strategy

The confirmed Gameweek 36 double — Crystal Palace and Manchester City playing twice — combined with the modest Bench Boost target list means that managers should be extremely selective about when, and if, they deploy their final chip. The Free Hit remains a potent weapon for navigating blanks, but with the confirmed blank gameweeks spread across the schedule rather than concentrated, the optimal deployment window is narrower than many anticipated.

The wildcard window continues to shrink, and those who have not yet used theirs should be urgently identifying their target squad — specifically the City assets and form players who will carry them through the final stretch. Given that Gameweek 36 is the only confirmed double of significance, managers should prioritise players who feature in that fixture pair while accepting that speculative transfers based on predicted doubles are high-risk ventures at this stage.

The season's fixture density means that Gameweek 37 and 38 offer 20 games each — plenty of opportunities for points accumulation even without doubles. Managers should not feel compelled to burn chips chasing marginal advantages; instead, a disciplined approach of loading up on City assets for Gameweek 36 and relying on form players for the remaining fixtures represents the most rational strategy.

Where Creators Disagree

Some believe Emery will prioritise league form in the final weeks once European commitments conclude, making Watkins and Konsa viable options again. Others argue that Villa's season is effectively over in terms of league positioning, and rotation will persist regardless — making a clean break the smarter move.

There is also debate about the value of Bournemouth assets beyond Junior Kroupi (£4.6m), who scored a penalty in their 3-0 victory over Crystal Palace on Sunday. Marcos Senesi Barón (£5.2m, 20.7% ownership) and Adrien Truffert (£4.7m, 4.5% ownership) have been consistent performers, but their £5.2m and £4.7m price points create tension with the budget options elsewhere. Some creators advocate for loading up on Cherries assets given their 15-game unbeaten run and favourable fixtures, while others suggest the form is due for regression and managers should look elsewhere.

The Mohamed Salah situation has also split opinion. Those who believe he will return before the season ends have held him as a prestige asset, while others moved him weeks ago and have reaped the benefits of having those £14.0m funds deployed more productively. With his expected return date now past the season's logical conclusion, the disagreement is largely academic — but it illustrates the ongoing tension between price preservation and active fund management.
