Rodri, Ruben, Gvardiol: Man City Injury Latest for FPL Gameweek 36

Rodri Remains a Doubt

Manchester City midfielder Rodri continues to be a doubt for the club's Double Gameweek 36 fixtures. According to manager Pep Guardiola, the Spanish midfielder has not fully recovered from a groin injury and remains uncertain for selection. Rodri was not seen in training on Thursday, raising further concerns about his availability. Guardiola noted that Rodri doesn't feel completely comfortable, suggesting the club may err on the side of caution with one of their most important players.

Ruben Dias Returns to Training

Defender Ruben Dias, who has been sidelined with a hamstring injury, was sighted on the training ground ahead of Gameweek 36. His return to training provides a positive update for Manchester City and Fantasy Premier League managers considering his defensive assets. However, his actual match readiness for the Double Gameweek remains to be confirmed.

Gvardiol Back in Training After Extended Absence

Josko Gvardiol has returned to training after spending many months out with a leg injury. The Croatian defender was also sighted on the training ground, offering hope for his imminent return. Guardiola confirmed the positive news, stating: "Yeah, he's good. He's training, he feels good."

The defender's versatility could prove valuable for FPL managers assessing their defensive options. Guardiola highlighted Gvardiol's flexibility, noting he can play as a central defender, full-back, or in different roles. The manager also reflected on Gvardiol's importance to the squad last season, mentioning: "Last season, he was an important player for us, with many injuries [elsewhere]. Played all the games."

Fantasy Implications

For FPL managers navigating Double Gameweek 36, the situation presents mixed signals. Rodri's continued doubt leaves a significant void in midfield selections, while the returns of Dias and Gvardiol strengthen defensive options. Guardiola's praise for Jeremy Doku adds context to City's attacking threat, as he noted: "Jeremy has been amazing this season, amazing. Not just the goals and assists, also his contributions defensively." Managers should monitor training updates closely before confirming their lineups.
