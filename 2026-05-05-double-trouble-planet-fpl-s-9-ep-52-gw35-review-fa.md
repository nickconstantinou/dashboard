# Double Trouble: GW35 Review and Season Run-In Strategy

## The Title Race and Arsenal's Margin for Error

The Premier League title race remains finely poised as we approach the business end of the season. According to the latest analysis, if Manchester City fail to beat Brentford on Saturday, Arsenal could afford to lose at West Ham and still win the league. This scenario highlights the increasingly favorable position Arsenal finds themselves in, provided City drop points in their fixture.

## Crystal Palace: A GW38 Warning

For Fantasy Premier League managers, Crystal Palace presents a significant concern heading into GW38. The team will probably have a Conference League final just three days after the final gameweek, which means the club will likely rest all of their players. Managers holding Palace assets should plan their transfers accordingly and avoid relying on any Eagles players for the season's finale.

## Nottingham Forest: Safe but Rotating

Nottingham Forest have secured their Premier League status following their result on the previous day. Their remaining fixtures on the run-in include Newcastle United, Liverpool, and Bournemouth. However, managers should note that Forest manager Nuno Espírito Santo and key player Gibbs-White are expected to be rested ahead of a Europa League final for their respective clubs. This rotation risk makes Forest assets unreliable for the final weeks of the season.

## Manchester City: Rotation Risk on Multiple Fronts

Manchester City's fixture list creates further complexity for FPL managers. There is potential for Manchester City to rotate their lineup against Crystal Palace in GW36 to prepare for the FA Cup final against Bournemouth. This early rotation in GW36 is worth monitoring closely.

More concerning for the final gameweek, there is a scenario in GW38 where both Manchester City and Arsenal may rotate players due to FA Cup final and Champions League final preparations. This could potentially leave FPL managers with nine players who might not play in that crucial final gameweek.

## The Season Timeline

The Fantasy Premier League season is winding down, but managers still have three more weeks to optimize their squads after GW37 and GW38. This makes strategic planning for the run-in essential, particularly given the rotation risks identified above.

## Strategic Implications

Managers should prioritize locking in players from teams with nothing to play for while avoiding assets from clubs competing in cup finals. The convergence of European finals and the season's conclusion creates a uniquely challenging environment where nine players potentially sitting out GW38 cannot be ruled out. Building squad depth and planning for underperforming captain options may prove crucial in the final stretches of this season.
