FPL Notes: Eze and Havertz Injury Latest

Arsenal Injury Update

Eberechi Eze and Kai Havertz both departed during the match with muscular niggles. Havertz left the field after 34 minutes, with Eze following him off early in the second half. Arsenal manager Mikel Arteta provided an update on their conditions, stating: "There are muscular niggles. We don't think it's too much. We have to wait and see if they're going to be available for Wednesday."

Riccardo Calafiori failed to appear in the matchday squad despite Arteta saying on Friday that he would probably be back.

Saka Returns

Bukayo Saka returned to the squad and came on as a substitute during the match. Arteta commented on his impact: "Someone who did make an appearance in the squad, and indeed off the bench as a substitute, was Bukayo Saka." The manager noted he had a very good impact and looks sharp.

Arsenal Set-Piece Threats

Arsenal's winner marked their 22nd set-piece goal of the 2025/26 season. Additionally, Arsenal went short with their first three corners at the start of the game.

Newcastle United Team News

Eddie Howe made five changes to his starting XI, with Nick Pope returning between the posts. Both fit recognised full-backs, Kieran Trippier and Lewis Hall, were only substitutes, with Dan Burn and Lewis Miley taking their roles in the side.

Howe explained his reasoning for benching Lewis Hall: "I felt today for a number of reasons that Dan was the right pick in terms of height and physicality and dealing with Arsenal's threats."

Martin Zubimendi came off later in the game due to sickness.
