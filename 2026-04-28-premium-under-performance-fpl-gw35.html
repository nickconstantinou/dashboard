    Introduction

    The latest episode of *Who Got The Assist* finds hosts Tom and Sam in reflective mood as they dissect a transformative couple of weeks in their Fantasy Premier League seasons. Recorded on Monday the 27th of April during the second half of the Manchester United versus Brentford match — a game that saw Thiago Silva turn "into a donkey" according to Tom — the podcast captures the euphoric momentum both hosts are currently enjoying after months of frustration.

    For Sam, the shift has been nothing short of miraculous. After failing to break the top one million in any single gameweek during the first 32 weeks of the season, he's now sitting at 98.5K with legitimate ambitions of a top 100K finish. "London buses," he notes, "they both come at once" — referring to the two consecutive weeks of exceptional returns that have transformed his season. His differential strategy, anchored by players like Morgan Gibbs-White and Jiri Hall, has paid off spectacularly during a period when many managers were constrained by chip usage and blank gameweeks.

    Tom, meanwhile, has climbed to around 40K following an 11K rise, putting him comfortably inside the top 50K with the pair now separated by approximately 20-30 points. Both hosts acknowledge that the Free Hit chip in gameweek 34 proved largely "sy" — as Tom describes it — with 98.5% of top 10K players deploying it, creating a compressed field where differential picks like Gibbs-White became crucial differentiators. The conversation then pivots toward analyzing whether premium players have genuinely underperformed this season, with Tom presenting data comparisons to previous years.

    The Free Hit Gameweek 34 Debrief

    The discussion opens with Sam expressing genuine disbelief at his recent fortunes. "I don't know what's happened," he admits, describing his state as "bes giddy" — a combination of bewildered and giddy that has characterized his unexpected ascent. He reveals that while he hasn't achieved a top one million finish in any single gameweek across the first 32 weeks, he now finds himself just outside the top 100K after back-to-back strong weeks.

    Tom confirms he managed 76 points to Sam's 65 on the double gameweek, with Bruno Fernandes delivering a 10-pointer and Thiago scoring for Brentford despite being one of the players Sam notes "should have buried one or two there" — an admission of fortune that both hosts laugh about. The key differentiator was Sam's inclusion of Jiri Hall and Gibbs-White over Tom's picks, with Gibbs-White's 13-pointer proving particularly decisive. "I think that kind of set it in train as being a really good week," Tom notes, explaining that the momentum from that score allowed Sam to build around it.

    Tom's own approach was notably passive — he essentially left his team untouched after discussing it on the pod the previous Wednesday. "I thought it was kind of all right then and then by the time we hit Friday, I hadn't changed anything," he explains, noting that tinkering would have been "massively counterproductive" in a gameweek where many strong picks essentially picked themselves. This contrasts with Sam's more active approach, where his "feeling" about Gibbs-White and Hall proved prescient.

    The hosts acknowledge that the Free Hit chip created some artificial gameweek dynamics, with Sam noting "a fair bit of variety" across teams while also observing that "a lot of the players that a lot of us own did pick themselves." The chip essentially compressed the field, meaning non-Free Hit players who happened to pick the right assets performed comparably to those who had deployed the chip. "So, gameweek 35 really will be back to normality," Tom concludes, suggesting that the remaining gameweeks will reward traditional value-seeking and fixture planning rather than chip optimization.

    Premium Performance: Underperformance or Statistical Illusion?

    The meat of the episode centers on a deep-dive into premium player performance data that Tom has compiled. The hosts acknowledge that there has been significant community discussion about whether premium players — those in the £8-12 million price range — have underperformed expectations this season, and they aim to test whether the data supports this perception or whether it's more of a narrative phenomenon.

    Tom's analysis compares this season's premium performance against previous campaigns, with the goal of determining whether "it has indeed fallen off a cliff as many do suspect and does the data support that." The hosts discuss the methodology behind measuring premium performance — likely examining expected goals (xG), expected assists (xA), minutes played, and overall points per million — and how this compares to historical baselines.

    The discussion reveals nuances in the premium player category, noting that some expensive assets have thrived while others have genuinely struggled, creating an aggregate effect that feels worse than the reality for individual standout performers. The hosts will "run through that" in detail during the episode, suggesting the data reveals some surprising findings that complicate the straightforward "premiums are terrible" narrative.

    One practical concern that emerges is Sam's upcoming stag do coinciding with the gameweek 35 deadline. He notes that the Leeds versus Burnley match kicks off at 8 PM on Friday night, meaning "I will be very unaware of what's going on during that game.     Tom remarks that this marks "two weeks in a row that I've been tipped off to a Friday deadline on this very pod," suggesting there's been a pattern of late-notification about deadline changes or that the hosts have been caught off-guard by scheduling shifts this season. The Friday deadline for GW35 creates particular challenges for those with active social calendars, reinforcing Sam's strategy of ensuring his team is finalized before his stag begins.

    Key Takeaways for FPL Managers

    The value of holding your nerve with team selection cannot be overstated. Tom's decision to stick with his initial Free Hit team rather than tinker produced a strong result, while Sam's contrarian picks — particularly Gibbs-White — created the separation between their ranks. When gameweeks present clear options, resisting the urge to make unnecessary changes often proves wiser.

    Differential picks matter more when effective ownership is compressed. With 98.5% of top 10K players using the Free Hit chip, the traditional ownership leaders became essentially invisible as differentiators. In this environment, Gibbs-White's 13-pointer (likely owned by a smaller percentage) provided the crucial edge that separated Sam from Tom despite their similar overall strategies.

    Premium player discussions deserve data-driven scrutiny. The hosts' deep-dive into premium performance suggests that the narrative of "premiums are dead" may be oversimplified. Individual premium players have thrived, and aggregate underperformance may mask significant variance within the category — managers should analyze specific assets rather than dismissing the entire price tier.

    Gameweek 35 marks a return to traditional value optimization. With chips exhausted for most competitive managers, the remaining gameweeks will reward traditional FPL skills: fixture planning, transfer efficiency, and captaincy choices. The chip-driven chaos of GW34 has passed, and "normal" FPL strategy resumes.

    Real-world circumstances require practical FPL preparation. Sam's stag do highlight the need to lock in teams well before deadlines when competing life events intrude. Managers should review their squads early in the week and ensure they're set before weekend obligations complicate access to team management.
