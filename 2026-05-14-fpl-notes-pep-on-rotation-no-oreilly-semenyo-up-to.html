FPL Notes: Pep on Rotation, No O'Reilly + Semenyo Up Top

Manchester City Rotation in Gameweek 36

Pep Guardiola made six changes in Gameweek 36, with one eye on the two games that follow. The heavy rotation had clear implications for Fantasy Premier League managers tracking City assets.

Erling Haaland (£14.7m) didn't make it off the bench despite returning from injury. The Norwegian striker remained unused throughout the match, while the team explored alternative shapes.

Nico O'Reilly (£5.3m) wasn't even in the matchday squad for Gameweek 36. The budget option had been a popular choice among FPL managers, but his absence leaves a gap in the City defence lineup.

Tactical Setup and Phil Foden's Role

Guardiola brought back the 4-2-2-2 formation for this match. Antoine Semenyo (£8.1m) and Omar Marmoush (£8.3m) were deployed up front together.

Phil Foden (£8.0m) played deeper in central midfield alongside Bernardo Silva. The England international supplied assists for goals one and two, showing creativity from a more withdrawn position rather than his usual advanced role.

The fit-again Josko Gvardiol (£5.8m) made his first start since early January, returning to the lineup after a lengthy spell on the sidelines.

Bournemouth Outlook for Gameweek 37

Bournemouth will be more front-foot than Crystal Palace were in Gameweek 37. This suggests a more attacking approach awaits Fantasy managers considering their assets for the upcoming fixtures.

Looking Ahead to Gameweeks 37 and 38

The rotation policy points clearly toward returning to more familiar setups in the coming matches. Haaland is expected to return up front in Gameweek 37 or 38. When he does, the Ghanaian international would likely be shunted back out wide, with Haaland to return up front, Doku to reinstate on the left and Cherki to reclaim the '10' role.

Marc Guehi (£5.1m) finished as the top-scoring City asset in Gameweek 36 with a 15-pointer, providing a reminder of the value found in the City defence at budget price points.

Key Takeaways for FPL Managers

  • Haaland appears set for a return to the starting XI soon
  • Foden may retain a deeper role even when fully rotated
  • Semenyo ended a six-match run of blanks, potentially finding form at a crucial time
  • O'Reilly's absence removes a cheap City route from consideration
