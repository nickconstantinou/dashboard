Every FPL player on international duty in March – and when

The Full Story

The final international break of the Fantasy Premier League season arrives this week, with a substantial contingent of FPL assets heading away on national team duty through late March and into early April. Unlike previous international windows this season, FPL managers can breathe slightly easier regarding player availability, as the timing provides an unusually generous recovery window before domestic football resumes.

The last international fixture concludes in the early hours of Wednesday, 1 April 2026, while Gameweek 32 does not kick off until Friday, 10 April. This nearly ten-day gap means jet lag and quick turnarounds should pose minimal concerns for managers sweating over flag updates. When Premier League action returns, it comes with the FA Cup quarter-finals, adding another layer of complexity to team selection.

Most nations involved in this break are scheduled for friendly matches, which should result in managed minutes and reduced injury risk. However, high-stakes World Cup 2026 qualifying play-offs cast a shadow over several FPL heavyweights. The final six places at next summer's tournament will be settled during this window—four from European play-offs and two from inter-confederation routes. This means several premium FPL assets face meaningful international commitments where rest cannot be guaranteed.

Why This Matters

For the approximately 8 million FPL managers worldwide, the international break represents both opportunity and anxiety. Understanding which players face genuine competitive pressure versus routine friendlies is crucial for wildcard and free-hit decisions. The World Cup 2026 qualifiers involve 41 Premier League players across 14 nations, including several assets who command significant ownership percentages.

Chelsea's Cole Palmer, Manchester City's Erling Haaland, and Liverpool's Mohamed Salah notably do not feature in competitive play-off situations, giving their managers peace of mind. However, clubs like Leeds United, Burnley, and Newcastle United have multiple players involved in high-pressure international fixtures, raising injury and fatigue concerns heading into the season's decisive stretch.

    Context and Background

    This international window arrives at a critical juncture in the FPL season. With approximately seven Gameweeks remaining after Gameweek 32, managers face their final major transfer decisions before the season's conclusion. The March international break has historically caused headaches for FPL players, particularly those from South American nations whose qualifying matches often conclude in the early morning hours UK time, leaving players exhausted for subsequent Premier League fixtures.

    However, this season's calendar provides unusual relief. The extended gap between international fixtures and domestic resumption—combined with the friendlies dominating most nations' schedules—means the traditional concerns about South American players returning exhausted do not apply this time. Nations like Brazil, Argentina, and Uruguay, which typically require lengthy flights and inconvenient kickoff times, are not included in this window's competitive fixtures.

    What's Next

    FPL managers should monitor World Cup qualifying results, particularly for Wales, Sweden, and the Republic of Ireland, whose players represent significant value across multiple price points. Arsenal managers will want updates on both Calafiori and Gyökeres, while Newcastle United's international contingent—particularly Tonali and Wissa—warrants close attention given their recent strong form.

    The return of Premier League football on 10 April marks the beginning of the season's most crucial phase, with Champions League and Europa League qualification still to be decided. Any injury or fatigue concern arising from World Cup duty could significantly shift the FPL landscape heading into the final weeks of the season.
