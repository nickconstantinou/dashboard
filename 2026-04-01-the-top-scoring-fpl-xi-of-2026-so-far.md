# The Top-Scoring FPL XI of 2026 So Far: A Complete Breakdown

## The Full Story

The Fantasy Premier League landscape has undergone a dramatic transformation through the first quarter of 2026, with the players dominating last year's rankings nowhere to be found in the current crop of high-performers. While Erling Haaland (£14.4m) claimed the 2025 crown with 224 points—17 ahead of Antoine Semenyo and 18 clear of Mohamed Salah—none of these premium assets have maintained their dominance into the new season. Instead, a mix of budget finds and mid-priced options now populate the points-per-pound leaderboard as the FPL community approaches the business end of the campaign.

In goal, Caoimhin Kelleher (£4.8m) has emerged as the standout value option, amassing 54 points across 12 appearances for Brentford since the turn of the year. The Irish international has been nothing short of spectacular, recording five clean sheets, five save points, and six bonus points—a remarkable return for a keeper priced under £5m. His standout moment came in January when he denied Enzo Le Fee with a audacious Panenka penalty save. After missing Gameweek 28 for the birth of his child, Kelleher returned to keep another clean sheet in a goalless draw with Bournemouth before adding another shutout at Leeds United prior to the international break. He has already seen three price rises in 2026, reflecting his growing popularity.

Among defenders, Gabriel Magalhaes (£7.2m) has been a revelation, collecting 76 points—seven more than any other defender in the game. The Arsenal centre-back has balanced defensive solidity with attacking ambition, contributing six clean sheets, 12 DefCon points, and nine bonus points in just 12 starts. His aerial threat has yielded an assist against Chelsea alongside additional attacking returns at the Vitality Stadium and Molineux. Liverpool's Virgil van Dijk (£6.3m) sits second among defenders with 69 points despite his side's struggles, having secured four clean sheets, six DefCon points, and five bonus points alongside three goals—all from set pieces. He also provided the assist for Alexis Mac Allister's late winner at the City Ground in February. Rounding out the back four are Bournemouth's budget revelation James Hill (£4.2m) with 60 points and his teammate Adrien Truffert (£4.7m) with 57 points, both reflecting the club's rise to defensive solidity in 2026.

## Why This Matters

The shift in FPL fortune carries significant implications for both casual players and serious competitors navigating the game's intricate transfer market. The emergence of budget options like Hill—priced at just £4.2m—demonstrates that massive returns remain achievable without breaking the bank, opening strategic pathways that weren't available when premium assets dominated. For those who invested heavily in Haaland, Salah, or other 2025 heavyweights, this transition serves as a crucial reminder that FPL success requires constant adaptation rather than passive loyalty to established stars.

The Brentford and Bournemouth defensive clusters also highlight how team form fluctuations create value opportunities in the FPL ecosystem. Brentford's Kelleher, despite three price increases, still represents exceptional value at £4.8m—a price point that frees up substantial funds for attacking firepower elsewhere. Meanwhile, Bournemouth's defensive resilience has lifted both Hill and Truffert into the top-scoring XI, suggesting that monitoring underlying metrics beyond mere clean sheet counts can reveal hidden gems before the market catches on.

## Context and Background

The 2025 FPL season belonged to premium forwards, with Haaland's 224-point haul representing the culmination of Manchester City's relentless attacking output. Semenyo's emergence as a £8.2m enabler and Salah's consistency at £14.0m provided viable alternatives to the Norwegian marksman. However, the 2025/26 campaign has seen a tactical shift across the Premier League, with more teams adopting defensive solidity as a foundation for results. This has particularly benefited defenders and goalkeepers, whose clean sheet potential has increased substantially compared to the high-scoring chaos of 2025.

Bournemouth's transformation under their current management has been particularly striking. The club's rise to defensive solidity has created multiple FPL assets simultaneously, with Hill emerging as a breakout star despite minimal prior recognition. His DefCon total of 146 leads all defenders in 2026, reflecting not just clean sheets but consistent defensive contributions that often translate to attacking returns. Arsenal's continued resilience at the back has maintained Gabriel's value, while Liverpool's inconsistent season has paradoxically boosted Van Dijk's individual statistics as he shoulders more responsibility from set-piece situations.

## What's Next

FPL managers should monitor several developing storylines as the season progresses. Kelleher's three price rises suggest the market has already identified his value, but his sub-£5m price point still offers substantial savings compared to premium goalkeeping options. Whether Brentford can maintain their defensive solidity through a crowded fixture schedule will determine whether Kelleher remains a viable long-term option or represents a peak-value moment to cash in on.

For defenders, the emergence of the Bournemouth defensive contingent alongside established premium options like Gabriel creates interesting squad construction decisions. The data suggests DefCon leaders like Alderete (Sunderland, 137 points) and Mavropanos (West Ham, 128 points) warrant closer attention as differential options with favorable upcoming fixture runs.
