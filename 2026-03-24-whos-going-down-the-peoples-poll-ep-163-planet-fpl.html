Who's Going Down? | The People's Poll ep. 163 | Planet FPL 2025/26

Introduction

In this episode of Planet FPL's "The People's Poll," hosts Serge and James tackle one of the most compelling questions facing Fantasy Premier League managers as the season reaches its critical final stages: which teams will be relegated? The podcast emerged from a listener poll where fans were given three voting options—the title race, European qualification, and the relegation battle—with the relegation battle winning overwhelmingly. This should come as no surprise, as the battle to avoid the drop has become genuinely fascinating, with multiple clubs separated by mere points and facing vastly different run-ins.

Both hosts approach the topic with a unique personal angle: Serge supports West Ham United while James is a Tottenham Hotspur supporter. This dual perspective creates an entertaining dynamic as each man attempts to remain objective while discussing his own club's precarious position. The conversation moves beyond simple predictions to examine the broader implications for FPL managers, including which players might be worth targeting or avoiding based on their teams' motivations in remaining matches.

The Relegation Battle: A Two-Horse Race?

The central thesis of this discussion is that the relegation battle has narrowed to essentially two teams: Tottenham Hotspur and West Ham United. Both hosts expressed skepticism that Leeds United or Nottingham Forest would be dragged into the fight despite their relatively modest point totals. James articulated this clearly, stating, "I don't think Leeds and Forest are going to get dragged into it. I think it's going to be your team and my team."

Serge acknowledged his bias would be "negatively against West Ham" when discussing the topic, yet he still expressed genuine uncertainty about his club's prospects. He admitted, "I don't think West Ham will go down. And that's not what I want to believe. I don't like—I'm trying to use my head here and my heart's like, 'Stop being an idiot, mate.'" This internal conflict between emotional attachment and rational analysis runs throughout the discussion, adding depth to their predictions.

James was more definitive in his assessment, declaring, "I do think it's going to be Spurs that get relegated." He qualified this by acknowledging that "football's not played on spreadsheets, so luckily there's going to be some twists and turns." This recognition that predictions mean little in the chaotic world of Premier League football demonstrates the humility required when discussing relegation battles, where any team can beat any other on their day.

Tottenham's Managerial Uncertainty

A significant factor complicating Tottenham's situation is the tragic circumstance surrounding their manager. The hosts discussed how "it was Igor Tudor's father that passed away unfortunately um at the weekend," and the club is naturally giving the manager "some breathing space" during this difficult period. This creates an air of unpredictability around the club, as no one knows what will happen next in terms of leadership.

The hosts explored several managerial possibilities, with Roberto De Zerbi emerging as a prominent candidate. James noted that "on the Friday night prior to the Tottenham draw at Liverpool, there was lots of talk about Roberto De Zerby and that's come up again quite a bit last night." The situation appears to be that Derby County would be "open to taking the job, but ideally wants to take it in the summer because he doesn't want to manage in the Championship." There is now thought that Tottenham "may be approaching it to say, 'Look, we'd like you to do it. We want you to have the job. If we go down, you're free to walk away.'"

Adi Hutler was mentioned as another candidate, though Serge wisely noted that "people thankfully are calling him Addi in most circles rather than Adolf because that would be particularly unfortunate for Tottenham at the moment." The hosts agreed that these external candidates seem "more likely options than Tottenham going down a former player route" like Ryan Mason or Harry Redknapp.

James expressed skepticism about a managerial bounce helping Tottenham avoid relegation, stating, "I don't think there's going to be a managerial bounce that I should be scared of." He further explained that "the players are not good enough. So, whoever you put in is not going to be able to get spurs out of it through tactics or coaching. It's about spirit and fight and just get—like, you know, go revert to the simplest tactics and get an effort out of them is the only way I can see it for Spurs."

Historical Context and Points Analysis

To provide mathematical context for their predictions, Serge offered a comprehensive breakdown of historical Premier League survival points. He explained that "36 points has been enough to survive in 18 of the 30 seasons" since the Premier League began. The last time 36 points was insufficient was "back to 2015-16" when "you needed 38 points." Over the last decade, however, 36 points has consistently been enough to avoid relegation, though "we've had some anomalies in there like the last two years—27 and 26 points respectively would have been enough."

The current standings paint a worrying picture for the clubs at the bottom. As Serge detailed: "West Ham on 29, Tottenham 30, Forest 32, Leeds 33, Palace on 39, and obviously the bottom two, Burnley on 20, Wolves on 17." Goal difference could prove decisive, with West Ham's minus 21 significantly worse than Tottenham's minus 11, Forest's minus 10, and Leeds' minus 12. Serge noted this means "at the moment, that does feel like an effective extra point against you" for West Ham.

Predictions and Run-In Analysis

Both hosts offered specific predictions for how they believe the season will conclude. Serge explained his mathematical model: "by my maths, I have Tottenham finishing on 36 and West Ham finishing on 37." This would mean Tottenham going down while West Ham survive. He elaborated, "I don't see it being massively higher, but I do think 37/38 is where we're going to be to stay up this season. 36. Get above 36 and we might be there or thereabouts."

James offered his own prediction: "I've got West Ham beating Wolves and they're not winning again till Leeds on the last day of the season and in between that only picking up draws." This would see West Ham accumulate just enough points to survive while Tottenham would need to improve significantly on this trajectory.

The discussion also touched on pivotal fixtures that could determine the outcome. Serge suggested, "I think Leeds will win their home games against Wolves and Burnley. And if they do, then I think when they come to your place a draw, there's every chance the way the fixtures were a result for Leeds will be their safety." This would change "the complexity of that Tottenham-Leeds game because if they're coming there thinking if we get a point we're staying up like they're going to be well up for that fixture."

Key Takeaways for FPL Managers

The relegation battle is effectively between Tottenham and West Ham. Despite Leeds sitting on 33 points and Forest on 32, both hosts believe these clubs have sufficient breathing room to avoid the drop. This means FPL managers should focus on these two clubs when considering defensive options or transfers involving their players.

Points totals around 36-38 will likely determine survival. Historical data suggests 36 points has been sufficient in most seasons, though the margin for error is slim. FPL managers should monitor the run-in closely, particularly for teams hovering around this threshold.

Goal difference may prove decisive. West Ham's significantly worse goal difference (-21 compared to Tottenham's -11) means Tottenham could overtake West Ham even with the same point total. This adds another layer of complexity to predictions and could influence FPL decisions around players from both clubs.

Managerial changes could provide either a boost or disruption. Tottenham's potential managerial change creates unpredictability. A new manager might inspire a turnaround, or the transition could further destabilize the squad. FPL managers should monitor this situation closely.

Pivotal fixtures include Tottenham vs. Leeds. This match could determine whether Leeds feel safe enough to rotate their squad or must fight for survival. The outcome will significantly impact the relegation battle's complexity.
