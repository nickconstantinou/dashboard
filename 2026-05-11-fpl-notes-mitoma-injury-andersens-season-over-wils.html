Brighton Tactical Shifts and Injury Concerns: FPL Notes

Brighton's Early Dominance

Brighton produced a commanding start, establishing a 2-0 lead within the opening five minutes of the match. This early advantage set the stage for several notable individual performances that Fantasy Premier League managers will want to note.

Maxim De Cuyper's Impactful Return

Maxim De Cuyper made his first start since Gameweek 25, coming into the side at left-back. The £4.3m defender made an immediate impact, providing assists for Brighton's first two goals. His deliveries set up strikes for Jack Hinshelwood and Lewis Dunk, making him a potentially valuable asset for FPL managers seeking budget options in defence.

Jack Hinshelwood's Goal Form

Jack Hinshelwood continued his impressive scoring streak with another goal in this match. The £5.1m midfielder now has three goals in as many matches, demonstrating excellent recent form that Fantasy managers may want to consider.

Yankuba Minteh's Positional Adjustment

Following a substitution, Yankuba Minteh (£5.5m) switched from his natural right-wing position to the left wing. This positional flexibility could prove relevant for FPL planning, as Minteh may see game time in multiple attacking roles depending on match situations and other team selections.

Kaoru Mitoma Injury Update

Brighton lost Kaoru Mitoma (£6.1m) to injury after 58 minutes of the match. The injury has been identified as a hamstring issue. Manager Fabian Hurzeler responded by bringing Joel Veltman (£4.3m) into the match. Ferdi Kadioglu (£4.4m), who had been playing at right-back, shifted forward to fill the right-wing role.

Season-End Risk for Mitoma

Given the hamstring nature of Mitoma's injury, there is a possibility that even a minor issue could result in the Japanese international missing the remainder of the season. FPL managers holding Mitoma should monitor Brighton's injury announcements carefully for updates on his expected recovery timeline.
