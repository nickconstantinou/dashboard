# FPL Gameweek 32 Team News: West Ham Injury Updates Provide Early Boost for Managers

## The Full Story

Fantasy Premier League managers received encouraging news ahead of Gameweek 32 as West Ham United manager Nuno Espirito Santo delivered positive injury updates during his Wednesday press conference. The Hammers face Wolverhampton Wanderers on Friday evening, making this early team news particularly valuable for FPL managers weighing up their squads for the upcoming gameweek.

Three key players—winger Crysencio Summerville, defender Jeanclair Todibo, and striker Callum Wilson—all sat out Sunday's FA Cup quarter-final against Leeds United but are now progressing well in their recovery. Nuno confirmed all three are improving and expressed optimism about their availability for Friday's fixture. "We are positive that they can be available," Nuno stated, while also revealing that Konstantinos Mavropanos has cleared the concussion protocol and is fit to play.

Additionally, Aaron Wan-Bissaka missed the Leeds game due to a late return from international duty with DR Congo, where the national team achieved a significant milestone in World Cup qualifying. Nuno indicated the right-back has returned and is in good condition, likely available for the Wolves match. Meanwhile, goalkeeper Alphonse Areola and defender Axel Disasi both struggled towards the end of the Leeds match, but Nuno attributed their issues to fatigue from the extra-time encounter rather than injury.

## Why This Matters

For FPL managers, this team news carries substantial implications. West Ham assets—particularly Summerville, Wilson, and Todibo—could prove valuable differential picks if they return for the Friday match. With Gameweek 32 spanning multiple days, early confirmation of availability allows managers to make informed transfer decisions before the deadline.

The timing is critical: West Ham's Friday kickoff means their assets are among the first to be locked in each gameweek, making injury news particularly impactful for captaincy selections and chip strategies. Additionally, Mavropanos' return from concussion provides defensive options at a time when clean sheet points are valuable for title contenders.
