FPL Gameweek 35 Wildcard: 3 of the best drafts

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

The Wildcard Landscape

According to Fantasy Football Scout research, over 30% of FPL players surveyed are planning to Wildcard in Gameweek 35. This high adoption rate suggests managers see significant value in restructuring their squads at this stage of the season.

Arsenal: The Common Thread

An Arsenal triple-up emerges as a consistent feature across all three Wildcard draft teams. This concentration reflects the favourable run of fixtures and the defensive stability that Arsenal have offered throughout the campaign. Managers using their Wildcard in Gameweek 35 appear to be prioritising Gunners assets as foundational pieces of their squads.

Leeds United Defensive Options

Among budget defensive options, Pascal Struijk stands out as probably the safest centre-half at Leeds at present, priced at £4.3m. His consistent place in the starting eleven makes him a reliable option for managers seeking cheap defensive coverage.

For those needing an even cheaper route into the Leeds defence at £3.9m, James Justin becomes relevant. His inclusion, however, is contingent on Gabriel Gudmundsson being ruled out, meaning managers should monitor team news before finalising their Wildcard selections.

Aston Villa: Timing the Entry and Exit

Aston Villa assets present a nuanced strategic decision. Morgan Rogers, priced at £7.5m, offers short-term value for the next two Gameweeks. After this window, managers are advised to consider benching or shipping him out ahead of Gameweek 37 or 38.

This advice aligns with Villa's broader fixture situation. Their fixtures stiffen after Gameweek 36, and the club could have a Europa League final scheduled between Gameweek 37 and 38. This fixture congestion raises concerns about rotation and intensity.

The recommended trajectory involves holding Ollie Watkins (£8.8m) through Gameweek 36, then potentially selling him for assets such as Bowen or Igor Thiago afterward. This approach captures Villa's remaining favourable fixtures while avoiding the uncertain period that follows.

Double Gameweek 36 Considerations

The source indicates that, on the condition that Manchester City and Crystal Palace receive a Double Gameweek 36, both teams merit attention. Palace assets may offer particular value given Oliver Glasner's apparent reluctance to rotate his defence, even around UEFA Conference League commitments. This consistency could prove valuable across two matches.

Gameweek 38 Endgame Planning

For managers planning beyond Gameweek 36, Everton and Fulham present decent Gameweek 38 fixtures against Tottenham and Newcastle respectively. These options may appeal to managers seeking clean sheet potential in the season's closing fixtures.
