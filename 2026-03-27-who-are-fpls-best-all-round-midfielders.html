Who are FPL’s best all-round midfielders?

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

The Full Story

Fantasy Premier League managers have received new guidance for identifying premium midfield assets heading into the crucial final stretch of the 2025/26 season. Fantasy Football Scout published an analysis on March 26, 2026, identifying midfielders who deliver across multiple scoring categories—making them particularly valuable given the position's inherent advantages (goals, assists, set-pieces, and penalties). The analysis incorporates the newly introduced Defensive Contribution (DefCon) points system, which has expanded the pool of viable midfield options by rewarding players who regularly contribute defensively alongside their attacking output.

The comprehensive assessment examined midfielders meeting a minimum threshold of 1,700 minutes played throughout the campaign. Analysts evaluated players on a per-90-minutes basis across eight key metrics: shots, attempts inside the box, chances created (CC), big chances created (BCC), corners, open-play crosses, expected goal involvement (xGI), and DefCon action (where reaching 12 defensive contribution points in a single match earns a two-point bonus). A supplementary six-match form guide highlighted players maintaining consistent performance across multiple categories, with those performing well indicated in green.

The analysis explicitly excluded injured midfielders Justin Kluivert (£6.9m) and Bruno Guimarães (£6.8m), who have been unavailable since Gameweeks 20 and 26 respectively. However, the detailed player rankings and specific statistical breakdowns remain behind Fantasy Football Scout's paywall, accessible only to Chief Scout Members.

Why This Matters

For the millions of FPL managers worldwide, identifying "all-round" midfielders represents a strategic edge in a game where differential picks often separate top-flight finishes from the pack. The introduction of DefCon points has fundamentally shifted FPL strategy by valuing defensive contributions that previously went unrewarded for outfield players—meaning midfielders who combine attacking threat with regular tackles, interceptions, or blocks now offer enhanced value compared to pure forwards or purely defensive players.

With the season approaching its decisive phase, managers face critical transfer decisions where selecting players with multiple scoring routes—rather than those dependent on a single metric—reduces variance and provides steadier returns. The article's focus on players excelling across the widest range of categories reflects a broader FPL philosophy: assets who can score through various means become increasingly valuable when individual attacking categories become harder to predict.

The timing is particularly significant given that the 2025/26 season is nearing its conclusion, meaning every transfer window represents a limited number of remaining gameweeks where accumulated points compound toward final rankings.
