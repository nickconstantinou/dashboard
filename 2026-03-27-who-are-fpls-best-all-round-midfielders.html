The Best All-Round Midfielders in Fantasy Premier League for the 2025/26 Season

FantasyFootballScout.co.uk Identifies Top Midfield Options with Multiple Points Avenues as Season Reaches Critical Stage

Fantasy Premier League managers seeking an edge in the final stretch of the 2025/26 season have received guidance on which midfielders offer the most comprehensive attacking and defensive contributions. FantasyFootballScout.co.uk published an analysis on March 26, 2026, identifying the best all-round midfielders who provide multiple routes to points—including goals, assists, set-pieces, penalties, and now defensive contributions following the introduction of the DefCon points system to FPL.

The analysis examined midfielders who have played a minimum of 1,700 minutes across the campaign, evaluating their per-90-minute performance across several key metrics: shots, attempts inside the box, chances created (CC), big chances created (BCC), corners, open-play crosses, expected goal involvement (xGI), and DefCon action. The publication also assessed recent form by looking at players who performed consistently across multiple categories over their last six appearances. Two notable absences from the shortlist were injured duo Justin Kluivert (£6.9m) and Bruno Guimaraes (£6.8m), who were removed from consideration after neither had featured since Gameweeks 20 and 26 respectively.

---

Why This Matters: The Strategic Value of Multi-Dimensional Assets

The introduction of DefCon points has fundamentally altered FPL strategy by expanding what constitutes a valuable midfield asset. Previously, FPL managers prioritized midfielders primarily for attacking returns—goals and assists. Now, defensive contributions such as tackles, interceptions, and blocks that accumulate to a threshold of 12 in a single match earn players an additional two-point reward. This means midfielders who contribute meaningfully at both ends of the pitch have become significantly more valuable, as they offer a safety net even when failing to register attacking points.

For FPL managers competing in mini-leagues and overall rankings, identifying players with "multiple routes to points" represents a key competitive advantage. These all-rounders reduce variance—on weeks when they don't score or assist, their defensive work may still generate meaningful returns. The timing of this analysis is particularly significant, as the 2025/26 season approaches its conclusion, making every transfer decision increasingly impactful on final rankings.

    Context: The Evolution of FPL Valuation

    This analysis reflects a broader trend in Fantasy Premier League strategy where the traditional binary of "attacking midfielder versus defensive midfielder" has become increasingly obsolete. The DefCon system, introduced to reward defensive work from outfield players, has effectively created a new category of FPL asset: the complete midfielder who can accumulate points through multiple channels.

    For budget-conscious managers, identifying all-rounders among mid-tier pricing (typically £6.5m-£7.5m) allows for salary cap efficiency—spending higher funds on premium forwards or goalkeepers while maintaining a reliable midfield presence. The article's focus on per-90 metrics rather than total season stats also accounts for players who may have missed games through rotation or injury, providing a truer picture of their underlying performance quality.
