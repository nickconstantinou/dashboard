Who Are FPL's Best All-Round Midfielders? A Complete Guide for Fantasy Managers

The Full Story

Fantasy Premier League managers have a new dimension to consider when selecting midfielders this season, thanks to the introduction of DefCon (defensive contribution) points to the game. This scoring addition has expanded the pool of viable midfield options beyond traditional goal-scorers and assist providers, creating opportunities for players who contribute across multiple areas of the pitch. Fantasy Football Scout's analysis for March 26, 2026 identifies the best all-rounders in midfield—players who deliver consistently across attacking metrics AND defensive contributions, making them particularly valuable assets for the crucial final stretch of the 2025/26 season.

The analysis employs a comprehensive methodology examining two timeframes. For season-long evaluation, midfielders must have logged a minimum of 1,700 minutes played to qualify, then assessed on a per-90-minutes basis across eight key categories: shots, attempts inside the box, chances created (CC), big chances created (BCC), corners, open-play crosses, expected goal involvement (xGI), and DefCon action. For recent form, the focus narrows to the last six match appearances, with consistently strong performers highlighted in green across multiple categories.

Notably, injured duo Justin Kluivert (£6.9m) and Bruno Guimaraes (£6.8m) have been removed from recent-form consideration, having not featured since Gameweeks 20 and 26 respectively. While their omission narrows the field, it also underscores the importance of selecting available players who maintain consistent output. The specific identities of the highest-ranking all-rounders remain in the paywalled Members Area, requiring Chief Scout membership to access.

Why This Matters

The DefCon introduction represents a fundamental shift in FPL strategy that many managers may not yet be maximizing. Since FPL awards midfielders 5 points per goal (compared to 4 for forwards), identifying players who combine attacking threat with defensive contributions creates a significant points-per-price advantage. A midfielder delivering clean sheet points, DefCon bonuses, AND attacking returns essentially offers three simultaneous routes to points—a proposition that can separate a manager's team from the pack during the run-in.

For the broader FPL community, this analysis matters because the final stretch of the season is when rank positions are won and lost. With most managers having similar squad values and wildcard chips exhausted, gaining marginal advantages through optimal player selection becomes critical. All-rounders provide both safety (multiple point-scoring avenues) and ceiling (potential for explosive returns across categories). The Fantasy Football Scout methodology—combining long-term reliability with recent form—offers a data-driven approach to identifying which of these multi-dimensional assets are actually performing now versus those whose good stats may be skewed by earlier-season output.
