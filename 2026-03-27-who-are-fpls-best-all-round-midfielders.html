Who are FPL’s best all-round midfielders?

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

The Full Story

The introduction of DefCon (defensive contribution) points to Fantasy Premier League has fundamentally shifted the landscape for midfield selection heading into the crucial final stretch of the 2025/26 season. Fantasy Football Scout's analysis reveals that several midfielders are now delivering consistent value across multiple statistical categories, making them particularly attractive assets for managers seeking to maximize returns during the season's most important period. The DefCon system, which awards a two-point bonus when a player reaches 12 defensive contribution actions in a single match, has opened the door for players who previously lacked the attacking output to justify their price tags.

The comprehensive analysis identifies midfielders who have accumulated a minimum of 1,700 minutes played throughout the campaign, then evaluates their per-90 performance across eight key categories: shots, attempts inside the box, chances created (CC), big chances created (BCC), corners, open-play crosses, expected goal involvement (xGI), and DefCon action. This multi-dimensional approach allows FPL managers to identify players who offer multiple routes to points rather than relying on a single source of returns.

Two notable players have been removed from immediate consideration due to injuries: Justin Kluivert (£6.9m) and Bruno Guimaraes (£6.8m). Kluivert has not featured since Gameweek 20, while Guimaraes has been absent since Gameweek 26, leaving managers who own these assets in difficult positions as the season reaches its climax. The analysis also includes a more granular six-match form review, with players highlighted in green demonstrating consistent performance across multiple categories.

Why This Matters

For the millions of FPL managers worldwide, the DefCon introduction represents one of the most significant rule changes in recent FPL history. Previously, pure defensive midfielders or defensive-minded wide players were largely ignored despite their consistent minutes, because they offered minimal attacking returns. Now, players who combine defensive industry with any level of attacking output become viable premium or mid-price options, fundamentally altering team construction strategies.

The timing of this analysis is critical. With the 2025/26 season approaching its decisive phase, managers face crucial decisions about squad composition, free transfers, and wildcard usage. Identifying all-round midfielders who can deliver points through multiple channels—whether goals, assists, clean sheets, or DefCon bonuses—provides a significant edge in a game where millions of managers compete and small percentage gains translate to meaningful rank improvements.

For the broader FPL community, this analysis highlights the increasing sophistication of FPL strategy. No longer can managers simply stack their squads with the highest-priced forwards and attackers. The position change for certain players, combined with DefCon opportunities, means midfield has become arguably the most strategic position to navigate. Managers who understand these nuances will have a structural advantage over those relying on traditional selection criteria.
