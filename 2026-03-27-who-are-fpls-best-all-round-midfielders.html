The Best All-Round Midfielders in FPL: A Complete Guide for the 2025/26 Season Stretch Run

Fantasy Premier League managers looking to gain an edge in the final weeks of the 2025/26 season now have an expanded pool of midfield options to consider, thanks to a significant rule change that has reshaped the position's value proposition. Fantasy Football Scout's analysis identifies midfielders who deliver across multiple statistical categories—including the newly-introduced defensive contribution (DefCon) points—as the most valuable assets heading into the season's decisive phase.

The DefCon Revolution: How FPL Midfielders Got More Valuable

The introduction of DefCon points to Fantasy Premier League has fundamentally altered the calculus for midfield investments. Previously, FPL managers focused primarily on attacking metrics—goals, assists, and chances created—when evaluating midfielders. Now, players who consistently contribute defensively while maintaining their offensive output have become significantly more attractive. The DefCon system rewards players who reach 12 defensive actions in a single match with a two-point bonus, effectively giving managers two ways to earn points from the same player.

This addition has elevated several midfield candidates into "must-have" territory, according to the analysis from Fantasy Football Scout. The position already provided inherent advantages—midfielders score goals and assists at the same rate as forwards while costing less, and many handle set-pieces and penalties. Now, with defensive contributions factored in, certain midfielders offer even more routes to points than many premium forwards.

Methodology: Identifying True All-Rounders

The analysis employs a rigorous statistical framework to isolate players who genuinely excel across multiple dimensions rather than specialists in just one area. To qualify for consideration, midfielders must have logged a minimum of 1,700 minutes across the season—ensuring the sample size is large enough to reflect consistent performance rather than small-sample luck or hot streaks.

The per-90-minute metrics evaluated include:

  • Shots: Total shooting attempts
  • Attempts inside the box: Quality scoring opportunities
  • Chances created (CC): Passes leading directly to shots
  • Big chances created (BCC): High-quality chances for teammates
  • Corners: Set-piece delivery opportunities
  • Open-play crosses: Crossing from general play
  • Expected goal involvement (xGI): Combined expected goals and assists
  • DefCon action: Defensive contributions earning bonus points

    • Players who perform consistently well across multiple categories represent the ideal FPL asset—they can accumulate points through various means, reducing reliance on any single avenue that might dry up from match to match.

    For recent form, the analysis examines performance over the last six matchweeks, with players highlighted in green indicating those who have coped well across multiple statistical categories. This recent performance snapshot helps managers identify players entering form at the crucial stage of the season.

    Why This Matters for FPL Managers

    The FPL community has grown into a multi-million player ecosystem where strategic advantage translates directly to satisfaction and competitive standing. The introduction of DefCon points represents one of the most significant mechanical changes in recent FPL history, creating a structural advantage for managers who quickly identified and exploited the new dynamics. Midfielders who were previously overlooked—budget options known more for defensive work than creative output—suddenly became viable picks, expanding the player pool and creating value opportunities.

    For the 2025/26 season specifically, the final stretch presents unique challenges. With European competitions, domestic cup runs, and accumulated fatigue affecting team selections across the league, managers need players who maintain consistent output regardless of circumstantial rotation. All-rounders who contribute across attacking and defensive metrics provide a floor beneath their ceiling—they may not hit double-figure hauls every week, but they're unlikely to post zeros either.

    The analysis also highlights a broader FPL truth: the most valuable assets are rarely those who specialize in a single category, but rather those who stack multiple point-scoring opportunities. A midfielder taking corners, penalty kicks, and now accumulating DefCon points while maintaining decent xGI represents extraordinary value at their price point.
