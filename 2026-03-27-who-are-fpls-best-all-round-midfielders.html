FPL's Best All-Round Midfielders: Complete Analysis

The Full Story

The introduction of DefCon (Defensive Contribution) points to Fantasy Premier League has fundamentally reshaped how managers evaluate midfield assets, according to a comprehensive analysis from Fantasy Football Scout. The new defensive metric has expanded the pool of viable midfielders by rewarding players who contribute meaningfully in defensive actions, adding a completely new dimension to the position that already provided multiple routes to points through goals, assists, set-pieces, and penalties.

The analysis identifies midfielders who perform consistently across numerous statistical categories over the 2025/26 season, using a minimum threshold of 1,700 minutes played to ensure statistical reliability. The study tracks players per 90 minutes across eight key metrics: shots, attempts inside the box, chances created (CC), big chances created (BCC), corners, open-play crosses, expected goal involvement (xGI), and DefCon action. DefCon specifically rewards players who reach 12 defensive contributions in a single match with a two-point bonus, creating incentive to target well-rounded performers rather than specialists.

Recent form analysis over the last six matches highlights which players have maintained consistency across multiple categories, with injured duo Justin Kluivert (£6.9m) and Bruno Guimaraes (£6.8m) removed from consideration after last featuring in Gameweeks 20 and 26 respectively. The analysis uses color-coded indicators to show which midfielders have coped well across numerous statistical categories during this recent window.

Why This Matters

The DefCon addition represents one of the most significant mechanical changes to FPL in recent memory, fundamentally altering the value proposition for midfield assets. Previously, FPL managers often prioritized forwards who offered consistent goal threat while midfielders without penalty-taking duties or exceptional assist numbers struggled to justify their price tags. The defensive contribution metric changes this calculus entirely, making previously overlooked midfielders suddenly attractive as they can now accumulate points through multiple channels.

For FPL managers targeting the final stretch of the 2025/26 season, identifying these all-round performers is crucial for maximizing points during the run-in. The article targets this specific audience—those looking to optimize their squads for the season's decisive phase when every point matters significantly in mini-leagues and overall rankings. The comprehensive per-90-minute approach accounts for differing game times and substitution patterns, providing a fair comparison across players regardless of their total minutes accumulated.
