FPL's Best All-Round Midfielders: A Complete Guide for Fantasy Managers

The Full Story

Fantasy Premier League managers now have additional midfielder options to consider following the introduction of DefCon (defensive contribution) points to the game. This new scoring category has expanded the pool of viable midfield assets, as the position already offers multiple point-scoring avenues including goals, assists, set-pieces, and penalty duties. FantasyFootballScout's analysis identifies midfielders who consistently deliver across multiple statistical categories, making them particularly valuable for the final stretch of the 2025/26 season.

The analysis employs a comprehensive methodology, examining midfielders who have played a minimum of 1,700 minutes across the campaign. These players are evaluated on a per-90-minutes basis across eight key categories: shots, attempts inside the box, chances created (CC), big chances created (BCC), corners, open-play crosses, expected goal involvement (xGI), and DefCon action. Players reaching 12 DefCon points in a single match receive a two-point reward, adding another potential points stream for well-rounded midfielders.

For near-term insights, the analysis also highlights players who have performed consistently across multiple categories in their last six appearances, with these performers marked in green for easy identification. Two notable midfielders—Justin Kluivert (£6.9m) and Bruno Guimaraes (£6.8m)—have been excluded from recent-form analysis due to injuries, having not featured since Gameweeks 20 and 26 respectively. Why This Matters

The introduction of DefCon points represents a significant shift in FPL strategy, particularly for midfield selections. Traditionally, managers faced a trade-off between premium attacking midfielders and budget options who might offer defensive security. Now, players who contribute at both ends of the pitch have become substantially more valuable, potentially offering returns across multiple scoring categories in a single match.

This development matters because it changes the fundamental value calculation for midfield assets. A midfielder who registers a goal, an assist, and enough defensive actions to trigger DefCon rewards could significantly outperform a pure attacker with equivalent attacking returns. For managers navigating the business end of the season, identifying these all-round contributors could mean the difference between climbing ranks and stagnation.

The timing is particularly crucial given we're in the "final stretch" of the 2025/26 season, when squad management and strategic transfers become paramount. Managers who adapt their midfield strategy to account for DefCon benefits may gain a competitive edge during this critical period.
