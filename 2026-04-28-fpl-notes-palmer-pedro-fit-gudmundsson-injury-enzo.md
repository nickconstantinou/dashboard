# FPL Notes: Chelsea Attackers Fit, Leeds Injury Concerns

## Chelsea: Palmer and Pedro Return to Fitness

Cole Palmer (£10.5m) has returned to fitness for Chelsea ahead of Gameweek 35. The England international was brought on as a 71st-minute substitute in a recent match and notably received a yellow card for time-wasting during his brief appearance. Both Palmer and Joao Pedro (£7.6m) are now confirmed fit and available for selection.

Pedro showed impressive form upon his return from injury. The Brazilian forward lasted almost the entire match, coming off only in injury time. His performance was highlighted by strong hold-up play and a consistent goal threat, including a first-half effort that struck the inside of the post. Chelsea appeared a more effective attacking side with Pedro leading the line.

## Enzo Fernandez Emerges Under New Management

Enzo Fernandez (£6.5m) delivered a goalscoring performance in Chelsea's recent fixture. The Argentine international operated in the number 10 role and was awarded the captain's armband by caretaker manager Calum McFarlane. This elevation in responsibility may signal continued opportunities for Fernandez moving forward.

## Fixture Schedule Note

Chelsea's progression to the FA Cup final creates a scheduling conflict with Gameweek 37. The Wembley final will take place on the same weekend as their scheduled Premier League fixture against Tottenham, meaning that match is now postponed. FPL managers holding Chelsea assets should note this blank when planning their squads.

## Leeds: Gudmundsson Injury Casts Doubt Over Availability

Gabriel Gudmundsson (£3.8m) is facing an extended period on the sidelines. The Swedish international suffered a hamstring injury that appears more serious than initially suspected cramp. Manager Daniel Farke declined to provide a specific timeframe for his return, describing the situation as "not great news" when speaking to media.

This development could have knock-on effects for Leeds' defensive structure. James Justin (£3.9m) and Jaka Bijol (£3.9m) both required substitution at half-time in a recent match. If Gudmundsson is ruled out, Justin is expected to shift to the left wing-back position, potentially affecting his clean sheet prospects.

## Stach Returns Early From Injury

Anton Stach (£4.8m) made a welcome comeback for Leeds, returning from a ligament injury earlier than originally anticipated. The midfielder was brought on at half-time and made an immediate impact, testing the opposition goalkeeper with a powerful strike. His earlier-than-expected return provides Leeds with additional midfield options during the crucial run-in.

## Leeds Survival Hopes

Leeds have the opportunity to move significantly closer to Premier League safety this weekend. A home victory against Burnley combined with a Tottenham defeat at Aston Villa would leave the club almost mathematically secure of their top-flight status for next season.

Tactically, Leeds adjusted their formation during a recent match, switching from a 3-4-2-1 to a 4-3-3 at half-time. Joe Rodon partnered Pascal Struijk at centre-back in this revised setup, suggesting Farke may continue to utilise this formation depending on availability and opponent requirements.
