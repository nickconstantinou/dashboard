## The Two Chip Strategy Paths: Which Route Will You Take?

Harry identifies two primary chip strategy frameworks that FPL managers are likely deploying in the closing weeks of the season, with the outcome of the upcoming FA Cup fixture between Liverpool and Manchester City serving as the primary determinant of which path makes more sense.

**Option One: Wildcard 32, Bench Boost 33, Free Hit 34**

This strategy assumes that Manchester City will defeat Liverpool in the FA Cup semi-final, effectively eliminating that fixture from creating a midweek clash. Under this scenario, gameweek 32 becomes a standard gameweek with no complications, gameweek 33 emerges as the major double of the season (with Arsenal, Chelsea, Manchester City, and Crystal Palace all currently predicted to double), and managers would deploy their Wildcard on gameweek 32 to build a squad optimized for the gameweek 33 double, then use the Bench Boost during that double before deploying the Free Hit in gameweek 34.

**Option Two: Free Hit 33, Wildcard 35, Bench Boost 36**

This alternative becomes more attractive if Liverpool defeat Manchester City in the FA Cup. When a Premier League team progresses in the FA Cup, their scheduled league fixture often gets postponed, creating ripple effects throughout the fixture list. If Liverpool advance and Manchester City exit, the fixture list reshuffles in ways that could push the biggest doubles into later gameweeks—hence why this strategy frontloads the Free Hit to gameweek 33, uses the Wildcard a week later when the landscape is clearer, and saves the Bench Boost for the revised double in gameweek 36.

Harry is careful to note that "this is not an exact science" because even if Manchester City win, some managers might still prefer Option Two based on their specific squad composition and risk tolerance. Similarly, a Liverpool victory doesn't obligate anyone to follow Option One. "But that generally is what one of the big deciding factors is around chip strategy," Harry explains, "because that will dictate what we expect to see for predicted doubles and blanks later on in the season."

## The Newcastle Dilemma: Why Premium Picks Don't Always Fit Your Plan

One of the most valuable insights Harry offers concerns the current popularity of Newcastle players—particularly talents like Miguel Almirón and Anthony Gordon—and why blindly following popular picks could actually harm your season.

Newcastle does indeed have favorable fixtures: a home game against Sunderland in gameweek 31, followed by Crystal Palace away in gameweek 32, and then a double in gameweek 33. On the surface, this looks like an ideal profile for any FPL manager looking to maximize returns. However, Harry urges managers to think about their broader chip strategy rather than individual gameweek optimization.

"If we end up on a chip strategy of option two where we free hit 33, wildcard 35, Newcastle players are not actually very useful to you because of course they have a good fixture this week. Then it's Sunderland at home. Then it's Crystal Palace away in gameweek 32. Gameweek 33 they're double—you'd free hit. So it doesn't even matter in gameweek 34 the blank. They don't have a fixture."

This logic is devastating in its simplicity: if your chip strategy involves Free Hitting in gameweek 33, Newcastle's double that week becomes irrelevant because you're already deploying your Free Hit to load up on other doubled teams. Their gameweek 34 blank means they provide zero value that week regardless of your plans. And if you're saving your Wildcard for gameweek 35 rather than 32, Newcastle's fixtures in 32 and 33 become much less strategically valuable.

Harry's team reflects this philosophy. "When I look at my team, I'm not forcing myself to buy Newcastle players this week because actually they don't really fit that well unless you go with strategy one." The takeaway is clear: buy Newcastle players only if you've already committed to Option One. Otherwise, you're spending transfers on assets whose value depends on a chip strategy you might not even use.

## The Golden Rule: Wait for the FA Cup

Harry's single most emphatic point comes when discussing Wildcard 32 strategy. "Please please please please do not make any early Wildcard 32 transfers until after the FA Cup," he pleads, underlining the importance with repetition. This matters because the FA Cup results will reshape everything.

The timeline reveals why patience is so critical. After the gameweek 31 deadline, the international break arrives, followed by the FA Cup fixtures, and then—three weeks from now—gameweek 32 begins. That midweek between the FA Cup and gameweek 32 is when the Premier League will announce confirmed doubles and blanks. "Please don't make any transfers until those FA Cup results are out of the way because if something happens that you don't expect and doubles and blanks go into weeks you don't think they will, it will change people's chip strategies."

The potential downside—locking yourself into a chip strategy that no longer makes sense after the FA Cup results—could cost far more. Harry's advice is categorical: "try and be as flexible as you can."
