FPL notes: Palace “tank was empty” + Kroupi praise

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

Bournemouth moved into the top six and European places with a routine 3-0 win over Crystal Palace at Vitality Stadium.

Match Summary

An own goal and a penalty from Eli Junior Kroupi (£4.6m) put the Cherries in control. Rayan's (£5.4m) smart finish confirmed the win in the second half. Bournemouth extended their unbeaten run to 15 Premier League games in the process.

Palace's First-Half Struggles

What unfolded was a really poor display from Crystal Palace, especially in the first half. The Eagles failed to register an attempt on goal during the opening 45 minutes. Manager Oliver Glasner made five alterations to his Palace line-up, with one eye on the second leg of their UEFA Conference League semi-final.

Glasner Explains Defeat

After the match, Glasner attributed the poor performance to fatigue. "Today the tank was empty. We could see it," he said. "We couldn't get the turnaround from Thursday evening." Palace had faced Shakhtar in the Conference League semi-final first leg on Thursday night.

Player Availability

Will Hughes (£4.7m) was unavailable due to illness. Normally Hughes would have started, but his illness ruled him out of the starting XI. Borna Sosa (£4.8m) also missed the match with a minor injury. Chadi Riad (£4.0m) was withdrawn during the game due to cramp.
