# What's Left to Play for in 2025/26 – and How It Could Affect FPL

With the Premier League season reaching its business end, there are still significant matters to be decided across the division. There are four Gameweeks of FPL remaining in the 2025/26 season, and several competing narratives could influence squad decisions in the final stretch.

## The Title Race

The battle for the Premier League title remains tight. Manchester City overtook Arsenal a few days later, based on goals scored. However, the Gunners took advantage of City's cup duties and could be six points ahead by the time Pep Guardiola's side next play on Monday night. This gap could prove pivotal depending on results in the coming Gameweeks.

## European Qualification Battle

A crowded battle for European spots continues to unfold. Right now, four points separate seven sides in the race for continental qualification. It's looking almost certain that Manchester United, Liverpool and Aston Villa will finish in the top five. However, sixth place could also mean Champions League qualification, depending on how Villa fare in the Europa League semis and potential final. This adds an extra dimension to the race for European positions.

## Fixture Congestion Concerns

Aston Villa have a two-legged Europa League semi-final coming, as well as a potential final in between Gameweeks 37 and 38. This scheduling creates uncertainty around squad rotation for Villa players, which FPL managers may need to consider when making final selections. Additionally, Manchester City and Chelsea meet at Wembley on the weekend of Gameweek 37 for the FA Cup final, which could impact both clubs' Premier League preparations and availability.

## Relegation Battle

At the foot of the table, the survival race intensifies. Last weekend's events have dragged Leeds United back into the equation, while the previously unthinkable idea of Tottenham Hotspur getting relegated is starting to feel real. FPL managers holding assets from these clubs may need to assess rotation risks and fixture difficulty in the remaining Gameweeks.

## Implications for FPL Strategy

With multiple competitions still in play, managers should factor in potential squad rotation, especially for clubs involved in cup competitions. The title race remains alive, European spots are tightly contested, and the relegation battle could see unexpected movements. Careful consideration of these competing priorities could prove decisive in the final Gameweeks.
