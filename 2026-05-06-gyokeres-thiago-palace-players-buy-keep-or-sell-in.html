Gyokeres, Thiago, Palace players: Buy, keep or sell in Gameweek 36?

Viktor Gyokeres: Buy

Viktor Gyokeres enters Gameweek 36 in excellent form, having contributed five goals across his last eight matches in all competitions. His attacking output has positioned him as the third-most transferred-in player ahead of this gameweek, reflecting the confidence Fantasy managers have placed in the Sweden international.

Gyokeres' underlying numbers reinforce his strong statistical profile. His minutes per goal ratio ranks as the fifth-best in the division this season among players with a minimum of 500 minutes played. Perhaps most notably for the upcoming fixture, over 85% of his strikes this campaign have come against teams currently positioned in the bottom half of the table.

Arsenal face a favourable run of fixtures, with West Ham United, Burnley and Crystal Palace their next three opponents, all of whom find themselves in the bottom six positions. This fixture congestion makes Gyokeres a compelling buy option for managers seeking forward coverage.

Igor Thiago: Keep

Igor Thiago remains Erling Haaland's closest rival for the Premier League Golden Boot heading into Gameweek 36. Despite being the second-most transferred-out player of the gameweek so far, his underlying metrics continue to suggest he deserves continued faith from Fantasy managers.

Thiago demonstrated his clinical edge by netting his 22nd Premier League goal in Saturday's 3-0 victory over West Ham. His recent form in front of goal has been backed by chance creation, with seven 'big chances' falling his way across his last six matches.

Perhaps most impressively, Thiago leads all players for expected goals (xG) in home matches this season, highlighting his dominance when playing on familiar turf. With Liverpool away on the horizon—a side who have kept just one clean sheet in their last eight league matches—holding onto Thiago represents a sound strategy for managers still in Golden Boot contention.

Crystal Palace Players: Sell or Avoid

Maxence Lacroix carries a verified DefCon success rate of 62.5% over the season at his current price of £5.2m. While this figure represents decent defensive involvement, the broader context of Crystal Palace's season complicates any recommendation to buy or hold their defenders.

Sarr and Mateta have been reduced to bench appearances in three of their last four Gameweeks, significantly limiting their Fantasy utility. For those considering Palace assets further forward, Daniel Munoz presents the higher-upside pick, having already accumulated seven attacking returns this season—though his underlying numbers warrant individual assessment before commitment.

Overall, with Brentford sitting seventh and Palace's attacking options in inconsistent rotation, selling or avoiding Palace players appears the prudent approach heading into Gameweek 36.
