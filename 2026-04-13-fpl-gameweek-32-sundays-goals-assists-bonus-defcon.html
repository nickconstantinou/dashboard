The Sunday fixtures of FPL Gameweek 32 delivered a mixed bag for managers weighing up their transfers and captain choices heading into a pivotal double gameweek. Four matches took place, with some unexpected results that will reshape the landscape for those chasing or defending their mini-league positions.

Chelsea 0-3 Manchester City

Manchester City brushed aside Chelsea with a commanding 3-0 victory. City players picked up substantial bonus points, with goalscorers and clean sheet contributors stacking up the returns. For FPL managers, City coverage remains essential heading into a favourable run of fixtures.

Sunderland 1-0 Tottenham Hotspur

Sunderland pulled off a surprise home win against Tottenham, handing the Lilywhites a rare blank. This result will sting managers who backed Tottenham assets, particularly those relying on their defence for clean sheet points.

Nottingham Forest 1-1 Aston Villa

A tight contest ended level at the City Ground. Both clubs picked up a point, with goals and assists shared between Forest and Aston Villa players. Defensively, this fixture delivered little for those prioritizing clean sheet points.

Crystal Palace 2-1 Newcastle United

Palace completed a memorable afternoon with a home win over Newcastle. Palace attackers profited from the clean sheet opportunity and goals, while Newcastle assets will be a concern for managers reassessing their squads.
