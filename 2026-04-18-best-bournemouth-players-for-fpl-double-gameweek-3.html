Bournemouth's Double Gameweek 33: Who to Trust in FPL

Bournemouth face the two most inviting fixtures of any Double Gameweek 33 side—and yet the FPL case for the Cherries isn't straightforward. Yes, Newcastle are in freefall, and yes, Leeds represent a manageable home test. But with Andoni Iraola showing a pattern of aggressive rotation on double weeks, picking the right Cherries asset requires knowing where the risk ends and the reward begins.

The Fixtures

Bournemouth's doubleheader pairs a trip to St James' Park with a home game against Leeds. Neither opponent inspires dread.

Newcastle have managed just one clean sheet in their last 18 matches across all competitions, and a three-week international break did nothing to arrest their decline. Their Gameweek 32 defeat at Selhurst Park left Eddie Howe's side 14th in the table. Underlying numbers tell an equally grim story: only Wolves have allowed more big chances in the last six matches, and the Magpies rank in the bottom five for set-piece goals conceded this season, with six of those 13 set-piece goals arriving in the last 10 Gameweeks. The timing looks auspicious.

There's also a historical angle worth noting. Bournemouth have scored nine goals across their last three visits to Tyneside and haven't lost to Newcastle in the league since 2019/20—well before Iraola took charge. These are, in the language of FPL differential hunting, the Cherries' bogey opponents.

Leeds, meanwhile, have tightened up defensively, conceding only four goals in their last six matches. Their issue is at the other end: the club has scored a league-low three goals in that same span. There's still value in the fixture, though. Both Bournemouth and Leeds sit bottom of the table for penalties conceded this season—six each—and Newcastle are only one behind. FPL managers targeting penalty outcomes will find this cluster of matches worth monitoring.

The Rotation Problem

This is where the optimism meets friction. Iraola's recent double gameweek behaviour offers a clear warning signal.

In both Gameweeks 25–26 and Gameweeks 28–29—when Bournemouth also had the following weekend free—the manager made just two changes across the two matches. More pointedly, both Eli Kroupi (£4.6m) and Ryan Christie (£4.9m) started only one of the two games on each occasion. If that pattern holds for Gameweek 33, managers backing either player are essentially buying a 50/50 shot at a start.

The backline is a different story. Rotation concerns are far lower for defenders, and that matters: if you're chasing clean sheet points against a Newcastle side shipping big chances, Bournemouth's defensive assets carry less variance than their attacking counterparts.

The article notes that goalkeeper options—Raya among them—merit attention given the fixture picture, but the source material cuts off before fully spelling out his price point or specifics. That gap is worth flagging if you're working from this as a planning document.

The Bottom Line

Bournemouth's Double Gameweek 33 looks promising on paper. Newcastle's defensive record is genuinely catastrophic, and the historical dominance at St James' Park adds a psychological layer. But Iraola's rotation habits, particularly around attacking midfielders like Kroupi and Christie, mean that value at £4.6m or £4.9m can evaporate if you're betting on the wrong half of the double.

For managers not playing chips, the safer play is Bournemouth's defensive spine—players who start both matches and benefit from a Newcastle side incapable of keeping teams out. The ceiling on those picks is lower, but so is the chance of a zero-pointer on one of your two games.

The fixtures are there. The rotation risk is real. Know which one matters more for your squad.
