FPL Notes: Saka Nets Again, Gyokeres "Immense" + Is Eze's Game-Time a Concern?

Arsenal's Defensive Record

Arsenal secured their 30th clean sheet of the campaign against Atletico Madrid, marking a remarkable achievement for the Gunners' defensive unit. This milestone underscores Mikel Arteta's emphasis on defensive solidity throughout the season.

Saka's Match-Winning Impact

Saka scored the match-winning goal against Atletico Madrid, in which he pounced on Leandro Trossard's saved shot to score. His latest contribution follows a goal and an assist against Fulham in Gameweek 35, demonstrating consistent offensive output.

However, a recurring pattern has emerged regarding Saka's game time. He was substituted before the 60th minute against Atletico Madrid, with Arteta again taking him off early against Atleti before the hour mark. This followed a similar situation in Gameweek 35 against Fulham, when the Arsenal boss hooked him at the interval. Fantasy managers should note this rotation pattern as the season reaches its conclusion.

Arteta kept the exact same starting XI from Gameweek 35 to the Atletico Madrid match, indicating his satisfaction with the lineup that delivered against Fulham.

Gyokeres Praised Despite Missed Chances

Arteta called Gyokeres "immense" after full-time, highlighting his overall contribution despite a frustrating performance in front of goal. Gyokeres missed the best of his three chances, blazing over from a Hincapie cross. The striker could and should have scored on multiple occasions, though his manager's post-match comments suggest confidence in his abilities moving forward.

Eze's Game Time Under Scrutiny

Eze was substituted in the sub-60-minute triple change alongside Saka and Calafiori, raising questions about his game-time reliability. The former Palace man was quiet on Tuesday, receiving a lone '6/10' rating in a performance full of 7s, 8s, and 9s from his teammates. This rating disparity and his early substitution suggest limited confidence from the manager, which could concern Fantasy managers considering him for their squads.
