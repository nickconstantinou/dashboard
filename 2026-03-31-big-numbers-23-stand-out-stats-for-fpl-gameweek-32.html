Big Numbers: 23 stand-out stats for FPL Gameweek 32

The Full Story

Fantasy Premier League managers received a welcome respite during the first extended break from league action in months, and Fantasy Football Scout has utilized this window to revive their acclaimed "Big Numbers" series—a deep-dive statistical analysis that has become essential reading for competitive FPL players. The March 30, 2026 article distills the most compelling Opta and StatsBomb data from the 2025/26 season into 23 eye-catching statistics across goalkeeper and defender positions, offering managers actionable insights as they navigate the critical run-in period.

In the goalkeeper category, Everton's Jordan Pickford (£5.6m) emerges as the standout performer of the season, leading all Premier League shot-stoppers with an extraordinary +5.10 expected goals prevented (xG) rate—the best season-long figure in the league. Pickford's stellar campaign continued with his 11th clean sheet of the season, solidifying his position as the second-highest FPL points scorer among goalkeepers. Meanwhile, Fulham's Bernd Leno (£4.9m) finds himself at the opposite end of the spectrum with a concerning -5.80 xG prevented, joined in the relegation zone of this metric by Wolverhampton Wanderers duo Sam Johnstone (-5.30) and Jose Sa (-3.00), as well as Tottenham's Guglielmo Vicario (-5.20).

Among defenders, Newcastle United's Malick Thiaw (£5.1m) has established himself as the most threatening center-back in the league, registering nine big chances—the highest total for any Premier League defender this term—while also sharing the lead for goals scored by defenders with four strikes. However, when examining only the last six Gameweeks, Manchester City's Nico O'Reilly (£5.0m) and Liverpool's Virgil van Dijk (£6.3m) have been the most dangerous, each creating four big chances during this recent purple patch. Nottingham Forest's Neco Williams (£4.7m) leads all defenders in total shots taken (35) but has converted just one, from 12 efforts inside the penalty area—highlighting volume without efficiency.

Why This Matters

For the millions of FPL managers worldwide, the run-in period from Gameweek 32 onwards often determines championship outcomes and mini-league battles. This statistical analysis provides crucial intelligence for squad optimization during these pivotal Gameweeks, where every transfer and captaincy decision carries amplified weight. The data reveals not just who is performing well, but which players are creating genuine match-winning opportunities versus those accumulating superficial statistics that fail to translate to FPL returns.

The goalkeeper analysis proves particularly valuable given the inverse relationship between price and performance in this position—Pickford's emergence as the best xG-preventing keeper at £5.6m represents exceptional value, while premium options like Donnarumma (£5.6m) may not justify their cost based on underlying numbers. For defenders, the distinction between Thiaw's efficient big-chance creation and Williams' shot-heavy but goal-light profile separates genuinely attacking assets from statistical outliers. These insights allow managers to make data-driven decisions rather than relying on gut feeling or recent form alone.

Furthermore, the article highlights tactical and psychological factors with real-world implications. Brentford's Caoimhin Kelleher (£4.8m) has saved three penalties this season—the only Premier League goalkeeper to stop more than one spot-kick—meaning opposition teams facing the Bees during the run-in must recalculate their approach from 12 yards. Similarly, Robert Sanchez's (£4.8m) emergence as a big-chance creator from goalkeeper positions signals a tactical evolution that managers should factor into their wildcards and free-hit deployments.
