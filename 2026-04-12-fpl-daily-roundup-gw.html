FPL Daily Roundup: April 12, 2026 — The Double Gameweek Countdown Begins

Injury Carnage at Old Trafford Leaves Managers in Limbo

The Fantasy Premier League landscape heading into this weekend has been reshaped by a wave of injury uncertainty, nowhere more acute than at Manchester United where Michael Carrick addressed the media from Dublin and delivered precisely the ambiguity that FPL managers dread.

The most pressing concern centres on Lisandro Martinez. The Argentine centre-back has returned to training after his calf problem but Carrick was deliberately non-committal about his availability for Monday's clash with Leeds United. "It's just that call we make," Carrick said. "We wouldn't be pushed into anything. It's important that the boys are ready, as important as the next game always is, the most important is always the bigger picture." Translation for FPL purposes: proceed at your own risk. Martinez is close, but not close enough to bank on for Gameweek 32.

More encouraging news emerged around Benjamin Sesko and Bryan Mbeumo, both of whom traveled with the United squad to Ireland despite pulling out of their respective national team camps on precautionary grounds. Their presence will fuel optimism among managers considering bringing them in this week, though Carrick offered no firm confirmation on their availability for the Leeds fixture. Sesko's potential return would be a significant boost given his attacking pedigree, while Mbeumo's involvement would solidify Brentford's firepower for any managers still holding the midfielder.

The picture is considerably darker for Matthijs de Ligt. The Dutch defender remained in Manchester and isn't yet back on the grass following his back issue. "Treatment and rehab," Carrick explained when pressed. "The reason he didn't come [to Ireland] is he's not quite on the grass." Patrick Dorgu has progressed further in his hamstring recovery and joined the squad in Ireland, though his participation level was limited to individual training work. Diogo Dalot didn't travel but the club website suggests he should be fit to feature against Leeds.

One certainty for Gameweek 32 is Harry Maguire's absence. The centre-back will serve a one-match suspension, robbing United of a defensive option at precisely the moment when their backline is already threadbare.

Moving to other Premier League clubs, Jaka Bijol at Leeds United carries a knock with only a 75% chance of playing, making him a risky selection this weekend. Joe Rodon is confirmed absent until May 1st with an ankle injury. From the Burnley v Brighton fixture news, Kaoru Mitoma featured off the bench for Brighton, suggesting he's building fitness after recent issues, while at Anfield, Mohamed Salah was confirmed to start against Fulham despite mounting speculation about his future.
