FPL Notes: Will Osula's Starting Streak and Brighton's Positives

Will Osula's Emerging Role at Newcastle

Will Osula has become an intriguing option for FPL managers following a remarkable run in Newcastle's starting lineup. The young forward has started the last four league matches, finding the net in three of those appearances. At his current price point of £5.4m, this strike rate represents solid value for budget-conscious managers.

However, it is important to understand Osula's position within Newcastle's attacking hierarchy. He remains the club's fourth-choice striker, typically behind Anthony Gordon, Woltemade, and Wissa in the pecking order. Despite being fourth in the queue, Osula has received an extended opportunity in recent weeks and has capitalised on the chances presented to him.

Newcastle's Tactical Setup Against Brighton

Newcastle secured a victory over Brighton in Gameweek 35, and several player movements caught the attention of fantasy managers. Dan Burn (£5.0m) started at left-back ahead of Lewis Hall (£5.3m) and delivered a memorable performance. Burn was solid defensively and even contributed a set-piece goal, nodding home from a corner. His continued presence in the starting eleven makes him a viable budget option at £5.0m.

The contrast between Burn and Hall is worth noting. Lewis Hall was hooked at half-time in Gameweek 33 and has subsequently warmed the bench, losing his place in the starting lineup. For managers considering Hall, his recent lack of minutes suggests he is not currently in favour for consistent game time.

Further forward, Joelinton (£5.9m) replaced Jacob Ramsey (£5.3m) on the left wing in Newcastle's lineup, demonstrating the club's willingness to rotate seasoned performers in attacking positions.

Anthony Gordon's Return

Anthony Gordon returned to matchday squads following a hip injury and was an unused substitute in the fixture against Brighton. At his price of £7.3m, his return to full fitness provides Newcastle with additional firepower, though managers should monitor his minutes carefully upon his return to active duty.

Bruno Guimaraes: Home Form Concern

Bruno Guimaraes returned after a two-month absence and made an immediate impact, supplying the corner from which Burn scored. At £6.8m, he remains a popular midfielder option. However, hisFixture sustainability raises concerns for the remainder of the season. A significant 14 of his 16 returns this campaign have come at home, highlighting a strong home-ground bias. Newcastle have only one home fixture remaining, which limits the appeal of chasing his home returns in the closing weeks.

Brighton's Positives Despite Defeat

Brighton performed well despite suffering a defeat, with Jack Hinshlerwood (£5.1m) left ruing missed opportunities. The midfielder admitted he could have had a hat-trick, with a huge early chance saved by Nick Pope. While the underlying performance suggests Brighton's attacking potential remains intact, managers invested in Brighton assets should temper expectations given the result.

Key Takeaways for FPL Managers

For those seeking budget options, Will Osula at £5.4m presents a viable differential given his recent starting streak and goal conversion rate. Dan Burn at £5.0m offers a combination of defensive solidity and set-piece involvement. However, managers should be cautious with Bruno Guimaraes, as his limited remaining home fixtures reduce his differential potential. Monitoring Anthony Gordon's minutes upon his return will be important for managers with Newcastle exposure in attacking areas.
