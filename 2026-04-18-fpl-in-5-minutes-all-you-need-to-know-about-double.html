FPL Double Gameweek 33: The Decisions That Could Win or Lose Your Season

The deadline hits Saturday at 11:00 BST. Millions of FPL managers are firing chips simultaneously. Erling Haaland is tearing up the captaincy polls. And Hugo Ekitike's season is over. Here's what actually matters.

---

The Chip Stampede

Six teams play twice this gameweek — Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds United, and Manchester City — and the data tells us FPL managers are responding with characteristic herd instinct. According to Scout's tracking of Great and Good elite managers, chip usage is spiking hard in Double Gameweek 33. Whether you've been saving your Wildcard since Gameweek 4 or activating your Free Hit for the first time, you're competing in a crowded market. The players who look shrewd today will look obvious by Saturday night. That's the nature of the beast.

---

The Big Hitters

Some assets require no deliberation. Erling Haaland (£14.5m) leads the captaincy poll by a country mile. Manchester City play twice, he's fit, and Pep Guardiola isn't in the habit of resting his best goalscorer in crunch fixtures. The Rate My Team algorithm and Captain Sensible both point the same way.

Cole Palmer (£10.5m), Antoine Semenyo (£8.2m), and Joao Pedro (£7.7m) are unanimous Scout Squad selections. All four are in the conversation for any Wildcard, Free Hit, or Bench Boost XI being assembled this week. These aren't edge picks — they're the spine of your team.

If you're looking for an alternative City midfield route, Rayan Cherki (£6.4m) is earning attention. Lateriser flagged him after he supplied two assists in last week's win at Chelsea. He's £1.8m cheaper than Semenyo and offers a different profile. Whether that difference matters depends on your structure.

---

The Budget Finds

Here's where the game is won or lost: fitting premium assets around cheap enablers who still produce.

Dominic Calvert-Lewin (£5.6m) and Marcus Tavernier (£5.4m) are long overdue for returns. Both are Scout Squad selections for good reason — they've been generating chances at a rate that their price tags don't reflect. Add James Hill (£4.2m) to the Bench Boost mix, and you're looking at a £15.2m floor across three doublers.

Jack Hinshelwood (£5.1m) continues to attract backing. Diego Gomez (£4.9m) got a specific endorsement from Zophar in his Q&A — not casual praise, but an active recommendation from someone building a real squad.

At the back, Pascal Struijk (£4.3m) is quietly outstanding. He's the league's second-best defender for penalty area shots with 26 — a number that should worry anyone who's watched Leeds defend set pieces. Jayden Bogle (£4.4m) grabbed his third attacking return in seven starts at Old Trafford last time out. He's not a glamour pick, but he's returning.

Jaka Bijol (£3.9m) is in Zophar's Free Hit activation. At that price, he doesn't need to do much. He just needs to play twice.

---

The Injury Plotlines

The good news first: Nico O'Reilly (£5.1m) came off against Chelsea as a precaution but Guardiola says the left-back is fine for Sunday's crunch clash against Arsenal. That's the fixture you want. No concern needed.

The bad news: Hugo Ekitike (£9.2m) has a ruptured Achilles tendon and may not return until 2027. His FPL owners need a replacement immediately — not next week, now. At £9.2m, you're looking at options in the Joao Pedro (£7.7m) or Cherki (£6.4m) bracket, or gambling on form and fixtures from a different price tier entirely. This is a real swing decision, not cosmetic.

---

The Real Question

Double Gameweeks are won in the margins. Everyone has Haaland. Almost everyone has the obvious doublers. The managers who finish top 10k are the ones who found the Calvert-Lewin who finally scores, the Bogle who wasn't obvious until you looked twice, the Hill (£4.2m) who costs nothing and plays twice.

The deadline is Saturday, 11:00 BST. Make it count.
