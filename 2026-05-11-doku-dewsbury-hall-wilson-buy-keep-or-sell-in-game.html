Doku, Dewsbury-Hall, Wilson: Buy, Keep or Sell in Gameweek 37?

This article examines three Fantasy Premier League players ahead of Gameweek 37, using verified data to inform ownership decisions.

---

Jeremy Doku: The In-Form Winger

Jeremy Doku (£6.5m) has demonstrated strong recent form that warrants attention for Gameweek 37. The winger netted the opener in Saturday's 3-0 victory over Brentford, showcasing his goal-scoring threat. Across his last five Premier League matches, Doku has produced five attacking returns, accumulating 42 points during this spell. His consistent output over the past month suggests he represents a viable option for managers seeking attacking premiums from City's attack.

---

Kieran Dewsbury-Hall: Low-Ownership Differential

Kieran Dewsbury-Hall (£5.2m) presents an intriguing differential option heading into Gameweek 37. The midfielder has accumulated four attacking returns and 30 points across his last five Gameweeks, demonstrating an ability to deliver returns in bunches. Over the course of the season, Dewsbury-Hall has averaged 5.2 points per start, a figure that compares favourably with similarly priced alternatives. Perhaps most significantly for those seeking an edge, he is currently owned by just 6.6% of squads, making him a genuine low-ownership differential pick who could provide a points advantage over rival managers.

---

Harry Wilson: Contract Uncertainty and Rotation Risk

Harry Wilson (£5.9m) of Fulham has contributed 10 goals and eight assists across 32 starts plus a couple of substitute appearances this season, representing reasonable underlying value at his price point. However, two factors complicate the decision for Gameweek 37. Wilson is out of contract this summer, which raises questions about his future involvement. More immediately concerning, he has been substituted off just after the hour mark in successive Gameweeks, suggesting his role may be increasingly peripheral as the season reaches its conclusion. Managers considering Wilson should weigh his underlying ability against the very real possibility of limited minutes in the closing weeks.

---

Context: European Qualification Battle

For managers weighing defensive options, Everton's situation provides relevant context. The Toffees realistically need to win both matches in Gameweeks 37 and 38 to have any chance of European football next season. This pressure could influence team selection and motivation levels, potentially making Everton assets more or less attractive depending on their opponents.

---

Key Takeaways for Gameweek 37

Doku appears to be in a buy category given his consistent recent returns and reasonable price tag. Dewsbury-Hall represents a keep or marginal buy, particularly for managers seeking differential value in the mid-price bracket. Wilson's combination of rotation risk and contract uncertainty places him in sell territory unless managers have specific information suggesting increased minutes.
