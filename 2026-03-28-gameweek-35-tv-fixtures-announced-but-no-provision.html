Premier League Confirms Gameweek 35 TV Fixtures, But FPL Managers Left Waiting on Double Gameweek Clarity

The Full Story

Fantasy Premier League managers hoping for clarity on potential Double Gameweeks received disappointing news on March 27, 2026, when the Premier League announced television picks and rescheduled dates for Gameweek 35 without confirming any provisional double fixtures. The announcement revealed a packed schedule spanning from Friday, May 1 through Monday, May 4, with eight Premier League matches spread across those four days.

The confirmed Gameweek 35 fixture list includes: Leeds United versus Burnley on Friday evening (Sky Sports), followed by Aston Villa versus Tottenham Hotspur on Saturday lunchtime (TNT Sports) — though this match would shift to Sunday, May 3 at 19:00 BST if Villa progress to the Europa League semi-finals. The Saturday afternoon slate features Bournemouth versus Crystal Palace, Brentford versus West Ham United, and Newcastle versus Brighton, with Arsenal hosting Fulham in the late Saturday slot (Sky Sports). Sunday sees Manchester United versus Liverpool (Sky Sports), while Monday rounds out the gameweek with Chelsea versus Nottingham Forest and Everton versus Manchester City (both Sky Sports).

The announcement also raised questions about Leeds United's fixture congestion. Daniel Farke's side are among the Premier League teams facing potential Gameweek 34 postponement should they reach the FA Cup semi-finals. If that occurs, Leeds would face Bournemouth in the league before potentially being scheduled for three matches within roughly 72 hours: the FA Cup semi-final on Saturday, April 25, the rescheduled league fixture against Bournemouth on Tuesday, April 28, and the Gameweek 35 fixture against Burnley on Friday, May 1.

Why This Matters

For the millions of Fantasy Premier League managers worldwide, fixture scheduling is paramount to success. Double Gameweeks — where teams play twice in a single gameweek — represent golden opportunities to accumulate points by loading up on players from clubs with two matches. Conversely, Blank Gameweeks, where certain teams have no fixture, can devastate a manager's weekly score if they've invested heavily in affected players.

The absence of a provisional Double Gameweek announcement means managers cannot yet plan their team structures around anticipated fixture swings. This uncertainty creates a strategic dilemma: should managers keep transfers in reserve, or proceed with transfers that might prove suboptimal once the full picture emerges? The Fantasy Football Scout article highlights that while Bournemouth versus Leeds shifting to another gameweek (thus creating a double for both sets of players) remains a genuine possibility, the timeline for confirmation stretches over the next ten days.

The fixture congestion issue also has real-world implications beyond fantasy football. Asking Leeds United to play three matches in six days with Premier League survival on the line raises legitimate sporting concerns about player welfare and competitive fairness. The 72-hour turnaround between matches is considered challenging even with full squad depth, and Leeds' survival battle suggests limited rotation options.
