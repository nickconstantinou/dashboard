Premier League Confirms Gameweek 35 TV Fixtures, But Double Gameweek Uncertainty Remains for Fantasy Managers

Full Story

Fantasy Premier League managers eagerly awaiting clarity on the fixture schedule received partial answers on March 27, 2026, when the Premier League announced television picks and new dates for Gameweek 35—but the confirmation came without the provisional Double Gameweek announcement many were hoping for. The fixtures, spread across four days from Friday May 1 to Monday May 4, now give FPL players their first concrete look at the upcoming schedule, though significant questions remain about potential fixture changes that could dramatically shift the landscape.

The confirmed Gameweek 35 fixture list includes seven Premier League matches, with four selected for television broadcast across Sky Sports and TNT Sports. Friday May 1 opens the Gameweek with Leeds United hosting Burnley in a crucial 20:00 kickoff on Sky Sports—a match that carries extra weight given Leeds' potential involvement in the FA Cup semi-finals. Saturday May 2 features the headline fixture of Aston Villa versus Tottenham Hotspur at 12:30 BST on TNT Sports, while Arsenal hosts Fulham in the evening slot at 17:30 on Sky Sports. The Sunday and Monday fixtures see Manchester United face Liverpool on Sky Sports at 15:30, followed by Chelsea against Nottingham Forest and Everton hosting Manchester City—both on Monday evening.

One notable complication threads through the schedule: the Aston Villa-Tottenham match carries an asterisk. Should Aston Villa progress to the Europa League semi-finals, this fixture will shift from Saturday May 2 to Sunday May 3 at 19:00 BST. More significantly, the Leeds-Burnley Friday fixture is part of a broader scheduling puzzle that could reshape the Gameweek entirely. Leeds United is one of the clubs whose Gameweek 34 match against Bournemouth may be postponed if they reach the FA Cup semi-finals, set for Saturday April 25. If that occurs, the scheduling logistics become complicated, with Leeds potentially facing three matches in just over a week—including the original Gameweek 34 fixture on Tuesday April 28 and the Gameweek 35 match against Burnley on Friday May 1, representing back-to-back 72-hour turnarounds with Premier League survival still mathematically on the line.

Why This Matters

For the estimated eight million Fantasy Premier League players worldwide, the Gameweek 35 announcement represents both valuable information and lingering frustration. The provisional Double Gameweek concept has become increasingly important in FPL strategy, as managers who can anticipate which teams will have double fixtures can load their squads with players from those clubs and gain significant rank advantages. The absence of this announcement means managers cannot yet plan for potential fixture swings, leaving many uncertain about whether to invest in players from clubs like Leeds United and Bournemouth who could see their fixture lists change dramatically depending on cup results.

The situation also highlights the cascading effects of cup competitions on domestic league schedules—a logistical challenge that becomes more pronounced as the season reaches its business end. For Leeds United, the possibility of fixture congestion comes at the worst possible time, with the club fighting to avoid relegation. Every match carries survival implications, and the physical toll of three matches in eight days could prove decisive in determining which clubs remain in the Premier League for next season.

    • Context and Background

    The Gameweek 35 announcement arrives amid one of the most complex fixture periods of the season, with the FA Cup quarter-finals, Europa League commitments, and Premier League survival battles all intersecting. The Premier League's decision not to announce a provisional Double Gameweek alongside the TV picks reflects the genuine uncertainty surrounding several clubs' schedules. Historically, the league has provided advance warning of fixture changes to help Fantasy managers prepare, but this year's timing means many decisions remain contingent on cup results that won't be determined for another two weeks.

    For FPL managers, the Double Gameweek has become a cornerstone of successful strategy. When a team plays twice in a single Gameweek, owners of their players effectively double their points potential from those assets. The most recent confirmed Double Gameweek occurred earlier this season, providing dramatic rank swings for managers who anticipated it correctly. The current uncertainty particularly affects those holding Leeds United and Bournemouth assets, as a postponed fixture could create a double for both clubs—but only if Leeds United specifically progress past the FA Cup quarter-finals.

    What's Next

    The next ten days will bring clarity to the fixture situation. The FA Cup quarter-finals, scheduled for the weekend of April 18-19, will determine whether Leeds United joins the semi-finalists, triggering the potential postponement of their Gameweek 34 fixture against Bournemouth. Once that result is known, the Premier League is expected to formally announce the Blank/Double Gameweek schedule, including rescheduling details for any postponed matches.

    Fantasy managers should monitor Leeds United's cup progress closely, as the club's continued FA Cup involvement creates both risk and opportunity. A double gameweek for Leeds and Bournemouth could arrive later in the season, potentially as late as Gameweek 37, giving managers time to position themselves accordingly. Until the official announcement arrives, the advice from Fantasy Football Scout suggests caution—hold off on definitive transfers until the fixture picture clarifies, but prepare contingency plans for both scenarios.
