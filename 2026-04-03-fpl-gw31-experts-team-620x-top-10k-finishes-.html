FPL Gameweek 31 Expert Analysis: Strategic Insights for the Blank Gameweek Run-in

As the Premier League season reaches its critical final stages, Fantasy Premier League managers face one of the most challenging stretches of the campaign. Gameweek 31 presents a blank gameweek for many teams, forcing managers to navigate transfer decisions without the safety net of a full fixture list. This video breaks down exactly how the top all-time FPL managers are positioning their squads for this crucial period, offering a window into the strategies that separate the best from the rest. With the business end of the season approaching and multiple blank and double gameweeks on the horizon, understanding expert movements has never been more valuable.

The analysis centers on the top-ranked FPL experts, specifically those competing in the established expert leagues, and examines their transfer patterns, captaincy choices, and chip strategies. What makes this particular episode valuable is that it comes during a genuinely tricky gameweek—one that theoretically requires careful planning but can actually be navigated without using any chips, preserving them for more advantageous later gameweeks. This creates an interesting tension between immediate optimization and long-term strategic positioning.

The Expert Landscape After Gameweek 30

Andrew Moore has ascended to the top of the expert rankings after Gameweek 30, achieving a score of 64 points which elevated him to 284th in the world. This represents an exceptional performance that significantly beat the average expert score of 59 points, while the global average sat at just 43 points. Moore's rise to the top spot demonstrates how even a single strong gameweek can dramatically reshape the competitive landscape among FPL's most skilled managers.

The video notes that approximately ten other experts sit just one or two points outside the top 10,000 threshold, highlighting how razor-thin the margins are at the elite level. These managers are performing at an extraordinarily high level, and the competition for those final ranking positions remains intensely competitive.

Perhaps most significantly, Moore currently fields fewer than 11 players for Gameweek 31, meaning he must make at least one transfer to field a valid squad. His specific move involves transferring out goalkeeper Guo and midfielder Rice, bringing in Bowen and Wilson—a pattern that reflects the broader trends among top managers this week.

Transfer Patterns and Market Movements

The transfer activity among experts reveals a clear narrative: 80% of top managers are making at least one transfer this gameweek, while 20% are rolling their transfer. This high transfer rate reflects the challenging nature of the blank gameweek, where managers must act to maintain viable squads while also positioning themselves for upcoming double gameweeks.

The video makes an important strategic observation about when to use transfers versus rolling them. Managers with a full 11 out and no major upside opportunities might actually benefit from leaving their team untouched, using the roll for a later double gameweek. The reasoning is straightforward: transferring from, for example, Audley to Anderson might yield only four additional points if Fulham keep a clean sheet or Sunderland concede, whereas rolling preserves that transfer for the more valuable double gameweeks in 33 and 36, and potentially even a smaller double in 34. The video emphasizes that "bank transfers are king when we head into the business end of the season."

Most Bought and Sold Players

The most purchased player among experts this week surprised even the presenter: Chiao from Fulham, with 33% of experts bringing him in. This represents a significant concentration of investment in a single player. The second most bought is Newcastle's Gordon at 26%, followed by Wilson, Anderson, and Bowen. This attack-focused buying pattern reflects the limited defensive options available in Gameweek 31.

On the selling side, the list reads like a catalog of players without fixtures or dealing with injuries: Rice, Timber, Harland, Semeno, Munoz, Sar, Chalaba, Gmanson, Dorgu, and Taroski. This mass exodus from injured or blanked players demonstrates the ruthless optimization top managers apply to their squads.

An important injury development involves Chalobil, who stretched off during Champions League midweek action and appears likely to be out for an extended period. Similarly, Salah was substituted during Liverpool's 4-0 Champions League win over Galatasaray, where he managed one goal and one assist but also missed a penalty in what manager Slot described as a "strange game" where Salah was "asking for a substitution... because he felt something." His availability for the weekend remains uncertain, representing a significant wildcard for managers.

Defensive Target Analysis: Fulham vs Newcastle

The video devotes considerable attention to the defensive options for Gameweek 31, identifying Fulham and Newcastle as the only teams with significantly better clean sheet odds than the competition. Newcastle faces a disadvantage given their Champions League fixture against Barcelona on Wednesday, which ended 7-2 and likely drained significant energy from the squad before the weekend derby.

The analysis argues that Fulham's defense makes "the most sense" given their 4% better clean sheet odds, but the presenter cautions that this is "a bit more nuanced than that." Anderson emerges as the best pick from Fulham, but beyond him, options become less appealing. Tete and Bassie are only hitting Defcon (clean sheet bonuses) half as frequently as Anderson—Bassie manages just 7.8 Defcons per 90 minutes compared to Chiao's significantly higher rate.

Chiao carries substantially more goal threat than his defensive teammates, having scored four goals this season (joint most among defenders, one more than Gabriel). The video presents a nuanced case for choosing Chiao over Anderson: while Anderson might offer slightly better defensive returns this specific gameweek, Chiao has superior fixtures beyond Gameweek 31, with Palace away and Bournemouth home, compared to Liverpool away and Brentford away for Anderson. More importantly, Chiao has a strong chance of a double in Gameweek 33, whereas Anderson and Fulham do not.

This fixture differential and double gameweek potential makes Chiao the more robust pick for managers thinking beyond just this individual gameweek.

Chip Strategy: The Critical Decision

One of the most valuable sections addresses the evolving chip strategy debate. The video notes that "interestingly, there are no chips active this week at all on the entire experts league"—a remarkable finding that confirms this gameweek can be navigated without chip expenditure, preserving them for more advantageous situations.

The presenter outlines two competing strategies that top managers are debating:

Strategy One: Save the wildcard and instead free hit double gameweek 33, then wildcard in Gameweek 35, with bench boost in Gameweek 36. This approach avoids the wildcard in Gameweek 32 and preserves maximum flexibility for the double gameweeks.

Strategy Two: The more commonly discussed approach—wildcard in Gameweek 32, bench boost in 33, and use a hit in 34. This has been the prevailing wisdom but may not be optimal for all managers.

The video presents a compelling case for the first strategy, particularly for managers who don't own many players from teams likely to blank in Gameweek 34 (Man City, Arsenal, Chelsea). The concern with wildcarding in 32 is filling five or six squad spots with players from Brighton, Newcastle, and Burnley specifically for bench boost in 33, then spending the rest of the season trying to fix a suboptimal squad.

A crucial variable involves the Manchester City versus Crystal Palace fixture. If Palace lose in the Europa Conference League and Man City defeat Liverpool in the FA Cup, that match could move from Gameweek 36 to as early as Gameweek 32. The video estimates roughly a 5% chance of this happening, noting that "there's probably about a 5% chance that Man City versus Palace stays in game 31." However, the presenter doesn't expect it to happen, as the Premier League could just as easily drop it in a later gameweek. If it does occur, the announcement would likely come before the deadline.

Projected Goals and Attacking Options

The latest projected goals odds reveal Fulham, Newcastle, and Manchester United leading with 1.9 expected goals, followed by Villa at 1.8, Liverpool at 1.7, and Chelsea at 1.6. This creates a clear hierarchy of attacking potential for the gameweek.

The specific players highlighted include Wilson and Gordon as the primary targets, along with Bruno Fernandes from Manchester United, and potentially Rogers as a one-week punt. Zabosi, Mechik, Jab, Pedro, and Palmer all receive mention as good options based on the projected goal outputs.

Captaincy Analysis: Bruno's Dominance

The captaincy data shows overwhelming confidence in Bruno Fernandes, with 91% of experts selecting him as their captain. Wilson receives 4%, Gordon gets 2%, and Palmer, Salah, and Thiago each have just 1%.

The reasoning for Bruno's dominance is comprehensive. He leads all players with ten double-digit returns this season—Harland has nine. Bruno has created 24 big chances, nine more than any other player, and carries direct goal threat, ranking top five for shots taken by midfielders. His expected goal involvement of 18.02 ranks top of all midfielders this season. As the presenter states, "It's really hard to look past Bruno with the season he's having."

For managers targeting the Burnley fixture specifically, Wilson emerges as the best option, though the video notes that Raul from Fulham "got some potential as the striker there... but we are expecting an early sub as usual, so you're banking on him getting the job done early."

Key Strategic Insights

The video concludes with actionable advice from FPL Fade, who emphasizes the crucial upcoming fixtures: the Crystal Palace Europa Conference League game and the Manchester City versus Liverpool FA Cup match. These outcomes will significantly determine optimal chip strategy.

If Palace go out of Europe and Man City progress in the FA Cup, the calendar opens up significantly. The analysis suggests that for managers without many big-team players likely to blank in Gameweek 34, "free hit 33, wildcard 35, and bench boost 36" might be better than the wildcard 32, bench boost 33, three-hit 34 approach.

The presenter also notes their personal position: "I can already feel a strong outfield team in game 34, I just need a goalkeeper transfer." For those going the wildcard 32 route, they express wariness about "filling five or six spots in my squad with players from Brighton, Newcastle, and Burnley for bench 33 and then spending the rest of the season trying to fix my team."

The flexibility to switch strategies based on European competition results represents a key strategic advantage, and managers are advised to monitor these outcomes closely.
