# FPL Daily Round-Up: May 12, 2026

## The Decision That Changes Everything

Morgan Gibbs-White's facial injury has created the season's most consequential dilemma. The Nottingham Forest midfielder—priced at £7.5m, owned by just 8.5% of managers, and averaging 9.2 form—faces a 50% chance of playing in the next fixture. His 172 season points tell only half the story. The other half is pure panic among managers who built their squads around Forest's electric form.

This is the moment that separates gameweeks. Hold and risk a blank. Sell and lose a 9.2-form differential. The deadline is unforgiving.

## The Blank and Double Calculus

The fixture landscape tells a brutal story. Arsenal, Crystal Palace, Manchester City, and Wolves all blank in Gameweek 31. Gameweek 34 will be a nightmare for anyone holding Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds, and Manchester City assets. The flip side offers salvation: Gameweek 33 features a genuine mega-double with six teams in action, making it the season's most critical gameweek for chip deployment.

## Confirmed Out: Act Now

Noah Okafor (£5.6m, 2.1% ownership) is ruled out with a calf problem and not expected back until May 24th. His season is effectively over for FPL purposes.

Joachim Andersen (£4.5m) remains suspended until August 29th. Drop him immediately. His £4.5m price tag offers zero value for the remaining fixtures.

Leeds United's offensive struggles compound Okafor's absence. Their creative output has been broadly ineffective, and managers should view Leeds attacking assets as extremely risky propositions heading into the final stretch.

## The Bournemouth Case

Bournemouth under Andoni Iraola has emerged as the standout differential play for Gameweek 33. Defender Adrien Truffert (£4.7m, 4.7% ownership) sits near the top of the form rankings at 7.2, while Rayan Vitor Simplício Rocha (£5.4m, 1.5% ownership) offers attacking upside from defence with 7.0 form.

Marcos Senesi Barón (£5.2m, 21.2% ownership) leads the Cherries charge with 169 season points and 7.0 form, making him exceptional value at his price point. James Hill (£4.2m, 7.5% ownership) provides the absolute floor route into what looks like a productive double gameweek. Junior Kroupi (£4.6m, 6.4% ownership) offers attacking potential from Bournemouth's forward line at a price that won't break the bank.

Analyst consensus: Bournemouth's handling of Alex Jimenez's situation was measured and responsible. The club chose not to select him for matchplay while an investigation remains ongoing.

## Manchester City: The Core Remains

City assets remain the backbone of elite squads despite their fixture complications. Erling Haaland (£14.7m) is owned by 64.4% of managers with 230 season points, though his recent 5.5 form won't terrify anyone.

The interesting City punts come from budget options. Marc Guéhi (£5.1m, 32.7% ownership) at centre-back offers both clean sheet potential and attacking contributions with 6.0 form. Nico O'Reilly (£5.3m, 19.9% ownership) has quietly assembled 158 season points. Ferdi Kadıoğlu at Brighton (£4.4m, 1.3% ownership) provides exposure to a Brighton side with a double gameweek coming, and his 5.8 form represents decent value at minimum price.

## Captain Recommendations

Arsenal assets dominate the premium conversation. Gabriel dos Santos Magalhães (£7.3m, 45.3% ownership) leads with 202 season points and delivers at both ends of the pitch. His 6.2 form reflects consistent involvement in north London clean sheets and set-piece danger. David Raya Martín (£6.1m, 35.6% ownership) in goal offers a more affordable route into Arsenal's defensive infrastructure at £1.2m cheaper than their premium options while maintaining 6.5 form.

Viktor Gyökeres (£9.0m, 15.6% ownership) represents the Arsenal attacking premium despite his slightly lower 5.0 form. The Swedish forward has accumulated 126 season points and offers a different profile to the established premium options.

In midfield, Dominik Szoboszlai (£7.0m, 13.0% ownership) continues to fly under the radar with 149 season points and a 5.2 form that doesn't fully capture his ceiling potential. Casemiro (£5.9m, 5.4% ownership) at Manchester United has found form at precisely the wrong time for his ownership, with 6.0 form suggesting he's worth considering despite his modest 5.4% ownership.

## Where Creators Disagree

The Manchester City fixture rotation divides opinion. With Guardiola's side blanking in both Gameweek 31 and Gameweek 34, some analysts favour avoiding City assets entirely until after those blanks. Others argue that their Gameweek 33 double and Gameweek 36 double make them essential holds even through the dry spells. Both positions have merit depending on your squad structure and chip inventory.

The Gibbs-White situation has created similar division. His 9.2 form is untouchable when fit, but the 50% chance of playing makes him a liability. Some managers are willing to take the risk given Forest's favourable fixtures. Others are pivoting to safer options with similar points potential.

## Chip Strategy: Timing Is Everything

The free hit chip becomes a valuable weapon with confirmed doubles in Gameweek 33 and Gameweek 36. Managers should be planning their free hit squad specifically for Gameweek 33 when six teams are doubling, allowing them to load up on Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds, and Manchester City assets without sacrificing their core squad.

The wildcard timing is more nuanced. Managers without free hits remaining should consider deploying their wildcard in the window before Gameweek 33, allowing them to build a squad specifically targeting the double gameweek while ensuring they don't hold dead assets for the blanks in Gameweek 31 and Gameweek 34.

Bench boost strategy should focus on Gameweeks where your bench fodder has double fixtures. With limited doubles remaining, bench boost is becoming less valuable and may be best saved for the final double or abandoned entirely.

## Your Three Actions This Week

Assess your blank gameweek exposure immediately. If you hold players from Arsenal, Crystal Palace, Manchester City, or Wolves, you need to act now. These teams all blank in Gameweek 31, and if you don't have a free hit ready, you could be looking at single-digit returns from multiple squad slots.

Identify your double gameweek targets. Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds, and Manchester City all double in Gameweek 33. Whether through the free hit chip or through strategic transfers, loading up on assets from these sides before the double could be transformative for your season.

Make a decision on Gibbs-White before the deadline. His 50% playing chance creates unacceptable risk for most managers. If he's confirmed out, pivoting to someone like Elliot Anderson (£5.6m, 7.1% ownership, 6.0 form) at Nottingham Forest or Szoboszlai at Liverpool gives you similar attacking exposure without the injury anxiety. If he's confirmed fit, hold and captain him with confidence.
