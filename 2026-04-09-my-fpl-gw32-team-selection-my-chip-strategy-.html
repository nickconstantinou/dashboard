The Defence: Building Around Arsenal's Brilliant Season

The back three for this Gameweek 32 team features Raya between the posts, with Gabriel, O'Reilly, and Van Dijk forming the defensive line. Let's examine why each player was selected and what makes them valuable for this particular fixture.

Raya delivered a sensational performance in the Champions League as Arsenal edged past Sporting CP 1-0 away from home—a result that extended their remarkable home record. Before that match, Arsenal had been unbeaten in 23 consecutive home games across all competitions, with 17 wins in a row at the Emirates. This defensive solidity translates directly to FPL value, with Arsenal having conceded only 71 expected goals (xG) all season. As the creator notes, "After losing the League Cup final and then dropping out the FA Cup, Arsenal really are fighting on the two fronts. Now, every game is crucial at this stage." This means Arsenal will field strong sides in every Premier League match, making their players reliable options.

Gabriel has recovered from the knee knock he picked up in the FA Cup that forced an early substitution and required ice treatment on the bench. He should start at home against Bournemouth, a fixture that holds particular significance given a humorous anecdote shared: "Last time that I went to this fixture I literally just sat down at my seat. I tweeted how many gunners or something like that. I doubled Arsenal defense and my FPL team as well. I looked up and Bulmer had put the ball in our net after 11 seconds." Despite that memory, the data strongly favors an Arsenal clean sheet, with the Gunners currently at 44% clean sheet probability for Gameweek 32—the highest in the league.

The O'Reilly situation is particularly interesting and represents one of the key decision points for wildcarders. Overall, he has played left back 20 times in the Premier League this season, making it his most popular position. However, "recently we've seen Pep using him in midfield, and that's where he's really excelled lately. Obviously came away with that brace in the League Cup final to win it for City." This positional flexibility creates both opportunity and confusion: O'Reilly could easily end up in many wildcard teams this week alongside Sevilla and Haaland, but the choice between him (Chelsea away) and Muki (Spurs home) in the starting XI is a genuine dilemma. The creator has gone with O'Reilly in their starting 11, reasoning: "We'll surely see some kind of bounce from Spurs with Deserby now in charge and given O'Reilly's upside if plays in midfield."

Van Dijk completes the back three despite a concerning recent run. Liverpool have now lost two consecutive matches across all competitions—4-0 to Manchester City and 2-1 to Brighton—leaving the creator to observe: "Starting to feel like Slot is very much on borrow time unless Liverpool managed to win the Champions League, which is incredibly unlikely in my opinion." However, Van Dijk remains valuable for those on Free Hit 33 chip strategies because he plays at home this week against Fulham and also has a Double Gameweek 34 at home to Crystal Palace. The clean sheet odds confirm Liverpool at 34%, tied with West Ham and Brighton for third place behind Arsenal (44%) and Manchester United (36%).

Key Takeaways for FPL Managers

First, the Double Gameweek 33 announcement fundamentally reshapes wildcard timing. Managers who were planning to wildcard in Gameweek 32 should now strongly consider waiting, as loading up on the six doubling teams (Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds, Man City) represents significant value. The creator's original plan made sense before confirmation, but the landscape has changed.

Second, Arsenal assets remain elite despite their trophy failures. The loss of the League Cup and FA Cup exits actually strengthen the Premier League focus, and their 44% clean sheet probability against Bournemouth makes Gabriel and Raya must-haves. The only concern is rotation, but with "every game crucial," Arsenal should field strong sides.

Third, Manchester City's O'Reilly represents a wildcard pivot point. His ability to play both left back and midfield creates unpredictability, but his midfield form (including a League Cup final brace) suggests ceiling. Expect him to appear in many wildcard teams alongside Sevilla and Haaland.

Fourth, Liverpool assets require extreme caution. The combination of poor recent form, Champions League distractions, and uncertainty about player minutes makes Van Dijk, Núñez, and Salah risky propositions. Even Wilson at Anfield—normally a strong fixture—becomes complicated given Liverpool's schedule.

Fifth, chip strategy depends entirely on your team composition. Whether you wildcard now or wait depends on your current squad. If you have many doubling team assets, the first option works. If you need to rebuild around the double gameweek, the second approach may suit better. The key is recognizing there's no universal "correct" answer.
