Double Gameweek 33 confirmed for up to eight teams

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

Premier League confirms rescheduling plans that could reshape the Fantasy Premier League landscape for thousands of managers

The Premier League has confirmed that the upcoming FA Cup quarter-final fixtures will create a massive Double Gameweek 33 scenario, with up to eight teams potentially playing twice in a single gameweek while their opponents blank. The announcement, made on Thursday afternoon, provides FPL managers with critical information they need to plan their crucial season run-in—though the exact makeup of the double gameweeks won't be known until after this weekend's FA Cup quarter-final matches are completed.

Under the confirmed arrangements, any team that progresses to the FA Cup semi-finals will have their Premier League fixture moved from Gameweek 34 into Gameweek 33, creating a double fixture for both the advancing team and their original Gameweek 34 opponent. This scheduling quirk stems from the FA Cup semi-finals being scheduled for the same weekend as the blank Gameweek 34, forcing rescheduling to accommodate the cup competition.

The timing of Thursday's announcement has been described as a significant relief for FPL managers, as the Gameweek 32 deadline falls on Friday, April 10, 2026—meaning players will know exactly which teams are doubling and blanking before finalizing their lineup decisions. This advance notice allows for strategic chip deployment that would otherwise be impossible with typical short-notice scheduling changes.

Why This Matters: The Strategic Chess Match of FPL Season's Endgame

For the estimated millions of FPL managers worldwide, this announcement transforms the final weeks of the season from a straightforward fixture management exercise into a complex strategic puzzle requiring careful chip allocation. The Double Gameweek 33 scenario presents what many consider a golden opportunity for managers who have been saving their Bench Boost chip, potentially allowing them to field 15 active players for a single gameweek—a massive advantage in head-to-head matchups and overall rankings.

The implications extend beyond single-gameweek strategy. FPL managers must now consider a three-gameweek sequence spanning Gameweeks 32, 33, and 34, with the optimal play potentially involving cascading chip usage: a Gameweek 32 Wildcard to prepare for the doubles, followed by Bench Boost deployment in Gameweek 33 to maximize points from the double fixtures, and then a Gameweek 34 Free Hit to field a complete squad despite the blank gameweek affecting multiple teams.

Those without the Bench Boost chip face a different but equally viable strategy centered on Wildcard and Free Hit combinations. A Gameweek 33 Wildcard allows managers to load up on players from the doubling teams, followed by a Gameweek 34 Free Hit to navigate the blank gameweek with a specialized squad. The flexibility to adapt these strategies will likely determine success in both mini-leagues and the overall rankings.

Additionally, the announcement serves as a reminder that Manchester City's postponed home match against Crystal Palace—a result of the EFL Cup final rescheduling in Gameweek 31—still requires completion. This fixture "should" result in another Double Gameweek further down the line, potentially benefiting the handful of managers who have preserved their Triple Captain chip for maximum impact.

Key Details: The Complete Scenario Breakdown

The confirmed scenarios demonstrate just how dramatic this gameweek could become depending on this weekend's FA Cup results:

If Arsenal beat Southampton: Arsenal would have a double (home against Manchester City, away at Newcastle), while Newcastle would also double (home against Bournemouth, away at Arsenal).

If Chelsea beat Port Vale: Chelsea would double (home against Manchester United, away at Brighton), with Brighton also doubling (home against Tottenham, away at Chelsea).

If Manchester City beat Liverpool: Manchester City would double (home against Arsenal, home against Burnley), while Burnley would also double (home against Nottingham Forest, away at Manchester City).

If Liverpool beat Manchester City: Liverpool would double (away at Everton, home against Crystal Palace), with Crystal Palace doubling (home against West Ham, away at Liverpool).

If West Ham beat Leeds: West Ham would double (home against Crystal Palace, away at Everton), with Everton also doubling (home against Liverpool, away at West Ham).

If Leeds beat West Ham: Leeds would double (home against Wolves, away at Bournemouth), while Bournemouth would also double (home against Newcastle, away at Leeds).

The most chaotic scenario—if Arsenal, Chelsea, Manchester City, and Liverpool all win their quarter-final ties as expected—would result in eight teams doubling in Gameweek 33, with all of those same teams blanking in Gameweek 34. This maximum chaos scenario would leave virtually every FPL manager scrambling to field a competitive squad in Gameweek 34 without the benefit of Bench Boost protection.

Context: The Annual Scheduling Conundrum Created by English Football's Cup Competition

This situation represents an annual fixture nightmare that the Premier League and Football Association have navigated for years. The FA Cup, as England's premier knockout competition, takes precedence over league fixtures when scheduling conflicts arise, particularly during the semi-final stage when fewer teams remain. The quarter-final weekend typically sees several Premier League fixtures postponed or rescheduled, with the rescheduled matches often compressed into existing gameweeks.

For FPL purposes, this creates a predictable but complex planning challenge. The gameweeks immediately following FA Cup quarter-final weekends traditionally feature blanks and doubles, as teams involved in cup progression receive additional fixtures while their opponents miss out. What makes this year particularly significant is the scale of potential doubles—up to eight teams representing a substantial portion of the league's playing pool.

The Premier League's decision to confirm the rescheduling arrangement before the Gameweek 32 deadline represents an unusually transparent communication approach, acknowledging the importance of advance planning for the FPL community. This advance notice allows managers approximately one week to research, strategize, and execute their chip plays before the first fixtures of this pivotal sequence kick off.

What's Next: The Critical Weekend That Determines Everything

All becomes clear after this weekend's FA Cup quarter-final matches conclude. The results will immediately determine which teams enter the Double Gameweek 33 rotation and which face a Blank Gameweek 34, allowing managers to finalize their chip strategies with certainty rather than relying on projections and probabilities.

Those with Triple Captain still unused may want to hold the chip for the yet-to-be-confirmed Manchester City versus Crystal Palace double gameweek, which could arrive later in the season when fixture congestion and European commitments further complicate scheduling. The patience could prove extremely valuable, though it requires confidence that those specific assets will remain fit and featured in the interim period.

The coming days will see FPL content creators and community analysts dissecting each potential scenario, identifying the optimal player targets from doubling teams, and modeling expected point differentials from various chip combinations. For managers willing to plan ahead, this announcement represents both opportunity and obligation—the chance to gain meaningful ground on rivals who may not react strategically to the changing landscape.
