# West Ham Can Escape Relegation Zone Against Wolves as Premier League Resumes After Month-Long Gap

The Premier League returns tonight with stakes that could hardly be higher. West Ham United host Wolverhampton Wanderers at the London Stadium knowing that a victory will lift them out of the bottom three — at least until Sunday's fixtures conclude. For a club that has spent much of this season flirting with danger, the timing could not be more pressing: this is the first top-flight match since 22 March, a 19-day hiatus that felt far longer given the stakes.

Nuno Espirito Santo has responded to the break by making five changes to the side that started against Leeds United in the FA Cup. Mads Hermansen replaces Alphonse Areola in goal, while Konstantinos Mavropanos, Tomas Soucek, Pablo Felipe and Crysencio Summerville all return to the starting XI. Soungoutou Magassa, Freddie Potts and Adama Traore drop to the bench. Notably absent is Max Kilman, who is not in the matchday squad at all.

More encouraging for the hosts is the return of two players from injury. Jean-Clair Todibo and Callum Wilson are both among the substitutes, offering Nuno fresh options as West Ham attempts to climb clear of trouble.

Wolves' team news proves far more straightforward, but the decision to bench Mateus Mane is anything but trivial. Angel Gomes replaces him in the starting lineup, ending Mane's run of 14 consecutive Premier League starts. That streak represented one of the more reliable constants in Wolves' season, and its abrupt end signals a tactical shift or perhaps a fitness consideration from manager Vitor Pereira.

The lineups in full:

**West Ham United**: Hermansen, Walker-Peters, Mavropanos, Disasi, Diouf, Soucek, Fernandes, Bowen, Pablo, Summerville, Taty.

**Substitutes**: Areola, Wilson, Adama, Todibo, Magassa, Wan-Bissaka, Scarles, Potts, Kante.

**Wolverhampton Wanderers**: Sa, Mosquera, S Bueno, Krejci, Tchatchoua, Andre, J Gomes, H Bueno, Bellegarde, A Gomes, Armstrong.

**Substitutes**: Bentley, Wolfe, Hwang, Tolu, Lima, R Gomes, Toti, Mane, Edozie.

Kick-off is at 20:00 BST. For West Ham, the math is simple: three points changes everything. Anything less and the relegation fight drags on.
