# O'Reilly injury clouds Man City's Double Gameweek 33 as Cherki seizes his moment

Nico O'Reilly scored again. Then he went off injured. And nobody at Manchester City is saying a word about how serious it is.

The 19-year-old bundled in a superb header on Sunday to open the scoring in City's 3-0 win over Chelsea — his fifth Premier League goal of the season — only to pull up with what appeared to be a hamstring issue midway through the second half. He received treatment on the pitch, departed visibly limping, and paused to speak with Pep Guardiola, who smiled back at him. Comforting in the moment. Informative, it was not. There was no update from Guardiola in the post-match presser, nor from players in the mixed-zone interviews with the BBC or Sky. It's a fog that will unsettle any FPL manager holding O'Reilly at £5.0m ahead of Double Gameweek 33, where City face Arsenal and Burnley.

If O'Reilly misses out, the beneficiaries are obvious. Marc Guehi, who scored City's second goal and put in an excellent defensive performance, looks a solid replacement at £5.1m. Rayan Ait-Nouri at £5.7m is the other name doing the rounds. But the bigger story from Sunday night wasn't about who might be missing — it was about the player who emphatically wasn't.

Rayan Cherki delivered the two assists that turned the game after City's flat first half, taking his tally to 11 for the season. That's now the most of any player in 2025-26 except Bruno Fernandes. Guardiola, who rarely speaks at this length about individuals, offered a lengthy and revealing assessment afterwards.

"The numbers are incredible and the quality in the first season in the Premier League, he is something unique," Guardiola said. He went on to explain that Cherki needs to sustain his output across a full 95 minutes, noting the Frenchman's limitations in off-the-ball work compared to someone like Antoine Semenyo — but drew an instructive parallel: "I've been with Sergio Aguero and Sergio Aguero was not the incredible high press player but he tried his best… I ask is that — do your best."

It's a striking endorsement from a manager who rarely grants grace for defensive deficiencies. The point about Cherki's vision was reinforced immediately: for Guehi's goal, Guardiola admitted he couldn't even see the pass Cherki chose, but Cherki found it anyway. "He has something special," Guardiola said. "If Manchester City decide to bring that player in, it is because Manchester City is working really well." (City signed Cherki from Lyon last summer for around £45m — a figure that now looks increasingly shrewd.)

The practical upshot is straightforward: Cherki is a genuine differential for Double Gameweek 33. At £6.3m, he sits in an attacking quartet alongside Semenyo, Jeremy Doku, and Erling Haaland that has begun to look genuinely cohesive. The title race is still alive — City sit six points behind Arsenal with a game in hand — and a player who can unlock tight games with a single pass is exactly the kind of asset that matters in a run-in.

O'Reilly's injury updates, once available, will reshape the picture. For now, Cherki's emergence is the more compelling development — and the one worth acting on.
