FPL Gameweek 36 Differentials: 3 Low-Owned City, Arsenal + Spurs Picks

Fantasy managers looking for an edge in Gameweek 36 might consider three players with single-digit ownership who are positioned for opportunities this Double Gameweek.

---

Jeremy Doku – Manchester City | 3.8% Ownership | £6.4m

Manchester City face two matches in Gameweek 36, hosting Brentford and Crystal Palace. Among City assets, Jeremy Doku stands out as an under-owned option currently selected by just 3.8% of squads.

The Belgian winger arrives in strong form. Over his last four matches, Doku has scored three goals and provided one assist, accumulating 31 points during this period. His recent performances have earned praise from manager Pep Guardiola, who said Doku is "starting to be a winning player" and has gained his trust. Guardiola commented that while Doku has always been incredible, his end product is improving.

At £6.4m, Doku offers a budget-friendly route into City's attacking unit for this valuable Double Gameweek.

---

Ben White – Arsenal | 0.6% Ownership | £5.1m

Arsenal defender Ben White is owned by just 0.6% of Fantasy managers despite recent minutes and point returns. Priced at £5.1m, he has started successive Premier League matches for Arsenal, accumulating 12 points across those fixtures.

White has historically performed well at the London Stadium. Across his last two visits there, he delivered two assists and 15 points, making him a potential differential for Arsenal's fixture this Gameweek.

The Gunners have been dealing with injuries in their defensive line. Mikel Arteta confirmed that Jurrien Timber has "no chance for the weekend" and may not return this season, with his last appearance coming in Gameweek 30. This situation could mean continued opportunities for White in Arsenal's backline.

---

Mathys Tel – Tottenham Hotspur | 0.2% Ownership | £6.2m

Tottenham's Mathys Tel represents perhaps the deepest differential among the three picks, with ownership of only 0.2% and a price of £6.2m.

With Xavi Simons (£6.4m) injured, Tottenham's creative burden could shift toward Tel. The French forward has been thrust into a more prominent role and could benefit from additional responsibility in the final third. At his price point, he offers a low-owned route into Tottenham's attack for managers willing to take a calculated risk.
