# Liverpool Injury Roundup: Salah on the Cusp of Return as Liverpool Prepares for High-Stakes FA Cup Clash with Manchester City

## The Full Story

Liverpool manager Arne Slot has provided comprehensive injury updates on five key players as the club prepares for Saturday's FA Cup quarter-final showdown with Manchester City. The Dutch coach addressed the media with a mix of encouraging and concerning news regarding his squad's fitness heading into one of the most anticipated matches of the season.

Mohamed Salah appears poised for a remarkable comeback after initially being feared unavailable for the City fixture. The Egyptian forward, who has been sidelined, demonstrated his legendary professionalism by telling Slot he would be ready for the match despite initial doubts from medical staff. "I think I will be available for City," Salah told his manager, according to Slot's account. The Liverpool boss expressed admiration for his star striker's dedication, noting, "He just does so much for his body for such a long time that he recovers so fast." Salah is scheduled to rejoin full team training on Friday, with Slot confirming he would be available if Friday's session proceeds without issue.

Jeremie Frimpong's situation remains uncertain as he awaits scan results to determine the severity of the issue that forced his withdrawal during international duty with the Netherlands. While Netherlands' national team doctor indicated the substitution was precautionary, Liverpool will have clearer information following the scan conducted on Thursday. The full extent of Frimpong's injury will significantly impact Liverpool's defensive options for the cup tie.

In goalkeeper news, Alisson Becker has been ruled out for Saturday's match, dealing a blow to Liverpool's backline. However, there was better news regarding Federico Chiesa, who has recovered from the muscle problem that prevented him from playing for Italy during the international break. The Italian international trained with Liverpool on Thursday and will be available for selection against City.

Perhaps the most significant long-term news concerns Alexander Isak, who will return to full team training on Thursday for the first time since suffering an injury approximately three to four months ago. The Swedish striker's return comes at an ideal moment, with Slot expressing optimism about his recovery. Sweden's World Cup qualification on Wednesday evening added to the positive atmosphere surrounding Isak's comeback.
