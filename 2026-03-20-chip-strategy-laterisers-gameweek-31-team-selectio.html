Introduction

Panias, known in the Fantasy Premier League community as Lateriser, has established himself as one of the most consistent managers in the game, achieving three consecutive top 200 finishes. In this gameweek 31 team selection video, he breaks down his strategic approach for one of the most complex periods of the FPL season, where wildcard strategies and fixture congestion create difficult decisions for managers aiming to maximize their returns.

The video arrives at a critical juncture in the season, with Gameweek 30 still fresh in the memory. Lateriser achieved 58 points in Gameweek 30—a green arrow week that pushed him closer to the 500k ranking threshold for the first time this season, a milestone he hadn't even approached until recently. He reflects on the week with a mix of satisfaction and what-ifs, noting that he had saved a transfer but couldn't use it effectively because the available Chelsea attackers didn't fit his squad structure. His standout performers included Verb Rubin with seven points, Gabriel with nine, and Hill with an eight-pointer, while Timber's 35th-minute injury proved frustrating given Arsenal kept a clean sheet that otherwise would have yielded more points.

Gameweek 30 Review: Fortunes and Misfortunes

Looking back at his Gameweek 30 performance, Lateriser acknowledges both the positives and the unfortunate bounces that define FPL. He scored 58 points, which was enough for a green arrow—always welcome in the competitive environment where he operates. Verb Rubin delivered a respectable seven-pointer, while Gabriel exceeded expectations with nine points. However, Timber's injury in the 35th minute particularly stung, as the Arsenal defender was on course for a clean sheet before being forced off.

The Arsenal clean sheet itself felt fortunate to Lateriser, who believed his side experienced more than its share of bad luck that week. Orali should have accumulated some sort of points, he noted, having created chances without conceding much defensively. Holland similarly had opportunities, particularly after Cherki came on, but nothing materialized. On a more positive note, Thiago and Fernandez came through with points, and Hill's eight-pointer felt somewhat fortunate given the circumstances. With Richards on the bench, there was little else he could do but accept the result and move forward.

The Wildcard Dilemma: 32 or 35?

The central strategic question Lateriser faces revolves around which wildcard to play and when. His current team requires three transfers just to field a playable eleven without using a bench boost, and he's been conducting forward-looking analysis to determine whether a Wildcard 32 or Wildcard 35 strategy makes more sense. The decision isn't straightforward because it depends heavily on Crystal Palace's European results—if Palace lose in Europe, Lateriser doesn't anticipate selling his City assets and would likely lean toward a Wildcard 32 approach.

This decision carries significant weight because there's speculation about a potential Double Gameweek 32, which adds another layer of complexity to the planning. The uncertainty means he's focusing on what transfers make sense for this week rather than purely long-term considerations, though he acknowledges the need to eventually sell Timber, Harland, Richards, Orali, and Verbigan regardless of which wildcard path he chooses.

Liverpool and the Fixture Chaos

One key differentiator between Lateriser's team and those of other top managers like Zof and Process is their ownership of Liverpool assets—specifically Anderson and Virgil Van Dijk. Both players have favorable fixtures this week and decent-looking schedules in Gameweek 32, but Lateriser explains why this advantage has diminished somewhat.

The Liverpool fixture in Gameweek 32 became notably more difficult after their impressive performance against Atletico Madrid in the Champions League. Lateriser describes it as "one of the best performances of the season from Liverpool," with Spurs also delivering a strong attacking display in their Champions League match. The concern is that Liverpool now faces PSG in a Wednesday turnaround before their Gameweek 32 match, which creates meaningful rotation risk. Slot appears to have finally found his best starting XI, playing Salah, Diaz, and Gakpo together, and while Salah came off with a knock at the end, the outlook for his fitness looks positive for managers who already own him.

This rotation risk is significant for FPL managers considering Liverpool defenders. Combined with Fulham away not being an easy clean sheet fixture, Lateriser's inclination toward Liverpool defenders has reduced considerably.

Best Defensive Targets for the Run

For managers looking at the three-game window covering Gameweeks 31, 32, and 34, Lateriser identifies several teams worth targeting, though none offer perfect schedules.

Fulham defenders present a mixed picture. They play at home against Liverpool in Gameweek 31, which isn't ideal for clean sheet hopes, though Liverpool's attacking play suggests some goal-scoring potential for the opposition. In Gameweek 32, they face Liverpool away—not a favorable fixture by any means—followed by Aston Villa at home in Gameweek 34, another difficult matchup. They do have a decent chance against Burnley at home, but overall, Lateriser doesn't find Fulham particularly attractive for this three-game spell.

Spurs emerge as a more compelling option. Forest away in Gameweek 31 presents a good opportunity—Forest sit bottom three for expected goals conceded in the last six games despite creating numerous chances with low xG. Wolves in Gameweek 32 and Southampton in Gameweek 34 both represent favorable matchups. The return of Romero, Van de Ven, and Udogi means their first-choice back four is available again, with Romero particularly attractive for his set-piece involvement and bonus point potential.

Manchester United represents an interesting punt for the wildcard 35, free hit 33 strategy. They've improved defensively and sit in the top five or six for xGC in the last six games. While the Bournemouth fixture isn't ideal for a clean sheet, the fixture in Gameweek 32 (Leeds at home) and Gameweek 34 offers much better prospects. Budget options like Maguire or Dalot could become decent value picks if United's defensive improvement continues.

Attacking Options Worth Considering

In the attacking realm, Wilson catches Lateriser's eye as a particularly solid option. Newcastle's run of Burnley at home, Liverpool away, and Aston Villa at home represents a favorable sequence. Both Liverpool and Aston Villa are known for conceding numerous chances, making Wilson a reliable option across all three gameweeks despite the mixed fixture quality.

West Ham presents another attractive option, particularly for Bowen. Aston Villa's defense sits at the bottom of the xGC table in the last six games, making them vulnerable. Beyond that, West Ham has Wolves at home in Gameweek 32 and Everton at home in Gameweek 34, with Antonio expected to be fit by then—a significant boost to their attacking threat.

Spurs remain in the conversation given their favorable run, though the recent form of Solanke and Son provides mixed signals about their viability.
