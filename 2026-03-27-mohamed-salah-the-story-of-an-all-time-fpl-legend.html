Mohamed Salah: The story of an all-time FPL legend

The Full Story

Mohamed Salah's extraordinary Fantasy Premier League career is approaching its final chapter following the announcement on Tuesday evening that the Egyptian superstar will leave Liverpool at the end of the 2025/26 season. The news marks the end of an era for FPL managers who have relied on Salah as a cornerstone of their teams for nearly a decade, and FantasyFootballScout.co.uk has published a comprehensive retrospective celebrating his record-breaking Anfield tenure.

Salah, currently priced at £14.0m in FPL, has accumulated 2,216 total points during his Liverpool career—comprising 191 goals, 104 assists, and 232 bonus points. Though he will finish just short of catching Wayne Rooney (2,338) and Frank Lampard (2,318) at the top of the all-time FPL points table, his remarkable consistency across eight full seasons at Anfield has made him the undisputed greatest FPL asset in the game's history. Notably, only 34 of those career points came during his brief, underwhelming stint at Chelsea between 2013 and 2015, where he managed just 564 Premier League minutes before departing for Serie A—and ultimately returning to England with Liverpool in 2017 for an initial fee of £34m.

His 2024/25 season stands as the jewel in his FPL crown: 344 points from 29 goals, 18 assists, and 47 returns across 3,369 minutes, earning him 18 hauls of 10 or more points and a points-per-90 average of 9.19—the highest of any season in his career. That campaign surpassed even his legendary 2017/18 debut season (303 points, 32 goals, 44 returns) and included multiple explosive performances: tallies of 21, 18, 17, 16, 16, 15, 14, 14, and 14 points across various gameweeks. In total, Salah is the only player in FPL history to exceed 300 points in a single season—and he's done it twice.

Why This Matters

For the millions of FPL managers worldwide, Salah's departure signals the end of an era comparable to losing a franchise player in traditional fantasy sports. His remarkable availability (rarely missing matches through injury), elite-level goal involvement (averaging over a goal or assist per game throughout his Liverpool tenure), and consistent premium pricing have made him an almost mandatory pick in successful squads. His 2024/25 season demonstrated he remains the game's pre-eminent differential even at age 33, proving that managers who invested their precious budget in the Egyptian were rewarded with unparalleled returns.

The broader implications extend to FPL strategy itself. For years, Salah represented the "safe" premium option that allowed managers to load their squads with budget enablers elsewhere. His exit forces a fundamental recalibration of template teams and opens the door for other premium assets—likely Erling Haaland or Cole Palmer—to absorb that vacated budget. The final stretch of the 2025/26 season now takes on added significance as managers seek to maximize points with a player who may be playing his last handful of Premier League matches.

    • Context and Background

    Salah's FPL journey represents a remarkable transformation from bargain-basement pickup to generational asset. When Liverpool signed him from AS Roma in June 2017, FPL managers initially priced him at just £9.0m—reflecting skepticism about his ability to replicate his Serie A form in the Premier League. That doubt evaporated almost immediately: he scored a goal and an assist on his league debut against Watford, and by season's end had delivered 303 points, 32 goals, and the record-breaking 29-point haul against Watford that still stands as the FPL single-match benchmark.

    His arrival at Liverpool coincided with Jurgen Klopp's implementation of the high-pressing, counter-attacking system that would deliver Premier League and Champions League titles. Within that framework, Salah's pace, positioning, and prolific conversion rate made him the perfect right-wing striker, perpetually exploiting space behind opposition defenses. His consistency was staggering: even in supposedly "down" seasons like 2023/24 (211 points), he remained a top-tier asset that managers couldn't afford to bench.

    The decision to leave Liverpool comes after eight trophy-laden years that saw him win the Premier League, Champions League, FA Cup, two League Cups, and the Community Shield. At 33 years old, Salah appears to have several more elite seasons left in him, meaning his FPL legacy will continue elsewhere—potentially in the Saudi Pro League or another European league—after this season concludes.

    What's Next

    FPL managers face a bittersweet final stretch as Salah approaches his Anfield farewell. FantasyFootballScout's coverage suggests this is "time for a sentimental final hurrah" for managers still possessing the Egyptian king. With Liverpool pushing for the Premier League title in 2025/26, every remaining fixture carries added weight—not only for the club's trophy ambitions but for managers seeking to extract maximum points from their departing superstar.

    Upcoming fixtures against his favorite opponents offer particular appeal. Manchester United visit in Gameweek 35, a matchup where Salah has averaged 8.50 points across 16 previous encounters. Analytics expert Duncan Alexander noted that "including Man Utd players, Mohamed Salah is the eighth-highest Premier League scorer at Old Trafford in the 2020s"—a testament to his enduring dominance at the Theatre of Dreams.

    Beyond this season, the broader FPL landscape faces significant restructuring. Salah's eventual destination will determine whether managers can still access his services, while his departure creates a power vacuum at the top of the premium forward bracket. Managers should monitor his successor at Liverpool, transfer rumors, and the pricing of emerging premium options like Palmer and Haaland as they recalibrate strategies for 2026/27.
