Mohamed Salah's FPL Legacy: A Record-Breaking Era Comes to an End

The Full Story

Mohamed Salah's extraordinary Fantasy Premier League career is drawing to a close following the announcement on Tuesday evening that the Egyptian superstar will leave Liverpool at the end of the 2025/26 season. The news marks the end of an unprecedented era for FPL managers, who have relied on Salah as one of the most reliable and explosive assets the game has ever seen. The 33-year-old's departure from Anfield, where he arrived for an initial £34 million fee under Jurgen Klopp after a brief stint in Serie A, signals the conclusion of what can only be described as the greatest FPL tenure in the competition's history.

Throughout his Liverpool career, Salah accumulated 2,216 FPL points—comprising 191 goals, 104 assists, and 232 bonus points—placing him third on the all-time points list behind only Wayne Rooney (2,338) and Frank Lampard (2,318). Remarkably, his output at Chelsea before his Liverpool transformation accounts for just 34 of those points across 564 league minutes during the 2013/14 and 2014/15 seasons. From the moment he arrived at Liverpool priced at a modest £9.0m in 2017, Salah revolutionized FPL strategy and became the benchmark against which all other assets are measured.

The numbers from his eight full seasons at Liverpool tell a story of almost supernatural consistency. He is the only player in FPL history to exceed 300 points in a season—accomplishing this feat twice with 303 points in 2017/18 and a staggering 344 points in 2024/25. His lowest-ever seasonal total was 211 points in 2023/24, a figure that would represent an exceptional campaign for virtually any other player. His 29-point haul at home to Watford in 2017/18 remains the record for a single FPL match, a record he matched in Double Gameweek 24 last season.

Why This Matters

For the millions of FPL managers worldwide, Salah's departure represents more than just the loss of a premium asset—it signals the end of an era that fundamentally shaped how the game is played. For nearly a decade, Salah served as the cornerstone of countless championship-winning teams, his consistent point-per-game output and explosive ceiling making him an almost automatic selection in every manager's squad. His influence extended beyond mere statistics; he changed FPL psychology, making managers comfortable investing over 15% of their budget in a single player because his reliability made it worthwhile.

The broader implications ripple through the Premier League itself. Salah's departure from Liverpool marks the end of one of the most decorated eras in the club's history, during which he helped the Reds win the Premier League, Champions League, and numerous domestic trophies. From a pure footballing standpoint, his loss leaves a void that few players in world football can fill—both in terms of goal contribution and the intangible quality of consistently performing at the highest level match after match.

For the FPL community, the timing creates immediate strategic challenges. Managers who have built their squads around Salah must now scramble to redistribute his substantial £14.0m price tag, potentially reshaping team structures across the board. The ripple effects will be felt throughout the entire game, as thousands of managers make similar decisions simultaneously, creating unprecedented market volatility heading into the final stretch of the season.

    • Context and Background

    Salah's journey to FPL immortality began far from the spotlight he now occupies. His early Premier League tenure at Chelsea saw him receive limited opportunities, with the 564 minutes he played across two seasons insufficient to demonstrate his true capabilities. After being deemed not good enough for English football, Salah was shipped to Fiorentina and then Roma in Serie A, where he refined his game and transformed into one of Europe's most dangerous forwards.

    His £34m return to England in 2017 under Jurgen Klopp proved to be one of the greatest transfer bargains in Premier League history. From his very first full season, Salah demonstrated an insatiable appetite for goals and assists, combining electrifying pace with clinical finishing and an uncanny ability to find space in the most congested areas of the penalty box. His 32-goal debut campaign shattered records and established a standard of excellence that he has maintained ever since.

    The evolution of Salah's game over the years has been remarkable. While his goal-scoring prowess remained constant, he developed into an increasingly complete FPL asset, adding playmaking ability to his repertoire and accumulating assists that supplemented his inevitable goals. His 104 career assists at Liverpool—many arising from his signature cutback position—demonstrate how his game expanded beyond pure poaching to become a multi-dimensional attacking threat that consistently rewards managers regardless of formation or fixtures.

    Statistical analyst Duncan Alexander notably noted that "Including Man Utd players, Mohamed Salah is the eighth-highest PL scorer at Old Trafford in the 2020s," underscoring the remarkable nature of his away-day production. His predilection for destroying certain opponents—particularly Manchester United, Newcastle, and West Ham—made him an essential fixture in FPL squads whenever favorable fixture swings arose.

    What's Next

    As Salah enters the final stretch of his Liverpool career, FPL managers face the unenviable task of planning for a post-Salah world. The upcoming fixtures include a trip to Old Trafford in Gameweek 35, where Salah will look to add to his impressive 136 points against Manchester United and potentially deliver one last memorable haul against the club he has tormented throughout his Anfield tenure.

    For the sentimentally inclined, the remaining fixtures represent a farewell tour of sorts—an opportunity to appreciate one final chapter in what has been a historically significant FPL career. While concrete destination details remain uncertain, the announcement signals that managers should begin exploring budget reallocation strategies and identifying potential successors who might fill the void left by this extraordinary talent.

    The broader footballing world awaits Salah's next move with keen interest, as his departure from Liverpool opens a new chapter not only for the Reds but for the Premier League's competitive landscape. Regardless of where his career takes him next, the legacy he leaves behind in both real football and FPL terms is already firmly cemented as the gold standard against which future generations will be measured.
