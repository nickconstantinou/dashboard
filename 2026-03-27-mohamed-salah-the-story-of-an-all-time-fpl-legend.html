Mohamed Salah: The Story of an All-Time FPL Legend

The Full Story

Mohamed Salah's remarkable Fantasy Premier League career is approaching its conclusion following the Tuesday evening announcement that the Egyptian King will leave Liverpool at the end of the 2025/26 season. The 33-year-old, currently priced at £14.0m in FPL, departs as arguably the greatest asset the game has ever seen, having accumulated 2,216 total points through 191 goals, 104 assists, and 232 bonus points across his legendary tenure at Anfield.

The foundation of this legacy was laid in 2017 when Salah arrived from Serie A for an initial fee of £34m under manager Jurgen Klopp. Initially priced at just £9.0m by FPL Towers, he announced himself in spectacular fashion with a debut goal and assist at Watford—a match that would eventually yield his record-breaking 29-point haul. While 34 of his career points came during a brief, unsuccessful stint at Chelsea where he played just 564 league minutes across the 2013/14 and 2014/15 seasons, it was at Liverpool where he transformed into FPL royalty.

When all points are tallied at season's end, Salah will finish third on the all-time FPL points chart with 2,338 total points, trailing only Wayne Rooney (2,338) and Frank Lampard (2,318)—both of whom accumulated their totals across significantly longer Premier League careers. This remarkable achievement spans eight full campaigns at Liverpool, with his standout seasons including 303 points in 2017/18 (his record-breaking inaugural year) and an almost equally impressive 344 points in 2024/25, during which he notched 29 goals and 18 assists across just 3,369 minutes.

Why This Matters

For the millions of FPL managers worldwide, Salah's departure marks the end of an era comparable to losing a franchise player in traditional sports. His consistent excellence across multiple seasons—never finishing below 211 points in any of his eight full Liverpool campaigns—made him the closest thing the game has to a guaranteed weekly starter. His ability to deliver "haul" performances of 10 or more points became almost routine rather than exceptional, with his 2024/25 season alone featuring an extraordinary 18 such hauls, including towering scores of 21, 18, 17, 16, 16, 15, 14, 14, and 14 points.

The broader implications extend to how FPL managers will approach team construction in future seasons. Salah's consistent availability and productivity allowed managers to build their squads around him while maintaining flexibility elsewhere. His departure creates a significant void in the £14.0m price bracket and forces a complete recalibration of template teams that have reliably featured the Egyptian for seven years running. For those managers still competing in 2025/26, this announcement transforms the final stretch of the season into what the article describes as "a sentimental final stretch"—a last opportunity to benefit from one of the game's most prolific assets.

The timing of the announcement also carries strategic weight, coming during the business end of the season when many managers face critical decisions about captaincy, transfers, and wildcard usage. Those who hadn't yet capitulated their season now have a pressing reason to maximize whatever returns Salah can provide in his remaining fixtures.

    • Context & Background

    Salah's journey to FPL immortality traces an unconventional path. Following his initial Premier League spell at Chelsea, where limited opportunities resulted in just 564 minutes of action, he was deemed insufficient for the English top flight and transferred to Fiorentina in Serie A. His subsequent transformation into one of Europe's most devastating forwards came during two stellar seasons with Roma, where his clinical finishing and pace attracted Liverpool's attention in 2017.

    The £34m investment proved to be one of the most transformative signings in Premier League history. Beyond his FPL contributions, Salah helped propel Liverpool to Champions League glory in 2019 and their first league title in 30 years in 2020. His relationship with FPL proved symbiotic—his consistent goal-scoring and involvement in Liverpool's attacking moves translated directly to points, while his reliability made him a fixture in approximately 50% of all FPL squads at any given time.

    His consistency defied typical footballing patterns. Where other premium assets often suffer from rotation, form slumps, or injury problems, Salah maintained an almost machine-like reliability. Even seasons where he "underperformed" by his own extraordinary standards—like his 211-point 2023/24 campaign—would represent career-best years for most other players.

    What's Next

    For FPL managers, the immediate focus is on maximizing Salah's remaining fixtures. With Manchester United visiting in Gameweek 35—one of his favorite opponents where he's accumulated 136 points across 16 appearances—the Egyptian will be a near-mandatory captaincy choice for those still active in their leagues.

    Beyond this season, the FPL community faces an uncertain future without Salah as a cornerstone asset. Managers must identify potential replacements in the £14.0m bracket, potentially turning to other premium forwards or spreading budget across mid-priced options. The 2026/27 season will require entirely new team-building strategies, with no established template featuring a guaranteed premium starter at his price point.

    As noted in the article's reporting of a social media post from data analyst Duncan Alexander (@oilysailor): "Including Man Utd players, Mohamed Salah is the eighth-highest PL scorer at Old Trafford in the 2020s"—underscoring that even in his later years, Salah continues to rewrite the record books.

    For now, though, the sentiment is celebratory rather than strategic. The article frames the remaining weeks as an opportunity to honor "his record-breaking, title-winning Anfield career" and appreciate what may well be the greatest individual FPL asset the game will ever see.
