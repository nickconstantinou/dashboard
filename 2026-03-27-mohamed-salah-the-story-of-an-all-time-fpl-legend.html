Mohamed Salah: The Complete Story of an FPL Legend's Historic Liverpool Career

The Full Story

Mohamed Salah is set to depart Liverpool at the conclusion of the 2025/26 season, bringing an end to one of the most spectacular Fantasy Premier League chapters ever written. The Egyptian forward, priced at £14.0m in his final FPL campaign, will leave Anfield having accumulated 2,216 total points across his storied career—placing him third on the all-time FPL points list behind only Wayne Rooney (2,338) and Frank Lampard (2,318).

Salah's journey to FPL immortality began after a modest 564-minute stint at Chelsea during the 2013/14 and 2014/15 seasons, where he managed just one goal and one assist against Arsenal before being transferred to Serie A. Liverpool manager Jurgen Klopp then brought him to Merseyside for an initial £34m fee, and what followed became the foundation of FPL legend.

Throughout his eight full seasons with Liverpool, Salah demonstrated unprecedented consistency, becoming the only player in FPL history to exceed 300 points in a season—accomplishing this remarkable feat twice. His 2017/18 campaign stands as the gold standard: 303 points, 32 goals, 12 assists, 44 returns, and 15 hauls of 10 or more points, including a record-setting 29-point haul against Watford that still stands as the highest single-match score in FPL history. His 2024/25 season came close to matching this, with 344 points across 29 goals and 18 assists.

Why This Matters

For the millions of FPL managers who have built their seasons around Salah, his departure represents the end of an era equivalent to losing a guaranteed points machine. For seven consecutive seasons (excluding the current campaign), Salah never finished below 211 points—a level of consistency that transformed him from a mere player selection into a season-defining strategy for competitive FPL players.

His influence extends beyond individual team management. Salah's presence has shaped entire FPL meta-strategies, forced gameweek planning decisions, and created a fundamental question every manager must answer: do you captain Salah or risk falling behind those who do? His departure will fundamentally reshape how FPL managers approach the game, creating a vacuum at the top of the points-per-90 rankings and opening opportunities for other premium assets.

The timing of his announcement—during the final stretch of the 2025/26 season—adds another dimension. Managers now face the prospect of planning for life without Liverpool's talisman while simultaneously navigating their final gameweeks, potentially scrambling to identify capable replacements or pivot strategies entirely.

    Context and Background

    Salah's rise from Chelsea afterthought to global football icon mirrors his transformation in FPL circles. When he initially joined Liverpool in June 2017, FPL managers priced him at just £9.0m—a bargain that immediately rewarded early adopters with his debut goal and assist against Watford. Within weeks, his price began its relentless climb, never dropping below premium territory.

    His 2017/18 breakout season coincided with Liverpool's evolution into Premier League title contenders under Klopp, with Salah forming part of a devastating front three alongside Sadio Mané and Roberto Firmino. That attacking unit became FPL gold, with Salah emerging as the most reliable differential and captaincy option season after season.

    Throughout his Liverpool career, Salah demonstrated an uncanny ability to perform against specific opponents, particularly Manchester United, Newcastle, and West Ham. His visits to Old Trafford made him a fixture among the Premier League's top scorers at that venue during the 2020s, earning mention as the eighth-highest PL scorer at Old Trafford in that decade.

    Interestingly, his struggles against Burnley and Chelsea—teams he once represented or faced regularly—created rare pockets of differential value when managers sought to avoid him in those fixtures.

    FPL managers now face urgent decisions as the 2025/26 season reaches its conclusion. With Salah confirmed to depart, attention will immediately shift to identifying his long-term replacement—whether that means redistributing funds across other premium assets or banking on Liverpool's incoming forwards to maintain attacking output.

    The sentiment surrounding his final games at Anfield adds emotional weight to what were already crucial gameweeks. Managers face a unique dilemma: maximize Salah's remaining points potential in his farewell appearances while simultaneously planning for a Liverpool attack that will look dramatically different next season.

    Reaction from the FPL community has been swift, with #Salah and #FPL trending across social platforms as managers share memories of championship-winning seasons built around the Egyptian King. FantasyFootballScout's coverage will continue tracking his final appearances, providing analysis for those seeking one last hurrah from the greatest FPL asset in the game's modern era.

    For those managers still mathematically alive in their mini-leagues, Salah's remaining fixtures—particularly his visit to Manchester United in Gameweek 35—could prove decisive. His historic average of 8.50 points against United suggests one final memorable performance awaits, offering a fitting farewell to an FPL career that may never be matched.
