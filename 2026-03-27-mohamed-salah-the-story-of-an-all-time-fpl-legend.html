Mohamed Salah: The Story of an All-Time FPL Legend

The Full Story

Mohamed Salah's remarkable tenure at Liverpool is drawing to a close, with the Egyptian superstar confirming on Tuesday evening that he will depart Anfield at the conclusion of the 2025/26 season. The announcement marks the end of an era for Fantasy Premier League managers who have relied on the 33-year-old as perhaps the most dependable asset in the game's history. Originally acquired by Jurgen Klopp for an initial £34 million fee from Serie A, Salah's journey to FPL immortality began in earnest during the 2017/18 campaign, when he was priced at a mere £9.0 million by FPL administrators—perhaps the greatest bargain in the game's history.

Throughout his Liverpool career, Salah accumulated 2,216 total FPL points,comprising 191 goals, 104 assists, and 232 bonus points. These totals place him third on the all-time FPL points chart, trailing only Wayne Rooney (2,338) and Frank Lampard (2,318). Notably, 34 of those points came during his brief, uninspiring stint at Chelsea between 2013/14 and 2014/15, where he managed just 564 Premier League minutes before being shipped to Italy and eventually returning to England with Liverpool.

The statistics from his Anfield years are staggering. Salah is the only player in FPL history to exceed 300 points in a single season, achieving this feat twice—first with 303 points in 2017/18 (32 goals, 12 assists) and then with an extraordinary 344 points in 2024/25 (29 goals, 18 assists). His 29-point haul against Watford in 2017/18 remains the record for a single match, a performance that set the template for years of consistent excellence. Across his eight full campaigns at Liverpool, Salah never dipped below 211 points, demonstrating remarkable durability and sustained returns.

Why This Matters

For the millions of FPL managers who have selected Salah season after season, his departure represents the end of an era comparable to losing a franchise player in traditional fantasy sports. The Egyptian has served as the cornerstone of countless championship-winning teams, with his remarkable consistency making him arguably the safest captaincy option in the game's history. His combination of goals, assists, and bonus point accumulation created a virtual points machine that rewarded managers who showed unwavering faith in his abilities.

Beyond individual FPL implications, Salah's exit signals a profound shift in Premier League dynamics. Since his arrival from Roma in 2017, he has been a defining figure in English football's most-watched league, helping Liverpool secure Premier League and Champions League glory while establishing himself among the club's greatest-ever players. His departure will leave a creative and goalscoring void that Liverpool must somehow fill as they enter a new era under their legendary manager Klopp.

The timing of this announcement also carries significant FPL strategy implications. With the 2025/26 season nearing its conclusion as of the March 26, 2026 announcement, managers now face urgent decisions about how to maximize their remaining transfers and captaincy choices. The upcoming Gameweek 35 fixture against Manchester United—one of Salah's favorite opponents—takes on added significance as fans and managers alike prepare to bid farewell to a once-in-a-generation FPL asset.

    • Context and Background

    Salah's path to FPL immortality was far from linear. After arriving at Chelsea as a young prospect in January 2014, he struggled to establish himself in the Premier League, managing just 564 league minutes across two seasons before being deemed not good enough for the Blues' first team. The move to Serie A with Fiorentina, and later Roma, appeared to signal the end of his Premier League ambitions. However, Klopp's decision to bring him to Anfield in June 2017 proved transformative, not just for Liverpool's trophy ambitions but for the entire FPL landscape.

    The 2017/18 season remains the stuff of legend. Priced at just £9.0m, Salah exceeded even the most optimistic projections, finishing with 32 goals and 12 assists to accumulate 303 points. His explosive pace, eye for goal, and consistent involvement in Liverpool's attacking moves made him an essential pick that season and every season thereafter. What distinguished Salah from other premium forwards was his remarkable consistency across multiple dimensions—he provided goals, assists, and crucially, a high proportion of bonus points due to his tendency to score or create in matches where others might not.

    Throughout his Liverpool career, Salah demonstrated a particular penchant for certain opponents. His record against Manchester United is especially remarkable, with 136 points from just 16 appearances translating to an average of 8.50 points per match. Statistician Duncan Alexander noted that Salah is "the eighth-highest PL scorer at Old Trafford in the 2020s," underscoring his remarkable consistency at one of football's most challenging venues. His six-haul tally against West Ham further cements his status as a Fantasy favorite against specific opposition.

    Interestingly, Salah has shown notable struggles against certain teams, most notably Burnley. Despite facing the Clarets 11 times, he managed only two goals—a puzzling statistic given his prolific record elsewhere. Similarly, his five goals from 16 appearances against his former club Chelsea represent a surprisingly modest return, perhaps explained by the psychological complexity of facing former employers.

    What's Next

    For FPL managers, the immediate focus turns to maximizing Salah's remaining appearances in what has been a transitional 2025/26 season. With 97 points already accumulated this campaign from just five goals and six assists across limited minutes (1,828), his production rate of 4.78 points per 90 minutes suggests he remains highly effective when on the pitch. The Gameweek 35 fixture against Manchester United at Old Trafford represents a particularly attractive captaincy option, given his exceptional record against the Red Devils.

    The broader question surrounding Salah's next destination remains unanswered. Reports do not specify where the Egyptian will continue his career following his Liverpool departure, though suitors across Europe's top leagues will surely emerge for one of the sport's most proven performers. Whether he joins another Premier League club (which would present fascinating FPL dynamics given his intimate knowledge of the league) or seeks a new challenge elsewhere, his remarkable consistency suggests he will remain a force to be reckoned with in whatever competition he participates in.

    For Liverpool and Klopp, the departure of their talismanic number 11 signals the beginning of a significant rebuild. The £34m investment in 2017 has yielded an immeasurable return both in trophies and astronomical FPL points, and replacing such a multifaceted attacking threat will prove one of the club's most challenging tasks. As for FPL managers, the era of automatic Salah selection is drawing to a close, leaving millions to contemplate how they will navigate future seasons without their most reliable ally.
