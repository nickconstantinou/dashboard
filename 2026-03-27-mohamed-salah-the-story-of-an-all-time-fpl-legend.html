Mohamed Salah: The story of an all-time FPL legend

The Full Story

Mohamed Salah's unprecedented run as Fantasy Premier League's greatest asset is approaching its conclusion. On Tuesday evening, it was announced that the Egyptian superstar will leave Liverpool at the end of the 2025/26 season, bringing an end to a remarkable chapter in both the Reds' history and FPL's record books. The announcement marks the beginning of what FPL managers are calling "a sentimental final stretch" for a player who has fundamentally shaped how the game is played and strategized.

Salah arrived at Anfield from AS Roma in 2017 for an initial fee of £34 million, signed by Jurgen Klopp, and proceeded to transform himself from a Chelsea reserve into arguably the most consequential player in FPL history. His journey began with an initial price of just £9.0m, and his very first involvement saw him score and provide an assist at Watford—the beginning of a love affair with the Hornets that would produce his record-breaking 29-point haul in 2017/18. Before his Liverpool era, Salah had accumulated only 34 FPL points during 564 Premier League minutes at Chelsea across 2013/14 and 2014/15, with modest contributions against Arsenal and Stoke City.

The statistics of his Anfield career paint a picture of almost superhuman consistency. Across his eight completed seasons at Liverpool through 2025/26, Salah has accumulated 2,216 total FPL points, including 191 goals and 104 assists, along with 232 bonus points. He will conclude his career as the third-highest FPL scorer of all time, behind only Wayne Rooney (2,338 points) and Frank Lampard (2,318 points). His 2024/25 season stands as the second-highest scoring campaign in FPL history with 344 points, trailing only his own 2017/18 record of 303 points—the only two times any player has broken the 300-point barrier in a single season. His 18 hauls of 10+ points in 2024/25 alone demonstrates the regularity with which he returns explosive scores.

Why This Matters

For the millions of FPL managers who have built their seasons around his presence—or suffered the consequences of excluding him—this departure signals the end of an era that has defined competitive FPL strategy for nearly a decade. Salah's consistency has been so extraordinary that his ownership rates and captaincy selections have become a defining feature of each season's meta. Managers who invested in him often gained a significant advantage; those who didn't found themselves constantly chasing his points total.

The implications extend beyond individual team management. FPL pricing algorithms, differential strategies, and overall game theory have all been shaped by Salah's sustained excellence. His departure will force a fundamental recalibration of how managers approach team construction, budget allocation, and premium player selection. The question of who becomes the next " Salah-type" asset—someone who offers both reliability and explosive potential at premium pricing—will dominate FPL community discourse for seasons to come.

For Liverpool, his exit marks the end of a trophy-winning era, with Salah having contributed significantly to their Premier League title, Champions League victory, and numerous other honors during his Anfield tenure.

    • Context and Background

    Salah's rise to FPL dominance began after a transformative spell at AS Roma, where he rebuilt his reputation following an underwhelming initial stint at Chelsea. Klopp's tactical system proved the perfect environment for Salah's explosive pace, positioning, and finishing ability. His integration into Liverpool's gegenpressing approach created an endless supply of goal-scoring opportunities, while his set-piece duties—including penalties—provided a reliable floor of points even in matches where he didn't score.

    The FPL community has adapted its strategies around Salah's presence. His price has grown from that initial £9.0m to his current £14.0m valuation, yet his points-per-million ratio has remained exceptional. His ability to deliver "hauls" (matches worth 10+ points) became the gold standard for premium assets—his 2024/25 season featured 18 such performances, with his biggest tallies including scores of 21, 18, 17, 16, 16, 15, 14, 14, and 14 points in individual matches.

    His psychological impact on the game cannot be overstated. Some managers have built entire team structures around "being Salah-proof"—accepting that they would never match his ceiling but trying to minimize the damage of his consistent returns. Others have made him the cornerstone of their squads, often sacrificing depth elsewhere to afford his premium price.

    What's Next

    With Liverpool still competing in the 2025/26 season, FPL managers face a unique challenge: how to maximize Salah's remaining fixtures while planning for a future without him. His final appearances include a visit to Manchester United at Old Trafford in Gameweek 35, where his record of 136 points from 16 matches suggests one last opportunity for an explosive return. As noted by statistics expert Duncan Alexander, Salah ranks as the eighth-highest Premier League scorer at Old Trafford in the 2020s including matches against United, underscoring his reliability at hostile venues.

    For the broader FPL landscape, the post-Salah era presents both challenges and opportunities. New premium assets will emerge to fill the void he leaves as a consistent captaincy option. Budget templates will need revision. The hunt for "the next Salah"—a player who combines reliability, ceiling, and favorable fixture scheduling—will become the game's central obsession.

    FPL managers are advised to pursue what some are calling "one last hurrah" from Salah's remaining appearances while simultaneously beginning the difficult process of transitioning their squads for life after the Egyptian King. His departure, when it comes, will mark the end of an era that redefined what premium FPL assets could achieve and how the game could be strategically approached.
