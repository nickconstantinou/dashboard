FPL Challenge best picks + team reveal – Gameweek 32

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

The beauty of FPL Challenge lies in its volatility. While the standard FPL game locks after the deadline, this variant stays open until kick-off—meaning managers can react to team news, late injuries, and, most importantly, the specific format twist for that week. That flexibility is both a curse and a gift: it rewards those who understand the unique scoring mechanics, and punishes those who simply copy-paste their main squad.

This week, the challenge format appears to reward two specific things: clean sheets and shots from outside the box. That's a narrower lens than some weeks, and it changes the calculus entirely. Goalkeepers with clean sheet potential become essential, while defenders who supplement that defensive solidity with long-range attempts move to the top of the shopping list.

Here's how to approach Gameweek 32.

Goalkeepers: Clean Sheets Are Everything

In a format where goalkeepers aren't expected to attempt shots from outside the box, the path to points is straightforward: keep a clean sheet, or don't bother.

David Raya stands out immediately. Arsenal host Bournemouth at the Emirates, and the visitors' attacking output has been toothless on the road this season. Raya's underlying numbers are strong, and Arsenal's defensive structure under their current setup has been notably solid. He's the clearest route to goalkeeper points this week.

Brighton also present a compelling option. Second-from-bottom Burnley look destined for relegation—just as they appeared at the start of the season—and the Seagulls have kept back-to-back Premier League clean sheets. Bart Verbruggen has been reliable between the posts, and the fixture couldn't be much friendlier.

For those looking elsewhere, West Ham's Mads Hermansen hosts Wolves at home, Brentford's Caoimhin Kelleher faces an Everton side struggling for goals, and Liverpool's Giorgi Marmardashvili welcomes Fulham to Anfield. All three have clean sheet potential, though the fixtures vary in attractiveness.

Defenders: The Dual Threat

This is where the format twist gets interesting. The challenge appears to reward defenders who not only keep clean sheets but also take shots from outside the box—the kind of player who can accumulate points through multiple avenues.

Neco Williams is the standout. Nottingham Forest's full-back has produced more shots from outside the box than any other defender in the game, and Forest host Aston Villa this week. That's a remarkable stat, and it makes Williams the most complete option for this format: defensive solidity plus genuine attacking threat from distance.

Ferdi Kadioglu fits a similar profile. Brighton face Burnley, and with the Seagulls having kept consecutive clean sheets, Kadioglu offers the rare combination of shut-out potential and long-range shooting ability. He's underowned in many squads, which makes him even more attractive for differential purposes.

Other considerations include West Ham's Konstantinos Mavropanos against Wolves, Gabriel at Arsenal, Virgil van Dijk of Liverpool, and Manchester City's Nico O'Reilly versus Chelsea. These are primarily clean sheet plays—you're not expecting them to rifle in screamers from 25 yards—but the defensive points, possible DefCons, and occasional penalty-box presence add up.

Midfielders: Szoboszlai and Fernandes Lead the Way

Dominik Szoboszlai is the marquee pick this week. No midfielder in the Premier League has produced more shots from outside the box than the Hungarian this season. Liverpool host Fulham, a fixture that should offer plenty of possession and shooting opportunities. Szoboszlai's ceiling is enormous—he can score from distance, hit the post, earn bonus points for attempts, and contribute to clean sheets. In a format that rewards exactly what he does best, he's arguably the first name on the team sheet.

Bruno Fernandes is equally compelling. Leeds United have conceded nearly two goals on average away from home—a catastrophic record that makes Manchester United attackers extremely appealing. Fernandes has been one of the best assets in FPL this season due to his goal involvement, but in this challenge format, his set-piece-taking and occasional long-range efforts add another layer of scoring potential.
