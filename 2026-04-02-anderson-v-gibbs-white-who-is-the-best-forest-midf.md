# Anderson v Gibbs-White: Who is the Best Forest Midfielder in FPL?

## The Full Story

Nottingham Forest's upcoming fixture run presents a compelling opportunity for Fantasy Premier League managers, with the team set to face Aston Villa, Burnley, and Sunderland across the next three Gameweeks—all three opponents currently reside in the bottom half of the form table over the last six matches, with Sunderland having lost three consecutive home games. This favorable schedule has FPL managers weighing whether to invest in Elliot Anderson (£5.5m) or Morgan Gibbs-White (£7.4m) as their Forest asset of choice.

The statistical comparison reveals a stark contrast between the two midfielders' attacking output. Gibbs-White emerges as the dominant goal threat, having scored his ninth league goal of the season—a strike against Tottenham Hotspur prior to the international break that made him Forest's top scorer in the top-flight by a considerable margin. His underlying numbers are equally impressive: the England international registers a goal every 292.9 minutes, a shot every 40.6 minutes, and has accumulated 12 Opta-defined "big chances" across the campaign. His minutes per expected goal (xG) stands at 316.1, indicating consistent involvement in high-quality chances.

Anderson, by contrast, operates in a deeper midfield role that has historically limited his goal threat. However, under manager Vitor Pereira, he has been granted marginally more freedom to push forward, recording a shot every 48.1 minutes during the Gameweeks 27-31 period. The critical caveat is that all of his attempts have come from distance, with his xG per shot measuring just 0.04—suggesting low-quality opportunities rather than genuine goal-scoring chances. The paywalled "Assist Potential" section would presumably contain deeper creative metrics, but the available data clearly favors Gibbs-White for those prioritizing goal contributions.

## Why This Matters

This comparison matters because FPL managers face constant value-versus-potential calculations, and the £1.9 million price difference between these two Forest assets creates a significant budget impact. Gibbs-White's superior goal threat makes him the default choice for managers seeking attacking returns, but his premium price point forces difficult trade-offs elsewhere in the squad. Anderson's budget-friendly valuation (£5.5m) could allow managers to upgrade key positions elsewhere while still maintaining a stake in Forest's favorable run of fixtures.

The underlying tactical context—Pereira's appointment not diminishing Gibbs-White's attacking numbers—also suggests the former Wolves loanee represents the safer long-term hold, even if the immediate fixture swing makes Anderson tempting for short-term gains.

Morgan Gibbs-White's breakout season comes under the guidance of Vitor Pereira, who took over at Forest midway through the 2025-26 campaign. Notably, Pereira's appointment has not dampened Gibbs-White's attacking output—the player's shot map during Gameweeks 27-31 demonstrates continued involvement in dangerous areas. FPL managers should monitor Anderson's role evolution under Pereira—if the Portuguese manager continues to grant him additional attacking license, his ceiling could rise significantly.
