Mbeumo Dropped as Carrick Resets United Side for Old Trafford Showdown

Bryan Mbeumo finds himself on the bench for the first time since Michael Carrick took charge at Manchester United, as the Red Devils make four changes for Monday's visit of Leeds United.

The Cameroon international has been a staple of United's line-up under Carrick, but finds himself demoted as the manager shuffles his attacking options. Benjamin Šeško comes into the side alongside the returning Lisandro Martínez, with Noussair Mazraoui and Manuel Ugarte also drafted in from the start.

Carrick's hand has been forced in two areas. Harry Maguire is suspended, while Kobbie Mainoo suffered an injury in training over the weekend and misses out entirely. Diogo Dalot's demotion to the bench may also have a fitness dimension — the Portuguese full-back has been ill in the past week.

For Leeds, four fresh faces enter from the side that lost to West Ham in the FA Cup last Sunday. Karl Darlow, Dominic Calvert-Lewin, Gabriel Gudmundsson, and Brenden Aaronson all return, with Lucas Perri and Lukas Nmecha dropping to the bench. Joe Rodon and Anton Stach join Leeds' growing injury list.

Notably, Gudmundsson, Jaka Bijol, and Noah Okafor have all overcome minor issues to take their places in the starting XI.

Manchester United XI

Lammens, Mazraoui, Yoro, Martínez, Shaw, Casemiro, Ugarte, Diallo, Fernandes, Cunha, Šeško.

Subs: Bayindir, Dalot, Mount, Zirkzee, Malacia, Mbeumo, Heaven, Fletcher, Lacey.

Leeds United XI

Darlow, Justin, Bijol, Struijk, Bogle, Tanaka, Ampadu, Aaronson, Gudmundsson, Calvert-Lewin, Okafor.

Subs: Perri, Longstaff, Piroe, Nmecha, Bornauw, Byram, Gnonto, Buonanotte, Gruev.

---

The fixture rounds off Gameweek 32 at Old Trafford, with kick-off at 20:00 BST. For FPL managers, the benching of Mbeumo — a player likely heavily weighted in squad constructions — could have significant implications depending on how the evening unfolds.
