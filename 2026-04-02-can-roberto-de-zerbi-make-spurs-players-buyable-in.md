# Can Roberto De Zerbi Make Tottenham's Struggling Players Viable in Fantasy Premier League?

## The Full Story

Tottenham Hotspur has appointed Roberto De Zerbi as their new head coach in what represents a dramatic attempt to pull the club away from the Premier League relegation zone. The appointment replaces Igor Tudor, whose brief 44-day reign has ended as Tottenham finds itself in genuine danger of top-flight demotion for the first time in nearly 50 years. Currently sitting just one point above 18th place, Spurs have managed only five points from their last 13 matches and remain winless since December 28th, leaving fans of rival clubs no longer able to joke about the club's precarious position.

The appointment comes despite Tottenham having been in third place as recently as Gameweek 9, suggesting that last season's 17th-place finish—their worst Premier League performance—was far from a statistical anomaly. Over the subsequent 22 matches, the club has managed just two wins and 13 points, a collapse that has coincided with severe regression in both attack and defense. While the squad has outperformed expected goals metrics (scoring 40 goals against an expected 32.51), recent form shows only five goals in their last seven outings, and defensively the situation is catastrophic. Since Gameweek 21, Tottenham are the sole Premier League team without a single clean sheet, with 10 of their last 11 matches seeing at least two goals conceded—a league-high 26 goals against an expected 21.06.

De Zerbi arrives with a substantial managerial pedigree, most notably from his time at Brighton & Hove Albion where he guided the club to a remarkable sixth-place finish in the 2022/23 Premier League season. His most recent role was at Olympique Marseille, where he led the club to second place in Ligue 1 last season before departing midway through the current campaign with the team sitting fourth. The Italian coach is known for his attacking, possession-based philosophy, but also for a volatile personality that earned him nine yellow cards and two red cards during his time in England.

## Why This Matters

This appointment carries massive implications for both Tottenham's immediate survival hopes and the broader Fantasy Premier League landscape. For real-world football fans, the question is whether De Zerbi can arrest a freefall that has seen a historically stable top-flight club face the very real prospect of relegation—a fate that would have seemed unthinkable months ago. The coach has proven he can transform attacking teams and extract premium performances from creative players, but he inherits a defensive nightmare that has seen the team concede at an alarming rate.

For the millions of FPL managers worldwide, De Zerbi's arrival potentially reshapes the value proposition of several Tottenham assets. The Italian's tactical system at Brighton produced multiple premium FPL picks, and his appointment could theoretically revive players who have become undraftable due to the club's structural collapse. However, the dual concerns of relegation risk (which would remove many players from FPL consideration entirely) and De Zerbi's well-documented disciplinary issues create significant uncertainty. Managers face a complex calculus: is this an opportunity to capitalize on suppressed player values before a potential turnaround, or a trap that could see investment wiped out by the twin specters of the drop zone and managerial volatility?

## Context & Background

Tottenham's dramatic fall from third place to relegation danger represents one of the most spectacular collapses in recent Premier League history. The club has been ever-present in the top flight since the Premier League era began, and it has been almost 50 years since their last top-flight demotion. This historical stability makes the current situation all the more shocking for supporters and observers alike.

The managerial chaos compounds the problem. Tudor was appointed as the successor to the previous head coach, but his tenure lasted less than six weeks, suggesting deep-seated structural issues within the club that go beyond tactical matters. De Zerbi's willingness to take charge immediately—rather than waiting until summer—indicates both the severity of the situation and perhaps the former Shakhtar Donetsk and Sassuolo manager's confidence in his ability to implement rapid change.

De Zerbi's reputation was built largely on his innovative tactical approach at Brighton, where he transformed a mid-table Premier League side into European contenders through an attacking, high-possession style that produced numerous quality chances. His subsequent move to Marseille showed mixed results, with strong domestic league finishes but questions about whether he could consistently compete at the highest European level. Now he faces arguably his greatest challenge: turning around a club in freefall while simultaneously addressing catastrophic defensive frailties that seem at odds with his natural attacking instincts.

## What's Next

FPL managers will be closely monitoring several developments in the coming weeks. The most pressing question is whether De Zerbi can implement meaningful defensive improvements quickly enough to prevent further hemorrhaging of goals. His historical record suggests attacking prowess, but Tottenham's current defensive statistics represent an extreme outlier that even the most optimistic manager would struggle to ignore.

The fixture run offers some hope—Spurs have the seventh-best run-in according to the Fixture Ticker, and with four of their final seven matches away from home (where they've actually performed slightly better), there are paths to points. However, the psychological damage of a season-long collapse combined with a new manager's tactical demands could produce immediate growing pains.

For FPL purposes, the key watchpoints are whether any Tottenham players emerge as reliable attacking threats under the new system, whether the defensive situation stabilizes enough to occasionally keep clean sheets, and—most critically—whether Tottenham can accumulate enough points to avoid relegation altogether. A failure on that front would render all other considerations moot for FPL purposes, as relegated players become unavailable for the following season's game. Managers considering Tottenham assets must weigh the potential upside of De Zerbi's attacking philosophy against the very real possibility that their investment could disappear from the game entirely.
