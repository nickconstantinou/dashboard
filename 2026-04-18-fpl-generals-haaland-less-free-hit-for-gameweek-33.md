# The Contrarian's Gamble: FPL General's Bold Free Hit Without Haaland

**Erling Haaland is owned by nearly everyone. FPL General owns nobody. Here's why he thinks that still makes sense.**

When Double Gameweek 33 arrives, the smart money will be on Haaland. Owned by the majority of Fantasy Premier League managers, Erling Haaland (£14.5m) represents the obvious play for a week with two matches on the board. The Norwegian is reliable, prolific, and—when fixtures double—a fixture worth loading up on.

FPL General isn't loading up. He's Free Hitting around the big man entirely.

It's a strategy he's stuck with all season, and one he sees no reason to abandon now—even when the calendar offers what looks like a Haaland celebration.

"I've avoided him all year," FPL General explained. "Double Gameweek 33 isn't going to change that."

The approach has cost him. It may continue to cost him. But after climbing from 2.2 million in Gameweek 24 to a top-500k finish, he's not about to flip the script on principle alone.

---

## Gameweek 32: The Climb Continues

The Troops entered the world's top 500k after Gameweek 32—a milestone that's been "very enjoyable," FPL General said. The week delivered 75 points, a jump from 639k to 434k, with contributions across the pitch.

The headline move was Jarrod Bowen (£7.6m), brought back in for Mateus Mane (£4.3m). The West Ham talisman rewarded the timing immediately, delivering three assists and some defensive contributions on his return. It was a textbook case of identifying a player at the right moment rather than chasing names on reputation.

"Perfectly timed," FPL General said of the Bowen move.

The week's supporting cast did the heavy lifting. Nico O'Reilly (£5.0m), Virgil van Dijk (£6.3m), and Igor Thiago (£7.3m) all contributed, keeping the climb alive while FPL General conserved his second free transfer.

One close call: Emiliano Martinez (£5.1m) was lost during Aston Villa's warm-up, a heart-stopping moment for any manager with the Villa keeper locked in. Martinez returned between the sticks in midweek, and the situation resolved without damage to the squad.

The more lasting concern came from elsewhere. Hugo Ekitike (£9.2m) suffered a serious injury, ending his usefulness for the immediate future. FPL General held onto him through the week—Ekitike would've been valuable for Blank Gameweek 34—but the transfer out is coming next week. The likely replacement: Ollie Watkins (£8.5m).

## The Free Hit Calculus

The Free Hit is now active for Double Gameweek 33. And it's deployed in a way that commits further to the anti-Haaland stance.

For most managers, a Double Gameweek Free Hit means one thing: load up on players from the teams playing twice, squeeze in Haaland if humanly possible, and extract maximum points from a compressed schedule. The logic is sound. Two matches. More minutes. More chances. More returns.

FPL General's logic runs differently. The Free Hit lets him optimize for his own squad structure without compromising the season-long plan. It means fielding a team built around different assets—ones that fit the broader vision rather than chasing one week's obvious play.

It's not that Haaland is bad. It's that building a squad around Haaland creates constraints that FPL General isn't willing to accept. The price point, the captaincy decisions, the opportunity cost of alternatives—all of it shapes a team that doesn't match how he wants to play the season.

The Free Hit gives him flexibility within those constraints. He can experiment, adapt, and maximize without breaking the framework he's maintained for 33 weeks.

---

## What's At Stake

The strategy carries risk. Haaland in a Double Gameweek is almost designed to punish anyone who isn't invested. If he hauls—two goals, an assist, a bonus point or three—he'll widen the ranks of managers who've backed him while FPL General watches from the sideline.

But FPL General has navigated 32 weeks this way. He's sitting inside 500k, climbing fast, and still believes the long game matters more than one week's pressure.

The Free Hit for Gameweek 33 isn't a rejection of the Double Gameweek opportunity. It's a different way of accepting it—one that stays consistent with everything he's built so far.

Whether that pays off won't be known until Saturday's deadline passes and the points tallies emerge. But FPL General isn't guessing. He's committed. And in FPL, sometimes that's the only edge that matters.
