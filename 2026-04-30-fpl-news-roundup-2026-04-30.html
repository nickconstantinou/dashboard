FPL News Roundup — 2026-04-30

Liverpool have confirmed that Mohamed Salah is expected to be fit before the end of the season, easing concerns over his recent injury absence. The Egyptian has been sidelined in recent Gameweeks, but FPL managers can breathe a sigh of relief knowing their talisman should return to action at some point before the campaign concludes. Given the significance of Double Gameweek 35, where Liverpool face two fixtures, managers holding Salah will be desperate to see him passed fit ahead of those double appearances.

That DGW35 promises to be pivotal, with 16 Premier League teams in action across both Gameweeks 35 and 36. The upcoming double fixture list presents a crucial window for managers considering their chip strategy. While the popular approach has been deploying the Wildcard in GW35 followed by Bench Boost in GW36, alternative strategies exist for those who've already used one or both chips. Managers should weigh whether holding transfers now to target specific DGW assets makes more sense than acting immediately, particularly if they have limited funds and need to restructure their squads.

Meanwhile, Daniel Forke has offered updates on several attacking options heading into the run-in, with Gabriel Gudmundsson and Noah Okafor among those being monitored. These injury situations warrant close attention as teams finalise their approaches across the remaining fixtures. With the season entering its decisive phase, the majority of clubs remain in contention for European qualification, European qualification places, or fighting to avoid the drop, meaning rotation risks vary significantly between sides and should factor heavily into transfer and captaincy decisions.
