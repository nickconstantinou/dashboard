Pras' Gameweek 34 Team Selection: Freehit Active

Introduction

The Freehit chip is now active in Gameweek 34, presenting a key tactical opportunity for Fantasy Premier League managers. With the ability to field a completely new squad for a single gameweek before reverting to your original lineup, this chip demands careful consideration of fixtures, form, and captaincy options.

Chip Strategy Overview

For managers with remaining chips, the strategy picture is becoming clearer. The Bench Boost chip remains available and represents an important future consideration. If deployed in Gameweek 35, the Bench Boost would require approximately 20 points from bench players to maximize value. This means managers should plan their squad building accordingly, ensuring they have viable bench options heading into that gameweek.

Template vs. Custom Approach

In an ordinary time, defaulting to the template selection would be the standard approach. However, for this Freehit draft, the strategy departs from convention. Rather than simply copying popular picks, this approach looks to identify specific matchups and individuals that may be undervalued in the broader player pool.

Attacking Teams to Target

When building a Freehit squad for Gameweek 34, fixture analysis points toward four primary teams to target in attack. Liverpool, Arsenal, Manchester United, and Tottenham all represent favorable matchup opportunities. These sides have shown defensive vulnerabilities or offer attacking assets in strong form, making them logical foundations for Freehit selections.

Forward Options Analysis

The forward line presents several compelling options for this gameweek. The four main attacking targets are Bowen, Sulanki, Watkins, and Thiago. Among these, Bowen stands out as particularly valuable. He is on penalties for his team and represents the best differential option among non-premium forwards. His penalty duties provide a reliable baseline for returns regardless of match flow.

Additional Viable Assets

For managers seeking to diversify their Freehit selections, several non-template viable options exist. Dango, Ibumo Kuna, Thiago, and Harry Wilson all represent strong alternatives. These players offer comparable attacking potential to more popular picks while potentially flying under the radar in captaincy discussions.

Player Form Notes

Brighton's Gross has emerged as an interesting budget option. The Brighton side has performed well defensively with two clean sheets in recent matches, while Gross himself has contributed three assists. This combination of defensive solidity and creative output makes him a viable defensive or midfield option for Freehit squads.

Saka's recent performance in Gameweek 33 included an assist, suggesting he remains in productive form despite Arsenal's challenging fixture schedule. He came close to additional returns, indicating his ceiling remains high.

Captaincy Considerations

When selecting an armband for Freehit Gameweek 34, the captaincy debate centers on premium options versus differential picks. Bowen's penalty involvement and "best of the rest" status outside the elite tier makes him a strong candidate for those seeking to differentiate from popular captaincy choices. His underlying statistics support the case for reliable returns rather than pure ceiling dependency.

Conclusion

The Freehit chip in Gameweek 34 demands a calculated approach that moves beyond template selections. With Bench Boost still available for future deployment, this chip offers a chance to exploit specific fixtures while maintaining flexibility for later gameweeks. Prioritizing teams in good attacking form, securing penalty-takers, and identifying differential options can maximize the value of this one-time squad refresh.
