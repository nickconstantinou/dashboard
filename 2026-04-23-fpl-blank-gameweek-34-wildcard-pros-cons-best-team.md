# FPL Blank Gameweek 34 Wildcard: Pros, Cons + Best Team

## Understanding Blank Gameweek 34

Blank Gameweek 34 presents a unique challenge for Fantasy Premier League managers. Only seven top-flight matches will take place during this gameweek, creating a "blank" for teams without fixtures. The six teams that played twice in Gameweek 33 will be absent from Gameweek 34: Bournemouth, Brighton and Hove Albion, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds United and Manchester City. This concentration of blank teams significantly limits the player pool available to managers who have accumulated assets from those clubs.

## Pros of Activating the Wildcard

Activating the Wildcard in Gameweek 34 allows managers to completely restructure their squads ahead of the run-in. Over 6% of poll responders are considering activating the Wildcard chip for Gameweek 34, indicating many managers see value in acting now.

The timing could prove advantageous for several reasons. The postponed Man City v Crystal Palace meeting from Gameweek 31 will soon be rearranged, and this fixture will likely create a Double Gameweek 36. Planning ahead with a Wildcard now positions managers to exploit that future double gameweek by acquiring assets from Man City or Crystal Palace before prices shift.

## Cons of Activating the Wildcard

However, there are important considerations before committing to a Wildcard. The gameweek itself presents fixture challenges. Owning more than four assets from Leeds, City and Brighton would mean not fielding an immediate full XI, which could cost valuable points during the blank.

Additionally, several key players carry uncertainty heading into the deadline. Bryan Mbeumo limped off against Chelsea, potentially limiting his availability. Anthony Gordon might miss another match because of his hip issue, affecting Newcastle assets. Omar Alderete is also a doubt for Sunderland.

Other squad concerns exist beyond immediate selection. Hugo Ekitike's season is over, eliminating a budget forward option. Jurrien Timber didn't make Gameweeks 32 and 33, leaving Arsenal's defensive options depleted for managers relying on their players.

## Fixture Planning Considerations

Brighton offer a specific fixture opportunity. Brighton are at home to Wolverhampton Wanderers in Gameweek 36, making them a valuable target for managers using their Wildcard now to prepare for that fixture. Acquiring Brighton assets during the Wildcard allows managers to field a full lineup in Gameweek 34 while building toward the favorable Gameweek 36 matchup.

## Recommended Team Structure

Given the constraints, a Wildcard team should prioritize players from teams with Gameweek 34 fixtures while ensuring sufficient coverage of teams likely to feature in the upcoming Double Gameweek 36. Managers should limit exposure to the six blank teams, particularly avoiding more than four assets from Leeds, City, and Brighton until after the blank resolves.

Budget defenders and midfielders from playing teams offer the best value, with premium funds reserved for proven assets in favorable positions. The Wildcard approach works best when it simultaneously solves the blank gameweek problem and positions the squad for the anticipated double fixture from the rearranged Man City match.

## Conclusion

The Gameweek 34 Wildcard offers a strategic opportunity to address blank gameweek challenges while preparing for future double gameweeks. However, player availability concerns and fixture concentration require careful planning. Managers must balance immediate needs against long-term positioning, particularly regarding the Double Gameweek 36 that the rearranged Man City v Crystal Palace fixture will likely create.
