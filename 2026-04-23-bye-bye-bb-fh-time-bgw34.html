Bye Bye BB? ~ FH Time! | BGW33 - Fantasy Premier League Podcast Summary

The Return of Fortune: How One Double Gameweek Changed Everything

For many Fantasy Premier League managers, the 2024-25 season has been a study in perseverance. After months of frustrating results, missed opportunities, and the constant feeling that the FPL gods had conspired against you, there's nothing quite like a breakthrough gameweek to remind you why you play this maddening game. In this episode of the popular FPL podcast recorded during the halftime of Double Gameweek 33, hosts Tom and Sam dive into what promises to be one of their most satisfying gameweeks of the entire season.

The podcast opens with Tom still firmly in the waiting room of fatherhood, his wife approaching her due date with what he quips has lasted "longer than the entire reign of Liam Ross and you're at Chelsea." It's a self-deprecating joke that sets the tone for an episode that's ultimately about celebration and renewed hope. The hosts immediately address the elephant in the room: this has been a brutal season for many managers, and Gameweek 33 finally delivered the goods. Sam notes that while Southampton has extended their unbeaten run, his automatic promotion hopes have "slightly gone out the window" following a draw the night before—yet even this disappointment can't dampen the mood because, as he puts it, "finally had a good FPL gameweek."

The episode arrives at a critical moment in the season. With approximately five weeks remaining, managers are making final pushes toward their goals. Some are fighting for top 100k finishes, others are simply trying to salvage pride from what has been a difficult campaign. The timing of this recording captures the midway point of the remaining double gameweek fixtures, where early results have already begun to separate the winners from the also-rans. For Tom, the timing is particularly significant—he nearly missed the Friday deadline for Gameweek 34 entirely, a reminder of how easy it is to let administrative details slip through the cracks during the chaos of a busy season.

The Anatomy of a Great Gameweek: Brighton, Bench Boost, and the Numbers Behind Success

When Tom begins detailing his Gameweek 33 performance, the numbers tell a story that few FPL managers have experienced this season. "This is the first top 1 million rank of the season for a single game week," he explains, acknowledging that he'd come close six or seven times before but always fell just outside the mark. This time, things were different. The gameweek was shaping up to be a top 100k ranking or "very, very close to" that milestone—a remarkable turnaround for someone who had been struggling at 317k just days earlier. By halftime of the Bournemouth match, he'd already cut that deficit in half, with his rank sitting around 150k.

The source of this transformation? Brighton's double gameweek performance proved to be the gift that kept on giving. "Brighton have really come in clutch," Tom says, noting that their clean sheet against Chelsea in the opening fixture provided crucial points for bench boost players. The Seagulls' defensive solidity across both matches formed the foundation upon which many successful gameweeks were built. For Tom specifically, having Gross on his team proved to be exceptionally valuable—the Brighton defender midfielder accumulated two assists across the two games plus five bonus points, representing "very very decent indeed for 25% EO" (Expected Ownership). That Gross was held at just 25% ownership meant he provided valuable differentiation while still delivering substantial returns.

Sam's gameweek followed a similar trajectory, though with slightly different personnel choices. Where Tom had Sesko, Sam had strikers who delivered instead. Both hosts found themselves in the rare position of watching their teams perform at optimal levels simultaneously. Sam notes this was a "transformative" gameweek, using language that captures the magnitude of the shift in fortunes. The discussion reveals an important truth about FPL: differentiation often feels uncomfortable in the moment, but when those differential picks come through, the rewards are substantial. Sam reflects on this irony, acknowledging that while he was "a little bit disappointed in the perceived lack of differentiation" last week, the reality is that when things go well, "all those things just kind of fall by the wayside and you just feel like you know everything's all right with the world again."

The Bench Boost Reckoning: What Could Have Been and What Actually Was

The bench boost chip has been one of the most anticipated fixtures of the season, and Gameweek 33 represented its deployment for many managers. For Tom, the chip's performance tells a complicated story. While the overall return was strong, the absence of J Pedro from action represented a missed opportunity. "J Pedro is a no-show," Tom explains, "Technically means Van Heck comes off my bench on the bench boost." The Portuguese forward failing to appear meant that Tom's bench boost score would be reduced by the points Van Dijk would have contributed had he started instead. The calculation is stark: instead of potentially 27 points from the bench, Tom was looking at around 14 points, with that figure likely dropping to 26 after Van Dijk loses his bonus point.

This mathematical reality highlights one of the peculiar features of bench boost strategy in FPL. When your bench players don't play, you're essentially penalised twice—once for the missed opportunity and again when substitutes are forced into your starting lineup. "I don't really do that whole calculation thing about who comes off my bench because I see it like every player is therefore valid," Sam explains, offering a philosophical counterpoint to Tom's more analytical approach. This reflects a broader debate within the FPL community about how to value bench assets. Some managers obsess over every point that might transfer between bench and starting XI, while others take a more holistic view of their squad's value.

The episode also touches on the controversial topic of red cards, specifically referencing Gabriel's performance. "Gabrielle getting zero and should have probably been even less than that if he got the sending off," Tom notes, expressing a desire "to live in a world where there are less red cards if possible" while acknowledging that given current refereeing standards, he was surprised a red wasn't shown. This commentary reflects the ongoing conversation about officiating consistency in the Premier League, an issue that directly impacts FPL outcomes when key players are dismissed.

Looking Forward to Gameweek 34: The Free Hit Dilemma and Beyond

As the conversation shifts toward Gameweek 34, both hosts reveal they are deploying their free hit chips—a significant strategic decision that warrants careful consideration. The free hit allows managers to field an entirely new team for a single gameweek before returning to their previous squad. With a Friday deadline that Tom admits he had "forgotten" entirely (prompting his gratitude for the podcast format that forces him to stay organised), the hosts prepare to reveal their intended teams to each other for the first time.

The hosts make clear that while this episode will showcase their free hit strategies, they're not planning to make the entire discussion exclusively about that chip. "We're going to be talking about players that non-free hitters will also probably want to buy," Tom explains, acknowledging that many of the same considerations apply whether you're using a free hit or spending precious transfers. This approach reflects the reality that Gameweek 34 presents interesting options regardless of chip strategy—there's value in understanding the fixture landscape even if you're not making wholesale changes to your squad.

The timing of Gameweek 34 fixtures becomes a focal point of discussion. With the second halves of remaining double gameweek 33 fixtures being played out while they record, and a midweek off for many sides creating a compressed schedule, the landscape is complex. Friday deadlines are always at risk of being missed by distracted managers, and the hosts acknowledge this potential pitfall explicitly. Sam's observation that "there is a Friday deadline which until reading that I'd actually forgotten" serves as a valuable public service announcement to listeners who might be similarly distracted.

Lessons Learned and the Road Ahead: What This Gameweek Teaches Us

As the episode draws toward its conclusion, several key lessons emerge that extend beyond this specific gameweek. First, there's the reminder that FPL fortunes can change remarkably quickly. Tom's journey from 317k to approximately 150k in a single gameweek demonstrates how rapidly the standings can shift when multiple pieces fall into place simultaneously. For managers in similar positions of desperation, this should serve as both encouragement and caution—you can climb the rankings fast, but others can too.

Second, the episode highlights the value of owning assets in teams with favorable fixture runs. Brighton's double gameweek came at precisely the right time for managers who had invested in their assets, and the returns justified those investments. The hosts' discussion of ownership percentages reveals another important dynamic: while highly owned players provide safety in terms of points correlation, the truly transformative weeks often come from differential picks that land. Gross at 25% ownership provided excellent service, but managers who had him felt the satisfaction of differentiation working alongside solid returns.

Third, and perhaps most importantly, the hosts remind us that FPL is a game of long-term strategy punctuated by moments of opportunistic decision-making. The discussion about bench boost and free hit reflects this duality—each chip represents a carefully timed opportunity, and the difference between success and disappointment often comes down to execution timing. Both Tom and Sam acknowledge that while they're pleased with their current positions, they're already thinking about how Gameweek 35 might feel different, especially if their returns from free hit transfers leave them wanting.

Who This Episode Serves Best

This podcast episode speaks most directly to Fantasy Premier League managers who have experienced a frustrating season and are looking for signs of hope. Managers sitting in the 100k to 500k range will find particular resonance in Tom's journey, as his description of being "close several times, just outside the top million" captures a common experience. The discussion of chip strategies—bench boost regrets and free hit planning—provides practical value for anyone still navigating the remaining weeks of the season.

Beyond the tactical content, the episode offers something equally valuable: the reminder that FPL is meant to be enjoyable. Both hosts express genuine delight at their improved fortunes, with Sam noting this was "a nice reminder of what FPL can feel like if it does go your way for once." For managers currently struggling, this empathetic acknowledgment that the game can feel brutal serves as a form of solidarity. For those succeeding, there's validation that the strategy and patience are finally paying off.

The episode also serves managers who are still weighing chip deployments for the remaining weeks. With both hosts revealing their free hit plans, listeners gain insight into how experienced managers approach the decision of when and how to use their most powerful chips. Whether you're committed to using your free hit in Gameweek 34 or are still debating alternatives, the thought process demonstrated here offers a template for making these critical decisions.
