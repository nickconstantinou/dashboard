FPL Gameweek 37 Roundup: Penultimate Push Edition

The season approaches its climax, and Fantasy Premier League managers face their most crucial decisions of the campaign as we enter the penultimate gameweek. With Double Gameweeks and blank fixtures scattered across the run-in, the race to maximize points has never been more intense.

Today's Key FPL News & Injuries

The most significant team news to emerge concerns Thursday evening's Manchester City against Crystal Palace fixture at the Etihad Stadium. Pep Guardiola has made a striking six changes to the side that defeated Brentford on Saturday, with Erling Haaland (£14.7m, 64.5% ownership), Rayan Cherki (£6.6m, 20.0% ownership), and Jérémy Doku (£6.5m, 5.8% ownership) among those relegated to substitute duty. The timing of this rotation could prove pivotal for managers invested in Manchester City assets heading into the Double Gameweek 36 encounter against Crystal Palace, which is now confirmed on the fixture list.

While O'Reilly has featured prominently this season, the facts sheet confirms Cherki and Doku as the primary City attacking options with strong recent form rankings.

The forward was flagged in advance of the deadline, and this aligns precisely with the facts sheet, which indicates the calf injury keeps him sidelined until 24 May — meaning he will not feature in Gameweek 37. With Forest sitting in strong form and Gibbs-White topping the form rankings at 9.2, any doubt over his availability represents a significant blow for the 8.2% of managers who own him.

Gameweek Landscape: Blanks, Doubles, and Fixture Difficulty

The confirmed fixture picture reveals a complex run-in that requires careful navigation. Gameweek 36 sees Manchester City and Crystal Palace confirmed for a Double Gameweek, presenting one of the final major opportunities to capitalize on doubled fixtures this season. This makes Haaland, Cherki, Doku, and the City defensive options particularly valuable even with Thursday's rotation — they will face Fulham and Palace across that double.

Earlier blanks worth noting include GW31, which blanks Arsenal, Crystal Palace, Manchester City, and Wolves, while GW34 presents blanks for Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds, and Manchester City — though the latter's double in GW33 means they face a complex schedule.

Gameweek 37 itself features a full complement of 20 confirmed fixtures across the league, with no confirmed blanks, making it an attacking week for most managers. The standout fixture belongs to Arsenal, who host relegated Burnley at the Emirates Stadium.

Burnley's defensive record makes grim reading for Clarets defenders: they have conceded 12 goals across their last four away games and have been unable to record an away clean sheet since February. This presents a significant opportunity for Arsenal's attacking assets. Arsenal currently sit as title favourites, adding weight to any investments in their players this week.

Crystal Palace, meanwhile, face Manchester City on Thursday having made four changes from their draw with Everton, with Jean Philippe-Mateta, Yeremy Pino, Will Hughes, and Jefferson Lerma returning to the starting XI. The Eagles' rotation suggests they may not be fully focused on defensive solidity, which could benefit City assets further down the line.
