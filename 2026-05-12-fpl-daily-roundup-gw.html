FPL Daily Roundup

The Big Picture: Fixture Chaos Creates Trading Opportunity

The final stretch of the 2025-26 Premier League season is throwing FPL managers into their most complex puzzle yet, with overlapping blanks and doubles demanding surgical precision in the transfer market. The confirmed fixture list reveals some stark realities: Gameweek 31 sees Arsenal, Crystal Palace, Manchester City, and Wolves all blank, while Gameweek 34 will be a nightmare for anyone holding assets from Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds, and Manchester City as they all sit idle that week.

The flip side presents opportunity. Gameweek 33 offers a genuine mega-double with Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds, and Manchester City all in action, while Gameweek 36 delivers a more modest double featuring Crystal Palace and Manchester City. For managers with free chips burning holes in their pockets, this fixture congestion is the story of the season's denouement.

Today's Key FPL News and Injury Update

The headline injury concern this week centers on Morgan Gibbs-White of Nottingham Forest, priced at £7.5m and sitting at just 8.5% ownership despite topping the form charts with a remarkable 9.2 average. The 25-year-old midfielder faces a facial injury that gives him only a 50% chance of playing in the next fixture. Forest's form has been electric, and Gibbs-White's 172 season points have managers sweating over whether to hold or move him. The timing of this injury could hardly be worse given that his £7.5m price point represents excellent value when he's firing.

Leeds United's Swiss striker Noah Okafor (£5.6m, 2.1% ownership) is ruled out with a calf problem and is not expected to return until May 24th, effectively ending his season for most managers. Meanwhile, Joachim Andersen of Fulham remains suspended until August 29th, making him undraftable despite his modest £4.5m price tag.

In team news, Bournemouth's handling of Alex Jimenez's situation has drawn attention from FPL analysts, with the club choosing not to select him for matchplay while an investigation remains ongoing. The consensus among analysts was that this measured approach was appropriate and responsible, representing correct course of action from the Cherries hierarchy.

Chip Strategy: Timing Is Everything

With confirmed doubles in Gameweek 33 and Gameweek 36, the free hit chip becomes a valuable weapon. Managers should be planning their free hit squad specifically for Gameweek 33 when six teams are doubling, allowing them to load up on Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds, and Manchester City assets without sacrificing their core squad.

The wildcard timing is more nuanced. Managers without free hits remaining should consider deploying their wildcard in the window before Gameweek 33, allowing them to build a squad specifically targeting the double gameweek while ensuring they don't hold dead assets for the blanks in Gameweek 31 and Gameweek 34.

Bench boost strategy should focus on Gameweeks where your bench fodder has double fixtures. With limited doubles remaining, bench boost is becoming less valuable and may be best saved for the final double or abandoned entirely.

Where Creators Disagree

The debate over Manchester City's fixture rotation continues to divide opinion. With Guardiola's side blanking in both Gameweek 31 and Gameweek 34, some analysts favour avoiding City assets entirely until after those blanks, while others argue that their Gameweek 33 double and Gameweek 36 double make them essential holds even through the dry spells.

The Gibbs-White situation has also created division. His 9.2 form is untouchable when fit, but the 50% chance of playing makes him a liability. Some managers are willing to take the risk given Forest's favourable fixtures, while others are pivoting to safer options with similar points potential.

These teams all blank in Gameweek 31, and if you don't have a free hit ready, you could be looking at single-digit returns from multiple squad slots.

Whether through the free hit chip or through strategic transfers, loading up on assets from these sides before the double could be transformative for your season.
