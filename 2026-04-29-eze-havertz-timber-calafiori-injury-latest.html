Team Training Update

On April 28, Arsenal's training session provided a mixed picture for the squad's fitness ahead of the Champions League semi-final against Atletico Madrid. Three players were notably absent: Jurrien Timber, Kai Havertz, and Mikel Merino. However, a positive development saw Riccardo Calafiori, Eberechi Eze, and Bukayo Saka all participating in the session.

Mikel Merino remains a long-term absentee with a foot injury, continuing his extended spell on the sidelines.

Kai Havertz: Out for Atletico Clash

Kai Havertz came off with a muscle injury in Gameweek 34 and has been ruled out for Arsenal's Champions League clash with Atletico Madrid. Manager Mikel Arteta confirmed: "Kai is out, and Ebz is ready to go." The German midfielder was priced at £7.3m in Fantasy Football at the time of the announcement.

Eberechi Eze: Available for Selection

Despite also coming off with a muscle injury in Gameweek 34, Eberechi Eze is ready to play for Arsenal. Arteta's statement that "Ebz is ready to go" confirms the English midfielder's availability. Eze was priced at £7.2m in Fantasy Football.

Riccardo Calafiori: Returns to Training

Riccardo Calafiori missed Saturday's match despite Arteta previously saying he would "probably" be available. However, the Italian defender has returned from an unspecified issue and was involved in training on April 28. Calafiori was priced at £5.6m.

Bukayo Saka: Successful Return

Bukayo Saka marked his comeback from an Achilles injury with a substitute appearance in Gameweek 34. The England international came through his cameo unscathed and participated fully in training on April 28. Saka was priced at £9.8m.

Jurrien Timber: No Timeline for Return

Jurrien Timber is missing out again due to an ankle injury, and it remains unknown when he will return. According to updates: "He's doing some stuff on the pitch at the moment, but we need to get the gears up and be able to do more things before he can compete with us." The Dutch defender continues rehabilitation work but is not yet integrated into full team training.
