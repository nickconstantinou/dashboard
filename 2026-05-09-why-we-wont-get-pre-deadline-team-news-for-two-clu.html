Why Pre-Deadline Team News Will Be Missing for Two Clubs in Gameweek 37

An Unusual Gameweek Structure

Gameweek 37 breaks from the standard Premier League schedule in a significant way. According to Fantasy Football Scout, there are no Saturday matches scheduled for this gameweek. This departure from the typical fixture pattern creates a unique challenge for Fantasy Premier League managers seeking team news before the deadline.

Four Clubs Affected by Postponement

The unusual scheduling means that four Premier League clubs will not contest their Gameweek 37 fixtures until Tuesday. This represents a substantial delay compared to the normal matchday timeline, and it directly impacts when managers can expect official team news from these clubs.

The FA Cup Final Connection

The reason for this delay becomes clear when examining the broader fixture calendar. Manchester City and Chelsea, the FA Cup finalists, both have fixtures pushed back to Tuesday. Chelsea's Gameweek 37 fixture against Tottenham Hotspur has been rescheduled to Tuesday, while Manchester City's fixture against Bournemouth has similarly been pushed back to the same date. The FA Cup final takes place between these two clubs, which explains why their Premier League fixtures cannot be played on the standard weekend schedule.

The Press Conference Problem

For Fantasy managers, the complication extends beyond just the match postponements. Bournemouth's Andoni Iraola and Tottenham Hotspur's Roberto De Zerbi will not hold their pre-match press conferences until Monday, 18 May. This date falls three days after the Gameweek 37 deadline passes, meaning managers will not receive pre-deadline team news from either Bournemouth or Tottenham before submitting their squads.

Practical Implications

This situation presents a distinct challenge for Fantasy Premier League managers. When Bournemouth and Tottenham play their fixtures on Tuesday, the team news will not arrive until after the Gameweek 37 deadline has closed. Managers must therefore make their selections for these two clubs without the benefit of official pre-match information from the managers themselves.
