Who are FPL’s best all-round midfielders?

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

The Full Story

Fantasy Premier League managers looking to maximize their squad value for the final stretch of the 2025/26 season now have an expanded pool of midfield options to consider, according to analysis from FantasyFootballScout.co.uk published March 26, 2026. The introduction of Defensive Contribution (DefCon) points to FPL has fundamentally altered the midfield landscape, adding a new dimension to player valuation that rewards players for defensive work alongside their traditional attacking contributions.

The analysis identifies midfielders who deliver value across multiple categories—goals, assists, set-pieces, penalties, and now defensive actions—making them particularly valuable assets for managers seeking consistent point returns. Using data from the Members Area with a minimum threshold of 1,700 minutes played, the study evaluates players on a per-90-minutes basis across eight key metrics: shots, attempts inside the box, chances created (CC), big chances created (BCC), corners, open-play crosses, expected goal involvement (xGI), and DefCon action. Players who have coped well across multiple categories in their last six appearances are highlighted in green on the detailed charts.

Two notable midfielders were explicitly excluded from the recent-form analysis due to injury: Justin Kluivert (priced at £6.9m) and Bruno Guimaraes (£6.8m), neither of whom has featured since Gameweeks 20 and 26 respectively. The detailed breakdown of which players qualified as the best all-rounders remains restricted to Chief Scout Members, with the full rankings, specific statistics, and player-by-player analysis available only through the subscription service.

Why This Matters

This analysis addresses one of the most consequential decisions FPL managers face: allocating their limited budget across squad positions. Midfielders represent significant investments—many premium options cost £8.0m or more—and identifying players who can accumulate points through multiple avenues rather than relying on a single category (goals OR assists, for instance) can be the difference between a championship-winning squad and an also-ran.

The DefCon metric, introduced by FPL, has been a game-changer for positional strategy. Previously, defenders offered the safest floor through clean sheets while midfielders provided higher ceiling potential through attacking returns. Now, midfielders who track back and contribute defensively can effectively offer both—consistent floor points from DefCon actions combined with the upside of goals and assists. This has essentially blurred the line between defensive and attacking positions, making "all-rounder" midfielders increasingly valuable.

For the 2025/26 season specifically, managers racing toward the finish line need reliable assets who won't blank in any given gameweek. Players performing well across diverse categories provide that stability, as they have multiple ways to score even when their primary attacking threat is neutralized by opposition tactics or game flow.
