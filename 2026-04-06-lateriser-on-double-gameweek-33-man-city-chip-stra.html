FPL Strategy: Lateriser's Complete Guide to Double Gameweek 33 and Man City Coverage

The Full Story

Three-time top 200 FPL finisher Lateriser has released his comprehensive analysis for Double Gameweek 33, centering his strategy around Manchester City's favorable fixture situation while acknowledging that Arsenal's shock loss to Southampton has completely disrupted typical chip strategy planning. In his latest article for Fantasy Football Scout, Lateriser argues that Manchester City—now eliminated from European competition—represents the most reliable source of returns for the crucial run-in, as Pep Guardiola's side will prioritize the Premier League and FA Cup for the remainder of the season.

The analysis hinges on the observation that Guardiola appears to have settled on his preferred starting XI after the club's two convincing cup performances against Arsenal and Liverpool. Across those two matches, the Catalan manager made only one change—bringing in Marc Guehi (£5.1m) at centre-back for the Carabao Cup final due to John Stones' ineligibility—while keeping the front six and full-backs identical. This consistency suggests City fans can invest in three players from the club with confidence, particularly given their upcoming double gameweek and another double scheduled for later in the season.

Why This Matters

This analysis matters because Double Gameweek 33 represents a critical pivot point in the 2025-26 FPL season, where managers must decide whether to deploy their Wildcard, Free Hit, or other chips to maximize returns. With Manchester City now seemingly focused solely on domestic competitions and Guardiola appearing to have abandoned his experimental two-up-front system, investing in City's attacking options could provide the differential needed to climb the rankings during the season's decisive phase.

The broader implications extend to chip strategy itself: with Arsenal now having no blanks or doubles in the remaining schedule, managers who already used their Wildcards early are in a "fairly decent" position, while those holding their chips must navigate an increasingly complex landscape where no single team offers an obvious template strategy.
