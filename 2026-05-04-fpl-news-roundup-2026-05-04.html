FPL News Roundup — 2026-05-04

Newcastle striker Alexander Isak is out for the rest of the season after suffering a groin injury in Monday's 2-1 defeat to Aston Villa, dealing a major blow to managers who had relied on the Swedish striker for goals and rotation flexibility. This adds to a growing list of absentees heading into the final weeks of the season. Aston Villa themselves rang the changes for the reverse fixture against Tottenham on Sunday, benching both Ollie Watkins and Ezri Konsa while leaving skipper John McGinn entirely out of the squad — eleven changes in total suggesting Villa are prioritising Thursday's Europa League second leg against Lyon over their league commitments. The Gunners, meanwhile, travel to Liverpool knowing they need to keep pace with Sunday's dominant display.

Crystal Palace's 3-0 defeat to Bournemouth on Sunday underlined the importance of squad depth at this stage of the campaign, with manager Oliver Glasner admitting the "tank was empty" after an intensive run of fixtures. Jean-Philippe Mateta offered little in attack and Palace looked a shadow of the side that had climbed into European contention. On the opposite side, Bournemouth's Evanilson was electric and Milos Kerkez continued his surge as one of the season's standout bargain defenders — a player many managers may have overlooked when the prices were set. Sunday's results pushed several teams into unwanted territory on the DefCon scale, with Nottingham Forest and Brighton appearing particularly exposed heading into the run-in.

For managers still hunting points after GW35, the Fantasy EFL Play-Offs game is now live, offering prizes across three gameweeks for those wanting a secondary interest. It is a useful distraction but the real work is in the Premier League where the schedule is offering little respite. With Liverpool still very much in the title race and Arsenal facing a tricky trip to Anfield, the next few gameweeks could be decisive for both the title race and the scramble for European qualification — making every transfer and captain choice at this stage count double.
