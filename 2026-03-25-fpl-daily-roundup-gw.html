FPL Gameweek 31/32 Roundup: The Fixture Frenzy Files

Navigating the Blank and the Chaos as the Season Reaches Its Brutal Conclusion

The Fantasy Premier League season has entered its most unforgiving phase, and managers across the globe are discovering exactly what separates a respectable finish from a title challenge. After a bruising Gameweek 31 that left even the most seasoned veterans reaching for comfort, the community finds itself at a critical juncture where patience, precision, and nerve will determine who finishes the campaign in triumph and who collapses in a heap of regret and missed differentials.

---

The Landscape: Blank Days and Fixture Farce

The confirmation arrived exactly as the community had anticipated: Arsenal, Wolves, Manchester City, and Crystal Palace will all blank in Gameweek 31, leaving managers scrambling to navigate a fixture landscape that resembles more of a maze than a pathway. FP Harry, whose comprehensive analysis consistently ranks among the most-watched FPL content, released his complete guide dissecting exactly what this means for squad strategy, and the message was unambiguous—the blank gameweeks are here, and ignoring them will cost you dearly.

The confusion surrounding which teams would play and which would sit idle had been gnawing at managers for days, but with the picture now crystallized, the real work begins. Tom and Sam, hosts of one of FPL's most-listened podcasts, sounded the alarm about the impending blank with their characteristic blend of humor and genuine strategic concern. "The Blank Is Nigh" they declared, capturing the mood of a community facing the unenviable task of fielding competitive lineups while nearly half the Premier League takes a weekend off.

What makes this period particularly treacherous is the overlay of European competitions. Champions League and Europa League commitments continue to fragment the fixture calendar, meaning even the teams not blanking may face rotation concerns that could undermine your captain selections and clean sheet hopes. The predictability that managers crave during the run-in has been replaced by a chaotic tapestry of rescheduled matches, FA Cup interruptions, and squad management decisions that seem to change by the hour.

---

The Relegation Battle: Where the Real Drama Lives

In a fascinating departure from conventional FPL discourse, Serge and James of Planet FPL conducted a listener poll asking which storyline would dominate the final weeks of the season—the title race, European qualification, or the relegation battle. The results were emphatic: relegation won in a landslide. This speaks volumes about where the genuine excitement lies, and savvy managers are taking notice.

The battle to avoid the drop has become genuinely spellbinding, with multiple clubs separated by mere points and facing vastly different run-ins that could dramatically reshape the FPL landscape. Wolves, currently embroiled in that very relegation conversation, blank this gameweek, but their fixtures beyond that point represent precisely the kind of opportunity that separates shrewd managers from the pack. Leicester City, another side living dangerously near the bottom three, present similar considerations—cheap defenders from these teams could prove absolute gold as the season reaches its climax.

The logic is compelling: relegation-threatened sides fight hardest when cornered, meaning defensive discipline often improves dramatically in survival scenarios. Conversely, their opposition may also approach these matches with appropriate caution, further increasing the likelihood of low-scoring encounters where clean sheets become achievable. FP Harry's guidance on this matter has been clear—don't sleep on the bottom-feeders, because their run-in fixtures often contain hidden value that the casual manager will overlook.

---

Captaincy Conundrums: Who to Trust When Everything's Uncertain

With five Premier League teams not playing this gameweek, the captaincy debate has taken on added significance. Every point you leave on the bench is a point your rivals might gain, and the pressure to make the correct call has never been more acute.

The current world number one manager, Eric—who claimed the top spot in Gameweek 29 and successfully defended it in Gameweek 30, extending his lead to 15 points over second place—represents the gold standard of decision-making in this environment. His approach to captaincy during this chaotic period emphasizes fixtures over form, reasoning that a player with a favorable matchup will outperform a star player facing a defensive wall regardless of recent scoring history.

Tom and Sam debated captaincy options extensively, with their conclusion centering on the importance of identifying teams with genuinely soft fixtures during this compressed period. The logic extends beyond the immediate gameweek: if you're choosing between a player facing Manchester City and one facing a mid-table side with nothing to play for, the latter option should always prevail when navigating blanks and doubles.

FP Harry has been particularly vocal about avoiding the temptation to captain premium players simply because of their price tag. In a blank gameweek where your premium options may be sitting idle entirely, the difference between a successful captain choice and a disastrous one could amount to thirty or forty points—a swing that would crush any manager's ambitions.

---

Transfer Strategy: Short-Term Pain for Long-Term Gain

The data from elite managers—those with 620 or more top 10k finishes between them—reveals that 80% are making at least one transfer this gameweek, underscoring that standing still is not a viable strategy even during a blank. The question isn't whether to act, but how to act without undermining your long-term prospects.

Eric, speaking from the unique vantage point of world number one, has emphasized the importance of maintaining flexibility while avoiding panic moves. His own transfer strategy has centered on identifying players whose fixtures align with the upcoming double gameweeks while ensuring he doesn't strand himself with players facing blanks when he could have prepared differently.

The short-term versus long-term debate has dominated creator content, with FP Harry's guide breaking down exactly how managers should approach this decision. His recommendation: accept some short-term pain during Gameweek 31 if it means being optimally positioned for the fixtures that follow. This might mean owning players from teams that blank this week but have exceptional schedules in Gameweeks 32 through 37.

Tom and Sam discussed their own transfer dilemmas with refreshing candor, acknowledging that even experienced managers find themselves paralyzed by the complexity of the situation. Their consensus: if you have a free hit available and haven't yet used it, now is the time to seriously consider deployment. The alternative—cobbling together a weak squad and hoping for the best—rarely ends well.

For those without chips remaining, the strategy shifts toward maximizing value from the players you already own. Look for the cheap enablers who can slot into your lineup without breaking the bank, freeing up funds for one or two differentials who might haul this gameweek when others cannot.

---

Players to Avoid: The Cautionary Tales

Every gameweek produces its casualties, and Gameweek 30 delivered some particularly painful reminders about the dangers of blind faith in premium assets. Tom and Sam recorded their "Let's Get Back On Track" episode immediately after GW30's conclusion, and the tone was unmistakably one of damage limitation.

The message from creators across the board has been consistent: avoid players from teams in poor form regardless of their price or reputation. A £12 million price tag means nothing when the underlying numbers suggest regression is imminent. The community has seen too many managers cling to premium players based on name recognition alone, only to watch their ranking crumble as cheaper alternatives outscore them week after week.

FP Harry specifically warned against overcommitting to players facing blank gameweeks, noting that the temptation to hold expensive assets "just in case" leads to squad imbalances that prove impossible to rectify later in the season. If a player blanks in GW31 and then again in GW33, the opportunity cost of not transferring them out becomes staggering.

Serge and James, despite their own struggles reflected in sitting 17th and 18th in their respective mini-leagues, offered valuable lessons about the emotional dimension of FPL decision-making. The frustration of GW31 has clearly affected their judgment, and they've acknowledged that managers in similar positions should be especially wary of making rash transfers motivated by short-term disappointment rather than long-term strategic planning.

---

Chip Strategy: The Wildcard Dilemma That Defines Seasons

Perhaps no decision looms larger than when to deploy the wildcard, and FP Harry has emerged as the leading voice advocating patience during this critical three-week window. His reasoning is compelling: the international break and subsequent FA Cup weekend will fundamentally reshape the fixture landscape in ways that are currently impossible to predict.

The outcomes of upcoming FA Cup ties will determine which teams receive double gameweeks and which face additional blanks. Deploying your wildcard before this clarity emerges risks wasting a valuable chip on outdated information. Harry's own progression from 167k to 36k suggests that patience, rather than panic, remains the winning strategy even when rankings are moving in uncomfortable directions.

Tom and Sam addressed the wildcard question from a different angle, noting that managers who used their chip earlier in the season are now operating with a significant advantage—they know exactly what their strongest XI looks like and can plan transfers with precision. For those who saved their wildcard, the pressure to use it correctly has never been higher, and the FA Cup variables add an entire layer of complexity that demands respect.

The free hit chip presents an interesting alternative for managers with squads particularly ill-suited to the blank gameweek. Deploying it this week and immediately rebuilding for the fixtures ahead represents a legitimate strategy, though it requires accepting that you'll have no safety net for future blanks. The bench boost, meanwhile, remains best saved for confirmed double gameweeks where playing a full squad becomes maximally advantageous.

---

Where the Community Disagrees

The beauty of FPL lies in its capacity for legitimate disagreement among experts, and this gameweek has produced some fascinating divisions of opinion.

The wildcard timing debate remains unresolved, with FP Harry's patience-first approach sitting in direct contrast to those who believe the time to act is now. His critics argue that waiting for perfect information means accepting the certainty of underperformance this gameweek, while his supporters counter that premature wildcarding locks you into a team that may no longer reflect optimal strategy by the time the season resumes.

Eric's approach to captaincy—prioritizing fixtures above all else—has faced some resistance from creators who advocate for continuing to back premium players through their difficult periods. The argument that a £12 million striker will eventually turn things around and score big holds intuitive appeal, but the opportunity cost during compressed gameweeks can prove devastating.

The relegation-battle defenders strategy also generates debate. While FP Harry and others see genuine value in cheap defenders from struggling sides, skeptics point out that these teams concede goals when desperate, potentially negating any clean sheet potential. The nuance lies in identifying which relegation candidates are fighting intelligently versus those simply awaiting their inevitable fate.

---

Your Three Key Actions: The Road to Glory

First, accept the blank and move forward with conviction. You cannot change the fact that Arsenal, Wolves, Manchester City, and Crystal Palace sit idle this gameweek. What you can control is ensuring that every pound of your £100 million budget works as hard as possible. Target cheap defenders from teams with favorable run-ins, including those scrapping for survival, and structure your squad to survive the blanks while positioning yourself for the doubles that will follow.

Second, wait for the FA Cup clarity before using your wildcard. FP Harry's wisdom on this matter should not be dismissed. The next three weeks will reveal which teams face double gameweeks and which face additional blanks. Your wildcard is most powerful when deployed with complete information, not when rushed based on incomplete data. Use your free hit if you have one and need immediate salvation, but save the wildcard for when the fixture landscape becomes legible.

Third, identify your captain for GW32 now, even before the blank concludes. The managers who will finish the season strongest are those who plan beyond the immediate crisis. Look at the teams playing in GW32, identify who faces the weakest opposition, and begin mentally constructing your captaincy hierarchy for that pivotal gameweek. Eric, the world's best manager, succeeds not through luck but through constant forward planning. Your work this week should ensure that when GW32 arrives, you're not scrambling—you're executing a plan built during the quiet moments when others panic.

The season's business end has arrived, and the managers who maintain their composure while others lose theirs will reap the rewards. Navigate the blanks with patience, plan for the doubles with precision, and remember that FPL is a marathon that rewards those who think clearly when circumstances conspire to make clarity impossible.
