FPL Gameweek 31/32 Roundup: Navigating the Blank, the Burn, and Everything Between

The Fantasy Premier League season has arrived at its most treacherous stretch, where blank gameweeks collide with European distractions, FA Cup chaos looms on the horizon, and the relegation battle has captured the attention of managers far more than any title race. As nearly 13 million FPL managers contemplate their next moves, the community's most trusted voices have been dissecting every angle—and their collective wisdom paints a fascinating picture of a season entering its decisive phase.

---

The Blank Arrives: Four Teams Sit This One Out

The fixture list has delivered its latest cruel blow. Arsenal, Wolves, Manchester City, and Crystal Palace will all blank in Gameweek 31, leaving managers with approximately 15 active teams to choose from. This confirmation, analyzed extensively by FP Harry in his comprehensive Gameweek 31 guide, represents both a challenge and an opportunity—though the scales tip decidedly toward challenge for anyone still clinging to assets from those four clubs.

The timing could hardly be worse. European competitions—the Champions League and Europa League—remain in full swing, creating knock-on effects that ripple through domestic fixture schedules. This congestion has contributed to the fixture pile-up that makes the final stretch so treacherous for FPL managers trying to plan more than a week ahead. Serge and James from Planet FPL captured the mood perfectly with their episode titled "I Need A Cuddle"—this has been a bruising period, and the emotional toll on dedicated managers is real. Both hosts found themselves sitting 17th and 18th in their respective mini-leagues, a reminder that even the most engaged community members are not immune to the chaos of the run-in.

The People's Poll, also from the Planet FPL stable, put the question to their listeners: which aspect of the season's final stages concerns you most? The options were the title race, European qualification, and the relegation battle. The result was decisive—relegation dominated the voting by a huge margin. This makes perfect sense for FPL purposes, as teams fighting to stay in the Premier League represent some of the most motivated assets, with fixtures against similarly desperate clubs creating both opportunities and landmines for managers trying to maximize returns.

---

The Landscape: Where We're At and Where We're Going

The gameweek landscape has never been more complex. Gameweek 31 presents the immediate challenge of the blank, but the conversation among top creators has shifted dramatically toward what happens after—not just in Gameweek 32, but across the remaining seven weeks of the season.

FP Harry has been particularly vocal about the strategic implications of the FA Cup. The tournament's quarterfinals arrive immediately after the international break, and Harry argues that the outcomes of those cup ties will fundamentally reshape the fixture landscape for the remainder of the season. Upsets, comfortable progressions, and replays could all create unexpected double gameweeks or additional blanks, making any long-term planning before those results are known essentially guessing. This is why Harry believes the next three weeks represent a period where managers should seriously consider doing absolutely nothing with their teams—patience, he suggests, may be the greatest asset right now.

The current world number one, Eric, offers a contrasting perspective. Having claimed the top spot in Gameweek 29 and defending it through Gameweek 30 with a 15-point lead over second place, Eric's approach demonstrates the kind of calculated aggression that separates sustained elite performance from random fortune. His team selection for Gameweek 31 reveals a manager unwilling to accept blank gameweek mediocrity, instead using transfers strategically to maintain momentum even when the fixture list works against him.

---

Captain Recommendations: The Battle for the Armband

When it comes to the captaincy in blank gameweeks, the calculus shifts dramatically. With fewer active assets, the differential potential of your captain choice becomes amplified—one player's explosive haul can mean the difference between a monster gameweek and a catastrophic one.

The top 10,000 managers globally offer a fascinating window into elite thinking. Analysis of their Gameweek 31 plans reveals that 80% of these proven managers are making at least one transfer this week, demonstrating that even the most experienced FPL minds cannot simply absorb a blank without action. The transfers they make—and crucially, the captains they choose—provide invaluable insight into where the smart money is moving.

Without specific fixture announcements for the blanked teams, captains in Gameweek 31 will necessarily focus on the 15 teams in action. Teams with favorable run-ins, particularly those involved in the relegation scrap, become more attractive for captaincy purposes—motivation levels in those matches tend to be higher, and the defensive vulnerabilities on display often create goal-scoring opportunities.

FP Harry's approach emphasizes the short-term versus long-term trade-off. Managers must decide whether to chase this week's points with players from active teams or accept the blank and protect squad value for the fixtures that follow the international break. This decision should be influenced heavily by your overall rank, your chip status, and your tolerance for risk.

---

Transfer Strategy: Who the Experts Are Backing

The transfer market in blank gameweeks operates under different rules than normal weeks. Value preservation matters less than immediate utility, and managers with blanked players face a simple choice: bring in replacement assets from active teams or accept the hit if the differential potential justifies it.

FP Harry's transfer philosophy centers on fixture confidence. Rather than chasing the most expensive or fashionable assets, Harry advocates for identifying players whose upcoming fixtures provide genuine belief in returns. This approach becomes especially critical when European and cup competitions create uncertainty—Harry's insistence that managers wait before making major moves reflects a belief that patience during the FA Cup period will reveal clearer pathways than attempting to guess the chaos.

The top 10,000 managers' consensus transfer activity provides a useful benchmark. With 80% of them making moves, the message is clear: standing still in a blank gameweek carries its own costs. The question is not whether to transfer, but which direction to move.

For managers looking at the longer horizon, Harry's GW32 First Thoughts video addresses wildcard strategy directly. Having climbed from 167k to 36k in ranking without using his wildcard, Harry is candid about whether he needs the chip at all—a refreshingly honest assessment that contrasts with the reflexive wildcard-early mindset that catches many managers out. His draft approach suggests that sometimes the best wildcard is the one you never play.

---

Players to Avoid: The Warning Signs

The community's warnings about certain assets offer equally valuable guidance. Tom and Sam, hosts of the popular podcast that recorded their GW30 thoughts on March 10th, have been particularly vocal about the risks of chasing points that no longer exist. Players whose teams have blanked or who face particularly difficult fixtures represent obvious sells, but the more subtle warning involves assets whose ownership has become too concentrated.

When a player is owned by 30% or 40% of the FPL population, their potential to differentiate your rank diminishes dramatically. Even if Mohamed Salah or Erling Haaland returns 15 points in a gameweek, so does everyone else who owns them. The captains and transfers that truly matter are the ones that nobody else is making—differentiation, not simply following the herd, is what separates good gameweeks from great ones.

European competition involvement presents another category of risk. Teams juggling Champions League or Europa League commitments often rotate in domestic fixtures, creating uncertainty that FPL managers cannot afford during the business end of the season. Any player whose minutes are uncertain should be treated with extreme caution.

---

Chip Strategy: The Wildcard Question

The wildcard dominates the chip conversation at this stage of the season, and the opinions among top creators diverge sharply—though the emergence of consensus around timing reveals genuine strategic wisdom emerging from the community.

FP Harry has positioned Gameweek 32 as the key wildcard moment for many managers, but with crucial caveats. The FA Cup results will dramatically reshape the fixture landscape, and deploying a wildcard before those results are known could leave managers with a squad that no longer matches the fixture schedule that emerges. Harry's recommendation to wait and watch—to let the chaos resolve itself before committing to a 15-player overhaul—represents a patient approach that will frustrate managers desperate for immediate action but may prove prescient in the long run.

The wildcard draft Harry outlined in his GW32 thoughts video suggests a more conservative approach than many managers expect. Rather than wholesale changes, the best wildcard strategies at this stage often involve targeted surgery—moving away from blanked teams, addressing clear injury or form concerns, and adding assets with favorable run-ins. The days of spending the entire wildcard in one night, replacing half your squad with unproven differentials, are largely behind most experienced managers.

The free hit chip presents different considerations. For managers with limited blanked players, using the free hit in Gameweek 31 simply to maximize one week of points often does not math out—taking a -4 hit and potentially making future transfers more difficult rarely justifies the short-term gain. The free hit makes most sense as a chip to save for a future double gameweek where the fixture advantage is truly significant.

Bench boost strategy remains speculative without confirmed double gameweeks, but managers who have already used their free hit or wildcard should begin planning for potential bench boost opportunities in the run-in. The fixtures will eventually reveal themselves; patience is the bridge between chaos and clarity.

---

Where the Experts Disagree

The FPL community's beauty lies in its diversity of strategic approaches, and this gameweek proves no exception. The most significant disagreement centers on timing: should managers wildcard now, in Gameweek 31, or wait until after the FA Cup results reshape the fixture list?

FP Harry's patient approach—do nothing for the next three weeks, let the FA Cup resolve, and only then consider major moves—contrasts sharply with the aggressive positioning of managers like Eric, the world number one, who appears unwilling to accept gameweek mediocrity regardless of fixture complications. Harry's climb from 167k to 36k without using his wildcard suggests that sometimes restraint outperforms action, but Eric's sustained success at the summit tells a different story about the value of calculated aggression.

Tom and Sam's more immediate focus on Gameweek 30 recovery contrasts with the longer-term strategic thinking that dominates Harry's analysis. For managers in different rank positions, these approaches carry different weights—someone fighting to avoid relegation to a lower league has different priorities than someone targeting a top 10,000 finish or pushing for overall rank glory.

The debate over whether to take hits in blank gameweeks also continues. Some managers swear by the -4 as an acceptable cost for maintaining momentum; others view any hit in a gameweek where many of their rivals will also blank as unnecessary. Both perspectives have merit depending on individual squad construction, overall strategy, and the specific players involved.

---

Your Three Key Actions for the Week

After synthesizing the strategies, warnings, and philosophical debates from the FPL community's most respected voices, three clear priorities emerge for managers navigating Gameweek 31 and beyond.

First, identify your blanked players and make the minimum necessary moves. If Arsenal, Wolves, Manchester City, or Crystal Palace players occupy your squad, you face a choice: accept zero returns from them this gameweek, or spend a transfer to bring in replacement assets from active teams. For most managers, a single -4 hit to replace one blanked player with a functional alternative represents better value than attempting to overhaul your entire squad. The top 10,000 managers' consensus—80% making transfers—confirms that inaction carries its own costs when the fixture list turns hostile.

Second, do not wildcard this week. FP Harry's argument is compelling: the FA Cup results will fundamentally reshape the fixture calendar for the remainder of the season, and wildcarding before those results are known risks creating a squad that no longer matches the schedule that emerges. Use the international break to research, plan, and prepare your wildcard strategy for Gameweek 32 or later. If your squad is genuinely broken, a carefully targeted transfer or two beats a panic wildcard every time.

Third, identify three assets with favorable run-ins and target them for your endgame squad. The relegation battle offers particularly attractive fixtures—teams fighting to stay up face each other with desperation, creating high-scoring opportunities that more stable teams in mid-table might not provide. Whether you wildcard in Gameweek 32, 33, or beyond, having a clear picture of your target assets will allow you to execute your plans efficiently when the time comes. The managers who finish the season strongest are not those who reacted to every piece of news, but those who maintained strategic discipline and pounced when the moment was right.

The blank gameweek is upon us, but the season is far from over. Seven weeks remain, and the opportunities for differentiation will only increase as chaos reshapes the fixture list. Trust your preparation, ignore the noise, and remember: in FPL, patience is not passive. Patience is strategy.
