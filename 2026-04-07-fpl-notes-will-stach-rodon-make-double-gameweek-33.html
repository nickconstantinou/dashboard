FPL notes: Will Stach + Rodon make Double Gameweek 33?

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

The Full Story

Leeds United secured their first FA Cup semi-final appearance in nearly 40 years following a dramatic penalty shootout victory over West Ham United at the London Stadium. The match ended 2-2 after extra time, with Leeds prevailing 4-2 on penalties to set up a clash against Chelsea. However, the victory came at a significant cost as two of Leeds' most affordable Fantasy Premier League assets—midfielder Anton Stach (£4.8m) and defender Joe Rodon (£3.9m)—both suffered ankle injuries during the match.

Stach, who ranks joint-second in the Premier League for creating chances (58), was forced off in the 38th minute after a nasty follow-through from West Ham defender Max Kilman caught his ankle. Manager Daniel Farke confirmed the German was later seen on crutches wearing a protective boot, sharing a photo of his swollen ankle on Instagram. Farke stated: "Anton should have been rewarded with a penalty, hit against his ankle, probably a ligament injury. We have to assess how bad it is. I hope it's not too bad. He's a key player for us."

Rodon, similarly valued at just £3.9m and considered ideal for FPL chip strategies, rolled his ankle during the fixture. Farke noted: "Joe Rodon rolled his ankle, we have to assess him. Thank God there's a bit of time before the next game."

Also absent from the match was Gabriel Gudmundsson (£3.8m) due to a minor groin issue.

Why This Matters

The timing of these injuries is particularly troublesome for FPL managers. Leeds now have a Double Gameweek 33 scheduled against Wolverhampton Wanderers and Bournemouth—their most favorable run since the blanks begin. Managers planning to activate theBench Boost chip during this double gameweek were counting on Stach and Rodon as cheap fifth-midfielder and fifth-defender options, respectively. Both players offer exceptional value at under £5m while maintaining starting positions and high chance-creation metrics (Stach) or clean sheet potential (Rodon).

Additionally, Dominic Calvert-Lewin (£5.6m) was being considered for Goals Imminent entry as he searches for form. The striker has managed just one attacking return from nine league outings, with a particularly costly penalty miss in Gameweek 30 contributing to Leeds' four-match scoring drought.
