Let's Get Back On Track: FPL Gameweek 30 Strategy and FA Cup Implications

Introduction

The latest episode of this popular FPL podcast finds the hosts navigating another irregular week in the Fantasy Premier League calendar, as the FA Cup weekend has once again disrupted the traditional fixture schedule. Recorded on Tuesday the 10th of March, the episode addresses the peculiar timing challenge facing FPL managers, with the hosts noting that "it's been a minute" since their last recording and acknowledging the unusual scheduling that saw matches scattered across Sunday and into Monday evening. The podcast brings together the core trio—Tom, Sam, and their recurring co-host—to dissect the fallout from Gameweek 29, analyze the FA Cup results and their ramifications for the fixture calendar, and begin formulating strategies for the critical period ahead.

The timing of this episode is particularly significant because we're entering one of the most consequential phases of the FPL season. With blank gameweek 31 looming on the horizon and double gameweeks 33 and 34 approaching rapidly, managers need to be making decisions now that will determine their success in the season's final stretch. The hosts emphasize that this is the period where championship-winning strategies are either executed or abandoned, and the podcast aims to provide the strategic framework needed to navigate these upcoming challenges.

Gameweek 29 Performance Review

Tom's Week

Tom opens up about his Gameweek 29 experience, describing it as "not very good" while acknowledging that his goalkeeper choices—specifically Tottenham assets—contributed to his struggles. He scored 60 points for the week, a score he characterizes as "all out" and defined primarily by the performance of Pedro or no Pedro, depending on how the fixtures played out. The week saw him treading water, having rolled his transfer in the end after initially planning to make a move. He ended up benching Hill, who came off the bench to score eight points, while playing Chaliba in his starting lineup. Tom admits that despite the early goal for Villa, he believes he made "the right decision" even though Chiao second on his bench got Defcon—though he acknowledges that in hindsight, the differences were negligible.

The captaincy decision proved particularly costly for Tom, as he went with Smeno (Semenyo) who hit the woodwork and came "one Sangari arm away" from a goal that would have resulted in a haul. Tom reflects that this miss meant he didn't get bonus points even if he had scored, but notes that at least Smeno returned something. His team also included an assist for Bruno and clean sheet points from Ry Gabrielle and Hill coming off the bench, bringing his total to 60 points and pushing him down to the 140k mark in overall ranking. Tom expresses frustration that he's "bumping up and down holding position" without many differentials, describing his current state as "a bit like Bork's quiet for me at the minute" with not much going on. He's now considering using both transfers in Gameweek 30 to break out of what he describes as an "interminable weight" dragging on his wildcard.

Sam's Week

Sam's Gameweek 29 was similarly underwhelming, though he managed to limit the damage to around 8.2k in rank drops, finishing with 62 points—a gray arrow week that saw him slide slightly but nothing catastrophic. Looking at his team in isolation, Sam says he's "pretty happy with the performances," noting that several players who were due a haul finally delivered. Keller kept a clean sheet against Bournemouth despite what Sam describes as an "onslaught from Tavanir in particular," earning a six-pointer. His Arsenal defensive assets performed exceptionally well, with Gabrielle scoring 11 points and Timber—whom Sam singles out for particular praise—earning 13 points through an attacking return, clean sheet, defcon, and bonus points.

Sam's midfield proved more problematic, with Virgil Van Dijk delivering just one point, forcing him to start with the wrong Anderson in his lineup. Fortunately, Anderson didn't come off the bench, allowing Sam to get some bench value instead. He brought in Anderson for Anderson and received 12 points from that transaction—a move he admits was questionable given his previous decisions with this player. Sam expresses particular regret about benching Elliot Anderson, who he describes as "my boy" and "my favorite player of the season," noting that Elliot finally got his goal with "a well taken finish" in Gameweek 29. Sam apologizes to Elliot for benching him and declares he won't make that mistake again. The rest of his squad delivered mixed results: Fernandez scored eight points while Rogers and Burma were "both really disappointing." The biggest disappointment, however, was Harland, who started and played 90 minutes but had no attacking returns, including missing a 0.9 xG chance that Sam describes as "pretty much an open goal."

FA Cup Results and FPL Fixture Implications

The FA Cup results over the weekend have created significant ripples through the FPL fixture calendar, and the hosts spend considerable time analyzing how these results will affect upcoming gameweeks. The draw has confirmed some key matchups that will directly impact which teams have blank fixtures and which enjoy double gameweeks. The most significant outcome is that Manchester City and Liverpool have been drawn together in the FA Cup, meaning one of these powerhouse clubs will be eliminated and potentially free up fixtures in the coming weeks. This has "a lot of ramifications" according to Sam, who notes that it affects "who's in who's out" when trying to identify favorable fixture combinations.

Chelsea will face Port Vale in what appears to be a favorable draw, while Arsenal faces a trickier assignment away at St. Aries. West Ham, after winning on penalties against Brentford, also has a potentially favorable draw. The hosts note that we now have "some kind of fixtures for 34 which are now confirmed" and can identify probable doublers in Gameweek 33 based on these results. This is crucial information for managers looking to activate their chips in the most profitable windows.

European Competition Impact on Rotation

With Champions League football ongoing during this recording—Spurs were currently 4-1 down in their match—the hosts discuss how European commitments will affect rotation in Gameweek 30. Tottenham's heavy defeat means their players face a quick turnaround, though the hosts note that "it doesn't look like TUDA's going to be around for much longer" given the scoreline. This European football aspect adds another layer of complexity to squad selection, particularly for managers considering players from clubs still in continental competition.

The discussion highlights the interconnected nature of all competitions and how results in one competition directly affect FPL decisions. Managers need to be aware of which players might be rotated ahead of important European matches, and which clubs might prioritize domestic league positions over European commitments.

Blank Gameweek 31 and Double Gameweeks

The upcoming blank gameweek 31 is a major focus of the episode, with the hosts exploring strategies for managing players from teams that will have no fixtures that week. There's particular discussion about whether managers should consider moving away from Liverpool players a week early, ahead of their fixtures against Tottenham at home. This is especially relevant given Liverpool's struggles as the hosts record—the team was losing "in the background" while the podcast was being recorded, which Sam admits made them "quite glad" they hadn't been more bullish on MoSalah before seeing how that match transpired.

The hosts discuss the timing of chip usage, noting that Gameweek 33 and 34 will feature double gameweeks for several teams. This creates a strategic puzzle: should managers hold players for the doubles, or should they prepare for the blank by moving to teams with guaranteed fixtures in Gameweek 31? The analysis suggests that we're entering "chips season" where successful navigation of these double and blank gameweeks will likely determine overall rankings.

Captaincy Discussion and Alternatives to Haaland

A significant portion of the episode addresses the eternal FPL question: should Haaland be captained? The hosts acknowledge that despite his blank in Gameweek 29, Haaland remains the default captain choice for most managers. However, they explore alternatives for those looking to take a differentiated approach, discussing players who might offer strong captaincy options if bypassing the Manchester City striker.

The conversation includes analysis of form versus fixtures, examining which players have favorable matchups in upcoming gameweeks. Phil (presumably Phil Foden) gets a mention as a potential differential captain option, though the hosts acknowledge that the premium price point and ownership levels make him a less attractive alternative for most squads.

Transfer Strategies and Team Plans

Both hosts reveal their thinking around Gameweek 30 transfers, with Tom explicitly stating he "need[s] a big one" and "really need[s] a big game week soon." He's considering using both transfers to make a significant change to his squad, potentially moving away from his current strategy. The discussion reveals that Tom is sitting on two free transfers, which gives him flexibility to either make one major change or two smaller moves.

The hosts discuss potential targets for the upcoming week, including the possibility of bringing in Liverpool players ahead of their fixture against Tottenham. This represents a shift in thinking given Liverpool's recent struggles, but the favorable fixture might make them attractive for the double gameweeks that follow. There's also discussion of European fixtures affecting availability and the need to consider rotation risks when selecting players from clubs with multiple competitions.

Key Strategic Insights

First, the FA Cup results have significant implications for the fixture calendar, with Manchester City and Liverpool potentially providing doublers in the coming weeks depending on their FA Cup elimination. Managers should identify which teams will have favorable fixture runs and position their squads accordingly.

Second, blank gameweek 31 preparation is already underway, and managers need to be making decisions now about which players to keep from teams facing blanks and which to move away from. The hosts suggest that even premium players like Liverpool assets might need to be moved a week early if it helps prepare for the blank.

Third, the chip activation window is approaching, with double gameweeks 33 and 34 offering the most potential for big returns. Successful managers will need to balance holding players for these doubles against the risk of carrying dead weight through blank gameweek 31.

Fourth, individual player selection matters less than overall team structure during this period. Both hosts emphasize that they're treading water despite having decent individual performances, suggesting that squad construction and fixture planning are more important than any single player's form.

Fifth, the captains reveal personal regrets about benching players who then delivered—Tom with Smeno and Sam with Elliot Anderson—demonstrating that even experienced managers struggle with the timing of their decisions. This serves as a reminder that FPL success often comes down to trusting your initial judgments rather than constantly second-guessing.

Who This Content Is For

This podcast episode is essential listening for Fantasy Premier League managers competing in serious mini-leagues or aiming for top-tier overall rankings. The content is particularly valuable for those in the 100k to 500k ranking range who, like the hosts, find themselves "treading water" and need a strategic breakthrough to climb the leaderboard. The episode provides specific tactical guidance on navigating the upcoming blank and double gameweeks, with actionable insights about which teams to target and which players to avoid.

The value extends beyond simple team recommendations to strategic framework: managers will understand *why* certain decisions matter (the interconnected nature of FA Cup results, European fixtures, and domestic schedules) and *how* to apply this understanding to their own squads. Whether you're looking to confirm your existing strategy or need a complete reset before Gameweek 30, this episode provides the analytical foundation for making informed decisions during the season's most critical period.
