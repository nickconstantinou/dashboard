# FPL Gameweek 35: Thursday's Live Injury Updates

## Fulham Injury Watch

Fulham faces Arsenal in Gameweek 35, and the injury situation at Craven Cottage remains concerning for Fantasy Premier League managers.

**Ryan Sessegnon** (£5.4m) is confirmed out for the Arsenal fixture. The defender sustained a muscle injury that has ruled him out of Thursday's match, and indications suggest he will likely miss the subsequent game as well. Managers should not expect Sessegnon to return to action in the near term.

**Alex Iwobi** (£6.2m) continues to be unavailable due to a hamstring problem. The midfielder remains sidelined and is not expected to feature in the upcoming fixture.

**Kenny Tete** (£4.5m) offers a glimmer of hope for Fulham fans. The right-back has recovered from a foot injury and could be involved as a substitute against Arsenal. However, he is considered too short on match fitness to start the game. His return provides defensive cover for managers weighing their transfer options.

A player identified only as Kevin also remains out with a foot injury, with no timeline given for his return.

## Sunderland Outlook

Sunderland's injury picture is more optimistic ahead of the gameweek.

**Romaine Mundle** (£4.9m) has suffered a significant setback. The midfielder faces a likely extended spell on the sidelines due to a hamstring injury, with reports suggesting he could be out for the remainder of the season.

However, the club expects three of their four injured wingers to become available soon. **Jocelin Ta Bi** is likely to make his return this weekend from an ankle injury, potentially giving Sunderland a boost in attacking options.

**Bertrand Traore** and **Nilson Angulo** (£5.0m) are both candidates for return this weekend or the following week. Traore has been recovering from a knee injury while Angulo has been sidelined, with the club set to make decisions on their availability closer to matchday.

Managers considering Sunderland assets should monitor these updates closely, as the potential return of multiple attacking options could shift the team's prospects in the final fixtures.
