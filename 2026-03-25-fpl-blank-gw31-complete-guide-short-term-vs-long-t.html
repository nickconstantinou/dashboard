Understanding the Blank Gameweek 31 Confirmation

The European results this week proved decisive in determining the fixture landscape for the coming weeks. Crystal Palace's progression through Europa League competition meant that their fixture against Manchester City could not be played in either gameweek 31 or 32, confirming what many suspected but needed official verification. Arsenal, Wolves, Manchester City, and Crystal Palace will all blank in gameweek 31, creating a significant challenge for managers who have players from these clubs. The fixture cannot be rearranged into an earlier midweek slot because both teams remained in European competition—Manchester City were knocked out, but Crystal Palace's continued European involvement meant the game remained postponed.

Looking ahead, gameweek 32 will be a normal gameweek with all teams in action, while gameweek 33 is expected to become the biggest double gameweek of the season. The exact composition of doubles and blanks will be confirmed before the gameweek 32 deadline, but predictive models from FPL.team and Planet FPPL are already offering guidance. The uncertainty around future gameweek compositions is why FP Harry emphasizes that managers should not be making any definitive transfer decisions until more information becomes available. The FA Cup results, particularly the quarter-final tie between Liverpool and Manchester City, will be a major determining factor in which teams get doubles and when those doubles occur. Until those results are known and the Premier League announces the official fixture schedule, attempting to predict the optimal transfer strategy is essentially guesswork.

Chip Strategy Options: Two Main Paths Forward

The core strategic debate for FPL managers revolves around when to deploy their remaining chips, particularly the wildcard and free hit that most managers still have available. There are two primary strategies being discussed in the FPL community, each with distinct implications for transfer timing and player selection.

Option One involves using the wildcard in gameweek 32, followed by bench boost in gameweek 33, and then the free hit in gameweek 34. This strategy becomes more attractive if Liverpool beat Manchester City in the FA Cup, as it would likely create more favorable double gameweek opportunities for teams outside the Manchester City orbit. Under this strategy, managers would be looking to build a team specifically optimized for gameweek 33, which appears likely to be the biggest double of the season with teams like Arsenal and Chelsea potentially doubling.

Option Two takes a more conservative approach, using the free hit in gameweek 33 to maximize that double gameweek, then wildcarding in gameweek 35, and finally using bench boost in gameweek 36. This option becomes more appealing if Manchester City beat Liverpool in the FA Cup, as it would likely result in different teams getting the double gameweeks. The Manchester City victory scenario creates more uncertainty about which teams will have favorable fixtures in gameweek 33, making the wildcard in gameweek 35 potentially more valuable.

FP Harry notes that this is not exact science—even if Manchester City win, some managers might still prefer Option One, and vice versa. The general framework provides a useful guide for thinking about chip deployment, but individual circumstances and team compositions will vary significantly. What matters most is understanding which scenario is more likely and how that impacts the types of players managers should be targeting in the short term.

Critical Transfer Advice: Patience is Key

The most important message for managers considering transfers this week is to exercise patience and avoid premature moves. FP Harry explicitly states that managers should "please please please please do not make any early wild card 32 transfers until after the FA Cup." This means waiting until at least midweek between gameweek 31 and 32, when the Premier League will start announcing which fixtures have been rearranged and which teams will be doubling.

The reasoning is straightforward: making transfers now provides minimal upside while potentially locking managers into suboptimal strategies. The maximum value gain from transferring early is perhaps 0.2 to 0.5 million in player value—trivial compared to the potential upside of making better-informed decisions once the fixture picture becomes clearer. More importantly, transferring early could force managers into picking players for specific chip strategies without knowing whether those strategies will even be optimal.

This is particularly relevant for players like those from Newcastle United. Many managers have been looking at Newcastle players like Malik Cho or Anthony Gordon as obvious picks for this week. While these are good players with favorable fixtures—Newcastle host Sunderland at home in gameweek 32 before playing Crystal Palace away—FP Harry explains that Newcastle players "don't really fit that well unless you go with strategy one." If a manager ends up using the free hit in gameweek 33 rather than the wildcard, Newcastle's lack of a gameweek 34 fixture (which is currently blank) becomes a significant problem. The blank in gameweek 34 means Newcastle assets would need to be transferred out before using the free hit, potentially creating complications.

The recommendation is to maintain flexibility by avoiding players who are heavily tied to specific chip strategies. Instead, managers should focus on acquiring players who provide value regardless of which strategy they eventually adopt. This means not forcing Newcastle players unless absolutely committed to strategy one, and instead looking for players with more long-term viability across different scenarios.
