# FPL Notes: West Ham Luckless Against Brentford; Keane Lewis-Potter Impresses

## Match Overview

Brentford secured a convincing 3-0 victory over West Ham United in Gameweek 35, but the result arguably flattered the home side. West Ham experienced significant misfortune during the match, hitting the woodwork on three occasions and suffering from unfortunate officiating decisions that contributed to their downfall.

## West Ham's Misfortune

West Ham United were desperately unlucky during their trip to Brentford. Konstantinos Mavropanos (£4.5m) saw an equaliser chalk off for a marginal offside decision, while he also put through his own net to compound the visitors' problems in the build-up to the first goal. Beyond individual errors, the Hammers dominated certain phases of play without reward.

Taty Castellanos (£5.5m) typified West Ham's frustration, accumulating a match-high five shots without finding the net. The striker will feel he should have returned at least one goal from such frequency. Crysencio Summerville (£5.5m) also rattled the bar as West Ham created numerous chances without conversion.

From a Fantasy perspective, Jarrod Bowen (£7.8m) has offered little in recent weeks. The West Ham forward has recorded no big chances since Gameweek 24, suggesting his owners may want to explore alternatives.

## Brentford Assets Worth Monitoring

Keane Lewis-Potter (£4.8m) has emerged as a genuine budget option following his return to the starting lineup. The defender assisted Brentford's third goal against West Ham and has impressed from left-back since reclaiming his place in the side from Gameweek 30 onwards. At just £4.8m, he offers rock-bottom floor appeal.

Dango Ouattara (£5.8m) continues to warrant attention. The winger and his strike partner have won seven penalties between them during the 2025/26 campaign, creating regular penalty-scoring opportunities for Igor Thiago. Speaking of the Brazilian striker, Thiago converted from the spot to claim his 22nd league goal of the season against West Ham.

## Transfer Trends

FPL managers appear to be losing faith in two assets following this match. Igor Thiago and Jarrod Bowen are second and third for transfers out heading into the Gameweek 36 deadline, suggesting many managers are looking elsewhere despite the Brentford striker's impressive goal tally.
