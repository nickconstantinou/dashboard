# FPL Gameweek 32: 23 Stand-Out Statistics Reveal Hidden Gems for Fantasy Managers

## The Full Story

The latest edition of Fantasy Premier League's "Big Numbers" series has surfaced with Gameweek 32 delivering a treasure trove of statistical insights that could prove invaluable for managers navigating the season's critical final stretch. The analysis pulls from comprehensive Opta and StatsBomb data to identify the most eye-catching performances across all positions.

Goalkeeper statistics reveal Everton shot-stopper Jordan Pickford as the Premier League's standout performer in preventing expected goals, with an remarkable +5.10 xG prevented across the season—the best rate among all Premier League goalkeepers. Pickford recently recorded his 11th clean sheet of the campaign, vaulting him to second place in the FPL points standings for his position. At the opposite end of the spectrum, Fulham's Bernd Leno sits at a troubling -5.80 xG prevented, alongside Wolves duo Sam Johnstone (-5.30) and Jose Sa (-3.00), and Tottenham's Guglielmo Vicario (-5.20).

In an extraordinary offensive contribution from the goalkeeper position, Chelsea's Robert Sanchez has created six Opta-defined "big chances"—double the next-best tally among Premier League goalkeepers—becoming one of just three goalkeepers to register an FPL assist this term. Meanwhile, Brentford's Caoimhin Kelleher has saved three penalties this season, making him the only Premier League goalkeeper to have stopped more than a single spot-kick, despite his team conceding a joint league-high seven penalties overall.

Among defenders, Newcastle United's Malick Thiaw leads with nine big chances registered—the most any Premier League defender has been afforded this term—while also scoring the joint-most goals (four) for his position. Nottingham Forest's Neco Williams has taken 35 shots, the most of any defender, though his conversion rate shows just one goal from 12 efforts inside the penalty area. Manchester City's Nico O'Reilly has been far more clinical, scoring four goals from 24 attempts inside the box.

## Why This Matters

These statistics represent more than interesting trivia—they provide actionable intelligence for FPL managers making critical transfer decisions. Pickford's +5.10 xG prevented suggests he's facing shots well but also benefiting from defensive structure, making him a premium asset despite Everton's inconsistent season. The revelation that just three goalkeepers have recorded assists this term underscores how rare offensive contributions from this position truly are, making Sanchez's six big chances created particularly significant.

For managers chasing differential picks, Kelleher's three penalty saves from Brentford's seven conceded presents a fascinating risk-reward scenario—opposing takers will be wary, but the workload remains high. Among defenders, the Big Numbers data reveals Thiaw as an under-the-radar option combining attacking threat (nine big chances) with goal-scoring returns (four goals), while O'Reilly's underlying numbers suggest his four league goals come from sustainable positions rather than luck.

## Key Details

- **Jordan Pickford**: +5.10 xG prevented (best in Premier League), 11 clean sheets, FPL points rank #2 among goalkeepers
- **Robert Sanchez**: 6 big chances created (double next-best goalkeeper), one of three goalkeepers with an FPL assist
- **Caoimhin Kelleher**: 3 penalties saved (only PL keeper with more than one), Brentford conceded 7 penalties (joint league-high)
- **Malick Thiaw**: 9 big chances (most defender this term), 4 goals (joint-most for position)
- **Neco Williams**: 35 shots (most defender), 1 goal from 12 shots inside penalty area
- **Nico O'Reilly**: 4 goals from 24 shots inside box, recent strong form (4 big chances in last 6 Gameweeks)
