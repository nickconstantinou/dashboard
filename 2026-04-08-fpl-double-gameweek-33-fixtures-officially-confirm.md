# FPL Managers Rejoice as Double Gameweek 33 Fixtures Finally Confirmed

## The Full Story

Fantasy Premier League managers received the official confirmation they had been waiting for on April 7, 2026, as the Premier League rubber-stamped the fixtures for Double Gameweek 33. The announcement comes after last week's provisional confirmation that any postponed Gameweek 34 fixtures would shift to the midweek of Gameweek 33, and the league has now locked in the exact dates and times for three critical make-up matches.

The confirmed fixtures see Brighton and Hove Albion host Chelsea on Tuesday, April 21 at 20:00 BST, while Bournemouth welcome Leeds United and Burnley entertain Manchester City on Wednesday, April 22, also at 20:00 BST. These three matches complete a double gameweek for six Premier League clubs, presenting FPL managers with a crucial opportunity to maximize their points returns through strategic player selection.

## Why This Matters

For the millions of FPL managers worldwide, double gameweeks represent golden opportunities to gain significant ground on rivals—or extend leads—by loading up on players from clubs playing twice in a single gameweek. The confirmation of Gameweek 33's fixtures immediately triggers a rush to identify which players offer the best value, factoring in not just the quantity of matches but also the quality of opponents and, critically, turnaround times between fixtures.

The timing of these matches carries particular weight because three clubs—Chelsea, Leeds United, and Manchester City—are also contending with FA Cup semi-finals scheduled for the weekend of April 25-26. This creates a complex puzzle for FPL managers: players from these clubs may face rotation concerns depending on how much rest they receive between their final Gameweek 33 match and their cup commitments at Wembley. The turnover times become essential data for making informed transfer decisions.

## Key Details

**Confirmed Fixtures (all times BST):**

- Tuesday 21 April, 20:00: Brighton and Hove Albion v Chelsea
- Wednesday 22 April, 20:00: Bournemouth v Leeds United
- Wednesday 22 April, 20:00: Burnley v Manchester City

**Turnaround Times (hours between full-time and next kick-off):**

- Chelsea: 70 hours (shortest recovery time)
- Manchester City: Slightly more than 70 hours
- Burnley: Similar timeframe to Manchester City
- Brighton and Hove Albion: Similar timeframe
- Bournemouth and Leeds United: Best situation, playing Saturday-Wednesday

**Rest Considerations:**

- Bournemouth, Brighton and Burnley have the weekend off after their final Gameweek 33 fixtures
- All six clubs have free midweeks before Gameweek 33, providing recovery periods before their double-headers commence
- FA Cup semi-finals for Chelsea, Leeds United, and Manchester City will determine how much rest these sides receive before Wembley trips

## Context & Background

This double gameweek situation arose from the fixture congestion caused by postponed matches earlier in the season. When Gameweek 34 fixtures were postponed, the Premier League made the logical decision to reschedule those matches into the midweek slot traditionally reserved for Gameweek 33, creating an unexpected double gameweek for the affected clubs.

For FPL managers, double gameweeks have historically been moments of significant strategy shifts. Those who successfully identify undervalued assets from doubling teams—particularly those with favorable fixtures and adequate recovery time—often gain substantial advantages in overall rankings. The current situation is particularly complex because the FA Cup semi-finals create uncertainty around player selection for three of the six clubs involved.

## What's Next

FPL managers should now be actively assessing their squads ahead of Gameweek 33, with particular attention to the recovery situations facing each club. The scheduling of FA Cup semi-finals for Chelsea, Leeds United, and Manchester City (expected on the weekend of April 25/26) will be the next significant piece of information to monitor, as these kick-off times will reveal exactly how much rest these players receive between their final gameweek match and potential cup appearances.

The next major consideration comes in the form of team news and rotation risks, particularly for the three clubs with FA Cup semi-finals looming. Managers must weigh the temptation of doubling up on players from these clubs against the risk of rotation if those players feature heavily in cup matches and then rest for the following weekend. Meanwhile, Bournemouth and Leeds United players—given their favorable Saturday-to-Wednesday turnaround—may emerge as the safest options for those seeking to minimize rotation risk while still accessing double gameweek points.
