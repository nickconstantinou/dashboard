FPL Gameweek Roundup: Salah's Farewell, Cherki's Rise, and the Run-In Strategy

The Premier League landscape stands at a fascinating crossroads as we approach the business end of the season, with Mohamed Salah's impending departure from Liverpool creating both emotional weight and tactical uncertainty across the FPL community.

---

Today's Key FPL News & Injuries

The international break has provided time for reflection, but the news that broke before this week's podcast recordings will dominate FPL conversation for weeks to come: Mohamed Salah will leave Liverpool at the end of the 2025/26 season. The Egyptian's announcement represents one of the most significant shifts in FPL history, with the Liverpool star having been a permanent fixture in countless championship-winning squads over previous campaigns. His departure—confirmed as we approach the season's final stretch—leaves managers scrambling not only for the remainder of this term but also beginning to contemplate how their template teams will need to reconstruct themselves for next season.

On the injury front, three Arsenal defenders face race-against-time fitness calls ahead of their blank gameweek. Gabriel dos Santos Magalhães (£7.2m, 43.1% ownership) is dealing with a knee injury but carries a 75% chance of playing according to current assessments. Jurriën Timber (£6.3m, 26.9% ownership) is nursing an ankle problem with the same 75% availability probability, while William Saliba (£6.1m, 13.2% ownership) faces an ankle injury concern also rated at 75% likely to feature. This is particularly significant given Arsenal blank in Gameweek 31 alongside Crystal Palace, Manchester City, and Wolves. Mikkel Damsgaard (£5.6m, 0.9% ownership) of Brentford has a knock but is also given a 75% chance of playing.

---

Gameweek Landscape: The Blank GW Reality

The fixture picture for the run-in presents managers with a challenging puzzle. Gameweek 31 features four blanking teams: Arsenal, Crystal Palace, Manchester City, and Wolves. With no doubles confirmed across Gameweeks 29-38 according to current schedules, the traditional blank gameweek template strategy of loading up on bench fodder from those teams faces diminished returns.

Nottingham Forest emerges as perhaps the most compelling side from the form data, with Morgan Gibbs-White (£7.4m, 5.0% ownership) posting a form rating of 7.0 alongside teammates Elliot Anderson (£5.5m, 8.4% ownership) at 5.0 form and Igor Jesus Maciel da Cruz (£5.8m, 1.3% ownership) also at 5.0 form. Under Vítor Pereira, Forest have become genuine contenders for Champions League qualification, making their assets increasingly attractive for the run-in.

Everton present exceptional value across multiple price points, with James Garner (£5.2m, 4.2% ownership) leading their form charts at 6.5 alongside Kiernan Dewsbury-Hall (£5.1m, 5.0% ownership) and Ilian Iliman Ndiaye (£6.2m, 4.3% ownership) both at 5.0 form. James Tarkowski (£5.7m, 11.3% ownership) continues to offer solid defensive returns at 6.0 form, while the budget option Norberto Bercique Gomes Betuncal (£5.0m, 2.6% ownership) has exploded to 7.2 form despite minimal ownership.

---

Captain Recommendations

The captaincy debate has become more complex given the Salah situation and Manchester City's League Cup final victory over Arsenal, which showcased Ryan Cherki as a genuine differential option. The Frenchman, who arrived at City in January, drew effusive praise from Planet FPL hosts James and David, with David admitting he "wouldn't be surprised if Cherki was sitting here in 12 months going he should be the player of the year." James described Cherki as "unlike any other player in this league at the moment" with technical qualities compared favorably to Paris Saint-Germain's Ousmane Dembele.

However, Cherki does not currently appear in the top 50 form players list, meaning his recent ascendancy represents fresh opportunity rather than established template. Antoine Semenyo (£8.2m, 53.6% ownership) leads many ownership charts at 5.7 form and remains a reliable captaincy option given his consistent involvement in City's attacking transitions under Pep Guardiola.

For those seeking alternatives, Bruno Borges Fernandes (£10.3m, 44.3% ownership) continues his remarkable season with an extraordinary 11.0 form—the highest on offer—accumulating 189 total points for Manchester United. João Pedro Junqueira de Jesus (£7.8m, 50.4% ownership) at Chelsea represents the most-owned midfielder in the game and offers a blend of reliability and ceiling that makes him a consistent armband contender.

---

Transfer Strategy: Value Detection and Fixture Planning

João Victor Gomes da Silva (£5.3m, 0.3% ownership) of Wolves has emerged as the form player across the entire database, with an 8.0 form rating at rock-bottom pricing. While Wolves blank in Gameweek 31, his trajectory suggests he could become an essential budget enabler once fixtures resume. Managers should monitor whether his recent improvement under Rob Edwards represents genuine underlying change or statistical noise.

Anthony Gordon (£7.4m, 7.3% ownership) at Newcastle has climbed to 7.5 form, with Eddie Howe's side presenting an attractive run of fixtures. His relatively low ownership makes him a genuine differential for those seeking to differentiate from the Bruno Fernandes/João Pedro template.

Harry Wilson (£6.1m, 25.0% ownership) continues to fly under the radar at Fulham with 6.0 form and 152 total points, while Adrien Truffert (£4.7m, 3.2% ownership) at Bournemouth offers the cheapest route into an Andoni Iraola side that has been exceptional defensively, with Truffert posting 6.8 form and 122 points at £4.7m.

Hugo Ekitiké (£9.3m, 35.5% ownership) at Liverpool represents an interesting post-Salah asset if Liverpool's creative burden redistributes following the Egyptian's departure. His 4.8 form is modest, but the underlying situation could unlock significantly higher returns.

---

Players to Avoid or Sell

Managers should exercise caution with Bukayo Saka (£9.8m, 8.0% ownership), who has dropped to 4.7 form following Arsenal's cup final defeat and ongoing fitness concerns. The England international was not at full fitness during City's Wembley victory, and with Arsenal facing a blank in Gameweek 31, Saka's continued presence in squads represents locked-up capital without immediate returns.

The Gabriel-Timber-Saliba trio presents a difficult sell-or-hold decision. With all three nursing injuries and Arsenal blanking, managers face a choice between maintaining faith in Arsenal's title challenge or pivoting to sides with confirmed fixtures. The 75% availability probability for each creates significant bench uncertainty heading into the blank.

---

Chip Strategy: Timing and Resource Management

The international break provides crucial planning time, with Planet FPL promising a Chip and Strategy special on April 7th that should offer definitive guidance. Tom Campbell's appearance on Who Got the Assist revealed the consequences of aggressive chip deployment: having burned through his free hit, wildcard, and bench boost, he finds himself with limited tactical options for the run-in despite sitting in the top 500,000.

Campbell's approach—using a free hit on a triple Fulham defense with a captaincy gamble—represents the extreme end of "swinging for the fences" philosophy, which he openly acknowledged "not something I would advocate your listeners would have done or anyone in their right mind would do." His season, which he candidly described as "relatively catastrophic," nonetheless reignited his joy in the game after previous FPL fatigue.

For managers still holding chips, the absence of confirmed doubles in the remaining gameweeks suggests caution before deploying wildcards or free hits speculatively. The April 7th strategy special will likely clarify optimal timing once more fixture information becomes available.

---

Where Creators Disagree

Both podcasts emerged from the international break with broadly aligned perspectives: Salah's departure represents the dominant storyline, Cherki represents City's emerging differential, and the run-in requires careful fixture-based planning rather than emotional attachment to established assets.

However, philosophical differences emerged around Campbell's contrarian season approach. While Planet FPL's hosts generally advocate for calculated optimization, Campbell's "take huge swings" philosophy produced entertaining content even as his rankings suffered. The tension between maximizing expected points and maintaining FPL enjoyment represents an ongoing debate in the community—Campbell argued successfully that rediscovering joy transformed his relationship with the game, even if the spreadsheet disagreed.

---

Your 3 Key Actions This Week

1. Assess the Arsenal Injury Situation Before Confirming Your Blank Gameweek Strategy With Gabriel, Timber, and Saliba all carrying 75% fitness probabilities heading into their blank, managers need to make decisions about squad composition. If two or more are confirmed fit, holding Arsenal assets maintains value for the run-in. If fitness concerns persist, pivoting to Everton options like Tarkowski (£5.7m) or Garner (£5.2m) provides both fixture security and strong form.

2. Monitor Ryan Cherki's Development as a Differential for Post-Blank GWs While not yet established in the form rankings, Cherki's League Cup performance demonstrated the kind of ceiling that transforms seasons. Managers should track his minutes in upcoming City fixtures, as his emergence could force a reconsideration of the premium midfield landscape. Pep Guardiola's emotional investment in the Frenchman—visible in his celebrations at Wembley—suggests continued opportunities.

3. Begin Planning for Post-Salah Liverpool Even with fixtures remaining this season, the announcement that Salah departs creates immediate questions about creative distribution within Liverpool's attack. Hugo Ekitiké (£9.3m), Dominik Szoboszlai (£7.0m, 5.8 form), and Alexis Mac Allister (£6.3m, 5.0 form) could all benefit from increased offensive responsibility. Using this international break to project which Liverpool assets gain most from the changing dynamics provides strategic advantage for both the remainder of this season and next term's planning.
