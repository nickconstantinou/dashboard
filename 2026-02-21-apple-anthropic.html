Apple's AI Wearables + Anthropic's $380B Valuation

AI Apple Anthropic

Two big stories dropped this week that signal where AI is heading: Apple is getting into AI hardware, and Anthropic just raised $30B at a $380B valuation.

üçé Apple AI Wearables

According to Bloomberg, Apple's working on three new AI-powered devices:

  • Smart glasses - Camera-equipped, Siri-powered, no display
  • Wearable pendant - AI assistant you wear around your neck
  • AirPods with cameras - Built-in cameras for visual context
Key takeaway: These don't have displays - they're AI-first accessories that rely on your iPhone for processing. Production starts late 2026, launch in 2027.

The strategy seems to be: don't compete with Meta's Ray-Bans head-on. Instead, build accessories that feel natural and don't look "techy."

üí∞ Anthropic's $380B Haul

Anthropic raised $30 billion in Series G - more than double their last round. Some numbers:

  • $14B annualized revenue
  • $2.5B from Claude Code alone
  • Enterprise subscriptions up 4x

The bigger story: OpenClaw users are choosing Claude Opus 4.6 at scale. Over 90% of MyClaw users independently pick Opus 4.6 as their model. That's a strong market signal.

üìä What This Means

We're seeing two distinct AI strategies emerge:

  1. Hardware-first (Apple): Put AI into everyday objects, make it ambient
  2. Model-first (Anthropic): Make the model so good, hardware doesn't matter

Both can work. Apple's ecosystem keeps people locked in. Anthropic's model performance keeps developers locked in. The winner might be whichever approach pulls users deeper into their ecosystem first.

What I'm watching: whether Apple's wearables actually ship with real AI features, or if this is another AR Kit-style long game that never delivers.
