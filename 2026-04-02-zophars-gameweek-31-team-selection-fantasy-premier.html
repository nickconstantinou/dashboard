Zophar's Gameweek 31 Team Selection | FPL Tips 2025/26: Strategic Analysis for the Run-In

Introduction: The Calm Before the Storm

Zophar, the respected FPL content creator known as Zofa, returns with his team selection video for Gameweek 31, offering Fantasy Premier League managers a crucial strategic roadmap as the season enters its decisive phase. Recorded on a Friday morning with all European fixtures concluded, this particular episode carries extra weight—Gameweek 31 represents the final action before the international break, making it a pivotal moment for both immediate team decisions and long-term planning.

The timing matters significantly because the next FPL deadline falls on April 10th, leaving managers with limited time to finalize their approaches. Zophar opens by acknowledging his own Gameweek 30 performance, noting that despite making no transfers, he achieved his best gameweek rank of the entire season—a remarkable 209,000th place. This achievement came despite scoring only 68 points, demonstrating how valuable simply avoiding catastrophic decisions can be in FPL. His overall rank improved from approximately 500,000 to 343,000, a climb of nearly 160,000 places driven by a green arrow of close to 14 points.

What makes Zophar's analysis particularly valuable is his transparency about the inherent randomness in FPL success. "It's weird because maybe it was a weird week to have a good week," he admits. "It's not a very high scoring week but you take the points whenever you can get them." This pragmatic philosophy underpins his entire approach—focus on process over results, make sound decisions based on fixture analysis, and accept that variance plays a significant role in any individual gameweek.

European Results: What They Mean for FPL Managers

The midweek European results carry substantial implications for FPL planning, as Zophar meticulously analyzes how squad rotations and elimination from continental competition might affect Premier League output in the coming weeks.

Champions League Outlook

Liverpool and Arsenal emerged as the only English clubs to progress in the UEFA Champions League, while Manchester City, Chelsea, Newcastle, and Tottenham all suffered elimination. For FPL purposes, this means Arsenal's squad rotation concerns have diminished significantly—they can focus entirely on domestic competition. However, Liverpool's situation becomes more complex due to their Champions League progression, which Zophar explores in detail later.

Tottenham's elimination came via a 2-1 victory over Frankfurt in the second leg, a result that Zophar views with cautious optimism. "The crowd got behind Tuda," he notes, referencing Tottenham's changed tactical approach. "They did a more attacking system which I think will continue." This shift toward more expansive football could benefit their premium assets going forward. Notably, Solanke was left out of the squad due to injury, while Richarlison served a suspension—but both moves suggest Tottenham manager Ange Postecoglou is beginning to establish clearer squad hierarchy. Most encouragingly, Son Heung-min started the match, indicating his fitness after recent concerns.

Europa League Implications

The Europa League results proved equally significant for FPL planning. Aston Villa progressed comfortably against Lille with a 2-0 home victory, while Nottingham Forest somehow advanced despite fielding what Zophar describes as "a very very heavily rotated team" in a heavily-rotated lineup that included Williams, MGW, Callum Hudson-Odoi, and Elanga all coming on as substitutes around the 68th and 90th minute marks.

Forest's squad management strategy impresses Zophar considerably. "The minutes were pretty well managed," he observes. "Even Milenkovic got 60 [minutes]." His assessment is that fatigue should not significantly impact Forest's performance in their upcoming Premier League fixtures, particularly the crucial Sunday match against Tottenham. "I don't think it was a very difficult one, and I don't think the extra time plays a big factor over here now because you look at the starting XI—how many of these guys are actually going to start against Spurs? Probably just one. I think just Belohnovic."

Most critically for FPL managers, Crystal Palace progressed through their European tie, ensuring their fixture remains in Gameweek 36 rather than potentially shifting to an earlier gameweek. This has significant implications for the double gameweek landscape that Zophar analyzes extensively.

The Double Gameweek Landscape: Navigating Gameweek 33

Understanding the fixture scheduling permutations represents perhaps the most complex and consequential aspect of FPL strategy at this stage of the season, and Zophar dedicates substantial time to explaining the likely scenario.

Why Palace's Progression Matters

The concern among FPL managers centered on Crystal Palace potentially being eliminated from Europe, which would have freed up mid-April dates for their postponed fixture. Had Palace been knocked out, their match against Manchester City could have been scheduled during what was originally expected to be a free gameweek for both clubs.

However, since Palace progressed, "these slots now are taken up. So these games cannot go now in [Gameweek] 32." Zophar explains that the Premier League will announce the complete TV schedule after the FA Cup round on April 7th, but the most likely scenario—based on analysis from sources like FPL Spaceman—remains that Chelsea, Manchester City, Newcastle, Brighton, Arsenal, Burnley, and Aston Villa will have their double gameweek in Gameweek 33, with the corresponding blank in Gameweek 34.

Crucially, Zophar notes that this scenario has approximately a 52.6% probability, acknowledging that other permutations exist. However, this remains the baseline for strategic planning. The Palace versus Manchester City fixture most likely shifts to Gameweek 36, explaining why that particular double gameweek slot remains open in current projections.

The Manchester City, Chelsea, and Newcastle Uncertainty

Zophar identifies a specific complexity affecting three clubs: Manchester City, Chelsea, and Newcastle. These teams currently have a free slot in the schedule, which could theoretically be filled by games currently scheduled for Gameweek 32 or 34. However, the flexibility cuts both ways.

"They may not have the blank this week and they might not even have a double," Zophar warns, explaining that TV scheduling flexibility means these clubs could potentially have their postponed fixtures moved in either direction. This uncertainty makes investing heavily in their assets particularly risky without confirmed fixture clarity.

For managers operating on a strategy that assumes certain teams will blank in Gameweek 34, this represents a meaningful risk factor. Zophar recommends monitoring the official fixture announcement expected after the FA Cup round before committing significant resources to these teams.

Team-by-Team Fixture Analysis: The Strategic Landscape

Drawing on data visualization tools from Fantasy Football Scout (his longstanding data partner), Zophar methodically evaluates which teams present the most attractive options for the upcoming gameweeks.

Teams to Target

Tottenham, Forest, Sunderland, and Wolves emerge as the primary teams of interest based on their fixture run. Zophar specifically excludes Wolverhampton Wanderers from serious consideration—"Wolves not looking at obviously"—but identifies the other three as having favorable schedules that warrant investment.

Manchester United receives particular attention as a team with excellent fixtures in both Gameweek 32 and Gameweek 34. The schedule reads Sunderland (home), Newcastle (away), Tottenham (home), Forest (home), and Bournemouth (away)—a run that Zophar describes as "quite good, and even the Bournemouth fixture I don't look at as a poor one." This makes United players particularly attractive for managers planning their transfer strategy around the double gameweek period.

West Ham presents a more nuanced case. While their fixtures after Gameweek 31 include Wolves and Everton, which Zophar views positively, the scheduling uncertainty around their Gameweek 34 fixture makes him cautious. "Why? Because in [Gameweek] 33 they have the Monday night game. Sky likely don't like to move around too many TV fixtures in that case. So we think it's a very high chance of that [fixture staying in Gameweek 34]." However, he qualifies this assessment with a star notation, acknowledging it's still possible West Ham could have the double in Gameweek 33 and the blank in Gameweek 34. His recommendation is to wait until Gameweek 32 for clarity before committing to West Ham assets.

Teams to Avoid

Aston Villa receives short shrift from Zophar, who states bluntly: "Villa don't really want any of their players, the form's too poor." Despite their Europa League progression, Villa's Premier League form has been disappointing, making their assets difficult to justify regardless of fixture timing.

Liverpool presents a more complicated picture. While their fixtures in the abstract might be attractive, Zophar identifies significant rotation risk stemming from their Champions League commitments. The schedule analysis reveals a nightmare scenario: "They beat Manchester City in the FA Cup. The fixture could see some movement over here. But if Man City progress, it stays as is." More critically, after the international break, Liverpool face "Man City, Man City, PSG, Fulham, PSG in the space of what I think, 10 days."

This compressed schedule, combined with the first leg of their Champions League quarter-final against Paris Saint-Germain, means Liverpool are "going to be playing on Wednesday, then Fulham on Saturday, and they're going to be playing on Tuesday again against Galatasaray." The second leg is at home, but the cumulative fixture load makes meaningful rotation virtually inevitable. "You will see some rotation in the Fulham game. It's just inevitable," Zophar concludes.

Brighton and Everton similarly receive no interest, with Zophar dismissing them in rapid succession. "Brighton again, May Blank not interested. Everton, this is tied to West Ham's fate."

Zophar's Current Strategy: Free Hit 33, Wildcard 35

Having analyzed the fixture landscape, Zophar reveals his overarching tactical framework for the season's decisive phase.

The Strategic Framework

"I'm on Free Hit 33, Wild Card 35," Zophar explains, outlining his approach. This strategy acknowledges that Gameweek 32 and Gameweek 34 feature relatively straightforward fixtures for his current squad structure, while Gameweek 33 represents the optimal opportunity to maximize points through a free hit template built specifically around the confirmed double gameweek teams.

The wildcard in Gameweek 35 then allows him to reassess and reconstruct his squad for the final stretch of the season, potentially identifying different value plays or addressing accumulated injuries and suspensions.

Transfer Execution

Already having completed the sale of Arsenal's Declan Rice for Newcastle's Harvey Wilson, Zophar now has approximately 2.3 million in the bank with two free transfers available. However, he explicitly states his intention to use "the maximum of one free transfer" this gameweek.

The reasoning stems from the international break's approach. "There are a lot of UCL games etc to be played. We could see injuries. I don't want to turn up on the other side of this with a lot of injuries." The international break will feature playoff matches for various countries, meaning strong players across European leagues will face meaningful action rather than friendly matches. This increases injury risk, and maintaining a free transfer provides flexibility to respond to unexpected developments.

For his single transfer, Zophar's primary targets remain Manchester United players, with secondary interest in Tottenham assets. The West Ham move can wait until Gameweek 32 when fixture clarity improves and the entry point (facing West Ham's current fixture against Arsenal) becomes less challenging.

Key Takeaways: What Every FPL Manager Should Know

The free transfer discipline cannot be overstated. Zophar's decision to use only one of his two available transfers reflects sound risk management. The international break presents genuine injury uncertainty, and European competition means high-profile players across Premier League clubs may have participated in meaningful matches shortly before Gameweek 31. Holding a free transfer provides essential flexibility to respond to unexpected squad news without forcing difficult decisions or wasting valuable chips.

Manchester United represent the clearest value opportunity. With fixtures against Sunderland, Newcastle, Tottenham (twice), and Forest across Gameweeks 32 and 34, United players offer sustained attacking potential. Managers should prioritize Bruno Fernandes, Marcus Rashford, and potentially Alejandro Garnacho based on their involvement in United's attacking patterns. This schedule quality makes them viable across both the immediate and medium-term horizons.

Tottenham's tactical evolution warrants attention. Their Champions League exit came with a more attacking system that Zophar expects to continue. Combined with Son's apparent fitness and Solanke's pending return from injury, Tottenham assets may offer better value than their ownership percentages suggest. The Forest match on Sunday represents a particularly important data point for assessing their ceiling.

Liverpool rotation is unavoidable, not theoretical. Despite their quality, Liverpool's compressed schedule including Champions League matches makes them a avoid rather than a target. Managers with Liverpool assets should consider alternative options, particularly for the Gameweek 32 fixture against Fulham. The risk of Salah, Díaz, or Nunez being rotated mid-match significantly reduces their expected value.

Fixture planning should account for 52.6% certainty, not 100%. Zophar's analysis correctly identifies the most likely double gameweek scenario but acknowledges meaningful probability of alternative outcomes. Managers using free hit chips in Gameweek 33 should build flexibility into their wildcard teams and monitor official announcements closely. The difference between City having a double and not having one could swing many thousands of points.
