# Ask James | Thursday May 14th | Planet FPL 2025/26

## Fixture Focus: Arsenal's Prime Opportunity

Arsenal enter Gameweek 37 with what James identifies as the best fixture of the round. The Gunners face Burnley, presenting managers with a critical window to capitalize on favorable circumstances. James is clear on the strategic approach: with two free transfers available, managers should use one to acquire a third Arsenal asset. This reflects the premium placed on attacking options against a weakened opponent.

## Arsenal Rotation Risk for GW38

Managers holding Arsenal assets heading into the final gameweek should temper concerns about heavy rotation. James explains that Arsenal are unlikely to rotate significantly against Crystal Palace unless they secure the title in the preceding week. The reasoning centres on the Champions League final, which makes wholesale changes inadvisable for Mikel Arteta's side.

## Captaincy Considerations

Bruno Fernandes emerges as James's recommended captain for this gameweek, described simply as "captain's fine" when uncertainty arises. However, Saka presents a complicating factor. Arsenal have shown a tendency to withdraw Saka early when matches are comfortably under control. This pattern reduces his captaincy appeal, as managers risk losing points if he is substituted before completing the full ninety minutes against Burnley.

## Player Watch: Haaland and City Concerns

Manchester City's squad rotation raises questions ahead of the crucial run-in. Haaland did not start last night for City, with no reported injury issue identified at the time of recording. The situation with Nico Riley remains unclear. Managers considering City assets should monitor the official team news carefully before finalizing lineups.

## Additional Strategic Notes

On the transfer front, James strongly advises against entering Gameweek 38 with fewer than two free transfers. This conservative approach ensures flexibility to react to late injury news or fixture changes. The advice reflects the unpredictability of the final round and the importance of maintaining squad depth.

A notable trend emerged in GW36, when approximately 27% of the top 10,000 managers transferred in Sergio to Turkey. While this signals strong community confidence in that asset, James does not explicitly endorse the move, presenting it as informational intelligence for managers to consider alongside their own analysis.

Sunderland's form warrants attention despite their improved display against Manchester United last weekend. James notes they have been poor in recent weeks, though last Saturday's performance represented genuine progress. This inconsistency makes them a difficult team to assess for both offensive and defensive FPL considerations.
