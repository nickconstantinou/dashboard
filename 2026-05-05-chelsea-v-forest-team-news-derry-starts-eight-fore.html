Chelsea v Forest team news: Derry starts, eight Forest changes

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

Jesse Derry Earns First Professional Start

The headline news from Chelsea is that winger Jesse Derry makes his first professional start. The young player, priced at £4.5m in Fantasy Football Scout, will be looking to make an immediate impact in this fixture against Nottingham Forest.

Cole Palmer Returns to Starting XI

Cole Palmer also starts for Chelsea, having only been a substitute in the FA Cup semi-final. The £10.4m midfielder returns to the XI as Chelsea look to strengthen their attacking options for this Premier League encounter.

Chelsea Wide Options Depleted

The Blues have four senior wide-men out for the match. Pedro Neto and Alejandro Garnacho joining the absent contingent today compounds Chelsea's selection issues on the flanks. This depletion in wide attacking options makes Derry's first professional start particularly significant for the home side.

Colwill and James Return to Bench

Levi Colwill and Reece James are on the bench after recovering from injury. Both players provide Chelsea with experienced options among the substitutes as they manage their return to full fitness.

Major Forest Lineup Overhaul

Vitor Pereira makes eight changes from the Tricky Trees' UEFA Europa League semi-final first leg against Aston Villa. This represents a significant rotation from the Forest manager, likely managing player fatigue following their European commitments.

Key Forest Players on Bench

Chris Wood, Elliot Anderson and Morgan Gibbs-White are among those on the bench for Nottingham Forest. Wood, priced at £7.1m, represents a potentially valuable differential option if introduced from the bench during the match.

Forest Survivors from Thursday

The only survivors from Thursday night are Morato, Nicolas Dominguez and Igor Jesus. These three players will carry significant responsibility as they feature again after the Europa League semi-final first leg against Aston Villa.
