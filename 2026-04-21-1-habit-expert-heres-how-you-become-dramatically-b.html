# The Complete FPL Gameweek 33 Strategy Guide: Double Gameweek Analysis, Captaincy Picks, and Injury Updates

Fantasy Premier League managers across the globe face what is arguably the most consequential single gameweek of the entire season. Gameweek 33 presents a rare double gameweek opportunity for six teams simultaneously, transforming what could be a routine round of fixtures into a high-stakes chess match where strategic chip usage, careful player selection, and bold captaincy decisions could mean the difference between climbing the ranks and watching your season slip away. FPL Harry, one of the community's most respected analysts, breaks down every critical factor managers need to consider before locking in their teams—and the picture he paints is both fascinating and fraught with difficult choices.

The episode opens with breaking news from the Manchester City press conference held on Friday afternoon, just before recording began. Manager Pep Guardiola addressed several injury concerns that have been dominating manager conversations all week, and the updates carry significant implications for the thousands of FPL managers who have invested heavily in City assets. The most pressing question surrounded young defender Nico O'Reilly, who departed the match against Chelsea last weekend with what appeared to be a concerning injury. Guardiola confirmed that while Rubén Dias will not be available for the weekend's fixtures, O'Reilly is "fine" and available—a relief for the substantial portion of the FPL population who have him bookmarked, particularly those utilizing free hit chips to maximize their double gameweek exposure. Harry himself has O'Reilly in his squad and has no intention of selling, though he acknowledges there's still a slight possibility the defender might not complete both fixtures if he picked up a minor knock, potentially featuring against Arsenal but sitting out the Burnley match.

The Six Teams Defining Gameweek 33

This gameweek is unique in that exactly six teams face two matches each, and the data projections for offensive output have evolved throughout the week as more information has become available. Understanding which teams are expected to score the most goals—and which matchups favor clean sheets—is essential for managers deciding where to concentrate their investments.

Manchester City sit atop the projected goals chart with an expected 4.4 goals across their two fixtures: a demanding home match against Arsenal and a more favorable away trip to Burnley. The increase from earlier projections is notable, suggesting the model now accounts for both Burnley's defensive vulnerabilities and an increased expectation that City's attack can find success even against Arsenal's usually stingy backline. Harry finds it "crazy high" that City are predicted to score approximately two goals against Arsenal, given the Gunners' reputation as one of the league's best defensive units this season, but the underlying data continues to support robust output.

Chelsea remain relatively stable at 3.2 projected goals, with fairly even distribution across their home fixture against Manchester United and their away trip to Brighton. No significant developments over the past week have altered expectations for the Blues' attacking output, making them a steady option for managers seeking consistent goal involvement rather than explosive ceiling plays.

Bournemouth continue their climb up the projected goals rankings, now sitting third with 3.1 expected goals. Their fixtures split favorably toward their home match against Leeds, where the majority of their attacking production is anticipated to come, though Harry notes that Leeds' Monday night performance demonstrated they remain capable of competing at this level despite their precarious league position.

The most significant ranking movement this week comes from Brighton, who have overtaken Leeds to claim fourth position. The Seagulls have seen a meaningful increase in their projected goals from the beginning of the week, reflecting both their impressive recent form and growing confidence in their ability to breach defenses against Tottenham and Chelsea. Harry highlights Brighton's ascendancy as particularly noteworthy, suggesting their attacking returns could surprise managers who haven't been closely monitoring their upward trajectory.

Leeds and Burnley round out the six teams with double gameweeks, though both sit lower in the projected goals table. Leeds' fixture against Wolves at home represents their best opportunity for offensive returns, while their away trip to Bournemouth presents substantially more difficulty.

Defensive Probabilities and Clean Sheet Projections

For managers weighing whether to invest in defenders rather than attackers—or deciding which defenders represent the best value—the clean sheet percentages tell a more nuanced story than the raw offensive projections.

Manchester City maintain their position at the top of the clean sheet charts with a 62% chance of keeping at least one clean sheet across their double gameweek. This represents a slight increase from earlier in the week, with the model now slightly more confident in City's defensive capabilities even against Arsenal. For managers seeking defensive returns alongside attacking upside—particularly those with City's attacking assets like Haaland or O'Reilly—the data supports the premium pricing.

Leeds sit second with a 52% chance of at least one clean sheet, but the distribution tells an important story for those considering triple-ups on Leeds defense. Approximately 40% of that probability comes from their home fixture against Wolves, while the away trip to Bournemouth carries only a 22% chance. Harry cautions that managers who don't have high conviction in Leeds keeping a clean sheet at home might be over-investing relative to other options, particularly given the relatively modest ownership of Leeds defenders compared to more popular alternatives.

Brighton have crept into third position with a 41% clean sheet probability across their double, benefiting from Chelsea's odds beginning to shift unfavorably. The data now favors Brighton scoring in their matchup against Chelsea, reducing the Blues' clean sheet prospects and making Brighton's defensive assets more attractive by comparison.

Chelsea themselves sit next with a 39% probability, reflecting the new uncertainty around their away fixture at Brighton. Meanwhile, Bournemouth languish near the bottom of the defensive rankings, primarily due to their away match against Newcastle carrying only a 15% clean sheet probability. Harry reminds viewers that despite these unfavorable numbers, defenders like Haaland and Senesi remain excellent picks if they hit their defcon potential—meaning their individual attacking threat from set pieces and open play—because their ceiling can exceed what pure clean sheet percentages would suggest.

The Captaincy Conundrum: Haaland Dominance Versus Differential Strategy

Perhaps no decision in Gameweek 33 carries more weight than the captaincy choice, and the landscape presents a fascinating tension between safety and upside. Erling Haaland continues to command the vast majority of captain votes—Harry's YouTube poll showed the Norwegian striker at a staggering 80% captaincy rate, with substantial triple-captain usage among that group—and the underlying data provides compelling support for this conventional choice.

Manchester City are predicted to be the highest-scoring team across the entire double gameweek with 4.4 expected goals. Haaland maintains the highest probability of either scoring or assisting, and the model identifies him as the highest-scoring player expected in Gameweek 33. His recent FA Cup hat-trick against Liverpool—just a week or two ago now—demonstrates he remains in excellent goal-scoring form despite what the surface-level FPL data might suggest.

That surface data reveals an uncomfortable truth: Haaland has scored only one open play goal in his last ten Premier League matches, with the rest of his output coming from penalty kicks. For managers inclined to seek differential captaincy options, this dry spell provides ammunition. However, Harry emphasizes that Haaland doesn't distinguish between competitions when it comes to his scoring probability—he's no more likely to score in the FA Cup than in the Premier League—which means his recent hat-trick does bode well for continued Premier League goals, particularly against a Burnley defense that has struggled all season.

The primary differential candidates Harry identifies include Cole Palmer, whose Chelsea side faces a Manchester United team beset by absences and suspensions. Both Lisandro Martínez and Harry Maguire are suspended, while Kobbie Mainoo's fitness remains in serious doubt after his impactful absence was felt throughout the defeat to Leeds on Monday night. For managers willing to fade Haaland, Palmer represents the most logical pivot given United's defensive issues and Chelsea's home fixture advantage.

João Pedro offers another intriguing differential, with his actual goal-scoring form potentially superior to Palmer's when excluding penalties from the calculation. Harry also mentions Evanilson as an interesting option for Bournemouth, though the conversation quickly shifts back to City assets like Cherki and Semenyo, whose appeal is complicated by the simple fact that backing City's attack while fading Haaland means betting against the most likely goal scorer on the most likely scoring team.

Danny Welbeck emerges as Harry's most considered differential captain, with the Brighton striker appearing likely to start both matches in the double gameweek. His form is "pretty good," the minutes projection looks favorable, and the underlying data supports his inclusion in captaincy discussions despite Brighton's lower ownership compared to Haaland. The honest assessment, though, is that for most managers, the choice remains "Haaland or Palmer if you're going against the big man"—a binary decision that captures the risk-reward calculation facing the community.

Pep Guardiola's Title Race Comments and Their FPL Implications

Beyond individual player analysis, Guardiola's press conference comments about the Manchester City versus Arsenal fixture carry significant strategic weight for managers making decisions about City assets beyond just this gameweek. When asked if the match felt like a final, Guardiola responded that if City lose, "the title race is over for them," but if they win or draw, "they still have an opportunity." This framing suggests maximum effort from City, which reduces rotation risk for their key players.

The context matters enormously: Arsenal currently sit six points ahead of City in the league table, though City have a game in hand against Crystal Palace that is still to be rescheduled. Palace advanced to the next round of European competition on Thursday night, potentially opening a window for that rescheduled fixture to be played this very afternoon, though Harry notes no announcement has been made yet. Even a draw at the Etihad would leave Arsenal in pole position, but Guardiola's comments indicate he doesn't view that scenario as equivalent to losing.

For FPL purposes, this means managers holding Haaland, City defenders, or O'Reilly can expect something close to full strength from Guardiola's side. Harry's assessment is that unless Arsenal pull off a surprising away win—the kind of result that "not that many of us are predicting"—there should be "much rotation" against Burnley. The flip side is that if City were to lose to Arsenal, which managers won't know before locking in their teams, some rotation might occur in the more winnable Burnley fixture as Guardiola manages his squad with one eye on the FA Cup, where City face Southampton this weekend.

This uncertainty creates a fascinating strategic tension. City assets carry the highest projections, but the unknown rotation risk—if the unlikely Arsenal win were to occur—could leave managers with blanking players in their second fixture. Harry himself is proceeding with confidence in his City holdings, including the bench boost chip active, but acknowledges this residual risk exists for those seeking maximum safety.

Key Takeaways for Gameweek 33 Decision-Making

The six-team double gameweek demands selective investment rather than scatter-shot coverage. While City, Chelsea, Bournemouth, Brighton, and Leeds all offer viable attacking options, the data supports concentrating investment in Manchester City assets where possible given their 4.4 projected goals—the highest of any team in the double. Attempting to cover all bases dilutes the advantage available from focusing on the highest-probability returns.

Nico O'Reilly's fitness news is a significant positive, but rotation risk remains a qualified concern. With Guardiola signaling this is essentially a "final" for City's season, O'Reilly should feature prominently in both fixtures assuming the fitness update holds. Managers using free hit chips to bring him in can proceed with confidence, though those in longer-term squads should monitor for any late announcements that might indicate a knock limiting him to one appearance.

Defenders from Leeds and Brighton warrant consideration beyond the popular Chelsea options. With Brighton's odds improving against Chelsea and Leeds' clean sheet heavily weighted toward their Wolves fixture, the defensive calculus has shifted. Managers previously targeting Chelsea defenders might find better expected value in Brighton assets or a targeted investment in Leeds' home fixture rather than spread exposure.

Haaland remains the data-backed captain choice, but the gap between him and differentials is narrower than ownership suggests. His recent Premier League form presents a legitimate concern, but the FA Cup hat-trick demonstrates his continued ability, and City's projected output remains unmatched. For managers in mini-leagues where differentials matter, Palmer or Welbeck offer defensible pivot points—particularly given Manchester United's defensive absences—but fading Haaland requires accepting meaningful blank risk if he delivers as expected.

The Manchester United defensive crisis creates secondary value opportunities beyond the obvious targets. With Martínez and Maguire suspended and Mainoo doubtful, Chelsea's attacking assets gain additional appeal beyond just their clean sheet potential. João Pedro and Cole Palmer both benefit from facing a depleted United backline, making them stronger differential plays than their ownership percentages would suggest.

Whether you're fully committed to the double gameweek strategy or still deciding which chips to deploy, FPL Harry's comprehensive breakdown offers the data-driven foundation necessary to make informed choices in one of the season's most consequential rounds.
