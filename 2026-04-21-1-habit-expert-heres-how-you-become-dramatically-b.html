# FPL Gameweek 33 Complete Guide: Double Gameweek Strategy, Injury Updates, and Captaincy Picks

Introduction

Fantasy Premier League managers face what is arguably the most consequential Gameweek of the entire season. Gameweek 33 presents a unique opportunity with six teams carrying double fixtures, making it an absolute pivotal moment for anyone playing a chip—whether that's the wildcard, free hit, bench boost, or triple captain. The decisions made this week could make or break your season, particularly in a season where the title race is going down to the wire.

FPL Harry, a prominent content creator in the FPL community, delivered a comprehensive breakdown covering everything from pressing Manchester City press conference updates to detailed statistical projections for all six double teams. The video focuses on helping managers navigate the complexities of this critical gameweek while managing chip strategies and making optimal captaincy choices.

The timing of this analysis couldn't be more crucial. With Manchester City holding their fate in their own hands regarding the Premier League title race—facing Arsenal in what Pep Guardiola has essentially called a "final"—the implications stretch far beyond typical FPL decisions. This result could cascade into rotation concerns, fixture adjustments, and ripple effects across every double team in Gameweek 33.

The Manchester City Injury Update: Critical News for FPL Managers

The most significant breaking news heading into Gameweek 33 comes directly from Manchester City's Friday press conference, which FPL Harry watched live on YouTube. The first question posed to the coaching staff concerned the fitness of two key players: Ruben Dias and Nico O'Reilly. The updates were mixed but largely positive for FPL purposes.

Ruben Dias will not be available for the weekend's fixtures. This is a notable absence for City defenders, though it may not dramatically impact FPL strategy if you don't already own him. However, the news regarding Nico O'Reilly was far more encouraging. O'Reilly is fine and available, which represents a significant boost for the thousands of FPL managers who have invested heavily in the young defender.

The situation does carry some nuance that managers should understand. While O'Reilly has been confirmed fit, there's a slight possibility he may not play both matches in the double. If he picked up a slight knock that could be aggravated, there exists a scenario where he features against Arsenal but is rested for the Burnley match. Despite these minor concerns, FPL Harry maintains his own position on O'Reilly and advises against selling him. The underlying talent and City defensive quality remain strong enough to justify holding the asset, particularly given the close title race potentially going to the wire.

If City win against Arsenal, the significance of the remaining fixtures increases dramatically, and O'Reilly's continued involvement seems almost certain. The defender appears set to return to action for Manchester City this weekend, providing a genuinely positive update for the community.

The Six Double Teams: Projected Goals Analysis

Gameweek 33 centers entirely around the six teams blessed with double fixtures, and FPL Harry has updated his projections on expected goals across both matches. The data reveals clear tiers of opportunity that managers should target strategically.

Manchester City lead the pack with projected goals of 4.4 across their two matches. While some of this expectation is weighted toward the home match against Burnley, there's also been a slight increase in projected scoring for their challenging fixture against Arsenal. The fact that City are almost predicted to score two goals against Arsenal—widely considered the best defensive unit in the league this season—demonstrates the magnitude of this attacking opportunity. City represent the team most expected to score the highest number of goals across the entire double, making them the primary target for FPL investment.

Chelsea remain fairly static with projections of 3.2 goals across their double. Nothing significant occurred over the past week to alter their predicted data meaningfully. Their fixtures split relatively evenly between a home match against Manchester United and an away trip to Brighton, neither presenting an exceptional opportunity but both offering reasonable attacking potential.

Bournemouth sit third in the projected goals table with 3.1, essentially level with their earlier-week projections. Their expected output splits slightly toward their home fixture against Leeds, which presents the better attacking opportunity. However, Leeds showed promise on Monday night, suggesting this match won't be as straightforward as some might assume.

The most interesting movement in the projections comes from Brighton, who have overtaken Leeds in expected goals. Brighton have experienced quite an increase from the beginning of the week to now, reflecting both their own strong form and the vulnerabilities of their opponents. They now project just over three goals across matches against Tottenham and Chelsea, representing a genuine differential opportunity for managers seeking alternatives to the heavily-owned premium options.

Leeds round out the contenders with a strong home fixture against Wolves offset by a more difficult away match at Bournemouth. While they remain a viable target, the slight projection decrease reflects some of the concerns around their away fixture reliability.

Burnley sit in sixth position, making them the least attractive option among the six double teams despite their double status.

The takeaway for FPL managers is clear: while you can cherry-pick attacking options from Chelsea, Bournemouth, Brighton, and Leeds, Manchester City truly stand alone as the team most expected to deliver maximum returns this Gameweek.

Clean Sheet Probability Breakdown

Beyond projected goals, understanding clean sheet percentages provides crucial context for defensive selections. The data for one plus clean sheet across the double reveals a clear hierarchy that should inform squad construction.

Manchester City lead with a 62% chance of keeping at least one clean sheet across their two fixtures. There's been a slight increase in their percentage chance of a clean sheet specifically against Arsenal from earlier in the week, which is encouraging given the apparent difficulty of that matchup. If City perform as expected, their defensive assets could deliver massive returns alongside their attacking options.

Leeds sit second with a 52% chance of keeping at least one clean sheet, though this figure is heavily weighted toward their home match against Wolves at 40%, compared to just 22% away at Bournemouth. This distribution creates a strategic dilemma for managers considering Leeds defense. If you don't believe they'll keep a clean sheet in that away fixture, it may not be worth investing heavily in Leeds defensive assets despite the double. Many managers are currently triple-upping on Leeds defense, essentially focusing on the first home game as their primary expectation.

Brighton have crept up to third position with a 41% chance of keeping a clean sheet across their double. This increase comes partially at the expense of Chelsea, where odds have started swinging in favor of Brighton scoring more goals in their fixture while Chelsea become less likely to keep a clean sheet. As a result, Brighton defenders have become more attractive relative to their price points.

Chelsea sit next with 39%, a figure that has decreased slightly due to concerns about their away match at Brighton. Bournemouth occupy lower positions because their away fixture at Newcastle carries only a 15% chance of a clean sheet, dragging down their overall double percentage significantly. However, their home match against Leeds offers much better clean sheet odds.

The speaker emphasizes that clean sheet probabilities shouldn't be considered in isolation. Defenders like those from Bournemouth carry potential attacking threat value, particularly if players like Haaland and Senesi hit "decon" (the defensive coefficient metric used in FPL projections) across both matches. While Bournemouth's clean sheet odds aren't exceptional, they still represent strong picks if the underlying attacking threat is substantial.

Captaincy Analysis: The Haaland Decision and Alternatives

Choosing your captain in Gameweek 33 represents perhaps the most consequential decision of the entire season. The fundamental question facing every FPL manager is whether to trust Erling Haaland or pivot to a differential option.

The case for Haaland begins with pure statistical projection. He has the highest chance of a goal or an assist across all available players and is predicted to be the highest-scoring player this Gameweek. Manchester City, as previously established, represent the highest-scoring team across the entire double with 4.4 projected goals. The triple captain option remains viable with Haaland given these fundamentals.

However, the recent underlying data tells a different story. Haaland has scored just one open play goal in the Premier League across his last 10 matches, with a couple of penalties mixed in. This form dip is genuinely concerning for managers who have watched their premium asset blank repeatedly. But context matters here: just approximately two weeks ago, Haaland scored a hat-trick in the FA Cup against Liverpool. The player hasn't forgotten how to score goals.

An important philosophical point emerges from the analysis: Haaland doesn't distinguish between competitions when it comes to his goal-scoring ability. He's not more likely to score in the FA Cup than he is in the Premier League. The hat-trick against Liverpool demonstrates the potential ceiling available this weekend, boding well for his overall attacking prospects.

The community polling data reveals just how heavily favored Haaland remains. In a YouTube poll conducted yesterday, Haaland registered at 80% captaincy selection, an extraordinarily high figure. His ownership will be "crazy high" this Gameweek, with massive triple captain deployment across the community.

This creates the fundamental risk: if you go against Haaland, you're essentially hoping he blanks. If he doesn't, your Gameweek could be effectively over before it properly begins given the massive gap in expected returns.

For managers seeking alternatives, FPL Harry identifies two viable options for captaincy consideration. Cole Palmer represents the primary differential, particularly appealing given that Manchester United sound like they'll be without Martinez and Maguire (both suspended) and potentially Kobbie Mainoo in midfield. The absence of Mainoo massively impacted United in their Monday night match against Leeds, creating exploitable vulnerabilities that Palmer could capitalize on.

Danny Welbeck emerges as a genuinely interesting differential who the speaker admits he's "getting closer to thinking will start both games in the double." His recent form is pretty good, the minutes profile looks right, and the underlying data supports strong expected returns. Welbeck is one to seriously consider if you're looking to pivot away from the mass-owned options.

João Pedro could be an interesting differential, particularly given that his actual goal-scoring form excluding penalties is probably better than Palmer's. Semenyo at Bournemouth presents another differential option, though he carries the complication of being part of a Manchester City attack where you're backing the team to perform but betting against Haaland scoring the goals. Cherki represents another option, though the same fundamental issue applies—he'd need to score while Haaland blanks for the differential to pay off.

The stark reality, as FPL Harry states it, is that the only two players he would genuinely consider for captaincy this week are Haaland or Cole Palmer if going against the big Norwegian.

Press Conference Insights and Rotation Risk

The Manchester City press conference revealed crucial context beyond just player fitness. Pep Guardiola was asked directly whether the Arsenal match felt like a final, and his response carried significant implications for FPL planning.

Guardiola stated that if City lose this game, the title race will effectively be over for Arsenal. However, if City win or draw, they still maintain an opportunity. The title race remains genuinely alive regardless of this result. This framing matters because it suggests City will approach this match with full intensity rather than prioritizing any particular outcome.

The current situation sees Arsenal three points ahead of City, though City hold a game in hand against Crystal Palace that remains unscheduled. Palace progressed to the next round of European competition on Thursday night, creating a small chance their rescheduled match with City might occur this afternoon. However, managers should still wait for the official announcement regarding that double fixture.

The rotation question becomes critical if Arsenal win away at Manchester City. If that result occurs—though few managers are predicting it—there's a possibility we could see significant rotation from City in their away match at Burnley. This could impact Haaland's playing time in the second fixture of the double.

However, if City win or draw (which represents the most likely scenario based on community predictions), FPL Harry doesn't expect significant rotation against Burnley. The title race remains alive enough to warrant full strength selection for that match.

There's also an interesting complication with the FA Cup at the weekend. City face Southampton, who despite being a Championship team, are in incredible form. Anyone following the Championship will note Southampton look phenomenal at the moment. This isn't necessarily the straightforward fixture it might appear on paper if you're just comparing Premier League versus Championship status. City might need to allocate some resources to that competition, potentially affecting their Premier League squad preparation.

The key takeaway for FPL managers: rotation risk appears minimal if City avoid defeat against Arsenal. However, this remains a factor that won't be clarified until after the deadline, creating inherent risk in heavy City investments for the Burnley fixture specifically.
