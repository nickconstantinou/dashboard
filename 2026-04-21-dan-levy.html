# FPL Gameweek 33: The Ultimate Double Gameweek Strategy Guide

Introduction: The Most Critical Week of the FPL Season

For Fantasy Premier League managers, Gameweek 33 represents the pivotal moment that could define your entire season. Whether you're holding a wildcard, preparing a free hit, activating your bench boost, or considering a triple captain, this is the week where chip strategies converge and decisions carry maximum weight. FPL Harry, a leading voice in the FPL community, breaks down everything you need to know to navigate this complex gameweek, from team projections and injury updates to captaincy conundrums and press conference revelations.

The timing of this analysis couldn't be more crucial. Just hours before recording, key information emerged from Manchester City's press conference that directly impacts thousands of FPL teams. The availability status of Nico O'Reilly—Manchester City's emerging defensive asset—has been one of the most discussed topics among managers all week, particularly after he went off injured against Chelsea last weekend. Meanwhile, the title race between Manchester City and Arsenal has reached a fever pitch, with City's crucial home fixture against Arsenal this weekend potentially determining the championship's fate.

The Double Gameweek Landscape: Six Teams, Maximum Opportunity

Team Projections and Goal Expectations

The core appeal of Gameweek 33 lies in the six teams receiving a double gameweek: Manchester City, Chelsea, Bournemouth, Brighton, Leeds, and Burnley. As the week has progressed, FPL Harry explains that projections have been updated based on emerging data, particularly regarding expected goals across the two matches each team will play.

Manchester City sit atop the projected goals table with 4.4 expected goals across their double, which includes the high-profile fixture against Arsenal. This projection might seem surprisingly high given Arsenal's defensive reputation this season, but FPL Harry notes that the numbers reflect both the Burnley fixture—which City should dominate—and a slight upward adjustment for the Arsenal match based on how the week has unfolded. The data suggests City are essentially predicted to score two goals against Arsenal alone, a testament to their relentless attacking threat even against top-tier opposition.

Chelsea remain relatively stable at 3.2 projected goals, with games against Manchester United (home) and Brighton (away) presenting fairly balanced opportunities. Bournemouth occupy third position with 3.1 projected goals, slightly weighted toward their home fixture against Leeds, though FPL Harry cautions that Leeds showed promising form on Monday night. Brighton have emerged as an interesting case study, overtaking Leeds in recent days due to a significant increase in their projections. The data now suggests Brighton are expected to score just over three goals across their double against Spurs and Chelsea—a notable uptick that reflects both their recent form and the vulnerability of their opponents. Leeds round out the top five, maintaining a strong home fixture against Wolves but facing a trickier away trip to Bournemouth.

Burnley sit in sixth position, and FPL Harry notes they're primarily valuable through the Manchester City lens rather than as a standalone attacking option.

Clean Sheet Probabilities: The Defensive Outlook

When examining the likelihood of keeping at least one clean sheet across the double, the data tells a more nuanced story. Manchester City lead with a 62% chance of securing one or more clean sheets, with a slight increase in their clean sheet probability against Arsenal as the week progressed. This improvement likely reflects subtle shifts in the odds and perceived match dynamics, though FPL Harry acknowledges that City's defensive output can fluctuate.

Leeds occupy second position at 52%, but this figure is heavily skewed toward their home fixture against Wolves, where they're estimated at around 40% for a clean sheet, compared to just 22% for their away match at Bournemouth. This concentration matters for managers considering defensive triples, as FPL Harry warns that if you don't believe Leeds can keep a clean sheet away at Bournemouth, their overall appeal diminishes. The strategic question becomes whether to fully commit to Leeds defense or hedge with alternatives.

Brighton have climbed to third in the clean sheet rankings at 41%, a rise that coincides with Chelsea's slightly declining odds. FPL Harry highlights that Brighton have become more favored in their fixtures, with the data suggesting Chelsea are now less likely to keep a clean sheet in their away match against Brighton. Chelsea themselves sit at 39%, and Bournemouth occupy lower positions due to their 15% clean sheet probability against Newcastle. However, FPL Harry emphasizes that defensive picks shouldn't be evaluated purely on clean sheet odds—players like Bournemouth's Senesi, who offers significant attacking threat and differential (defcon) value, remain compelling options even when clean sheet percentages appear unfavorable.

Injury Updates and Press Conference Developments

Manchester City: O'Reilly Returns, Dias Remains Absent

The most significant news to emerge from Manchester City's Friday press conference concerned two key defenders. Manager Pep Guardiola confirmed that Ruben Dias will not be available for the weekend fixtures, maintaining his absence from the squad. However, the more critical update for FPL managers involves Nico O'Reilly, who was injured during the Chelsea match. Guardiola stated that O'Reilly is "fine," representing a major positive for the thousands of managers who have selected him.

FPL Harry, who has O'Reilly in his own team, views this as encouraging news, though he qualifies the optimism with some caveats. While O'Reilly appears ready to return, there's a possibility he might not start both matches in the double gameweek. If he sustained a slight knock, Manchester City might prioritize playing him against Arsenal while resting him for the Burnley fixture. Nevertheless, given the intensity of the title race and the importance of the Arsenal match, FPL Harry maintains that O'Reilly remains a strong pick. He's not planning to sell him and believes the asset is still valuable even with potential rotation risks.

Guardiola's Title Race Perspective

Perhaps the most revealing moment from the press conference came when Guardiola was asked whether the Arsenal match feels like a final. His response carried significant weight: if Manchester City lose, the title race is effectively over for them. However, if they win or draw, they retain an opportunity to compete. This framing suggests City will approach the fixture with maximum intensity, which has implications for both player availability and the likelihood of rotation in subsequent matches.

Crucially, City still have a game in hand against Crystal Palace—a fixture yet to be rescheduled. Palace advanced to the next round of European competition on Thursday night, which FPL Harry notes creates a small possibility that the rescheduled City fixture might be announced soon. This creates an interesting planning consideration for managers with City assets, as their double could potentially expand further.

The scenario becomes more complex if Arsenal win at the Etihad. FPL Harry suggests that in such a case—though this remains an unlikely outcome in most predictions—City might begin rotating players for the subsequent match away at Burnley, particularly with an FA Cup fixture against Southampton approaching at the weekend. Southampton, FPL Harry reminds viewers, are in exceptional form in the Championship and represent a more challenging opponent than their league position might suggest. However, if City win or draw against Arsenal, FPL Harry doesn't anticipate significant rotation against Burnley.

Captaincy Strategy: Haaland Dominance and Differential Alternatives

The Case for Erling Haaland

Captaincy in Gameweek 33 presents a familiar dilemma: do you follow the crowd with Erling Haaland, or do you attempt to differentiate? FPL Harry acknowledges that Haaland's recent Premier League form appears concerning on the surface—he's scored just one open-play goal in his last ten matches, with the rest coming from penalties. However, he urges managers to maintain perspective. Just two weeks ago, Haaland scored a hat-trick in the FA Cup against Liverpool, demonstrating that his goal-scoring ability remains undiminished regardless of competition.

The data supports Haaland's selection. He's the highest-projected scoring player across the double, has the greatest probability of a goal or assist, and Manchester City are predicted to score the most goals of any team in the gameweek. FPL Harry points to his YouTube poll from the previous day, where Haaland commanded approximately 80% of the captaincy votes—a remarkably high figure that reflects both his reliability and the difficulty of finding viable alternatives.

The risk of going against Haaland cannot be overstated. If you captain a differential and Haaland delivers a haul, your Gameweek could effectively be decided before you've truly begun. His ownership will be extraordinarily high, and many managers will triple-captain him, making the gap between following the crowd and contrarian picks enormous.

Differential Options Worth Considering

Despite Haaland's dominance, FPL Harry identifies specific scenarios where alternatives merit consideration. Cole Palmer stands out as the primary differential against Haaland, particularly if you're looking to gain ground in your mini-league through a bold captaincy choice. Manchester United's injury situation could work in Chelsea's favor—Martinez and Maguire are suspended, while Kobbie Mainoo appears unlikely to feature. Mainoo's absence proved costly in Manchester United's Monday night match against Leeds, suggesting Chelsea could exploit defensive vulnerabilities.

Danny Welbeck represents an intriguing option that FPL Harry admits he's personally getting closer to considering. Brighton face favorable conditions across their double, and Welbeck's actual goal-scoring form in recent weeks is arguably stronger than some of the top four players' form, who are collectively in what FPL Harry describes as "horrible form." If Welbeck starts both matches—which looks increasingly likely given his minutes and form—he could deliver significant returns at lower ownership.

João Pedro offers another differential angle, with his goal-scoring form potentially exceeding Palmer's once penalties are excluded from the equation. Similarly, Semenyo presents an interesting Bournemouth option, though he's more of a Manchester City coverage play than a true captaincy candidate.

The fundamental challenge with alternatives to Haaland, particularly at Manchester City, is that you're betting on City succeeding while hoping the goals don't come from Haaland himself. Given his 80% captaincy rate and likely triple-captain deployment from many managers, the math of going against him becomes extraordinarily difficult to justify unless you possess information suggesting he won't start both matches.

Strategic Implications for Different Chip Strategies

Wildcard Holders

For managers who deployed their wildcard in Gameweek 32, Gameweek 33 represents the first opportunity to leverage those new acquisitions. FPL Harry suggests focusing heavily on Manchester City assets, particularly if you're looking to maximize both fixtures. The combination of O'Reilly's probable availability, City's high projected goals, and their strong clean sheet probability creates a compelling case for significant City coverage.

Free Hit Deployment

Free hit managers face a more complex decision, as they must determine whether to spread investments across multiple double-gameweek teams or concentrate resources on the highest-probability options. FPL Harry's analysis suggests that City's offensive potential makes them the primary target, though the free hit's one-week nature means managers must be more selective about which specific players warrant investment.

Bench Boost Considerations

FPL Harry reveals that he has the bench boost active, creating additional motivation to optimize every position. For managers in similar positions, the key is ensuring that your bench comprises players from the double-gameweek teams who can contribute meaningfully if called upon. This means prioritizing Leeds, Brighton, or Chelsea defenders over single-gameweek options, even if those alternatives have superior immediate fixtures.

Triple Captain calculus

The triple captain decision remains heavily tilted toward Haaland, but FPL Harry acknowledges that managers using their triple captain on Haaland while others captain differentials create a significant points gap if Haaland performs. The mathematical reality is that triple-captaining a player who 80% of managers are also captaining limits your differentiation potential, though the safety of following form remains tempting.

Key Takeaways for Gameweek 33 Success

First, O'Reilly's availability transforms Manchester City's defensive appeal. The positive update from Guardiola's press conference means one of the season's most valuable defensive assets is likely to start at least one of City's double fixtures. Managers should hold firm rather than panic-selling, even with rotation risks.

Second, clean sheet percentages shouldn't override attacking potential for differential defenders. While Leeds' clean sheet odds appear favorable, the skewed nature of their double—concentrated in the home fixture against Wolves—means full commitment requires conviction that they can keep sheets away. Bournemouth's Senesi, despite lower overall clean sheet probability, remains valuable through his defcon potential.

Third, Haaland remains the default captaincy choice for most managers. His recent league form may look concerning, but his FA Cup hat-trick proves his scoring capability translates across competitions. The risk of going against him is substantial unless you have information suggesting he won't start both matches.

Fourth, consider Welbeck as a genuine captaincy alternative. Brighton's rising projections and Welbeck's improved form make him a credible option for managers seeking differentiation. His minutes and starting likelihood have increased, making him more than just a differential for the sake of it.

Fifth, monitor the Arsenal-City result for rotation implications. If City lose, the likelihood of rotation against Burnley increases substantially. Managers won't know this before deadline, but understanding the scenario helps manage expectations for City assets beyond Gameweek 33.
