# Will There Be a FIFA World Cup 2026 Fantasy Game?

## The Short Answer

Yes, there will almost certainly be an official FIFA World Cup 2026 Fantasy game. FIFA has a established pattern of launching Fantasy games for major tournaments, with official games available for the last four World Cups in 2010, 2014, 2018, and 2022.

## Historical Pattern of FIFA Fantasy Games

The evidence strongly suggests a 2026 edition is coming. FIFA has consistently launched official Fantasy games for World Cups, with releases confirmed for 2022, 2018, 2014, and 2010. This established track record makes it highly likely the governing body will continue the tradition for the 2026 tournament.

## When Might It Launch?

The launch timing for past tournaments provides a useful guide. In Qatar 2022, the Fantasy game went live nine days before the action began. At Russia 2018, that gap was even smaller—just seven days before the tournament started.

However, there are signs the 2026 edition may launch earlier. Work was spotted on the "Fantasy" section of the FIFA Play website in late April, suggesting development is underway. Additionally, the 2025 Club World Cup Fantasy game launched a notably longer 23 days before that tournament began, indicating FIFA may be expanding its pre-tournament window for Fantasy engagement.

## What Features Might Appear?

Early indicators suggest familiar chip mechanics could return. The 2026 game may include boosters known as "12th Man" and "Maximum Captain," building on features seen in recent editions.

## The 2026 World Cup Context

The tournament kicks off on Thursday 11 June, with Mexico and South Africa meeting at Estadio Azteca at 8pm BST in what appears to be the opening match. The 48-team group stage continues until 27 June, giving Fantasy players roughly a month of action to manage their squads.

## Summary

Based on FIFA's historical commitment to Fantasy games and recent development activity, players can expect an official World Cup 2026 Fantasy game. While exact launch dates remain unconfirmed, past patterns suggest a window of roughly one to three weeks before the tournament begins—though the more recent Club World Cup precedent hints at a potentially longer lead time for 2026.
