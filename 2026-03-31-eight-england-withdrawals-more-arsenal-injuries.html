Eight England Withdrawals and Rising Concerns: Arsenal's International Break Crisis Deepens

The Full Story

The March 2026 international break has descended into a fitness nightmare for Premier League clubs, with Arsenal bearing the brunt of a concerning wave of player withdrawals. A total of eight players have now exited England camp, according to a roundup published by Fantasy Football Scout on March 29, with the Gunners contributing five of those departures alone.

The situation began with planned returns for three non-injured players—Aaron Ramsdale and Dominic Calvert-Lewin, along with AC Milan's Fikayo Tomori—who simply completed their scheduled involvement before returning to their clubs. However, five others departed with genuine fitness concerns. The most alarming case is Noni Madueke, who was spotted at Wembley wearing a knee brace after being forced off after just 38 minutes of England's draw with Uruguay. Adam Wharton similarly picked up an injury in that same match and has returned to his parent club, while John Stones was forced to withdraw from the starting lineup with a calf issue.

The Arsenal contingent expanded the casualty list significantly when Declan Rice and Bukayo Saka—two of eleven players permitted to join up late on Friday as manager Thomas Tuchel managed their workloads—turned around and walked straight back out of camp, returning to north London for medical assessment. This leaves England potentially without two key attacking players for their friendly against Japan in midweek. Other late arrivals who weren't involved against Uruguay but may feature against Japan include Dean Henderson, Dan Burn, Marc Guéhi, Ezri Konsa, Nico O'Reilly, Elliot Anderson, Morgan Rogers, Anthony Gordon, and Bayern Munich's Harry Kane.

The injury crisis extends far beyond England's borders for Arsenal. Piero Hincapie and Martin Zubimendi have joined the growing list of Gunners returnees, taking the total to eleven Arsenal players who have exited international squads. Zubimendi withdrew from Spain's camp due to discomfort in his right knee, while Hincapie appeared to sustain a muscle injury during Ecuador's draw with Morocco. Meanwhile, Everton's Iliman Ndiaye has also returned early from Senegal duty, with the attacking midfielder experiencing discomfort in his left foot following an injury sustained at club level.

Why This Matters

This represents a significant crisis for Arsenal as they approach the crucial final stages of the Premier League season. With eleven players having returned from international duty early across multiple nations, Mikel Arteta faces a mounting injury list that could severely impact his squad's depth and preparation. The timing is particularly concerning given the condensed fixture schedule that typically follows international breaks, meaning clubs often have to field affected players before they've had adequate recovery time.

For fantasy football managers, the implications are immediate and substantial. Key assets like Saka, Rice, and Madueke—who are staples in countless squads—now face uncertainty over their availability and, even if fit to play, their match fitness. The knee injury to Madueke is especially worrying given the notorious difficulty of returning from such issues mid-season.

Beyond individual fantasy concerns, the broader pattern reflects growing tension between clubs and international teams over player welfare. The increasing frequency and volume of these mid-break withdrawals suggests the current international calendar may be pushing players beyond sustainable limits, raising questions about whether the sport's governing bodies will need to revisit the structure of international windows.

    • Context & Background

    This isn't the first time Arsenal has faced an international break injury crisis. The club has repeatedly found itself at the centre of fitness concerns during recent windows, partly due to the high number of players their key nations call upon and partly due to the physical demands of competing across multiple competitions. The decision to allow Rice and Saka to join England late was clearly an attempt to manage their workloads, yet both still required early returns—suggesting the problem may run deeper than mere fatigue.

    The pattern of late arrivals being sent straight back has become increasingly common across Europe's top clubs, highlighting the fraught relationship between domestic and international football when it comes to player management. Clubs invest significantly in their squads and naturally become frustrated when those investments are depleted during periods when they have no control over workload.

    What's Next

    England are scheduled to face Japan in a midweek friendly, where fans may see some of the late-arriving players who sat out the Uruguay draw, including Harry Kane. Arteta will be anxiously awaiting medical reports on his returning Gunners, with further updates expected before Arsenal's next Premier League fixture. Fantasy managers should monitor the situation closely and prepare contingency plans, particularly for Madueke, whose knee injury appears most serious. The next 48 hours will be critical in determining the full extent of the damage to Arsenal's squad and the availability of several premium fantasy assets.
