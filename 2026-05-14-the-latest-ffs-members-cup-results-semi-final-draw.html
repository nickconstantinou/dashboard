FFS Members Cup Quarter-Final Results and Semi-Final Preview

Quarter-Final Action

Gameweek 37 marks a significant milestone in the FFS Members Cup. Only four Fantasy Premier League managers remain in the competition as the tournament reaches the semi-final stage.

The quarter-finals delivered dramatic finishes and strategic chip plays. Two managers made bold moves by activating their Triple Captain chip during Double Gameweek 36. Both Manshizzle and his opponent Off the Clough deployed this high-risk strategy, making for an intense matchup.

The match between Manshizzle and Off the Clough came down to the wire. Off the Clough managed an impressive 89 points in Double Gameweek 36, yet still fell short. The decisive factors were a four-point hit and Dominic Calvert-Lewin's goal, which proved the difference in the outcome.

Meanwhile, other quarter-finalists posted strong scores. J Aravind accumulated 81 points, while UpminsterTrainman finished with 84 points during the double gameweek. Neither total was enough to advance past the matches featuring Triple Captain activations.

Team Dependant employed a different chip strategy, using Bench Boost to secure a comfortable 94-68 victory. This demonstrated how varied approaches can yield success in the knockout format.

Captaincy Decisions

Among the remaining managers, some made unconventional captaincy choices. Hawk Eye notably opted to captain Nico O'Reilly (£5.3m) rather than the more expensive Erling Haaland (£14.7m). This decision highlights the diverse strategies managers employ as they progress through the competition.

Looking Ahead

With four managers remaining, the semi-finals promise continued tactical intrigue. The results showcase how both chip timing and individual player selections can dramatically influence outcomes in the FFS Members Cup.
