Zophar's Gameweek 31 Team Selection | FPL Strategy Breakdown for the Run-In

Introduction: The Calm Before the Break

The fantasy football landscape is shifting once again, and Zophar—renowned FPL analyst and podcaster—has returned with his latest team selection video for Gameweek 31, recorded on a Friday morning just before the deadline. This particular gameweek carries special significance: it marks the final opportunity for managers to make moves before FPL enters a three-week break, with the next deadline falling on April 10th. For players watching this video, the timing couldn't be more critical. Every decision made now will echo through the remaining seven weeks of the season, and with European competitions reaching their business end, fixture congestion and squad rotation are about to become major factors in team-building strategy.

Zophar opens by reflecting on his Gameweek 30 performance with genuine satisfaction. After a disappointing run of form, he managed to score 68 points—a score he describes as solid rather than spectacular, but one that proved decisive given the week's overall low-scoring nature. "It's weird because maybe it was a weird week to have a good week," he admits, noting that you simply have to take your points whenever you can get them in FPL. The result pushed his overall rank from approximately 500,000 up to 343,000, representing his best gameweek ranking of the entire season. Crucially, he achieved this without making a single transfer, instead relying on tactical captaincy switches (opting for Bruno Fernandes over his usual choice) and strategic auto-sub usage. Morgan Rogers gave way to Destiny Omo-Troop as the XI changed, but it was the substitution of Luis Anderson for Muñoz that Zophar identifies as his primary catalyst for that green arrow, with his rank improving from 400K to 343K purely through those automatic replacements.

The European Landscape and Its FPL Implications

With all European fixtures now complete heading into this crucial stretch, Zophar dedicates significant attention to analyzing how continental results will impact domestic team selection going forward. The Champions League picture has narrowed considerably: Liverpool and Arsenal emerged as the only English clubs to progress past the quarter-finals, while Manchester City, Chelsea, Newcastle, and Tottenham all suffered elimination. For FPL purposes, this means different things for different squads. Tottenham, despite their exit, managed to win their second leg and showed encouraging signs with a more attacking system that Zophar believes will persist. The crowd notably rallied behind Tuta, and while Son Heung-min was left out due to injury and Richarlison served a suspension, Mathan Tel and Moises Moises both delivered respectable performances. Zophar doesn't anticipate immediate changes to the starting XI, however, as he expects Richarlison to return immediately.

The Europa League results carry equally important implications. Aston Villa progressed comfortably with a 2-0 home victory over Lille, while Nottingham Forest took a more pragmatic approach despite trailing 1-0 from the first leg. Forest fielded what Zophar describes as a "very, very heavily rotated team," with key players like Neco Williams, Murillo, Morgan Gibbs-White, Anderson, and Sarr all entering as substitutes in the second half—some as late as the 68th or even 90th minute. Even Nikola Milenkovic saw only 60 minutes of action. This careful resource management suggests Forest should arrive fresh for their crucial Sunday fixture against Tottenham, with fatigue unlikely to be a significant factor.

Most critically for fixture planners, Crystal Palace survived their European test narrowly, which Zophar emphasizes as the key development that has "cleared up the landscape at least a little bit in the short term for us FPL managers."

Fixture Permutations: Decoding the Double Gameweek Puzzle

The Crystal Palace result carries profound implications for the upcoming fixture schedule, and Zophar walks viewers through the logic carefully. The primary concern was whether Palace's elimination would have allowed their scheduled fixture to be moved into Gameweek 32, potentially disrupting double gameweek planning. However, with Palace progressing, that scenario is off the table. The most likely outcome—confirmed by FPL analyst sources including the JC FPL Spaceman—sees doubles in Gameweek 33 for Brighton, Chelsea, Arsenal, Newcastle, Burnley, and Manchester City. These same teams would then blank in Gameweek 34, while the Palace versus Manchester City fixture drops into Gameweek 36.

This represents a significant strategic constraint. Teams like Manchester City, Chelsea, and Newcastle face a peculiar situation where their midweek free slot means their GW33 fixtures could theoretically shift to GW32 instead, potentially eliminating both the double and the blank entirely. However, Zophar notes that the Premier League typically announces the full fixture schedule after the FA Cup round (approximately April 7th) and plans based on current European expectations. So unless Palace suffers elimination in their next European tie, the Palace-City double remains locked into GW36.

Arsenal presents a specific case: their GW34 fixture cannot remain in that slot because of their European commitments, so it automatically transfers to GW33, consolidating their double there. West Ham occupies a more ambiguous position, marked with a star in Zophar's analysis. The Hammers almost certainly retain their GW34 fixture (they have a Monday night game in GW33 that Sky are unlikely to rearrange) but there's still a possibility their double falls in GW33 with a blank in GW34. The star notation indicates this is a "maybe blank" that requires monitoring.

Strategic Target Teams for the Run-In

Given this fixture landscape, Zophar outlines his core strategic approach: deploying a Free Hit in Gameweek 33 for the double, then using his Wildcard in Gameweek 35. This positioning allows him to maintain a competitive team in the surrounding blank weeks (32 and 34) without overcommitting resources prematurely. The critical question then becomes which teams and players merit investment.

Favorably positioned squads include Tottenham (with the standout Forest fixture on Sunday), Forest themselves, Sunderland, and Wolves. Zophar is explicit that Wolves players are on his radar for specific reasons—their schedule presents genuine opportunities, unlike other teams he explicitly dismisses. On the other hand, he's "not really looking at" Fulham, Burnley, Liverpool, Aston Villa, and the lower-table teams like West Ham (with caveats), Everton, and Brighton, whose players don't fit his strategic framework.

Liverpool's schedule is particularly complex and merits individual attention. Beyond their domestic commitments, the Reds face a grueling stretch following the international break: Manchester City, Paris Saint-Germain, Fulham, and PSG again in roughly 10 days, following a UEFA Champions League draw that Zophar describes as "not been kind." They play Galatasaray on Wednesday, then Fulham on Saturday, then another Galatasaray match on Tuesday. This congestion makes rotation against Fulham "just inevitable," making Salah and company less attractive despite their quality.

Manchester United emerges as Zophar's primary focus, with favorable fixtures in Gameweeks 32 and 34 (Sunderland away, Newcastle home, Tottenham home, Bournemouth away, Forest home) and "two home games worth keeping their players." He's also interested in West Ham but prefers to wait until GW32 for clarity on their fixture situation, and notes that the current entry point isn't ideal anyway.

Current Transfer Strategy and Risk Management

Zophar has already executed his first move: transferring out Declan Rice (now injured and heading to Wilson) and banking two free transfers with approximately 2.3 million in the bank. However, he's deliberately not using all his remaining transfers at once, and there's a specific reason rooted in risk management.

With the international break approaching and playoff matches scheduled for various countries, "a lot of strong players, strong teams will be fielded, not much rotation over there." The concern is straightforward: significant injuries could emerge during these high-stakes internationals, and managers who spend all their transfers now might find themselves stranded with limited options to react. The same logic applies to Champions League and Europa League fixtures still to be played. By restricting himself to a maximum of one free transfer use before the break, Zophar preserves flexibility to respond to whatever developments occur across the football world over the next few weeks.

Key Takeaways for FPL Managers

First, European progression dramatically reshapes fixture planning, and Crystal Palace's advancement is the most consequential development for Double Gameweek 33. With Palace surviving, their double with Manchester City locks into GW36 rather than creating chaos in GW32 or GW34. This means managers can now plan with greater confidence around the most likely double scenario: Brighton, Chelsea, Arsenal, Newcastle, Burnley, and Man City in GW33.

Second, Nottingham Forest's squad rotation during their Europa League exit means they should arrive fresh for their crucial Tottenham matchup. Key players like Gibbs-White, Anderson, and Sarr all received limited minutes and can be trusted to start. This makes Forest assets more reliable than they might have appeared given their European involvement.

Third, Liverpool's schedule makes them a fade despite their quality. The compressed post-break fixture list—Man City, PSG, Fulham, PSG in 10 days—virtually guarantees rotation against Fulham. Managers targeting Liverpool assets should factor this reduced ceiling into their valuation.

Fourth, preserving at least one free transfer through the international break is strategically prudent. The risk of injuries to key players during high-stakes international matches and remaining European fixtures is real. Maintaining a buffer allows managers to react to developments rather than being locked into pre-break decisions that may no longer be optimal.

Fifth, Manchester United represents the best value across the blank weeks, with favorable fixtures in both GW32 and GW34. Two home games against Sunderland and Newcastle make United assets particularly attractive for managers not deploying Free Hits or Wildcards in those gameweeks.
