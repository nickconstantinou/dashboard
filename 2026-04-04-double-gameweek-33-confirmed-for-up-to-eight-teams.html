Double Gameweek 33 Set to Impact Up to Eight Premier League Teams, Creating Major FPL Chip Opportunity

Fantasy Premier League Managers Face Pivotal Planning Window as FA Cup Results Will Determine Fixture Chaos

Fantasy Premier League managers received crucial scheduling clarity on April 2, 2026, when the Premier League confirmed that this weekend's FA Cup quarter-final results will directly determine which teams receive Double Gameweek 33 fixtures—and which will subsequently blank in Gameweek 34. The announcement creates a scenario where up to eight Premier League clubs could play twice in Gameweek 33, with a guaranteed minimum of four teams receiving the double fixture. This scheduling arrangement stems from the FA Cup semi-finalists having their league matches against non-qualifying opponents moved into Gameweek 33, while those same semi-finalists' original opponents will blank that week.

The mechanics are straightforward but have massive implications for FPL strategy: any team that advances to the FA Cup semi-finals this weekend will see their Gameweek 34 opponent (the club they were originally scheduled to face in the league) get a blank in Gameweek 34. However, that blanked opponent will instead play in Gameweek 33 against the same opponent who also has a double that week. This creates a cascading effect where winning FA Cup quarter-finals generates double gameweeks while the corresponding league opponents face blanks—but those blanks flip to doubles for the next round.

Specific Scenarios Across Six Potential FA Cup Outcomes

The confirmed scenarios break down into six distinct possibilities depending on which FA Cup quarter-final winners emerge:

If Arsenal beat Southampton: Arsenal gets a double against Manchester City and Newcastle, while Newcastle doubles against Bournemouth and Arsenal.

If Chelsea beat Port Vale: Chelsea doubles against Manchester United and Brighton, with Brighton doubling against Tottenham and Chelsea.

If Manchester City beat Liverpool: Manchester City doubles against Arsenal and Burnley, while Burnley doubles against Nottingham Forest and Manchester City.

If Liverpool beat Manchester City: Liverpool doubles against Everton and Crystal Palace, with Crystal Palace doubling against West Ham and Liverpool.

If West Ham beat Leeds: West Ham doubles against Crystal Palace and Everton, while Everton doubles against Liverpool and West Ham.

If Leeds beat West Ham: Leeds doubles against Wolves and Bournemouth, with Bournemouth doubling against Newcastle and Leeds.

The announcement arrives before the Gameweek 32 deadline on Friday, April 10, allowing managers to plan their chip strategies with full knowledge of which teams will double before committing to their Gameweek 32 squads.

Why This Matters: The Chip Strategy Calculus

For the millions of FPL managers worldwide, this announcement represents arguably the most significant scheduling news of the season. The timing provides an extraordinary planning opportunity because managers now know—with certainty contingent only on Saturday's results—which teams will double before finalizing their Gameweek 32 lineups. This knowledge allows precise chip deployment that previously required educated guessing.

The consensus strategy emerging among FPL analysts centers on a three-chip sequence: deploy the Wildcard in Gameweek 32 to construct a squad optimized for Double Gameweek 33, activate the Bench Boost in Gameweek 33 to maximize points from doubled players, and then use the Free Hit in Gameweek 34 to field a temporary squad capable of navigating the blank gameweek without damaging overall rank. For managers without a Bench Boost available, an alternative approach involves using the Wildcard in Gameweek 33 after confirming the doublers, combined with a Free Hit in Gameweek 34.

Manchester City vs Crystal Palace Rescheduling Creates Additional Future Opportunity

Beyond the immediate Gameweek 33/34 planning, the announcement served as a reminder that Manchester City's home fixture against Crystal Palace—which was postponed from Gameweek 31 due to the EFL Cup final—still requires rescheduling. According to the report, this fixture "should result in another Double Gameweek further down the line" for those two clubs. For managers who have preserved their Triple Captain chip, this creates an additional strategic consideration, as holding the chip for that future double fixture could prove highly valuable.

What to Watch: Saturday's FA Cup Results

The critical information will emerge from Saturday's FA Cup quarter-final matches. The games that matter are Arsenal vs Southampton, Chelsea vs Port Vale, Manchester City vs Liverpool, and West Ham vs Leeds. Based on current form and seeding, Arsenal and Chelsea progressing as expected would create "maximum chaos" with eight doublers, but any upset results would shift the equation. Regardless of outcomes, the announcement means FPL managers can now plan with confidence rather than speculation, transforming what could have been a chaotic period into a structured opportunity for point-scoring.
