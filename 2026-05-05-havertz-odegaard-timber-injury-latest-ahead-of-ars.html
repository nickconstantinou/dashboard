Arsenal Team News: Havertz and Odegaard Fit for Atletico Showdown

Key Players Return to Fitness

Arsenal have received a significant boost ahead of their Champions League clash with Atletico Madrid, with both Kai Havertz and Martin Odegaard confirmed fit and available for selection. Despite neither player being spotted in training earlier on Monday, manager Mikel Arteta has confirmed both are ready for the rematch.

"Kai Havertz and Martin Odegaard are fit for the Champions League clash with Atletico Madrid," the report states. "They are available. They are in the squad, both of them."

Havertz, priced at £7.3m, has been managing a muscle injury, while Odegaard's specific issue was described as unspecified. Arteta expressed his relief at having both players back, stating: "It's great, because we need options. We need the capacity to play different games tomorrow, whether it's from the start or after. So it's really, really good news for us to have them both back."

Saka Passes Fitness Test

Bukayo Saka appears to have come through his first start since March unscathed. The £9.8m-rated winger made his return to action in recent fixtures and showed no signs of discomfort.

Arteta spoke optimistically about Saka's condition: "Great, that's what we need when we arrive in this stage of the competition – the players not only to be available, but to be in top condition to perform and make the difference, and Bukayo will certainly give us that."

Timber and Merino Still Absent

The Arsenal squad faces continued absentees on the defensive front. Jurrien Timber, nursing an ankle injury, is set to miss the Atletico encounter. The Dutch defender was also absent from Monday's training session.

Long-term casualty Mikel Merino remains unavailable, with the Spanish midfielder still recovering from a foot injury sustained earlier in the season. Both players were missing from training earlier on Monday, ruling them out of contention for the European tie.
