While many managers are focused on the immediate Gameweek 31 blank, the more strategic insight is that Gameweek 32 is also unlikely to feature a Palace double. The real double appears to be Gameweek 33, with another potential in Gameweek 36. This reshapes the entire chip strategy calculus, particularly for managers trying to decide between carrying Palace assets or cutting them loose.

**3. Your Chip Strategy Dictates Your Transfer Targets, Not the Other Way Around**

Rather than asking "who should I buy this week?" managers should first determine their chip plan and then let that plan determine their targets. The Solio analysis demonstrates this principle clearly: Liverpool and Manchester United assets make sense for Free Hit 33 managers, while Chelsea and Newcastle assets are the picks for Free Hit 34 managers. Both teams have attractive fixtures, but the right choice depends entirely on whether you plan to Free Hit in Gameweek 34. If you're using your Free Hit elsewhere or not at all, Newcastle is the better choice because you can bring them in now and bench them if your plans change. If you're committed to Free Hitting in Gameweek 34, Fulham's fixture advantage that week makes them the superior pick.

**5. Three Strategies Are All Viable — But Execution Clarity Matters More Than Perfect Strategy**

PRA explicitly states that all three chip strategies he outlines are legitimate options. Pick your strategy, wait for complete information if possible, and commit fully.
