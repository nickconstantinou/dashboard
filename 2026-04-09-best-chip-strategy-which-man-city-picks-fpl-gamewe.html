Best chip strategy? Which Man City picks? FPL Gameweek 32 Q&A

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

The Full Story

Eight-time top 10k finisher Zophar hosted his weekly FPL Q&A on Fantasy Football Scout (April 8, 2026), providing detailed analysis on the optimal chip strategy for Fantasy Premier League managers heading into the season's critical final stretch. With only seven Gameweeks remaining and two Double Gameweeks on the horizon (Gameweek 33 and likely Gameweek 36), managers face crucial decisions about how to deploy their remaining Wildcard, Bench Boost, Free Hit, and Triple Captain chips.

Zophar strongly advised against playing a Gameweek 32 Wildcard, arguing it would result in a weaker squad for the current Gameweek while only maximizing the Gameweek 33 Bench Boost opportunity—which he noted would feature only 15 available picks from five non-Burnley teams. He specifically cited concerns about including Chelsea's defense and Bruno Fernandes (£10.3m), which would significantly weaken the Gameweek 32 team compared to existing squads. He also expressed skepticism about the Gameweek 35 Wildcard combined with Gameweek 36 Bench Boost strategy that heavily invests in Crystal Palace assets, noting that Palace remain bottom of the Fixture Ticker between Gameweeks 35-38, making their Double Gameweek 36 appearance largely unappealing despite the potential insertion of a Manchester City away trip.

Why This Matters

The final seven Gameweeks of the FPL season represent the most consequential period for managers seeking to climb rankings or defend their positions. Chip strategy decisions can literally mean the difference between finishing in the top 10,000 or top 100,000—or winning mini-leagues among friends. With Double Gameweeks offering massive point-scoring potential, understanding when to deploy each chip has become increasingly critical as the season reaches its climax. The timing of these chips affects not just immediate Gameweek returns but also carries over implications across subsequent Double Gameweeks.

Key Details

Zophar specifically recommended a third strategy option for managers holding all four chips: using the Triple Captain in Gameweek 36. He noted this works particularly well for managers with strong Gameweek 32 lineups that contain weaker players in Gameweek 34 (such as Brentford and Crystal Palace assets). The analysis included specific player mentions: Gabriel Magalhaes (£7.2m), Bruno Fernandes (£10.3m), and Brighton duo Bart Verbruggen (£4.5m) and Jan Paul van Hecke (£4.5m) as potential Bench Boost options—though Zophar expressed concern about Brighton keeping clean sheets against Tottenham and Chelsea. He emphasized that waiting until Gameweek 35 to Wildcard provides valuable new information about which teams are "on the beach" versus chasing European qualification, though he acknowledged that at least three of Leeds, Chelsea, and Manchester City may still have something to play for.

Context & Background

This Q&A comes at a pivotal point in the 2025-26 Fantasy Premier League season. The fixture schedule has created two primary Double Gameweek opportunities in Gameweeks 33 and 36, making chip deployment timing critical. The Burnley team appears to have secured their Double Gameweek status, while the other five teams eligible for Gameweek 33 doubles create limited options for Bench Boost strategies. This is Zophar's regular weekly Q&A format, providing subscribers with strategic guidance based on his consistent top-10k finishes across multiple FPL seasons.

What's Next

The piece references a recent episode of "The FPL Wire" podcast where pros and cons of several chip strategies were discussed in greater detail. Managers should continue monitoring the Fixture Ticker for updates, particularly regarding Crystal Palace's fixture list and any changes to the Double Gameweek 36 schedule. The coming weeks will reveal which teams have secured their league positions and potentially "already beach" heading into the final matches—information that will be critical for managers planning their Gameweek 35 or 36 Wildcard deployments.
