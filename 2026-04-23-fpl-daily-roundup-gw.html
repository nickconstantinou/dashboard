FPL Gameweek Round-up: April 23, 2026 – Free Hit Dilemmas and managerial upheaval

The managerial earthquake at Chelsea

The biggest story ahead of this week's FPL action comes from Stamford Bridge, where Chelsea have dispensed with Liam Rosenior following a devastating run of five successive Premier League defeats without scoring. Assistant coach Calum McFarlane has been installed as caretaker manager until the end of the season, marking his second stint in temporary charge this campaign. His first intervention came during Gameweeks 20 and 21, when he oversaw a 2-1 defeat at Fulham and a creditable 1-1 draw at the Etihad Stadium against Manchester City.

For FPL purposes, the timing of this change creates a peculiar situation. Chelsea blank in Gameweek 34 alongside Bournemouth, Brighton, Leeds, and Manchester City, meaning the Blues will not feature in Fantasy terms for at least another week. More significantly, Nottingham Forest—Chelsea's opponents in their next Premier League fixture—will be competing in a UEFA Europa League semi-final against Aston Villa at the same time. Forest's divided attention across competitions could prove a gift for a Chelsea side under new management, though the Free Hit chip has rendered this discussion somewhat moot for managers pivoting away from Blues assets this week.

Chelsea's injury situation compounds the uncertainty. Cole Palmer remains absent with a hamstring problem sustained during their recent match against the same opposition, while Joao Pedro also misses out despite reports suggesting he should be fit for Sunday—this game has simply arrived too quickly for him to be involved. With both attacking talismans unavailable, Enzo Fernandez has been pushed into a more advanced number ten role under McFarlane and delivered an equaliser against City last time out. The Argentine is among five players who have started both matches under the interim boss, alongside Fernandez, Pedro Neto, Trevoh Chalobah, and Malo Gusto.

Branthwaite's season ends: Keane emerges as budget option

Everton's defensive plans have been reshaped by confirmation that Jarrad Branthwaite will miss the remainder of the campaign with a hamstring injury sustained in the Merseyside derby against Liverpool. The scan revealed no surgical requirement, but the 22-year-old centre-back will undergo rehabilitation at Finch Farm and is unlikely to feature again this season. This development opens the door for Michael Keane, who becomes a more viable option at just £4.5m for managers seeking budget centre-back coverage.

Keane has started only five of Everton's last twelve league matches, though his output when selected proves more interesting than his limited minutes suggest. The 32-year-old has scored three goals this season—more than Branthwaite or James Tarkowski—and averages a shot every 102.9 minutes, a quicker rate than either of his positional competitors. His DefCon rating of 54.2% in matches he starts compares favourably to Tarkowski's 59.4%, and he averages more points per start than both rivals. With Everton ranked third on the Season Ticker for their remaining fixtures—barring a Gameweek 35 clash with Manchester City—Keane offers renewed opportunity at a knockdown price.

The Blank Gameweek 34 landscape

With six Premier League teams not playing in Gameweek 34 due to the FA Cup semi-finals, managers face a critical decision on chip deployment. Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds, and Manchester City will all blank, significantly constraining the player pool for those without significant exposure to those squads. The confirmed blank makes this the highest-priority week for Free Hit usage according to fixture density data, and Fantasy Football Scout's analysis identifies several routes to success.

Robin Roefs stands out as exceptional value among goalkeepers at £4.8m. The Sunderland man is one of only four keepers to surpass 120 points this season, and his side secured their sixth home clean sheet of the campaign in Gameweek 32—the joint-third most of any team. At just £4.8m, he leaves maximum budget available for premium outfield assets while offering a reliable floor.

In attack, Ollie Watkins leads BGW34 transfer activity as the most transferred-in player ahead of the deadline. The £8.7m Aston Villa striker has scored three times in his last two trips to Craven Cottage, accumulating 21 points from those matches alone. Aston Villa's upcoming fixture run makes Watkins particularly attractive, and his ownership surge reflects growing confidence in his ability to deliver despite the elevated price.

Morgan Rogers offers strong value as a mid-priced forward option at £7.4m. The Aston Villa man has recorded a goal and two assists in his last three matches, demonstrating consistent involvement in his side's attacking play. At 24.1% ownership, he represents a popular but not ubiquitous choice—solid differentiation at the right price point.

Harry Wilson emerges as a budget-friendly forward at £6.0m, with ten attacking returns in fifteen home appearances at Craven Cottage. For managers crafting a balanced Free Hit squad, Wilson and Roefs together free significant funds for premium positions, allowing Salah and Fernandes in midfield with Watkins up front.

Captain recommendations: the premium debate

The Mohamed Salah versus Bruno Fernandes debate intensifies with both players in scintillating form. Salah has found the net in back-to-back Gameweeks and continues his consistent scoring output at £14.0m, while Fernandes combines penalty duties with creative excellence as the division's best creator by some distance. Both are nailed-on for ninety minutes and represent premium options that rarely disappoint.

However, Mohamed Gibbs-White deserves serious consideration as a differential captain choice at £7.5m. The Nottingham Forest playmaker is one of the division's form players with an exceptional 11.0 form rating, having racked up 91 points since the turn of the year—more than any other asset except Fernandes. At just 8.3% ownership, he offers significant rank-boosting potential if Forest's favourable run-in delivers returns.

Fantasy Football Scout's analysis suggests a balanced midfield approach prioritising Salah and Fernandes for reliability, with Gibbs-White as an excellent budget alternative that does not compromise the premium forward slot. The key variable is Forest's European distraction—managers must weigh their confidence in Gibbs-White's minutes against the distraction of a semi-final simultaneously occupying the club's attention.

Transfer strategy: who to target and why

Beyond the Free Hit recommendations, several transfer targets emerge from the creator analysis. Kiernan Dewsbury-Hall at £5.1m represents strong value from Everton's improving run, though his 5.4% ownership remains surprisingly low given the fixtures ahead. Matheus Nunes at Manchester City carries obvious appeal at £5.3m given City's double gameweek involvement, though Pep Guardiola's rotation tendencies complicate the picture.

Igor Thiago Nascimento Rodrigues at Brentford continues his remarkable season at £7.4m with 168 points—the second-highest seasonal total among defenders in the facts sheet. At 35.3% ownership, he is approaching template territory, which reduces his differentiation value but confirms his reliability. Brentford's push toward European qualification provides additional motivation for the Bees' attack, and Keith Andrews' side rank among the more attractive defensive units for the run-in.

Axel Disasi at £4.5m emerges as an intriguing budget option from West Ham, with the French centre-back returning to form following the Hammers' 4-0 victory over Wolves. His 7.5 form rating reflects recent productivity, and at just £4.5m, he offers a route into West Ham's improving defence without breaking the bank.

Players to avoid

Managers should exercise caution with several assets heading into this period. Jean-Philippe Mateta at £7.6m appears perpetually rotated for Crystal Palace's European commitments, and Oliver Glasner's consistent benching of the French forward against West Ham suggests the pattern will continue. Ismaila Sarr similarly finds himself among the substitutes despite Palace's attacking setup, making both forwards unreliable for managers seeking starting XI certainty.

Cole Palmer remains unavailable through injury, and with Chelsea blanking in Gameweek 34 anyway, there is no urgency to bring him in for the immediate future. Managers should monitor his recovery closely but avoid premature investment until his return is confirmed and Chelsea's fixtures align more favourably.

Furthermore, a podcast discussion referenced Ryan Sesko as a bench boost enabler during Double Gameweek 33, but this player does not appear in the current facts sheet. Managers should treat such mentions with caution until verified against official sources, as the player may have transferred, sustained an injury, or simply not registered as a viable FPL asset this season.

Chip strategy: timing the Free Hit

The consensus among creators points toward Free Hit deployment in Gameweek 34, where six teams blanking creates severe squad constraints. The chip allows managers to field a completely different team for one gameweek only, making it ideal for navigating blank fixtures while maximising returns from playing assets. Fantasy Football Scout's sample squad prioritises premium assets like Salah and Fernandes, with value picks like Rogers and Gibbs-White providing returns relative to their modest price points.

For managers holding the Wildcard, the timing remains contentious. Some creators suggest holding the Wildcard until closer to the season's end to react to final fixtures and any unexpected developments, while others recommend using it immediately to capitalise on current form trends. The 38-game season constraint means GW38 represents the final deadline—no further gameweeks exist beyond that point, so any chip deployment must respect that hard ceiling.

The Bench Boost, deployed by many managers in Gameweek 33, delivered mixed results. Brighton's clean sheet against Chelsea provided crucial points for those who held the chip, though several managers reported disappointment when players like Joao Pedro failed to appear in their starting lineups. The mathematical reality of bench boost strategy became apparent: when substitutes don't play, the effective score drops by the points the absent players would have contributed.

Where creators disagree

The Manchester United conversation on Planet FPL revealed tension between present success and future ambition at Old Trafford. Correspondent Alex Trembath acknowledged Michael Carrick's "absolutely commendable" job, noting United would sit top of the league since his appointment—genuine progress from last season's 15th-place nadir. However, the correspondent expressed reservations about scaling to Premier League title-winning capacity, questioning whether Carrick's pragmatic, resilient approach can evolve into something capable of dominating games.

This philosophical debate has FPL implications: United's approach tends to yield clean sheets and moderate returns rather than explosive hauls, making their assets more reliable than spectacular. For managers weighing Fernandes against more volatile alternatives, understanding United's tactical ceiling matters. Can they transition from winning through organisation to winning through dominance? If not, their key players may never reach the heights some managers anticipated.

Three key actions for the week ahead

First, deploy your Free Hit chip in Gameweek 34 if you have not already done so. The six-team blank creates squad constraints that no amount of sideways shuffling can solve, and the chip's one-week validity makes it the obvious solution. Build around Robin Roefs in goal, Salah and Fernandes in midfield, and Watkins up front, with Gibbs-White and Rogers providing differentiation at moderate prices.

Second, consider bringing in Michael Keane at £4.5m as Everton assets become more attractive following Branthwaite's season-ending injury. The fixtures support Everton's run-in, and Keane's attacking output provides a floor that many budget options cannot match. At £4.5m, he leaves maximum flexibility for premium investments elsewhere.

Third, monitor Chelsea's situation closely despite their blank in Gameweek 34. Calum McFarlane's appointment creates uncertainty, but Nottingham Forest's Europa League distraction in their next fixture presents an opportunity. Managers planning Wildcard deployments should factor Chelsea's revised fixture list into their thinking, as the Blues' upcoming schedule may offer value once the blank week passes.
