The Full Story

Fantasy Premier League managers have a powerful new factor to consider when selecting midfield assets: defensive contribution points, known as "DefCon." Fantasy Football Scout's analysis for March 26, 2026, identifies the best all-round midfielders who provide value across multiple statistical categories, giving FPL managers multiple routes to points beyond just goals and assists.

The DefCon system awards additional points when a player reaches 12 defensive contributions in a single match—a two-point reward that can significantly boost a midfielder's FPL returns. This creates incentive for managers to target midfielders who perform consistently across several areas rather than specialists in a single category.

The analysis examines midfielders meeting a minimum threshold of 1,700 minutes played throughout the 2025/26 campaign. For each player, the evaluation considers per-90-minute performance in eight key areas: shots, attempts inside the box, chances created (CC), big chances created (BCC), corners, open-play crosses, expected goal involvement (xGI), and DefCon action. This comprehensive approach aims to identify players who can deliver points through various means, reducing reliance on any single avenue like goalscoring.

The article also examines a shorter-term view—players who have performed well across multiple categories in their last six appearances, highlighted in green for easy identification. Two high-profile midfielders have been excluded from recent form analysis due to injuries: Justin Kluivert (£6.9m) and Bruno Guimaraes (£6.8m), who haven't featured since Gameweeks 20 and 26 respectively.

Why This Matters

The introduction of DefCon points represents a significant shift in FPL strategy, particularly for midfield assets. Previously, FPL managers often prioritized forwards for goalscoring potential while viewing midfielders primarily as assist providers or set-piece specialists. The DefCon system adds another dimension, making versatile midfielders with high work rates and defensive engagement even more valuable.

For managers competing in the final stretch of the 2025/26 season, identifying all-rounders provides a crucial competitive edge. Players who can accumulate points through multiple channels—goals, assists, creating chances, AND defensive contributions—offer more stable, consistent returns than those dependent on a single metric. A midfielder who doesn't score or assist in a match might still earn points through six DefCon actions, keeping a manager's points-per-game average competitive.

This analysis matters because the FPL landscape is increasingly sophisticated. With millions of players competing and information more accessible than ever, the difference between success and failure often comes down to identifying players other managers might overlook. All-round midfielders represent value in both the immediate term (they score points in multiple ways) and the medium term (they're less likely to go multiple gameweeks without returns compared to boom-or-bust options).

For FPL managers navigating the final fixtures, having assets who can deliver across categories also provides tactical flexibility. A manager holding a player who creates chances, takes set pieces, shoots regularly, AND contributes defensively has built-in protection against form fluctuations in any single area.

    Context and Background

    FPL's DefCon points system was introduced to reward outfield players for defensive work that typically went unnoticed in traditional fantasy formats. Actions tracked under DefCon include tackles, interceptions, blocks, clearances, and recoveries—stats that defensive midfielders and hard-working wide players often accumulate in abundance.

    Before DefCon, FPL managers typically viewed midfield assets through a narrow lens: premium options like Mohamed Salah or Bukayo Saka for their attacking output, or budget options like Morgan Gibbs-White (£6.5m) or Anthony Gordon (£7.2m) depending on team selection. The DefCon system has effectively expanded the midfielder pool, making players previously considered defensive specialists or workhorses suddenly viable FPL assets.

    This analysis arrives at a critical point in the FPL calendar. The 2025/26 season is approaching its conclusion, meaning:

  • Blank gameweeks (where some teams don't play) may be approaching
  • Double gameweeks could be imminent for teams with fixture backlog
  • Price changes have stabilized, making squad value more predictable
  • Differential picks—players owned by few managers—offer greater point-scoring leverage

    • The 1,700-minute threshold ensures only players with substantial pitch time are considered, filtering out those with impressive per-90 numbers but insufficient opportunity to contribute meaningfully over the season.
