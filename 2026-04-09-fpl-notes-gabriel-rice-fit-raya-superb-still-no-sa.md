# Arsenal's Narrow Champions League Victory Comes With Fresh Injury Concerns Ahead of Crucial FPL Deadline

## The Full Story

Arsenal secured a hard-fought 1-0 first-leg victory over Sporting CP in Lisbon on Tuesday night, ending a two-match losing streak but leaving Fantasy Premier League managers with more questions than answers ahead of Gameweek 32. The solitary goal came from Kai Havertz in the 91st minute, courtesy of a brilliant assist from substitute Gabriel Martinelli, who injected much-needed urgency into an otherwise toothless Arsenal attack.

The victory was achieved without two of Arsenal's most crucial players—Bukayo Saka and Jurrien Timber—neither of whom featured in the matchday squad. Saka, who withdrew from England duty during the international break for a "medical assessment," missed training on Monday and was not even on the bench in Portugal. Timber, meanwhile, has not played since suffering an ankle injury in Gameweek 30's win over Everton. Manager Mikel Arteta expressed cautious optimism, stating: "They're having trouble, they're not ready yet. Let's see, hopefully they're going to be ready for the weekend, if everything goes well, and that's a massive boost because in recent days we've lost so many important players and that's something that we need to change immediately."

Adding to Arsenal's mounting injury woes, captain Martin Odegaard required medical attention before being substituted in the 70th minute. Arteta is yet to comment on the severity of the Norway international's issue.

## Why This Matters

This match carries significant implications for FPL managers navigating a crucial period of the season. With Arsenal facing Bournemouth at home in Gameweek 32, the availability—or absence—of key players could dramatically alter strategies. Saka (£9.8m) and Timber (£6.3m) represent premium assets whose continued absence leaves substantial holes in many squads, while Odegaard's potential injury adds further uncertainty to an Arsenal midfield that has already been depleted.

The attacking struggles displayed in Lisbon represent a concerning trend for both real-world and FPL purposes. Arsenal created just 0.17 xG before Havertz's late winner, with the team's expected goals tally heavily inflated by that single opportunity. Viktor Gyokeres (£8.8m) remained anonymous despite his premium price tag, highlighting the systemic creative problems Arsenal currently face without their primary playmakers.

For FPL purposes, the return of Gabriel and Rice from their respective injury concerns provides some relief to managers who held faith through recent gameweeks, while David Raya's (£5.5m) exceptional performance between the posts reinforced his value as a budget goalkeeping option.

## Context & Background

Arsenal arrive at this critical stage of the season in the midst of an injury crisis that has severely depleted their attacking options. The Gunners suffered back-to-back defeats against West Ham in the Premier League and Southampton in the FA Cup prior to this Champions League fixture, with their attack particularly toothless in both matches.

The absence of Saka—valued at £9.8m in FPL—has proven particularly damaging, as the England international remains the focal point of Arsenal's creative output. His potential continued absence leaves a substantial hole in countless FPL squads, forcing managers to explore alternative premium options or pivot to budget enablers.

Sporting CP, meanwhile, looked the more threatening side in transition throughout the match, with their well-organized defensive structure neutralizing Arsenal's limited creative options. The Portuguese side managed more shots and marginally higher xG, suggesting Arsenal's victory was somewhat fortunate despite the scoreline.

## What's Next

The primary concern for FPL managers centers on the availability of Saka, Timber, and Odegaard for Arsenal's Gameweek 32 home fixture against Bournemouth. Arteta's suggestion that the pair "hopefully" return this weekend provides some hope, though their respective injury issues remain shrouded in uncertainty.

Should Saka and Timber fail to recover, managers holding these assets face a difficult decision: whether to maintain faith and accept a blank, or to pivot to alternatives before the deadline. Odegaard's situation adds another layer of complexity, with the Norway international's status unlikely to become clear until closer to the weekend.

Arsenal will also play the second leg at the Emirates Stadium, with the tie finely poised despite this narrow first-leg advantage. The Gunners will need to improve their creative output significantly if they are to progress to the Champions League semi-finals.
