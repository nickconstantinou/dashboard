Fantasy EFL Play-Offs Mini-League: How to Join and Win Prizes

What is the Fantasy EFL Play-Offs?

Fantasy Football Scout has announced an exciting opportunity for Fantasy EFL managers. The Fantasy EFL Play-Offs is a newly launched spin-off game from the regular Fantasy EFL game. This short-format game offers managers a chance to continue their fantasy football involvement even after the main season winds down.

Game Structure

The Fantasy EFL Play-Offs game runs for three Gameweeks, providing a compact but competitive experience. One key detail that levels the playing field is that all players start from zero points ahead of Gameweek 1 of the Play-Offs. This means every participant has an equal shot at the top spots from the very beginning.

Fantasy Football Scout's Mini-League

Fantasy Football Scout has created its own mini-league specifically for the Fantasy EFL Play-Offs. Managers can join this community competition to test their skills against other Fantasy Football Scout users. The mini-league also offers additional giveaways and prizes beyond the standard game rewards.

How to Join

To join Fantasy Football Scout's Play-Offs mini-league, managers will need to enter the following PIN code during the join process: 0D9I8JH3. This unique code ensures entry into the dedicated Fantasy Football Scout competition pool.

Available Prizes

The competition offers substantial rewards for top performers:

First Place: Annual Scout Membership plus a £100 Amazon voucher

Second Place: Annual Scout Membership

Third Place: Annual Scout Membership

Additionally, Premium Members who place in the top three of the mini-league will be reimbursed for their entry, providing extra incentive for existing subscribers to participate.

Additional Prize Opportunities

Beyond Fantasy Football Scout's offerings, the EFL are offering prizes themselves for the Play-Offs game. This means participants have multiple chances to win rewards from both Fantasy Football Scout and the official EFL.

Conclusion

With a fresh start for all players, competitive prize tiers, and involvement from both Fantasy Football Scout and the EFL, the Fantasy EFL Play-Offs presents a compelling reason to stay active in fantasy football through the post-season period.
