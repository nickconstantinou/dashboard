FPL Notes: James Hill Own Goal, Senesi Assist, and Bogle Injury Concern

James Hill's Own Goal Frustration Continues

Bournemouth defender James Hill experienced another unfortunate moment in his recent spell, scoring an own goal against Leeds United. This marked his second own goal in just four matches. Hill, priced at £4.2m, accidentally deflected Wilfried Gnonto's strike into his own net, compounding a difficult period for the young defender.

Jayden Bogle Substitution Raises Injury Concerns

Leeds United wing-back Jayden Bogle (£4.4m) was replaced at half-time, prompting concerns about his fitness. Joe Rodon (£3.9m) came on to replace him. Reports suggest Bogle took a knock to his right foot during the match, which requires monitoring ahead of future gameweeks.

Bournemouth's Unbeaten Streak Continues

Bournemouth extended their unbeaten league run to 14 matches following the fixture. However, eight of these results were draws, highlighting the Cherries' tendency toward stalemates rather than victories. This record positions them as a defensively solid but sometimes unpredictable Fantasy option.

Karl Darlow's Key Saves

Leeds goalkeeper Karl Darlow (£3.9m) produced an important performance with five saves during the match. Among his stops was a crucial intervention to deny David Brooks just before halftime, keeping his team competitive throughout the contest.

Dominic Calvert-Lewin Fails to Impact

Leeds striker Dominic Calvert-Lewin (£5.7m) had minimal influence on the match, remaining completely anonymous for the duration of his appearance. This continues a pattern of subdued performances that makes him a risky Fantasy selection at his current price point.

Senesi Outperforms Hill in Double Gameweek

Over the course of both Double Gameweek 33 fixtures, Marcos Senesi outperformed James Hill in Fantasy terms. Senesi accumulated 6 points compared to Hill's 5, with the difference of four points in the second match separating the two defenders across the double gameweek.

Rayan Makes Instant Impact

Brazilian teenager Rayan came on as a substitute and made an immediate impression, scoring in the 86th minute to give Bournemouth a 2-1 lead. He swept in Tyler Adams's cross from close range, demonstrating value from the bench.

Marcos Senesi's Assist

Marcos Senesi contributed to the scoring with a superb through ball that set up Eli Kroupi Junior's goal. This creative contribution adds to his defensive returns and makes him a dual threat for Fantasy purposes.

Gameweek 34 Absence

Neither Bournemouth nor Leeds United will feature in Gameweek 34, making both sets of players unattractive for short-term transfers. This blank gameweek should factor into Fantasy managers' planning and roster decisions.

Leeds' Relegation Buffer

Despite the result, Leeds United remain nine points ahead of the relegation zone, providing some comfort regarding their Premier League status. This cushion may influence team selection and tactical approaches in upcoming fixtures.
