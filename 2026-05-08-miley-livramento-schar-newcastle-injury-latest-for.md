# Newcastle Injury Latest for FPL Gameweek 36

Newcastle United faces a significant injury crisis heading into the crucial stages of the season, with several key players ruled out for the remainder of the campaign. Fantasy Premier League managers holding Newcastle assets will need to reassess their squads as the Magpies deal with multiple absentees.

## Lewis Miley

Lewis Miley will miss the rest of the season with a fractured fibula, dealing a major blow to Newcastle's midfield options. The young midfielder, priced at £4.4m in FPL, has been a popular budget option this season. His absence leaves a void in Newcastle's squad as they push for a strong finish.

## Tino Livramento

Tino Livramento is unlikely to return this season due to a thigh injury sustained earlier in the campaign. The defender, valued at £4.9m, had been a solid FPL option before his injury. Manager Eddie Howe indicated uncertainty about his availability, stating he was not confident Livramento would feature again this term, suggesting a cautious approach would be taken rather than rushing him back prematurely.

## Fabian Schär

Fabian Schär has developed an ankle or foot infection that has complicated his recovery timeline. The centre-back's situation remains uncertain, with officials unable to confirm whether he will feature again this season. Schär had been progressing toward fitness before developing this unusual medical issue, making his return timeline increasingly unclear.

## Emil Krafth

Emil Krafth remains on Newcastle's long-term injury list with a knee injury. The Swedish defender, priced at £4.3m, continues his recovery and is not expected to return during the current campaign. He joins the growing list of unavailable players at St James' Park.

## FPL Consideration: Will Osula

For managers seeking Newcastle exposure, Will Osula has emerged as an intriguing option. The forward has scored three goals in his past four matches, demonstrating promising form. Priced at £5.4m, Osula offers a budget-friendly route into Newcastle's attack. Manager Eddie Howe has praised his potential, describing him as "outstandingly quick" and "powerful" with the ability to score with both feet and compete in the air. Howe emphasized Osula's willingness to learn and improve, suggesting continued development is likely.

Managers should monitor Newcastle's injury situation closely as the season reaches its conclusion, with several players facing uncertain futures ahead of Gameweek 36.
