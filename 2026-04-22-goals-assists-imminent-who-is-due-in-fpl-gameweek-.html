Goals + assists imminent! Who is ‘due’ in FPL Gameweek 34?

With Blank Gameweek 34 looming on the Fantasy Premier League calendar, managers face a familiar dilemma: which underperforming assets might finally deliver before the fixtures dry up? The "Goals Imminent" methodology—a system that flags players generating high-quality chances without commensurate returns—has become one of the sharper tools in the FPL community's arsenal. And heading into a blank GW, pinpointing those due for a breakthrough carries extra weight.

Last Week's Hits

The approach paid off again in Gameweek 33. Dominic Calvert-Lewin (£5.7m) found the net, as did Marcus Tavernier (£5.4m) and the ever-present Erling Haaland (£14.5m). Morgan Rogers (£7.4m) grabbed his second goal in 16 matches—a reminder that even irregular scorers can surface on the Goals Imminent radar at the right moment. And Matheus Nunes (£5.3m) continued his quietly excellent season, extending his lead as the defender with the most assists in the game: eight.

Those weren't lucky guesses. They were calculated projections based on shot volume, chance quality, and underlying numbers that historically precede regression.

The Methodology

The Goals Imminent table identifies players who have accumulated at least nine shots over their last four matches—but scored no more than one goal in that window. It's a crude but effective filter: volume without output often signals a player caught in the gap between bad luck and bad finishing. Some are victims of tight margins, others are simply cold. The trick is separating the genuinely unlucky from the genuinely limited.

For Gameweek 34, that distinction matters more than ever.

Why This Week Feels Different

Blank Gameweeks compress decision-making. Managers with limited chips or thin benches can't afford to carry dead weight. Identifying players who are mathematically "due" offers a way to stay active with conviction rather than guessing blindly.

The targets from last week's successes—players priced between £5.3m and £14.5m, spanning budget enablers to premium options—illustrate the range of assets the table can surface. Calvert-Lewin and Tavernier offer budget-friendly upside. Haaland remains the ceiling. Rogers is the differential type. Nunes proves defenders can belong on the same list as forwards.

For managers navigating the blank, the question isn't just who to pick—it's who to trust when everyone else is scrambling. The data suggests last week's success stories weren't flukes. They were overdue.
