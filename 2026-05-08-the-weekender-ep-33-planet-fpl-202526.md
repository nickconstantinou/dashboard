# Arsenal's Champions League Progress: FPL Implications

## Arsenal Advance to Champions League Final

Arsenal secured their place in the Champions League final by defeating Atletico Madrid in Tuesday night's semifinal second leg. The Gunners deployed the same starting XI that earned victory against Fulham in their previous fixture. Arsenal will now meet Paris Saint-Germain in the final, marking a significant achievement as one of three English clubs to reach European finals this season, alongside Aston Villa and Crystal Palace.

## Tactical Notes and Player Roles

Declan Rice produced another standout performance in the semifinal, though his heat map revealed continued positioning in a deep midfield role. Lewis Scully retained his place in the starting lineup and delivered another solid performance. Riccardo Calafiori returned from injury to feature as left back in the match.

## Injury Concerns for Key Assets

Three of Arsenal's most exciting offensive options—Bukayo Saka, Eberechi Eze, and Jokarez—were all substituted before the hour mark against Atletico Madrid. This pattern raises concerns for FPL managers, particularly regarding Saka, who managed only 45 minutes against Fulham before his early withdrawal in the Champions League fixture. The consistent early substitutions suggest ongoing fitness management for these players.

## Havertz Update

Kai Havertz featured on the bench during the Atletico Madrid match and appears likely to be available for upcoming fixtures. His potential return could provide Arsenal with additional attacking options as they prepare for the Champions League final and their domestic schedule.

## FPL Considerations

The early substitutions of key offensive players warrant careful monitoring. Managers holding Saka, Eze, or Jokarez should track their training participation and match minutes closely. Havertz's availability may introduce squad rotation variables as Arsenal balances their final fixtures with Champions League commitments.
