# Chelsea's Dominant Victory and the Enzo Fernandez Situation: Key FPL Implications

Chelsea delivered a commanding 7-0 victory over League One side Port Vale in Gameweek 33, securing a double for both the Blues and Brighton and Hove Albion while both teams now blank in Gameweek 34. The comprehensive win at Stamford Bridge saw multiple players find the scoresheet, providing valuable insight into Chelsea's attacking options for FPL managers ahead of the next double gameweek.

## The Match Overview

Chelsea's emphatic 7-0 triumph over Port Vale featured goals from Hato, Pedro, an own goal, Tosin, Andrey Santos, Estêvão, and Garnacho (from the penalty spot). The attacking returns were supported by assists from Neto, Palmer, Gusto, and Estevao. The Blues fielded a strong lineup including Sanchez in goal, Gusto, Fofana, Tosin, Hato, Andrey Santos, Lavia, Estevao, Palmer, Neto, and Joao Pedro – each of the forward players getting approximately an hour of game time.

## Enzo Fernandez Update

The major storyline surrounding Chelsea involves Enzo Fernandez (£6.5m), who was notably absent from the matchday squad. Manager Liam Rosenior confirmed on Friday that the Argentine midfielder would not feature against Port Vale due to his international break comments, and he will also miss Gameweek 32.

Rosenior provided detailed comments on the situation, stating that he had a positive one-on-one conversation with Fernandez and that thetwo are "in a very good place." The manager emphasized that the dressing room remains sacred and that while he made clear his personal feelings about Fernandez as a person – describing him as a "top, top guy" – the focus must now shift to achieving the team's footballing objectives this season.

The sight of Enzo at Stamford Bridge on Saturday supporting his teammates from the sidelines has been interpreted as a positive step toward potential reconciliation and return.

## Team Selection Insights

Rosenior opted to field his strongest possible lineup given the circumstances, with two notable exceptions: Marc Cucurella (£6.0m) and Moises Caicedo (£5.7m) were among the substitutes despite being available. Rosenior hinted that their omission may have been discipline-related following recent comments from both players.

Cole Palmer (£10.6m), Joao Pedro (£7.8m), and Pedro Neto (£7.0m) all started and played an hour each, demonstrating their importance to the team. Malo Gusto (£4.9m) and Robert Sanchez (£4.8m) continued to benefit from the injuries to Reece James (£5.6m) and Filip Jorgensen (£4.3m), maintaining their regular places in the XI.

## Why This Matters for FPL Managers

This fixture provides critical intelligence for FPL managers planning their teams. The 7-0 victory demonstrated Chelsea's attacking potency against weaker opposition, with multiple goal-scoring options emerging. The exclusion of Cucurella and Caicedo suggests potential rotation risks for regular starters moving forward, while the hour chacun provided to key attackers indicates they are fully fit and ready for the run-in.

Most importantly, the Enzo Fernandez situation bears monitoring. While he will miss Gameweek 32, the manager's positive comments and the player's presence at the stadium suggest a return could come sooner rather than later – possibly for Double Gameweek 33.

## What's Next

Chelsea's next competitive fixture will occur in eight days, giving Rosenior his first clear week to work with the full squad in training. FPL managers should monitor news regarding Enzo Fernandez's potential return, as well as updates on Reece James and Filip Jorgensen's recovery timelines. The blanks in Gameweek 34 for both Chelsea and Brighton mean that managers may need to utilize their bench options strategically during that double gameweek period.
