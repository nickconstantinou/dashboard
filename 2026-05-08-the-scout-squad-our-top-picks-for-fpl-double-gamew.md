# The Scout Squad: Our Top Picks for FPL Double Gameweek 36

Fantasy Premier League managers face a crucial decision point as Double Gameweek 36 approaches. The Scout Squad analysis highlights players who offer the best combination of form, opportunity, and value for the run-in.

## Manchester City Attackers

Manchester City assets dominate the top recommendations this gameweek, with several players demonstrating excellent underlying statistics.

**Erling Haaland** continues to deliver for FPL managers. The league's top scorer has netted in three successive matches and remains priced at £14.7m. His consistent goal threat makes him a staple for any Double Gameweek strategy.

**Nico O'Reilly** represents excellent budget value at £5.2m. In his last seven Gameweeks, he has accumulated 18 penalty area shots—a remarkable total for a player at his price point. In fact, the next-best FPL defender has only 11 penalty area shots, achieved across two more appearances. O'Reilly's attacking output from defence makes him an exceptional differential pick.

**Rayan Cherki** has emerged as a compelling option at £6.6m. The Manchester City midfielder has registered four returns in four matches, alongside 17 shots and 14 chances created. His recent purple patch suggests he could be an excellent midfield differential for the Double Gameweek.

## Arsenal Options

Arsenal players also warrant attention for Double Gameweek 36.

**Bukayo Saka** is rediscovering his best form, scoring in consecutive matches. However, at £9.9m, managers should note he is still not completing full matches—failing to reach the 60-minute threshold in recent outings. This limits his ceiling despite his attacking quality.

**Viktor Gyokeres** has been a revelation in his debut Arsenal campaign, reaching 14 league goals. He offers premium forward coverage at £8.1m and should feature prominently across both Double Gameweek fixtures.

**Gabriel Magalhaes** posted his season-long best performance in Gameweek 35, registering three shots and 0.64 expected goals. For managers seeking Arsenal defensive coverage, his underlying numbers suggest potential for returns in the Double Gameweek.

## Budget Differentials

Several budget options present themselves for FPL managers seeking value.

**Taiwo Awoniyi** delivered a standout Gameweek 35, scoring a brace, providing an assist, and accumulating 16 points. At £5.2m, he represents exceptional value for Nottingham Forest's Double Gameweek fixtures.

**Richarlison** has produced four returns in seven matches where minutes were limited. While not a guaranteed starter, his point-per-minute ratio makes him an interesting differential option at his price point.
