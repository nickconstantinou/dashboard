# Thiago's Golden Boot Bid Overshadows Fulham Shake-Up in West London Derby

Igor Thiago has a chance to draw level with Erling Haaland in the Premier League goalscoring charts on Saturday — and a brace would put him outright at the top of the Golden Boot race. That individual storyline elevates what might otherwise be a routine Double Gameweek 33 fixture into something with real season-defining stakes. Kick-off at the Gtech Community Stadium is at 12.30pm BST.

Brentford can climb to sixth place — even if temporarily — with a win. Fulham, meanwhile, would move level on points with the Bees if they leave west London with all three points. The table implications are real, but the afternoon's most compelling subplot sits one name deep on the hosts' teamsheet.

Thiago has been quietly mounting a legitimate challenge to the league's most prolific marksman. One goal this afternoon draws him level with Haaland's tally. Two or more hands him the outright lead. For a player who arrived without the fanfare of a marquee signing, it represents a remarkable individual campaign — and one that will command attention well beyond the FPL community.

Brentford head coach Keith Andrews named an unchanged line-up from his side's last outing, keeping faith with the side that delivered the result. The only tweak to the matchday squad is the return of Aaron Hickey, who has been absent for two months and takes a place among the substitutes.

The more dramatic decisions came from the visitors. Marco Silva made four changes to his starting XI following the 2-0 defeat to Liverpool, benching Antonee Robinson, Josh King, Oscar Bobb, and Sander Berge. In their place come Ryan Sessegnon, Emile Smith Rowe, Sasa Lukic, and Tom Cairney — a reshuffle that signals both rotation and intent.

Raul Jimenez, the experienced Mexican striker, must settle for a place on the bench again. Rodrigo Muniz retains his place up front, suggesting Silva is leaning on youth and dynamism rather than proven Premier League firepower for this derby. Whether the changes are tactical or load-management will be a question asked in the post-match analysis.

## Brentford XI

Kelleher, Kayode, van den Berg, Collins, Lewis-Potter, Yarmoliuk, Jensen, Ouattara, Damsgaard, Schade, Thiago.

*Subs: Valdimarsson, Hickey, Pinnock, Nelson, Ajer, Donovan, Bentt, Shield, Stephenson.*
