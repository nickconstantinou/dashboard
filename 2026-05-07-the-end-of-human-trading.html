# The End of Human Trading? AI Agents, Tokenization, and the Democratization of Global Finance

Key Insights

  • eToro launched the first web trading platform before Interactive Brokers, and became the first regulated entity in Europe to offer Bitcoin trading, having traded Bitcoin since 2011 when it was priced at just $5 per coin
  • Only 10% of European households invest in capital markets versus 60% in the United States, revealing a massive global disparity in retail market access that shaped eToro's international expansion strategy for nearly two decades before entering the US in 2021
  • Yoni Assia experienced the dot-com bubble burst firsthand, losing 90% of the $300,000 he had accumulated as a high school student through early capital markets trading, a formative experience that shaped his long-term perspective on market cycles and risk
  • Meme stock phenomena like AMC and GameStop involved sophisticated gamma squeeze strategies identified by financially literate communities on Reddit, which ultimately prevented thousands of job losses by enabling those companies to raise capital and eliminate debt—sophistication often obscured by media narratives
  • eToro's founding vision statement from nearly twenty years ago remains unchanged: "to open the global markets for everyone to trade and invest in a simple and transparent way," a mission that guided their evolution from desktop app to web pioneer to mobile-first platform
