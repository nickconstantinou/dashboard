# Best Cheap Players for Gameweek 36 Bench Boost

## Introduction

Gameweek 36 presents an opportunity for Fantasy Premier League managers to deploy a Bench Boost chip. Selecting the right budget players can maximise points from substitutes while keeping costs low for star performers elsewhere. This analysis examines verified defensive options and fixture-based insights to inform those decisions.

## Verified Cheap Defenders

### Michael Keane (Everton – £4.5m)

Michael Keane represents a cost-effective defensive option at £4.5m. The Everton centre-back is averaging 10.65 defensive contributions per 90 minutes, a figure that outdoes his defensive partner James Tarkowski who is priced at £5.7m. This output suggests solid per-90 metrics that may translate to consistent points in favourable fixture scenarios.

### Kevin Danso (Tottenham Hotspur – £4.2m)

Kevin Danso offers the cheapest route into Tottenham's defence at £4.2m. The Austrian defender has delivered contributions in five of his last six starts, indicating consistent involvement in attacking moves despite his defensive positioning. This recent form makes him a viable budget option for managers looking to field four cheap substitutes.

## Tottenham Tactical Context

Since Ange Postecoglou's departure and the appointment of Des Zeroi, Tottenham have shown defensive improvement. Spurs have the league's lowest expected goals conceded (xGC) tally at 3.17 since the managerial change. This statistical context supports Danso's potential viability as a Bench Boost enabler, particularly if this defensive solidity continues.

Managers should note that his selection for Bench Boost purposes carries significant risk regarding attacking returns, despite his consistent starting place.
