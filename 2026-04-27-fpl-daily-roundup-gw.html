FPL Daily Roundup: Saka Returns, Pep Rotates, and the Run-In Begins

Today's Key FPL News & Injuries

The biggest story heading into this crucial period of the season is Bukayo Saka's return to the Arsenal matchday squad for the first time since March. The England international, priced at £9.8m according to Fantasy Football Scout sources, was named among the substitutes for Arsenal's clash with Newcastle United, providing a significant boost to the Gunners' attacking options. However, managers should temper expectations immediately — Saka did not start, coming on only as a sub, which suggests his fitness is being carefully managed by Mikel Arteta ahead of the run-in.

The same Arsenal-Newcastle fixture saw Riccardo Calafiori (£5.6m) miss out entirely despite Arteta suggesting on Friday that the Italian defender would likely be available. This represents a frustrating situation for the substantial number of managers who brought him in hoping for a clean sheet against Newcastle. Calafiori failed to even make the matchday squad, and Arsenal managers are advised to continue monitoring the situation closely rather than assuming he'll feature in the next gameweek.

Elsewhere in north London, there are concerns over Eberechi Eze and Kai Havertz, both of whom departed during the same match with muscular niggles. Havertz left the field after just 34 minutes, with Eze following early in the second half. Arteta provided a cautiously optimistic update, stating: "There are muscular niggles. We don't think it's too much. We have to wait and see if they're going to be available for Wednesday." The fact that both players were noted as leaving during an Arsenal match raises questions about their availability heading into what promises to be a crucial stretch of fixtures.

Pep Guardiola also offered insight into Manchester City's squad management ahead of their FA Cup semi-final against Southampton, where he made eight changes from the previous Premier League match. The Catalan manager explained that rotation was necessary because players cannot maintain the same intensity when playing every three days, stating it would not be possible to use the same players under such demanding conditions. This has significant implications for FPL managers holding City assets, as thefixture congestion across multiple competitions means rotation is virtually guaranteed for the foreseeable future.
