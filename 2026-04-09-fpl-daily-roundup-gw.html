Today's Key FPL News and Injuries

The injury picture heading into Gameweek 32 presents several significant decisions for managers, with Arsenal's defensive contingent drawing particular attention after their cup exits left them fighting only on Premier League and Champions League fronts.

The Gunners boast the highest clean sheet probability in the league for this gameweek at 44% against Bournemouth, making Gabriel and teammate David Raya essentially must-haves for anyone building around Arsenal assets.

The situation at Liverpool remains deeply complicated. Darwin Núñez is back in training but may not be ready to start, while the broader context of Champions League distraction against PSG has left managers guessing about playing time. Arne Slot's side have lost consecutive matches across all competitions—4-0 to Manchester City and 2-1 to Brighton—and the speculation about the manager's future has created additional uncertainty. With Fulham sandwiched between two Champions League legs, predicting which Liverpool forwards will start or feature from the bench has become almost impossible.

Several other players carry injury concerns into this weekend. Joe Rodon has a 75% chance of playing despite an ankle injury, while Iliman Ndiaye faces the same probability with a foot injury. Calvin Bassey's back problem leaves him at 75% availability, though the timing of his return could prove crucial for those on Free Hit strategies. For Arsenal, the news that Bukayo Saka and Oleksandr Timber remain unavailable means managers cannot yet return to the Arsenal attack, though the Champions League progress means every Premier League match remains crucial with trophy hopes diminished.

The Gameweek Landscape: Confirmed Doubles and Blanks

The fixture computer has delivered exactly what the FPL community expected, though the confirmation arrived later than many would have liked. Double Gameweek 33 is now officially confirmed with six teams playing twice: Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds, and Manchester City. These sides will form the foundation of most competitive teams over the next two gameweeks, though the complication arises because these same six teams will all blank in Gameweek 34.

This creates the central strategic puzzle of the season: how to navigate the double in Gameweek 33 while preparing for the blank in Gameweek 34. The confirmed Blank Gameweek 34 sees six teams without fixtures: Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds, and Manchester City—precisely the same six clubs that double in Gameweek 33. This symmetry means managers cannot simply load up on doubling teams and hold them through both gameweeks, as they would be left with a depleted squad for Gameweek 34.

The fixture density across the remaining gameweeks shows Gameweek 33 leading with 26 confirmed fixtures, followed by Gameweeks 32, 35, 36, and 37 each with 20 confirmed fixtures. For managers planning their chip strategies, this spread creates multiple viable approaches depending on squad composition and remaining chips.

Nottingham Forest's fixtures have drawn attention as a differential option, with their upcoming schedule offering favourable matchups that could catch opponents off guard. West Ham, meanwhile, have consecutive home games against Wolves and Everton in Gameweeks 32 and 34—making them attractive for managers not planning to use chips in Gameweek 33.

Transfer Strategy: The Chip Question Defines Everything

The transfer recommendations from this week's creators depend almost entirely on chip strategy, and the community has effectively split into two main schools of thought.

The first approach involves wildcarding in Gameweek 32, using Bench Boost in the confirmed Double Gameweek 33, and then deploying Free Hit in the blank Gameweek 34. This suits managers whose current squads contain few assets from the six doubling teams and who need to fundamentally rebuild before Gameweek 33. The advantage here is immediate—you get to play your strongest team in Gameweek 32 while also preparing for the double—but the disadvantage is leaving yourself exposed if other managers use their wildcards later and can target different fixtures.

The alternative strategy, which FPL Harry explains he is personally employing, involves Free Hitting in Gameweek 33 to navigate the double without holding those doubling teams, wildcarding in Gameweek 35 when the fixture situation stabilises, and potentially using Bench Boost in Gameweek 36. This approach requires holding onto your current team through Gameweek 32, which may feel suboptimal, but it allows you to completely reset after the chaos of the double-blank sequence.

For managers on the first strategy, the targets are clear: Manchester City assets, particularly Haaland, Phil Foden, and Mateo Kovacic, lead the way because they are expected to double not only in Gameweek 33 but also again in Gameweek 36. This additional fixture makes them stand out above other doubling teams. Leeds also warrant attention, with Burnley at home in Gameweek 35 representing a particularly attractive fixture. From Brighton, Pascal Gross remains the preferred option for minutes reliability, while Chelsea's Cole Palmer is essentially the only viable option from that squad given their defensive inconsistencies.

For the Free Hit 33 strategy, the focus shifts entirely to teams playing in Gameweeks 32 and 34, with West Ham at the top of the fixture ticker thanks to consecutive home games against Wolves and Everton. Jarrod Bowen emerges as the most popular West Ham asset given his attacking involvement, while Harry Maguire offers a budget defensive option from Manchester United. Sunderland also appears as a consideration for their favourable run, with defenders potentially providing value at lower ownership percentages.

The key insight across all strategies is that the fixture ticker must be personalised to your specific chip situation. A player who is fantastic for a Gameweek 33 free hitter might be completely useless for someone wildcarding in Gameweek 35, and the generic "best players" lists ignore this reality entirely.
