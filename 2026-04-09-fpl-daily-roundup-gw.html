FPL Gameweek 32 Roundup: Navigating the Double Gameweek Dilemma

The Fantasy Premier League landscape has shifted dramatically following the confirmation of Double Gameweek 33 and Blank Gameweek 34, leaving managers across the globe scrambling to adjust their strategies. With six teams confirmed to play twice in GW33 before blanking in GW34, the calculus for chip deployment has become considerably more complex than it appeared just days ago.

Today's Key FPL News and Injuries

The injury situation heading into Gameweek 32 presents several concerns that managers must factor into their decision-making. From the facts sheet, Joe Rodon of Leeds United is dealing with an ankle injury and has just a 75% chance of playing, while Iliman Ndiaye at Everton carries a foot injury at the same probability. Fulham's Calvin Bassey has a back injury ruling him out at 75% availability, and Newcastle's Sven Botman faces a facial injury with only a 25% chance of featuring. Perhaps most significantly, Brighton's Lewis Dunk is suspended until April 26, meaning he will miss this gameweek entirely.

Tom Hadley confirmed on Fantasy Football Scout that Gabriel and Rice are fit after minor concerns, while David Raya delivered a "sensational" performance in the Champions League against Sporting CP, keeping a clean sheet in a 1-0 victory that extended Arsenal's remarkable home unbeaten run to 23 games. However, Saka and Timber remain unavailable, leaving Arsenal's attacking options somewhat depleted for their fixture against Bournemouth.

The situation at Liverpool remains murky. Thiago has managed just one goal this league campaign despite starting the season at £6.0m, representing exceptional value, though his minutes are difficult to predict given the fixture congestion. Liverpool face Fulham at Anfield before and after their Champions League tie against PSG, and Arne Slot's rotation choices remain unpredictable. As one creator noted, "the problem is Liverpool have got Fulham home which is sandwiched in between two ties against PSG in the Champions League."

The Gameweek Landscape: Double and Blank Dynamics

The confirmed fixture data reveals a complex pattern that will dominate the remaining weeks of the season. Double Gameweek 33 features six teams playing twice: Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds, and Manchester City. These same six teams will then blank in Gameweek 34, creating a stark divide between those with access to doubling players and those without.

The implications for strategy are significant. As FPL Harry emphasized in his transfer targets video, managers must identify which approach aligns with their remaining chips. The teams confirmed for doubles represent a mix of established powers like Manchester City and Chelsea alongside smaller clubs like Bournemouth and Burnley, creating interesting value propositions across the price spectrum.

For those without chips remaining, the focus shifts to teams with no blanks across the remaining schedule. Arsenal, Newcastle United, and Nottingham Forest all play every gameweek without interruption, making them ideal for navigating the blank in GW34 when six teams will fail to play. Arsenal leads the league with 15 clean sheets and just 22 goals conceded in 2025/26, making their defenders relatively secure even against difficult opponents. Newcastle has exited all cup competitions both domestically and in Europe, meaning they can fully focus on the league with no fixture congestion.

Nottingham Forest offers particular value in Gameweek 33, where Neco Williams (£4.7m) and Morgan Gibbs-White (£7.4m) host Burnley at home—a fixture that could easily outscore some of the more popular "doubler" options at Bournemouth, Brighton, and Leeds.

Captain Recommendations: Who Creators Are Backing

The captaincy discussion centers overwhelmingly around Bruno Fernandes, and the reasoning is compelling. As one creator highlighted, Bruno now has 19.43 expected goal involvement, a figure that "can only be bettered by Haaland," and has created 24 big chances—"nine more than any player." He has delivered his 11th double-digit return of the season, continuing his remarkable campaign as the most reliable captain option in the game. The decision to hand him the armband for GW32 was described as "straightforward" given his underlying numbers.

Erling Haaland remains the most captained player, though one creator suggested this "shows the number of inactive teams at this point in the season," estimating there must be several million managers who have effectively given up on their squads. For those still actively managing, Haaland's double in GW33 makes him a compelling option, particularly given Manchester City's expected double in GW36 as well.

Among the differential options, Gabriel (£7.2m) stands out with a 44% clean sheet probability against Bournemouth—the highest in the division for GW32. His recovery from the knee knock picked up in the FA Cup appears complete, and he should be fit to start. David Raya (£6.0m) at 34.6% ownership represents the most popular Arsenal asset, though his Champions League commitments midweek add some fatigue risk.

Transfer Strategy: Who's Recommended and Why

The transfer recommendations split significantly based on chip strategy, but certain players emerge repeatedly across different approaches.

For managers planning to Free Hit in GW33, the focus shifts to teams with strong fixtures in GW32 and GW34 while ignoring the double. West Ham United tops this analysis, with Wolverhampton Wanderers at home followed by Everton at home in those respective gameweeks. Jarrod Bowen (£7.4m) emerges as the most popular West Ham pick, though defenders at the club provide excellent budget options. Sunderland also appears prominently with favourable home fixtures, making their defenders worth consideration.

For thoseWildcarding in GW32 with Bench Boost in GW33 and Free Hit in GW34, Manchester City assets become essential. Not only do they double in GW33, but they're expected to double again in GW36, effectively giving them an additional fixture over other clubs. Haaland (£14.4m), Jeremy Doku, and Phil Foden represent the primary targets. Neco O'Reilly (£5.0m) at Manchester City appears in many wildcard teams alongside Semenyo and Haaland, with the choice between O'Reilly away to Chelsea or Maddison home to Tottenham representing a key decision point.

The budget options for the wildcard strategy include Leeds United, with their very favourable home match against Burnley in GW35 making them attractive long-term. Bournemouth's Marcos Senesi (£5.1m) at 20.2% ownership represents a solid defensive option, while Brighton's Joao Pedro offers forward coverage.

One creator emphasized the importance of Dango's situation, expecting him to "come back into the starting 11 for Brentford" after his inexplicable benching against Wolves. This potential return strengthens the case for saving the Wildcard, as Dango represents "one of the few reasons that I like the idea of saving the Wildcard this week."

Players to Avoid or Sell

The creators identified several players to steer clear of, with Eberechi Eze at Crystal Palace featuring prominently. As one analysis noted, Eze has "nine goals, three assists" for the season, which is respectable, but "hasn't scored since GW25"—seven games ago—and has "blanked in his last four games in a row." His minutes have declined consistently: 85 minutes, then 76, then 74, and then 70 in his most recent appearance. Combined with Crystal Palace's potential involvement in UEFA Conference League semi-finals surrounding GW35, rotation risk makes Eze a faded asset.

Liverpool assets require careful evaluation given their fixture congestion and poor recent form. Van Dijk may be "on borrowed time" if Liverpool exit the Champions League to PSG, and his GW33 does not feature a double, making him less attractive for Free Hit strategies. The Champions League scheduling creates uncertainty around player minutes, making Liverpool a difficult team to trust for consistent returns.

The international break exposed some problematic selections, with one creator noting that Dango received an unexpected benching despite having been "their best player literally the game before against Wolves." Such unpredictable rotation makes players from teams with uncertain selection patterns risky propositions at this crucial stage.

Chip Strategy: Where Creators Agree and Disagree

The chip strategy discussion reveals both consensus and division among creators. There is broad agreement that the GW33 double has fundamentally reshaped the decision to use the Wildcard. As one creator explained, they were "very set on Wildcard in Gameweek 32" at the start of the international break, but the FA Cup results confirming six teams would double in GW33 made the calculation "wide open."

Two primary strategies emerge as preferred approaches. The first involves using the Wildcard immediately in GW32, followed by the Bench Boost in GW33 during the double, then the Free Hit in the blank GW34. This approach maximizes the immediately available doubles but commits to a squad reconstruction before seeing how the Double GW33 fixtures actually play out. The second approach follows inverse logic: Free Hit in GW33 during the double to load up on the six doubling teams, Wildcard in GW35 to reassess and rebuild based on what works, then Bench Boost immediately in GW36.

The distinction between having chips and being chip-less at this stage of the season can mean the difference between a top-10k finish and missing out on triple captain bonuses entirely. As the recency context emphasized, managers without chips must be more disciplined about fixture selection, focusing on teams that can deliver returns every single gameweek rather than those who might only shine in a single double.

Creators also highlighted the importance of monitoring Palace's European progress closely, as advancement past the semi-finals would increase rotation concerns. The current best Palace options appear to be goalkeeper Dean Henderson (£5.1m), full-back Daniel Munoz (£5.8m), defender Maxence Lacroix (£5.1m), and winger Ismaila Sarr (£6.3m), though their involvement in European competition adds uncertainty.

Where Creators Disagree

The primary point of disagreement centers on Wildcard timing. While some creators advocate using the Wildcard immediately in GW32 to lock in preferred doubling players, others argue strongly for waiting until GW35 to allow the GW33 double to play out and reveal which players actually deliver. The latter group accepts whatever returns come during GW33 with a potentially weaker squad but gains the advantage of informed decision-making based on actual performance rather than projections.

There is also debate around Liverpool assets. Some creators are completely off Liverpool given their Champions League commitments and poor recent form, including a 4-0 defeat to Manchester City followed by a 2-1 loss to Brighton before the break. Others see value in specific players like Van Dijk for those employing the Free Hit chip in GW33, noting he remains "handy given that he plays home this week and double in GW34" against Crystal Palace.

The conversation around Eberechi Eze also reveals divided opinion. While one creator strongly recommends avoiding him due to his seven-game goal drought and declining minutes, others acknowledge he may still be needed for those planning to Free Hit in GW34 where he would be essential.

Three Key Actions for This Week

First, if you have the Wildcard and Bench Boost remaining, seriously consider the Free Hit in GW33 approach rather than playing your Wildcard immediately. The ability to load up on all six doubling teams without using transfers, assess which players actually perform, and then Wildcard in GW35 based on real data represents a meaningful edge. Manchester City, Leeds, Brighton, Chelsea, Bournemouth, and Burnley all double in GW33, and the Free Hit allows you to select the optimal combination without depleting your transfer budget.

Second, secure Arsenal defensive coverage despite their Champions League commitments. Arsenal face Bournemouth at home with a 44% clean sheet probability—the highest in the division—before traveling to Newcastle. With the Premier League title and Champions League both still in play, every game remains crucial. Gabriel (£7.2m) at 42.9% ownership and David Raya (£6.0m) at 34.6% represent the most secure routes to Arsenal's defensive solidity.

Third, target Nottingham Forest assets for the GW33 double. While many managers will gravitate toward the more obvious doubling teams like Manchester City and Chelsea, Forest at home to Burnley represents excellent value. Neco Williams (£4.7m) at just 3.1% ownership and Morgan Gibbs-White (£7.4m) at 5.0% ownership offer differentiation opportunities with favourable fixtures. Combined with their blank-free run through the season's end, Forest provides a reliable foundation for navigating the upcoming fixture complexity.
