This immediately creates a headache for those who loaded up on players from those clubs, particularly as Manchester City assets were looking attractive at their price points. The blanks mean that managers with triple Arsenal coverage or heavy City investments will need to dip into the free hit or accept a hit to proceed.

The double gameweek situation has evolved significantly since last week. Similarly, Manchester City and Burnley will double in Gameweek 33 and blank in Gameweek 34. This creates an interesting dynamic where the triple Chelsea and Brighton stack becomes far more appealing, while those with City assets must plan for the blank in 34.

Any references to speculative doubles in Gameweek 36 or beyond should be treated with significant caution, as the confirmed data simply doesn't support that. Managers should operate on the assumption that the Gameweek 33 double is the final major swing in the season, making the decisions around that fixture block absolutely critical.
