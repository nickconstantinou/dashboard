# Arsenal Injury Crisis Deepens: Gabriel, Rice, and Trossard Return, But Saka and Timber Remain Unavailable

## The Full Story

Arsenal manager Mikel Arteta confirmed on Monday that defenders Gabriel Magalhaes, midfielder Declan Rice, and forward Leandro Trossard have all recovered from their respective injuries and will be available for Tuesday's UEFA Champions League tie against Sporting. The trio had all missed Saturday's FA Cup defeat to Southampton, with Gabriel hobbling off with a knee issue while Rice and Trossard withdrew from international duty earlier in the week.

However, the press conference delivered concerning news regarding Bukayo Saka and Jurrien Timber, who were absent from Monday's open training session. When asked about the pair's availability, Arteta stated plainly: "No, they're having trouble, they're not ready yet. Let's see, hopefully they're going to be ready for the weekend, if everything goes well, and that's a massive boost because in recent days we've lost so many important players and that's something that we need to change immediately."

Also missing from training were Crystal Palace midfielder Eberechi Eze and Arsenal's Piero Hincapie. Eze has been carrying an injury since the EFL Cup final in mid-March, though social media speculation suggests he could return for Gameweek 32. Hincapie suffered a muscle issue while representing Ecuador during the international break.

## Context and Background

The injury crisis stems from a combination of factors that converged over the past two weeks. Arsenal reached the EFL Cup final in mid-March, where both Timber and Eze sustained their injuries. The international break then saw several Arsenal players called up for national duty, with Rice and Saka both withdrawing from England squads, while Hincapie picked up a fresh muscle issue representing Ecuador.

This comes at a particularly bad time for Arsenal, who are pursuing success on multiple fronts. The Champions League tie against Sporting represents a crucial knockout stage fixture, while domestic challenges remain in the league and FA Cup. Arteta's frustration was evident in his comments about needing to "change immediately" the pattern of losing important players.

For FPL purposes, Arsenal assets have been among the most popular selections this season due to their consistent returns and favorable fixture runs. The uncertainty around Saka particularly has left many managers scrambling for alternatives or holding onto him hoping for a timely return.
