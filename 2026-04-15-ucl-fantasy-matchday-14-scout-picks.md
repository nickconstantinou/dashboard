# UCL Fantasy Matchday 14: The Scout Picks That Actually Matter

The Champions League quarter-final second legs arrive with an unusual problem: goalkeeper is a minefield. With fewer convincing options on Tuesday than most matchdays, budget strategy becomes everything—and the decisions made in net could define your week before a ball is kicked.

That's the real story here. Everything else flows from there.

## Goalkeepers: Go Cheap or Go Home

Matvei Safonov (€4.4m) is the standout budget play this round. PSG have kept clean sheets against both Chelsea and Liverpool in recent weeks, and their defensive structure looks genuinely solid heading into the second leg. At €4.4m, Safonov offers clean sheet upside without the salary commitment that limits flexibility elsewhere in your squad.

If Safonov feels like too much of a gamble, David Raya (€5.5m) at Arsenal is the reliable fallback. Arsenal have kept back-to-back Champions League clean sheets and continue to look like one of the most defensively sound sides in the competition. There's nothing flashy about Raya, but reliability has value when the alternatives are risky.

The key insight here is positional savings. The cheaper options free up budget for stronger plays in midfield and attack, where the real points lie this round.

## Defenders: Arsenal Tripling-Up Is No Longer Optional

The conventional wisdom has been to double up on Arsenal's defense. That thinking is outdated. Tripling up looks increasingly necessary given their consistency.

Gabriel Magalhães (€5.7m) and William Saliba (€5.9m) both offer more than clean sheet potential. Both carry genuine goal threat from set-pieces—Gabriel especially, given his heading ability on corners and free kicks. Even in matches where Arsenal are heavy favourites, that set-piece involvement provides a secondary route to returns that pure defenders lack. At their price points, the floor is high and the ceiling is underrated.

Geny Catamo (€5.4m) is a trickier case. Classified as a defender, he operates almost exclusively on the right wing for his team, giving him multiple angles to scoring and assisting points regardless of how the fixture plays out. If Arsenal keep a clean sheet, he benefits. If the game opens up, he benefits even more. At €5.4m, the floor is acceptable and the upside is significant.

For PSG, owning just one defender limits potential returns against their opponents. Achraf Hakimi (€5.9m) stands out as the most attractive option—he's contributed a goal or assist in four of his last five Champions League appearances. That's not a small sample quirk. It's a pattern.

On the budget end, Matteo Ruggeri (€4.3m) is worth a look despite a challenging fixture against Barcelona. He already demonstrated his attacking threat in the first leg, picking up an assist. At €4.3m, you're not paying for certainty—you're paying for a puncher's chance with a player who can hurt teams going forward.

## Midfielders: Liverpool at Anfield Demands Attention

Liverpool return to Anfield for the second leg, and the expectation is a stronger overall performance than what materialized in the first meeting. That changes the calculus on their attackers.

Dominik Szoboszlai (€7.0m) stands out as the most cost-effective route into Liverpool's attack. First impressions might suggest he's not a natural attacking pick. The numbers tell a different story: five goals and four assists in the Champions League so far. That's output that belongs in a much more expensive player. At €7.0m, you're getting premium production at a discount price, and with Anfield behind them, the conditions are favourable for another strong display.

## The Bigger Picture

Budget allocation matters more in quarter-final second legs than at almost any other point in the competition. The fixture dynamics—strong favourites, clean sheet odds, attacking potential—all stack in specific directions, and the smart play is to identify where the market is mispricing value.

Right now, that means going cheap in goal, loading up on Arsenal defenders with set-piece upside, targeting PSG's attacking full-backs, and finding Liverpool's most efficient attacking option at a reasonable price. The pieces connect. Build accordingly.
