FPL Team News + Predicted Line-ups

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

The deadline is approaching, and the decisions are getting harder.

Fantasy managers looking at this Gameweek's fixtures need to act fast. With pre-match press conferences this weekend set to clarify the fog around several key players, the final predicted line-ups will tighten—but for now, here's where the picture stands across the Premier League.

Arsenal vs Newcastle United

Mikel Arteta has confirmed that Bukayo Saka (Achilles) and Riccardo Calafiori (unspecified) are both likely to feature against Newcastle on Saturday. That's positive news for the 75% of managers who own each of them, though the uncertainty around their fitness until kickoff makes a late swap worthwhile for those on a wildcard or free hit.

Jurriën Timber (ankle) remains unavailable, as does the longer-term absentee Mikel Merino (foot), but neither was expected to displace established starters anyway.

The more interesting debate is tactical. Arsenal carved out more chances against Manchester City last weekend than they have against any other title rival in recent memory—a performance that suggests Arteta is unlikely to make sweeping changes. The suspicion is he opts for Ben White over Cristhian Mosquera at right-back, anticipating a more defensive Newcastle shape, possibly a 3-5-2 or 3-4-3, and wanting solidity over width.

The attack is where things get fluid. Noni Madueke was withdrawn at half-time at the Etihad, and even if Saka isn't ready to start, Arteta has options: Leandro Trossard, Max Dowman, or Gabriel Martinelli could come in. Martinelli's introduction at half-time last time out was notable—there's a path to him starting if the England winger doesn't make the cut.

There are also dissenting voices among supporters urging Arteta to push Martin Odegaard and Eberechi Eze into central roles and use Declan Rice as a lone six. But Arteta has started Martin Zubimendi in every league match bar one in 2025/26. It's a stretch to imagine that changes now, even against a side that may invite pressure.

Kai Havertz missed a gilt-edged chance at the Etihad but may have done enough to keep his place over Viktor Gyokeres in the No.9 role. That decision could prove decisive for the 28% of FPL managers holding the German.

Aston Villa vs Fulham

Unai Emery delivered a sparse press conference, but the picture is clearer than most. Boubacar Kamara (knee) is the only confirmed absentee. Emery reiterated what he's said for weeks: the Premier League is the priority. Even with Champions League qualification all but secured, expect a strong Villa side at Craven Cottage.

The main question mark is Ross Barkley, who continues to start in the league but is ineligible for European competition—a complication that could force rotation with one eye on the run-in, depending on how deep Villa's ambitions extend this season.

Ollie Watkins leads the line, and with Villa's attacking structure intact, he's a fixture worth trusting.
