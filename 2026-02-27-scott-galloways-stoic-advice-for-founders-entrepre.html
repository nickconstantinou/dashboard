Scott Galloway's Stoic Advice for Founders & Entrepreneurs | My First Million

In this episode, marketing professor and entrepreneur Scott Galloway shares his perspective on stoicism and how it applies to building businesses and living a fulfilling life. He explains that while many people are familiar with stoicism, they often struggle to understand how it practically applies to daily life. This mindset helps entrepreneurs focus their energy on what they can actually change rather than wasting energy on uncontrollable circumstances.

Galloway gets candid about his own struggles with social media, particularly Twitter. He expresses particular concern about social media's impact on teenagers, especially girls, warning that algorithms can quickly spiral users toward harmful content like extreme dieting or content normalizing depression and suicide.

The conversation takes a profound turn when Galloway discusses mortality and regret. He shares research from his NYU colleague Adam Alter, who studied people near the end of their lives in palliative care. The biggest regret? That people wished they'd been less hard on themselves‚Äîthey wished they'd forgiven themselves and allowed themselves to be happy. Galloway emphasizes that what truly matters isn't what Twitter thinks of you, but rather your relationship with your spouse and whether your kids are happy. His final takeaway is pragmatic yet compassionate: you'll be dead soon, so stop beating yourself up and squeeze as much joy from life as possible while being kind to yourself along the way.
