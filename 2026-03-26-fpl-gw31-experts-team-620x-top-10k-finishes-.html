FPL Gameweek 31: How Top All-Time Managers Are Navigating the Blank Gameweek

The Fantasy Premier League season is hurtling toward its most critical juncture, and for FPL managers across the globe, Gameweek 31 presents one of the season's most challenging puzzles yet. With multiple teams blanking this week due to cup competitions and fixture clashes, the majority of managers find themselves needing to make at least one transfer simply to field a complete XI. But what are the elite operators—the top all-time managers who consistently finish among the world's best—doing differently? In this comprehensive breakdown, we examine the strategies, transfers, and thought processes that separate the elite from the ordinary as the business end of the season unfolds.

Andrew Moore has seized the top spot among ranked experts after Gameweek 30, having delivered a impressive 64-point haul that vaulted him to 284th in the world rankings. For context, the average expert score last week sat at 59 points, while the global average across all FPL managers was a mere 43—meaning even many experienced managers struggled to navigate the fixture disruptions. Moore is joined in the top 10,000 by ten other experts, with a handful more sitting just one or two points outside that coveted threshold. The level of competition at the top has never been more intense, and the decisions made this gameweek could prove decisive in determining who finishes the season as a true elite manager.

The Blank Gameweek Dilemma: To Transfer or Not to Transfer?

The fundamental question facing every FPL manager this week is deceptively simple: should you make transfers or preserve your banked transfers for the lucrative double gameweeks that lie ahead? According to the analysis, approximately 80% of top experts are making at least one move this gameweek, while 20% are choosing to roll their transfers. The key insight here is that the optimal strategy depends heavily on your current squad composition and whether you're already able to field a full eleven players.

For those managers already blessed with eleven available players and no major upgrade opportunities, leaving the team unchanged emerges as the superior long-term play. The reasoning is straightforward: with double gameweeks 33 and 36 expected to be particularly valuable, plus a potential blank in gameweek 34, preserving transfers could prove transformative. For instance, the presenter explains that a transfer like switching Auditor to Anderson might yield only four additional points if Fulham keep a clean sheet and Sunderland concede, or potentially just one point if Fulham themselves concede. Rolling a transfer, by contrast, positions you ideally for the chaotic fixture swings ahead, where having maximum flexibility could mean the difference between a green arrow and a painful red.

However, many top managers find themselves in a different position entirely. Andrew Moore, for example, is currently fielding fewer than eleven players and must make at least one move. His specific solution involves swapping both Gündoğan and Rice to bring in Bowen and Wilson—a double transfer that addresses his immediate squad shortage while targeting players with favorable fixtures.

The Most Bought Players: Newcastle and Fulham Dominate

When examining the transfer activity among elite managers, the data reveals a fascinating pattern: Newcastle United players dominate the buy column, with two of the top three most transferred-in assets coming from St. James' Park. Chiao leads the way at 33% of experts acquiring his services, followed by Gordon at 26%. The remaining spots in the top five are filled by Wilson, Anderson, and Bowen.

On the defensive side, Fulham and Newcastle emerge as the only teams worth seriously targeting this gameweek based on clean sheet probability. The presenter notes that Fulham's defensive options make theoretical sense given approximately 4% better clean sheet odds compared to other teams, though the analysis quickly becomes more nuanced when examining individual player potential.

At Fulham, Anderson stands out as the clear best pick, having accumulated joint-most goals among defenders this season with four strikes—edging even Gabriel in this department. However, alternatives like Tete and Bassey present a different proposition. The data reveals that these players hit Defcons only half as frequently as Anderson, with Bassey managing just 7.8 Defcons per 90 minutes. Beyond defensive contributions, Anderson also offers significant goal threat from set pieces and corners, making him a genuinely multi-dimensional FPL asset.

Chiao, meanwhile, represents a slightly more robust selection depending on your chip strategy. The presenter explains that while Fulham face Burnley at home, their subsequent fixtures include tough away trips to Liverpool and Brentford with no guaranteed double gameweek opportunity. Chiao, by contrast, has favorable fixtures beyond gameweek 31—Crystal Palace away and Bournemouth at home—making him playable well into the double gameweek period where Fulham have a strong chance of doubling.

The Most Sold Players: Injuries and Blank Fixtures

The sell column tells an equally predictable but no less important story. Players without fixtures or those carrying injuries dominate the transfer-out activity, with Rice, Timber, Harland, Semenyo, Muñóz, Sarr, Chalaobah, Gustavo Nunes, Dorgu, and Tarahski among the notable exits. These departures reflect the harsh reality of navigating a blank gameweek: premium assets without fixtures cannot contribute, and managers are ruthless in their pursuit of active players.

Perhaps most concerning is the situation surrounding Chilwell, who was stretchered off during Champions League action midweek. The presenter suggests this doesn't bode well for his availability in Gameweek 31, potentially ruling him out for an extended period. Similarly, Salah requested substitution during Liverpool's commanding 4-0 victory over Galatasaray—a game where Liverpool appeared back to their devastating best, scoring four goals and creating numerous chances.

The Egyptian's performance was characteristically complicated: he managed one goal and one assist while also missing a penalty, a sequence that manager Slot described cryptically as Salah feeling "something." The presenter interprets this as genuinely ambiguous regarding his fitness for the weekend, making Salah a genuine captaincy risk despite his otherwise exceptional underlying statistics.

Chip Strategy: The Million-Pound Decision

Perhaps the most valuable strategic discussion in the video centers on chip deployment strategy for the run-in. With no active chips across the entire expert league this gameweek—a deliberate choice reflecting the relative navigability of a single blank week—the real decisions lie ahead.

The presenter outlines two competing schools of thought. The first, and currently more popular, involves playing the Wildcard in gameweek 32, Bench Boost in 33, and taking a hit in 34. The alternative strategy gaining traction is to save your Wildcard entirely, instead deploying a Free Hit in the double gameweek 33, Wildcarding in 35, and using Bench Boost in 36.

FPL Fade, whose insights are shared in the video, strongly advocates for the second approach. His reasoning is compelling: if you don't already own many players from the "big teams" likely to blank in gameweek 34 (Manchester City, Arsenal, Chelsea), then the Wildcard 32 route becomes less attractive. Filling five or six squad spots with players from Brighton, Newcastle, and Burnley specifically for a Bench Boost in 33, only to spend the rest of the season trying to fix your team composition, represents a significant opportunity cost.

FPL Fade's analysis extends to the broader fixture picture. He notes that two key events will dramatically reshape the landscape: the Crystal Palace Europa Conference League fixture and the Manchester City versus Liverpool FA Cup tie. If Palace progress in Europe, the calendar remains congested, but if they exit, the fixture list opens up significantly. Most intriguingly, if Palace go out and both they and Manchester City progress in their respective competitions, the City-Palace fixture could move forward from gameweek 36 to as early as gameweek 32, creating consecutive double gameweeks in 32 and 33 for managers with exposure to both sides.

Captaincy: Bruno Fernandes Reigns Supreme

When it comes to captain selection for this blank gameweek, the verdict is overwhelming: Bruno Fernandes commands a staggering 91% of expert captain votes, with Wilson a distant second at 4%, followed by Gordon at 2%, and Palmer, Salah, and Thiago splitting the remaining 1% each.

The case for Bruno is built on extraordinary season-long production. He leads all players with ten double-digit returns this season—Harland, his closest competitor, has managed just nine. His creative numbers are equally staggering: 24 big chances created, nine more than any other player in the league. Beyond playmaking, Bruno carries genuine direct goal threat, ranking in the top five for shots taken among midfielders, behind only Olise, Semenyo, Rogers, and Summerville. His 18.02 expected goal involvement ranks top of all midfielders this season, cementing his status as the season's most complete FPL asset.

For managers specifically targeting the Burnley fixture, Wilson emerges as the most logical alternative, though the presenter cautions that Fulham's striker rotation means Råsund would likely start and face early substitution, limiting his ceiling unless he scores quickly.

Key Takeaways

First, bank your transfers if your squad allows. With double gameweeks 33 and 36 offering potentially transformative opportunities, preserving your wildcard and free hit chips for those moments represents the highest expected value strategy. Only make transfers this week if you genuinely cannot field eleven players.

Second, target Newcastle and Fulham assets strategically. Chiao and Anderson emerge as the most complete options from these two teams, offering defensive returns, goal threat, and fixtures that extend beyond gameweek 31. Gordon and Wilson provide premium attacking alternatives with similar fixture runs.

Third, Bruno Fernandes is untouchable for captaincy. His 91% ownership among experts tells only part of the story. His underlying numbers—ten double-digit returns, 24 big chances created, 18.02 expected goal involvement—establish him as the season's most valuable FPL asset regardless of fixture.

Fourth, consider the alternative chip path carefully. The Free Hit 33, Wildcard 35, Bench Boost 36 strategy may outperform the traditional Wildcard 32, Bench Boost 33, hit 34 approach, particularly for managers without heavy exposure to the big teams likely to blank in gameweek 34.

Fifth, monitor injury news closely. Salah's substitution request and Chilwell's stretcher exit represent genuine risks that could dramatically reshape your team structure. The deadline is approaching, and last-minute information could prove decisive.
