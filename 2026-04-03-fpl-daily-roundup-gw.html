FPL Gameweek Roundup: DGW33 Hype Builds as Arsenal Crisis Deepens

April 3, 2026

---

The Injury Landscape: Gunners in Crisis Mode

The Fantasy Premier League community faces a familiar headache this week as Arsenal players dominate the casualty list heading into a blank gameweek. Gabriel, the Brazilian centre-back who has been an absolute machine for FPL managers this season, is nursing a knee injury that gives him a 75% chance of featuring. At £7.2m with form readings of 10.0 and 173 season points already banked, his availability—or lack thereof—could dictate whether thousands of managers wake up to a goose egg on Saturday.

Joining Gabriel in the treatment room are two more Arsenal defenders with the same 75% participation probability. Jurriën Timber, who has been sensational at £6.3m with 149 points and excellent underlying metrics, carries an ankle problem that Arteta will likely manage conservatively given the blank. William Saliba, priced at £6.1m, is also nursing an ankle complaint that leaves him touch and go. Piero Hincapie rounds out the Gunners' injury concerns at £5.1m with a muscle issue.

Further up the pitch, Bukayo Saka has taken a knock and is listed at 75% to play. The Arsenal talisman at £9.8m has underperformed his underlying numbers this season with only 133 points, but managers will be desperate for any attacking returns if he does make it to the pitch.

Crystal Palace, another blanking side this week, have Adam Wharton flagged with a knock at 75% availability, while Chris Richards (£4.4m) remains fit and available. Iliman Ndiaye (£6.2m) has a foot injury at 75% for Everton.

The Liverpool injury situation has been occupying minds, with Fantasy Football Scout running dedicated coverage on Alisson and the Reds' fitness picture ahead of their double fixture run.

---

The Gameweek Landscape: Blank GW31 Locks In

Four teams are confirmed sitting out Gameweek 31: Arsenal, Crystal Palace, Manchester City, and Wolves. This immediately renders any investment in Gabriel, Saka, David Raya, João Victor, or Chris Richards somewhat fraught unless managers have sufficient squad depth to carry them through.

The more tantalising prospect comes from Gameweek 33, where Fantasy Football Scout has confirmed a double gameweek involving up to eight teams. This is massive news for chip strategy, though it's worth noting that the official FPL API shows no doubles confirmed through GW38 at time of writing. FFS are clearly tracking something in the fixture calendar that hasn't yet filtered through to the API, so managers should treat GW33 as speculative but highly credible.

Beyond that, the fixture density remains remarkably stable. GW32 through GW36 all show 20 confirmed fixtures each, providing consistent scheduling across the run-in. The key for managers is identifying which teams play twice in GW33 before committing to any aggressive chip plays.

Captain Recommendations: Who's Rising to the Top

With Bruno Fernandes (£10.3m) sitting at the summit of the form table with a remarkable 10.3 rating and 189 season points, the Manchester United captain is making a compelling case for the armband. His 44.5% ownership means he remains template despite the premium price tag, and his underlying numbers continue to justify the faith. When a player is producing at this rate while his team sits in contention for European qualification, the case for captaincy practically makes itself.

Gabriel would be the obvious alternative if fit, with his 10.0 form reading and set-piece involvement making him a genuine premium option at £7.2m. But with his participation in serious doubt, managers face a gamble that many will be unwilling to take on a blank gameweek.

Anthony Gordon (£7.4m) at Newcastle continues to fly under the radar at just 7.5% ownership despite a 9.3 form rating. With favourable fixtures approaching and the Magpies pushing for Champions League qualification, those who jumped off the template may find themselves well-positioned for a differential explosion.

João Pedro (£7.8m) at Chelsea leads a significant 50.3% of squads, making him arguably the closest thing to a must-have at his price point. His 164 points represent exceptional value, and Chelsea's run of fixtures makes him a strong captaincy candidate whenever they have favourable matchups.

Transfer Strategy: The Price Points Worth Targeting

With the GW31 blank forcing decisions on Arsenal assets, many managers are pivoting toward Bournemouth assets after watching their season unfold. Ryan Christie (£4.9m) sits at a modest 0.1% ownership despite a 7.0 form rating, suggesting significant differential potential if the Cherries' fixture run cooperates. At under £5m, he represents pure price enabler while offering attacking upside under Andoni Iraola's ambitious system.

Adrien Truffert (£4.7m) at Bournemouth has been quietly excellent with 122 season points and a 7.5 form rating. At 3.3% ownership, he's precisely the kind of low-owned asset that can make the difference in mini-leagues. The French full-back's attacking contributions from left-back have been consistent, and Bournemouth's remaining schedule offers plenty of opportunity for clean sheet points.

For those shedding Arsenal assets, Michael Kayode (£4.5m) at Brentford presents an interesting budget option with 92 points already banked and a 7.0 form reading. Brentford under Keith Andrews have shown enough defensive solidity to suggest they're worth considering at the right price point, and at £4.5m, Kayode won't break any budget constraints.

Dominik Szoboszlai (£7.0m) at Liverpool continues to offer solid returns at a price point that hasn't quite caught fire with the community. His 7.0 form and 123 season points represent reasonable value, and Liverpool's push for the title under Arne Slot should provide plenty of goalscoring opportunities for their midfield creators.

Elliot Anderson (£5.5m) at Nottingham Forest has emerged as a genuine bargain at his price, with 138 season points and a 6.3 form rating. Forest under Vítor Pereira have been one of the surprise packages of the season, and Anderson's attacking output from midfield has been central to their success.

Players to Avoid: The Warning Flags

Managers should exercise considerable caution with any Arsenal player this week given the combination of the blank gameweek and their injury list. Paying £10.3m for Bruno Fernandes only to see him blank due to Manchester United's own fixture situation would be particularly painful.

Manchester City assets look unattractive for different reasons. Despite their quality, City have been erratic under Pep Guardiola this season, and the blank in GW31 removes any immediate urgency to invest. Bernardo Mota Veiga de Carvalho e Silva (£6.2m) and Rodri (£6.3m) remain on the radar for their quality but haven't produced the consistent returns that justify the price tags for managers working within tight budget constraints.

Wolves assets should be avoided entirely this week given their blank, and there are lingering concerns about their attacking output even when they do return to action. João Victor at £5.3m has decent form but Wolves have been inconsistent performers that make them a rotation risk rather than a reliable source of returns.

Chip Strategy: The GW33 Opportunity Beckons

The confirmed (per FFS) or highly predicted (given the contradiction with the FPL API) double gameweek for eight teams in GW33 represents the most significant chip opportunity remaining in the season. Managers who have been saving their wildcard should be mapping out exactly which teams are likely to play twice before making any moves.

The logic is straightforward: loading up on players from the teams with confirmed doubles in GW33, then benching them in the blank weeks surrounding it, allows managers to maximise returns from a concentrated fixture run. The free hit chip becomes particularly valuable in either GW31 (to navigate the blank without sacrificing long-term structure) or GW34 (to navigate the post-double blank).

The bench boost, by contrast, requires patience. With full fixture density confirmed through GW36, managers have plenty of runway to activate that chip without needing to force it prematurely. The ideal scenario would see the bench boost deployed in a week where all fifteen players have viable fixtures, allowing maximum points from the bench itself.

For those still holding the free hit, GW31 presents the most obvious use case given Arsenal, Palace, City, and Wolves all blanking. However, any manager with sufficient squad depth to navigate the blank without burning their chip may want to save it for greater flexibility later in the season.

Where the Community Stands Divided

The debate over Arsenal assets encapsulates the fundamental tension facing FPL managers right now. Some analysts argue that the Gunners' injuries are being managed conservatively and that all three key defenders will play through any discomfort to feature in crucial fixtures. Others point out that with Arsenal blanking this week anyway, there's no urgency to rush anyone back, and Arteta will use the international break as an opportunity to nurse his walking wounded back to full fitness.

The João Pedro ownership question also divides opinion. At 50.3%, he's approaching template territory but not quite there. The argument for owning him is his consistency and Chelsea's favourable run; the argument against is that everyone already owns him, making him a poor differentiator in mini-leagues. This is the eternal FPL tension between safety and edge.

The Az video from FPL Blackbox, though focused on pre-season reflections rather than current gameweek strategy, offers a broader lesson about resisting the template without proper justification. His admission that he has been "too slow" to react to premium picks like Salah in recent seasons should resonate with managers who find themselves perpetually behind the curve.

Three Key Actions for the Week

First, lock in your GW31 blank strategy immediately. With Arsenal, Crystal Palace, Manchester City, and Wolves all blanking, managers cannot afford to enter the gameweek with too many passengers. If you don't have the free hit chip available, ensure your starting XI features no more than a handful of players from those four clubs, and accept that some dead money in your squad is inevitable this week.

Second, prepare for GW33 by identifying the eight teams playing twice. Fantasy Football Scout's confirmation of a double gameweek involving up to eight teams means the wildcard—if still available—should be deployed to maximise exposure to those fixture-rich sides. Even managers who have already used their wildcard can begin the process of trimming unwanted assets and building toward a concentrated GW33 attack. The key teams to watch are those with favourable underlying numbers who may have escaped the public consciousness, and those with confirmed double fixtures deserve maximum investment.

Third, don't panic-sell injured Arsenal assets. Gabriel, Timber, Saliba, and Saka are all listed at 75% availability, which means they have a reasonable chance of playing this week despite their knocks. Panic-selling them for immediate replacements risks losing value on assets that will almost certainly return to full fitness after the international break. If you have sufficient squad depth to bench them through the blank, that patience is likely to be rewarded when Arsenal return with fixtures that make their quality assets must-owns once again.

The season is entering its decisive phase, and every transfer decision carries amplified weight. The blank gameweek creates short-term friction, but the emerging double gameweek opportunity in GW33 suggests the best may be yet to come for managers willing to plan ahead.
