# The Full Story

Fantasy Premier League managers navigating the 2025/26 season are benefiting from a transformed defensive landscape, according to an analysis from Fantasy Football Scout. The introduction of DefCon (defensive contribution) points has fundamentally shifted how fantasy managers evaluate defenders, moving beyond the traditional focus on clean sheets and occasional attacking returns. The article, published March 31, 2026, examines which defenders represent the best all-round assets as the season reaches its crucial final stretch.

The current campaign has seen elevated clean sheet numbers compared to recent seasons, with the rate standing at 5.21 per 10-match Gameweek—up from 4.68 and 4.13 in the previous two seasons respectively. This increase provides more consistent baseline returns for defensive picks, making clean sheet potential a more reliable factor in team selection.

Several teams have distinguished themselves in defensive solidity over the full season, with Arsenal, Brighton and Hove Albion, Manchester City, and Manchester United leading the way in preventing goals, attempts, shots on target, and big chances. Arsenal's defensive unit has been particularly impressive. However, Manchester United's position is puzzling—despite generally strong defensive metrics across the season, the Red Devils have managed only five clean sheets, with only the season's bottom three teams recording fewer shutouts.

Recent form tells an equally compelling story. Over the last six matches, Brighton, Leeds United, Arsenal, and Manchester City have formed the tightest backlines. Brighton has been exceptional, conceding just four goals and allowing only five big chances in this period, while posting the best expected goals conceded (xGC) figure of 5.50. Only Leeds has conceded fewer shots on target. Notably, while Bournemouth has also conceded just four goals recently, their xGC of 11.21 suggests significant positive variance—meaning their actual results have far outpaced underlying defensive performance and regression may be coming.

In terms of attacking returns, a group of players has accumulated the most goals and assists: Jurrien Timber (£6.3m), Reece James (£5.6m), Matheus Nunes (£5.3m), Nico O'Reilly (£5.0m), Gabriel Magalhaes (£7.2m), Diogo Dalot (£4.6m), and Nordi Mukiele (£4.5m). West Ham United has been identified as the unluckiest side by xGC delta (+6.87), though three clean sheets in seven matches suggests their fortunes may be turning.

# Why This Matters

The DefCon points system represents a significant evolution in FPL strategy. For the first time, managers have granular data on defensive contributions that go beyond the binary outcomes of clean sheets and goals. This means the difference between a defender who is solid but unspectacular versus one who actively disrupts opposition attacks now carries fantasy implications.

For FPL managers, this analysis is particularly valuable at this stage of the season. With limited Gameweeks remaining, every transfer decision carries amplified weight. Identifying defenders who combine clean sheet potential with attacking returns—and now defensive contribution points—can provide the marginal gains needed to climb rankings or hold onto valuable positions.

The data also highlights the growing importance of expected metrics over raw results. Teams like Bournemouth may appear attractive based on recent clean sheets, but their underlying numbers suggest their form is unsustainable. Conversely, teams like West Ham, despite poor actual results, may present differential value if their defensive work begins converting into returns.

# Context & Background

This analysis follows an earlier piece examining the best all-round midfielders in FPL, using the same methodology. The site uses Members Area statistics and DefCon data to move beyond surface-level metrics. DefCon points were introduced to reward defenders for activities that don't always result in clean sheets but nonetheless represent valuable defensive work—blocks, interceptions, tackles in dangerous areas, and pressure applied to opposition attackers.

The timing of this analysis, published March 31, 2026, places it squarely in the business end of the 2025/26 season when title races, European qualification battles, and relegation concerns all influence team selection and rotation risks.
