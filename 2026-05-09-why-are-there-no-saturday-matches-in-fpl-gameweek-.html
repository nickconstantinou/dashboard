Why Are There No Saturday Matches in FPL Gameweek 37?

The FA Cup Final Agreement

Fantasy Premier League managers who checked their gameweek 37 lineups this week may have noticed a complete absence of Saturday fixtures. This situation exists because the FA Cup final is taking place on the same day. The Premier League and the Football Association (FA) have an agreement whereby the FA Cup final gets "exclusivity" on the day it is contested. As a result, no Premier League matches are scheduled for Saturday during Gameweek 37. Typically, there is also no Friday night Premier League fixture on FA Cup weekend, as this also falls under the agreement between the FA and the Premier League.

Affected Fixtures

Several high-profile Gameweek 37 fixtures have been rescheduled as a direct consequence of this arrangement. Manchester City and Chelsea, the FA Cup finalists, have had their Gameweek 37 fixtures against Bournemouth and Tottenham Hotspur pushed back to Tuesday. This means that Fantasy managers holding players from these teams will need to adjust their strategies accordingly, as these matches will now fall outside the original Gameweek 37 window.

Aston Villa's Special Circumstance

Not all clubs were subject to the standard postponement arrangement. Aston Villa have been permitted to face Liverpool early in Gameweek 37 to help them prepare for the UEFA Europa League final the following week. This exception recognizes Villa's European commitments and allows them adequate preparation time for their most important match of the season.

Looking Ahead

There'll be no more Premier League fixtures on Saturday for the rest of the season. All 10 Gameweek 38 matches kick off at 16:00 BST on Sunday 24 May, bringing the season to a simultaneous conclusion across all clubs. Fantasy managers should plan their final transfers and captaincy choices with these compressed timelines in mind, particularly for those with assets in the affected teams.
