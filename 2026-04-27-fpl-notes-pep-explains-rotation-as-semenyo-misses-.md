# FPL Notes: Guardiola Explains Rotation as Semenyo Misses FA Cup Semi-Final

## Manchester City's FA Cup Semi-Final: A Heavily Rotated Display

Manchester City's FA Cup semi-final against Southampton saw Pep Guardiola employ significant squad rotation. The manager effectively sent out his B team, making eight changes from the previous Premier League match, with only three players keeping their places from the win over Burnley. This approach reflected the demanding fixture schedule facing City across multiple competitions.

## Guardiola's Reasoning for Rotation

Guardiola explained that rotation was necessary because players cannot maintain the same intensity when playing every three days. He stated that it would not be possible to use the same players under such demanding conditions. The manager hinted that this rotation policy may not end there this season, suggesting further squad management could be required due to ongoing fixture congestion.

## Key Player Performances and Management

Phil Foden (£8.0m) struggled as one of City's two attacking midfield positions and was substituted before the hour mark. Meanwhile, Erling Haaland (£14.5m) and Nico O'Reilly (£5.1m) only had late cameo appearances from the bench, indicating they were preserved for the Premier League run-in.

## Semenyo Absent from Squad

Antoine Semenyo (£8.2m) was not included in the matchday squad for the FA Cup semi-final against Southampton. Fantasy managers holding the midfielder will have been frustrated by his absence, though this development must be monitored for injury updates.

## Match Outcome and Late Impact

Despite the changes, the match saw late drama. Jeremy Doku made an impact when his deflected shot restored parity after Southampton had taken a shock 79th-minute lead. Nico Gonzalez (£5.9m) then secured victory with a powerful long-range effort to win the tie for City.

## Future Squad Implications

Guardiola suggested that Rayan Ait-Nouri (£5.7m) would probably not be in the first-choice XI once Rodri (£6.3m) returns to fitness. However, for Fantasy Premier League purposes, the City regulars will likely all return to the starting XI in Gameweek 35, presenting potential value opportunities for managers preparing for the run-in.
