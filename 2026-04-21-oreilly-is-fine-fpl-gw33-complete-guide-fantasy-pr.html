FPL Gameweek 33 Complete Guide: Everything You Need to Know About the Biggest Double Gameweek of the Season

The Most Critical Week of the FPL Calendar Has Arrived

For Fantasy Premier League managers, Gameweek 33 represents what FPL Harry describes as "probably the biggest week of the entire season for people playing a chip, whether it's the wildcard, the free hit, the bench boost, or potentially the triple captain this week." With six teams facing double fixtures and the Premier League title race hurtling toward its climax, this Gameweek presents both extraordinary opportunities and nerve-wracking decisions for the millions of managers navigating their squads toward the season's finish line.

The week has been dominated by one particular storyline: the fitness of Nico O'Reilly at Manchester City. After the young defender went off injured against Chelsea last weekend, the FPL community held its breath awaiting updates. That anxiety can now be laid to rest, at least partially, following Friday's Manchester City press conference where manager Pep Guardiola confirmed O'Reilly is "fine" and available for selection. This represents a significant boost for managers who have invested in the City defender, particularly those utilizing free hit or wildcard chips this week.

However, the title race implications extend far beyond individual player fitness. With Manchester City hosting Arsenal in what Guardiola himself described as a potential title-deciding fixture, the entire complexion of Gameweek 33 could shift dramatically depending on Saturday's result. Managers face the unenviable task of planning for scenarios they won't know the outcome of before their deadline passes—a challenge that demands careful portfolio construction and strategic chip deployment.

The Six Teams With Double Gameweeks: Projected Goals Analysis

Gameweek 33 features six teams blessed with double fixtures: Manchester City, Chelsea, Bournemouth, Brighton, Leeds United, and Burnley. Understanding which attacks offer the best expected returns separates elite managers from the pack, and the projections have shifted meaningfully since the week's beginning.

Manchester City leads the projected goals table with an expected 4.4 goals across their two fixtures. This figure has increased slightly as the week progressed, driven by improved expectations for their match against Arsenal—remarkably, the model suggests City are almost predicted to score two goals against what has been considered one of the league's meanest defensive units this season. Their second fixture against Burnley at Turf Moor represents what most consider a favorable matchup, though the implications of the Arsenal result could affect squad rotation.

Chelsea sit second with 3.2 projected goals, holding steady from earlier estimates. The fixtures present an interesting split: home to Manchester United followed by a trip to Brighton. However, there's growing concern among analysts about Chelsea's away fixture. The odds have "started to swing in favor of Brighton scoring more goals" in that matchup, potentially reducing Chelsea's clean sheet prospects significantly.

Bournemouth remain third for projected goals at 3.1, with the chances distributed unevenly across their fixtures. Their home game against Leeds represents the most favorable attacking opportunity, while their away trip to Newcastle presents considerably stiffer opposition. The key consideration for Bournemouth assets revolves around individual player potential rather than clean sheet floors.

Brighton have emerged as a fascinating option, having "overtaken Leeds" in projected terms as the week progressed. Their total of just over three goals across fixtures against Tottenham and Chelsea represents a notable increase from earlier predictions. This improvement reflects both Brighton's recent form and concerns about their opponents' defensive vulnerabilities. The Seagulls are "becoming more favorites with the way that their playing and their two opponents are playing," suggesting their attack may be undervalued in many draft teams.

Leeds United complete the upper tier, with their projections heavily weighted toward their home fixture against Wolves. Away at Bournemouth represents a more challenging proposition, which complicates decisions around Leeds defense. FPL Harry notes that "if you don't think they keep a clean sheet away at home to Wolves, it may not be worth going as much onto the Leeds defense as other people are with lots of double or potentially triple ups."

Burnley sit in sixth position, with their double against Manchester City and Leeds representing the week's most difficult fixture set.

Defensive Projections: Clean Sheet Percentages Across the Double

Beyond attacking returns, clean sheet probabilities offer crucial guidance for managers deciding which defenders to prioritize this week.

Manchester City dominate the clean sheet projections with a 62% chance of keeping at least one clean sheet across their double. This figure has actually increased slightly from earlier in the week, suggesting the model views their fixtures—even against Arsenal—as more favorable than initially anticipated. For managers seeking defensive coverage, City defenders offer the highest probability floor, though their cost implications force difficult squad construction decisions.

Leeds United claim second position at 52%, though this figure masks significant fixture-by-fixture variation. The 40% home clean sheet probability against Wolves contrasts sharply with just 22% away at Bournemouth, creating a scenario where "if you don't think they keep a clean sheet away at home to Wolves, it may not be worth going as much onto the Leeds defense as other people are with lots of double or potentially triple ups." The concentrated exposure to that first fixture represents both an opportunity and a risk.

Brighton have climbed to third with a 41% clean sheet probability across their double, benefiting from the deteriorating odds for Chelsea keeping sheets away at the Amex Stadium. Chelsea themselves sit at 39%, a figure that will concern managers heavily invested in their defense. Bournemouth occupy lower positions due to their "game away at Newcastle being very low at a 15% chance of clean sheet," dragging down their overall double-gameweek clean sheet probability despite favorable odds for their home fixture against Leeds.

The analysis emphasizes that pure clean sheet percentages shouldn't drive decisions in isolation. Players offering "potential attacking threat and particularly defcon" status warrant extra consideration. For example, Bournemouth assets like Haaland and Senesi remain compelling picks "if they hit defcon in both games," even without favorable clean sheet odds.

Captaincy Conundrums: The Haaland Dilemma and Differential Alternatives

Few decisions in FPL generate more anxiety than captain selection in pivotal double Gameweeks, and Gameweek 33 presents an especially complex scenario. At the center of the debate stands Erling Haaland, Manchester City's prolific Norwegian striker who remains the default choice for the vast majority of managers.

The case for Haaland rests on several pillars. He leads all players in probability of a goal or assist and is predicted to be the highest scoring player across the double. Despite concerns about recent open-play form—he has managed "one open play goal in the Premier League in the last 10 matches with a couple of penalties in there"—he recently scored a hat-trick in the FA Cup against Liverpool, demonstrating that his finishing ability remains undiminished. Crucially, FPL Harry emphasizes that "Haaland doesn't distinguish between the competitions. He's not more likely to score in the FA Cup than he is in the Premier League." The model projects Manchester City to score 4.4 goals across the double, and Haaland's expected share of those returns makes him the statistical captaincy leader.

The case against Haaland centers on ownership and the mathematical implications of captain selection. In FPL Harry's YouTube poll, Haaland received 80% of the captaincy vote, with significant portions of managers planning to triple captain him. This creates a scenario where differential captaincy becomes mathematically attractive—if everyone captains Haaland and he delivers, everyone gains equally. Going against the consensus with a successful differential captain could provide the point differential needed to climb ranks.

Cole Palmer emerges as the primary alternative for managers seeking to "go against it." Chelsea face Manchester United in Gameweek 33, with United facing a defensive crisis. Martínez and Maguire are suspended, while Kobbie Mainoo is "likely to be without... which massively impacted them" during their match against Leeds on Monday night. This depleted backline significantly improves Chelsea's attacking prospects, potentially elevating Palmer's goal-scoring expectations.

Danny Welbeck represents an intriguing differential that FPL Harry admits he's "getting closer to thinking" about. The Brighton striker offers several advantages: "His form is pretty good. The minutes look right. The data is definitely there." With Brighton's fixtures against Tottenham and Chelsea presenting favorable attacking opportunities, Welbeck could outperform his ownership percentage substantially if he starts both games.

João Pedro offers another differential pathway, particularly given that his "actual goal scoring form recently is probably better than Palmer once you exclude penalties." With Brighton climbing the projected goals table and facing potentially vulnerable opponents, the Brazilian could deliver explosive returns at low ownership.

The critical consideration, FPL Harry notes, is that "if you go against him [Haaland], you're effectively needing him to blank or your Gameweek could be almost over." The risk-reward calculation depends heavily on each manager's rank, overall strategy, and risk tolerance. For those chasing leaders, Haaland remains the safer play. For those in commanding positions seeking to extend advantages, differential captaincy offers the ceiling needed to maintain leads.

Press Conference Revelations: Manchester City's Title Race Calculus

The Friday press conference from Manchester City provided crucial information beyond the O'Reilly fitness update. Guardiola's comments about the significance of the Arsenal fixture revealed the title race's precarious mathematics.

When asked if the match "feels like a final," Guardiola's response carried significant weight: "If we lose this game, it will basically be the title race is over for them [Arsenal]. However, if they win or draw, they still have an opportunity." This assessment reflects the current standings: Arsenal sit six points ahead of City with a game in hand against Crystal Palace still to be rescheduled. A City win would narrow the gap to three points with that game in hand remaining. A draw preserves Arsenal's advantage. A City loss would effectively hand Arsenal their first league title in two decades.

These implications create uncertainty for FPL purposes. If City win or draw against Arsenal, FPL Harry expects "I don't expect there to be much rotation against Burnley" in the subsequent fixture. However, the scenario where City lose changes the calculus entirely. Guardiola might prioritize the FA Cup fixture against Southampton at the weekend, potentially resting key players against Burnley to ensure freshness for a cup competition the club desperately wants to win.

Southampton's current form adds another layer of complexity. The Championship leaders, who "look incredible at the moment," could provide stiffer resistance than a simple Premier League versus Championship comparison suggests. This means the FA Cup fixture isn't the guaranteed rotation opportunity some might assume.

The Palace rescheduling adds final uncertainty. "There's a small chance we might get it this afternoon now that Palace are through to the next round of Europe last night on Thursday." An announcement on this rescheduled fixture could arrive at any moment, potentially affecting City assets' long-term value.

For managers, this uncertainty emphasizes the importance of not over-committing to City players based purely on the double. Having coverage across multiple double-gameweek teams provides resilience against rotation scenarios that won't be resolved before Saturday's deadline.

Key Strategic Takeaways for Gameweek 33

First, prioritize Manchester City attackers but construct your squad to absorb potential rotation. The 4.4 projected goals and 62% clean sheet probability make City the week's standout team. However, the Arsenal result introduces genuine rotation risk for the Burnley fixture. Building squad depth or holding players on your bench who could activate if rotation occurs provides important downside protection.

Second, consider Brighton as an undervalued double-gameweek asset. Their projected goals have increased throughout the week, and they now sit above Leeds in the rankings. With favorable fixture splits and attacking form that has attracted model attention, Brighton's assets may be underweighted in many draft teams. Welbeck specifically offers differential captaincy potential at manageable ownership.

Third, treat Chelsea's away fixture against Brighton with caution. The odds have shifted against Chelsea keeping clean sheets in that matchup, and their overall defensive prospects have decreased slightly. While Palmer remains a viable captaincy alternative to Haaland, the fixture split doesn't support heavy defensive investment in Chelsea players.

Fourth, concentrate Leeds defensive exposure toward their home fixture. With 40% clean sheet probability against Wolves compared to just 22% away at Bournemouth, the value concentrates in the first fixture. Managers considering triple-ups on Leeds defense should plan accordingly, potentially benching assets if the first fixture doesn't deliver the expected returns.

Fifth, differential captaincy requires conviction and rank-appropriate strategy. Going against Haaland means "needing him to blank" for the differential to pay off. This strategy makes more sense for managers in commanding positions seeking to extend leads than for those chasing the pack, where Haaland's floor provides essential security. Players like Palmer, Welbeck, João Pedro, and Semenyo offer viable alternative captaincy routes for those willing to accept the additional variance.
