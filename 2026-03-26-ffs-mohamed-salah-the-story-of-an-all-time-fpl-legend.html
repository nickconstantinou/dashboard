Mohamed Salah: The story of an all-time FPL legend

📖 Read original on Fantasy Football Scout →

The Full Story

Mohamed Salah's illustrious chapter with Liverpool and the Fantasy Premier League is drawing to a close following Tuesday's announcement that the Egyptian king will leave Anfield at the end of the 2025/26 season. At 33 years old, Salah departs as the undisputed greatest FPL player of all time, having accumulated 2,216 total points across his memorable Liverpool tenure—a run that transformed the fantasy game and set standards that may never be matched.

The journey from Premier League also-ran to FPL deity began when Liverpool manager Jurgen Klopp brought Salah back to England for an initial fee of £34m following his remarkable redemption arc at AS Roma. The 564 league minutes Salah managed at Chelsea during the 2013/14 and 2014/15 seasons yielded just 34 FPL points, with a goal against Arsenal and a goal-plus-assist against Stoke City—the barest hint of the devastation to come. Priced at a mere £9.0m at the start of his Liverpool career in 2017, Salah's FPL trajectory would become the most spectacular in the game's history.

His Liverpool years produced a statistical record that reads like fiction: 191 goals, 104 assists, and 232 bonus points across eight full seasons. Salah sits third on the all-time FPL points leaderboard behind only Wayne Rooney (2,338) and Frank Lampard (2,318)—both of whom accumulated their totals over far longer careers with far more gameweeks available. The Egyptian has never finished below 211 points in any campaign, a consistency remarkable even by his stratospheric standards. His 2024/25 season of 344 points (29 goals, 18 assists across 18 hauls of 10+ points) and 2017/18 season of 303 points (32 goals, 12 assists with 15 such hauls) stand as the only two instances of a player breaking the 300-point barrier in FPL history.

Why This Matters

For the millions of FPL managers who have built their seasons around Salah's devastating consistency, his departure represents the end of an era that fundamentally changed how the game is played. Salah didn't just accumulate points—he created an entirely new strategic paradigm where building a team around a single premium asset became not just viable but essential. His remarkable points-per-90 average of 7.47 across his Liverpool career, peaking at 9.19 last season, meant that every week, regardless of fixture difficulty or form, managers could rely on Salah as their foundation.

The FPL community is losing more than a player; it's losing a strategic constant around which entire philosophies have been constructed. For eight seasons, Salah has punished managers who didn't own him, rewarded those who captained him, and delivered unforgettable hauls—including the record 29-pointer against Watford in 2017/18—that decided championships. His final season, though currently showing "only" 97 points from five goals and six assists in 1,828 minutes, still reflects the kind of underlying numbers (4.78 points per 90) that lesser players would celebrate as career bests.

Beyond individual managers, the announcement reshapes the entire FPL landscape for next season. The question of who becomes the new premium must-have, the £14m+ anchor around which budgets are constructed, will dominate pre-season strategy discussions. No player in the current game has demonstrated Salah's ability to sustain premium returns year after year, meaning FPL managers face unprecedented uncertainty as they plan for 2026/27.

    • Context and Background

    Salah's rise to FPL immortality traces back to his 2017 arrival at Anfield, where Klopp deployed him in a free-roaming role that maximized his explosive pace, positioning sense, and clinical finishing. What followed was a transformation not just of Liverpool's attacking identity but of the entire FPL meta-game. Before Salah, premium pricing at £10m+ was considered a luxury; after his debut 303-point season, having at least one premium forward became standard practice.

    His remarkable durability deserves particular attention. Salah has missed minimal game time across eight seasons, averaging 3,089 minutes per campaign and never dropping below 2,557 league minutes in any season. This availability, combined with his ceiling potential—evidenced by 16 separate occasions scoring 14+ points in a single match—made him the ultimate "set and forget" asset whose ownership became less about differential strategy and more about risk management against his inevitable returns.

    The tweet from statistics expert Duncan Alexander captures the Salah effect succinctly: "Including Man Utd players, Mohamed Salah is the eighth-highest PL scorer at Old Trafford in the 2020s." That a player can rank among the all-time greatest scorers at one of football's most difficult venues—even including defenders and midfielders from every club—is testament to his sustained excellence against top opposition. His 8.50 average points per game against Manchester United alone speaks to a ability to deliver in high-stakes matches that FPL managers could always trust.

    What's Next

    As Salah approaches his final weeks in a Liverpool shirt, FPL managers face the unenviable task of extracting maximum value from their £14.0m investment while simultaneously planning for a post-Salah world. His remaining fixtures—including a crucial Gameweek 35 visit to Old Trafford to face Manchester United, one of his favorite opponents—offer one last opportunity to profit from the Egyptian's brilliance.

    The upcoming summer will see FPL Towers face their own challenge: pricing the next generation of premium assets without Salah's established pedigree as a reference point. Who steps into the £13-14m bracket? Does Darwin Núñez, Cody Gakpo, or Diogo Jota inherit Salah's Liverpool minutes and penalty duties? Could this create value elsewhere in the squad? These questions will dominate the FPL community through the close season.

    For now, the sentiment articulated by the article rings true: this is "time for a sentimental final stretch" as the FPL community celebrates one of the greatest contributors the game has ever known. The 21 comments on the original article suggest an emotional send-off is underway, with managers sharing memories of Salah's unforgettable hauls, his 24-point explosions, and the countless weeks where he alone justified an entire team structure. Whatever comes next in Salah's career—and wherever he goes—his FPL legacy is secure as the gold standard against which all future premium assets will be measured.
