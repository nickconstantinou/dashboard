# FPL Notes: Why Saka Came Off at Half-Time + Double Injury Boost

## Saka's Substitution Explained

Bukayo Saka only lasted 45 minutes in Arsenal's clash with Fulham, failing to emerge for the second half. However, his substitution was game-time management and not a fresh injury. The Arsenal midfielder had been out since March with an Achilles issue and was building match fitness. Manager Mikel Arteta confirmed the plan was to have him on the pitch, adding: "We need to ramp up his load but we need to be careful because we need him on that pitch." This suggests Saka's appearance was always likely to be limited as Arsenal carefully manage his return from a lengthy layoff.

## Impactful First-Half Display

Despite his early exit, Saka delivered significant value during his time on the pitch. Supplying the run and cross for Viktor Gyokeres' close-range opener, he then turned goalscorer – again combining with the Swede – for the Gunners' second. The England international was also back on corners for Arsenal, indicating his involvement in set-piece duties. Manager Mikel Arteta described the first half as probably one of the best 45 minutes of the season for Arsenal.

## Double Injury Boost on the Horizon

Arsenal were without Martin Odegaard and Kai Havertz against the Cottagers. However, it sounds like both will be back for Tuesday's Champions League clash with Atletico Madrid. This represents a welcome double boost for FPL managers ahead of what could be a pivotal European fixture.

## Additional Tactical Note

Another positive for the Arsenal attack was Myles Lewis-Skelly operating in midfield. The young player offers an interesting option at just £5.0m.
