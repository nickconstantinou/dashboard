Premier League Run-In: What Each Club Has Left to Play For in the 2025/26 Season

Arsenal's Title Destiny in Their Own Hands Despite City Threat

Arsenal head into the final stretch of the 2025/26 Premier League season with a nine-point advantage over Manchester City, holding what appears to be a commanding position in the title race. However, Pep Guardiola's side retains a game in hand against Crystal Palace at home, meaning the margin could theoretically shrink to six points before the two sides meet in what promises to be a decisive Gameweek 33 clash at the Etihad Stadium. Beyond the points gap, Arsenal's superior goal difference of +39 compared to City's +32 provides an additional layer of security, suggesting that even if City win their game in hand, Arsenal would still hold the upper hand in any scenario involving dropped points.

The Gunners' remaining fixture list offers a mixed bag of challenges. Their run-in includes home matches against Bournemouth, Newcastle, and Fulham, sandwiched between the potentially title-deciding trip to Manchester City and a season-concluding home fixture against Crystal Palace. Notably, Arsenal only leave London once during the run-in—the trip to the Etihad—making their remaining schedule relatively favorable for a team operating across multiple fronts. Mikel Arteta's side must balance their Premier League ambitions with Champions League commitments and FA Cup involvement, raising the possibility of minute management in the closing weeks. If Arsenal progress to the Champions League semi-finals, Gameweek 35's fixture against Fulham will be bracketed by another European double-header, potentially forcing difficult rotation decisions.

Manchester City, despite their inferior position, remain defiant. Nico O'Reilly, speaking after City's EFL Cup final victory over Arsenal, encapsulated the mood at the Etihad: "The blood never went – we've always smelt blood. We're confident in ourselves, we know we can do it, they've got to come to our place which is a tough place to come to as everyone has seen this season. So, we do smell blood and we've got to keep going. It does a lot for us, build momentum and just push on now. Obviously, it's a big blow for them. They were going for everything, just as we were." City's remaining fixtures include away trips to Chelsea, Burnley, and Everton, alongside home matches against Brentford, Bournemouth, Aston Villa, and the crucial Arsenal encounter.

European Qualification Race Remains Wide Open

Behind the title contenders, the battle for Champions League qualification has emerged as one of the season's most compelling subplots. For the second consecutive year, it appears increasingly certain that the Premier League's top five teams will secure qualification for Europe's premier club competition, reflecting the league's growing financial muscle and competitive depth. Currently, Manchester United sit third with 55 points, Aston Villa occupy fourth on 54 points, and Liverpool hold the final Champions League spot in fifth place with 49 points—though the Reds' recent form has raised serious questions about their prospects.

Liverpool's charge has faltered spectacularly ahead of the run-in, with Arne Slot's side failing to win any of their last three matches before the international break. This dip in form has opened the door for pursuing clubs, and sixth-place Chelsea, despite their own struggles including two successive defeats, remain firmly in contention just one point behind Liverpool on 48. Perhaps most remarkably, Brentford and Everton—both on 46 points—have emerged as genuine European contenders, with Everton's potential qualification representing what one source described as "unbelievable" given the club's recent history.

Chelsea's position is particularly intriguing given their substantial investment in the squad. Despite occupying sixth place and possessing the resources to attract world-class talent, their recent performances suggest inconsistency that could prove costly against more experienced European rivals. The Blues' remaining fixtures will likely determine whether their season ends with Champions League qualification or a disappointing miss of the top four entirely.

Why This Matters for Fantasy Football and the Premier League Landscape

For Fantasy Premier League managers, the run-in presents both opportunities and minefields. Clubs with Champions League commitments face the risk of rotation, while those eliminated from European competitions can fully focus on domestic matters. Arsenal's potential semi-final involvement could see Arteta rest key players in Gameweek 35 against Fulham, while City, having exited European competition earlier, may have fresher legs for their crucial final fixtures. The title race's resolution could come as early as Gameweek 36, when Arsenal visit West Ham if they defeat City in the previous meeting and win their other matches.

The broader implications extend beyond this season. A second consecutive year of top-five Champions League qualification signals a potential shift in the Premier League's competitive hierarchy, with the financial benefits of guaranteed European football potentially creating a self-reinforcing cycle that further concentrates talent among the leading clubs. For smaller teams like Brentford and Everton, European qualification would represent not just a one-season achievement but potentially a transformational moment that could reshape their long-term ambitions and ability to retain key players.

What to Watch in the Coming Weeks

The international break provides a temporary respite before the season's defining period. Upon resumption, all attention will turn to Gameweek 32 deadlines and the fixtures that follow. Manchester City's game in hand against Crystal Palace offers the first opportunity to narrow the gap before the title showdown in Gameweek 33. For clubs like Liverpool and Chelsea, reversing their poor form becomes paramount, while Brentford and Everton will seek to maintain their unexpected challenges. The FA Cup semi-finals and potential Champions League progression for Arsenal add further layers of complexity to an already congested fixture list that could determine multiple trophies and the futures of several managers heading into the summer transfer window.
