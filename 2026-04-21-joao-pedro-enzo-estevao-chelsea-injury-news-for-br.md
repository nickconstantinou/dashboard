# Chelsea Injury Update: Estevao Ruled Out, Joao Pedro a Doubt for Brighton Trip

Chelsea striker Joao Pedro will be a late call for Tuesday's Premier League clash at Brighton after missing Saturday's defeat to Manchester United with a minor thigh issue. Head coach Liam Rosenior confirmed the Brazilian was back on the training pitch on Monday, but a final decision will not be made until Tuesday morning.

"Joao will be a late call," Rosenior said. "He was out on the pitch today and we'll make a decision on him tomorrow morning. So, a good positive, because he will be back sooner rather than later."

The update offers a glimmer of hope for those with Joao Pedro on their radar, though his participation remains uncertain. Rosenior stopped short of guaranteeing his involvement, leaving the striker's availability genuinely in doubt until matchday.

In more reassuring news, Enzo Fernandez escaped serious concern after hobbling off during the loss to Manchester United. "Enzo is absolutely fine," Rosenior said, providing clear reassurance on the Argentine midfielder's fitness.

The less positive news surrounds Estevao. The Brazilian teenager, still awaiting his full Premier League debut after an earlier hamstring problem disrupted his start at the club, has suffered another setback. "With Este, he's definitely out for tomorrow, which is a real shame," Rosenior said. "We're doing our further tests and investigation into his injury." The lack of a timeline for his return and the reference to ongoing tests suggest this is not a straightforward minor complaint.

There were no new injury concerns arising from the weekend fixture, with the club reporting no further issues beyond those already on the list. Rosenior also offered an update on Levi Colwill, who played 60 minutes for Chelsea's under-21 side last Friday in his first meaningful action since undergoing knee surgery. "Very hopefully, yes, you can see him before the end of the season," Rosenior said of a potential first-team return. "Sixty minutes was a really, really good start for him. We want him to be involved in the next game on Saturday with the under-21s and build him up. It's been a really bad injury that he's come back from."

No fresh information was available on Reece James (hamstring), Jamie Gittens (hamstring), or Filip Jorgensen (groin), all of whom remain on the club's long-term injury list.

For anyone tracking Chelsea's squad ahead of Tuesday's game, the picture is mixed: Enzo is confirmed available, Joao Pedro is a genuine 50-50, and Estevao is simply not an option. The broader recovery arcs — Colwill edging closer, Estevao facing fresh uncertainty — suggest this squad's availability issues are not yet fully resolved.
