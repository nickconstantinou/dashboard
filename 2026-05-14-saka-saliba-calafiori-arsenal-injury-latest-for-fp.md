# Arsenal Injury Latest for FPL Gameweek 37

## Calafiori Uncertainty After Half-Time Withdrawal

Riccardo Calafiori's availability for Gameweek 37 remains uncertain after he came off at half-time last weekend. Manager Mikel Arteta has expressed hope that the issue is not serious, though the club are yet to confirm whether he will be available. Calafiori is priced at £5.6m in FPL and managers will need to await further updates before the deadline.

## Saka and Saliba Miss Thursday Training

Bukayo Saka and William Saliba were not sighted in training on Thursday, raising questions about their availability for Arsenal's fixture against Burnley. Both players were absent alongside Martin Odegaard, though Sky has downplayed these absences. Saka is priced at £10.0m while Saliba carries a £6.2m price tag. Managers holding either player should monitor for further team news.

## Ben White Ruled Out Long-Term

Arsenal have confirmed that Ben White suffered a significant knee injury during the win over West Ham United last Sunday. Manager Mikel Arteta has indicated the injury appears to be a long-term issue, stating the defender is unlikely to return "in many, many weeks." Arteta emphasized the focus is on determining when White can return to action, but his season appears effectively over.

## Timber Has Slim Chance of Return This Season

Jurrien Timber remains sidelined with an ankle injury, with no specific timeline for his return. Arteta acknowledged there is a chance Timber could feature again this season, though he was unable to specify how realistic that possibility is. The defender's FPL ownership remains limited due to his prolonged absence.

## Merino Continues Recovery

Mikel Merino remains a long-term absentee with a foot injury and received no specific update in the latest press conference. He is not expected to feature in FPL Gameweek 37 or the remainder of the season.

## Fixture Preview: Burnley Clash

Away from the injury concerns, Arsenal face Burnley in Gameweek 37. Arteta was asked whether the team would push to boost their goal difference in the fixture, with the title race potentially going to the final day. This fixture context may influence rotation decisions regardless of player fitness.
