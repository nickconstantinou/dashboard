Overview

The Cafe Analogy

Atesh uses a practical example: imagine you own a small cafe serving 50 customers a day. Business is going well until a celebrity visits, posts about your cafe on Instagram, and suddenly you're getting 500 to 5,000 customers daily. Most people recognize this as a "scalability issue," but Atesh challenges viewers to think deeper‚Äîwhy exactly can't you handle this increased load? He walks through critical questions: Is it insufficient tables? Kitchen capacity? Not enough chefs? Not enough staff? Not enough parking? The key insight is that you must identify the specific bottleneck before you can solve the problem.

The video stresses that "what cannot be measured cannot be improved"‚Äîa fundamental principle of system design. Atesh demonstrates how monitoring tools like Site 24x7 allow you to track various system components including databases, containers, networks, APIs, and load balancers. Just as you would monitor different aspects of a cafe (kitchen output, seating capacity, staff efficiency), in software systems you must identify which components need monitoring to understand where your scaling challenges actually lie.
