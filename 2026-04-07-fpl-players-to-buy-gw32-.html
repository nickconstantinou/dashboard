Understanding Double Gameweek 33 and Its Implications

The fundamental appeal of double gameweeks in Fantasy Premier League lies in their point-multiplying potential. When a player features in two matches within a single gameweek, all their points from both appearances are counted separately—meaning that if Erling Haaland scores against Arsenal and then bags a hat-trick against Burnley, those points don't merely stack; they become doubled through the captaincy, with appearance points, bonus points, and goal contributions all receiving the multiplier effect.

For managers preparing their wildcards, this creates a fascinating strategic calculus. The six teams doubling in Gameweek 33 will all blank in Gameweek 34, which fundamentally shapes the two prevailing chip strategies circulating among the FPL community. The first approach involves playing the wildcard in Gameweek 32 to set up for a bench boost in the double gameweek, then using a free hit in the subsequent blank—a sequence that allows managers to load up on doubling players while maintaining flexibility for the blank week. The alternative strategy skips the Gameweek 32 wildcard, instead using a free hit in Double Gameweek 33 itself, then wildcarding in Gameweek 35 after the blank, and finally bench boosting in what would be Gameweek 36's expected double involving Manchester City and Crystal Palace.

The second strategy has gained considerable appeal because it allows managers to retain their high-performing players with favourable fixtures for another week rather than selling them to accommodate doubling assets. Players like Bruno Fernandes, Gabriel Martinelli, Declan Rice, Bukayo Saka, and Julian Alvarez can be kept through Gameweek 32, then the free hit enables a complete transformation of the squad for Double Gameweek 33 while still being able to field a full eleven players in the blank Gameweek 34.

However, there's a notable complication: teams still competing in European competitions who play midweek before Gameweek 32 include Arsenal, Liverpool, Aston Villa, Nottingham Forest, and Crystal Palace. This midweek Champions League fixture for Arsenal against Sporting CP is particularly relevant, as the Gunners then face Bournemouth at home in Gameweek 32 with an early Saturday kickoff—a timing issue that could impact their freshness and rotation risk.

The Crystal Palace situation deserves special attention. Should they progress against Fiorentina in the Europa Conference League, that European commitment could create rotation concerns around their expected double in Gameweek 36, since the league will effectively be dead for them by then and they'll have only European competition left to play for.

The goalkeeper position presents a fascinating opportunity in Gameweek 32, particularly for those deploying their wildcard. The speaker identifies two primary budget options who both happen to be doubling in Gameweek 33, making them exceptionally valuable for bench boost strategies.

The reasoning is straightforward: Burnley at home this week represents a favourable fixture, the double in Gameweek 33 provides security for bench boost activation, and at 4.5 million, he represents genuine budget value. He's a classic case of a player who makes sense purely through fixture combination and price point.

Darlow from Leeds represents the other doubling budget option worth considering. The Leeds goalkeeper has now started ten consecutive matches following Perry's transformation into the cup keeper, and Leeds' double in Gameweek 33—comprising a home fixture against Wolves and an away game at Bournemouth—is reasonably attractive. However, the speaker expresses personal hesitation, stating: "I honestly wouldn't have him on a wild card despite him doubling. The doubles aren't fantastic really. Like the two best teams that double are Chelsea and Man City, and in their cases, you'd rather look at outfield players more upside, wouldn't you?"

This point speaks to a broader philosophical consideration in FPL: when the strongest teams in a double aren't particularly strong defensively, the value proposition shifts toward outfield players rather than their goalkeepers. Manchester City and Chelsea both play each other in Gameweek 33, creating an inherently difficult fixture pairing that further reduces clean sheet probability.

Ryan from Arsenal tops the goalkeeper table this week with four points, as Arsenal host Bournemouth at home. However, the speaker flags the concerns: "They've got the early kickoff on Saturday which isn't ideal given they're having to play midweek in the Champions League as well against Sporting." For managers not wildcoding this week and still possessing their bench boost, Ryan remains a viable option, though the rotation risk from European commitments cannot be ignored.

The recommended keeper combination for wildcard managers involves starting with either Buneman or Ryan, then using Darlow as the bench option to enable bench boost activation in Gameweek 33.
