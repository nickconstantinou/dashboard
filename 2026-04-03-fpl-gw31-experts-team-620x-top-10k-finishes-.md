## Transfer Patterns and Market Movements

The transfer activity among experts reveals a clear narrative: 80% of top managers are making at least one transfer this gameweek, while 20% are rolling their transfer. This high transfer rate reflects the challenging nature of the blank gameweek, where managers must act to maintain viable squads while also positioning themselves for upcoming double gameweeks.

The video makes an important strategic observation about when to use transfers versus rolling them. Managers with a full 11 out and no major upside opportunities might actually benefit from leaving their team untouched, using the roll for a later double gameweek. The reasoning is straightforward: transferring from, for example, Audley to Anderson might yield only four additional points if Fulham keep a clean sheet or Sunderland concede, whereas rolling preserves that transfer for the more valuable double gameweeks in 33 and 36, and potentially even a smaller double in 34. The video emphasizes that "bank transfers are king when we head into the business end of the season."

## Chip Strategy: The Critical Decision

One of the most valuable sections addresses the evolving chip strategy debate. The video notes that "interestingly, there are no chips active this week at all on the entire experts league"—a remarkable finding that confirms this gameweek can be navigated without chip expenditure, preserving them for more advantageous situations.

The presenter outlines two competing strategies that top managers are debating:

**Strategy One**: Save the wildcard and instead free hit double gameweek 33, then wildcard in Gameweek 35, with bench boost in Gameweek 36. This approach avoids the wildcard in Gameweek 32 and preserves maximum flexibility for the double gameweeks.

**Strategy Two**: The more commonly discussed approach—wildcard in Gameweek 32, bench boost in 33, and use a hit in 34. This has been the prevailing wisdom but may not be optimal for all managers.

The video presents a compelling case for the first strategy, particularly for managers who don't own many players from teams likely to blank in Gameweek 34 (Man City, Arsenal, Chelsea). The concern with wildcarding in 32 is filling five or six squad spots with players from Brighton, Newcastle, and Burnley specifically for bench boost in 33, then spending the rest of the season trying to fix a suboptimal squad.

A crucial variable involves the Manchester City versus Crystal Palace fixture. If Palace lose in the Europa Conference League and Man City defeat Liverpool in the FA Cup, that match could move from Gameweek 36 to as early as Gameweek 32. The video estimates roughly a 5% chance of this happening, noting that "there's probably about a 5% chance that Man City versus Palace stays in game 31." However, the presenter doesn't expect it to happen, as the Premier League could just as easily drop it in a later gameweek. If it does occur, the announcement would likely come before the deadline.
