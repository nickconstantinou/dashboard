FPL Gameweek 33 Round-Up: The Double Gameweek That Could Define Your Season

April 18, 2026 — The Fantasy Premier League season has reached its most consequential juncture, and managers worldwide are facing decisions that will reverberate through their overall ranks for the remaining weeks. Gameweek 33 presents a genuine bonanza: six Premier League clubs face double fixtures, creating an unprecedented opportunity to maximize returns from a single gameweek. Whether you're Wildcarding, Free Hitting, or activating your Bench Boost, the choices made before Saturday's deadline carry compound weight that no algorithm can fully calculate.

---

The Double Gameweek Landscape: Six Teams, Maximum Opportunity

The confirmed fixture data reveals the scope of what managers are navigating. In Gameweek 33, Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds, and Manchester City all play twice, making them the primary targets for investment. The fixture density is extraordinary—26 confirmed fixtures across the gameweek—meaning managers who load up on these doubling sides gain a significant structural advantage over those who don't.

The complementary blank gameweek picture adds another layer of complexity. Arsenal, Crystal Palace, Manchester City, and Wolves blank in Gameweek 33, which limits options for those without double coverage. However, Gameweek 34 presents the inverse: Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds, and Manchester City blank that week, while Arsenal, Crystal Palace, and Wolves return to action. This creates the strategic case for the Free Hit chip—managers can deploy it in Gameweek 34 to field an entirely fresh squad while preserving their core assets.

Understanding this sequencing has divided the FPL community. Some managers, like Zophar, argue that Wildcarding in Gameweek 33 produces essentially the same team as a Free Hit would, meaning the Wildcard provides superior long-term value by enabling subsequent chip activations. Others prefer the immediacy of Free Hitting this week to maximize the double gameweek without burning the Wildcard. The honest assessment from elite managers is that the optimal path depends entirely on your current squad structure—and that neither strategy is clearly superior in isolation.

Today's Key FPL News: Injuries, Suspensions, and Confirmations

The week's most significant news emerged from the Manchester City press conference on Friday afternoon, where Pep Guardiola delivered welcome confirmation regarding Nico O'Reilly. The midfielder, who had been flagged as injured after limping off against Chelsea, was declared "fine" by Guardiola and available for selection. This resolution carries substantial weight for the FPL community—O'Reilly ranks among the most-owned City assets and represents exceptional value at £5.1m given his recent returns of 139 season points and a form rating of 14.0. FPL Harry emphasized that holding O'Reilly remains the correct strategy even with the injury scare, arguing that cycling to alternatives like Matheus Nunes or Abdukodir Khusanov represents unnecessary disruption given his confirmed availability.

However, Guardiola also confirmed that Rúben Dias would remain unavailable, leaving City's defensive options potentially constrained. This makes Marc Guéhi (£5.1m) at 34.8% ownership—currently the top-form player in the entire game with a 15.0 form rating and 150 season points—particularly attractive. The City defender is essentially guaranteed 90 minutes in every match, a valuable trait for defensive assets in a double gameweek.

From the Arsenal perspective, Bukayo Saka has been confirmed out for the Gunners' fixture against Manchester City. His absence creates complications for managers invested in Arsenal assets, as the £8.8m Viktor Gyökeres becomes a more complex proposition without his primary creative supply line. The title race context adds intensity to this fixture—Guardiola acknowledged that a defeat to Arsenal would effectively end City's championship aspirations, meaning City will field their strongest possible XI against Arsenal before potentially rotating against Burnley depending on that result's outcome.

Manchester United face a defensive crisis heading into their fixture against Chelsea. Harry Maguire received an additional one-match ban for comments made to the fourth official after his red card against Bournemouth, while Lisandro Martínez is also suspended. Kobbie Mainoo appears likely to miss the match through injury, leaving United's backline severely depleted. Planet FPL's James noted that the likely center-back pairing would be "Yuro and maybe Heaven"—an "inexperienced" combination that significantly weakens United's defensive structure. This creates genuine opportunity for Chelsea attackers, with Cole Palmer's penalty involvement and consistent chance creation making him the standout option despite his £6.7m price tag.

The situation at Newcastle United has introduced uncertainty around Anthony Gordon, who has apparently been "flirting with Bayern Munich" according to Pras from FBL Wire. Manager Eddie Howe has stated that players must be "fully committed" to play, raising questions about Gordon's guaranteed minutes heading into the double gameweek. This represents a legitimate concern for managers holding the £7.4m midfielder, particularly given his rotation risk in a season where consistency matters more than ever.

Finally, Andoni Iraola's decision to leave Bournemouth at the end of the season introduces potential volatility into the Cherries' approach. Planet FPL suggested that a manager with "nothing to lose might be more willing to take risks," potentially increasing minutes for attacking players on the periphery or alternatively prioritizing blooding younger talent. This uncertainty makes Bournemouth assets somewhat riskier than their fixture list alone would suggest.

Captain Recommendations: Haaland Dominates, But Alternatives Exist

The captaincy conversation for Gameweek 33 begins and largely ends with Erling Haaland, who commands approximately 80 percent of captaincy votes according to FPL Harry's YouTube poll. The Manchester City striker's underlying numbers have raised concerns—he has managed just one open-play goal in his last ten Premier League matches—but the fixture picture overrides statistical caution. City's 4.4 projected goals across their two matches represents the highest of any team, and Haaland remains the most likely individual beneficiary of any scoring opportunity.

The case against Haaland centers on his recent drought and the fact that his recent scoring has been sustained primarily by penalty conversions. Danny Welbeck (£6.3m) has emerged as a differential alternative with superior recent goal-scoring form when penalty contributions are excluded, while Joao Pedro offers genuine differential captaincy potential given his recent output exceeds Cole Palmer's when penalties are stripped from consideration.

Cole Palmer represents the primary alternative for managers choosing to pivot away from the consensus. Manchester United's defensive crisis—without Maguire, Martínez, and likely Mainoo—creates an inviting fixture for Chelsea's set-piece specialists. The £6.7m midfielder's penalty involvement and consistent chance creation make him viable, though Harry notes that managers backing alternatives effectively need Haaland to blank, creating significant pressure on differential strategies.
