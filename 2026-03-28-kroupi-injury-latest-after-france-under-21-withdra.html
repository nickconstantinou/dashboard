Eli Junior Kroupi Injury Update: France Under-21 Withdrawal Confirmed as Bournemouth Await Further News

The Full Story

Bournemouth's budget FPL forward Eli Junior Kroupi has become the latest player to withdraw from international duty, dealing a potential blow to Fantasy Premier League managers who invested in the £4.7m French striker. Kroupi exited the France under-21 camp before Les Bleuets' clash with Luxembourg on Thursday, raising immediate concerns about his availability. The young forward took to social media to personally confirm the injury, providing reassurance to worried Fantasy managers: "Forced to leave the gathering prematurely due to physical discomfort, further examinations were reassuring," he posted in French.

The translation of Kroupi's message suggests the injury is not considered serious, with follow-up medical examinations returning positive results. However, Bournemouth are yet to issue any official update on their striker's condition. The timing is particularly concerning for FPL managers given Kroupi's status as one of the budget options in the forward position, making his potential absence significant for squad construction and transfers ahead of a crucial run of fixtures.

Why This Matters

For the FPL community, Kroupi's situation represents yet another headache during an international break that has seen multiple player withdrawals across the league. Budget FPL assets are precious commodities, and losing a £4.7m forward could disrupt managers' strategies significantly. The uncertainty around his recovery timeline makes it difficult for Fantasy managers to plan their transfers with confidence heading into the final stages of the season.

From Bournemouth's perspective, the club face a critical Premier League run-in with games against top opponents. Manager Andoni Iraola will be keen to assess his squad's fitness before their crucial clash against Arsenal on April 11. The potential loss of Kroupi, even temporarily, narrows Iraola's attacking options at a vital stage of the campaign when survival or European qualification battles could be decided.
