# Gameweek 35: Lateriser on Cherki, Keeping Pedro + Clubs to Target

*Fantasy Football Scout's Lateriser picks have been a mixed bag this season. Here's a focused look at the key decisions ahead of Gameweek 35, using available data to guide transfers and captaincy.*

---

## Chelsea's Fixture Run Makes Them Attractive

Chelsea's final two fixtures offer a compelling reason to invest in their assets. They face a **poor-at-the-back Tottenham Hotspur**, followed by **Sunderland** — a side who have **conceded nine goals in their last two matches**. That double gameweek is a significant fixture swing worth planning around.

There's also a secondary angle: Chelsea face **Nottingham Forest in Gameweek 36, a team that might be distracted by their Europa League semi-finals**. That combination of strong fixtures and potential opponent fatigue makes Chelsea one of the clearest club targets heading into the run-in.

---

## Keep Pedro: The xGI Case

The strongest data-backed argument this week centres on **Joao Pedro**.

Joao Pedro has the **highest xGI (5.08) in the league for his latest six non-injured outings**. Of that figure, **4.84 comes from expected goals (xG)**, suggesting the underlying attacking threat is real and concentrated in finishing rather than assists.

Keeping Pedro makes sense if you already own him. The price and fixture profile need to be weighed personally, but the raw chance creation metrics are among the strongest in the league for his sample.

---

## Morgan Gibbs-White: Volume Worth Backing

**Morgan Gibbs-White** ($7.6m, Nottingham Forest) offers another strong tactical case. In his last six matches, he has:

- **Scored six goals from 2.43 xG**
- **Provided one assist from 0.18 xA**

The goals scored outpacing his xG by a wide margin could signal overperformance, but the volume itself is elite. Forest's run of fixtures — including the Chelsea game against a side with European distraction — makes Gibbs-White worth serious consideration as a mid-price enabler.

---

## Jarrod Bowen: Assist King, Zero Goals

**Jarrod Bowen** (£7.8m, West Ham) is one of the most contradictory picks in the game right now.

In his last six matches, he has **seven FPL assists from 1.18 xA** — a remarkable creative output. However, **he has not scored a goal in his last 10 matches**. That goal drought is real and uncomfortable to hold.

Despite that, **only seven players have scored more FPL points than Jarrod Bowen in that timeframe**. He is accumulating returns through assists at a rate that compensates for the blank up front. Whether you hold or pivot depends on squad structure and alternative options, but the underlying assist chain remains one of the best in the league.
