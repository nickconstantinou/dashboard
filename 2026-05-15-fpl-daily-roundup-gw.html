FPL Gameweek 37 Roundup: The Penultimate Push Begins

As the Fantasy Premier League season hurtles towards its climax, managers face their most consequential decisions of the campaign. With only two gameweeks remaining, every transfer carries weight, and the captain's armband has never felt more precious. The latest intelligence from Fantasy Football Scout's reporting team reveals a complex landscape of injury concerns, emerging differentials, and tactical puzzles that will define the final stretch of the 2025-26 season.

Today's Key FPL News and Injuries

The injury picture heading into Gameweek 37 has sharpened considerably, with several high-profile concerns demanding immediate attention from managers with deadlines looming.

Arsenal find themselves in a state of uncertainty heading into their fixture against Burnley. Riccardo Calafiori came off at half-time last weekend, leaving his availability genuinely in doubt for the deadline. Manager Mikel Arteta has expressed hope that the issue is not serious, though the club has yet to confirm whether he will be available. The Italian defender is priced at £5.6m, and managers holding him will need to monitor late updates before committing. More concerning is the absence of Bukayo Saka and William Saliba from Thursday's training session, with both players alongside Martin Ødegaard not sighted. Sky sources have downplayed these absences, suggesting they may be precautionary rather than genuine injury concerns, but the timing raises obvious anxiety among the 16.4% of managers who own Saliba and the substantial contingent invested in Saka's attacking returns. Perhaps most troubling is confirmation that Ben White suffered a significant knee injury in the win over West Ham United last Sunday, ending his season and removing a valuable defensive option for the run-in.

Nottingham Forest received a significant boost with the return to training of Morgan Gibbs-White, who was photographed with his team wearing a protective mask following his facial injury. Manager Vítor Pereira confirmed the player has adapted well, stating: "He started today to work with the team, with the mask. It is strange when you use the mask but, in the end, I spoke with him and he said, 'I'm okay with the mask, no problem'." Given Gibbs-White's extraordinary form of 9.2—the highest in the game—and his 75% chance of playing according to FPL's latest assessment, his availability would provide Forest with their most potent attacking threat against Manchester United. Dan Ndoye could also return from a short-term unspecified issue, though Ola Aina remains unavailable and Murillo continues his absence with a muscle injury. Pereira himself expressed uncertainty about whether Murillo and Ibrahim Sangaré will feature again this season, a concerning development for Forest's Champions League ambitions.

Brighton face their final two matches without Kaoru Mitoma, who will miss both games due to a hamstring injury picked up last Saturday. The Japanese winger's absence removes a premium differential option from the market. Diego Gomez, however, has recovered from his knee injury and is available for selection, while Mats Wieffer at £4.9m could potentially return for Sunday's match. The club is awaiting confirmation over the next two to three days before making a final call on Wieffer's participation. Jack Hinshelwood enters the gameweek as a compelling individual narrative—aiming to become the first Brighton player to score in four successive Premier League matches, with his price of £5.1m and form of 6.2 making him an accessible option for those seeking attacking returns from the Seagulls' defence.

Leeds United will be without Noah Okafor, whose calf injury keeps him sidelined until May 24th—a timeline that makes him unavailable for both remaining gameweeks. However, Dominic Calvert-Lewin continues his strong run of form with 5.2 form and 13.0% ownership, while James Justin and Ethan Ampadu offer budget routes into a Leeds side with attractive fixtures.

Bournemouth continues to be one of the game's most productive sources of value. Adrien Truffert at £4.7m with 7.2 form leads the budget options, while Marcos Senesi Barón at £5.2m has accumulated 169 season points for the 21.1% of managers brave enough to invest in the Cherries' defence. Rayan Vitor Simplício Rocha, available at £5.4m, offers an alternative route into Andoni Iraola's side.

Gameweek Landscape

Gameweek 37 presents as a full fixture gameweek with all twenty teams in action, providing a clean slate for managers across the board. The confirmed fixture data shows both Gameweek 37 and Gameweek 38 featuring twenty confirmed fixtures each, meaning there are no blank or double gameweek complications in either of the remaining rounds.

Looking at the broader fixture picture, the confirmed blank gameweeks earlier in the season—Arsenal, Crystal Palace, Manchester City, and Wolves blanking in GW31, with Bournemouth, Brighton, Burnley, Chelsea, Leeds, and Manchester City blanking in GW34—have already passed, as have the double gameweeks that scattered throughout the season. Crystal Palace and Manchester City did enjoy a double in GW36, but that opportunity has now closed.

The implications for strategy are significant. Managers who have been saving their wildcard or building their squad around specific double gameweek leverages now operate in a straightforward environment: twenty teams, twenty matches, no complications. The final two gameweeks offer equal fixture density, making bench boost decisions simpler than in seasons where blank and double gameweeks have cluttered the run-in.

Captain Recommendations

The captaincy debate for Gameweek 37 has crystallised around several distinct options, with FFS community polling revealing fascinating manager sentiment.

Bruno Fernandes currently commands approximately 32% of the captaincy poll, making him the most popular choice among FFS users. His case rests on extraordinary recent consistency—he hasn't blanked at Old Trafford since Gameweek 12, providing a home record that spans several months. With Manchester United facing Nottingham Forest at home, Fernandes presents a favourable fixture against a side whose Champions League qualification hopes may falter without Gibbs-White at full capacity. The Portuguese international needs just two assists to break the all-time Premier League assist record in a single season, adding a milestone narrative that could motivate both player and team in the final games. At £10.4m with 4.8 form and 47.6% ownership, Fernandes is expensive but almost universally held by top managers.

Erling Haaland delivered his first double-digit haul since Gameweek 25 in Double Gameweek 36, suggesting the goals may finally be flowing again at the critical moment. Though his ownership of 64.5% makes him a relatively crowded choice, his fixture against West Ham at home presents a genuine chance of returns. His form of 6.2 is respectable if not spectacular, but his ceiling remains the highest of any player in the game.

Bukayo Saka sits second in the captaincy poll and has been transferred in by more than 100,000 managers ahead of Friday's deadline. Both Saka and Viktor Gyökeres have started each of the last two gameweeks and notched double-digit hauls in their last match at the Emirates, suggesting Arsenal's attacking output against Burnley could be substantial. With Arsenal likely to push for goal difference improvements, the fixture carries both clean sheet and goal potential. Saka's ownership has climbed as managers chase his recent returns, though his uncertain training participation on Thursday adds an element of risk that the 100,000+ transfer Ins have apparently deemed acceptable.

Gyökeres at £9.0m continues his strong finish to the season, with 5.0 form and 126 season points suggesting the Swedish striker has found his rhythm. His 16.5% ownership makes him a genuine differential for those seeking an alternative to the crowded Haaland and Fernandes picks.

Chip Strategy

With both Gameweek 37 and Gameweek 38 confirmed as full fixture weeks with twenty games each, the traditional chip strategies around blank and double gameweeks do not apply in the traditional sense. Managers without remaining chips should simply optimise their squads for the next two gameweeks. Those with unused chips—wildcards, free hits, bench boosts—must weigh the opportunity cost of deploying them in a straightforward environment against the potential gains of having a fully optimised squad for consecutive rounds. The wildcard remains a powerful tool for those with significant injury doubts, particularly around Arsenal's uncertain trio, while a free hit could theoretically be used to load up on players with the most favourable run-in—but with no blanks or doubles to exploit, the relative value of such moves is diminished.
