FPL Gameweek 37 Q&A: Sell Rogers? Saka or Gyokeres?

Sell Morgan Rogers?

Morgan Rogers (£7.4m) has been a solid FPL asset this season, but managers may be considering moving him on. When evaluating whether to sell Rogers, one factor to consider is his price point relative to alternatives.

Morgan Rogers (£7.4m) is perhaps more sellable than, say, Joao Pedro (£7.5m), given the fixture run ahead.

For managers looking to upgrade the position, Bruno Guimaraes (£6.8m) is the favoured replacement for Morgan Rogers. Newcastle's Brazilian midfielder offers a different profile at a lower price, potentially freeing up funds for upgrades elsewhere in the squad.

Saka or Gyokeres?

This is one of the key questions heading into Gameweek 37, particularly for managers with wildcard or free hit chips remaining.

Price Context

Gyokeres is priced at £9.0m and Saka at £10.0m, creating a £1.0m price differential that may influence decisions for budget-stretched squads.

Arsenal's Fixture

Arsenal are projected to score as many goals this Gameweek as most teams are over the next two, making them an attractive attacking option.

Saka's Context

The absence of Ben White's (£5.1m) overlapping runs on the right wing does dent Saka's output a bit. This tactical shift has been noted as a factor affecting Saka's ceiling in recent matches.

Gyokeres as Differential

If chasing, I prefer Viktor Gyokeres (£9.0m) to Bukayo Saka (£10.0m) as a differential captain. Gyokeres provides an alternative route for managers looking to differentiate their squads from the pack.

Gyokeres Performance Profile

Gyokeres has been afforded seven big chances, with only two players ahead of him in terms of opportunities created. However, his scoring record reveals a specific pattern.

Gyokeres is a flat-track bully with just one of his goals coming against a team in the top 10 (Bournemouth). This is worth considering when evaluating his captaincy potential against stronger opposition.

Penalty Duties

I also think he might be the designated penalty taker now, ahead of Saka. If confirmed, this would add significant value to Gyokeres' expected goal involvement.

Key Takeaways for Gameweek 37

Managers weighing up Rogers versus alternatives should note the Bruno Guimaraes swap as a viable budget option.

For the Saka-Gyokeres debate, the decision may hinge on whether managers are chasing the pack or building a lead. Gyokeres offers the differential appeal and potentially penalty involvement, while Saka remains a premium option with a stronger record against top teams.

William Saliba (£6.2m) continues to offer budget-friendly defensive coverage for Arsenal assets, though his output is primarily defensive rather than attacking.
