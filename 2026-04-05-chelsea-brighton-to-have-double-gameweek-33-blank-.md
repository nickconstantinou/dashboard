# Chelsea and Brighton Set for Double Gameweek 33, Blank Gameweek 34: What FPL Managers Need to Know

## The Full Story

Chelsea's emphatic victory over Port Vale in the FA Cup quarter-finals on Saturday has directly resulted in a Double Gameweek 33 for both Chelsea and Brighton and Hove Albion, with the two clubs set to blank in Gameweek 34. The Blues' progression through the FA Cup competition means they will now have additional fixtures scheduled, and as their semi-final opponents, Brighton automatically receive the same treatment—creating a valuable double gameweek for Fantasy Premier League managers targeting assets from both sides.

The fixture list for Double Gameweek 33 confirms Brighton will face both Chelsea and Tottenham Hotspur, presenting a mixed bag of attacking potential. Notably, both Chelsea and Tottenham represent attractive attacking matchups; the two clubs are the only Premier League sides without a clean sheet in the last six Gameweeks, suggesting defensive vulnerabilities that Brighton's forwards could exploit. Chelsea, meanwhile, will face Brighton in what serves as a distraction directly before their FA Cup semi-final commitments, with manager Liam Rosenior under pressure to secure Champions League qualification or risk losing his position.

Brighton arrives at this Double Gameweek in strong form under Fabian Hurzeler, having won four of their last five Premier League Gameweeks. The Seagulls are not involved in European competition and have exited the FA Cup, meaning their sole focus is domestic league performance—a potentially significant advantage as the season enters its crucial final stretch. Chelsea, by contrast, faces a more complicated balancing act between their league position and cup ambitions, with Champions League qualification mathematically achievable but far from guaranteed.

## Why This Matters

For Fantasy Premier League managers, Double Gameweeks represent the most valuable scheduling windows of the entire season. Players from teams with two matches in a single Gameweek can theoretically double their points output, making investment in assets from Double Gameweek teams a cornerstone of successful FPL strategy. The combination of Chelsea and Brighton both receiving this double fixture creates a concentrated opportunity that could separate managers who have prepared from those scrambling to react.

The significance extends beyond simple point accumulation. Blank Gameweeks—scheduling slots where a team has no fixture—can devastate an FPL squad if a manager lacks coverage. With Chelsea and Brighton both blanking in Gameweek 34, managers must ensure they have sufficient alternatives in their squads to avoid wasting a Gameweek entirely. This creates cascading transfer decisions that affect team structure for multiple weeks.

For the broader Premier League context, these fixture confirmations also illuminate the title race and European qualification battles. Chelsea's push for Champions League football directly impacts which clubs qualify for continental competition next season, influencing everything from transfer budgets to managerial appointments across the league.

## Context and Background

Fantasy Football Scout's coverage highlights that this Double Gameweek 33 situation remains partially contingent on other FA Cup quarter-final results. The publication notes that additional Double Gameweek 33 opportunities could emerge depending on outcomes in matches involving Arsenal, Newcastle, West Ham, Everton, Leeds, and Bournemouth. Should Arsenal defeat Southampton, Arsenal and Newcastle would gain double fixtures. West Ham defeating Leeds would benefit West Ham and Everton, while a Leeds victory would create doubles for Leeds and Bournemouth.

The international break preceding this period represents a potential disruption factor, particularly for Brighton. Fantasy Football Scout's deputy editor Tom Freeman suggests that momentum built during Brighton's impressive recent run could be interrupted by international call-ups and travel, though the Seagulls' focused league-only schedule offers some protection against fixture congestion.

Chelsea's situation appears more precarious. Manager Liam Rosenior's job security is directly tied to Champions League qualification, creating intense pressure that could manifest in unpredictable team selection. The uncertainty surrounding Enzo Fernandez's disciplinary status—Rosenior has "banished" the Argentine midfielder for Gameweek 32—adds another variable that FPL managers must monitor closely before committing to assets from Chelsea's midfield.

The defensive picture at Chelsea remains volatile. The goalkeeper situation is described as "a volatile one" with no clear starter emerging as consistently nailed. Trevoh Chalobah, once considered a defensive stalwart, has seen his availability compromised by injury, while Reece James continues to struggle with fitness issues. Wesley Fofana's inability to complete three matches in a week further complicates Chelsea's defensive planning, leaving Rosenior with limited reliable options at the back.
