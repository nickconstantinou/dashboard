# Chelsea and Brighton Secure Double Gameweek 33, Face Blank in Gameweek 34

## The Full Story

Chelsea's emphatic victory over Port Vale in the FA Cup quarter-finals on Saturday has locked in a coveted Double Gameweek 33 for both the Blues and Brighton and Hove Albion, though both clubs will subsequently blank in Gameweek 34. This fixture double comes as a significant development for Fantasy Premier League managers, who now have the opportunity to leverage matches against two struggling sides when building their squads for the upcoming period.

In Double Gameweek 33, Brighton will host Chelsea before traveling to Tottenham Hotspur Stadium, representing what could be a favorable fixture swing against two clubs currently experiencing defensive woes. Tottenham and Chelsea are the only Premier League sides without a clean sheet in the last six gameweeks—a striking statistic that will excite Fantasy managers looking to exploit these vulnerabilities. Chelsea, meanwhile, welcome Brighton to Stamford Bridge before making the short trip toface Tottenham, with manager Liam Rosenior mindful that Champions League qualification remains the priority amidst a potential FA Cup semi-final distraction looming in Gameweek 34.

## Why This Matters

For Fantasy Premier League managers, this announcement represents a critical squad-planning opportunity. Double Gameweeks are among the most valuable assets in FPL strategy, offering the chance to accumulate returns from two matches within a single gameweek—effectively doubling captaincy options and points potential. The combination of Chelsea and Brighton both doubling in Gameweek 33 makes their players immediate transfer targets, particularly those facing the aforementioned defensive struggles of Tottenham and Chelsea.

The subsequent Blank Gameweek 34, however, introduces a strategic complication. Both Chelsea and Brighton will have no fixtures that week, meaning managers must plan for cover options or ensure their squads feature players from clubs active in Gameweek 34. This creates a delicate balancing act: investing in Chelsea and Brighton assets for Double Gameweek 33 while simultaneously preparing bench coverage for Gameweek 34 when those players become unusable.

## Context and Background

This Double Gameweek materializes from Chelsea's successful FA Cup quarter-final campaign against Port Vale, where their victory secured the Blues' place in the semi-finals. The Premier League's fixture scheduling algorithm treats FA Cup quarter-final weekends as blank gameweeks for the teams involved, unless additional matches can be scheduled midweek—which Chelsea and Brighton have secured for Double Gameweek 33.

The situation for Brighton is particularly interesting from a strategic standpoint. The Seagulls are no longer in Europe and have exited the FA Cup, meaning manager Fabian Hurzeler can dedicate full attention to Premier League fixtures without rotation concerns or fatigue from European travel. Their strong recent form—four wins from five gameweeks—suggests they're entering this double at an optimal moment, though the international break may have disrupted their momentum.

Chelsea's position is more complex. Manager Liam Rosenior faces the delicate task of balancing Double Gameweek 33 preparations with the looming FA Cup semi-final in Gameweek 34. Failure to secure Champions League qualification would likely cost Rosenior his job, creating added pressure on league performances. The goalkeeper situation remains volatile, and the Enzo Fernandez situation—with the Argentine banished for Gameweek 32—requires resolution before the double arrives.
