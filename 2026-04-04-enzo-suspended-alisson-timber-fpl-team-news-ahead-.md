# FPL Team News: Enzo Fernandez Suspended, Liverpool Lose Alisson for Extended Period as Premier League Clubs Prepare for FA Cup Quarter-Finals

## The Full Story

Fantasy Premier League managers face a critical decision point heading into Gameweek 32 as six Premier League clubs provided crucial team news updates ahead of this weekend's FA Cup quarter-final fixtures. The most significant development emerged from Stamford Bridge, where Chelsea have suspended midfielder Enzo Fernandez for two matches following controversial public comments about his future at the club.

Head coach Liam Rosenior confirmed the decision, stating: "As a football club, with me as part of the decision, he won't be available for tomorrow's game or Manchester City next Sunday. It's disappointing for Enzo to speak that way. I have got no bad words to say about him but a line was crossed but a line was crossed in terms of our culture and what we want to build." Despite the suspension, Rosenior emphasized that "the door is not closed" on the Argentine international.

Meanwhile, Liverpool manager Arne Slot delivered concerning news regarding goalkeeper Alisson Becker, revealing the Brazilian will miss an extended period with a hamstring injury. "He is unavailable and also out of the PSG game. He will be out for a bit longer. We expect him back towards the end of the season," Slot confirmed, suggesting the absence will span beyond both Champions League quarter-final legs against Paris Saint-Germain. On a positive note, Mohamed Salah is available after recovering from a muscle injury—his first appearance since announcing his imminent departure from the club. Additionally, striker Alexander Isak returned to training for the first time in 101 days following an ankle injury and may feature in some capacity.

At Arsenal, manager Mikel Arteta provided mixed updates: Eberechi Eze and Yonko Hincapie remain unavailable, while Noni Madueke's injury is not considered serious, and Martin Odegaard and Jurrien Timber could be fit. Manchester City manager Pep Guardiola offered no updates on John Stones, Josko Gvardiol, or Ruben Dias, while Leeds United face uncertainty over striker Dominic Calvert-Lewin (hamstring doubt, but Anis Stach should be available) and West Ham will assess Fabio Summerville before their FA Cup fixture.

## Why This Matters

For Fantasy Premier League managers, these updates carry immediate financial and strategic implications. Enzo Fernandez, now suspended for two matches, is expected to be among the most popular transfers in Gameweek 32 as managers seek to move him on. His suspension means Chelsea will be without both Fernandez and Mykhailo Mudryk (also suspended) for the upcoming fixtures, potentially affecting the club's attacking output and, consequently, their players' FPL viability.

Alisson's extended absence represents a significant blow for Liverpool's defensive stability during a crucial period. With the PSG Champions League tie adding additional strain, manager Arne Slot must navigate a busy fixture schedule without his first-choice goalkeeper. This opens the door for Caoimhin Kelleher to establish himself between the posts, though his FPL ownership and point-scoring potential become relevant considerations for managers weighing up Liverpool assets.

The return of Salah, despite his announced departure, raises questions about his motivation and performance levels for the remainder of the season. Managers who retained him through injury will now need to decide whether to captain him or capitalize on his potential sale value during a favorable fixture run.
