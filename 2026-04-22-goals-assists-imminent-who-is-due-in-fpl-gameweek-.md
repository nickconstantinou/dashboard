## The Methodology

The Goals Imminent table identifies players who have accumulated at least nine shots over their last four matches—but scored no more than one goal in that window. It's a crude but effective filter: volume without output often signals a player caught in the gap between bad luck and bad finishing. Some are victims of tight margins, others are simply cold. The trick is separating the genuinely unlucky from the genuinely limited.

For Gameweek 34, that distinction matters more than ever.

## Why This Week Feels Different

Blank Gameweeks compress decision-making. Managers with limited chips or thin benches can't afford to carry dead weight. Identifying players who are mathematically "due" offers a way to stay active with conviction rather than guessing blindly.

The targets from last week's successes—players priced between £5.3m and £14.5m, spanning budget enablers to premium options—illustrate the range of assets the table can surface. Calvert-Lewin and Tavernier offer budget-friendly upside. Haaland remains the ceiling. Rogers is the differential type. Nunes proves defenders can belong on the same list as forwards.
