# 5 Strategies to Boost Your Fantasy Premier League Rank in the Final Seven Gameweeks

## The Full Story

With the Premier League set to resume after an international break, Fantasy Premier League managers across the globe are scrambling to identify the best strategies for maximizing points in the season's crucial final stretch. In an in-depth analysis published on Fantasy Football Scout, five-time top 1,000 finisher Tom Freeman outlines five potential approaches for managers looking to climb the rankings during the remaining seven Gameweeks.

The first strategy centers on investing heavily in Brighton's defense. Manager Fabian Hurzeler's side presents a compelling case for Fantasy managers, particularly those operating under a Gameweek 32 Wildcard, Double Gameweek 33 Bench Boost, Blank Gameweek 34 Free Hit strategy. Brighton visit Burnley in Gameweek 32, with subsequent fixtures against Tottenham Hotspur and Chelsea that could constitute a double gameweek. The Seagulls' fixtures don't stop there—additional favorable matchups include a home encounter against Wolverhampton Wanderers in Gameweek 36 and a trip to goal-shy Leeds United in Gameweek 37.

Recent form supports this defensive investment. Brighton have kept two clean sheets while conceding just four goals in their last six Premier League matches, ranking them top for expected goals conceded (xGC) in that period. Freeman argues that rather than splurging on Gameweek 32's most-bought player Danny Welbeck (£6.2m)—who may not feature in both matches of Double Gameweek 33 due to the tight turnaround—managers should consider loading up on the Albion backline instead.
