# Estevao Makes First Premier League Start as Chelsea Host Man City

Chelsea's teenage Brazilian sensation Estevao has been handed his first Premier League start for Sunday's clash with Manchester City at Stamford Bridge, with kick-off at 16:30 BST.

The 18-year-old, who joined from Palmeiras in a deal that could rise to around £51 million, lines up in an attacking quartet alongside Cole Palmer, Pedro Neto and Joao Pedro as interim manager Liam Rosenior makes two changes from Chelsea's FA Cup win over Port Vale. Marc Cucurella and Moises Caicedo come into the side, with Tosin Adarabioyo and Romeo Lavia dropping to the bench. Jorrel Hato shifts to centre-back to accommodate the changes.

The most notable absence from the matchday squad is Enzo Fernandez, who is unavailable following recent public comments about his future at the club. The Argentine midfielder's remarks, made outside the club's control, have left him sidelined as Chelsea navigate another turbulent episode in their rebuild.

For City, Pep Guardiola has made just one switch from their FA Cup fixture: Gianluigi Donnarumma replaces James Trafford in goal. The rest of the side remains unchanged, with Antoine Semenyo, Rayan Cherki and Jeremy Doku again supporting Erling Haaland in attack.

**Chelsea XI:** Sanchez, Gusto, Fofana, Hato, Cucurella, Caicedo, Santos, Estevao, Palmer, Neto, J Pedro
**Subs:** Sharman-Lowe, Acheampong, Tosin, Sarr, Essugo, Lavia, Garnacho, Guiu, Delap

**Manchester City XI:** Donnarumma, Nunes, Khusanov, Guehi, O'Reilly, Bernardo, Rodri, Semenyo, Cherki, Doku, Haaland
**Subs:** Trafford, Reijnders, Ake, Marmoush, Kovacic, Nico, Ait-Nouri, Savinho, Foden

Estevao's elevation to the starting XI represents the clearest signal yet that Chelsea view him as central to their project, even at this early stage of his adaptation to English football. How he combines with Palmer—the team's talisman—will be the tactical subplot worth monitoring in west London.
