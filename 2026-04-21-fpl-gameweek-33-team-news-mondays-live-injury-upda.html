FPL Gameweek 33: Estevao blow and Mitoma relief — Monday's live press conference updates

Chelsea and Brighton managers Liam Rosenior and Fabian Hurzeler addressed the media on Monday ahead of Tuesday's clash at the Amex Stadium — and for FPL managers, the headlines cut in opposite directions.

---

Chelsea: Estevao ruled out, but Pedro closer to a return

The most significant news from Cobham is on Estevao, whose hamstring problem has now been confirmed as a definite absence for the Brighton fixture. It is, as Rosenior put it, "a real shame." Further tests are ongoing, and the teenager's season is suddenly in serious doubt.

> "With Este, he's definitely out for tomorrow, which is a real shame. We're doing our further tests and investigation into his injury."

That leaves Chelsea's attacking options thinner than many would have hoped coming into a Double Gameweek. The club have no other fresh concerns from Saturday's defeat to Manchester United, but Estevao's omission is a blow to any manager who banked on him as a Differ.

More encouraging is the situation around Joao Pedro. The Brazilian striker missed the United loss with a minor thigh complaint but was back on the training pitch on Monday.

> "Joao will be a late call. He was out on the pitch today and we'll make a decision on him tomorrow morning. So, a good positive because he will be back sooner rather than later."

That phrasing — *sooner rather than later* — suggests Chelsea's medical staff are quietly optimistic. A late call means he won't be confirmed until Tuesday morning, which leaves FPL managers in a holding pattern, but the trajectory is positive.

Enzo Fernandez hobbled off at the weekend but has been cleared by Rosenior with a dismissive "absolutely fine." No concern there.

One name worth tracking for longer-term horizons: Levi Colwill played 60 minutes for the under-21s last Friday and is due to feature again this weekend as he builds match fitness following a long-term knee injury.

> "Very hopefully, yes, you can see him before the end of the season."

For those with an eye on pre-season, Colwill's recovery is a storyline worth monitoring. For GW33 purposes, he's not relevant.

No fresh updates were available on Reece James (hamstring), Jamie Gittens (hamstring), or Filip Jorgensen (groin) — all remain sidelined.

---

Brighton: Mitoma cramp clears, but Diego Gomez joins the casualty list

Brighton's press conference delivered better news on Kaoru Mitoma, who limped out of the Tottenham Hotspur game having only just come on as a substitute. Manager Fabian Hurzeler was quick to downplay the incident.

> "He just had some cramps. He felt a little bit in the warm-up but I think he had a very effective short performance on the pitch, being on the pitch scoring, helping us to get the equaliser, also had some good actions. So he just had minor, minor issues. He had a cramp and he will be available tomorrow."

That should reassure the considerable number of FPL managers who hold Mitoma. He is a confirmed starter for Brighton's Gameweek 33 double — and with Brighton one of the two clubs with a full double this week, his availability matters.

The flip side is Diego Gomez, who is out of the Chelsea match with a knee injury. The cautious optimism comes from the fact the problem is not as severe as initially feared.

> "Diego, he won't be available but it's quite positive news that it is not as bad as we expected."

The 21-commented version of the article was cut mid-sentence, so details on the precise nature of the injury — and his expected return timeline — are not yet available. What is clear is that he misses Tuesday's fixture at minimum.

Also confirmed absent are James Milner and March, as previously reported.

---

What it means for Gameweek 33

This is a Double Gameweek for both clubs, meaning every confirmed availability or absence carries extra weight. For Chelsea, Estevao's withdrawal directly impacts the一个人 — a popular budget midfielder for FPL purposes — and forces a rethink on squad construction. Joao Pedro's near-return adds a layer of uncertainty that will only resolve on Tuesday morning, leaving managers with a difficult call on whether to risk a bench spot.

For Brighton, Mitoma being fit is the main positive. Diego Gomez's injury is a setback but not a crisis, particularly given Hurzeler's relatively optimistic tone about the severity. The bigger question — what the Brighton attack looks like without Gomez and March — remains partly open.

Both managers return to the press on Tuesday after Tuesday's match for post-match reaction ahead of the next set of fixtures.
