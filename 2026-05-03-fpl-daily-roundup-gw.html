FPL Daily Roundup

Key FPL News and Injury Updates

The FPL landscape continues to shift as we approach the business end of the season, with several crucial injury developments demanding manager attention heading into this weekend's action.

Will Osula (£5.4m) has emerged as the standout budget option following another goal in Newcastle's 3-1 victory over Brighton. The young striker has now scored in three of his last four starts, representing remarkable value at his price point. However, Fantasy Football Scout's analysis highlights an important caveat: Osula remains Newcastle's fourth-choice striker in the attacking hierarchy, typically behind Anthony Gordon, Woltemade, and Wissa in the pecking order. This rotation risk means managers cannot rely on him as a set-and-forget option, though his current form makes him difficult to ignore as a differential.

Anthony Gordon (£7.3m) returned from a hip injury but was an unused substitute against Brighton, suggesting he is not yet ready for a starting role. Dan Burn (£5.0m) has capitalized on Lewis Hall's fall from favour, starting at left-back and scoring a set-piece goal. Lewis Hall (£5.3m) was hooked at halftime in Gameweek 33 and has warmed the bench since, making him a definite sell for anyone still holding him.

Jack Hinshelwood (£5.1m) came close to a hat-trick against Newcastle with a huge early chance that was saved by Nick Pope, indicating Brighton's attacking threat from full-back remains viable despite the defeat.

Brighton's Mats Wieffer (£4.9m) has a 50% chance of playing due to an ankle injury, making him a risky inclusion at his budget price. Mohamed Salah (£14.0m) remains out with a muscle injury and is not expected back until May 24th, effectively ruling him out for the remainder of the season and making him a candidate to ship out for more active alternatives.

Arsenal midfielder Martin Ødegaard has been ruled out entirely for the Fulham clash, presenting managers with a tricky selection headache for their Gunners assets. Despite these concerns, both Bukayo Saka and Eberechi Eze started against Fulham despite earlier fitness concerns, suggesting their issues are manageable.
