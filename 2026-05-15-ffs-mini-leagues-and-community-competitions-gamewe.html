FFS Mini-Leagues and Community Competitions – Gameweek 36

This article summarizes key highlights from Fantasy Football Scout's community competitions and mini-leagues during Gameweek 36, drawing from the approved claim ledger for this period.

---

Triple-Captain Trends and Haaland's Performance

Erling Haaland (£14.7m) remained the dominant captaincy choice among FPL managers in Gameweek 36. A total of 1,166,802 FPL managers selected him as their triple-captain, including six out of fifteen of the featured mini-leagues' leaders. Despite this overwhelming confidence, Haaland became a victim of significant Pep rotation during the gameweek, which impacted managers who had heavily invested in him.

---

FFS Members Cup: Quarter-Finals and Semi-Final Lineup

Gameweek 36 featured the quarter-finals of the 20th FFS Members Cup. The competition continued with the semi-finals seeing J Aravind (ranked 71,792nd) facing Manshizzle (ranked 936,427th), while Team Dependant (ranked 489,334th) took on UpminsterTrainman (ranked 82,699th).

---

League 1 Leadership Changes

Ahmed Shahin, operating under the team name Professor Bear, regained the lead in League 1 following a narrow 72-71 victory against Alex Merchant, who had held the top position the previous week.

---

Head-to-Head League Records

Jon Nash, competing in League 7 Division 64, became the new highest scorer in the Head-to-Head Leagues format, accumulating 93 points out of a possible 108.

---

TorresMagic's Last Man Standing

In TorresMagic's Last Man Standing competition, the safety score for Gameweek 36 was 83 after applying hits.

---

Individual Performance Highlights

Ben K, representing the WVA team, was the highest scorer for the gameweek with 88 points, achieved by triple-captaining Haaland.

FFS Live Hall of Fame and Mini-League Rankings

The FFS Live Hall of Fame now ranks 55,814 FPL managers who participate in Fantasy Football Scout and Fantasy Football Scout Members mini-leagues. New entrant Chris Field leads the main Fantasy Football Scout mini-league (access code: ql9e6k) and has risen to 19th position worldwide.
