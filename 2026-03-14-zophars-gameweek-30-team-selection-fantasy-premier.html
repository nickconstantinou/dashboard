Zophar's Gameweek 30 Team Selection | Fantasy Premier League Tips 2025/26

Introduction

Fantasy Premier League managers up and down the country are facing one of the most chaotic periods of the season, and Zophar is no exception. In his latest team selection video for Gameweek 30, recorded on a Friday morning, the content creator walks viewers through a week that saw his personal FPL season hit its lowest point while simultaneously throwing the entire FPL calendar into disarray through European results. The video captures a manager attempting to navigate uncertainty—specifically the ripple effects from midweek Champions League and Europa League matches that have cast doubt over which fixtures will actually occur in upcoming gameweeks.

Last Gameweek was, by Zophar's own admission, an absolute disaster. He describes it as his worst gameweek of the season by "some distance," finishing with a rank of 10.8 million—essentially 11 million—and a meager 37 points. The only returners from his squad were Gabriel and Bruno Fernandes, both at approximately 100% ownership, meaning he gained absolutely no rank whatsoever. His rank plummeted from 250,000 to around 500,000 in a single gameweek. The transfer strategy that led to this point, particularly his move for the men's transfer and subsequent decisions, has been "an absolute train wreck," with Timber O'Reilly and other replacements racking up points while his original picks blanked repeatedly.

European Results and Their FPL Ramifications

The English teams' performances in Europe this week have created a complex puzzle for FPL managers, and Zophar dedicates significant time to analyzing exactly what happened and what it means for fixture planning. Liverpool suffered a 1-0 away defeat to Galatasaray, though Zophar believes this is a result they can overturn in the second leg at Anfield. Arsenal secured a 1-1 draw away to Leus, a result Zophar characterizes as "not bad"—the Gunners will be heavy favorites to progress and potentially face Bordo in the next round, a tie he finds "interesting" given Bordo's form as the strongest team in Europe right now.

However, two results stand out as particularly concerning for FPL purposes. Chelsea's 5-2 defeat to PSG was "very bad," especially considering they were level at 2-2 at one point before conceding three goals in the final 15-20 minutes—a collapse Zophar describes as "absolutely calamitous." Meanwhile, Manchester City suffered a significant defeat to Real Madrid, and while City will attempt to overturn the deficit, Zophar acknowledges that "I don't think city will be over able to overturn such a large deficit now."

The scheduling implications are substantial. Both Chelsea and Manchester City played on Wednesday and are scheduled to play again on Tuesday in Gameweek 30—a incredibly tight turnaround. The critical question becomes: how will these teams react? Will they accept that a three-goal deficit in the Champions League essentially ends their European hopes and prioritize the Premier League? Or will they continue to field strong lineups in case they can mount an improbable comeback?

Zophar's assessment is nuanced. He believes both clubs "have to play a very much first choice" lineups against West Ham and Newcastle respectively, because jeopardizing their league positions while also failing to progress in Europe would represent a worst-case scenario. However, he expects early substitutions for key players like Haaland, Pedro, and Palmer. The specific fixtures matter here—West Ham versus Manchester City and Newcastle versus Chelsea are far from straightforward matches given how those teams are currently performing. For Arsenal, the home fixture against Everton causes less rotation concern, while Liverpool's situation is "a little bit interesting" given they played Tuesday against Galatasaray and don't play again until Sunday against Tottenham, with a second leg against Galatasaray following on Wednesday.

Chip Strategy Up in the Air

Prior to this week's European results, Zophar had developed a chip strategy centered around his current squad composition. He explained that rather than wildcarding in Gameweek 32 as originally planned, he was considering a Free Hit in Gameweek 33 followed by a Wildcard in Gameweek 35. The reasoning stemmed from his roster construction—he has accumulated several Manchester United players who have favorable fixtures in both Gameweek 32 and Gameweek 34, and similarly identified Brentford and Liverpool as potential transfer targets based on their fixture runs.

However, the European results have introduced significant doubt into this approach. The core uncertainty revolves around whether certain fixtures will remain in their current gameweeks or get moved. The situation is fluid: Champions League and Europa League fixtures could shift to Gameweek 32 if teams are eliminated, creating potential double gameweeks, or they could remain in their original slots if teams progress. This creates what Zophar describes as "impending chaos in the near future."

The fundamental challenge is that the next few gameweeks are "free midweeks" that were pencilled in for European matches. If teams get eliminated, those slots become available for Premier League fixtures. This means the entire fixture list could look dramatically different depending on Champions League and Europa League results over the next two weeks. Zophar acknowledges he cannot predict exactly what will happen with the schedule, stating: "There's way too many things that can happen in terms of the gains being moved to 32, gains being moved to remaining in 34."

His solution is to approach transfers with a fixture-agnostic mindset—building a team that will perform well regardless of which fixtures ultimately occur. As he puts it: "So pretty much good irrespective of a fixture." The goal now is to "sail through blank Gameweek 31 using minimal amount of transfers and still keep my options open for 32 and 34." This means prioritizing teams with good fixture runs across the entire period rather than focusing on any specific gameweek.

Fixture Analysis: Teams to Target

Zophar presents a detailed fixture ticker analysis, removing Gameweek 33 from consideration since it appears to be a blank gameweek for most teams. Several teams emerge as attractive options for the upcoming run.

Liverpool presents what Zophar considers the preferred option. Despite acknowledging that "Liverpool is just very difficult to predict away from home," he sees value in their upcoming fixtures: Tottenham (home), Brighton (away), Fulham (home), and Crystal Palace (home). That's three home games in this fixture block, with the away match at Brighton being one they traditionally perform well in. Liverpool attackers represent the best investment, though Zophar notes concern about Salah after Liverpool's European performance—"really really poor"—and mentions that Liverpool podcasts are "not very confident of a Salah start" with some fans even asking for him to be dropped. This represents a worrying sign for one of FPL's most owned players.

Fulham emerges as another good option, particularly if Harry Wilson is fit for Gameweek 30. Zophar identifies three good home games in their upcoming schedule and rates Wilson as his "top transfer target" if available. The appeal lies in Fulham's favorable fixture run without the volatility inherent in Liverpool's away performances.

Bournemouth offers a potentially excellent fixture against Burnley—which Zophar calls "the best one in the league"—though he rates them "lower down my pecking order" compared to Liverpool and Fulham due to a tougher run including an Arsenal away fixture.

Chelsea presents a more complicated picture. While they're an option for the short term in Gameweeks 30 and 31, Zophar expresses hesitation: "So, not so keen on investing in Chelsea assets." The concerns stem from their tight schedule and the uncertainty around rotation following their heavy Champions League defeat.

Newcastle and Leverton are mentioned as decent options but come with a significant drawback—they could still blank in Gameweek 34, which makes them "a little bit offputting" for managers building fixture-proof squads.

Current Team Analysis and Decision Points

Zophar walks through his current squad setup for Gameweek 30, which provides insight into the specific decisions he's facing. His team features two free transfers with £9 million in the bank, a relatively healthy position that provides flexibility.

In goal, he has Verbugen, while his defensive setup includes Virgil (van Dijk), Gabriel, and Munoz—though Munoz doesn't play this week, meaning Anderson will sub on instead. Zophar notes that "a lot of people think he won't potentially start against Forest, but I still think he's their best center back," indicating he'll play Anderson and hope for the best while monitoring the situation for Gameweek 31.

His midfield and forward options include Rice, Bruno Fernandes (who delivered in Gameweek 29), Bumo (Nicolas Jackson), Dango, Krooppy (James Tavernier), Holland, Thiago with Dubrfaka on the bench, Rogers (Marcus Rashford), Anderson, and Moki (Moises Caicedo). This construction reflects his earlier decisions to invest in Manchester United assets, though the returns have been disappointing.

The Krooppy situation deserves special attention given its importance to Zophar's immediate plans. Planet FPL conducted a special clash of the correspondents this week featuring the Burnley and Bournemouth experts, and the Bournemouth representative Neil Grover—whom Zophar describes as "quite knowledgeable"—expressed confidence that Krooppy will start the next two matches. Grover noted that the only time Krooppy has been benched in the last ten gameweeks has been when Bournemouth had midweek games, and with a full week of rest before the Manchester United match, he believes Krooppy is likely to start.

Zophar's conclusion: "So I don't really want to sell Ky when he's playing Burnley. The minutes will likely be reduced but the potential is still there. So let's let him stick for now." This represents a calculated gamble that the fixture against Burnley and Krooppy's likely continued starts make holding worthwhile despite the potential for reduced minutes.

Key Takeaways for FPL Managers

The European results have fundamentally altered the FPL landscape. Manchester City's likely Champions League elimination could create a double in Gameweek 32, while Chelsea's precarious position creates similar uncertainty. Managers cannot rely on their original fixture plans and must adopt a more flexible approach.

Fixture-agnostic transfers are essential right now. Rather than targeting specific gameweeks, managers should build teams that will perform regardless of fixture movements. This means focusing on teams with generally favorable runs across the entire period rather than chase single gameweek spikes.

Liverpool attackers remain the preferred option despite concerns. While Salah's recent form is worrying and Liverpool's away performances are unpredictable, their fixture run of three home games against Tottenham, Fulham, and Palace represents the most favorable block in the league. The potential for rotation exists but the ceiling remains high.

Krooppy represents a hold rather than a sell. Despite reduced minutes being likely, the combination of favorable fixtures (Burnley at home, then Manchester United) and the likelihood of continued starts makes selling premature. The Bournemouth expert's insight that midweek rest leads to starts is valuable context.

Chelsea assets require caution. The combination of their Champions League disaster, tight fixture turnaround, and potentially rotated lineups makes investing in Chelsea players risky for managers seeking stability during this chaotic period.
